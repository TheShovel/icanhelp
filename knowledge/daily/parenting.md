# Parenting Basics

## Newborn (0-3 months)
- Feed every 2-3 hours (breast/formula) — newborn stomach = size of cherry
- Sleep: 14-17 hours/day in 2-4 hour stretches — they'll wake to feed
- Safe sleep: on back, firm mattress, no blankets/pillows/toys in crib (SIDS prevention)
- Tummy time: supervised, start with 1-2 min, build up — prevents flat head, builds neck strength
- Soothing: swaddle, shush, swing, side-lying (the 5 S's — Harvey Karp)
- Diapers: change 8-12/day in early weeks, prevent rash with barrier cream
- Signs of hunger: rooting, sucking hands, smacking lips (crying is late sign)
- Burp after feeding, hold upright 20-30 min after (reflux)
- Trust your gut — you know your baby better than anyone

## Baby (3-12 months)
- Solids start ~6 months (sit up with support, lost tongue thrust reflex)
- First foods: pureed, one ingredient at a time (avocado, sweet potato, banana)
- BLW (Baby Led Weaning): skip purees, offer soft finger foods (steamed broccoli, toast strips)
- Never honey before 1 year (botulism risk)
- Milestones: roll (4m), sit (6m), crawl (8m), stand (10m), walk (12m) — ranges vary widely
- Teething: starts ~6 months, symptoms (drool, fussy, chew everything) — cold teether, Tylenol if painful
- Sleep training: Ferber (graduated checks), chair method, pick-up-put-down — from ~4-6 months
- Baby-proof: outlet covers, corner guards, secure furniture to wall, lock cabinets, gate stairs

## Toddler (1-3 years)
- Discipline: redirection > punishment. Set clear, consistent boundaries
- Tantrums: normal brain development — they can't regulate emotions yet
  - Stay calm, name feeling ("you're frustrated"), offer choice ("do you want the red cup or blue?")
  - Don't give in to tantrum (reinforces behavior), but validate the feeling
- Potty training: start when ready (stays dry 2h, interested, can pull pants down)
  - Readiness varies 18-36 months — don't push, signaling from child matters
- Independence: let them make choices (two options, both OK)
- Picky eating: normal — keep offering without pressure, exposure matters, one bite rule
- Language: 50 words by 2 years, 2-3 word sentences by 2.5 — if concerned, talk to pediatrician

## Child (3-12 years)
- School readiness: follow instructions, share, take turns, hold pencil, recognize letters
- Friendships: practice sharing, empathy, conflict resolution, taking turns
- Homework: establish routine, check-in but don't hover, praise effort not outcome
- Social media: delay as long as possible (recommended minimum 13, but many wait to 16)
- Chores: age-appropriate responsibilities build competence and contribute to family
- Allow boredom: unstructured time fosters creativity, problem-solving
- Self-esteem: praise effort ("you worked hard") not fixed traits ("you're so smart")
- Discipline: natural consequences (if you don't wear coat, you'll be cold) > punishment

## General Parenting Principles
- **Attachment**: secure attachment = consistent, responsive caregiving — foundation for all relationships
- **Connection before correction**: when child misbehaves, connect first then address behavior
- **Emotional coaching**: validate all feelings, set limits on behavior ("it's OK to be angry, it's not OK to hit")
- **Repair**: after conflict, reconnect — model apologies ("I'm sorry I yelled, I was frustrated")
- **Consistency**: rules and consequences consistent across times and caregivers
- **Autonomy support**: let child do things for themselves (as much as safely possible)
- **Time-in vs time-out**: sit with child to calm down vs isolate — more effective for emotional regulation

## Common Parenting Challenges
- **Sleep regression**: common at 4m, 8m, 18m, 2yr — growth spurts + developmental leaps disrupt sleep — temporary
- **Separation anxiety**: peaks ~9-18 months — normal, show confidence when leaving, don't sneak out
- **Biting/hitting**: > usually communication breakdown — teach words, remove from situation, stay calm
- **Lying**: normal developmental phase (ages 3-7 can't distinguish fantasy/reality) — focus on honesty, not punishment
- **Whining**: sign of need (tired, hungry, overstimulated) — address need, don't reward whining with attention
- **Screen time**: under 2: avoid (except video calls). 2-5: 1 hr/day. 6+: consistent limits, prioritize sleep/exercise/social

## Safety
- Car seat: rear-facing as long as possible (min 2 years, max height/weight of seat)
- Water: children can drown in 2 inches — constant supervision near water
- Sun: SPF 30+ mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide), hats, UPF clothing
- Poison: 1-800-222-1222 (US Poison Control), keep all chemicals/meds locked
- Choking: no whole grapes, hot dogs, nuts, hard candy before 4 (cut into half lengthwise then quarters)
- Stranger danger: outdated — most child abuse is by known adults — teach body autonomy, correct names for body parts
- Internet: monitor, talk about online safety, use parental controls (not as replacement for conversation)
