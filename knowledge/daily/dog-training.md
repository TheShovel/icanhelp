# Dog Training

Basic principles and techniques for dog behavior training.

## Positive Reinforcement
- **Reward desired behavior**: Treat, praise, toy, or play immediately after dog performs correct action
- **Timing is critical**: Reward within 1 second of behavior — dog associates reward with action
- **Variable reinforcement**: Once behavior is learned, reward intermittently — makes behavior more persistent
- **No punishment**: Yelling, hitting, alpha rolls damage trust and increase anxiety — can cause aggression
- **Redirection**: Replace unwanted behavior with acceptable alternative — chew toy instead of shoe

## Core Commands
- **Sit**: Hold treat above dog's nose, move back over head — butt lowers automatically. Mark ("Yes!") + reward
- **Stay**: Ask for sit, palm out "Stay", step back one foot, wait 1 sec, return, reward. Gradually increase distance and duration
- **Down**: Take treat from nose straight down to floor between paws — dog follows into down. Mark + reward
- **Come (Recall)**: Excited tone "Come!", run backward, reward when they reach you. Practice on long leash first
- **Leave it**: Treat in closed fist, dog sniffs — wait for them to back off, mark "Yes!", reward from other hand. Progress to floor, then moving objects
- **Heel**: Walk with loose leash, dog at your side — reward for checking in/eye contact, change direction when they pull

## Crate Training
- **Make crate positive**: Feed meals in crate, toss treats inside, never use as punishment
- **Gradual closing**: Start with door open, then close for 1 min while feeding, slowly increase duration
- **Puppy schedule**: 1 hour awake → potty break → crate for 2 hours (age in months = max hours they can hold it)
- **Never let dog cry it out** for long periods — crying indicates need for potty or distress

## Potty Training
- **Frequent potty breaks**: Every 1-2 hours for puppies, after eating, drinking, waking, playing
- **Designated spot**: Always take to same outdoor location — scent triggers elimination
- **Supervision**: Tether dog to you indoors — if they start circling/sniffing, immediately take outside
- **Accidents**: Clean with enzymatic cleaner (Nature's Miracle) — regular cleaners leave scent attracting repeat marking
- **Schedule**: Regular feeding times = predictable elimination times

## Common Behavior Issues
- **Jumping**: Turn away, cross arms, no eye contact — reward when all four paws on floor. Consistency across all family members
- **Pulling on leash**: Stop walking when leash tightens. Resume only when leash loosens. "Be a tree" method
- **Barking**: Teach "Quiet" — reward silence, then use "Speak" to teach on/off control. Address root cause (boredom, fear, alert)
- **Chewing**: Provide appropriate chews (Kong, bully stick, Nylabone), rotate toys to prevent boredom, manage environment (put shoes away)
- **Resource guarding**: Trade up — drop low-value item, get high-value treat. Never chase or pry mouth open
- **Separation anxiety**: Practice short departures, desensitize departure cues (jingle keys, put on shoes), leave Kong with frozen treats

## Socialization (Puppy Critical Period 3-16 weeks)
- **Positive exposure**: People (all ages, appearances), dogs (vaccinated, calm), surfaces (grass, concrete, tile), sounds (vacuum, traffic, thunder), handling (ears, paws, mouth)
- **Puppy classes**: Supervised play with other puppies — teaches bite inhibition, dog social skills
- **One new experience per day** — keep sessions short, positive, treat-rich
- **Avoid flooding**: If puppy shows fear, retreat and create positive association from distance

## Clicker Training
- **Charge the clicker**: Click + treat repeatedly — dog learns click = reward coming
- **Capture**: Click when dog naturally does desired behavior (sits on own) — marks exact moment
- **Shape**: Reinforce successive approximations — reward moving toward ultimate behavior
- **Free shaping**: Let dog offer behaviors, click and reward what's wanted — requires patience, builds creativity

## Body Language (Stress Signals)
- **Displacement behaviors**: Lip licking, yawning, scratching (when not itchy) — signs of stress
- **Whale eye**: Showing whites of eyes — discomfort, possible impending bite
- **Tail position**: High stiff = alert/aggressive; low/tucked = fear; loose wag = happy
- **Ears**: Pinned back = fear; forward = alert; relaxed = comfortable
- **Mouth**: Panting with corners pulled back = stress; relaxed open mouth = happy; closed tense = anxiety
- **Shake off**: Full body shake after stressful event — releases tension, like reset button
