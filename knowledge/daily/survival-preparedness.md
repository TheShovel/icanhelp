# Emergency & Disaster Preparedness (Advanced)

## Bug-Out Bag (BOB)
- **Purpose**: survive 72 hours if you need to evacuate immediately (wildfire, flood, hurricane, chemical spill, earthquake). Keep packed and ready by exit door. "Go bag" is immediate survival, not long-term
  - One per person, plus car kit. Grab and leave. Also called "Get Home Bag" if at work — keep at office too
- **Contents**: water (3L, plus filter/purification tablets for refills). Food (3-day no-cook: protein bars, nuts, dried fruit, MREs, freeze-dried meals). First aid kit (trauma supplies + medications). Shelter (emergency bivvy/space blanket, mylar tent, lightweight tarp). Fire (lighter + waterproof matches + ferro rod). Light (headlamp + extra batteries, flashlight). Multi-tool + knife. Navigation (paper map of area + compass — GPS may fail). Communication (whistle (signal), AM/FM radio (hand-crank/battery/solar), cell phone + portable charger), Cash ($100-300 in small bills — ATMs + credit cards may not work). Extra clothes (socks, underwear, insulating layer, rain gear). Personal documents (copies of ID, insurance, medical info, emergency contacts in waterproof bag). Sanitation (wipes, hand sanitizer, trash bags, TP). Sun protection. Dust masks (N95)
- **Backpack**: comfortable (you may carry for miles). Not tactical-looking (blends in if needed). Water-resistant or rain cover
- **Maintenance**: check every 6 months — rotate food/water expiration, update documents, adjust for season (warm vs cold weather clothes)

## INCH Bag (I'm Never Coming Home)
- **Extended survival**: if you can't return home for weeks+ (major disaster, evacuation lasting months, SHTF). 50-70L pack, heavier, more comprehensive
  - All BOB items plus: shelter (tent, tarp, sleeping bag rated for weather). More food + water (7-14 days). Water filter (Sawyer Squeeze, Grayl, Katadyn). Cooking pot + stove (isobutane/MSR MicroRocket or alcohol stove. Or Esbit solid fuel tabs for lighter weight) + fuel. More comprehensive first aid (tourniquet, chest seal, suture kit, dental repair, meds: broad-spectrum antibiotics (if prescribed) , tramadol, inhaler + antihistamine + epi). Cordage (paracord 100 ft). Repair items (duct tape, sewing kit, zip ties, wire). More tools (folding saw or wire saw, hatchet or handsaw). Waterproof storage (dry bags, ziplocs). Maps (regional — know multiple exit routes for each likely scenario). Means to signal (signal mirror, whistle, flares, strobe/light, orange signal panel). Hygiene (soap, toothbrush, hand sanitizer, wet wipes, menstrual products). Cash + silver coins (barter)

## Shelter-in-Place
- For: chemical spill, tornado, active shooter (different — "shelter" against wind vs contamination). For radioactive event: go to basement and wait (gamma radiation). For chemical: go upstairs, seal room (plastic + tape over doors/windows). For tornado: basement/interior room, no windows, cover head. For active shooter (lockdown): hide + lock door + barricade + silence phone
- **Preps**: 2 weeks food + water (minimum, 1 gal/person/day). Medical supplies + RX meds 30 days. Backup power (generator, solar, power station). Heat without power (propane heater with CO detector, wood stove, extra blankets, sleeping bags rated for cold). Communication (ham radio, FRS/GMRS, CB). Cash + important documents. Skills over gear

## Water Purification
- **Boil**: 1 minute rolling boil (3 min above 6,500 ft). Kills bacteria, viruses, protozoa. Requires fuel, time, cooling. Best emergency option if you have fuel
- **Chemical**: iodine tablets (effective against bacteria + viruses, questionable against Cryptosporidium — cryptosporidium resistant to chlorine + iodine. Iodine: leaves taste, not safe for pregnant women/long-term). Chlorine drops (AquaMira: chlorine dioxide — effective against all pathogens, no taste, works 4 hours). Also: Pool shock (calcium hypochlorite): treat bulk water without added iodine. NOT for regular use long-term (too high dose)
- **Filter**: removes bacteria + protozoa (0.2-0.5 micron). Sawyer Squeeze (lightweight, fast, backflushable, lasts years. Can thread onto standard water bottle. Best budget personal filter). Katadyn Hiker/BeFree (sturdy, faster, replaceable cartridge). Platypus GravityWorks (for groups, gravity-fed — no pumping, good base camp). Grayl (press-filter, also removes viruses)
  - Viruses: tiny, most filters DON'T remove (unless rated 0.02 micron or smaller). Chemical treatment or UV needed for virus protection. Most US/Canadian wilderness water no virus risk
- **UV**: SteriPEN — UV light kills all microorganisms (30-90 seconds). Requires batteries, water must be clear (pre-filter if sediment present), good for speed + taste. Doesn't remove sediment

## Food Storage
- **Long-term storage**: low oxygen + cool (<70°F) + dark + dry. Oxygen absorbers (200-2000cc) in mylar bags → long-term (25-30yr for grains/beans). Rotate stock (first in, first out — FIFO)
  - Packaging: 5 gallon buckets + gamma seal lids (easy access). Mylar bags + oxygen absorbers (seal with iron, store in buckets). Vacuum seal jars (canning jars with food saver accessory). Glass jars + oxygen absorbers + light-free
- **Staples**: white rice (25-30 yr). Hard red wheat (30+ yr). Rolled oats (15-20 yr). Pasta (10-15 yr in sealed bucket). Dry beans (5-10 yr if sealed well). Lentils (5-8 yr). Sugar/honey (indefinite). Salt (indefinite). Powdered milk (10-20 yr in #10 cans). Dehydrated vegetables. Freeze-dried meat (15-20 yr)
  - Rotate: #10 cans of freeze-dried/dehydrated (Mountain House, Augason Farms — 10+ years. #10 cans store well). Store bulk ingredients, not just prepackaged. Build on what you eat: pasta, pancake mix, canned goods (5-yr shelf life). Store foods you actually cook with
- **Amounts**: 2,000-2,500 cal/person/day. 300-400 lbs of grains/beans for 1 year (family of 4: 400 lbs + 100 lbs fat/sugar). 1 lb grain provides ~1,600 cal; 1 lb beans ~1,500 cal. Needs to be supplemented with fat + oil for palatability

## Medical Preparedness
- **Skills**: CPR + basic first aid (Red Cross courses, Stop the Bleed + Wilderness First Aid + CERT (Community Emergency Response Team — FEMA). Learn: suturing (home: use butterfly strips + steri-strips; don't deep suture unless trained — infection risk). Tourniquet application (CAT Gen7 — effective one-handed, $25-30). Treating: wound care, splinting fractures (SAM splint), dealing with shock (head down, feet up, warm, elevate bleeding limb), hypothermia (warm, dry, warm sweet drinks). Dental: temporary filling material (Dentemp, Cavit), toothache kit (clove oil, zinc oxide)

## Communication
- **Ham radio (amateur radio)**: need license (Technician class = no Morse code test, multiple choice written exam). Repeaters: extend range to ~20-100 miles depending. HF: world-wide, Extra Class allowed on HF bands above 30 MHz. During disaster: ham radio operators support emergency comms (ARES/RACES)
  - Baofeng UV-5R ($25-35): cheap, functional, but limited power (5W), poor receiver front end. Better: Icom V80, Yaesu FT-60R — higher build quality, ease of use
- **FRS/GMRS**: no license needed (FRS)/license required (GMRS, $70 FCC, 10 year, covers family). Range 1-2 miles (FRS) to 5-15 miles (GMRS with repeater). Good for family communication during emergencies (check on relatives, meet at rendezvous)
- **Satellite communicator/PLB**: Garmin InReach (two-way texting + SOS, subscription ~$15/mo). ZOLEO (global satellite texting + SOS, similar). Personal Locator Beacon (RescueLink, ACR, $250-400, one-time purchase, no subscription, transmit emergency position to SAR via COSPAS-SARSAT). For backcountry travel, PLB is cheaper than subscription but can receive/transmit only during emergency. For family comms + emergency: InReach

## Skills Over Gear (Most important)
- **Water**: finding + purifying (dig in dry riverbed, collect rain, solar still). Source: first check: near human settlement — pipes, ponds.
- **Fire**: multiple ways — ferro rod (practice), bow drill (primitive, requires much practice — 10+ attempts for many, hours first time). Practice: tinder types (birch bark, fatwood, cattail fluff, processed cotton balls with petroleum jelly, char cloth)
- **Shelter**: natural (lean-to, debris hut). Selecting site: windbreak, water source, flat ground, avoid widowmakers (dead trees). Insulating from ground is more important than overhead cover
- **Navigation**: map + compass (practice before need — orienting map, measuring bearings, triangulation (resection: find direction to two landmarks, intersect). Reading terrain (contour lines, ridges, draws). Celestial navigation (sun rises east, sets west, Polaris north)
- **First aid**: treating injuries without hospital. Wound cleaning (sterile irrigation, honey has some antibacterial, maggots as debrider — only if no alternative). Infection management (antibiotics only in real emergency)
- **Gardening**: growing food — start practicing now, don't expect to learn after SHTF when immediate survival is at stake
- **Security/hunting**: trapping (snares, deadfalls — practice ID animal tracks), fishing (multiple methods: trotline, limb line, fish hooks set at night). Self-defense (basic, within legal means). IGNORE THE RUMBLE: discipline + situational awareness > weapons
