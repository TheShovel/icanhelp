# Cooking Fundamentals & Techniques

## Overview
Cooking is the application of heat to food to make it safe, digestible, and delicious. Understanding the science behind techniques allows improvisation and consistent results.

## Heat Transfer Methods

| Method | Mechanism | Temp Range | Best For |
|--------|-----------|------------|----------|
| **Conduction** | Direct contact | 300-500°F | Searing, pan-frying |
| **Convection** | Fluid circulation | 200-450°F | Roasting, baking, air fryer |
| **Radiation** | Electromagnetic waves | 500°F+ | Broiling, grilling |
| **Combination** | Multiple | Varies | Most cooking |

### Maillard Reaction
- **Temp**: 280-330°F (140-165°C)
- **Requires**: Protein + reducing sugar + low moisture + heat
- **Creates**: 1000+ flavor compounds, browning
- **Not caramelization** (sugar only, 320°F+)

### Caramelization
- **Temp**: 320-360°F (160-180°C)
- **Sugars only**: No protein needed
- **Creates**: Nutty, complex sweetness

### Technique
- **Pinch grip**: Thumb + forefinger on blade, wrap fingers
- **Claw hand**: Fingertips tucked, knuckles guide blade
- **Rock chop**: Tip on board, rock heel through
- **Keep sharp**: Sharp = safe (less force, more control)

## Cooking Methods

### Dry Heat

#### Searing / Pan-Frying
```text
Keys:
- Dry surface (pat protein, air-dry in fridge)
- Hot pan, then oil (shimmering, not smoking)
- Don't crowd (steams instead of sears)
- Don't move until release (Maillard forms bond)
- Finish in oven if thick (>1.5")
```

#### Roasting / Baking
```text
Roasting: 400-450°F, uncovered, caramelization
Baking:   300-375°F, often covered, gentle

Convection: -25°F or -25% time
```

#### Grilling / Broiling
```text
Grilling: Heat from below, lid down = convection
Broiling: Heat from above, door cracked

Zones: Direct (sear) + Indirect (finish)
```

#### Deep Frying
```text
Temp: 325-375°F (use thermometer)
Oil: High smoke point (peanut, canola, avocado)
Don't crowd: Drops temp → greasy food
Drain: Wire rack, not paper towels (traps steam)
```

### Moist Heat

#### Boiling (212°F / 100°C)
```text
Rolling boil: Large bubbles, vigorous
Simmer: 185-205°F, small bubbles at edges
Poach: 160-180°F, no bubbles, shimmer
```

#### Steaming
```text
Gentle, preserves nutrients/color
212°F but no agitation
Basket above water, lid tight
```

#### Braising / Stewing
```text
Braise: Large cuts, partial liquid, 300-325°F oven
Stew: Small pieces, fully submerged, stovetop/oven

Steps: Sear → Aromatics → Deglaze → Liquid → Low & Slow
```

#### Sous Vide
```text
Precision: ±0.1°F
Pasteurization: Time × Temp (e.g., 130°F × 2hr = safe)
Finish: Quick sear for crust
```

## Flavor Building

### The Flavor Triangle
```
SALT        → Enhances, suppresses bitterness
ACID        → Brightens, balances fat/richness
FAT         → Carries flavor, mouthfeel
HEAT        → Transforms, develops complexity
```

## Stocks & Sauces

### Basic Stock Ratio
```
Bones : Mirepoix : Water = 1 : 1/4 : 2-3 (by weight)
Simmer: 4-6hr (chicken), 6-8hr (beef), 30-45min (fish/veg)
Skim impurities, don't boil (cloudy)
Cool fast: Ice bath → fridge → freeze
```

### Mother Sauces (French)
1. **Béchamel** - Milk + roux (white)
2. **Velouté** - Stock + roux (blond)
3. **Espagnole** - Brown stock + brown roux
4. **Tomato** - Tomato + aromatics
5. **Hollandaise** - Egg yolk + butter emulsion

### Modern Pan Sauce
```
1. Sear protein, remove
2. Pour off excess fat, leave fond
3. Sauté shallots/garlic
4. Deglaze (wine, stock, vinegar) - scrape fond
5. Reduce by 1/2-2/3
6. Finish: Butter (mount), herbs, acid
```

### Emulsions
| Type | Ratio | Examples |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Vinaigrette** | 3 oil : 1 acid | Salad dressing |
| **Mayo** | 1 yolk : 1 cup oil | Aioli, sauces |
| **Hollandaise** | 1 yolk : 3-4oz butter | Eggs Benedict |
| **Beurre Blanc** | 1/2 cup wine reduction : 1 lb butter | Fish |
| **Pan sauce** | Reduction + butter mount | Steak, chicken |

**Keys**: Temperature control, slow addition, agitation

## Eggs

### Methods
| Method | Temp | Time | Notes |
|--------|------|------|-------|
| **Soft boil** | 212°F | 6-6:30 min | Ice bath immediately |
| **Jammy** | 212°F | 7-7:30 min | Slightly runny yolk |
| **Hard boil** | 212°F | 10-12 min | Green ring = overcooked |
| **Poach** | 180-190°F | 3-4 min | Vortex, vinegar, fresh eggs |
| **Scramble** | Low | 3-5 min | Constant stir = small curds |
| **Fried** | Med-low | 2-3 min | Sunny: cover, Over: flip |
| **Omelet** | Med-high | 1-2 min | Roll, don't fold |

### Custard Ratio
```
Quiche/Flourless: 1 egg : 1/2 cup dairy
Crème brûlée:     1 yolk : 1/3 cup cream
Pastry cream:     1 yolk : 1/2 cup milk + starch
```

## Pasta

### Water Ratio
```
4-6 quarts water per pound pasta
Salt: 1-2 tbsp per 4 quarts (should taste like sea)
Oil: Don't add (prevents sauce adhesion)
```

### Cooking
```
1. Boil, salt heavily
2. Add pasta, stir immediately
3. Cook 1-2 min LESS than package
4. Reserve 1 cup pasta water
5. Drain, DON'T rinse (starch = sauce adhesion)
6. Finish in sauce + pasta water (emulsifies)
```

### Shapes & Sauces
| Shape | Sauce |
|-------|-------|

| Tubular (penne) | Chunky, meat/veg |
| Ribbons (fettuccine) | Cream, cheese |
| Shapes (farfalle) | Pesto, light cream |
| Small (orzo) | Soups, salads |
| Stuffed (ravioli) | Butter/sage, light tomato |

## Meat Cookery

### Doneness Temps (Remove 5°F before target)
| Doneness | Beef/Lamb | Pork | Poultry |
|----------|-----------|------|---------|
| Rare | 125°F | - | - |
| Medium-rare | 135°F | 145°F | - |
| Medium | 145°F | 150°F | - |
| Medium-well | 150°F | 155°F | - |
| Well | 160°F | 160°F | 165°F |
| Ground | 160°F | 160°F | 165°F |

### Resting
```
Thin (steak): 5-10 min
Thick (roast): 15-30 min
Rule: 1 min per 100g or 5 min per inch
Internal temp rises 5-10°F (carryover)
```

### Reverse Sear (Thick Cuts >1.5")
```
1. 225°F oven to 10-15°F below target
2. Rest 10 min
3. Screaming hot pan, 45-60s/side
```

### Brining
| Type | Ratio | Time |
|------|-------|------|
| **Wet brine** | 4-6% salt (40-60g/L) | 1hr/lb (max 24hr) |
| **Dry brine** | 0.5-1% salt by weight | 12-48hr uncovered fridge |

## Vegetables

### Blanching
```
1. Salted boiling water
2. Cook until bright + crisp-tender
3. Shock in ice water (stops cooking, sets color)
4. Drain, pat dry
5. Reheat in butter/pan before serving
```

### Roasting
```
425-450°F, single layer, don't crowd
Cut uniform size
Toss with oil, salt
Flip halfway
Finish with acid/herbs
```

### Don't Overcook
- Green veg: Bright green = done (7 min max boil)
- Root veg: Fork tender
- Cruciferous: Sweet, not sulfurous

## Baking Basics

### Measurements
```
WEIGHT > VOLUME
Flour: 1 cup = 120-125g (spoon & level)
Sugar: 1 cup = 200g
Butter: 1 stick = 113g = 1/2 cup = 8 tbsp
```

### Key Ratios
| Item | Ratio (by weight) |
|------|-------------------|
| **Pie crust** | 3 flour : 2 fat : 1 water |
| **Biscuits** | 3 flour : 1 fat : 2 liquid |
| **Muffins** | 2 flour : 2 liquid : 1 egg : 1 fat |
| **Pancakes** | 2 flour : 2 liquid : 1 egg : 1/2 fat |
| **Cookies** | 3 flour : 2 fat : 1 sugar : 1 egg |
| **Cake (pound)** | 1 flour : 1 butter : 1 sugar : 1 egg |
| **Sponge** | 1 flour : 1 sugar : 1 egg : 0 butter |

### Leavening
| Agent | When | Amount per cup flour |
|-------|------|---------------------|
| Baking powder | Double-acting, liquid present | 1-1.5 tsp |
| Baking soda | Acid present (buttermilk, yogurt) | 1/4 tsp |
| Yeast | Bread, long ferment | 0.5-2% of flour weight |
| Egg whites | Meringue, soufflé, angel food | Mechanical |

### Temperature Zones
```
325°F: Slow, even (pound cake, custards)
350°F: Standard (cookies, cakes, bread)
375°F: Quick browning (muffins, scones)
400-425°F: High heat (pie, biscuits, puff)
450-500°F: Pizza, artisan bread
```

## Food Safety

### Danger Zone
```
40°F - 140°F (4°C - 60°C)
Max 2 hours (1 hour if >90°F)
```

### Internal Temps
```
Poultry: 165°F
Ground meat: 160°F
Whole cuts: 145°F + 3 min rest
Fish: 145°F or opaque/flakes
Eggs: 160°F (yolk firm)
Leftovers: 165°F
```

### Thawing
1. **Fridge** (1 day per 5 lbs) - Safest
2. **Cold water** (30 min/lb, change water 30 min) - Fast
3. **Microwave** - Cook immediately
4. **Never**: Counter, hot water

### Storage Times (Fridge 40°F / Freezer 0°F)
| Food | Fridge | Freezer |
|------|--------|---------|
| Raw chicken/ground | 1-2 days | 4-12 mo |
| Raw beef/pork | 3-5 days | 4-12 mo |
| Cooked meat | 3-4 days | 2-6 mo |
| Eggs (in shell) | 3-5 weeks | Don't freeze |
| Milk | 1 week | 3 mo |
| Butter | 1-3 mo | 6-9 mo |
| Hard cheese | 3-4 wks | 6 mo |
