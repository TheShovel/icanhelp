# Job Hunting & Career

## Resume Tips
- Keep to 1 page unless 10+ years experience
- Use bullet points, not paragraphs — start with action verbs (built, led, optimized, designed, implemented)
- Quantify achievements: "increased revenue 20%", "reduced load time 40%", "managed team of 5"
- Tailor resume to each job posting (match keywords from description)
- Reverse chronological order: most recent first
- Include: name/contact, summary (2-3 lines), experience, education, skills
- Remove: objective (unless career change), "references available", graduation year if 10+ years ago
- Save as PDF unless told otherwise
- ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) scan resumes — use standard fonts, no tables/graphics

## Job Search
- Use multiple platforms: LinkedIn, Indeed, company websites, industry-specific boards
- Network: 70-80% of jobs are not publicly listed
- Informational interviews: ask for advice, not a job — leads often follow
- Set daily application goal (e.g., 5 quality applications per day)
- Track applications: company, role, date applied, follow-up date, status
- Follow up 1 week after applying if no response
- Recruiters: respond to InMails even if not interested — build relationships

## Interview Prep
- Research company: products, culture, recent news, competitors
- Prepare stories using **STAR method**: Situation, Task, Action, Result
- Practice common questions: "Tell me about yourself", "strengths/weaknesses", "why this company?"
- Prepare 3-5 questions to ask interviewer (shows engagement)
- For tech: practice coding problems (LeetCode, HackerRank), system design, portfolio
- Mock interviews with friend or recording yourself
- Know your resume cold — be ready to discuss every line

## Salary Negotiation
- Never give first number if possible ("I'd like to learn more about the role first")
- Research market rate: Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, Blind, LinkedIn Salary
- Consider total comp: base salary + bonus + equity + benefits + 401k match + vacation
- Negotiate after offer, not before — express enthusiasm first
- Have a target range (e.g., "$120-130k") — be ready to justify with data
- Don't accept same day — "Let me think about it, I'll get back to you tomorrow"
- Other negotiables: sign-on bonus, relocation, remote days, title, start date
- 10-20% above initial offer is typical negotiation range

## Remote Work
- Set up dedicated workspace (separate from relaxation areas if possible)
- Establish routine: start/end time, lunch break, walk breaks
- Overcommunicate: written status updates, async communication
- Use calendar blocking for deep work
- Boundaries: don't let work bleed into evenings (or vice versa)
- Video on for meetings builds connection
- Async-first culture: document decisions, write things down

## Career Growth
- Manager track: lead people, grow teams, organizational influence
- Individual Contributor (IC) track: deepen expertise, mentor, technical leadership
- Both are valid — choose based on what you enjoy
- Seek regular feedback (ask manager "what should I start/stop/continue?")
- Find mentors at different levels
- Build visible work: present at meetings, write docs, share wins
- Learn continuously: 30 min/day reading/courses

## Side Hustles
- Freelancing: Upwork, Fiverr, Toptal (for skilled professionals)
- Consulting: leverage expertise in specific domain
- Teaching: online courses (Udemy), tutoring, bootcamps
- Digital products: templates, ebooks, Notion/Notion templates, print-on-demand
- Content creation: YouTube, newsletter, blog, podcast (takes time to monetize)
- Always check employment contract for moonlighting clauses

## Leaving a Job
- Give 2 weeks notice (standard in US), 1 month+ for senior roles
- Write formal resignation letter: "I'm resigning from [position] effective [date]. Thank you for the opportunities."
- Don't burn bridges — you may cross paths again
- Exit interview: be honest but professional, don't vent
- Transition docs: document ongoing work, key contacts, handoff notes
- Stay until last day — don't coast (reputation follows you)
