# Common Tech Terms & Concepts Explained

## Internet Basics
- **ISP**: Internet Service Provider (Comcast, AT&T, Verizon) — they connect your home to the internet
- **Bandwidth**: how much data can move per second (bigger = faster Netflix, downloads)
- **Latency/ping**: delay before data transfers (lower = better gaming, video calls) — measured in ms
- **Upload vs Download**: download = receiving (watching video, loading sites), upload = sending (posting photos, video calls)
- **Wi-Fi vs Ethernet**: Wi-Fi = wireless (convenient, slower, more interference), Ethernet = cable (fastest, most reliable)
- **Mesh Wi-Fi**: multiple nodes covering house vs single router + extender = better coverage, seamless roaming
- **Mbps vs MBps**: lowercase b = bits, uppercase B = bytes. 100 Mbps = 12.5 MB/s. 8 bits = 1 byte
- **IP address**: your device's address on the internet (IPv4: 192.168.1.1, IPv6: longer hex)

## Computer Basics
- **CPU**: brain of computer — executes instructions (GHz = speed, cores = multitasking)
- **GPU**: graphics processor — renders images, critical for gaming, AI, video editing
- **RAM**: short-term memory — more RAM = more programs open smoothly (8GB minimum, 16GB good, 32GB+ heavy)
- **Storage**: SSD (fast, no moving parts, $), NVMe (very fast, new), HDD (slow, mechanical, cheap for bulk)
- Internal vs external: internal = inside computer, external = plug in via USB
- **Motherboard**: main circuit board connecting all components
- **BIOS/UEFI**: firmware that boots computer, configures hardware
- **OS (Operating System)**: Windows, macOS, Linux — manages hardware and runs apps

## Phone Basics
- **5G vs 4G/LTE**: 5G = faster (up to 10x), lower latency, but less coverage
- **GSM vs CDMA**: outdated distinction — most carriers now use LTE/5G (all compatible)
- **Unlocked phone**: can use any carrier (buy from manufacturer, not carrier)
- **eSIM**: digital SIM card (no physical card) — easier to switch carriers, travel with local plans
- **IP rating**: dust/water resistance (IP68 = dust-tight, submersible 1.5m/30min)
- **OLED vs LCD**: OLED = deeper blacks, better contrast, more expensive
- **Refresh rate**: 60Hz (standard), 90/120Hz (smoother scrolling)
- **Battery health**: Li-ion degrades over time — avoid 0-100% daily, keep 20-80% for longevity, don't charge overnight every night

## Social Media & Algorithms
- **Algorithm**: computer program that decides what you see — based on engagement (likes, time watched, shares)
- **Filter bubble**: algorithm shows you content that reinforces your beliefs, hides opposing views
- **Engagement bait**: posts designed to get comments/ reactions (algorithm loves them)
- **Shadowban**: your content is hidden without you being notified (usually for violating guidelines subtly)
- **DM**: direct message (private). Mention: @username (public). Hashtag: #topic (searchable)
- **FYP (For You Page)**: TikTok/Instagram's algorithmic feed based on your behavior, not who you follow

## Privacy & Security
- **VPN**: encrypts your internet traffic, hides your IP from websites — useful for public Wi-Fi, not a magic "anonymous" tool
- **HTTPS vs HTTP**: HTTPS = encrypted connection (lock icon in browser) — every site should use it
- **2FA/MFA**: two-factor authentication — password + something you have (phone, security key)
- **Phishing**: fake email/text that looks real to steal passwords — check sender address, don't click links
- **Malware**: viruses, ransomware, spyware — use trusted antivirus (Windows Defender is fine), keep OS updated
- **Ad blocker**: browser extension that blocks ads, trackers — uBlock Origin is best
- **Cookies**: small files websites store on your browser — first party (the site you're visiting) vs third party (trackers)
- **Private/Incognito mode**: doesn't save history on YOUR device — doesn't hide browsing from ISP or employer
- **Data broker**: companies that collect and sell your data — opt out services can help
- **Password manager**: generates and stores strong passwords — Bitwarden (free), 1Password, KeePassXC

## Web & Apps
- **Browser**: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge — Firefox has best privacy, Chrome most popular
- **Search engine**: Google, DuckDuckGo (private), Bing — use search operators (site:, filetype:, -word)
- **Cloud**: data stored on remote servers not your device — Google Drive, iCloud, Dropbox, OneDrive
- **AI/Chatbot**: large language model (LLM) trained on text — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini
- **Streaming**: Netflix, Hulu, Spotify — vs downloading (own the file) vs renting (buy digital copy, can be revoked)
- **Open source vs closed source**: open = anyone can see/ modify code (Linux, Firefox), closed = company owns code (Windows, Chrome)
- **Freemium**: basic free, pay for premium features. Free trial: full access limited time
- **SaaS**: Software as a Service — pay monthly instead of buying once (Adobe, Office 365, Slack)

## Gaming
- **Console**: PlayStation, Xbox, Nintendo Switch — plug and play, exclusive games, more expensive games
- **PC**: customizable, better graphics (with good GPU), cheaper games (Steam sales, bundles), more types of games
- **Cloud gaming**: play on remote servers streamed to device (GeForce Now, Xbox Cloud) — needs good internet
- **AAA vs Indie**: AAA = big budget, big studios (Call of Duty, Zelda). Indie = smaller, creative (Hades, Stardew Valley)
- **FPS**: frames per second — 30 (playable), 60 (smooth), 144 (competitive). Must match monitor refresh rate
- **V-Sync**: syncs game FPS to monitor refresh rate — prevents screen tearing but can cause input lag
- **DRM**: copy protection — Denuvo (controversial, can hurt performance)
- **Microtransactions**: small in-game purchases — cosmetics (skin, emotes) vs pay-to-win (competitive advantage)
- **Battle pass**: seasonal tier system with rewards (players pay, play to unlock)
