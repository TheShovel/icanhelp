# Skateboarding & Action Sports

## Skateboarding
- **Deck**: 7.5-8.5" wide (street: 7.75-8.0", vert/transition: 8.0-8.5+). Maple wood (7-ply Canadian maple). Concave (curved shape — holds feet). Length: 30-32". Kicktail (angled ends for tricks). Nose is slightly wider/steeper than tail
- **Trucks**: axle width matches deck width (close to edge). Height: low (stable, less wheel bite, carved) vs high (more turning clearance). Material: aluminum alloy. Bushings: soft (turn easier) vs hard (stable for speed). Hardness ≈ durometer rating for bushings
- **Wheels**: hardness (durometer: 78a-101a). Soft (78-87a) = smooth ride, cruiser, absorbs bumps, less slide. Medium (88-95a) = all-around, street/ park. Hard (96-101a) = skatepark, technical tricks, slides easily. Diameter: 50-60mm (small = lighter, accelerate fast; big = faster, roll over cracks, heavier)
- **Bearings**: ABEC rating (1-9, higher = more precision, but not necessarily better for skateboarding). ABEC 5/7 standard. Material: steel (cheap, rust), ceramic (fast, expensive, don't rust)
- **Safety**: helmet (CPSC certified). Wrist guards (most common break — fall on outstretched hand). Knee pads. Elbow pads. Skate shoes (flat soles, suede, board feel). Start with basics: pushing, turning, stopping (use tail drag!), tic-tac, kickturn. Learn to fall: roll, not catch with arms, pad out, slide

## Surfing
- **Board types**: longboard (9'+ ft, stable, easy paddling, nose riding, turn slowly). Shortboard (5-7 ft, maneuverable, fast turns, harder to paddle + catch waves). Fish (shorter, wider, swallowtail — good for small waves, speed). Funboard (mid-length 7-8 ft, hybrid of long+short, great for progressing). Gun (long, narrow, for big waves >15 ft)
  - Volume (liters) = floatation. Beginner: large volume (60-80L for adults, 30-50+L). Intermediate: 30-50L. Advanced: 20-30L + low rocker (flatter) for speed. Shortboard rocker (curvature) = high rocker for steep drops, low rocker for speed + small waves
- **Wetsuit**: thickness (1-5mm). Summer: 2/3mm shorty or springsuit. Spring/fall: 3/2mm full. Winter: 4/3-5/4/3mm + boots + gloves + hood. Materials: neoprene (water-resistant, stretch). Silicon/glued seams: sealed taped = better for cold water
- **Reading waves**: swell direction + period (longer = more powerful). Wind: offshore (blowing from land, holds waves open) → best. Onshore (chops waves). Tide: low tide = shallow, waves may close out; high tide = deep, slower waves but gentler. Best for beginners: waist-high waves on sand bottom, small swell, offshore winds. Dangerous: shallow shorebreak (slam to bottom), waves over head-high for beginners, reef/rock bottom
  - Rip current: look for channels of choppy, brown water, less breaking waves between sets. Swim parallel to shore (not against rip — it flows past break). Raise arm if you need help

## Snowboarding
- **Board types**: camber (curved up — pop, edge hold, classic). Rocker (curved up at nose/tail — loose, playful, floats in powder, less catch edge). Hybrid (combination). Directional (longer nose, shorter tail — for powder/freeride). Twin (symmetrical — for park/switch)
- **Stance**: regular (left foot forward) vs goofy (right foot forward). Duck stance (back foot angled, forward foot same/toes out) for switch. Angles: adjust for comfort. Width: shoulder width apart. Stance: +15° front / -6° back (duck) all-mountain. +18 / 0 for directional. +12 / -9 for park
- **Bindings**: strap (strap over foot) vs step-on (click in, convenience, limited boot compatibility). Flow (rear-entry)
- **Terrain**: green (beginner — mellow slope). Blue (intermediate — steeper). Black (expert — steep + moguls). Double black (extreme — extreme terrain, cliffs, trees, avalanche risk). Beginners: green slopes, falling leaf (sideslip across the hill to control speed), J turns, garlands

## Rock Climbing
- **Types**: bouldering (short routes, no rope, crash pads, ~15 ft max). Sport climbing (rope + bolts pre-placed in rock. Lead climbing: clip quickdraws into bolts as you climb, rope from belayer catches you). Trad climbing (place own gear — cams, nuts, hexes — in rock cracks. For routes without fixed bolts). Top rope (rope already anchored from above — easiest, safest, for learning). Free solo (no rope — extremely dangerous, one mistake = death. NOT recommended, even for advanced climbers. Alex Honnold free soloed El Capitan 2017)
- **Grades**: YDS (Yosemite Decimal System, 5.0-5.15). 5.5-5.7 = beginner. 5.8-5.10 = intermediate. 5.11-5.12 = advanced. 5.13-5.15 = elite. Bouldering: V0-V17 (V0 = beginner, V14+ = world class)
- **Gear**: climbing shoes (tight, edging + smearing, aggressive down turn for steep). Harness (leg loops + waist belt). Belay device (ATC, Grigri — auto-locking). Carabiners (locking for anchors, non-locking for quickdraws). Quickdraws (two carabiners + sling). Rope (dynamic — stretches to absorb fall). Helmet (for falling rock + head impact). Chalk bag (chalk on hands for grip)
  - Belay technique: PBUS (Pull, Brake, Under, Slide) for ATC. For Grigri: feed slack up, brake hand stays on rope, brake by pulling down on Grigri handle if needed. Catch fall: let rope slide through braking hand gradually (soft catch) vs sudden stop (hard catch)
- **Techniques**: footwork first (stand on toes, precise, quiet feet). Edging (stand on small holds with inside edge of shoe). Smearing (rubber sole flat on rock for friction). Flagging (free leg counterbalance). Dynamic movement (deadpoint — grab next hold at highest point of momentum). Route reading: look for holds, plan sequence. Fall practice: controlled, don't catch hands, stay upright
  - Advanced: drop knee, heel hook, toe hook, gaston (push outward with palm, elbow out). Layback (pull sideways on crack, step up). Mantle (push down on hold to stand up)
- **Anchor**: equalized, redundant, no extension if one component fails. Top rope anchor: two bolts with quickdraw/slings, master point in center. Equilibrate: load equal across all points. Anchor building: quad cord, alpine draws. SERENE/ERNE: Solid, Equalized, Redundant, No Extension, Efficient (strong vs simple)
