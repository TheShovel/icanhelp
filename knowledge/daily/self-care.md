# Self-Care & Wellbeing

Practices for maintaining physical, emotional, and mental health through regular self-care.

## Physical Self-Care
- **Sleep hygiene**: 7-9 hours for adults. Consistent bedtime/wake time (even weekends). Cool room (65-68°F), dark (blackout curtains), quiet (white noise if needed). No screens 30-60 min before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin). No caffeine after 2pm, limit alcohol before bed (disrupts REM)
- **Movement**: Daily movement that feels good, not punishing. 20-30 min walking (most underrated exercise). Stretching (5 min morning, 5 min night). Regular exercise (improves mood via endorphins + BDNF — brain-derived neurotrophic factor). Rest days — recovery is productive
- **Hydration**: 8+ cups water daily (more with exercise, heat). Keep water bottle on desk. Herbal tea counts. Limit excessive caffeine (anxiety, sleep disruption). Signs of dehydration: headache, fatigue, dry skin, dark urine
- **Nutrition**: Eat regular meals (don't skip — blood sugar crashes trigger mood swings). More whole foods (fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein). Less processed food (sugar, refined carbs → inflammation + energy crashes). Omega-3s (fatty fish, walnuts, flax) support brain health
- **Rest**: Schedule breaks throughout day (5-10 min every hour). Real vacation (no work email). "Doing nothing" is restorative, not lazy. Listen to your body — fatigue, tension, pain are messages

## Emotional Self-Care
- **Feel your feelings**: Name emotions to tame them (affect labeling — "I'm feeling frustrated" activates prefrontal cortex, reduces amygdala). All emotions are valid — no "bad" emotions, only unhelpful responses. Cry when needed (emotional tears release stress hormones). Emotions pass like weather — observe without judgment
- **Boundaries**: Say no to things that drain you. Protect your time and energy. You're not responsible for others' feelings. "No" is a complete sentence. Boundaries = self-respect, not selfishness
- **Joy practices**: Do something daily just because you enjoy it (Hobby, music, walk, game). Savor positive moments (pause 10+ seconds to absorb). Keep a "joy list" of activities that recharge you. Schedule joy (not just when energy allows)
- **Self-compassion**: Talk to yourself like you'd talk to a friend. "May I be kind to myself" instead of self-criticism. Kristin Neff's three components: self-kindness (vs self-judgment), common humanity (vs isolation), mindfulness (vs over-identification). Self-compassion > self-esteem (not contingent on success)

## Mental Self-Care
- **Mental stimulation**: Learn something new regularly (language, instrument, skill). Read books (fiction improves empathy, non-fiction grows knowledge). Puzzles, games, creative projects. Avoid doomscrolling (mindless social media consumption — increases anxiety)
- **Mindfulness**: Present-moment awareness without judgment. 5-10 min daily meditation (Headspace, Calm, Insight Timer). Informal practice: mindful tea/walk/shower — focus on senses. Not about emptying mind, about noticing when mind wandered + returning
- **Journaling**: Stream of consciousness (write whatever comes for 10 min). Gratitude journal (3 things you're grateful for daily — rewires brain for positivity). Challenging negative thoughts (write thought, evidence for/against, balanced alternative). Not "dear diary" if that doesn't resonate — any writing helps
- **Digital boundaries**: Phone-free morning (first 30-60 min). No phone in bedroom (use alarm clock). App limits (30 min/day social media). Notification OFF (not silent — OFF). Grayscale display (reduces phone appeal). Remove social media from home screen

## Social Self-Care
- **Connection**: Schedule time with friends (not just digital). Deep conversations > surface small talk. Quality > quantity of interactions. Vulnerability deepens bonds (share feelings, ask for help)
- **Community**: Belonging to groups (club, team, congregation, volunteer). Shared activities build bonds without small talk pressure. Regular group = stable social anchor
- **Solitude**: Time alone is not loneliness. Recharges batteries for extroverts AND introverts (introverts need it more). Self-discovery, reflection, creativity. Solitude ≠ isolation (balance)
- **Saying yes**: Accept reasonable invitations even when you'd rather stay home. "Yes" to new experiences, people, opportunities. But don't overcommit (balance with downtime)

## Environmental Self-Care
- **Living space**: Declutter (visual clutter = mental clutter). Make bed daily (small win sets tone). Pleasant lighting (warm, layered, not harsh overhead). Plants improve air + mood. Clean space = calmer mind
- **Work space**: Ergonomic setup (monitor at eye level, chair supports lower back, feet flat). Plants, photos, or art that pleases you. Tidy desk reduces cognitive load. Noise level matches your work type (quiet for deep, ambient for admin)
- **Nature**: Daily exposure to green space (even 5 min reduces cortisol). Forest bathing (shinrin-yoku — Japanese practice of immersing in forest). Sunlight (10-15 min early — sets circadian rhythm, vitamin D). Gardening as therapy (grounding, nurturing, patience)

## Seasonal Self-Care
- **Winter**: Light therapy (10,000 lux lamp 20-30 min morning — for SAD). Vitamin D supplement. Warm baths, cozy blankets, hot tea. Social connection (cabin fever avoidance). Hibernation mode is OK
- **Spring**: Deep cleaning (physical + mental). Outdoor time (nature reawakening). New projects, goals, energy. Allergen management (pollen = antihistamines, nasal rinse)
- **Summer**: Early morning exercise (before heat). Sun protection (SPF 30+, hat, shade 10-2). Hydration extra. Social events, travel, outdoor activities
- **Fall**: Routine reset (back to school energy). Cozy hygge (candles, warm drinks, blankets). Reflection (review year, adjust goals). Prepare home for winter (weather stripping, furnace check)
