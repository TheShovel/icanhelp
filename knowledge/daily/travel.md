# Travel Tips

## Packing
- **Carry-on only** for trips under a week (avoid checked bag fees + lost luggage)
- Packing cubes: compress clothes, organize by category
- Roll clothes instead of folding — saves space, reduces wrinkles
- 3-1-1 liquids rule (TSA): 3.4 oz (100ml) max per container, all in 1 quart bag, 1 bag per person
- Essentials in personal item: medications, chargers, change of clothes, valuables
- Shoes: wear bulkiest pair, pack only 1-2 extra pairs
- Layering: base layer (moisture wicking) + mid (insulation) + outer (waterproof/windproof)

## Flights
- Book incognito/private window — some sites track cookies and raise prices
- Cheapest days to fly: Tuesday, Wednesday, Saturday
- Cheapest months: January, February, September (avoid holidays/summer)
- Set price alerts: Google Flights, Kayak, Skyscanner
- Book 1-3 months in advance for domestic, 2-8 months for international
- Red-eye flights: cheaper, less crowded
- Check baggage fees before booking — budget airlines charge for everything
- Download airline app for mobile boarding pass
- Arrive 2 hr before domestic, 3 hr before international

## Accommodation
- Hotels: flexible cancellation, amenities (breakfast, gym, pool), predictable
- Hostels: cheapest, social, shared dorms or private rooms — good for solo travelers
- Airbnb/VRBO: full apartments/ homes, kitchen (save on food), more space
- House sitting: free accommodation in exchange for pet care (TrustedHousesitters)
- Location matters more than luxury — stay central near public transit
- Read recent reviews (last 3 months), not just overall score
- Check for hidden fees (resort fees, cleaning fees)

## Money & Documents Abroad
- Notify bank of travel (some block foreign transactions)
- Use credit card with no foreign transaction fee
- Carry some local currency for small vendors/taxis
- ATM withdrawal in local currency (usually best exchange rate)
- Never use currency exchange at airports (worst rates)
- Make digital copies of passport, visa, IDs — store in cloud + separate bag
- Carry physical passport + photocopy separately
- Register with STEP (Smart Traveler Enrollment Program) if US citizen

## Staying Safe
- Research area safety before booking
- Keep valuables in hotel safe, not on person
- Use front pocket or money belt for wallet in crowded areas
- Share itinerary with someone at home
- Google Maps offline: download maps before trip
- Learn basic phrases: "hello", "thank you", "help", "where is", numbers
- Know local emergency numbers (not always 911)
- Travel insurance: medical, trip cancellation, lost luggage — ~5% of trip cost
- Keep medications in original prescription bottles

## Jet Lag
- Adjust sleep schedule 2-3 days before trip
- Eastbound (e.g., US→Europe): hardest — go to bed earlier, get morning sunlight day 1
- Westbound (e.g., Europe→US): easier — stay awake until local bedtime
- Melatonin 0.5-3mg at target bedtime (start low)
- Avoid alcohol on flight (dehydrates + disrupts sleep)
- Drink water — flights are very dehydrating
- Set watch to destination time immediately on boarding
- Force yourself to stay awake until local bedtime on arrival

## Sustainable Travel
- Bring reusable water bottle with filter (many airports have water stations)
- Refuse single-use plastics (straws, bags, cutlery)
- Use reef-safe sunscreen if swimming in ocean
- Support local businesses (eat local, buy local)
- Choose direct flights (takeoffs burn most fuel)
- Pack reusable shopping bag
- Leave places better than you found them
- Don't ride elephants, swim with captive dolphins, or visit animal shows
