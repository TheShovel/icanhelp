# Etiquette & Manners

## Introductions
- Stand when greeting someone (unless elderly or disabled)
- Make eye contact, smile, firm handshake (2-3 pumps, not crushing or limp)
- "Nice to meet you" — repeat their name ("Nice to meet you, Sarah") to remember it
- Introduce: younger to older, junior to senior, colleague to client
  - "Dr. Smith, I'd like you to meet my colleague, John. John, this is Dr. Smith."
- If you forget someone's name: "I'm so sorry, please remind me of your name" — better than avoiding them
- Business: use title + last name until invited to use first name
- Handshakes: web-to-web grip, brief, eye contact. In some cultures, bowing (Japan), cheek kissing (Europe/Middle East), Namaste (India), wai (Thailand)

## Dining
- Napkin on lap as soon as seated, left of plate when leaving temporarily, on plate when done
- Utensils: outside → in with courses. Fork left hand (European) or switch (American)
  - Resting: fork and knife crossed on plate. Done: parallel on right side of plate
- Wait for host to start eating (or everyone is served)
- Bread: tear off small pieces, butter one piece at a time (not whole roll)
- Soup: spoon away from you, sip from side, no slurping
- No elbows on table while eating — elbows OK between courses while talking
- Phone: face down, silent, off the table — if urgent, excuse yourself
- Compliment the host, offer to help, send thank-you note within 24 hours

## Tipping (US customary)
- **Restaurant**: 15-20% of pre-tax bill (18% standard, 20% for good service)
  - Tip on the total, not the subtotal. Check for auto-gratuity (parties of 6+)
- **Bar**: $1-2 per drink or 15-20% of tab
- **Taxi/Uber/Lyft**: 10-15% (Lyft/Uber = tip in app, cash for taxi)
- **Hotel**: $2-5/night for housekeeping (daily, left with note), $1-2/bag for bellhop, $5 for valet
- **Delivery**: $3-5 minimum or 15-20% (especially in bad weather)
- **Hair/nails/ spa**: 15-20% of service total
- **Buffet**: $1-2 per person (or 10%)
- **Coffee counter**: $1 per drink or drop change in jar
- Tipping is NOT expected in: Japan, South Korea, China, Australia, most of Europe (round up is fine)

## Hosting & Being a Guest
- **Host**: greet at door, offer drink within 2 minutes, introduce people, ensure everyone has what they need
  - Dietary restrictions: ask in advance, accommodate if possible, label dishes
  - RSVP deadline: 2-4 weeks before event. If someone doesn't RSVP, text them
- **Guest**: reply to invitation within 24 hours, never show up empty-handed (wine, flowers, dessert, host gift)
  - Arrive on time (not early — host may still be preparing), or 5-15 min late for parties
  - Offer to help (kitchen, cleanup) — if host says no, accept gracefully
  - Don't overstay: ask "should I head out?" or watch for host cues (yawn, cleaning up)
  - Thank-you note/text within 24 hours

## Work & Office
- Be on time (5 min early = on time, on time = late, late = unacceptable)
- Respect others' time: meetings should have agenda, start/end on time
  - Don't book back-to-back meetings — leave 5-10 min buffer
- Door policy: open = welcome, closed = do not disturb, glass = visible — knock anyway
- Email: clear subject line, actionable request in first 2 lines, reply within 24 hours
  - "Reply all" sparingly (only when everyone genuinely needs to know)
  - CC = FYI, TO = action needed
- Meetings: don't interrupt, one person talks at a time, stay off phone, muting when not speaking
- Kitchen: clean up after yourself (wash dishes, wipe counter, don't leave food in fridge to rot)
- Restroom: flush, wash hands, leave clean

## Digital Etiquette
- **Email**: respond within 24 hours (or set auto-reply if out of office)
  - Professional: salutation, clear body, signature. No emojis in formal email
  - Don't use BCC to exclude someone from an email that affects them
- **Text**: respond within a few hours to 24 hours (urgent = call)
  - Don't send long voice memos without asking first
  - Good morning/goodnight texts: only with close partners
  - Double text: wait for response before sending again
- **Social media**: don't post about others without permission (especially photos)
  - No screenshots of private conversations without consent
  - Think before posting: would I say this to their face?
- **Group chat**: mute if too active, don't reply to everything, don't share others' info
- **Phone calls**: unexpected calls can be intrusive — text first to ask if it's a good time
  - Voicemail: identify yourself, state purpose, leave call-back number (slowly)

## Gifts
- **Birthday/wedding/holiday**: consider recipient's taste, not your own
  - Gift receipt included — giving permission to exchange is gracious, not rude
- **Housewarming**: wine, plant, candle, kitchen item, local gift certificate
- **Thank you**: hand-written note > email > text (for significant gifts, occasions, or favors)
  - Within 24 hours for dinner party, 1 week for gifts
- **Regifting**: OK if item is new, unused, and recipient would genuinely like it — don't regift to original giver
- **Corporate gifts**: check company policy (many limit to $25-50)
- **No gift occasions**: "no gifts please" on invitation = really no gifts

## Apologizing
- A real apology: "I'm sorry for [specific action]. I was wrong because [why]. Next time I'll [change]."
  - No "but" — "I'm sorry but you..." is not an apology
  - No "if" — "I'm sorry if you were offended" is not an apology (blames the other person)
- Acknowledge impact, not just intent ("I didn't mean to" doesn't undo the harm)
- Make amends: ask "What can I do to make this right?"
- Give space: after apologizing, let the person process — don't pressure for immediate forgiveness
- Forgive when you can — not for them, for your own peace (forgiveness ≠ reconciliation)

## Difficult Conversations
- Choose time and place: private, enough time, both calm
- Start with positive intent: "I value our relationship, and I want to talk about something that's been on my mind"
- Use "I" statements: "I feel [emotion] when [situation] because [reason]"
- Listen: let them respond fully without interrupting
- Seek to understand: "Help me understand your perspective"
- Find common ground: "We both want [shared goal]"
- End with action: "Going forward, let's [specific agreement]"
- If conversation gets heated: pause, take break, resume when calm
