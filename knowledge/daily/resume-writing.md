# Resume Writing

How to craft resumes and cover letters that pass ATS filters and impress hiring managers.

## ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems)
- **Use standard headings**: "Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Summary" — avoid creative titles
- **Keywords match job description**: Include exact terms from the posting — tools check skill match %
- **Simple formatting**: No tables (text flows wrong), no images/photos, no fancy fonts, no columns (parses left→right)
- **File format**: PDF is safe for human readers; `.docx` is safer for ATS parsing (some ATS mangle PDFs)
- **Headers/footers**: ATS often ignores content in headers/footers — put contact info in body
- **Standard fonts**: Arial, Calibri, Tahoma, Georgia — size 10-12pt body, 14-16pt name

## Resume Sections
- **Header**: Name, phone, email (professional address), LinkedIn URL, GitHub/portfolio — no full address, just city/state
- **Professional summary** (2-3 sentences): Who you are, top skills, target role — optional for experienced hires, required for career changers
- **Skills section**: Bulleted list of hard skills — "Python, React, AWS, PostgreSQL, Docker" — group by category (Languages, Frameworks, Tools)
- **Experience** (reverse chronological): Job title → Company → Dates → Bulleted accomplishments
- **Education**: Degree, major, school, graduation year — GPA if >3.5 or recent grad
- **Certifications**: Only relevant certs with expiration dates — AWS, PMP, CPA, etc.
- **Optional**: Languages, volunteer work, publications, patents — include if relevant or gap filler

## Bullet Point Formula (XYZ Method)
- **Action + What + Result/Impact**: "Led migration of 50+ microservices from Docker Swarm to Kubernetes, reducing deployment time by 60% and improving resource utilization by 25%"
- Start each bullet with a strong action verb: "Developed", "Implemented", "Optimized", "Architected", "Spearheaded"
- **Quantify everything**: $ amounts, % improvements, time savings, team sizes, user counts
- **Avoid weak verbs**: "Responsible for", "Helped with", "Worked on", "Was involved in"
- **Keywords**: Include industry-specific tools, methodologies, and certifications from job description

## Tailoring for Each Job
- **Match keywords**: Extract 10-15 key skills from job description, ensure they appear naturally in your resume
- **Reorder skills**: Put most relevant skills first — recruiters scan top of list
- **Reorder bullets**: Move most impressive/relevant accomplishment to top of each role
- **Modify summary**: Reference target role and company — "Frontend engineer with 5 years of React experience seeking role at [Company]"
- **Remove irrelevant**: Cut old roles (>10 years) or unrelated experience — keep space for relevant content

## Resume Length
- **<5 years experience**: 1 page — every line must add value
- **5-10 years**: 1-2 pages — strongest content, cut weakest bullets
- **10+ years**: 2 pages max — summarize early roles, focus on last 10-15 years
- **Academic/Research**: CV = 2+ pages, includes publications, presentations, grants, teaching

## Cover Letters
- **Custom per application**: Never generic — reference company, role, why you're excited
- **Structure**: 3-4 paragraphs max — Opening (role + company), Body (relevant achievements), Close (enthusiasm + call to action)
- **Don't repeat resume**: Add context to bullets — tell the story behind the achievement
- **Show research**: Mention specific company product, news, or challenge — "I was excited to see [Company] is expanding into [market]"

## Design Tips (for human-read resumes)
- **White space**: Generous margins (0.5-0.75"), line spacing (1.0-1.15), section spacing
- **Consistency**: Same formatting for all dates, headings, bullet points — no mixing of styles
- **Bold sparingly**: Use for job titles, company names, section headings — not for entire list
- **No photos**: In US/UK/Canada, photos encourage bias; expected in parts of Europe/Asia
- **File name**: "Jane-Doe-Resume-2024.pdf" not "resume_final_v3_2_FINAL.pdf"
