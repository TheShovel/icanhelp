# Meatless Cooking & Plant-Based Protein

## Complete Protein
- Myth: plants don't have complete protein — most plant foods DO contain all essential amino acids, just in varying proportions
  - Soy (tofu, tempeh, edamame): complete protein (~36g protein/100g for soybeans)
  - Quinoa, buckwheat, amaranth, hemp seeds, chia seeds, spirulina: all complete proteins
  - "Complementary proteins" (rice + beans = complete) doesn't need to be eaten at same meal — body maintains amino acid pool
- **Protein per 100g**: seitan (25g), tempeh (20g), tofu firm (12g), lentils (9g), chickpeas (8g), black beans (7g), quinoa (4g)

## Protein Sources

### Tofu
- **Types**: silken (smooth, for sauces/smoothies/desserts), soft (misosoups), medium (braises), firm/extra-firm (grilling, stir-fry, baking, crumbling)
  - Extra-firm has most protein, best for "meat-like" texture
- **Prep**: PRESS it (wrap in towel, put heavy pan on top, 15-30 min) — removes water, allows flavor absorption
  - Freeze tofu (then thaw, then press): changes texture to spongy, absorbs marinade better — more "meaty"
- **Marinate**: soy sauce + garlic + ginger + sesame oil (Asian), lemon + oregano + olive oil (Mediterranean), chipotle + lime + cumin (Mexican)
  - Minimum 30 min, better overnight. Panfry, bake (400°F 20-30 min), air fry, or grill
- **Scramble**: crumble firm tofu, sauté with onion + garlic + turmeric (yellow color) + nutritional yeast (cheesy flavor) + black salt (kala namak = eggy flavor)

### Tempeh
- Fermented soybean cake — nutty, firm, higher protein than tofu, more fiber
- Steam or boil 10 min before cooking (removes bitterness), then marinate
- Crumble for "ground meat" in chili, tacos, bolognese. Slice thin for bacon, cubes for stir-fry
- Oil + soy sauce + maple syrup + liquid smoke makes excellent "bacon" (bake at 375°F 15 min each side)

### Seitan (Wheat Gluten)
- High-protein (25g/100g), chewy meat-like texture — make from vital wheat gluten flour
- Steamed (chicken-like), boiled (roast-like), baked (firm), fried (cutlets)
- NOT gluten-free — not suitable for celiac/gluten sensitive
- Basic recipe: 1 cup vital wheat gluten + 2 tbsp nutritional yeast + 1 tsp garlic powder + 1 tsp paprika + 3/4 cup broth + 2 tbsp soy sauce. Knead, simmer in broth 30 min

### Legumes
- **Lentils**: brown (hold shape, earthy), green (peppery, hold shape), red (split, cook fast, mushy — soups/dals), French/Puy (firm, best for salads)
  - Ratio: 1 cup lentils : 3 cups water. Brown/green: 20-30 min. Red: 10-15 min
- **Chickpeas**: roast whole (crispy snack), mash (falafel), blend (hummus), tofu alternative (aquafaba — bean water whips like egg whites!)
  - Aquafaba: chickpea can liquid. 3 tbsp = 1 egg white. Whips into meringue, mayo, mousse
- **Black beans**: tacos, burritos, burgers, chili. Mash some for creaminess
- **Beans from dry**: soak overnight (or quick soak: boil 2 min, sit 1 hr), drain, simmer 1-2 hours until tender. Don't add salt until end (toughens skins)
  - Canned: convenient but higher sodium. Rinse well to reduce sodium by 40%

### Nuts & Seeds
- Hemp seeds: 10g protein/3 tbsp, omega-3s, sprinkle on anything
- Chia seeds: 5g protein/2 tbsp, absorb 10x liquid → pudding
- Nutritional yeast: "nooch" — deactivated yeast, cheesy/nutty flavor, B12 fortified, sprinkle on pasta/popcorn/salads
- Tahini (sesame seed paste): dressings, sauces (tahini + lemon + garlic = magic)

## Cooking Techniques

### Stir-Fry
- High heat, small oil, cook in batches (crowding = steaming, not searing)
- Order: aromatics (garlic, ginger, chili) → protein (tofu/tempeh) → hard vegetables (broccoli, carrot) → soft vegetables (bell pepper, snap peas)
- Sauce: soy sauce + rice vinegar + garlic + ginger + cornstarch slurry. Add at end, toss 30 sec

### Roasting
- Vegetables: toss in oil + salt, spread in single layer, 400-425°F
  - Root veg (potatoes, carrots, beets): 30-45 min. Cruciferous (broccoli, cauliflower): 20-25 min. Tender (asparagus, cherry tomatoes): 10-15 min
  - Don't crowd pan (steams instead of roasts) — use multiple pans or cook in batches
- Tofu/tempeh: bake at 400°F, 20-30 min, flip halfway — gets golden crispy

### Sheet Pan Meals
- Everything on one pan: protein + veg + starch, seasoned together, roasted
- Timing matters: add faster-cooking items later (don't put asparagus in for 45 min with potatoes)

## Replacing Meat in Classic Dishes
- **Burgers**: black bean (mash beans + breadcrumbs + onion + spices, panfry), lentil-walnut (better texture), portobello mushroom cap, store-bought (Beyond/Impossible — closest to meat)
- **Tacos**: crumbled tempeh, seasoned lentils, jackfruit (pulled pork texture), mushrooms (carne asada-ish)
- **Bolognese/Chili**: crumbled tempeh, green lentils, chopped mushrooms + walnuts (all give meaty texture)
- **Sandwich "meats"**: marinated + baked portobello, smoked carrot slices ("lox"), seasoned tofu slices, seitan roast
- **Sushi**: avocado, cucumber, sweet potato (roasted or tempura), tofu, tamago (egg — if not vegan), pickled vegetables
- **Tuna salad**: mash chickpeas + vegan mayo + celery + onion + nori flakes (seaweed taste) + lemon

## Flavor Boosters (Umami)
- Mushrooms (especially dried shiitake powder), tomatoes (sun-dried, paste), soy sauce/tamari, miso paste, nutritional yeast, marmite/vegemite, seaweed (nori, kombu), aged balsamic vinegar, red wine, smoked paprika, liquid smoke, MSG (monosodium glutamate — it's fine, it's naturally occurring)
  - MSG myth: safe for most people, occurs naturally in tomatoes/cheese/mushrooms, "Chinese restaurant syndrome" debunked by meta-analyses

## Essential Pantry for Meatless Cooking
- Canned: chickpeas, black beans, kidney beans, lentils (pre-cooked), diced tomatoes, coconut milk
- Dry: lentils (red, brown, French), quinoa, rice (various), pasta, rolled oats
- Tofu (firm + silken), tempeh (refrigerated or shelf-stable)
- Nuts/seeds: almonds, cashews, walnuts, sesame seeds/tahini, hemp seeds, chia seeds
- Condiments: soy sauce/tamari, rice vinegar, miso paste, hot sauce, maple syrup, olive oil, sesame oil, vegetable broth powder
- Spices: cumin, smoked paprika, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, turmeric, coriander, nutritional yeast
- Frozen: edamame, peas, spinach, broccoli, berries (smoothies), corn
