# Gift Giving

Principles and strategies for meaningful gift selection across relationships and occasions.

## Gift Principles
- **Thoughtfulness > cost**: A $10 gift that shows you listened (they mentioned wanting a specific book) > $100 generic item. Best gifts prove you know the person
- **Gift categories**: Experiences (concerts, cooking class, spa day) — create memories, no clutter. Consumables (gourmet food, wine, coffee, candles) — enjoyed then gone, no clutter. Practical (exactly what they need — quality version of something they use daily). Sentimental (photo album, framed map of meaningful place, handwritten letter)
- **The 4-gift rule (for kids/partners)**: Something they want (main gift), something they need (practical), something to wear (clothing/accessory), something to read (book/magazine)
- **Anti-gifts**: For people who have everything: charity donation in their name, "IOU" for a future experience together, subscription box, plant subscription

## Occasion-Specific
- **Birthdays**: Focus on the person's current interests/hobbies — not generic. "I saw this and thought of you" is powerful. Milestone birthdays (30, 40, 50) — bigger celebrations, experiences, group gifts
- **Holidays (Christmas, Hanukkah)**: Set expectations (budget limits, gift exchange styles: White Elephant/ Secret Santa/ Yankee Swap). Family traditions matter more than gifts. Stocking stuffers: small practical items or treats
- **Weddings**: Registry items (buy early — best selection). Cash/check (appropriate in most cultures, amount should cover your plate + extra). Group gifts (honeymoon fund, appliance). Avoid: unsolicited heirlooms, art without consulting couple
- **Housewarming**: Practical for new home (plant, quality kitchen tool, welcome mat, tool kit). Gift card to home store (they know what they need). Not: decor unless you know their style well
- **Baby shower**: Registry first. Diapers (in various sizes, they grow fast). Amazon/ Target gift cards (parents will realize they forgot items). Meal delivery gift card (new parents are exhausted). Not: clothes without knowing style/size
- **Anniversaries**: Traditional anniversary gifts by year (paper 1st, cotton 2nd, leather 3rd, silk 4th, wood 5th, etc). Experiences: recreate first date, weekend away. Handwritten letter about what you appreciate

## Gift Strategy by Relationship
- **Partner (spouse/SO)**: Pay attention all year — note when they say "I like this" or point at something. Anniversary = sentimental. Birthday = something on their wish list. "Just because" gifts (random flowers, favorite snack) often mean more than holiday gifts
- **Parents**: Quality time (take them to dinner, help in garden). Photo gifts (digital frame loaded with family photos, photo book of grandkids). Experiences (show, concert they'd enjoy). Practical upgrades (robes, electric kettle for tea drinker)
- **Kids (your own)**: Experience gifts (membership to zoo/museum, swimming lessons, trip) — research shows experiences > toys. For toys: open-ended (LEGO, blocks, art supplies — spark creativity). Books (physical, personalized). Quality over quantity (fewer toys played with more)
- **Friends**: Based on shared interests — book you loved, board game for game night, hot sauce for spice lover, quality coffee for coffee snob. "I saw this and thought of you" gifts are best. No pressure to match spending
- **Coworkers**: Keep professional and modest ($15-25 range). Consumable (fancy chocolate, coffee, wine). Desk accessory (nice pen, plant, mug). Avoid: perfume/cologne, clothing, overly personal items, gag gifts unless very close
- **Boss**: Usually not expected or appropriate — follow company culture. Team gift is safer. If giving: consumable, modest, something that can be shared (office coffee, treats for team)

## Wrapping & Presentation
- **Skill matters**: Neat wrapping shows effort. Use quality paper (thicker = easier to wrap cleanly), double-sided tape, sharp creases. Watch tutorials for tricky shapes (bottles, round boxes)
- **Fabric wrapping (Furoshiki)**: Japanese cloth wrapping — reusable, waste-free. Beautiful presentation. Especially good for irregular shapes
- **Gift bags**: Acceptable for most occasions — saves time, looks good. Add tissue paper in complementary color. Remove price tags
- **Tag etiquette**: "To [name], From [you]". For group gifts, list all givers. For gifts from "Santa": handwriting same as real handwriting (kids notice)

## Gift Giving Taboos
- **Regifting**: Only OK if item is new, in original packaging, and you're SURE recipient would like it. Never regift within same social circle (someone will notice). Better to donate
- **Pets as gifts**: Never surprise someone with a pet. Pet = 15+ year commitment. Always discuss in advance, meet the animal, prepare the home
- **Weight loss / fitness gifts**: Implies recipient needs improvement. Unless explicitly asked, avoid: gym membership, diet books, fitness tracker, clothing in smaller size
- **Money**: Appropriate for: wedding (varies by culture), graduation, children (small amounts). For adults in most personal relationships, thoughtful gift preferred over cash. Cash feels impersonal unless for specific purpose (travel fund)
- **Vouchers for services**: "Massage" from non-partner = awkward. "Coupon for help moving" = can be good if recipient actually needs help. "Home-cooked meal" = can be very thoughtful for new parents

## Gift Budgeting
- **Set limits per person**: Communicate for group exchanges (Secret Santa $25 limit). Plan annual gift budget (holidays + birthdays + special occasions). Save monthly in sinking fund
- **Homemade gifts**: Cost-effective but only if you have the skill. Knitted scarf (if you knit well), jam (if you can jam), artwork (if artist). Low-quality homemade gifts seem cheap
- **Experiences over things**: Often same cost, more memorable, no clutter. Concert tickets, escape room, cooking class, museum membership, national park pass
- **Group gifting**: Pool resources for larger gift (big-ticket item for parents). Use online collection tools (Venmo, Splitwise). One person buys, others contribute

## Etiquette
- **Gratitude**: Always send thank-you note (handwritten is gold standard, within 2-3 weeks). Acknowledge receipt even if gift hasn't arrived yet. "Thank you for the generous gift" not "Thank you for the $50"
- **Unwanted gifts**: Keep, donate, or regift appropriately. Never complain about gift to giver or in their presence. Return if possible (no receipt = store credit). If asked: "It's lovely, thank you" — white lie is kind
- **No-gift requests**: "Please no gifts" — respect sincerely. For kids' birthdays: specify "no gifts" or "books only" or "donation to charity." Some guests will still bring gifts — have a gracious response ready
- **International gifting**: Business gifts taboo in some cultures (bribery perception). In Japan: gift-giving ritual important, wrap beautifully, give/receive with both hands. In China: avoid clocks (death symbolism), white flowers (funerals). In Middle East: use right hand only. Research ahead
