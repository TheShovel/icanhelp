# Pet Care (Dogs & Cats)

## Choosing a Pet
- **Dogs**: higher maintenance than cats — need daily walks, training, attention, more expensive ($500-3,000+/year)
  - Good for: active people, families, those who want a companion for outdoor activities
  - Puppy vs adult: puppy needs more time/patience (potty training, destructive phase). Adult personality is known, often already trained
  - Rescue (shelter) vs breeder: rescue saves lives, cheaper, mixed breeds often healthier. Reputable breeder: health testing, predictable temperament, more expensive ($1,000+)
- **Cats**: more independent — need litter box, play, attention but less than dogs, cheaper ($300-1,000/year)
  - Good for: smaller spaces, busier people, those who want a pet that's more independent
  - Indoor vs outdoor: indoor cats live 2-3x longer (12-18 vs 3-7 years). Outdoor dangers: cars, predators, disease, poison, fights
  - Two cats: often better than one (companionship, play), especially if they're siblings or properly introduced

## Dog Care
- **Feeding**: 2 meals/day for adults, 3-4 for puppies. High-quality commercial food (check AAFCO statement)
  - Avoid "grain-free" unless vet recommends (linked to DCM — dilated cardiomyopathy)
  - No: chocolate (theobromine toxic), grapes/raisins, onions/garlic, xylitol (gum/candy — can kill), macadamia nuts, avocado
- **Exercise**: varies by breed — herding/sporting/working breeds need 1-2 hours/day, toy/brachycephalic need less (but still daily walks)
  - Mental stimulation equally important (puzzle toys, training, nose work, chew toys)
  - A tired dog is a good dog — behavior problems often = insufficient exercise
- **Training**: positive reinforcement (treats, praise, play) — punishment-based training causes fear and aggression
  - Sit, stay, come, leave it, loose-leash walking are essentials
  - Crate training: gives dog a den/safe space, helps with potty training, anxiety, travel — NOT cruel (hours matter — 4-6 hours max during day)
  - Socialization: expose puppy to many people, places, sounds, dogs (before 16 weeks critical period)
- **Health**: annual vet visits, core vaccines (rabies, distemper, parvovirus, adenovirus), flea/tick/hw prevention year-round
  - Spay/neuter: before first heat (6 months) for most, benefits (reduces certain cancers, behavioral issues). Discuss timing with vet
  - Common issues: dental disease (80% of dogs by 3 years — brush teeth!), ear infections (floppy ears prone), allergies (itching, ear infections, licking paws), hip/elbow dysplasia (large breeds), obesity (60% of US dogs overweight!)
  - Lifespan: small breeds (12-18 years), medium (10-15), large (8-12), giant (6-10)

## Cat Care
- **Feeding**: 2 meals/day, high-quality protein-rich wet food > dry (hydration, fewer urinary issues)
  - Cats are obligate carnivores — MUST eat meat. No vegan cat food (can cause severe health problems)
  - No: onions/garlic, grapes/raisins, chocolate, xylitol, lilies (extremely toxic — kidney failure), raw eggs/bones
- **Litter box**: 1 box per cat + 1 extra, scooped daily, fully changed weekly
  - Placement: quiet, private, not near food/water. Most cats prefer unscented clumping litter
  - Not using box: medical issue FIRST (UTI, kidney disease), then stress, dirty box, box location/type, multi-cat issues
- **Enrichment**: vertical space (cat trees, shelves), scratching posts (multiple, different materials), window perches, puzzle feeders, interactive play daily (15-20 min, wand toys)
  - A bored cat is a destructive cat — scratching furniture, over-grooming, aggression
  - Catify your home: create highways (shelves, bridges), hiding spots, sunbathing spots
- **Health**: annual vet visits, core vaccines (rabies, FVRCP), flea prevention
  - Indoor cats still need vaccines (rabies required by law, FVRCP airborne)
  - Spay/neuter: around 5-6 months — prevents spraying, roaming, certain cancers, unwanted litters
  - Common issues: chronic kidney disease (very common in older cats — watch water intake), hyperthyroidism (older, weight loss + increased appetite), diabetes, dental disease, urinary crystals/blockages (male cats — emergency: can't pee, painful, fatal without treatment)
  - Lifespan: indoor 12-18 years (many live to 20+). Outdoor 3-7 years

## Introductions
- **Dog to cat**: slow, scent first (swap bedding), parallel feeding, visual through barrier, then supervised face-to-face — can take weeks to months
  - Never force it — let them set the pace
- New cat to existing cat: separate room for days-weeks, scent exchange, controlled visual (cracked door), then face-to-face supervised
- New dog to existing dog: neutral territory (park), parallel walk, then home together, supervise resources

## End of Life
- Quality of life assessment: eating, drinking, mobility, pain, enjoyment of favorite activities, good days > bad days
- Signs of suffering: breathing difficulty, not eating/drinking 24h+, hiding, crying, unable to stand, severe pain
- Euthanasia: peaceful, painless — sedative first (relaxed, sleepy), then overdose of anesthetic (heart stops)
  - Can be at home (mobile vet) or clinic — many prefer home for less stress
  - You can be present — it's not scary, vet checks heart after, no sudden movements
  - Grief is real and valid — pet loss support groups, memorialize (photos, paw print, urn)
