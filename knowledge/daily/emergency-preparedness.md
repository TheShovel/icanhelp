# Emergency Preparedness

## Emergency Kit (Go Bag)
- **Water**: 1 gallon/person/day, 3 days minimum
- **Food**: 3-day supply non-perishable (canned, protein bars, dried fruit) + can opener
- **First aid**: bandages, antiseptic, pain meds, prescription meds (7 day supply), tourniquet
- **Tools**: flashlight + extra batteries, multi-tool, whistle, duct tape, dust masks
- **Communication**: battery-powered radio (NOAA weather radio), portable charger, power bank
- **Documents**: IDs, insurance, medical info, cash (small bills, ATMs may not work)
- **Hygiene**: wipes, hand sanitizer, trash bags, feminine products, toothbrush
- **Warmth**: emergency blankets, change of clothes, sturdy shoes, rain poncho
- **Other**: matches/lighter (waterproof), map of area, local contacts list, pen/paper
- **For pets**: food, water, leash, carrier, meds, vaccination records, collar with ID

## Home Emergency Supplies
- **Fire extinguisher**: ABC type (all-purpose), near kitchen, check pressure monthly
- **Smoke detectors**: test monthly, replace batteries twice/year, replace unit every 10 years
- **CO detector**: each floor, near bedrooms, same maintenance
- **Fire escape ladder**: if above ground floor, store near window
- **Carbon monoxide**: odorless, symptoms = headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion — if suspect, leave and call 911
- **Shutoff tools**: wrench for gas shutoff, know where water main shutoff is
- **Sandbags**: for flood-prone areas

## Natural Disasters by Region
- **Earthquakes** (West Coast, Alaska, Pacific): drop, cover, hold on (under sturdy table, away from windows)
  - After: check for gas leaks, expect aftershocks, stay off phones for emergencies
  - Secure heavy furniture to walls (bookshelves, water heaters, TVs)
- **Hurricanes** (Gulf, East Coast, Puerto Rico): prepare board windows, know evacuation zone, fill gas tank
  - Category 1 (minimal) to 5 (catastrophic) — evacuate if told
  - After: avoid floodwater (electrified, contaminated), no generator indoors (CO)
- **Tornadoes** (Midwest, South, Plains): go to basement/lowest level, interior room, no windows
  - Cover head, get under something sturdy. Mobile homes are NOT safe
  - Watch vs warning: watch = conditions favorable, warning = tornado spotted, take cover NOW
- **Wildfires** (West, Australia, Mediterranean): have go bag ready, know evacuation routes
  - Close all windows/doors, move furniture away from windows, turn on lights (visibility for firefighters)
  - If trapped: find clear area (no vegetation), lie face down, cover with blanket
- **Floods** (anywhere near water, low-lying areas): NEVER drive through floodwater (6" moves car, 12" sweeps it)
  - Turn Around Don't Drown — leading cause of flood deaths
- **Blizzards** (Northern US, mountains): stay inside, layer clothing, prevent pipes freezing (drip faucets)
  - If stranded in car: stay in car (visible), run engine 10 min/hour (clear exhaust pipe first), bright cloth on antenna

## Power Outage
- Keep fridge/freezer closed: fridge 4 hours, freezer 24-48 hours (if full)
- Generator: NEVER indoors or in garage (CO kills). Let dry before using after rain
- Unplug electronics (surge when power returns). Leave one light on (know when power's back)
- Gas appliances: don't use oven/stove for heat (CO risk)
- Water: fill bathtub/pitchers before outage if possible
- Food safety: when in doubt, throw it out (40-140°F "danger zone" > 2 hours = unsafe)

## Active Shooter (Run, Hide, Fight)
1. **RUN**: have escape route, leave belongings, keep hands visible, don't stop for injured
2. **HIDE**: locked door, barricade, lights off, silent (phone on vibrate/silent), behind hard cover (not just walls)
3. **FIGHT**: last resort, improvised weapons (fire extinguisher, chair, scissors), commit fully, aim for head
- If law enforcement arrives: empty hands up, no sudden movements, follow commands

## Medical Emergencies At Home
- **Severe allergic reaction**: epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) → outer thigh, call 911, second dose if needed
- **Stroke**: FAST — Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911
- **Heart attack**: chest pressure/pain, shortness of breath, nausea, jaw/arm pain — call 911, aspirin (if not allergic)
- **Severe bleeding**: direct pressure, tourniquet only for life-threatening limb bleeding (can cause limb loss if misapplied)
- **Seizure**: clear area of dangerous objects, time it, don't put anything in mouth (they won't swallow tongue), call 911 if >5 min or first seizure
- **Hypoglycemia** (low blood sugar in diabetics): confusion, shakiness, sweating — give sugar (juice, glucose tabs, candy)
- **Opioid overdose**: naloxone (Narcan) nasal spray — available without prescription at pharmacies

## Emergency Plans
- Family communication: out-of-state contact person (everyone calls them)
- Meeting points: one near home, one outside neighborhood
- Know: evacuation routes, shelter locations, school/work emergency plans
- Practice: fire drills (2x/year), earthquake drill, review plan with kids
- For elderly/disabled: plan for mobility needs, medication access, medical equipment backup power
- For pets: pet-friendly shelters, boarding facilities, list of hotels that accept pets
- Medical info: allergies, conditions, medications, emergency contacts — wallet card + phone lock screen
