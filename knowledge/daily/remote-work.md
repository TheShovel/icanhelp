# Remote Work & Distributed Teams

## Home Office Setup

### Ergonomic Workstation
- Monitor at arm's length, top of screen at eye level
- External keyboard and mouse (avoid laptop-only posture)
- Chair: adjustable height, lumbar support, seat tilt
- Standing desk: alternate sitting/standing every 30–60 min
- Lighting: avoid glare on screen, use bias lighting behind monitor
- Webcam at eye level (stack books under laptop or use monitor arm mount)

### Audio & Video
- Microphone: external USB mic (Samson Q2U, Blue Yeti) or headset mic
- Headphones: closed-back for noisy environments (Audio-Technica ATH-M50x, Sony WH-1000XM)
- Background: neutral wall, bookshelf, or virtual background with good keying
- Lighting: key light in front (not overhead), softbox or ring light
- Noise: mute when not speaking; use push-to-talk in large meetings

### Connectivity
- Wired Ethernet preferred over WiFi (lower latency, no interference)
- Router: enable QoS, prioritize video conferencing traffic
- Powerline/ MoCA adapters if WiFi doesn't reach the workspace
- Backup internet: phone hotspot as fallback
- UPS (uninterruptible power supply) for routers, modem, workstation

## Communication Practices

### Async-First Mindset
- Default to async (Slack messages, email, docs) over sync (meetings)
- Write thorough messages: context, goal, deadline, blocking items
- Use threaded replies to keep conversations organized
- Document decisions in shared wiki/Notion/Linked docs (not chat)
- Record video updates for complex topics (Loom, Screen Studio)

### Meetings
- Agenda required for any meeting; shared before the meeting
- Timebox: 25 min default (end at :55), 50 min for workshops
- Assign a note-taker; share notes after the meeting
- Camera recommended but not mandatory (respect neurodiversity)
- Record async standups via Slack/Loom/Geekbot instead of live daily standups

### Written Communication
- Subject line that indicates action needed: `[FYI]`, `[Action Required]`, `[RFC]`, `[Decision]`
- Include key info at the top: deadline, who decides, dependencies
- Use tables and bullet lists for scannability
- Provide enough context for someone joining the thread later

## Focus & Productivity
- Define work hours clearly; communicate them on your calendar
- Use focus time blocks (2-hour minimum) with Do Not Disturb mode
- Pomodoro: 25 min work / 5 min break, or 90 min deep work sessions
- Separate work apps from personal (work profile on phone, different browser)
- Have a "shutdown ritual": review tomorrow's tasks, close tabs, sign off
- Avoid context switching: batch similar tasks (email, code review, writing)

## Remote Team Culture
- Over-communicate status: daily check-in, progress updates, blockers
- Virtual water cooler: optional 15-min coffee chats, co-working rooms (Cocoon)
- Recognition: public praise in team channel, kudos tools (Mattermost Awards)
- Onboarding: buddy system, async onboarding docs, first week intro calls
- Psychological safety: encourage asking questions, no penalty for async replies
- Time zone awareness: rotate meeting times fairly, record everything

## Common Pitfalls
- **Zoom fatigue**: replace more meetings with async updates + Loom videos
- **Loneliness**: join virtual co-working, team socials, local coworking spaces
- **Overwork**: set hard stop time, no Slack after hours (disable notifications)
- **Miscommunication**: when tone is ambiguous, pick up the phone
- **Scope creep**: write down scope in PRD/requirements before building
- **Visibility bias**: ensure async contributions are recognized, not just meeting presence

## Tools Comparison for Startups
- **Small team (<10)**: Slack + Google Workspace + Linear + Notion
- **Growing (10–50)**: add Zoom, Miro, 1Password Business, Okta
- **Scaling (50+)**: add Okta/SSO, Datadog for monitoring, PagerDuty for on-call
- Open-source stack: Mattermost + Nextcloud + Plane + Outline
