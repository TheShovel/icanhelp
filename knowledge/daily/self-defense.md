# Self-Defense & Personal Safety

## Awareness (Most Important)
- **Situational awareness**: know what's happening around you — don't be lost in phone/screens when in public
  - Cooper Color Code: White (unaware), Yellow (relaxed alertness — baseline), Orange (specific alert), Red (action)
  - Stay in Yellow in public — scanning, not paranoid
  - "The best self-defense is not being there." — avoid dangerous situations before they develop
- **Trust your instincts**: if something feels wrong, it probably is — leave, cross street, go into store
  - Gut feeling = your brain processing subtle danger cues you haven't consciously noticed
  - It's OK to be rude to escape a potentially dangerous situation — politeness can get you hurt
- **Predator selection**: criminals look for easy targets — look confident, aware, purposeful. Walk with head up, scan, steady pace
  - Predators test: ask for directions, time, help with something — if someone makes you uncomfortable, disengage

## De-escalation
- **Don't escalate verbal confrontations**: if someone insults you, threatens you, or yells — walk away
  - Pride costs nothing; your safety is worth more than winning an argument
  - Apologize for something you didn't do if it gets you out safely
- **If confronted**: stay calm, maintain 6-10 ft distance (outside grab range), hands visible (not in pockets = less threatening, also shows you're not hiding a weapon)
  - Speak firmly: "I don't want any trouble. I'm leaving now."
  - Break eye contact and walk away at an angle — maintains awareness, shows disinterest
  - If they follow: cross street, go into open business, yell for help
- **If they demand your stuff** (mugging): give it — wallet, phone, car. None of it is worth your life
  - Throw wallet/keys one direction, run the other (distraction)
  - If they want you to go somewhere else (second location): run and scream — your chance of survival plummets if you go to second location

## Physical Defense Basics
- **Goal**: CREATE DISTANCE and RUN. Not to "win" a fight
  - Self-defense classes teach you to escape, not fight
  - Every second you delay an attacker is a second for someone to hear/help, or for you to run
- **Targets** (if physical escape is the only option):
  - Eyes: fingers to eyes, thumbs in (blinding, even temporarily)
  - Throat: strike to throat (windpipe, causes choking/gagging)
  - Nose: palm-heel strike upward (causes tears, backward head movement)
  - Groin: kick/knee/ punch (pain, incapacitation)
  - Knees: side kick to knee (can dislocate, stops pursuit)
  - Instep/ankle: stomp with heel (small bones, very painful)
- **Strikes to practice** (don't need strength):
  - Palm-heel strike: palm of hand, arm straight, drive upward into nose/chin — less chance of breaking your hand than a punch
  - Elbow strike: strongest strike with least telegraphing — to face/neck when grabbed from behind or close range
  - Knee: to groin or thigh when close
  - Side kick: to knee — push not snap
- **If grabbed**: pull away from thumb (weakest part of grip). For wrist grabs: rotate arm toward thumb side and pull
  - Bear hug from behind: drop weight, elbow back to ribs, stomp foot, turn and strike

## Chokes & Holds
- **Choke from front**: turn chin into the crook of attacker's elbow (protects airway), drop weight, two-hand push on attacker's hips or groin strike
  - Do NOT try to pull hands off — you'll make it tighter
- **Choke from behind**: tuck chin down (prevents airway compression), turn into attacker (break the hold), elbow to ribs, escape
- **Headlock**: turn face toward attacker's body (protects airway), furthest arm reaches around back and grabs groin/pinches inner thigh, other arm pushes attacker's knee

## Weapons (Pepper Spray)
- **Pepper spray**: effective (OC spray, 2+ million SHU), range 8-12 ft, momentary incapacitation
  - Know: wind direction (don't spray into own face!), practice drawing from holster (fumbling = bad)
  - Stream > fog > gel (stream less affected by wind, gel can be wiped off)
  - Check expiration date (pressure drops over time). Replace every 2-3 years
- **Personal alarm**: 130+ dB, attached to keychain — the noise deters most attackers (draws attention = risk for them)
- **NOT recommended**: firearms without extensive training (can be taken from you, escalation of violence), "tactical" pens/brass knuckles (often illegal, need to be too close)

## Car Safety
- **Before entering**: approach with keys in hand, check back seat (open door, look before getting in), check around car
- **Driving**: keep doors locked, don't stop for stranded motorists (call 911 for them instead), leave space in front at stops (escape route)
  - If someone tries to enter your car at stop: honk, drive away
  - If followed: don't go home (don't lead them to your home). Drive to police station, open gas station, crowded area
- **Road rage**: don't engage, don't make eye contact, slow down/let them pass, call 911 if you're being followed/frightened
- **Parking lot**: park in well-lit areas near entrance, be aware of surroundings, look underneath car as approach, use parking-lot escort service if available

## Home Security
- **Doors**: solid core or metal, deadbolt (at least 1" bolt), strike plate reinforced (longer screws into stud — common weak point)
  - Sliding glass door: dowel/bar in track, security pin through frame ($5 fix, very effective)
  - Lock every door every night — even attached garage
- **Windows**: locks, pin (drill hole through overlapping frames, insert nail — cheap effective secondary lock)
  - First floor: consider window film (security film makes glass harder to break through)
  - Open bedroom windows from top (not bottom) on first floor
- **Entry**: never put identifying information on keychain (name, address). Don't hide spare key in obvious places (under mat, fake rock, above door frame)
- **Lights**: exterior lights with motion sensors, timer for interior lights when away
  - Light is the #1 deterrent — burglars avoid well-lit homes
- **Smart doorbell** (Ring, Nest): see who's at door before opening, recorded video evidence if incident
- **When away**: stop mail (hold at post office), don't announce on social media ("gone for the weekend!"), have someone collect packages, use light timers

## Digital Safety
- **Passwords**: unique for every site, use password manager, 2FA enabled (prefer authenticator app > SMS)
  - NEVER use same password for email and financial accounts (email reset = all accounts)
- **Phishing**: don't click links in unexpected emails/texts. Verify by typing URL directly
  - Government/banks will NEVER ask for password, SSN, or payment via email/text
- **Social media**: don't post travel plans in advance (wait until you're back), check location-sharing settings, don't post your home address, children's full names/schools, vacation dates
  - Check: Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat — all have location services that can share where you live (doxxing)
  - Photo metadata (EXIF data) can include GPS coordinates — social media strips this, but original files may have it
- **Sextortion**: do NOT send intimate photos with face visible. If blackmailed: DO NOT PAY (they never stop), block, report to FBI/IC3, tell someone
- **Cyberstalking**: document everything, don't respond, block on all platforms, police report, consider restraining order, check for tracking devices (phone, car)
