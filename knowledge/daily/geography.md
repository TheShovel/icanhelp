# World Geography & Cultures

## Continents
- **Asia**: largest (30% of land, 60% of population). Russia, China, India, Japan, Korea, SE Asia
- **Africa**: 54 countries, 2nd largest population. Sahara (largest hot desert), Nile (longest river)
- **North America**: US, Canada, Mexico, Caribbean, Central America — diverse climates (Arctic to tropics)
- **South America**: Amazon rainforest, Andes mountains, Brazil (largest country, Portuguese-speaking)
- **Antarctica**: no permanent population, research stations only, 98% ice-covered
- **Europe**: ~50 countries, high population density, many cultures in small area. EU = 27 countries, single market
- **Australia/Oceania**: Australia, NZ, Pacific islands (Melanesia, Micronesia, Polynesia)

## Time Zones
- 24 time zones, each 15° longitude
- UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) = GMT (Greenwich Mean Time)
- UTC+0: UK, Portugal, Iceland, West Africa
- UTC-5: US Eastern (NY, DC). UTC-8: US Pacific (LA, SF)
- UTC+1: Central Europe (Paris, Berlin, Rome). UTC+2: Eastern Europe
- UTC+5:30: India. UTC+8: China, Singapore, Malaysia
- International Date Line: switches calendar day — crossing west = day forward, east = day back
- Daylight Saving Time: spring forward +1h March, fall back -1h November — not observed everywhere (most of Asia, Africa, and S. America don't observe)

## Major Biomes
- **Tropical rainforest**: Amazon (SA), Congo (Africa), SE Asia — warm/wet year round, highest biodiversity
- **Savanna**: Africa (Serengeti), S. America, Australia — grasslands with scattered trees, rainy + dry seasons
- **Desert**: Sahara (Africa), Arabian, Gobi (Asia), Atacama (S. America), Mojave (US)
  - Cold deserts: Gobi, Patagonia — hot days, freezing nights
- **Temperate forest**: Eastern US, Europe, East Asia — four seasons, deciduous trees
- **Taiga (boreal forest)**: Canada, Russia, Scandinavia — largest biome, coniferous, long cold winters
- **Tundra**: Arctic circle — permafrost, no trees, mosses/lichens, extremely cold
- **Mediterranean**: California, Mediterranean basin, Chile, S. Africa, Australia — mild wet winters, hot dry summers
- **Grassland**: prairies (US), steppes (Russia/Eurasia), pampas (Argentina) — fertile soil, vast grain production

## Major Water Bodies
- **Oceans**: Pacific (largest, deepest), Atlantic (2nd), Indian, Southern (Antarctic), Arctic (smallest)
- **Largest lakes**: Caspian Sea (salt, 371k km²), Superior (fresh, 82k km²), Victoria (Africa, 69k km²)
- **Longest rivers**: Nile (6,650 km), Amazon (6,400 km), Yangtze (6,300 km), Mississippi (3,730 km)
- **Deepest point**: Mariana Trench (Pacific) — 11,034 m

## Mountain Ranges
- **Himalayas**: Asia, 8 peaks over 8,000m (Everest 8,849m) — formed by India colliding with Asia
- **Andes**: S. America, 7,000 km longest continental range (Aconcagua 6,961m)
- **Rockies**: N. America, 4,800 km
- **Alps**: Europe, Mont Blanc 4,809m
- **Urals**: Russia, divide Europe/Asia
- **Atlas**: NW Africa
- **Hindu Kush**: Afghanistan/Pakistan

## Cultural Geography Concepts
- **Cultural diffusion**: spread of cultural traits (language, religion, food, technology) between societies
- **Globalization**: increasing interconnectedness — economic, cultural, political
- **Colonialism**: European powers colonized most of world (16th-20th century) — shaped language, borders, economies
- **Nation vs state vs country**: nation = cultural group, state = political entity, country = sovereign state
- **Ethnic enclaves**: neighborhoods with concentrated ethnic/cultural group (Chinatown, Little Italy)
- **Border types**: natural (rivers, mountains), geometric (straight lines, US/Canada border), cultural (language/ethnic boundaries)
- **Geopolitics**: how geography influences politics and international relations (access to sea, resource control, strategic chokepoints like Strait of Hormuz, Suez Canal, Panama Canal, South China Sea)
- **Megacities**: cities with 10M+ population — Tokyo (37M), Delhi (32M), Shanghai (29M), São Paulo (22M), Mexico City (22M), Cairo (22M), Mumbai (21M), Beijing (21M), Dhaka (21M), Osaka (19M)
