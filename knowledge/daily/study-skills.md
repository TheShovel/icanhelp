# Study Skills

Effective techniques for learning and retaining information efficiently.

## Active Recall
- **Test yourself** instead of re-reading: cover material and recall from memory
- **Flashcards**: Physical or digital (Anki uses spaced repetition algorithm) — prompt on front, answer on back
- **Closed-book retrieval**: Read a section, close the book, write down everything you remember
- **Blurting**: Write everything you know about a topic without notes, then check gaps

## Spaced Repetition
- Review material at increasing intervals: 1 day, 3 days, 7 days, 14 days, 30 days
- Anki algorithm (SM-2): Each card has ease factor — successful recalls increase interval, failures reset to 1 min
- **Leitner system**: Physical boxes — cards answered correctly move to next box (longer review interval)
- Best for: vocabulary, facts, formulas, anatomy, programming syntax

## Pomodoro Technique
- **25 minutes focused work** → 5 minute break → repeat; after 4 cycles take 15-30 min break
- During break: stand up, stretch, walk, hydrate — avoid screens
- Helps with procrastination and maintaining focus for hours of study

## Note-Taking Methods
- **Cornell**: Divide page into Cue (left), Notes (right), Summary (bottom) — review by covering notes and recalling from cues
- **Outline**: Hierarchical headings (I, A, 1, a) — good for structured lectures
- **Mind map**: Central topic, branches for subtopics, keywords on lines — shows connections, good for brainstorming
- **Zettelkasten**: Atomic notes (one idea per card), linked via IDs — builds a web of interconnected knowledge
- **Digital**: Obsidian, Logseq, Roam Research — bidirectional links, graph view, backlinks

## SQ3R Method
- **Survey**: Skim headings, subheadings, summaries, figures — get the big picture (2-5 min)
- **Question**: Turn each heading into a question — "What is X?" drives active reading
- **Read**: Read to answer your questions, not passively — focus on key concepts, examples
- **Recite**: After each section, summarize aloud in your own words — checks comprehension
- **Review**: Review entire chapter — re-read summaries, test yourself on questions

## Deep Work Principles (Cal Newport)
- **Schedule deep work blocks** (90-120 min) — no distractions, phone away, notifications off
- **Quit social media** during study sessions — context switching costs 20+ minutes to refocus
- **Embrace boredom**: Don't grab phone during every idle moment — train concentration muscle
- **Measure deep work hours**: Track daily hours of undistracted focus, target 4+ hours/day

## Environment Optimization
- **Dedicated study space**: Desk clear of distractions, good lighting, comfortable chair
- **Background noise**: Silence for complex material, white noise/brown noise/low-fi for moderate focus
- **Temperature**: 68-72°F (20-22°C) — too warm induces sleepiness
- **Digital tools**: Freedom/Cold Turkey block websites; Forest app gamifies focus; LeechBlock for browser

## Memory Techniques
- **Method of Loci (Memory Palace)**: Associate items with locations in a familiar space — walk through mentally to recall
- **Chunking**: Group info into meaningful chunks (phone numbers: xxx-xxx-xxxx instead of single digits)
- **Acronyms**: PEMDAS (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction)
- **Story method**: Turn abstract info into a narrative — engages multiple brain regions
- **Dual coding**: Combine words with images/diagrams — two memory traces = better recall
