# Fashion & Personal Style

## Building a Wardrobe (Capsule)
- **Capsule wardrobe**: 30-40 items that all mix and match (not including underwear, workout wear, outerwear)
- **Rule**: buy quality over quantity — cost per wear matters more than price tag
- **Core pieces**: dark jeans, chinos/ trousers, white button-down, plain tees (white + black + gray), blazer, dark dress shoes, sneakers, boots, neutral sweater
- **Color palette**: choose 3-4 neutrals (navy, gray, black, beige) + 2-3 accent colors — everything coordinates
- If you'd wear it today, buy it. If you're waiting for "someday", don't
- **One in, one out**: maintain wardrobe size limit

## Fit (Most Important)
- **Shoulders**: seam should align with shoulder bone (not hanging off or squeezing)
- **Sleeves**: end at wrist bone (shirt) or just below (jacket) — 1/2" shirt cuff visible under jacket
- **Pants length**: slight break (crease at front) for dress pants, no break for slim, cuffed for casual
- **Jacket length**: covers seat of pants, buttons without pulling
- **T-shirts**: sleeve ends at mid-upper arm, shoulder seam at shoulder bone, not baggy
- **Jeans**: snug but comfortable — sagging after 2 hours wear means they're too big
- **Alterations**: $15-30 for hemming transforms cheap clothes into well-fitting clothes

## Men's Style Basics
- **Suits**: navy and charcoal first (most versatile), then gray, then patterns
  - 2-button > 3-button (classic). Notch lapel (standard), peak (formal), shawl (tuxedo)
  - Fit: shoulders flat, no pulling at buttons, jacket covers seat
- **Shoes**: oxford (formal), derby/blucher (semi-formal), loafer (casual), boot (casual/smart)
  - Color: brown for versatility, black for formal
  - Shoe care: polish, shoe trees, don't wear same pair two days in a row
- **Accessories**: watch (simple, leather or metal), belt (matches shoes), socks (match pants not shoes)
  - Tie width: should match lapel width (2.5-3" modern standard)

## Women's Style Basics
- **Dress shapes**: sheath (body-con, professional), A-line (flared, flattering), wrap (universally flattering), shift (loose)
- **Hemlines**: knee-length is most versatile. Midi (calf) is trendy, maxi (floor) is casual
- **Blouses**: silk/ satin (dressy), cotton (casual), with blazer (professional)
- **Pants**: straight leg (most versatile), wide leg (trendy), skinny (dated but fine with boots)
- **Jeans**: high-rise (most flattering), mid-rise (comfortable), low-rise (polarizing)
- **Shoes**: pointed toe (lengthens leg), round toe (cute), block heel (walkable), stiletto (evening only)
- **Jewelry**: one statement piece (earrings, necklace, bracelet) > lots of small pieces

## Fabric Quality
- **Cotton**: higher thread count = softer, more durable. Egyptian/ Pima/ Supima = longer fibers = stronger
  - Linen: lightweight, wrinkles easily, breathes well — fiber of summer
  - Denim: raw/unwashed (fades over time, lasts years), washed (soft from start, fades faster)
- **Wool**: merino (soft, non-itchy, temperature regulating, moisture wicking), cashmere (luxuriously soft, delicate), tweed (heavy, rugged)
- **Leather**: full grain (best, patinas), top grain (good, corrected), genuine/bonded (low quality)
- **Silk**: natural protein fiber, breathes, luxurious feel, delicate (dry clean or hand wash)
- **Synthetic**: polyester (cheap, doesn't breathe, holds odor), nylon (strong, stretchy), elastane/spandex (stretch)
  - Performance wear benefits from synthetics (sports, outdoor). Daily wear: natural fibers preferred
- **Seams**: French seam / flat-felled (durable, finished) > overlock/serged (standard) > raw edge (cheap, frays)

## Dressing for Body Type
- **General rule**: emphasize your favorite features, de-emphasize ones you're less confident about
- **Vertical lines** (long necklaces, V-necks, pinstripes): make you look taller, leaner
- **Horizontal lines** (wide stripes, belts across waist): make you look wider, shorter
- **Dark colors** recede (slimming), light colors advance (highlight)
- **Proportions**: if you wear loose top, wear fitted bottom — and vice versa

## Shoe Care
- Leather: clean dirt, condition (keep supple), polish (color + shine), shoe trees (absorb moisture, maintain shape)
  - Don't wear same shoes two days in a row (let them dry 24 hours)
- Suede: brush (suede brush), protect (spray waterproof), avoid water (stains)
- Sneakers: wash gently (warm water + mild soap), air dry (not in dryer), stuff with paper to maintain shape
- Rotate shoes: each pair lasts longer (materials rest and recover between wears)

## Shopping Strategy
- **Thrift / secondhand**: best value, unique finds, sustainable — check higher-end neighborhoods for better selection
- **Sales**: end of season (Jan/July) = deepest discounts. Don't buy just because it's on sale
- **Investment pieces**: coat, shoes, bag, watch — spend more. Trendy items: spend less
- **Try before buying**: sizes vary wildly between brands. Know your measurements
- **Return policy**: check before buying (especially online). Don't wear tags, keep packaging
- **Minimalist tip**: 90% of the time you wear 10% of your wardrobe — identify those 10% and buy more of that

## Grooming Basics
- **Skin**: cleanse (gentle, not stripping), moisturize (daily), sunscreen (daily, even in winter)
  - Face: separate routine for face vs body (face products are gentler)
- **Hair**: wash less frequently (2-3x/week for most), conditioner on ends only, heat protectant before styling
  - Barber every 4-6 weeks for short haircuts — tell barber what you like (photos help)
- **Beard**: trim regularly, shape neckline (above Adam's apple), moisturize beard oil, keep clean
- **Nails**: clean, filed (not sharp), cuticles pushed back — hands are visible, notice
- **Fragrance**: one spray on pulse points (wrist, neck, behind ears) — don't overspray
  - Lighter scents for day (citrus, aquatic), heavier for evening (wood, amber, leather)
