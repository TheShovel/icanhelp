# Goal Setting

Frameworks for setting and achieving meaningful goals.

## SMART Goals
- **Specific**: Clear, unambiguous. "Run a 5K in under 30 minutes" not "get in shape"
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable progress. Track distance, time, weight, revenue, pages written
- **Achievable**: Challenging but realistic given resources/time. Stretch but not impossible
- **Relevant**: Aligned with values, long-term vision, current priorities. "Why does this matter?"
- **Time-bound**: Deadline + milestones. "By June 30" not "someday"
- Example: "I will save $5,000 by December 31 by auto-transferring $416/month to my HYSA"

## OKR (Objectives and Key Results)
- **Objective**: Inspiring, qualitative goal — "Become the fastest runner in our club"
- **Key Results**: 3-5 quantitative measures of progress — "Improve 5K time from 22:00 to 19:30", "Run 40 miles per week consistently", "Place top 3 in next 3 races"
- KRs should be stretch goals (70% completion = success) — if always 100%, not ambitious enough
- Used by Google, Intel, startups. Google's original OKR: "Build the world's best search engine" (Objective). Key results: "Index 50M+ pages", "P99 latency <500ms", "90%+ user satisfaction"

## Goal Categories
- **Career**: Position, skills, network, income, side project
- **Health**: Fitness, nutrition, sleep, mental health, medical checkups, substance goals
- **Financial**: Income, savings, investments, debt payoff, net worth targets
- **Relationships**: Partner, family, friends, community, new connections
- **Personal growth**: Learning (language, skill, degree), reading, habits, therapy, spiritual
- **Experience**: Travel, events, adventures, new activities — experiences > possessions (more lasting happiness)
- **Contribution**: Volunteering, mentoring, donations, environmental impact

## Macro vs Micro Goals
- **Life goals (10+ years)**: "Own a home", "Be financially independent", "Write a book"
- **Long-term goals (3-5 years)**: "Buy house in 3 years", "Get promoted to director"
- **Short-term goals (3-12 months)**: "Save $30k down payment", "Complete certification"
- **Weekly actions**: "Call realtor", "Review listings", "Tour 2 houses this weekend"
- **Daily habits**: "Save $100 automatically", "Review budget", "Study for 30 min"
- Break big goals into tiny steps — each step is non-negotiable this week

## Implementation Intentions
- **If-then plans**: "If [situation], then I will [behavior]". Example: "If it's 7 AM, then I will go for a 20 min run" — much more effective than vague goals
- Triggers: Time, location, preceding event, emotional state, other people present
- Implementation intentions automate decisions — no willpower needed when the moment comes
- Meta-analysis: 2-3x more likely to follow through with if-then plans

## Tracking
- **Habit trackers**: Streaks are motivating — mark X on calendar each day you do the thing. Don't break the chain. Apps: Habitica, Streaks, Loop Habits
- **Weekly review**: Sunday — what worked, what didn't, adjust for next week. Celebrate wins. Most important habit for goal achievement
- **Progress not perfection**: Missing a day doesn't matter — missing two in a row does. "Never miss twice" rule
- **Metrics dashboard**: Visualize progress (weight chart, savings graph, pages written). Seeing progress = motivation

## Common Pitfalls
- **Too many goals**: Focus on 1-3 major goals at a time. "If everything is important, nothing is." Each additional goal dilutes attention
- **Vague goals**: "Get healthy" → "Exercise 4x/week, eat 5+ servings veggies daily, sleep 8 hours"
- **Unrealistic timeline**: Parkinson's Law + optimism bias = underestimate time needed. Double your estimate
- **All or nothing thinking**: One slip doesn't mean failure. "I missed a workout, might as well skip the week" — pernicious
- **External goals**: Goals based on others' expectations (family, society) lack intrinsic motivation → abandoned. Ask "Do I really want this?"
- **No system**: Goals without systems = wishes. Build habits, routines, environment that make the goal inevitable

## Values-Based Goal Setting
- **Ask why**: Dig deeper — "I want to be a manager" → "Why?" → "To mentor others" → "Why?" → "To pass on what I've learned". Values: growth, contribution, legacy
- **SMART goals on top of values**: Values provide direction (north star), goals provide milestones. If goal conflicts with value, reconsider goal
- **Life wheel**: Rate satisfaction (1-10) in 8 areas (health, finance, relationships, career, growth, fun, environment, spirituality). Focus on lowest slices
- **Regret minimization**: Jeff Bezos' framework — imagine being 80, which decision would you regret not making? Do that thing. Separates fear-based from value-based choices

## Tools
- **Digital**: Notion (goal tracker, OKR templates), Todoist (tasks with due dates + projects), Habitica (gamified habits), Strides (SMART goal tracker), Year Compass (annual review workbook)
- **Analog**: Bullet journal (future log → monthly → weekly → daily), Pen + paper goal sheet (see it daily), Vision board (images of goals on physical board), Journal (weekly check-in)
- **Accountability**: Partner (weekly check-in), Coach (professional), Group (mastermind, running club), Public (posting progress online)
