# Grilling & BBQ

## Types of Grills
- **Charcoal**: highest heat, smoke flavor, requires skill to manage temp (vents = airflow control). Weber kettle = classic, versatile
  - Lump charcoal: pure wood, burns hotter, less ash, better flavor (burns faster, harder to keep consistent)
  - Briquettes: consistent heat, longer burn, easier to light (some with additives — avoid match-light/self-lighting, chemical taste)
  - Chimney starter: best way to light charcoal (no lighter fluid taste!). Crumple newspaper under, fill with charcoal, light. Ready in 15-20 min
- **Gas**: convenient, instant-on, temperature control — less smoke flavor (use smoker box with wood chips)
  - Burners: 2-4, infrared searing burner (sides/back). BTUs: 30,000-60,000 for standard 3-burner
- **Pellet**: wood pellets, electric/set temp, smoke + grill — "set it and forget it" (good for smoking, less searing power)
  - Temp range: 180-500°F, automatic auger feeds pellets, thermostat holds ±10°F
- **Kamado** (Big Green Egg, Kamado Joe): ceramic, holds heat like crazy, charcoal, grill + smoke + bake (very versatile, expensive, heavy)
- **Flat top** (Blackstone): griddle, not grill — smash burgers, breakfast, stir-fry, hibachi, pancakes. Great for even cooking, no flare-ups

## Fire Management
- **Direct heat**: food directly over coals/burner — for thin cuts (steaks, burgers, chicken pieces, skewers, veggies), high heat, quick cooking
  - Temp: medium-high (400-450°F) for direct grilling
- **Indirect heat**: food AWAY from coals/burner, lid down — for thick cuts (whole chicken, ribs, brisket, pork butt), slow cooking (250-350°F)
  - Setup: coals on one side, food on other — Weber method. Grill thermometer at grate level (not lid — huge difference)
- **Two-zone fire**: coals on half the grill — start over high heat for sear, move to cool side to finish without burning
  - Essential for temperature control — gives escape from flare-ups
- **Smoke**: add wood chips/chunks (soaked 30 min) to coals, smoker box (gas), or foil packet with holes
  - Wood: hickory (strong, pork/ribs), mesquite (very strong, beef — use sparingly), apple (mild, sweet, poultry/pork), cherry (mild, fruity, poultry/beef), oak (medium, versatile, brisket), pecan (mild, nutty, fish/poultry), maple (mild, sweet, poultry/vegetables)
  - Soaking wood: debated whether necessary — wet chips smoke slower but don't add more flavor. Dry chips burn faster, hotter
- **Temperature control**: lid vents — open (more oxygen = hotter), closed (less = cooler). Exhaust vent on top controls airflow. For gas: burner knobs
  - Target temps: low = 225-275°F (smoking), medium = 325-375°F (grilling chicken/pork), medium-high = 400-450°F (steaks/burgers/veggies), high = 500°F+ (searing)

## Steak (Best on charcoal)
1. Pat dry (critical for sear), season generously with salt + pepper, let sit 30-60 min (or dry brine overnight in fridge)
2. Set up high-heat direct zone (500°F+), grill grate clean + oiled
3. Sear 3-4 min per side (don't move — let crust form), flip once
4. Move to indirect side for thicker steaks (1.5"+), cook to desired temp
5. Internal temps: rare 120-125°F, medium-rare 130-135°F, medium 140-145°F, well done 160°F+ (don't)
6. REST 5-10 min before slicing (juices redistribute). Slice against the grain
   - Carryover: steak continues cooking 3-5°F after removed from heat. Pull at 125°F for medium-rare

## Burgers
- 80/20 ground beef (20% fat = juicy, 80% lean). Handle minimally (overworking = tough). Make patty slightly larger than bun (shrinks), indent center (prevents puffing up)
  - Smash burgers: thin patty on hot flat top/griddle, press thin with spatula, salt + pepper, 2 min per side, add cheese, serve with pickles/onions
- Season just before grilling (salt draws out moisture if applied too early)
- High heat, 4-5 min per side. Cheese on last min (cover with dome/foil to melt). Internal temp 160°F (ground beef)
  - For medium: 145°F — BUT ground meat requires 160°F for food safety (bacteria mixed throughout)

## Chicken
- **Thighs/drumsticks**: best for grilling (dark meat = forgiving, stays moist). 375-400°F, indirect heat, 30-40 min, internal 175-185°F. Baste with sauce last 10 min (sugar burns)
- **Breasts**: brine 30-60 min (salt water = more moisture). Pound to even thickness. 400-450°F direct, 6-8 min per side, internal 160-165°F. Don't overcook = dry
  - Marinate: yogurt (tenderizes), oil + herbs + citrus. 30 min to 4 hours (acid longer than 4h = mushy texture)
- **Whole chicken**: spatchcock (remove backbone = flat, cooks evenly). 350°F indirect, 45-60 min, internal 165°F breast/175°F thigh. Rub with oil + spices
  - Beer can chicken: whole chicken over open beer can (vertical) — moisture from beer steams inside. Fun but doesn't add much flavor. Better: spatchcock

## Pork
- **Ribs**: remove membrane (back of ribs, paper towel grip). Dry rub. 225-250°F indirect, 3-4 hours (baby back) or 4-5 hours (spare ribs/St. Louis cut)
  - 3-2-1 method: 3 hours smoke, 2 hours wrapped in foil (with apple juice/honey/butter), 1 hour unwrapped with sauce
  - Done when: bend test (rib bends 90°, meat cracks on surface) or toothpick goes through like butter
- **Pulled pork (pork shoulder/butt)**: 225-275°F, 8-12 hours (or more), internal 200-205°F (collagen melts). Wrap when stall (160-170°F — temp plateaus, wrap in foil or butcher paper to push through)
  - The stall: evaporative cooling — temp stays same for hours while moisture evaporates. Wrapping shortens stall
  - Rest 30-60 min (or cooler 2-4+ hours), shred, mix in some juices/sauce

## Vegetables
- **Corn**: husk on (soak 30 min first) or husk off (oil + season), direct medium heat 12-15 min, rotate. Husk on = steamed, husk off = charred
  - Cut kernels off cob, toss in butter + lime + chili powder + cotija cheese (Mexican street corn/elote style)
- **Asparagus**: oil + salt + pepper, direct high heat 4-6 min, roll once. Cook in batch or on skewers (don't lose through grates)
- **Bell peppers**: whole (char skin, steam in bowl covered, peel = roasted peppers) or sliced (direct heat 5-7 min)
- **Zucchini/eggplant**: sliced lengthwise 1/4" thick, oil + salt, direct medium heat 3-4 min per side. Watch for flare-ups with oily vegetables
- **Potatoes**: parboil 10 min, smash slightly, oil + herbs + salt, indirect 400°F 20-30 min. Or foil packet with butter + herbs (30-40 min on indirect heat)
- **Pizza**: grill on high heat, crust one side (3-4 min), flip, add toppings, close lid until cheese melts (3-5 min). Gas/charcoal pizza = smoky, crisp

## Safety
- **Fire**: keep grill 10+ ft from house/deck rails/overhang. Never grill indoors or in garage (CO kills — even with door open)
- **Flare-ups**: keep spray bottle of water, move food to indirect side, close lid (starves oxygen)
  - For bad flare: close lid, slide food away, let fire die down — don't spray water into grease fire (spreads)
- **Food safety**: separate raw meat from cooked, wash hands/tongs/plates after touching raw, cook to internal temp (thermometer!), don't leave food out >1 hour in summer
  - Carry two sets of tongs/spatulas: one for raw, one for cooked. Use plate-swap to avoid cross-contamination
- **Meat thermometer**: most important grilling tool. Instant-read (Thermapen = best, $100; ThermoPop = great, $30-40). Leave-in probe (for longer cooks, connected to remote monitor)
