# Automotive Maintenance & Repair

## Fluid Checks (Monthly)
- **Engine oil**: pull dipstick, wipe, reinsert, pull. Level between marks. Color: amber/translucent = good. Dark = old (change). Milky = coolant leak (head gasket - bad). Metallic = internal wear
- **Coolant**: reservoir tank (marked min/max). NEVER open radiator cap when engine hot (pressurized, boiling coolant sprays out). Color: green (old IAT) or orange/dex-cool (OAT). Don't mix types
- **Brake fluid**: reservoir (clear/light yellow = new). Dark brown = old (absorbs moisture, lowers boiling point → brake fade under heavy use). Low = worn brake pads (pads thin, caliper extends = reservoir level drops as pads wear down)
- **Power steering**: dipstick or reservoir (ATF or special PS fluid — check owner's manual). Low = leak (hose, pump, rack). Whining noise = low fluid or air in system
- **Transmission**: dipstick (engine running, warm, park/neutral). Red/pink = good. Brown/burnt smell = service needed (fluid overheat, clutches slipping). CVT: special fluid ONLY
- **Washer fluid**: refill with -20°F rated in winter (prevents freezing). Not just water

## Tire Care
- **Pressure**: check monthly (cold, before driving). Doorjamb sticker (NOT sidewall max). Under-inflation = heat buildup, blowout risk, worse fuel economy, edge wear. Over-inflation = center wear, harsh ride
- **Tread depth**: penny test (insert with Lincoln's head down — if you see full head = <2/32", replace). Wear indicators: bars appear across tread at 2/32". Replacement: 4/32" to 6/32" in wet climate (1.6 mm minimum legal in most states; but braking in rain decays at 4/32" tread depth, 2/32" is borderline unsafe in wet)
- **Rotation**: every 5,000-7,500 miles (with oil change). Patterns: front-wheel drive: front → back (cross or straight depending)
- **Alignment**: car pulls to one side, steering wheel off-center, uneven tire wear. Not same as tire balance (vibration at highway speed). Alignment: adjust suspension angles (camber, caster, toe) for proper geometry, prevent tire wear. $80-150
- **Winter tires**: below 45°F (7°C), all-season rubber hardens = less grip. Winter tires stay flexible, have deeper tread + sipes for snow/ice. Studded winter tires: best for ice. Studless winter tires (Blizzak, X-Ice, Hakkapeliitta) > studded on most winter surfaces

## Battery
- **Terminal cleaning**: baking soda + water paste, wire brush. Corrosion = green/white crust (leaking/outgassing terminals). Clean with brush, tighten connections. Apply dielectric grease after cleaning to prevent corrosion
- **Testing**: with multimeter (12.6V+ = full, 12.4V = 75%, 12.0V = discharged). Load test (at auto parts store, free). CCA (Cold Cranking Amps) rating — match or exceed OEM spec. Battery age: usually 3-5 years; replace proactively especially if living in cold climate (cold cranking strain)
- **Jump start**: red to dead (+), red to donor (+), black to donor (-), black to dead car GROUND (unpainted metal, not battery). Remove in reverse order

## Brakes
- **Squealing**: wear indicator (metal tab rubs rotor when pads are ≤3mm). Replace pads immediately (rotors may be salvageable)
- **Grinding**: metal-to-metal = pads completely worn. Rotors damaged — need replacement + new pads. Don't drive longer than necessary (rotor replacement more expensive than pads)
- **Vibration/pulsation when braking**: warped rotors (from overheating — hard braking downhill or heavy vehicle). Replace or resurface rotors
- **Soft/mushy pedal**: air in brake lines (bleed brakes). Low fluid (check for leak). Bad master cylinder (fluid doesn't increase pressure)
- **Hard pedal**: brake booster failure (vacuum hose, diaphragm, or check valve). Power brakes rely on engine vacuum — if booster fails, pedal very hard
- **Brake fluid**: flush every 2-3 years (hygroscopic — absorbs moisture, lowers boiling point, corrodes internal components). DOT 3/4/5.1 (glycol, incompatible with silicone-base DOT 5 — purple, not for most road cars with ABS). Use same type as in system!

## Engine Diagnostics
- **Check engine light**: get code read (auto parts store free, or OBD-II scanner $20-30). Common: P0420 (catalyst efficiency below threshold — often O2 sensor or no issue but monitor catalyst). P0300 (random misfire — plugs, coils, vacuum leak). P0171/0174 (system too lean — vacuum leak, MAF, fuel issue). P0455 (gross EVAP leak — loose gas cap). Not all codes = urgent — but flashing CEL = catalyst-damaging misfire = stop driving soon
- **Overheating**: stop driving! Causes: low coolant, coolant leak, bad thermostat, failed water pump, clogged radiator, failed cooling fan, blown head gasket. If temp gauge rises: turn off AC, turn on heat (max heat + fan high — helps pull heat out of engine by using heater core as extra radiator). Pull over if still rising. Do NOT open radiator cap while hot (scalding steam)
- **Oil pressure light**: STOP IMMEDIATELY. Oil pump failure, low oil, or clogged pickup. Engine damage in minutes. Tow

## Belts & Hoses
- **Serpentine belt**: drives alternator, water pump, AC compressor, power steering. Check: cracks, fraying, glazing (shiny = slipping). Replace every 60,000-100,000 miles (sooner if cracks appear — can snap without warning, stops car, may also overheat engine because water pump stops). Some cars have timing belt (inside engine — rubber, drives camshaft). Replace per schedule (60-100k) or engine damage (piston strikes valve) — if interference engine. Timing chain (most modern): lasts longer, not maintenance free completely
- **Hoses**: squeeze when cold (should be firm, not soft/mushy or hard/brittle). Leaks at connection points (clamps). Replace if bulging, cracked, or >10 years old

## Maintenance Schedule (General)
- **Every Oil Change (5-7.5k miles)**: check tire pressure + tread. Check all fluid levels. Inspect belts + hoses. Check air filter. Rotate tires. Check wiper blades
- **15,000 miles**: cabin air filter replacement (dust/pollen). Engine air filter
- **30,000 miles**: coolant flush, transmission fluid (some automatics: 50-100k. Check owner's manual). Spark plugs (in some vehicles: 60-100k). Brake fluid flush (every 2-3 years regardless of mileage)
- **60,000 miles**: timing belt (if equipped). Spark plugs. Coolant. Transmission service. Differential fluid (AWD/RWD)
- **100,000 miles +**: coolant, transmission, differential, transfer case, spark plugs/coils, suspension components (struts, shocks, control arm bushings, ball joints — "wear items"), oxygen sensors, water pump (when doing timing belt)
