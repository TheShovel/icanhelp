# Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

## What Is EQ
- Ability to recognize, understand, manage, and use emotions effectively (in yourself AND others)
- Four components (Daniel Goleman):
  1. **Self-awareness**: recognizing your emotions as they happen
  2. **Self-management**: regulating emotions, adapting
  3. **Social awareness / empathy**: understanding others' emotions
  4. **Relationship management**: influencing, inspiring, connecting
- EQ predicts success better than IQ in many domains — leadership, relationships, collaboration
- EQ can be developed at any age (unlike IQ which is largely fixed)

## Self-Awareness
- **Emotion labeling**: name the specific emotion, not just "I feel bad"
  - Anger (frustrated, irritated, furious, resentful) vs Sadness (disappointed, lonely, hurt, grief)
  - Anxiety (worried, nervous, overwhelmed, panicked) vs Joy (content, grateful, excited, proud)
  - The more specific, the more you can process it (studies show naming emotions reduces their intensity)
- **Body scan**: emotions show up physically — tight chest (anxiety), knot in stomach (fear), heat in face (anger)
  - Notice where in your body you feel things — it's a quicker read than your thoughts
- **Emotional triggers**: what situations consistently push your buttons? (criticism, feeling ignored, unfairness, loss of control)
  - Identifying triggers = you can prepare for them, not be blindsided
- **Values check**: when you feel off, ask: what value of mine is being violated? (integrity, respect, autonomy, excellence)
- **Journaling**: 5 min free-write about what you're feeling and why — increases self-awareness

## Self-Management
- **Pause before reacting**: count to 10, take a breath, walk away for 5 minutes
  - The gap between stimulus and response is where your freedom lives
- **Name it to tame it**: "I'm feeling angry right now because..." — acknowledging it reduces its power
- **Impulse control**: ask "Will this matter in 5 minutes? 5 hours? 5 days?" before acting on emotion
- **Stress management**: exercise, sleep, nature, social connection, meditation (non-negotiables for emotional regulation)
  - Stressed you is a different person — don't make important decisions when stressed
- **Self-talk**: "I can handle this" vs "This is a disaster" — reframe without toxic positivity
  - "This is hard. I've handled hard things before. I'll figure this out."
- **Accountability**: "I lost my temper and I'm sorry. That wasn't fair to you." — owning it builds trust

## Empathy (Understanding Others)
- **Perspective-taking**: "What might they be feeling right now? What might be driving their behavior?"
  - Often: people aren't trying to hurt you — they're trying to meet their own needs
- **Validation**: "That makes sense" / "I can see why you'd feel that way" — you don't have to agree to validate
  - Validation ≠ agreement. It's "I hear you and your feelings are real"
- **Active listening**: fully present, not planning response, not interrupting
  - Reflect: "It sounds like you felt..." — check if you understand
- **Curiosity**: instead of judging, ask "Can you help me understand your perspective?"
- **Non-verbal cues**: tone of voice (38% of communication), body language (55%), words (7%)
  - Crossed arms, avoiding eye contact, tense jaw, fidgeting — all signals
- **Micro-expressions**: brief (1/15-1/25 sec) involuntary facial expressions — universal across cultures
  - 7 universal emotions: happiness, sadness, anger, fear, surprise, disgust, contempt

## Relationship Management
- **Conflict resolution**: calm + specific + collaborative
  - "I feel [emotion] when [situation]. What I'd like is [request]. What do you think?"
  - Focus on problem, not person. Attack the issue, not each other
- **Influence**: people do things for THEIR reasons, not yours — find the overlap
  - Ask questions, understand their goals, show how your idea serves them
- **Inspiration**: share vision, show enthusiasm, recognize contributions, lead by example
  - People remember how you made them feel more than what you said
- **Collaboration**: set shared goals, establish clear roles, celebrate wins together
  - Trust is built slowly (consistency over time) and destroyed quickly (one betrayal)
- **Feedback**: specific + kind + actionable
  - "When you X, it had Y impact. Could you try Z instead?"
  - The feedback sandwich (positive → negative → positive) is patronizing — just be direct with care

## Handling Difficult Emotions
- **Anger**: signal that a boundary has been crossed — what's the boundary?
  - Take time-out (20+ min to cool down physiologically)
  - Healthy expression: "I'm angry because..." — not yelling, not suppressing
- **Anxiety**: signal of future threat — what are you afraid might happen?
  - Grounding: 5-4-3-2-1 senses. Breathing: 4-7-8. Worst case / best case / most likely case
- **Sadness**: signal of loss — what did you lose? (relationship, identity, opportunity, safety)
  - Let yourself cry (releases stress hormones, feels better after)
  - Connection > isolation when sad (but may need solitude first to process)
- **Guilt**: signal you violated your values — what value? How can you do better?
  - Guilt can be productive (motivates change) — shame (feeling bad about WHO you are) is not
- **Jealousy/envy**: signal of what you want — what do they have that you want? What's your path to it?
  - Can be motivating if channeled — destructive if dwelled on

## Building EQ Daily
- **Morning check-in**: "What am I feeling right now? Why? What do I need?"
- **Evening reflection**: "What was the most emotional moment today? How did I handle it? What could I do better?"
- **Name emotions** in conversation: "I'm feeling frustrated" or "That makes me happy" — normalizes emotional vocabulary
- **Read fiction**: stories build empathy (simulates social experience, broadens understanding of human experience)
- **Seek feedback**: ask trusted people "How do I come across? What could I improve?"
- **Practice curiosity**: every day, ask someone "How are you really doing?" and listen fully
- **Repair ruptures quickly**: if you hurt someone, apologize within 24 hours (before resentment builds)

## EQ at Work
- Self-awareness: know your strengths, weaknesses, triggers, and how others perceive you
  - Ask for feedback regularly — what should I start/stop/continue?
- Self-management: don't escalate situations when emotional — take time to cool off
  - Professionals handle frustration without making it everyone's problem
- Empathy: understand your boss's pressures, your colleague's challenges, your team's needs
  - People quit managers, not companies — bad EQ is the #1 reason
- Relationship management: network authentically, resolve conflicts directly, celebrate others
  - Give credit publicly, take criticism privately
- **Emotional culture**: teams with psychological safety (members feel safe to take risks, be vulnerable) outperform others dramatically (Google's Project Aristotle)
