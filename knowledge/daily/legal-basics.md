# Legal Basics for Everyday Life

## Contracts
- Elements: offer, acceptance, consideration (something of value exchanged), capacity, legality
- Written > verbal (easier to prove). Some contracts must be in writing (real estate, >1 year)
- Read before signing — if pressured to sign without reading, red flag
- Fine print: one-sided terms, arbitration clauses, auto-renewal, liability waivers
- Breach of contract: you can sue for damages (money to make you whole), not criminal
- Cooling-off period: 3 days to cancel certain contracts (door-to-door sales, some refinances) — NOT most purchases

## Renting (Tenant Rights)
- **Lease**: binding contract (read before signing). Month-to-month: more flexible, less notice needed
- **Security deposit**: typically 1 month rent, must be returned within 14-30 days (varies by state)
  - Landlord can deduct for damage beyond normal wear and tear (not normal cleaning/painting)
  - Required to provide itemized list of deductions
- **Repairs**: landlord must maintain habitable living conditions (heat, water, electricity, no mold/vermin)
  - If urgent (no heat in winter): notify in writing. If no action: some states allow repair-and-deduct or rent escrow
- **Eviction**: requires court order (landlord cannot lock you out, turn off utilities, or remove belongings)
  - Notice period varies (3-30 days depending on reason and state)
  - If going to court: housing aid and legal aid available for low income
- **Rent increase**: needs proper notice (30-60 days, varies), may have limits in rent-controlled cities
- Discrimination: landlord can't refuse based on race, religion, gender, family status, disability, sexual orientation

## Employment
- **At-will employment**: employer OR employee can end relationship anytime, any reason (except illegal)
  - Exceptions: discrimination, retaliation, breach of contract, public policy
- **Wage/hour**: federal minimum wage $7.25/hr, many states higher. Overtime (1.5x) for >40hr/week (non-exempt)
- **1099 vs W-2**: independent contractor (1099) vs employee (W-2)
  - Misclassification: if employer controls your schedule/tools, you're probably W-2 (they owe payroll taxes)
  - 1099 workers: pay self-employment tax (15.3%), no benefits, no unemployment
- **Discrimination**: protected classes (federal): race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age 40+, disability
  - Many states add: sexual orientation, gender identity, marital status
  - File complaint with EEOC (federal) or state agency
- **Workplace harassment**: unwelcome conduct based on protected class that's severe/pervasive — employer has duty to prevent
- **FMLA (Family Medical Leave Act)**: 12 weeks unpaid leave / year for serious medical condition, family care, or new child
  - Applies to companies with 50+ employees, must have worked 12 months/1250 hr
- **Unemployment**: if laid off/ fired through no fault of your own — file immediately (waiting period, weekly benefits)
- **Non-compete**: agreement not to work for competitors (unenforceable in some states, limited duration/scope in others)
- **Non-disclosure (NDA)**: can't share confidential info — common in tech, can be mutual or one-way

## Consumer Rights
- **Warranty**: express (stated) vs implied (unspoken — product must be usable)
- Lemon law: if new vehicle has recurring defect that can't be fixed after reasonable attempts, manufacturer must replace/refund
- **Fraud**: if scammed, report to FTC, state AG, local police, bank (dispute charge)
  - Credit card chargebacks: 60-120 day window to dispute
- **Debt collection**: Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA) protects you
  - Collectors can't: call before 8am/after 9pm, threaten violence, use profanity, call your work (if told not to)
  - You can request debt validation (prove you owe it), dispute in writing to stop calls
- **Credit reporting**: free annual report at annualcreditreport.com from each bureau (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion)
  - Can dispute errors — bureau must investigate within 30 days

## Identity Theft
- If compromised: freeze credit at all 3 bureaus (free, prevents new accounts)
- Report: identitytheft.gov (FTC), local police, affected banks/ credit cards
- Fraud alert: place on credit report (90 days, creditors must verify identity)
- Common scams: phishing (fake emails/texts), IRS impersonator (they call? don't pay — IRS sends mail), "your computer has a virus"

## Will & Estate Planning
- **Will**: names executor, beneficiaries, guardian for minor children
  - Without will (intestate): state decides who gets your assets (spouse, children, parents)
  - Basic will: ~$200 online (LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer) or free form in some states, best with lawyer for complex
- **Trust**: more control over assets, avoids probate, privacy — more expensive to set up
- **Power of Attorney**: someone makes financial decisions if you're incapacitated
- **Healthcare directive / living will**: medical decisions if you can't communicate
- Beneficiary designations (401k, life insurance, bank accounts) override your will — keep updated

## Traffic & Law Enforcement
- Traffic stop: pull over safely, hands on wheel, provide license/registration/insurance, don't consent to search
  - Can refuse: consent to search (say "I do not consent to a search")
  - Cannot refuse: breathalyzer (implied consent laws — license suspended if refuse, varies by state)
- Police questioning: you have right to remain silent, right to lawyer (say "I'm exercising my right to remain silent")
  - Lying to police is crime; not answering usually isn't (confirm you're not under arrest)
- Arrest: ask for lawyer immediately, don't answer questions without lawyer present

## Small Claims Court
- For disputes up to $5,000-$10,000 (state dependent, no lawyer required)
- File in county where defendant lives or where incident occurred
- Filing fee $30-150, can serve papers by sheriff/certified mail
- Bring evidence: contract, photos, receipts, texts/emails, witnesses
- If you win: getting paid can still be difficult (may need to garnish wages/levy bank account)
