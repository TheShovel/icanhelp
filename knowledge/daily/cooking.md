# Cooking Basics

## Measurement Conversions
- 1 tbsp = 3 tsp
- 1 cup = 16 tbsp = 237 ml
- 1 fl oz = 30 ml
- 1 stick butter = 113 g = 8 tbsp
- 1 lb = 454 g
- 1 kg = 2.2 lb
- 1 oz = 28 g

## Common Substitutions
- 1 cup buttermilk: 1 cup milk + 1 tbsp lemon juice/vinegar, sit 5 min
- 1 tsp baking powder: 1/2 tsp cream of tartar + 1/4 tsp baking soda
- 1 cup sour cream: 1 cup plain yogurt
- 1 cup brown sugar: 1 cup white sugar + 1 tbsp molasses
- 1 tbsp cornstarch (thickener): 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
- 1 cup heavy cream: 3/4 cup milk + 1/4 cup melted butter
- 1 cup self-rising flour: 1 cup AP flour + 1.5 tsp baking powder + 1/4 tsp salt
- 1 clove garlic: 1/2 tsp garlic powder
- 1 tbsp fresh herbs: 1 tsp dried herbs
- 1 cup tomato sauce: 1/2 cup tomato paste + 1/2 cup water

## Cooking Methods
- **Sauté**: high heat, small oil, quick cook, constant movement
- **Sear**: high heat, no movement until crust forms, then flip
- **Roast**: dry heat in oven, 350-450°F, even browning
- **Braise**: sear then simmer in liquid, low heat 300-350°F, covered
- **Steam**: cook over boiling water, retains nutrients, no oil
- **Blanch**: boil briefly then ice bath — sets color, loosens skins
- **Parboil**: partially boil before another cooking method
- **Poach**: 160-180°F liquid, gentle, delicate foods (eggs, fish)
- **Simmer**: 185-205°F, small bubbles, soups/ stews
- **Boil**: 212°F at sea level, rolling bubbles, pasta/rice
- **Deep fry**: 350-375°F oil, food fully submerged

## Meat Temps (let rest 5 min after)
- Chicken breast: 165°F
- Chicken thigh: 175°F
- Beef steak rare: 125°F, medium-rare: 135°F, medium: 145°F
- Beef burger: 160°F
- Pork chop: 145°F
- Salmon: 125-130°F
- Lamb: 145°F medium

## Knife Skills
- Chef's knife for most tasks, paring knife for detail
- Claw grip: fingers curled inward, knuckles guide blade
- Rock chop: tip stays on board, blade rocks up and down
- Slice: smooth single motion from heel to tip
- Dice: slice → sticks → cubes (uniform size for even cooking)
- Sharpen: whetstone 1000/6000 grit, 15-20° angle, 10 strokes per side

## Pantry Staples
- Oils: olive (saute/dressing), vegetable (frying), sesame (finish)
- Spices: salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, cumin, oregano, chili flakes, cinnamon
- Canned: diced tomatoes, coconut milk, beans, broth
- Dry: rice (long grain, basmati, arborio), pasta, lentils, flour, sugar
- Condiments: soy sauce, vinegar (balsamic, white, apple cider), mustard, hot sauce
- Aromatics: onion, garlic, ginger, shallots, scallions

## Common Mistakes
- Pan not hot enough before adding food (no sear)
- Crowding the pan (steams instead of sears)
- Not resting meat after cooking (juices run out)
- Over-seasoning / under-seasoning (salt gradually)
- Opening oven constantly (loses heat)
- Not tasting as you go
