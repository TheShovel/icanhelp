# Social Media & Digital Presence

## Platform Overview
- **Instagram**: visual (photos + short video/reels). Demographics: 18-44, slightly more women. Best for: brands, lifestyle, art, fashion, food, travel, influencers
  - Content: grid posts, Stories (24hr), Reels (short vertical video — highest reach), carousel (multiple images/swipe). Hashtags 3-10 (used to be more, algorithm now groups fewer)
  - Algorithm: engagement (likes, saves, shares, comments > views. DMs = strongest signal)
- **TikTok**: short-form video, raw/authentic > polished. Demographics: 16-34, strong Gen Z. Best for: entertainment, educational, behind-the-scenes, trends, discovery
  - Algorithm: most powerful on any platform — learns preferences FAST (For You Page = highly personalized). Hashtags help discovery but watch time + completion rate drive reach
  - Strategy: hook in first 2 seconds, use trending sounds, don't overthink production. 21-34 sec optimal length
- **Twitter/X**: real-time text, news, hot takes, threads. Demographics: 25-54, news/politics/tech focused. Best for: experts, journalists, thought leaders, customer service
  - Threads (Meta): Twitter clone, growing, integrated with Instagram, less politics
  - Content: short posts, threads for depth, engage with replies. Timing: live events/news
- **LinkedIn**: professional network, B2B, job search, industry content. Demographics: 30-55, educated professionals
  - Best for: career, recruiting, B2B marketing, professional authority building
  - Content: long-form posts (1,500-3,000 chars perform well), professional insights, career advice, industry news
- **YouTube**: long-form video, tutorials, reviews, vlogs. Demographics: all ages. 2nd largest search engine after Google
  - Content: searchable (how-to, tutorials — evergreen), discoverable (trending), subscriber-based. Most work to produce, longest shelf life (videos years later still get views)
  - Algorithm: watch time + click-through rate (thumbnail + title) are king. YouTube Shorts (vertical, fast way to grow channel)

## Content Strategy
- **Pick 1-2 platforms** to focus on (don't try to be everywhere — burnout guaranteed)
  - Best effort: choose where your audience actually is, not where you "should" be
- **Content pillars**: 3-5 categories you consistently post about (e.g., tips, behind-scenes, personal stories, product, industry news)
  - Each pillar should serve: your audience's interests, your brand/god, and platform's algorithm
- **Content repurposing**: one long-form piece → multiple mini pieces across platforms
  - Blog post → Twitter thread → LinkedIn post → Instagram carousel → TikTok talking point
  - Podcast episode → YouTube clips → audiograms → quote graphics → blog summary
- **Posting frequency**: consistency > volume. Post 3-5x/week on Instagram/TikTok, 1-3x/day on Twitter/X, 1-2x/week on LinkedIn/YouTube
  - Quality destruction threshold: posting more often doesn't work if quality drops — maintain standards
- **Best times to post**: varies by audience, test and check analytics — generally weekday mornings/evenings (commute), lunch (12-1), after work (5-7). Mornings for LinkedIn/Twitter, weekends for lifestyle. Post when YOUR audience is online (check platform analytics)

## Growth Strategy
- **Organic**: consistent posting, engagement (reply to comments/DMs, engage with others), collaborations, hashtags, SEO (titles, captions, descriptions)
  - Post when target audience is online (check analytics)
  - Engagement pods/groups: NOT effective — algorithm detects + penalizes
  - Follow/unfollow: banned by most platforms now
- **Paid**: ads (Instagram/Facebook ads, TikTok ads, Google Ads, LinkedIn ads — CPC/CPM). Boost posts (less effective than full ad manager)
  - Start small ($5-10/day test budget), target specific audience, A/B test creatives
  - Retarget: people who visited website, engaged with content, etc.
- **Trend jacking**: participate in trends/relevant conversations while they're hot — not every trend fits every brand
- **Cross-promotion**: collaborate with similar-sized creators in your niche (shoutouts, duets, joint lives, guest appearances) — most effective growth tactic for small accounts

## Content Creation
- **Video**: simple > overproduced. Good lighting (face a window or ring light), clear audio (mic > phone mic), steady (tripod/gimbal)
  - Apps: CapCut (free, powerful, mobile + desktop), Premiere Rush (Adobe, easy, sync to Premiere Pro), InShot, DaVinci Resolve (free, pro desktop)
  - Hook: first 2-3 seconds decide if they watch or scroll. Start with the most interesting thing (not "Hi guys welcome back to my channel")
- **Photos**: good lighting (golden hour, diffused natural light), rule of thirds, edit lightly (don't oversaturate/filter to death)
  - Editing: Lightroom Mobile (free, powerful), VSCO (presets), Snapseed (Google, free)
  - Flat lay: overhead photo of arranged items — popular for food, products, lifestyle
  - Portrait phone: portrait mode (f/2.8-f/4), subject off-center, natural expression
- **Copywriting**: short sentences, line breaks, emojis sparing, call-to-action (what should they DO? — save, share, comment, click link)
  - Specific > general. "I made $1,247 from one post" > "I made good money"
  - Questions increase comments. Lists increase saves

## Analytics & Optimization
- **Key metrics**: reach (how many saw it), engagement (likes/comments/shares/saves/rate), click-through rate (CTR), conversion (desired action), follower growth
  - Vanity vs real: followers/likes = vanity (look good, don't pay bills). Engagement rate, saves, shares, DMs, conversions = real (actual value)
  - Engagement rate = total engagement / reach × 100%. Good = 3%+ average (varies by platform)
- **A/B test**: test different thumbnails, titles, hooks, posting times, formats — same content, different package
- **Audit monthly**: what performed? what didn't? double down on what works, stop what doesn't
  - Look for patterns: specific topics, formats, lengths, times that consistently outperform

## Pitfalls & Burnout
- **Comparison**: someone's highlight reel vs your behind-scenes — unfollow accounts that make you feel bad
  - Remember: you only see others' successful posts, not their failures/frustration
- **Algorithm stress**: reach drops? Normal. Platforms change algorithms constantly — don't chase, stay consistent
- **Burnout signs**: dreading posting, feeling anxious about content, posting for algorithm not audience, spending hours consuming
  - Set boundaries: batch create content (one day/week), no social media 1 hour before bed
- **Trolls**: don't feed them — don't engage. Block, mute keywords, restrict accounts. Report if threats/harassment
  - If serious threats: document (screenshots), report to platform, consider police if threats are credible
- **Authenticity vs performance**: audiences detect inauthenticity instantly. You can't fake it for long — post what genuinely interests/ excites you

## Platform-Specific

### Instagram Algorithm (Current)
- Posts shown based on: interest (similar content user engaged with), relationship (accounts they interact with), timeliness (recent), frequency (how often they use app)
  - Feed posts: favor photos and carousels. Reels: favor original audio + trending sounds
  - Do: post reels (longer reach), use 3-5 relevant hashtags, post carousels (high saves), interact with followers (reply, DMs, stories)
  - Don't: use #followforfollow, over-filter photos, post too infrequently

### TikTok Algorithm
- For You Page = personalized based on: user interactions, video info (captions, sounds, hashtags), device/account settings
  - Weighted heavily: watch time (especially full video), rewatches, shares, likes, comments
  - DO NOT: involve watermarks from other platforms (TikTok suppresses cross-platform watermarked content)
  - Discovery is democratic — new accounts with 0 followers can get millions of views (unlike other platforms)

### YouTube Algorithm
- Suggested videos (70% of watch time): based on what viewers watched after similar content. Search (20%): SEO matters (title, description, tags, captions)
  - Title: compelling + accurate (clickbait punished). Thumbnail: bright, high contrast, emotional face, text (bigger text = less effective)
  - First 48 hours critical for ranking (impressions, CTR, watch time)
