# Cat Behavior

Understanding feline behavior, communication, and training.

## Cat Communication
- **Tail position**: Upright with slight hook = happy greeting; puffed = fear/aggression; tucked = fear/sickness; slow swish = focused; fast thrash = annoyed
- **Ears**: Forward = interested/alert; flattened sideways (airplane ears) = irritated; flattened back = fear/defensive
- **Eyes**: Slow blink = trust/affection ("cat kiss") — slow blink back to communicate safety; dilated pupils = excitement/fear; half-closed = relaxed
- **Vocalizations**: Meow (for humans, rarely for other cats) — varies pitch/urgency; purr = contentment (also self-soothe when hurt/stressed); hiss/growl = fear/warning; trill = greeting; caterwaul = mating/territorial
- **Scent marking**: Cheek rubbing = friendly marking; chin rubbing = ownership; scratching = visual + scent mark; urine spraying = stress/territory; bunting (headbutt) = affection, marking you as safe

## Litter Box
- **Rule of N+1**: Provide one more box than number of cats — minimum 2 boxes for 1 cat
- **Box placement**: Quiet, low-traffic, away from food/water — avoid corners where cat can be ambushed
- **Litter depth**: 2-3 inches — most cats prefer unscented, clumping, fine-grain litter
- **Cleaning**: Scoop 1-2x daily; fully dump and wash with soap (no bleach/ammonia) every 2-4 weeks
- **Box type**: Open boxes are preferred — covered boxes trap odors, cause aversion; hood optional if cat likes privacy
- **Refusal reasons**: Dirty box, covered box, too small, location change, stressful household change, medical issue (UTI)

## Scratching
- **Normal behavior**: Sharpens claws, stretches muscles, marks territory (visual + scent glands in paws)
- **Provide alternatives**: Cardboard scratchers (flat or angled), sisal rope posts (tall enough for full stretch), corrugated cardboard
- **Placement**: Near sleeping areas, entryways, where cat already scratches — vertical + horizontal options
- **Deterrents**: Double-sided tape on scratched furniture (cat hates sticky paws); Sticky Paws brand
- **Never declaw**: Amputates last toe bone — causes pain, arthritis, litter box aversion — illegal in many countries
- **Nail caps**: Soft vinyl caps (Soft Claws) — glued on, last 4-6 weeks — humane alternative for destructive scratchers

## Environmental Enrichment
- **Vertical space**: Cat trees, shelves, window perches — cats are semi-arboreal, feel safe up high
- **Hiding spots**: Boxes, cat caves, tunnels — essential for shy/ stressed cats
- **Window access**: Bird feeder outside window = cat TV — mental stimulation
- **Puzzle feeders**: Food-dispensing toys — mimics hunting, prevents obesity, reduces boredom
- **Catios**: Enclosed outdoor patio — safe outdoor access for supervised fresh air
- **Rotation**: Rotate toys weekly to maintain novelty — cats habituate quickly to same toys

## Multi-Cat Household
- **Proper introduction**: Separate room for new cat → scent swap (rub cloth on each, place in other's space) → visual contact (baby gate or crack door) → supervised meetings → full integration. Takes 1-4 weeks
- **Resources**: Multiple food/water stations, litter boxes, resting spots — avoid competition
- **Food aggression**: Feed separate bowls in separate locations — or use microchip feeders
- **Hissing/growling on first meeting**: Normal — separate and re-introduce more gradually
- **Warning signs of real fighting**: Ears flat, growling/yowling, fur puffed, chasing with intent to fight (not play)

## Common Behavior Problems
- **Urinating outside box**: Medical first (UTI, kidney, diabetes) → stress → box issues. Vet visit required
- **Aggression**: Play aggression (stalking, pouncing, biting) → use toy wand, never hands. Fear aggression → identify trigger, create safe space. Petting-induced aggression (sudden bite while petting) → limit belly/vicinity petting, watch tail twitch
- **Night waking**: High energy at dawn/dusk (crepuscular) — play session before bed, automated feeder for early morning
- **Counter surfing**: Remove food rewards, provide cat-accessible alternatives (cat shelf near kitchen), use motion-sensor air spray (SSSCAT)
- **Excessive grooming**: Medical (allergies, pain) → stress → boredom → consult vet if bald spots or skin irritation

## Training Cats
- **Clicker training**: Same as dogs — small treats (pureed chicken, freeze-dried fish), short sessions (2-5 min)
- **Target training**: Teach nose touch to target stick — useful for behavior shaping, vet exams
- **Tricks cats can learn**: Sit, high-five, spin, come when called, fetch (some cats fetch naturally)
- **Harness training**: Start indoors with harness only (not leash), positive association, short sessions — patience critical
- **Carrier training**: Leave carrier out with bedding + treats — elevator gradually build positive association before vet visits

## Health Indicators
- **Appetite changes**: Sudden increase (hyperthyroid, diabetes) or decrease (dental, kidney, pancreatitis)
- **Water intake**: Excessive drinking = kidney disease, diabetes, hyperthyroid
- **Litter box changes**: Straining = urinary blockage (emergency for males); blood in urine = UTI; large volume = kidney/diabetes
- **Weight loss**: Over 1lb loss in adult cat = vet visit — can indicate serious conditions
- **Hiding**: Unusual hiding = pain or illness — cats hide weakness as survival instinct
- **Bad breath**: Dental disease — most common condition in cats over 3 years old
