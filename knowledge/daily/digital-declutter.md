# Digital Declutter

Strategies for reducing digital noise and improving focus through intentional technology use.

## Digital Minimalism (Cal Newport)
- **30-day digital declutter**: Take a 30-day break from optional technologies (social media, streaming, news apps, gaming). Rules: no use unless for work or specific need (GPS for trip). After 30 days, reintroduce only those providing significant value. Identify what you missed vs what you didn't
- **Sparsely use social media**: Schedule specific times (Saturday morning 30 min) rather than constant access. Use web version (not app) — reduces engagement loops. Unfollow all but closest friends and most meaningful accounts
- **Solitude deprivation**: Constant connectivity prevents deep thinking. Schedule phone-free walks, commute without headphones, time without screens. Brain needs boredom for creativity and reflection

## Notification Management
- **Turn off ALL non-personal notifications**: Default: nothing should interrupt you except calls/messages from family. Social media, news, games, shopping apps — all notification-less
- **Batch processing**: Check messages/email at scheduled times (10am, 2pm, 4pm) not constantly. Email: 2-3 times daily max. Set expectation with colleagues ("I check email 3x daily — call/IM for urgent")
- **Notification categories**: Critical (calls from family, alarms, calendar events — allow interruptions), Important (messages from close friends, work IM — banner but schedule), Low (email, social media, news — silent, check on schedule), None (everything else — remove permission)
- **Do Not Disturb**: Schedule during focus blocks (9am-12pm), sleep (10pm-7am). Allow repeated calls (calls twice within 15 min = emergency)
- **App notification audits**: Go to Settings → Notifications weekly. Ask "Do I need to know this immediately?" If no, turn off. Most don't. Average phone receives 63+ notifications/day

## Phone Optimization
- **Remove social media apps**: Use web browser for occasional checks (more friction = less usage). If you must keep: move to last page of home screen, in a folder called "Limbo" or similar
- **Grayscale**: Settings → Accessibility → Display → Color filters → Grayscale. Removes dopamine colors from phone. Makes phone less appealing (staring at monochrome screen feels boring)
- **Home screen minimal**: Only essential utility apps (Phone, Messages, Maps, Camera, Calendar, Notes). All other apps in folders on second page. Type to find anything (use search)
- **App limits**: iOS Screen Time / Android Digital Wellbeing — set 15-30 min daily limit per social media app. Hard limit (block after expiry for the day)
- **One device at a time**: No phone while watching TV, eating, in meetings, or before bed. Leave phone in another room during focus work. Use real alarm clock (not phone) to avoid bedtime scrolling

## Email Management
- **Inbox zero**: Process inbox to zero daily. Four Ds: Delete (unsubscribe/spam), Do (reply in <2 min), Delegate (forward), Defer (schedule time to respond for >2 min)
- **Unsubscribe ruthlessly**: Every commercial email → click unsubscribe. Use Unroll.me or manual unsubscribe. Goal: <20 emails/day (per day, not per hour)
- **Filters & labels**: Auto-archive newsletters (read when you choose), auto-label bills, auto-label notifications. Only primary inbox contains human-to-human messages
- **Send later**: Write emails during batch time, schedule for next morning. Avoid late-night sends (encourages 24/7 response expectation)
- **Short replies**: Respond quickly with brief but complete answers. "Got it, thanks." "Can't make it, sorry." "Yes, Thursday works." Not every email needs a novel

## Computer Organization
- **Desktop zero**: No files on desktop (slows boot, visual clutter, cognitive load). Desktop = workspace for current project only, cleaned weekly
- **Downloads folder**: Weekly purge — file documents to proper folders, delete temporary files. Use "Downloads → Keep → Sort" system. Don't let it accumulate years of cruft
- **File naming convention**: `YYYY-MM-DD_ProjectName_Version_Description.ext` — sortable, searchable. `2024-03-15_Q1Report_Draft_v2.docx`
- **Single inbox philosophy**: Minimize number of places with unread items. Email, SMS, Slack, Discord, Teams, Messenger, Instagram DM — audit where people can reach you. Unify where possible
- **Bookmark hygiene**: Monthly cleanup of bookmarks. Use bookmark folder structure: Work/Personal/Reference. Delete unused. Use search (browser address bar) instead of bookmarks for most navigation

## Social Media Strategy
- **1-2 platforms max**: Pick the one(s) that genuinely add value (connect with family, professional networking). Delete the rest. Having account on 5+ platforms is noise
- **Scheduled check**: Specific time block(s) per week. 15 min Saturday morning to catch up. No browsing during work, meals, or before bed
- **Unfollow / mute**: Unfollow anyone who doesn't add value. Mute triggering topics, people, or words. Your feed = your responsibility to curate
- **No doomscrolling**: Set app to open to a specific purpose (check notifications for someone specific, then close). "I'm opening this to check X" not "let me see what's new"
- **Algorithm training**: Interact (like, comment) with content you want more of. Mute/hide content you don't. Or just delete the app entirely

## News Consumption
- **Scheduled news**: Once daily (morning or evening), not throughout day. Use RSS reader (Feedly) or weekly newsletter (The Sample, 1440). Avoid push news notifications
- **Source variety**: 3-5 quality sources across spectrum. Local news + 2-3 national/international. Avoid single-source information diet
- **Deep reading > headlines**: Read article, not just headline. Better to read 3 full articles than 30 headlines. Reduces anxiety (headlines sensationalize)
- **News fast**: 1 day/week (Sunday) with no news. Everything important will still be there Monday. Reduces cortisol, improves sleep

## Digital Sabbath
- **24 hours offline**: Sunset Friday to sunset Saturday (or any 24-hr period). No screens: no phone, computer, TV, tablet. Activities: nature, reading (physical books), board games, conversation, walks, cooking, hobbies
- **Benefits**: Resets dopamine sensitivity. Reminds you life is rich without screens. Family/friends reconnect. Sleep quality improves dramatically
- **Phased approach**: Start with 4 hours → half day → full day. Some professionals can't do full day — do device-free evening (6pm-10pm daily) instead
