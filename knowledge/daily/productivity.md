# Personal Productivity & Organization

## Time Management Methods
- **Pomodoro Technique**: 25 min focused work, 5 min break, every 4th break 15-30 min
- **Time blocking**: schedule specific tasks in calendar blocks (include breaks)
- **Eat the frog**: do hardest/most important task first thing
- **MIT (Most Important Tasks)**: pick 3 most important things each day, do them before anything else
- **Ivy Lee Method**: at end of day, write 6 tasks for tomorrow, prioritize, next day do #1 first
- **GTD (Getting Things Done)**: capture everything, clarify next action, organize, review, engage
- **2-minute rule**: if a task takes < 2 min, do it immediately

## Task Management Systems
- Digital: Todoist, Things, TickTick, Microsoft To Do, Notion
- Physical: Bullet journal, plain notebook, index cards
- Kanban board: columns for To Do → Doing → Done (Trello, GitHub Projects)
- Capture system: use ONE inbox (app, notebook, or voice note) — never trust memory
- Weekly review: go through all inboxes, update projects, plan next week

## Focus & Deep Work
- **Deep work**: uninterrupted, high-concentration, cognitively demanding tasks
- **Shallow work**: emails, meetings, Slack — necessary but low value
- Schedule deep work blocks (90-120 min) — protect them like appointments
- Block distraction apps during deep work (Cold Turkey, Freedom, self-control)
- Phone on silent, face down, in another room if possible
- Do not multitask — task-switching costs 20-40% productivity loss
- Single monitor for deep work; multiple monitors = more context switching

## Email Management
- Check email 2-3 times/day (not constantly)
- **Inbox Zero**: process to inbox empty — delete, delegate, respond, defer, file
- Use filters/rules to auto-sort newsletters, notifications, etc.
- Respond to important emails within 24 hours
- Keep emails brief: 1-3 sentences often enough
- Use templates for common responses
- Unsubscribe from mailing lists you don't read
- For internal communication: prefer Slack/Teams over email

## Decision Making
- **Eisenhower Matrix**:
  - Urgent + Important: do now
  - Not urgent + Important: schedule
  - Urgent + Not important: delegate
  - Not urgent + Not important: delete
- **Pareto Principle (80/20 rule)**: 80% of results come from 20% of efforts
- **Decision fatigue**: make routine decisions automated/consistent (what to eat, what to wear)
- **Conscious vs unconscious decisions**: major decisions need sleep on it, writing pros/cons
- Minimum Viable Decision: pick the option that meets minimum criteria and move on
- Avoid analysis paralysis: done > perfect

## Habit Formation
- Cue → Craving → Response → Reward (habit loop)
- Make it obvious (cue), attractive (craving), easy (response), satisfying (reward)
- Inversion: make bad habits invisible, unattractive, difficult, unsatisfying
- Never miss twice — consistency > intensity
- Environment design > willpower (willpower is finite)
- Tracking: simple checkbox on calendar (don't break the chain)
- 66 days average to form new habit (range 18-254)

## Energy Management
- Manage energy, not just time
- Ultradian rhythms: 90 min focus cycles, then 15-20 min rest
- Peak energy: most people have 2-3 hours of peak focus in morning
- Low energy times: 1-3 PM (post-lunch dip) — use for admin/meetings/shallow work
- Napping: 10-20 min power nap, or 90 min full cycle (avoid 30-60 min — sleep inertia)
- Physical energy: exercise, sleep, hydration, nutrition — foundational
- Emotional energy: positive relationships, meaningful work, breaks from negativity

## Digital Organization
- Files: consistent naming convention (YYYY-MM-DD-project-description)
- Folder structure: broad categories, no more than 3 levels deep
- Desktop: zero files (it's not a filing system)
- Bookmarks: use folders, review/clean quarterly
- Passwords: use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password, KeePassXC)
- Notes: single note-taking system (Obsidian, Notion, Apple Notes, OneNote)
- Photos: delete duplicates and blurry, organize by date/event
