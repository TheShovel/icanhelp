# Tea & Coffee

## Coffee

### Coffee Bean Types
- **Arabica**: 60-70% of world production — smoother, sweeter, more complex flavor, grown at higher altitudes, more expensive
  - Varieties: Typica, Bourbon, Caturra, Geisha (most expensive, floral/tea-like), SL28 (African, berry-like)
  - Flavor notes: chocolate, caramel, fruit, floral, nutty, citrus — depends on origin + processing
- **Robusta**: stronger, more caffeine (2x), bitter, earthy, cheaper — used in espresso blends (crema + caffeine) and instant coffee
  - More disease-resistant, higher yield, lower altitude
- Regions: Ethiopia (fruity, floral, wine-like — birthplace of coffee), Colombia (balanced, caramel, nutty), Kenya (bright, acidic, berry), Brazil (chocolate, nutty, low acid — largest producer), Costa Rica (clean, bright), Sumatra/Indonesia (earthy, spicy, full body), Guatemala (complex, chocolatey), Jamaica Blue Mountain (mild, smooth, expensive), Yemen (ancient, wild, complex — Mocha original)

### Roast Levels
- **Light**: high acidity, complex flavor (origin notes), less body — bright, tea-like. Best for: pour-over, drip, single-origin appreciation
  - Cinnamon roast: barely roasted, grassy, sour — not common
- **Medium**: balanced acidity + body, more developed sweetness, caramel, chocolate notes. Most popular (what grocery "medium roast" usually is)
- **Medium-dark**: slight oil on surface, heavier body, less acidity, bittersweet chocolate notes
- **Dark**: shiny oil surface, low acidity, heavy body, roasty/ smoky flavors — origin notes largely lost. Best for: espresso (traditional Italian), bold flavor (French, Italian, Spanish roasts), milk drinks
  - French roast: very dark, smoky. Italian roast: even darker, almost charred. Spanish roast: darkest, oily

### Brewing Methods
- **Pour-over** (V60, Chemex, Kalita Wave): clean, bright, flavorful — requires gooseneck kettle, filter paper, precise technique. Coffee ground medium (table salt consistency)
  - Ratio: 60-70g coffee per 1L water. Water temp: 195-205°F (off boil 30 sec). Bloom: pour 2x coffee weight, wait 30-45 sec (gas release). Pour in stages (circles, center out, don't touch filter edge
  - Chemex: thicker filters = cleaner cup, less oil. V60: cone shape, fast flow. Kalita: flat bottom, more even extraction, forgiving
- **French press**: full body, all oils, heavier mouthfeel — coarse grind, steep 4 min, plunge slowly. No paper filter = more oils + fine particles (some studies suggest unfiltered coffee raises cholesterol slightly)
  - Clean: disassemble fully, rinse, scrub mesh. Oily residue = rancid next use
- **Espresso**: fine grind, high pressure (9 bar), 25-30 sec extraction, 1:2 ratio (18g in → 36g out). Crema = emulsified oils + CO₂ (sign of fresh coffee, proper extraction)
  - Dialing in: adjust grind (finer = slower extraction, more bitter), dose, yield. 25-30 sec from first drop
  - Common drinks: espresso (single shot ~1oz), Americano (espresso + hot water), latte (espresso + steamed milk), cappuccino (espresso + steamed milk + foam, equal parts), flat white (espresso + microfoam, no foam cap), cortado (espresso + equal steamed milk), macchiato (espresso + small amount of foam), mocha (latte + chocolate), affogato (espresso over ice cream)
- **AeroPress**: versatile, quick (2 min), easy cleanup, adjustable recipe — can make espresso-like or drip-like coffee. Paper or metal filter
- **Cold brew**: coarse grind, steep 12-24 hours at room temp or fridge, filter. Less acidic, smoother, concentrate (dilute 1:1 with water/milk). Not iced coffee (which is hot brewed then poured over ice — more acidic, different flavor)

### Freshness & Storage
- **Buy whole beans**, grind just before brewing (ground coffee loses flavor within minutes — huge surface area exposed to oxygen)
- Coffee should rest 3-7 days after roasting (degassing), peak flavor 1-3 weeks after roast date
- Store: air-tight container, cool/dark/dry. Not fridge/freezer (moisture + odors). Keep in original bag with valve (one-way valve lets CO₂ out, air in) or airtight canister
- Roast date (not "best by" date) is what matters — best within 4 weeks of roast date. Buy from local roaster or reputable online

## Tea

### Tea Types (all from same plant: Camellia sinensis)
- **White**: least processed — wilted + dried. Delicate, light, floral. Silver Needle (pure buds, premium), white peony (buds + leaves). Steep: 175°F, 3-5 min
- **Green**: steamed/pan-fired (stops oxidation). Fresh, grassy, vegetal, sometimes vegetal or toasty. Japanese: Sencha (grassy), Matcha (powdered, shade-grown, whisked), Gyokuro (shade-grown = sweeter, umami). Chinese: Longjing/Dragon Well (pan-fired, nutty, flat leaves), Biluochun (curly, fruity)
  - Steep: 160-180°F, 2-3 min (boiling water = bitter!). Cover pot to keep heat. Multiple infusions possible (get better 2nd/3rd steep)
- **Oolong**: partially oxidized (10-80%) — broad range from green-like to almost black. Complex, floral, fruity, creamy. Tieguanyin (Iron Goddess, floral, 15-30% oxidized), Da Hong Pao (Big Red Robe, roasted, mineral, 60-80%), Oriental Beauty (fruity, honey, insect-bitten leaves)
  - Gongfu brewing: high leaf-to-water ratio, short steeps, many infusions. Yixing clay pot (seasoned for one tea type)
- **Black**: fully oxidized — robust, malty, bold. English Breakfast (blend, strong — Assam + Ceylon + Kenyan). Assam (malty, strong — India). Darjeeling (muscatel, "champagne of teas, lighter). Earl Grey (black + bergamot oil). Ceylon (bright, citrusy — Sri Lanka)
  - Steep: 205°F (near boiling), 3-5 min. Traditionally with milk in Britain/India
- **Pu'erh**: fermented/aged — earthy, smooth, complex. Sheng (raw, aged naturally — can improve 20+ years), Shou (ripe, accelerated fermentation). Compressed into cakes. Gongfu brewing recommended, many steeps
  - Origin: Yunnan province, China. Increasingly popular, can be aged like wine
- **Herbal "tea"** (tisane): not from Camellia sinensis. Chamomile (calming, apple-like), peppermint (refreshing, digestive), rooibos (South African, red, sweet, nutty, caffeine-free), hibiscus (tart, red, high vitamin C), ginger (spicy, warming, anti-nausea), lavender (floral, calming), yerba mate (South American, caffeine, earthy, social — drunk from gourd with bombilla)

### Water & Steeping
- **Water quality matters**: filtered or spring water (tap with chlorine = bad tea). If your tap water tastes off, your tea will too
  - Soft water = better extraction. Hard water = dull flavor, scum on surface
- For high-quality loose leaf: 3-5g per 8oz cup, or 1 tsp per cup. Western style: 2-3 min, multiple steeps possible
  - Gongfu: 5-8g per 100ml, 20-30 sec first steep, increase each subsequent. Many high-quality teas can be resteeped 5-10+ times (each steep reveals different notes)
- Teaware: gaiwan (lidded bowl, best for all teas — versatile, easy), teapot (ceramic/cast iron/Yixing), infuser basket (best: large, mesh, lets leaves expand. Ball infusers restrict expansion = weaker tea)

### Caffeine Content
- Coffee: 95mg per 8oz (drip). Espresso: 63mg per shot. Instant: 62mg per cup. Decaf: 3-7mg
- Black tea: 40-70mg. Oolong: 30-50mg. Green/white: 15-30mg. Matcha: 60-80mg (whole leaf, more concentrated). Herbal: 0mg (except mate with ~85mg)
  - Myth: black tea has more caffeine than green — depends on varietal, growing conditions, brewing. Actually: tea plant stores more caffeine in younger leaves (white tea buds may have MORE caffeine than black)
  - Caffeine in tea: binds with L-theanine (amino acid) → slower absorption → smoother, longer energy without coffee's crash
