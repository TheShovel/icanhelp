# Event Planning & Party Hosting

## Event Types & Planning Phases
- **Casual gathering** (small dinner, game night, movie): 1-2 weeks lead time
- **Party** (birthday, holiday, housewarming): 3-6 weeks
- **Formal event** (wedding, gala, corporate): 6-12 months
- **Planning phases**: concept & budget → date & venue → guest list → vendors/food → logistics → day-of execution

## Budgeting
- **Line items**: venue (if applicable), food, drinks, decorations, entertainment/music, invitations, staffing, photography, contingency (10-15% of total)
  - DIY vs hire out: where does your time go? Invitations (DIY = cheap, time) vs catering (hire = less stress)
- **Per-person cost**: casual dinner $10-30/person at home, catered party $30-75/person, wedding $150-300+/person
  - Reduce cost: afternoon vs evening (less alcohol), buffet vs plated, BYOB vs open bar, potluck vs catered, off-season vs peak
- **Hosting at home** = cheaper but prep + cleanup costs (cleaning, rentals, supplies, parking, noise permits)

## Guest List
- **Capacity**: standing cocktail = 6 sq ft/person, seated dinner = 12-15 sq ft/person, buffet = much more space needed
  - Living room: usually holds 4-8 seated, 10-15 standing
  - Outdoor: more space forgiving, but need weather backup plan
- **Invites**: 3-4 weeks for parties, 6-8+ weeks for formal/out-of-town
  - Non-RSVP percentage: typically 50-75% attend. For formal: ask for RSVP response
  - Follow up with non-responders 5-7 days before event
  - Final headcount for venue/food: 3 days before
- Plus-ones: optional for large parties, expected for weddings/formal events

## Food Planning
- **Timing**: cocktail hour (guests arrive, snacks + drinks while waiting). Appetizers (3-5 types, portion: 4-6 pieces/person for pre-dinner, 10-15 if replacing dinner)
  - Dinner: 1 hour appetizers → 1.5-2 hours dinner → 45 min dessert
  - Late-night snack (for events going past 10 PM)
- **Servings**: cocktail party (10-15 pieces/person total). Buffet (1.5x portions). Plated (standard = you decide portion). Potluck (coordinate to avoid 5 potato salads)
- **Appetizer types**: dips + chips/crudite, skewers, bite-sized pastries, cheese board, charcuterie, crudite, deviled eggs, meatballs
- **Cheese board**: 3-5 cheeses (different types: aged cheddar, soft brie, blue, goat), crackers/bread, fruit (grapes, figs, dried apricot), nuts, honey, olives — take cheese out 30-60 min before serving for best flavor

## Beverage Planning
- **Full bar**: vodka, gin, whiskey, rum, tequila, vermouth (sweet + dry), triple sec, bitters — plus mixers (tonic, soda, juice, ginger beer), garnishes (citrus, cherries, olives)
  - For large event: 1 bottle liquor per 20 guests per 2 hours (for full bar, moderate drinking)
- **Beer + wine only**: 1 bottle wine per 2 guests (half red, half white/rosé), 2-3 beers per guest for party (adjust for known preferences)
- **Non-alcoholic**: at least 2-3 options beyond water/soda — sparkling water with citrus, mocktail, iced tea, NA beer/wine/spirits
  - Make NA options feel special (same garnish, same glassware)
- **Cocktail for party**: batch it (multiply recipe, serve from pitcher), pre-dilute with water to account for melting ice (about 15-20% of volume)
- **Spirits for large crowd**: handle serves liquor (handle = 1.75L = ~40 shots). Keep track of consumption — designate a bartender or self-serve with measures

## Decorations
- **Centerpieces**: below eye level (guests can see each other). Candles (always popular, flattering light)
  - Fresh flowers: buy at grocery day before, trim stems, arrange in water. Florist = $75-300+
  - Non-floral: books, candles, fruit, branches, seasonal items (pumpkins, pinecones)
- **Lighting**: dimmable > single setting. Candles, string lights, lamps without overhead on
  - Warm lights (2700-3000K). No fluorescent
- **Table setting**: charger plate under dinner plate, fork left, knife + spoon right, water/wine glass above knife, napkin left of forks or on plate
  - Budget option: nice paper plates + napkins for casual, ceramic for semi-formal+, rentals for large events
- **Furniture arrangement**: conversation groupings (circle > row). Clear flow: bar, food, seating in different zones. Dance floor if needed

## Timeline (Day of Event)
- **Morning**: final clean, set up tables/chairs, prepare food components (chop, marinate, set up serving dishes)
- **3-4 hours before**: ice delivery, arrange flowers/decor, set tables, sound check
- **1-2 hours before**: set out snacks/appetizers, prep final dishes, chill drinks, change into event clothes
- **30 min before**: lighting on, music on low, greet vendors (caterer, photographer, DJ), final walk-through
- **Start time**: open door, welcome first guests by name within 30 seconds
- **During**: circulate, ensure everyone has drink and is introduced, keep food coming, manage flow
- **After**: thank guests as they leave, don't start major cleanup until last guest gone

## Managing Vendors
- **Contracts**: date, time, services, deliverables, payment schedule, cancellation policy, insurance
- **Caterer**: menu tasting (book 1-2 months before), final headcount 3+ days before, service time, dietary accommodations
  - Ask about: staff ratio (1 server per 15 guests for plated, per 30 for buffet), linens/dishes included, waste removal
- **Photographer**: shot list (group photos, couple/family, candids), arrival/departure time, delivery timeline
  - Candid shots > staged group only (capture the feeling of the event)
- **Florist**: bloom availability by season, delivery/setup time, vase pickup/return

## Virtual/Hybrid Events
- **Platform**: Zoom (most familiar), Google Meet, Teams — check attendee familiarity
- **Structure**: shorter (45-60 min max for seated), more interactive (polls, Q&A, breakout rooms)
- **Catering**: no, unless budget for food delivery to attendees
- **Recording**: if valuable content (lecture, workshop), record and share with attendees who can't make it
- **Tech check**: test camera/mic/lighting/screen share before event, have backup presenter who can take over if host has tech issues
- **Photography/camera positioning**: camera at eye level, good light source in front of face (window + ring light), clean background, wired internet connection (WiFi drops)

## Post-Event
- **Cleanup plan**: trash bags, recycling, leftovers (have takeaway containers for guests), return rentals next day
  - Ask 1-2 trusted guests if they can stay 30 min after (if they're OK with helping)
- **Thank-yous**: within 1 week for gifts, within 2 weeks for attendance (if small gathering)
  - For large events: generic "thanks for coming" on social media or email
- **Deposits**: refundable deposits (venue, rentals) — check condition, return on time
- **Evaluate**: what worked? what would you change? cost overruns? logistics problems? — learn for next time
