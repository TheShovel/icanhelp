# Car Basics for Non-Mechanics

## Dashboard Warning Lights
- **Check engine**: many things (loose gas cap to serious engine problem). If solid: not urgent but check soon. If flashing: serious (misfire, catalytic converter damage risk) — get towed
  - Get code read at auto parts store (free), then decide
- **Oil pressure can (red)**: STOP immediately — low oil pressure = engine damage. Check oil level, add if low, if light stays on → tow to shop
- **Battery light**: charging system problem (alternator, battery, belt). Car may run for 20-60 min then die — head to shop
- **Temperature/thermometer**: engine overheating — PULL OVER safely, turn off engine, wait 15+ min before opening coolant reservoir (HOT! burns!), check coolant level. Tow if needed
  - Driving with overheating engine can cause permanent damage (warped head, blown head gasket — $2k-5k repair)
- **ABS**: anti-lock brake system issue — brakes still work but may lock up in emergency stop — check soon
- **Brake (!)**: parking brake on, or low brake fluid, or brake system problem. Check fluid, if low → brake pads may be worn out
- **Tire pressure**: one or more tires significantly low — check and inflate. Driving on low tire risks blowout
- **Airbag**: airbag system malfunction — airbags may not deploy in crash. Get fixed
- **Traction/stability control**: system off or malfunction — usually fine to drive but be careful in slippery conditions

## When to Take to a Mechanic
- **Immediately (tow)**: car won't start (and jump doesn't work), engine overheating (especially if coolant is full), loss of brakes, any fluid leak the size of a dinner plate or larger, smoke from engine, transmission slipping, check engine light flashing
- **Soon (this week)**: grinding noise when braking (pads worn to metal → rotor damage $$$), squealing when braking (wear indicator), persistent check engine light, vibration at high speeds (wheel balance? alignment?), fluid leaks (small puddles)
- **Routine**: oil change (3,000-10,000 miles depending on oil type — check manual!), tire rotation (every oil change), brake inspection, transmission fluid, coolant flush, spark plugs, belts/hoses, battery test

## What You Can Do Yourself
- **Check oil**: pull dipstick, wipe, reinsert, pull again — level between marks. Can be done every gas fill
  - Normal: honey-colored. Bad: milky (coolant leak = head gasket?), gritty (old), smells like gas
- **Jump start**: red clamp to dead battery (+), red to donor battery (+), black to donor (-), black to UNPAINTED metal on dead car (ground) — IN THIS ORDER. Remove in reverse order
  - Step-by-step:
    1. Connect red (+) to dead battery
    2. Connect red (+) to donor battery
    3. Connect black (-) to donor battery
    4. Connect black (-) to unpainted metal on dead car (struts work well — NOT near battery)
    5. Start donor car, let run 2 min, rev a little
    6. Start dead car. If no start: check connections, wait longer, try again
    7. Remove cables in REVERSE order
    8. Drive 20+ min to recharge battery
- **Check tire pressure**: use gauge, fill to PSI on doorjamb sticker (NOT sidewall max), check when tires cold (before driving)
  - Under-inflated: worse gas mileage, faster wear on edges, overheating risk
  - Over-inflated: harsh ride, faster wear in center, less traction
- **Change wiper blades**: easy clip-on, $10-20 each, replace every 6-12 months or when streaking
- **Replace air filter**: open box under hood, pull old, insert new — $15, takes 2 min, improves gas mileage + engine life
- **Replace cabin air filter**: behind glove compartment — $15, 5 min, improves HVAC air quality
- **Check fluid levels**: coolant, brake fluid, power steering, windshield washer — reservoirs are marked, fill to lines
- **Change a tire** — watch YouTube for YOUR car BEFORE you need to do it on the side of the highway

## How to Jump a Car (if stuck without cables)
- Manual transmission: push start (clutch in, put in 2nd gear, get pushed/bump start, pop clutch)
- Automatic: no push start possible — need cables or portable jump pack (recommended to keep one in car, $50-100)
  - Portable jump packs: NOCO, DeWalt — charge at home, keep in car, jump without a second car

## Gas Station Basics
- Know which fuel your car needs (check manual, gas cap, or inside fuel door)
  - Regular (87 octane) for most cars. Premium (91-93) for turbo/high compression engines. Using regular when premium is required = knock = engine damage long-term
  - Using premium when regular is fine = waste of money (no benefit)
- Never run tank to empty (sediment from bottom, fuel pump cooled by fuel, can leave you stranded)
- Diesel nozzle is larger than gasoline — can't accidentally misfuel (but if you do: DON'T START ENGINE — get towed and fuel system drained)
- Gas cap: tighten until it clicks — loose cap = check engine light + evaporative emissions leak

## Emergency Kit for Car
- Jumper cables or portable jump pack
- Tire repair kit (plug + compressor) or spare tire + jack + lug wrench
- Flashlight + extra batteries (or rechargeable headlamp — better, hands free)
- First aid kit
- Blanket (emergency/thermal) — especially winter
- Bottled water (1 gallon — drink + radiator)
- Multi-tool or basic tools (screwdrivers, pliers, socket set)
- Reflective triangles or road flares (warn other drivers)
- Ice scraper (winter)
- Phone charger cable
- Duct tape, zip ties
- Paper towels
- Cash ($20-40, some places don't take cards)

## Buying a Car
- **New**: depreciates 20-40% in first 3 years, full warranty, latest features, no hidden history
- **Used (1-3 years old)**: best value — already took biggest depreciation hit, still reliable, usually still under warranty
  - Certified Pre-Owned (CPO): manufacturer inspected + warranty extension — worth premium
  - Standard used: cheaper, no warranty — get pre-purchase inspection (PPI) from independent mechanic ($100-200, best money you'll spend)
- **Check maintenance history** before buying any used car — consistent oil changes = good sign
- **Test drive**: highway merging, stop/go traffic, turning radius, smooth shifting, brake feel, listen for unusual noises, check all electronics (AC, radio, windows, locks)
  - Drive on different road types (highway, bumpy road, hill)
  - Park on incline, check for fluid leaks after parking
- **Negotiate**: research market value (Kelley Blue Book, Edmunds, TrueCar), point out flaws, get quotes from multiple dealers
  - Buying at end of month/quarter/year = more motivated salespeople
  - Financing: get pre-approved from credit union/bank before going to dealer (they may beat rate, but have a baseline)
