# Particle Physics

Fundamental particles, forces, and the Standard Model.

## Standard Model Particles
- **Fermions (matter particles, spin ½)**: Quarks + Leptons. 3 generations (families) — higher mass each generation. First generation: up (u), down (d), electron (e⁻), electron neutrino (ν_e). Second: charm (c), strange (s), muon (μ), muon neutrino (ν_μ). Third: top (t), bottom (b), tau (τ), tau neutrino (ν_τ)
- **Quarks**: Fractional electric charge (+2/3: u, c, t; -1/3: d, s, b). Confined inside hadrons — never observed free. Color charge (red, green, blue — strong force). Six flavors. Top quark heaviest (173 GeV/c² — mass of gold atom)
- **Leptons**: Integer charge (electron, muon, tau = -1; neutrinos = 0). Electron = stable, muon (lifetime 2.2μs), tau (0.3ps — decays to hadrons + other leptons). Neutrinos: nearly massless (tiny mass ~<1 eV — oscillate between flavors, Nobel 2015)
- **Gauge bosons (force carriers, spin 1)**: Photon (γ — electromagnetic, massless), W⁺/W⁻/Z⁰ (weak force, massive ~80/91 GeV), Gluons (8 types — strong force, massless, carry color charge). Graviton (gravity — spin 2, hypothesized, not yet observed)
- **Higgs boson (spin 0)**: Gives mass to other particles via Higgs mechanism. Discovered at CERN 2012 (Nobel 2013, Higgs & Englert). Higgs field fills universe — particles interact with it = mass. Higgs boson mass 125 GeV/c²

## Fundamental Forces
- **Strong nuclear force**: Strongest (coupling constant α_s ~1 at low energy). Binds quarks into protons/neutrons, protons/neutrons into nucleus. Mediated by gluons. Range ~10⁻¹⁵ m. Confinement: quarks always bound (increasing separation increases energy until new quark-antiquark pair produced). Asymptotic freedom: force weakens at short distances (high energy)
- **Electromagnetic force**: Coupling α ≈ 1/137. Acts on charged particles. Mediated by photons. Infinite range. Binds electrons to nucleus (atoms). Responsible for chemistry, electricity, magnetism, light, most everyday forces (contact forces = electromagnetic repulsion between electron clouds)
- **Weak nuclear force**: Short range (~10⁻¹⁸ m), mediated by W/Z bosons (massive → short range). Responsible for radioactive beta decay (n → p + e⁻ + ν̄_e). Neutrino interactions. Changes quark flavor (d → u converts neutron to proton). Essential for solar fusion (p + p → deuterium)
- **Gravity**: Weakest force (α_G ~10⁻³⁹ relative to EM between two protons). Attractive only. Mediated by graviton (hypothetical, spin 2). Dominates at large scales (stars, galaxies) due to being always attractive + additive. Not in Standard Model — need quantum gravity theory

## Hadrons
- **Baryons**: 3 quarks (qqq). Protons (uud, charge +1, spin ½), Neutrons (udd, charge 0, spin ½). Also: Lambda (Λ, uds — strangeness -1), Sigma, Xi, Omega baryons. All baryons except proton decay (predicted by GUTs, not yet observed — limits >10³⁴ years)
- **Mesons**: Quark-antiquark pair (q̄q). Examples: Pion π⁺ (u d̄ — mediates strong force between nucleons), Kaon K⁺ (u s̄ — strange), J/ψ (c c̄ — charm-anticharm). Pions are lightest mesons (~140 MeV). Many mesons are unstable (lifetimes 10⁻⁸ to 10⁻²³ seconds)
- **Exotic hadrons**: Tetraquarks (qqq̄q̄), Pentaquarks (qqqqq̄) — observed at LHC (2003 for tetraquarks, 2015 for pentaquarks). Not predicted by simple quark model. Studying reveals QCD dynamics
- **Resonances**: Very short-lived excited states of hadrons (10⁻²³ sec). Detect as peaks in scattering cross-sections. Hundreds known

## Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD)
- **Color charge**: Red, Green, Blue (quarks). Anti-red/anti-green/anti-blue (antiquarks). Color confinement: only color-neutral combinations exist (baryons RGB, mesons R-antiR, G-antiG, B-antiB, glueballs)
- **Gluons**: Carry color + anti-color (8 types). Self-interact (gluons have color, unlike photons which have no charge). Self-interaction makes QCD calculations very difficult at low energy (non-perturbative)
- **Lattice QCD**: Numerical calculation on spacetime grid. Best tool for low-energy QCD (hadron masses, quark interactions). Supercomputer-intensive
- **Chiral symmetry breaking**: Explains why protons/neutrons mass (~1 GeV) is much larger than constituent quark masses (u/d ~5 MeV). Mass comes from QCD binding energy: E = mc²

## Electroweak Theory
- **Unification**: Electromagnetic + weak forces unified at high energy (~100 GeV). SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y symmetry. Higgs mechanism breaks symmetry → W/Z gain mass, photon remains massless
- **Weak mixing angle (Weinberg angle)**: θ_W ≈ 28°. Determines ratio of W and Z masses (m_W = m_Z cos θ_W). Explains neutral current vs charged current interaction strengths
- **Parity violation**: Weak force violates parity (mirror images behave differently). Only couples to left-handed particles and right-handed antiparticles. Discovered by Wu (1957, Nobel to Lee & Yang)

## Beyond the Standard Model
- **Supersymmetry (SUSY)**: Each fermion has boson partner and vice versa (selectron, photino, gluino...). Solves hierarchy problem (why Higgs mass is so small). Predicts dark matter candidate (neutralino). Not observed at LHC yet — limits getting tight
- **Dark matter**: 27% of universe. Particle candidates: WIMPs (weakly interacting massive particles ~10-1000 GeV), Axions (very light ~μeV — solve strong CP problem), Sterile neutrinos (heavier, don't interact via weak force). Direct detection experiments (XENONnT, LZ) search for WIMP-nucleon scattering
- **Neutrino mass**: Neutrinos have mass (from oscillations) but far lighter than any other fermion. Seesaw mechanism: heavy right-handed neutrinos (~10¹⁵ GeV) give tiny masses to left-handed ones. Absolute mass scale unknown — KATRIN experiment measuring
- **Grand Unified Theories (GUTs)**: Unify strong + electroweak at high energy (~10¹⁶ GeV). SU(5), SO(10) models. Predict proton decay (not yet seen). May explain matter-antimatter asymmetry of universe
- **String theory**: Fundamental objects are 1D strings (not point particles). Requires extra dimensions (10 or 11). Unifies all forces including gravity. No experimental predictions yet. M-theory (11D) unifies string theories

## Particle Accelerators
- **LHC** (CERN): 27km circumference, protons at 6.5 TeV each (13 TeV collision). Discovered Higgs 2012. ATLAS + CMS general-purpose detectors. ALICE (heavy ions), LHCb (b-physics). Run 3 started 2022, Run 4 targets 2030s (High Luminosity LHC)
- **Detector components**: Tracking (inner — measure charged particle paths), Calorimeters (measure energy — EM for electrons/photons, hadronic for protons/pions/neutrons), Magnet (bend charged particles for momentum measurement — solenoid 4T at CMS)
- **Fermilab** (US): Tevatron shut down 2011 (discovered top quark). Now: muon g-2 experiment (muon magnetic moment anomaly — hints of new physics), DUNE (neutrino oscillation, 1300km beam to South Dakota)
- **Future colliders**: FCC (CERN — 100km, 100 TeV), ILC (Japan — linear e+e- collider), CEPC (China — circular e+e-)
