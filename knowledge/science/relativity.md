# Relativity

Special relativity and general relativity — principles, effects, and applications.

## Special Relativity (1905, Einstein)
- **Two postulates**: (1) Laws of physics are the same in all inertial reference frames. (2) Speed of light in vacuum is constant (c = 299,792,458 m/s) regardless of observer's motion or source's motion
- **Time dilation**: Moving clocks run slower. Δt = Δt₀ / √(1 - v²/c²). Experimental confirmation: muons from cosmic rays reach Earth's surface (their 2.2μs half-life dilated to ~30μs at near-c speed). GPS satellites tick faster by ~7μs/day due to special relativity (and slower by 45μs from general relativity — net 38μs/day corrected)
- **Length contraction**: Moving objects contract along direction of motion. L = L₀√(1 - v²/c²). Objects at relativistic speeds appear shortened
- **Relativistic mass**: Mass increases with velocity: m = m₀/√(1 - v²/c²). Becomes infinite as v→c. Objects with mass cannot reach speed of light
- **Mass-energy equivalence**: E = mc². Small amount of mass = enormous energy. Nuclear reactions (fission, fusion) convert tiny mass to huge energy. 1 gram matter = 90 trillion joules (21.5 kilotons TNT)
- **Lorentz transformation**: Equations relating space and time coordinates between moving reference frames. Replace Galilean transformation for high speeds
- **Relativity of simultaneity**: Events simultaneous in one frame may not be in another moving frame. "Now" is relative
- **Twin paradox**: Traveling twin ages less than Earth-bound twin — not symmetrical because traveling twin must accelerate to turn around (breaks inertial frame)

## General Relativity (1915, Einstein)
- **Equivalence principle**: Gravitational acceleration is locally indistinguishable from acceleration due to propulsion. Being in accelerating rocket = standing on Earth. Light bends in gravitational field
- **Spacetime curvature**: Mass/energy warps spacetime — gravity is not a force but geometry. Objects follow geodesics (shortest paths) in curved spacetime. "Spacetime tells matter how to move; matter tells spacetime how to curve"
- **Einstein field equations**: G_μν + Λg_μν = (8πG/c⁴)T_μν. Relates spacetime curvature (left) to stress-energy tensor (right). Λ = cosmological constant (dark energy)
- **Schwarzschild metric**: Solution for spherical non-rotating mass — predicts black holes. Event horizon at r = 2GM/c² (Schwarzschild radius). For Sun: ~3km. For Earth: ~9mm
- **Gravitational waves**: Ripples in spacetime from accelerating masses (binary black hole mergers, neutron star collisions). Detected by LIGO (2015) — first direct observation. Travel at speed of light. Quadrupolar — stretch space in one direction, squeeze perpendicular
- **Gravitational lensing**: Light bends around massive objects — creates Einstein rings, multiple images, magnification. Used to study dark matter distribution (weak lensing), exoplanets (microlensing)

## Experimental Tests
- **Perihelion precession of Mercury**: Mercury's orbit precesses 43 arcseconds/century more than Newton predicts — GR explains it completely (first test, 1915)
- **Light deflection**: Starlight near Sun during solar eclipse bends 1.75 arcseconds — confirmed by Eddington (1919), made Einstein famous
- **Gravitational redshift**: Light climbing out of gravitational well loses energy → shifts to longer (redder) wavelength. Pound-Rebka experiment (1959) measured redshift climbing 22.5m tower at Harvard. GPS corrections: clocks at different altitudes must be synchronized for relativistic correction
- **Frame dragging (Lense-Thirring)**: Rotating mass drags spacetime around it. Gravity Probe B (2004-2010) measured spin-precession of gyroscopes in Earth orbit, confirmed to 19% accuracy

## Black Holes
- **Event horizon**: Boundary where escape velocity = c. Nothing (including light) escapes. Radius = 2GM/c²
- **Singularity**: Center where density becomes infinite, GR breaks down (needs quantum gravity — unknown physics). Penrose-Hawking singularity theorems: singularities inevitable under GR
- **Types**: Stellar-mass (3-100 solar masses — from supernova collapse), Supermassive (millions to billions solar masses — galactic centers. Sgr A* in Milky Way: 4.3 million solar masses), Intermediate (100-100,000 — rare, poorly understood)
- **Hawking radiation**: Quantum effect — virtual particle pairs near event horizon: one falls in, one escapes. Black holes evaporate over immense timescales. Temperature inversely proportional to mass. Supermassive black holes colder than CMB (absorb more than they emit). Micro black holes would explode
- **No-hair theorem**: Black holes completely described by mass, charge, spin. All other information (the "hair") is lost
- **Black hole information paradox**: Hawking radiation carries no information → if black hole evaporates, information destroyed? Violates quantum unitarity. Resolution likely from holographic principle / AdS/CFT correspondence

## Cosmology
- **Expanding universe**: Hubble-Lemaître law: v = H₀d — galaxies recede proportional to distance. H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc (Hubble tension: 67.4 vs 73.0 depending on method)
- **Big Bang**: Universe began ~13.8 billion years ago from hot, dense state. Cosmic microwave background (CMB) = afterglow at 2.725 K. First detected 1965 (Penzias-Wilson). Detailed map by WMAP (2003) and Planck (2013)
- **Dark matter**: 27% of universe — invisible, interacts only gravitationally. Evidence: galaxy rotation curves (stars orbit too fast for visible mass), gravitational lensing, CMB structure. Candidates: WIMPs, axions, sterile neutrinos. Not yet detected directly
- **Dark energy**: 68% of universe — causes accelerated expansion. Λ in Einstein's equations. Discovered 1998 from Type Ia supernova observations (Nobel 2011). Nature unknown (vacuum energy? quintessence?)
- **ΛCDM model**: Standard cosmological model — Lambda (dark energy) + Cold Dark Matter. Fits all current observations. Parameters: H₀, Ω_m, Ω_Λ, Ω_b, σ₈, n_s (spectral index)
