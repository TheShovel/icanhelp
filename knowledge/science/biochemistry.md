# Biochemistry

## Biomolecules

### Carbohydrates
- **Monosaccharides**: glucose (blood sugar, primary cellular fuel), fructose (fruit, sweetest), galactose (milk sugar component). General formula CH₂O. Structure: aldose (aldehyde group) vs ketose (ketone). D/L isomers — biology uses D-sugars. Linear vs ring (cyclic hemiacetal) forms — ring dominates in solution. Anomers: α vs β (OH position on anomeric carbon)
- **Disaccharides**: sucrose (glucose + fructose — table sugar), lactose (glucose + galactose — milk sugar), maltose (glucose + glucose — starch breakdown product). Glycosidic bonds: α vs β linkage — determines digestibility. Lactose intolerance = lactase enzyme deficiency
- **Polysaccharides**: starch (plant energy storage — amylose linear α-1,4 + amylopectin branched α-1,6), glycogen (animal energy storage — highly branched, stored in liver/muscle), cellulose (plant structural — β-1,4 glucose, humans can't digest — dietary fiber), chitin (arthropod exoskeleton, fungal cell walls — N-acetylglucosamine). Fiber: soluble (oats, apples, beans — dissolves, lowers cholesterol) vs insoluble (wheat bran, veggies — bulks stool)
- **Functions**: energy (4 Cal/g), energy storage (glycogen/starch), structure (cellulose, chitin), signaling (glycoproteins, blood types), cell recognition (glycocalyx).

### Lipids
- **Fatty acids**: long hydrocarbon chain + carboxyl group. Saturated (no double bonds — solid at RT, animal fats — butter, lard). Monounsaturated (1 double bond — olive oil, avocado). Polyunsaturated (≥2 double bonds — fish oil, flaxseed, sunflower). Trans fats: artificial partially hydrogenated (increase LDL, decrease HDL — banned in US 2018). Omega-3 (first double bond at carbon 3 — anti-inflammatory, EPA/DHA in fish). Omega-6 (pro-inflammatory — linoleic acid, balanced ratio matters)
- **Triacylglycerols (triglycerides)**: glycerol + 3 fatty acids. Main energy storage (9 Cal/g — >2x carb/protein). Adipose tissue stores triglycerides (white fat = energy, brown fat = thermogenesis)
- **Phospholipids**: glycerol + 2 fatty acids + phosphate head group. Amphipathic (hydrophilic head, hydrophobic tails) — form lipid bilayers of cell membranes. Major membrane lipids: phosphatidylcholine, phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylserine (negatively charged), sphingomyelin. Cholesterol: modulates membrane fluidity (stiffens at high temp, fluidizes at low)
- **Steroids**: four fused rings. Cholesterol (membrane, precursor), testosterone (androgen), estradiol (estrogen), cortisol (stress hormone), aldosterone (salt balance), vitamin D, bile acids (detergent for fat digestion)
- **Eicosanoids**: signaling molecules from arachidonic acid. Prostaglandins (pain, inflammation), thromboxanes (blood clotting), leukotrienes (allergy, asthma). NSAIDs (ibuprofen, aspirin) block COX-1/COX-2 enzymes that produce them

### Proteins
- **Amino acids**: 20 standard. Each has amine (NH₂), carboxyl (COOH), side chain (R — determines properties). Classification: nonpolar/hydrophobic (Gly, Ala, Val, Leu, Ile, Met, Phe, Trp, Pro), polar uncharged (Ser, Thr, Cys, Asn, Gln, Tyr), positively charged (Lys, Arg, His), negatively charged (Asp, Glu). Essential (9 — must get from diet: His, Ile, Leu, Lys, Met, Phe, Thr, Trp, Val). Conditionally essential (Arg, Cys, Gln, Gly, Pro, Tyr — needed under stress/illness)
- **Peptide bonds**: condensation reaction between COOH of one AA and NH₂ of next. Forms backbone: N-terminus → C-terminus. Residues can rotate around phi (φ) and psi (ψ) angles — Ramachandran plot shows allowed combinations. Disulfide bonds: cysteine S-S bridges stabilize structure
- **Functions**: enzymes (catalysis), structural (collagen, keratin), transport (hemoglobin, albumin), signaling (hormones, receptors), defense (antibodies), movement (actin, myosin), regulation (transcription factors)

### Nucleic Acids
- **Nucleotides**: nitrogenous base (purine — A,G / pyrimidine — C,T,U) + pentose sugar (ribose in RNA, deoxyribose in DNA) + phosphate(s). ATP (energy currency), GTP (signaling, protein synthesis), cAMP (second messenger), NAD⁺/NADH (redox), FAD/H (redox)
- **DNA**: double-stranded helix (Watson-Crick). Antiparallel (5'→3' one strand, 3'→5' other). Major/minor grooves. Base pairing: A=T (2 H bonds), G≡C (3 H bonds — stronger). 10 base pairs per turn. DNA forms: B-DNA (most common, right-handed), A-DNA (dehydrated), Z-DNA (left-handed)
- **RNA**: single-stranded (can fold into complex structures — tRNA cloverleaf, rRNA complex). Types: mRNA (messenger — carries genetic code to ribosome), tRNA (transfer — brings amino acids, anticodon), rRNA (ribosomal — catalytic, forms ribosome). snRNA (splicing), miRNA/siRNA (gene regulation), lncRNA (regulatory)

## Enzymes
- **Active site**: specific 3D pocket where substrate binds. Induced fit: enzyme changes shape on binding (different from old lock-key model). Transition state stabilized — lowers activation energy (does NOT change equilibrium)
- **Co-factors**: inorganic ions (Zn²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺ — essential for catalysis). Co-enzymes: organic molecules (NAD⁺, FAD, CoA, vitamins). Prosthetic groups: tightly bound (heme, biotin). Holoenzyme = apoenzyme + cofactor
- **Kinetics**: Michaelis-Menten: v₀ = Vmax[S]/(Km+[S]). Vmax = maximum rate (all enzyme saturated). Km = substrate concentration at ½ Vmax (inverse affinity — low Km = high affinity). Turnover number kcat = molecules catalyzed per second per enzyme. kcat/Km = catalytic efficiency (diffusion limit ~10⁸-10⁹ M⁻¹s⁻¹)
- **Inhibition**: Competitive (binds active site — increased Km, same Vmax — overcome with more substrate. Statins compete with HMG-CoA reductase). Noncompetitive (binds elsewhere — same Km, lower Vmax. Cannot overcome with substrate). Uncompetitive (binds only ES complex — lower both). Mixed (different Km + Vmax). Irreversible (covalent — poison, nerve gas, some drugs like aspirin)
- **Regulation**: allosteric (effector binds non-active site — changes activity. Hemoglobin: O₂ binding cooperative, 2,3-BPG negative allosteric). Phosphorylation (kinases add P, phosphatases remove — activates or deactivates). Feedback inhibition (end product inhibits earlier enzyme — first committed step). Zymogen: inactive precursor cleaved to activate (trypsinogen → trypsin, digestive enzymes)

## Major Metabolic Pathways

### Glycolysis
- **Location**: cytoplasm. Net: 1 glucose (6C) → 2 pyruvate (3C) + 2 ATP + 2 NADH. 10 enzyme steps. Two phases: energy investment (uses 2 ATP, steps 1-5), energy payoff (makes 4 ATP + 2 NADH, steps 6-10). Key regulatory enzymes: hexokinase/glucokinase (step 1), phosphofructokinase-1/PFK-1 (step 3 — rate-limiting, activated by AMP/fructose-2,6-bisP, inhibited by ATP/citrate), pyruvate kinase (step 10)
- **Anaerobic fate of pyruvate**: lactate (lactic acid fermentation — in low O₂, regenerates NAD⁺ to keep glycolysis running. Cori cycle: lactate → liver → gluconeogenesis). Ethanol (yeast fermentation — pyruvate → acetaldehyde → ethanol + CO₂)
- **Aerobic fate of pyruvate**: enters mitochondria → pyruvate dehydrogenase (PDH complex — 3 enzymes, 5 cofactors) → acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH. PDH inhibited by ATP/NADH/acetyl-CoA, activated by pyruvate

### TCA Cycle (Krebs / Citric Acid Cycle)
- **Location**: mitochondrial matrix. Each turn: acetyl-CoA (2C) + oxaloacetate (4C) → citrate → → oxaloacetate (regenerated) + 3 NADH + 1 FADH₂ + 1 GTP + 2 CO₂. 8 reactions
- **Key enzymes**: citrate synthase (oxaloacetate + acetyl-CoA → citrate — regulated by ATP, NADH), isocitrate dehydrogenase (produces α-ketoglutarate, regulated by ATP/NADH), α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase (similar to PDH complex, regulated similarly)
- **Anaplerotic reactions**: replenish TCA intermediates (e.g., pyruvate carboxylase: pyruvate → oxaloacetate, important when intermediates drawn off for biosynthesis)

### Oxidative Phosphorylation
- **Location**: inner mitochondrial membrane. Electron transport chain (ETC): Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase — NADH → Q), Complex II (succinate dehydrogenase — FADH₂ → Q — also TCA enzyme), Complex III (cytochrome bc1 — Q → cytochrome c), Complex IV (cytochrome c oxidase — cytochrome c → O₂ → H₂O). Electrons flow downhill in redox potential — energy pumps protons (H⁺) across inner membrane
- **Proton gradient**: higher H⁺ in intermembrane space, lower in matrix. Creates electrochemical gradient (proton motive force). ATP synthase (Complex V): F₀ (proton channel) + F₁ (catalytic core). Protons flow through → drives rotor → conformational changes synthesize ATP. ~3 H⁺ per ATP. Chemiosmotic theory (Peter Mitchell, Nobel 1978)
- **NADH vs FADH₂**: NADH enters at Complex I → 10 H⁺ pumped → ~2.5 ATP. FADH₂ enters at Complex II → 6 H⁺ pumped → ~1.5 ATP. Total per glucose: ~30-32 ATP (variable, not 36-38 as older textbooks)
- **Uncoupling**: certain molecules (thermogenin/UCP1 in brown fat) allow H⁺ to leak back without making ATP → heat (non-shivering thermogenesis). DNP (dinitrophenol) uncouples — dangerous weight loss drug, causes hyperthermia

### Gluconeogenesis
- **Location**: mainly liver, some kidney cortex. Makes glucose from non-carb precursors (lactate, amino acids, glycerol). Basically reverse glycolysis but 3 bypass reactions replace irreversible glycolytic steps. Occurs when glycogen depleted (fasting, starvation, intense exercise). Hormonal regulation: glucagon/cortisol stimulate, insulin inhibits

### Fatty Acid Oxidation (β-oxidation)
- **Location**: mitochondria (short/medium chains) and peroxisomes (very long chain). Process: fatty acid → fatty acyl-CoA → transported into mitochondria via carnitine shuttle (CPT1 rate-limiting). Each cycle: removes 2 carbons as acetyl-CoA + 1 FADH₂ + 1 NADH. Palmitate (C16) yields 8 acetyl-CoA → ~106 ATP. Ketone bodies (acetoacetate, β-hydroxybutyrate) made in liver from excess acetyl-CoA during starvation — fuel for brain when glucose scarce

## Protein Structure
- **Primary**: linear sequence of amino acids (genetically encoded). Determines all higher structure. Mutations change primary structure → may alter function (sickle cell: Glu→Val in β-globin)
- **Secondary**: local folding patterns. α-helix: right-handed spiral, 3.6 residues/turn, H-bonds between NH of n and CO of n+4. β-sheet: parallel (strands same direction) or antiparallel (opposite), H-bonds between strands. β-turns: 180° direction change (4 residues, proline/glycine common). Loops/motifs: irregular but functional (helix-turn-helix for DNA binding, zinc finger, leucine zipper)
- **Tertiary**: 3D folding of single polypeptide. Stabilized by: hydrophobic interactions (core), H-bonds, ionic bonds, disulfide bridges (Cys-Cys), van der Waals. Domains: independently folding structural units (~50-300 AA). Protein folding: Anfinsen's dogma — sequence dictates fold. Chaperones (Hsp70, Hsp60/GroEL) help folding, prevent aggregation. Misfolding → disease (Alzheimer's Aβ plaques, Parkinson's α-synuclein, prion diseases, amyloidosis)
- **Quaternary**: multiple polypeptide chains (subunits). Hemoglobin: α₂β₂ tetramer, 4 heme groups, cooperative O₂ binding. Antibodies: heavy + light chains. Multienzyme complexes: pyruvate dehydrogenase (3 enzymes, 60 subunits). Protein-protein interactions: interfaces, allostery

## Central Dogma of Molecular Biology

### DNA Replication
- **Semiconservative**: each parent strand serves as template for new daughter. Meselson-Stahl experiment (1958) proved it (¹⁵N labeling, CsCl gradient)
- **Proteins involved**: helicase (unwinds), topoisomerase (relieves supercoiling — topotecan targets cancer), single-strand binding proteins (stabilize), primase (RNA primer), DNA polymerase III (E. coli — main replicase, 5'→3', proofreads 3'→5' exonuclease), DNA polymerase I (removes RNA primers, fills gaps), ligase (seals nicks). Leading strand: continuous. Lagging strand: Okazaki fragments (~1000 bp) — multiple primers
- **Eukaryotic replication**: multiple origins (ARS sequences). Replication forks. Polymerases: α (primase), δ (lagging), ε (leading). Telomeres: repetitive TTAGGG — protect chromosome ends. Telomerase adds repeats (active in germ/stem/cancer cells, not most somatic cells — limits Hayflick ~50 divisions)
- **Proofreading**: 3'→5' exonuclease corrects mismatches. Error rate: 10⁻⁷ before repair → 10⁻⁹ after mismatch repair (MutS, MutL, MutH in E. coli)

### Transcription
- **RNA synthesis**: DNA-dependent RNA polymerase. 5'→3', no primer needed. Template strand (read 3'→5'), coding strand (same sequence as RNA, T→U). Prokaryotes: single RNA polymerase (σ factor for promoter recognition). Eukaryotes: RNA pol I (rRNA), II (mRNA), III (tRNA)
- **Promoter & regulation**: TATA box (-30 bp in eukaryotes — TFIID binds). Enhancers (increase transcription, can be far away — DNA looping brings to promoter), silencers (decrease). Transcription factors bind regulatory sequences. RNA pol II requires general TFs (TFIIA, B, D, E, F, H) to initiate. Mediator complex connects regulatory TFs to Pol II
- **Processing (eukaryotic mRNA)**: 5' cap (7-methylguanosine — protects, helps export/translation). 3' poly-A tail (~200 As — stability, export). Splicing: introns removed, exons joined. Spliceosome (snRNPs U1, U2, U4, U5, U6). Alternative splicing: one gene → multiple proteins (95% of human genes alternatively spliced). Enhancer sequences within exons/introns regulate splice site choice

### Translation
- **Ribosome**: rRNA + proteins. Prokaryotic: 70S (50S + 30S). Eukaryotic: 80S (60S + 40S). Three binding sites: A (aminoacyl-tRNA enters), P (peptidyl-tRNA — holds growing chain), E (exit — deacylated tRNA leaves)
- **Steps**: (1) Initiation: small subunit + mRNA + initiator tRNA (Met-tRNA/Met-tRNAi) + initiation factors → start codon (AUG) in P site → large subunit joins. Kozak sequence in eukaryotes (ACCAUGG). Shine-Dalgarno in prokaryotes (AGGAGGT — on mRNA, base pairs with 16S rRNA). (2) Elongation: aminoacyl-tRNA enters A site → peptide bond formed (peptidyl transferase — rRNA catalytic, ribozyme) → translocation (ribosome moves 3') → repeat (EF-Tu, EF-G in bacteria). (3) Termination: stop codon (UAA, UAG, UGA) → release factor → polypeptide released. Polyribosome: multiple ribosomes on single mRNA
- **Genetic code**: 64 codons, 61 specify amino acids, 3 stop. Degenerate (multiple codons per AA) — wobble in third position. Universal with minor variations (mitochondria, some protists)
- **Post-translational modification**: phosphorylation (Ser, Thr, Tyr), glycosylation (Asn — N-linked, Ser/Thr — O-linked), acetylation, ubiquitination (marks for proteasome degradation), lipidation (GPI anchors, myristoylation, prenylation — membrane attachment), proteolytic cleavage (prohormones, zymogen activation), disulfide bond formation

## Signaling Pathways
- **Receptors**: G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs — 7 transmembrane, largest family. Gα, Gβγ subunits. Adenylyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA. Phospholipase C → IP₃ + DAG → Ca²⁺ + PKC). Receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs — ligand binds → dimerization → autophosphorylation → SH2 domain proteins bind → Ras/MAPK pathway, PI3K/Akt pathway). Ion channel receptors (nicotinic acetylcholine receptor — rapid synaptic transmission). Nuclear receptors (steroid/thyroid hormone — ligand enters cell, binds receptor in nucleus, regulates transcription)
- **Second messengers**: cAMP (synthesized by adenylyl cyclase, degraded by PDE — caffeine inhibits PDE), IP₃ (releases Ca²⁺ from ER), DAG (activates PKC), Ca²⁺ (universal — calmodulin, CaMK), cGMP (vision, vasodilation — NO→cGMP, Viagra inhibits PDE5)
- **Common pathways**: MAPK/ERK (growth factors → Ras → Raf → MEK → ERK → transcription → cell proliferation. Mutated in many cancers — BRAF V600E in melanoma). PI3K/Akt/mTOR (growth/survival, insulin signaling — mTOR inhibited by rapamycin). JAK/STAT (cytokines, interferons). Wnt/β-catenin (development, stem cells). Notch (cell fate, lateral inhibition). Hedgehog (patterning, growth — cyclopamine inhibits). TGF-β/Smad (growth inhibition, fibrosis)
- **Signaling concepts**: amplification (one receptor activates many G proteins, each activates enzyme producing many second messengers). Specificity (scaffold proteins, compartmentalization). Desensitization (receptor internalization, degradation, dephosphorylation). Crosstalk (pathways interact, converge, diverge). Signal termination (phosphatases, PDEs, GTPase, receptor downregulation). Aberrant signaling = hallmark of cancer, diabetes, autoimmune diseases
