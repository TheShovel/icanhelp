# Psychology & How the Mind Works

## Major Perspectives
- **Behaviorism**: behavior is learned through conditioning (Pavlov's dogs, Skinner box). Focus on observable behavior, not internal mental states
- **Cognitive**: mental processes (thinking, memory, perception, problem-solving). The brain as information processor
- **Psychodynamic**: unconscious drives, childhood experiences shape personality (Freud, Jung, Adler)
- **Humanistic**: inherent drive toward growth, self-actualization (Maslow's hierarchy, Rogers' person-centered therapy)
- **Biological**: brain structure, neurotransmitters, genetics, hormones drive behavior and mental processes
- **Evolutionary**: behaviors evolved because they helped our ancestors survive and reproduce
- **Sociocultural**: how culture, society, and social groups shape behavior and cognition

## The Brain
- **Forebrain**: cerebrum (thinking, language, voluntary movement), thalamus (sensory relay), hypothalamus (hunger, thirst, temperature, hormones), limbic system (emotion, memory)
- **Midbrain**: visual/auditory reflexes, movement coordination
- **Hindbrain**: brainstem (life support — breathing, heart rate), cerebellum (balance, coordination, fine motor)
- **Four lobes**: frontal (decision-making, personality, movement), parietal (sensation, spatial), temporal (hearing, language, memory), occipital (vision)
- **Plasticity**: brain can rewire itself throughout life — neurons that fire together wire together
  - After injury: other parts can take over functions (more in younger brains)
  - Learning: creates new neural connections, strengthens existing ones
- **Lateralization**: left hemisphere (language, logic, detail), right hemisphere (holistic, visual-spatial, emotion, music)
  - "Left-brain vs right-brain" is oversimplified — most functions use both hemispheres
- **Neurotransmitters**: chemical messengers
  - Dopamine: reward, motivation, movement, pleasure (dysregulated in addiction, Parkinson's, schizophrenia)
  - Serotonin: mood, sleep, appetite, social behavior (low in depression, treated by SSRIs)
  - Norepinephrine: alertness, arousal, fight-or-flight (ADHD, depression)
  - GABA: inhibition, calming (low in anxiety — benzodiazepines boost GABA)
  - Glutamate: excitation, learning, memory (excess = excitotoxicity in stroke, ALS)
  - Acetylcholine: memory, muscle activation (low in Alzheimer's)
  - Endorphins: pain relief, pleasure ("runner's high")

## Memory
- **Sensory memory**: brief (<1 sec) — what you see, hear, feel in raw form. Most is forgotten immediately
- **Short-term / working memory**: 20-30 seconds, 7±2 items (The Magical Number Seven, Miller)
  - Chunking increases capacity: 555-123-4567 is easier than 5551234567
- **Long-term memory**: unlimited capacity, permanent
  - Explicit (declarative): facts, events — hippocampus processes, cortex stores
    - Episodic: personal experiences (your first day of school)
    - Semantic: general knowledge (Paris is capital of France)
  - Implicit (procedural): skills, habits — cerebellum + basal ganglia (riding a bike, typing)
- **Forgetting curve** (Ebbinghaus): we forget ~50% in an hour, ~70% in 24 hours, ~90% in a week
  - Spaced repetition (review at increasing intervals) dramatically improves retention
- **False memories**: memory is reconstructive, not replay — every recall changes it slightly
  - Leading questions, suggestion, imagination can create vivid false memories (Loftus research)
  - Eye-witness testimony is notoriously unreliable — different people see same event differently
- **Sleep and memory**: sleep consolidates memories — deep sleep (facts), REM (skills + emotional processing)

## Learning
- **Classical conditioning** (Pavlov): neutral stimulus + unconditioned stimulus → conditioned response
  - Bell + food → salivation → bell alone → salivation
  - Phobias can form this way (dog bite → fear of all dogs)
- **Operant conditioning** (Skinner): behavior → consequence → behavior change
  - Positive reinforcement: add something good (praise → child cleans room more)
  - Negative reinforcement: remove something bad (stop alarm by getting up — get up more)
  - Punishment: add bad or remove good (yelling → may suppress behavior but causes resentment)
  - Reinforcement > punishment for lasting behavior change
- **Observational learning** (Bandura): we learn by watching others (modeling)
  - Bobo doll experiment: children watched adult hit doll → children imitated
  - This is why role models matter — children learn more from what you DO than what you SAY
- **Growth mindset** (Carol Dweck): belief that ability can be developed
  - Fixed mindset: "I'm just not good at math" → avoids challenge, gives up easily
  - Growth mindset: "I can get better at math with practice" → embraces challenge, persists
  - Praise effort ("you worked hard") not fixed traits ("you're so smart")

## Motivation
- **Intrinsic**: doing something because it's inherently interesting or satisfying (curiosity, mastery, autonomy, purpose)
  - More sustainable, leads to deeper learning and creativity
- **Extrinsic**: doing something for external reward or to avoid punishment (money, grades, praise, pressure)
  - Can undermine intrinsic motivation (overjustification effect: pay someone to do what they love → they love it less)
- **Self-determination theory** (Deci & Ryan): humans need autonomy (control over choices), competence (feeling capable), relatedness (connection with others) for intrinsic motivation
- **Maslow's hierarchy**: physiological → safety → belonging → esteem → self-actualization
  - Lower needs must be reasonably met before higher needs motivate
  - Not strictly linear — people can be motivated by multiple levels
- **Expectancy-value theory**: motivation = expectancy of success × value of success
  - If you don't think you can do it OR if you don't care → no motivation
- **Grit** (Angela Duckworth): passion + perseverance for long-term goals — predicts success beyond talent and IQ

## Personality
- **Big Five** (OCEAN): Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism
  - Conscientiousness is the best predictor of life outcomes (health, wealth, longevity)
  - Personality is moderately stable but changes across lifespan (more conscientious with age, less neurotic)
- **Temperament** (Kagan): inborn personality style — easy, difficult, slow-to-warm-up
  - Highly reactive infants → more cautious, anxious children → can change with supportive parenting
- **Nature vs nurture**: both — personality is 40-60% heritable, rest environment (shared environment surprisingly small effect, non-shared + peer + random factors larger)

## Mental Health Treatment
- **CBT** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy): most evidence-based — identify distorted thoughts → challenge them → change behavior
  - Effective for anxiety, depression, OCD, PTSD, eating disorders — typically 12-20 sessions
- **ACT** (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy): accept difficult thoughts/feelings, commit to values-based action
  - "Don't stop the waves, learn to surf" — useful for chronic pain, anxiety, depression
- **DBT** (Dialectical Behavior Therapy): combines CBT with mindfulness — emotion regulation, distress tolerance, interpersonal skills
  - Originally for borderline personality disorder, now used for many conditions
- **Medication**: SSRIs (depression, anxiety — takes 4-6 weeks), SNRIs (depression, pain), stimulants (ADHD), antipsychotics (schizophrenia, bipolar), mood stabilizers (bipolar)
  - Medication + therapy = better than either alone for most conditions
  - Finding the right medication often takes trial — don't give up if first doesn't work
- **EMDR**: eye movement desensitization and reprocessing — for PTSD, involves bilateral stimulation while recalling trauma
- **Psychodynamic**: insight from unconscious patterns, past relationships — longer-term, less evidence than CBT but helpful for some

## Social Psychology
- **Milgram experiment**: 65% of people administered (what they thought were) dangerous electric shocks to a stranger because an authority figure told them to
- **Stanford prison experiment**: college students became abusive guards / passive prisoners in simulation — power dynamics shape behavior dramatically (ethics of this study are now condemned)
- **Bystander effect**: more people present = less likely anyone helps (diffusion of responsibility)
  - If you need help: single someone out ("YOU in the red shirt, call 911")
- **Conformity** (Asch): people give obviously wrong answer to match group — social pressure is powerful
- **Cognitive dissonance**: discomfort when beliefs and actions conflict — we change beliefs to match actions
  - "I smoked even though I know it's bad → smoking can't be that bad" (rationalization)
  - Foot-in-the-door: get someone to agree to small thing → they're more likely to agree to bigger (self-perception changes)
- **Fundamental attribution error**: we attribute others' behavior to personality, our own to circumstances
  - They're late because they're rude. I'm late because of traffic.
- **Halo effect**: one positive trait (attractiveness, expertise) colors everything (beautiful people assumed smarter, kinder)
- **Stereotype threat**: fear of confirming a negative stereotype about your group → anxiety → worse performance
