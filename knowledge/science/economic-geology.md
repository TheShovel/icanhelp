# Geology & Natural Resources

## Ore Deposit Types

### Magmatic
- **Chromite/PGE** (Bushveld South Africa, Stillwater Montana, Great Dyke Zimbabwe): Layered mafic intrusions, crystallized from magma. Chromite + platinum group elements (Pt, Pd, Rh, Os, Ir, Ru). Bushveld: world's largest PGE + chromite + vanadium resource
- **Nickel-copper sulfides** (Sudbury Canada, Norilsk Russia, Kambalda Australia): Associated with komatiitic/basaltic magma, sulfide liquid separates from silicate melt at high temperature → nickel-copper-iron sulfides form. Sudbury: caused by meteorite impact 1.8 bya, immense Ni-Cu-PGE deposit. Norilsk: world's largest nickel deposit — Siberian Traps flood basalt volcanism

### Hydrothermal
- **Porphyry copper** (Chile/Andes: Chuquicamata, Escondida; SW US: Morenci, Bingham): Low grade (0.3-1.5% Cu) + millions of tons. Copper ± molybdenum ± gold. Associated with subduction zone magmas, intrusive stocks, extensive hydrothermal brecciation + stockwork quartz veinlets + alteration halos (potassic, phyllic, argillic, propylitic). Largest source of copper + molybdenum globally
- **Epithermal gold** (Carlin trend Nevada, Ladolam PNG, Hishikari Japan): Shallow <1km, boiling of hydrothermal fluids produces gold deposition. Low sulfidation (quartz ± adularia, galena, sphalerite (often associated with silver) and high sulfidation (enargite, pyrite, gold, tellurides). Nevada: Carlin-type — disseminated gold in silty/limestone host rock, invisible gold, best in decarbonized/oxidized soluble pyrite-bearing rock, heat + CO₂ release → gold precipitation from dissolved gold-bisulfide complexes
- **Volcanogenic massive sulfide (VMS)** (Kuroko Japan, Flin Flon Canada, Rio Tinto Spain): Submarine volcanic exhalative (black smokers). Zinc + copper + lead + silver + gold (± barite). Massive sulfide lens (pyrite, chalcopyrite, sphalerite, galena). Found along mid-ocean ridges, back-arc basins, island arcs

### Sedimentary
- **Banded iron formations (BIFs)**: 2.5-1.8 bya (Proterozoic), layered silica + iron oxides (hematite, magnetite). Largest source of iron ore (Australia, Brazil, India). Precipitated when photosynthetic cyanobacteria released oxygen → combined with dissolved Fe²⁺ in ocean → Fe³⁺ oxides precipitated. Iron deposits peak before the oxygen atmosphere rose further
- **Mississippi Valley-Type (MVT) lead-zinc**: Carbonate-hosted, galena + sphalerite, low temperature. Southeast Missouri, Pine Point Canada, Viburnum Trend
- **Uranium**: sandstone-hosted (roll-front): uranium dissolved in oxidized groundwater, precipitates where redox front meets organic-rich rock. Wyoming/Nebraska/ Texas, Central Asia (Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan). In-situ recovery (ISL): pump oxygenated water into the ground, uranium dissolves, pumped to surface, processed. In-situ uranium mining: 45% of world supply

### Placer
- **Gold placers**: pan for gold in stream/river gravels (gold density ~19 g/cc, so accumulates in high energy traps: behind boulders, in crevices, on bedrock). Placer gold: Alaska, California (1849 Gold Rush), Klondike, Russia. Dredge: large-scale suction dredge diverts river. Suction dredging still controversial in many rivers for environmental impact (habitat disturbance, mercury residues from historic operations)
- **Heavy mineral sands**: titanium (rutile, ilmenite), zircon, monazite (thorium, REE), tin (cassiterite). Coastal placer deposits in Australia, India, South Africa, Florida

## Exploration Methods
- **Geophysical**: gravity (density contrast). Magnetics (iron content, magnetite: measure Earth's magnetic field variations to map rocks). Electromagnetic (conductivity — sulfide detection, massive sulfides have high conductivity. Airborne EM: helicopter or fixed-wing survey). Seismic (sound waves reflect off rock layers — best for sedimentary basins, oil/gas). Radiometrics (uranium, thorium, potassium: measure gamma radiation from near-surface)
- **Geochemical**: soil/stream sediment/rock sampling — measure trace element concentrations. Anomaly (values above background = target). Biogeochemistry: analyze plants (some accumulate metals — e.g., conifer needles for Cu). Pathfinder elements: elements that help locate deposits (e.g., As+Sb+Hg for Carlin gold)
- **Drilling**: diamond core drilling (core sample: rock cylinder, best for hard rock, expensive $50-200/m). Reverse circulation (RC) drilling: crushed rock sample, cheaper $10-40/m. Air rotary, auger for shallow overburden
- **Resource/reserve classification**: Inferred (geological evidence, not confirmed). Indicated (sampling at close spacing, reasonable continuity). Measured (detailed sampling, high confidence). Proven/Probable Reserves = economically mineable, technically feasible, legal, socially acceptable
