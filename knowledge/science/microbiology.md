# Microbiology & Infectious Disease

## Overview
Microbiology studies microorganisms (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites, prions) and their roles in health, disease, ecology, and biotechnology.

## Bacteria

### Classification
| Characteristic | Gram-Positive | Gram-Negative |
|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| Cell Wall | Thick peptidoglycan | Thin peptidoglycan + outer membrane |
| Gram Stain | Purple | Pink/Red |
| Endotoxin | No | Yes (LPS) |
| Exotoxins | Common | Less common |
| Antibiotic Target | Penicillin-binding proteins | Harder to penetrate |
| Examples | Staph, Strep, Clostridium | E. coli, Pseudomonas, Salmonella |

| Category | Oxygen | Examples |

### Key Pathogens

#### Staphylococcus aureus
- Gram-positive cocci in clusters
- Catalase +, Coagulase +
- Skin/soft tissue, pneumonia, endocarditis, toxic shock
- **MRSA**: Resistant to β-lactams (mecA gene)
- Treatment: Vancomycin, Linezolid, Daptomycin, Ceftaroline

#### Streptococcus pyogenes (Group A Strep)
- Gram-positive cocci in chains
- Catalase -, β-hemolytic
- Pharyngitis, impetigo, cellulitis, necrotizing fasciitis, rheumatic fever
- Treatment: Penicillin (drug of choice)

#### Streptococcus pneumoniae
- Gram-positive diplococci, α-hemolytic
- Optochin sensitive, bile soluble
- Pneumonia, meningitis, otitis media
- Vaccine: PCV13, PPSV23

#### Escherichia coli
- Gram-negative rod, lactose fermenter
- UTI (most common), sepsis, meningitis (neonates), diarrhea (EHEC O157:H7)
- ESBL producers: Resistant to 3rd gen cephalosporins

#### Pseudomonas aeruginosa
- Gram-negative rod, non-lactose fermenter, oxidase +
- Green pigment (pyocyanin), grape odor
- Hospital-acquired: pneumonia, burn wounds, UTI
- Intrinsically resistant (efflux pumps, low permeability)
- Treatment: Pip-tazo, Cefepime, Meropenem, Ciprofloxacin

#### Clostridioides difficile
- Gram-positive, spore-forming, anaerobic
- Antibiotic-associated diarrhea, pseudomembranous colitis
- Toxins A & B
- Treatment: Vancomycin (oral), Fidaxomicin; recurrence → Bezlotoxumab

#### Mycobacterium tuberculosis
- Acid-fast bacillus (Ziehl-Neelsen stain)
- Slow growth (weeks), cord factor
- Primary TB → Latent → Reactivation
- Treatment: RIPE (Rifampin, Isoniazid, Pyrazinamide, Ethambutol) × 2mo, then RI × 4mo
- MDR-TB: Resistant to R + I; XDR-TB: + fluoroquinolone + injectable

### Antibiotic Classes

| Class | Mechanism | Spectrum | Key Drugs | Resistance |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Penicillins** | Cell wall (PBPs) | G+ > G- | Penicillin G, Amoxicillin, Ampicillin, Pip-tazo | β-lactamase |
| **Cephalosporins** | Cell wall | Generations: 1st G+ > 4th broad | Cefazolin, Ceftriaxone, Cefepime | ESBL, AmpC |
| **Carbapenems** | Cell wall | Broadest β-lactam | Meropenem, Imipenem, Ertapenem | Carbapenemase (KPC, NDM) |
| **Fluoroquinolones** | DNA gyrase/topo IV | Broad | Ciprofloxacin, Levofloxacin, Moxifloxacin | gyrA/parC mutations |
| **Aminoglycosides** | 30S ribosome | G- aerobic | Gentamicin, Tobramycin, Amikacin | Modifying enzymes |
| **Glycopeptides** | Cell wall (D-Ala-D-Ala) | G+ only | Vancomycin | VanA (enterococci) |
| **Oxazolidinones** | 50S ribosome | G+ | Linezolid, Tedizolid | rRNA mutation |
| **Lipopeptides** | Membrane depolarization | G+ | Daptomycin | mprF mutation |
| **Macrolides** | 50S ribosome | Atypicals, G+ | Azithromycin, Clarithromycin | erm methylation |
| **Tetracyclines** | 30S ribosome | Broad | Doxycycline, Minocycline, Tigecycline | Efflux, ribosomal protection |
| **Sulfonamides** | Folate synthesis | Broad | TMP-SMX (Bactrim) | Dihydropteroate synthase |
| **Metronidazole** | DNA damage (anaerobes) | Anaerobes, protozoa | Metronidazole | Nitroreductase loss |

### Resistance Mechanisms
1. **Enzymatic inactivation**: β-lactamases, aminoglycoside-modifying enzymes
2. **Target modification**: PBP2a (MRSA), gyrA (fluoroquinolones), 23S rRNA (macrolides)
3. **Efflux pumps**: Tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones, macrolides
4. **Decreased permeability**: Porin loss (carbapenems in G-)
5. **Bypass pathways**: Alternative enzymes

## Viruses

### Structure
| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Capsid** | Protein shell (icosahedral, helical, complex) |
| **Genome** | DNA or RNA (ss/ds, linear/segmented) |
| **Envelope** | Lipid bilayer from host (glycoproteins) |
| **Enzymes** | Polymerase, reverse transcriptase, integrase |

### Classification (Baltimore)
| Group | Genome | Replication | Examples |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------|
| I | dsDNA | Nuclear | Herpes, Adeno, Pox, Papilloma |
| II | ssDNA | Nuclear | Parvovirus |
| III | dsRNA | Cytoplasmic | Reovirus, Rotavirus |
| IV | (+)ssRNA | Cytoplasmic | Corona, Picorna, Flavi, Hepatitis A/C/E |
| V | (-)ssRNA | Cytoplasmic | Influenza, Paramyxo, Rhabdo, Filo |
| VI | ssRNA-RT | Nuclear (DNA intermediate) | Retroviruses (HIV, HTLV) |
| VII | dsDNA-RT | Nuclear (RNA intermediate) | Hepatitis B |

### Key Viruses

#### Influenza
- (-)ssRNA, segmented (8), enveloped
- HA (hemagglutinin) & NA (neuraminidase) subtypes
- Antigenic drift (point mutations) vs shift (reassortment)
- Treatment: Oseltamivir (neuraminidase inhibitor), Baloxavir (cap-dependent endonuclease inhibitor)
- Vaccine: Annual quadrivalent (IIV, LAIV)

#### SARS-CoV-2 (COVID-19)
- (+)ssRNA, Coronaviridae
- Spike protein binds ACE2
- Variants: Alpha, Delta, Omicron sublineages
- Treatment: Nirmatrelvir/ritonavir (Paxlovid), Remdesivir, Molnupiravir
- Vaccine: mRNA (Pfizer, Moderna), Protein subunit (Novavax)

#### HIV
- Retrovirus (Group VI), (+)ssRNA → DNA → integrates
- Targets CD4+ T cells
- Stages: Acute → Clinical latency → AIDS (CD4 <200 or OI)
- Treatment: ART (2 NRTI + INSTI/NNRTI/PI)
  - First-line: BIC/TAF/FTC, DTG/ABC/3TC, DTG + TDF/3TC
- PrEP: TDF/FTC or TAF/FTC daily; Cabotegravir LA injectable
- PEP: 3-drug × 28 days (start ≤72 hrs)

#### Hepatitis Viruses
| Virus | Type | Transmission | Chronic? | Vaccine | Treatment |
|-------|------|--------------|----------|---------|-----------|
| HAV | Picorna | Fecal-oral | No | Yes | Supportive |
| HBV | Hepadna | Blood, sexual, perinatal | Yes (5-10% adult) | Yes | Entecavir, Tenofovir |
| HCV | Flavi | Blood | Yes (75-85%) | No | DAAs (sofosbuvir/velpatasvir) - cure >95% |
| HDV | Defective | Requires HBV | Yes | HBV vaccine prevents | Bulevirtide |
| HEV | Hepe | Fecal-oral (zoonotic) | Rare | No (China) | Ribavirin |

| HSV-2 | Genital herpes | Sacral ganglion | Genital lesions | Acyclovir, Valacyclovir |
| VZV | Chickenpox, Shingles | Dorsal root ganglion | Shingles (dermatomal) | Acyclovir, Valacyclovir |
| CMV | Mononucleosis, congenital | Myeloid progenitors | Immunocompromised | Ganciclovir, Valganciclovir |

## Fungi

### Classification
| Type | Morphology | Examples |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Yeasts** | Unicellular, budding | Candida, Cryptococcus |
| **Molds** | Filamentous (hyphae), conidia | Aspergillus, Mucor, Rhizopus |
| **Dimorphic** | Yeast at 37°C, mold at 25°C | Histoplasma, Blastomyces, Coccidioides |
| **Dermatophytes** | Keratinophilic | Trichophyton, Microsporum, Epidermophyton |

### Key Pathogens

#### Candida albicans
- Commensal → opportunistic (antibiotics, immunocompromise)
- Thrush, vaginal yeast, invasive candidiasis
- Treatment: Fluconazole (non-invasive), Echinocandins (invasive), Amphotericin B (refractory)

#### Aspergillus fumigatus
- Ubiquitous mold, conidia inhaled
- Allergic bronchopulmonary aspergillosis (ABPA), aspergilloma, invasive aspergillosis
- Galactomannan antigen test
- Treatment: Voriconazole, Isavuconazole

#### Pneumocystis jirovecii
- Fungus (formerly protozoan)
- PCP in HIV (CD4 <200), immunocompromised
- Prophylaxis: TMP-SMX
- Treatment: TMP-SMX (high dose), Pentamidine, Atovaquone

#### Dimorphic Fungi (Endemic Mycoses)
| Fungus | Endemic Area | Disease | Treatment |
|--------|--------------|---------|-----------|
| Histoplasma capsulatum | Ohio/Mississippi River valleys | Pulmonary, disseminated | Itraconazole, Amphotericin B |
| Blastomyces dermatitidis | Great Lakes, SE US | Pulmonary, cutaneous | Itraconazole, Amphotericin B |
| Coccidioides immitis | SW US (Valley Fever) | Pulmonary, meningitis | Fluconazole, Amphotericin B |
| Paracoccidioides brasiliensis | Latin America | Mucocutaneous, pulmonary | Itraconazole, Amphotericin B |
| Sporothrix schenckii | Worldwide (soil/plants) | Lymphocutaneous | Itraconazole |

### Antifungals
| Class | Mechanism | Spectrum | Key Drugs |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **Polyenes** | Bind ergosterol → pores | Broad (fungicidal) | Amphotericin B, Liposomal AmB |
| **Azoles** | Inhibit ergosterol synthesis | Broad (fungistatic) | Fluconazole, Itraconazole, Voriconazole, Posaconazole, Isavuconazole |
| **Echinocandins** | Inhibit β-glucan synthase | Candida, Aspergillus | Caspofungin, Micafungin, Anidulafungin |
| **Flucytosine** | Inhibit DNA/RNA synthesis | Narrow (synergy) | Flucytosine (5-FC) |
| **Allylamines** | Inhibit squalene epoxidase | Dermatophytes | Terbinafine |

## Diagnostic Methods

### Microscopy
- **Gram stain**: Bacteria (30 min)
- **Acid-fast (ZN/Kinyoun)**: Mycobacteria, Nocardia
- **India ink**: Cryptococcus capsule
- **KOH prep**: Fungal hyphae (skin, nail)
- **Wet mount**: Trichomonas, clue cells, yeast
- **Blood smear**: Malaria, Babesia, Trypanosoma

### Culture
- **Blood culture**: Aerobic/anaerobic bottles (BACTEC)
- **Urine culture**: >10⁵ CFU/mL = significant
- **Sputum culture**: Gram stain first (Q >25 PMN, <10 squamous)
- **Stool culture**: Salmonella, Shigella, Campylobacter, Yersinia
- **Throat culture**: Group A Strep
- **Anaerobic culture**: Prereduced media, gas pak

### Molecular (PCR/NAAT)
- **Multiplex panels**: Respiratory (BioFire), GI (FilmArray), Meningitis, Bloodstream
- **Single-target**: C. difficile, MRSA, GBS, TB (Xpert MTB/RIF)
- **Quantitative**: HIV RNA, HCV RNA, HBV DNA, CMV, EBV

### Serology
- **Antibody**: Past/exposure (IgM = acute, IgG = past/immune)
- **Antigen**: Current infection (HBsAg, HIV p24, Crypto, Legionella urine)
- **Western blot**: Confirmatory (HIV, Lyme)

### Susceptibility Testing
- **Disk diffusion (Kirby-Bauer)**: Zone diameters → S/I/R
- **MIC (Broth microdilution)**: Exact concentration (µg/mL)
- **E-test**: Gradient strip (MIC)
- **Automated**: VITEK, Phoenix, MicroScan

## Infection Control

### Standard Precautions
- Hand hygiene (ABHR preferred, soap if C. diff/Norovirus)
- PPE (gloves, gown, mask, eye protection)
- Respiratory hygiene
- Safe injection practices
- Sharps safety

### Transmission-Based Precautions
| Type | Pathogens | PPE | Room |
|------|-----------|-----|------|
| **Contact** | MRSA, VRE, C. diff, Norovirus, RSV | Gown + gloves | Private preferred |
| **Droplet** | Influenza, Pertussis, Neisseria, SARS-CoV-2 | Surgical mask | Private |
| **Airborne** | TB, Measles, Varicella, SARS-CoV-2 (aerosol) | N95/PAPR | Negative pressure (AIIR) |

### Sterilization/Disinfection
| Level | Kills | Methods | Use |
|-------|-------|---------|-----|
| **Sterilization** | All (including spores) | Steam (121°C), ETO, H₂O₂ plasma, Radiation | Critical (surgical) |
| **High-level Disinfection** | All except spores | Glutaraldehyde, OPA, Peracetic acid, H₂O₂ | Semi-critical (endoscopes) |
| **Intermediate-level** | Mycobacteria, viruses, fungi | Bleach, Alcohol, Phenolics | Non-critical (surfaces) |
| **Low-level** | Most bacteria, some viruses | Quats, Phenolics | Non-critical (floors) |
