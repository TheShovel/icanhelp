# Microbiology

Bacteria, viruses, fungi, and other microorganisms — structure, function, and impact.

## Bacteria
- **Prokaryotes**: No nucleus or membrane-bound organelles. Single circular chromosome (nucleoid). Plasmids (small circular DNA — horizontal gene transfer). Cell wall (peptidoglycan — unique to bacteria, target of antibiotics like penicillin)
- **Shapes**: Cocci (spherical — strep, staph), Bacilli (rod — E. coli, salmonella), Spirilla (spiral — Treponema, Borrelia), Vibrio (comma — V. cholerae), Spirochetes (corkscrew)
- **Gram stain**: Gram-positive (thick peptidoglycan — purple, retain crystal violet. Staphylococcus, Streptococcus, Bacillus, Clostridium). Gram-negative (thin peptidoglycan + outer membrane — pink, lose crystal violet. E. coli, Pseudomonas, Salmonella, Neisseria). Gram-negative outer membrane contains endotoxin (LPS — lipopolysaccharide)
- **Metabolism**: Aerobic (require O2), Anaerobic (cannot tolerate O2 — Clostridium, Bacteroides), Facultative (can switch — E. coli, Staphylococcus), Microaerophilic (low O2 — Helicobacter, Campylobacter)
- **Reproduction**: Binary fission (asexual — ~20 min for E. coli under ideal conditions). Generation time varies (20 min to 24+ hours for slow growers like M. tuberculosis)
- **Horizontal gene transfer**: Transformation (uptake free DNA from environment), Transduction (by bacteriophage — bacterial virus), Conjugation (direct cell-cell via pilus — transfers plasmids). Allows rapid spread of antibiotic resistance

## Viruses
- **Structure**: Nucleic acid (DNA or RNA, single/double stranded, linear/circular) + capsid (protein coat) ± envelope (lipid membrane from host cell). Virion = complete viral particle
- **Classification**: Baltimore system — Group I: dsDNA (herpes, poxviruses), II: ssDNA (parvovirus), III: dsRNA (rotavirus), IV: +ssRNA (COVID, polio, hep C — can act as mRNA), V: -ssRNA (flu, rabies, Ebola — need RNA-dependent RNA polymerase), VI: ssRNA-RT (HIV — reverse transcriptase makes DNA), VII: dsDNA-RT (Hep B — uses reverse transcription)
- **Replication cycle**: Attachment (bind to host receptor) → Entry (fusion, endocytosis, injection) → Uncoating (release genome) → Replication/translation (hijack host machinery) → Assembly (new virions) → Release (lysis or budding). Lytic cycle (bursts cell) vs lysogenic (integrates into genome, dormant — like HIV, herpes)
- **Host range**: Narrow (cold virus infects only upper respiratory, specific cells) vs broad (rabies infects all mammals — zoonotic)
- **Antiviral drugs**: Target viral-specific processes — polymerase inhibitors (acyclovir for herpes, remdesivir for COVID), protease inhibitors (HIV), neuraminidase inhibitors (Tamiflu for flu), reverse transcriptase inhibitors (HIV), entry/fusion inhibitors. Most antivirals are virostatic (inhibit replication, don't kill virus — immune system clears it)

## Fungi
- **Eukaryotic**: Chitin cell walls (distinct from plants cellulose, bacteria peptidoglycan). No photosynthesis. Reproduce by spores (sexual + asexual)
- **Types**: Molds (multicellular hyphae — mycelium network. Penicillium, Aspergillus), Yeasts (unicellular, budding — Saccharomyces cerevisiae/brewer's yeast, Candida albicans), Dimorphic (grow as mold at 25°C, yeast at 37°C — Histoplasma, Blastomyces, Coccidioides — systemic pathogens)
- **Fungal infections**: Superficial (dermatophytes — ringworm, athlete's foot, jock itch — topical antifungals). Subcutaneous (sporotrichosis — rose gardener's disease). Systemic (histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis — in immunocompromised, can be fatal). Opportunistic (Candida — oral thrush, vaginal yeast; Aspergillus — invasive in neutropenic patients)
- **Antifungals**: Azoles (fluconazole, itraconazole — inhibit ergosterol synthesis, cell membrane), Amphotericin B (binds ergosterol — potent, kidney toxic), Echinocandins (caspofungin — inhibit cell wall synthesis), Terbinafine (inhibit ergosterol synthesis — for dermatophytes). Fungal cells similar to human cells → antifungals more toxic than antibiotics

## Protists (Protozoa)
- **Single-celled eukaryotes**: Amoebas (move by pseudopods — Entamoeba histolytica causes dysentery), Flagellates (Giardia, Trichomonas, Trypanosoma/sleeping sickness, Leishmania), Ciliates (Balantidium coli), Apicomplexans (Plasmodium/malaria, Toxoplasma, Cryptosporidium)
- **Life cycles often complex**: May require multiple hosts (Plasmodium in mosquito + human). Different stages (trophozoite, cyst, gametocyte)
- **Antiprotozoal drugs**: Chloroquine/hydroxychloroquine (malaria — resistance widespread), Artemisinins (artemisinin combination therapy/ACT for malaria), Metronidazole (Giardia, Trichomonas, amoebas), Benznidazole (Chagas)

## Prions
- **Infectious proteins**: Misfolded PrP (prion protein) induces normal PrP to misfold → aggregate → neurodegeneration. No nucleic acid. Extremely resistant to heat, radiation, chemicals (can survive autoclaving)
- **Diseases**: Creutzfeldt-Jakob (humans), Mad Cow/BSE (cattle), Scrapie (sheep), Chronic Wasting Disease (deer/elk), Kuru (human cannibalism — Fore people of Papua New Guinea). All are fatal, no treatment, long incubation (years to decades)
- **Transmission**: Ingestion (contaminated meat), Iatrogenic (contaminated surgical instruments, growth hormone), Genetic (PRNP gene mutation — familial CJD). Sporadic CJD = spontaneous misfolding, cause unknown

## Beneficial Microbes
- **Human microbiome**: 10x more microbial cells than human cells (mostly bacteria in gut). Gut microbiome: digests fiber, produces vitamins (B, K), trains immune system, protects against pathogens, influences mood via gut-brain axis. Firmicutes + Bacteroidetes dominate. Dysbiosis linked to obesity, IBD, allergies, depression
- **Probiotics**: Live beneficial bacteria (Lactobacillus, Bifidobacterium, Saccharomyces boulardii). Modest evidence for: antibiotic-associated diarrhea prevention, IBS symptoms, ulcerative colitis. Strain-specific effects — not all probiotics same
- **Prebiotics**: Fiber that feeds beneficial bacteria (inulin, FOS, GOS — garlic, onion, banana, oats, leeks)
- **Biotechnology**: E. coli for insulin production. Yeast for bread, beer, wine, biofuel. CRISPR-Cas9 from bacteria (adaptive immune system → gene editing tool). Fermentation (yogurt, cheese, kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha)
