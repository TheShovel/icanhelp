# Chemistry Basics

## Atoms & Elements
- Atom: nucleus (protons + neutrons) + orbiting electrons
- Atomic number: number of protons (defines element)
- Mass number: protons + neutrons
- Isotopes: same protons, different neutrons (e.g., C-12, C-13, C-14)
- Electron configuration: electrons fill shells (2,8,8,18,18,32...) — outer shell determines reactivity
- Periodic table: rows = periods (energy levels), columns = groups (same properties, e.g., noble gases column 18)
- Metals (left + center): conductive, malleable, shiny
- Nonmetals (right): brittle, poor conductors
- Metalloids (staircase): intermediate properties (Si, Ge, As)

## Chemical Bonds
- **Ionic**: electron transfer, metal + nonmetal, strong, high melting point (NaCl — salt)
- **Covalent**: electron sharing, nonmetal + nonmetal, can be polar or nonpolar (H₂O, CO₂)
- **Metallic**: delocalized electrons among metal atoms (conductivity, malleability)
- **Hydrogen bond**: attraction between H in one molecule and O/N/F in another (water, DNA)
- **Van der Waals**: weak temporary attractions between all molecules (gecko feet adhesion)

## Chemical Reactions
- **Synthesis**: A + B → AB (2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O)
- **Decomposition**: AB → A + B (2H₂O₂ → 2H₂O + O₂)
- **Single displacement**: AB + C → AC + B (Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂)
- **Double displacement**: AB + CD → AD + CB (AgNO₃ + NaCl → AgCl + NaNO₃)
- **Combustion**: hydrocarbon + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O (burning fuel)
- **Redox**: transfer of electrons (oxidation = lose electrons, reduction = gain)
  - OIL RIG: Oxidation Is Loss, Reduction Is Gain
- Conservation of mass: atoms are rearranged, not created/destroyed

## Acids & Bases
- **Acid**: pH < 7, donates H⁺, sour, corrosive (HCl, H₂SO₄, vinegar, lemon juice)
- **Base**: pH > 7, accepts H⁺, bitter, slippery (NaOH, baking soda, soap)
- pH = -log[H⁺] — logarithmic (pH 3 is 10x more acidic than pH 4)
- Strong acids/bases: fully dissociate (HCl, NaOH)
- Weak acids/bases: partially dissociate (acetic acid, ammonia)
- Neutralization: acid + base → salt + water (HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O)
- Buffer: resists pH change (weak acid + its conjugate base) — blood uses H₂CO₃/HCO₃⁻

## States of Matter
- **Solid**: fixed shape/volume, particles vibrate in place
- **Liquid**: fixed volume, takes container shape, particles slide past
- **Gas**: fills container, particles move freely
- **Plasma**: ionized gas, electrons stripped from atoms (stars, lightning, neon signs)
- Phase changes: melting (s→l), freezing (l→s), evaporation (l→g), condensation (g→l), sublimation (s→g), deposition (g→s)
- Phase diagram: shows solid/liquid/gas regions by temperature + pressure (water: triple point 0.01°C, 611 Pa)
- Boiling point: depends on pressure (water boils at 100°C at sea level, ~90°C at 3000m)

## Organic Chemistry
- **Hydrocarbons**: only H + C
  - Alkanes: single bonds (CₙH₂ₙ₊₂) — methane CH₄, ethane C₂H₆
  - Alkenes: double bonds (CₙH₂ₙ) — ethene C₂H₄
  - Alkynes: triple bonds (CₙH₂ₙ₋₂) — ethyne C₂H₂
- **Functional groups**: replace H with other atoms/groups
  - Alcohol (-OH): ethanol (drinking alcohol), methanol (toxic)
  - Carboxylic acid (-COOH): vinegar, citric acid
  - Aldehyde (-CHO): formaldehyde, vanilla
  - Ketone (C=O): acetone (nail polish remover)
  - Amine (-NH₂): amino acids
  - Ester (-COO-): fruit flavors, fragrances
- **Polymers**: long chains of repeating units — plastics (polyethylene, nylon), proteins, DNA
- **Isomers**: same formula, different structure (C₄H₁₀ = butane vs isobutane)

## Biochemistry
- **Water**: universal solvent, polar, high specific heat, ice floats (less dense as solid)
- **Carbohydrates**: sugars and starches (C:H:O = 1:2:1) — glucose C₆H₁₂O₆
- **Lipids**: fats, oils, phospholipids — cell membranes, energy storage
- **Proteins**: amino acid chains — enzymes, structure, signaling, transport
- **Nucleic acids**: DNA (double helix), RNA — store and transmit genetic info
- **ATP**: adenosine triphosphate = energy currency of cells

## Lab Safety
- Always wear goggles — chemicals in eyes is #1 lab injury
- Read SDS (Safety Data Sheet) before using unknown chemicals
- Work in fume hood for volatile/toxic chemicals
- Never add water to concentrated acid (exothermic, can explode) — add acid to water
- Wash skin contact with water 15 min minimum
- Know location of: fire extinguisher, eye wash station, shower, first aid kit
- Label everything with contents + date + your name
- Don't eat/drink in lab
- Dispose of chemicals properly — never down sink unless specified
