# Climate Science & Meteorology

## Climate vs Weather
- **Weather**: short-term (hours-days). Climate: long-term (30-year averages). "Climate is what you expect; weather is what you get"
- **Climate vs weather attribution**: a single storm isn't "caused" by climate change, but climate change makes extreme events more likely + intense. Event attribution: scientists run climate models (with/without anthropogenic forcing) to estimate how much climate change increased probability of a specific event (e.g., 2017 Hurricane Harvey rainfall: climate change made 3x more likely, 15% more intense). Heatwaves: today's record-breaking heatwaves are 10-50x more likely than 1850 climate

## Greenhouse Effect
- **Mechanism**: solar radiation (shortwave) passes through atmosphere → absorbed by Earth → re-radiated as infrared (longwave) → greenhouse gases (CO₂, H₂O, CH₄, N₂O, O₃) absorb + re-emit some IR → some back to surface → warming
  - Without greenhouse effect: Earth would be ~33°C colder (average -18°C vs actual +15°C)
- **CO₂**: most abundant long-lived GHG from human activity. Atmospheric concentration: 280 ppm (pre-industrial, 1750) → 420+ ppm (2024). Highest in ~3 million years (Pliocene, when sea level was 25m higher). Fossil fuel burning + deforestation are primary sources. About 50% of emitted CO₂ stays in atmosphere, 25% absorbed by oceans, 25% by land (biosphere). This fraction varies year-to-year
- **Methane (CH₄)**: 80x more potent than CO₂ over 20 years (but shorter-lived ~12 years vs CO₂'s centuries). Sources: agriculture (livestock enteric fermentation + rice paddies), fossil fuel extraction (natural gas leaks), landfills, wetlands (natural). Methane leaks from oil/gas industry underestimated. Satellites now detect massive leaks (e.g., Permian basin). Reducing methane = fastest way to slow near-term warming
- **Nitrous oxide (N₂O)**: 300x CO₂, agricultural fertilizer (nitrogen-based) and livestock manure. Also: industrial processes, some natural. Persists in atmosphere ~120 years
- **Water vapor**: strongest GHG, but concentration determined by temperature (positive feedback: warmer air holds more water vapor → MORE warming). Not directly human-emitted

## Climate Feedbacks
- **Positive feedbacks** (amplify warming): ice-albedo (less ice = less sunlight reflected = more warming = more ice melt). Water vapor (warmer air = more water vapor = stronger greenhouse effect). Permafrost thaw (releases CH₄ + CO₂ = more warming = more thaw). Cloud feedback (uncertain — net effect likely amplifying, but not fully quantified). High clouds trap heat; low clouds reflect sunlight. How clouds change with warming is biggest uncertainty in climate sensitivity
- **Negative feedbacks** (reduce warming): Planck response (warmer surface = more infrared radiation to space). Increased plant growth (CO₂ fertilization — but limited by nutrients + water). Carbon cycle feedback (warmer ocean + land take up less CO₂ → more CO₂ stays in atmosphere — this is a positive feedback)
- **Climate sensitivity**: equilibrium temperature increase for doubling CO₂ (~560 ppm). IPCC AR6: most likely 3°C (range 2.5-4°C). If we stopped emitting tomorrow: warming continues ~decades due to thermal inertia, oceans continue warming + sea level continues rising for centuries. CO₂ stays in atmosphere for centuries to millennia

## Climate Modeling
- **Global Climate Models (GCMs)**: divide Earth into 3D grid cells (~100 km resolution typical for CMIP6). Solve physical equations for atmosphere, ocean, land, sea ice, ice sheets. Compute: air temp, pressure, humidity, wind, radiation. Coupled models: fully linked ocean + atmosphere + ice + biosphere. Each component exchanges fluxes (heat, momentum, water, carbon)
  - Earth System Models (ESMs): include carbon cycle, vegetation dynamics, chemistry, aerosols. More comprehensive, more uncertainty
- **CMIP6** (Coupled Model Intercomparison Project Phase 6): standard experimental framework used by 100+ modeling groups worldwide. Scenarios: SSP1-1.9 (below 1.5°C, very low emissions), SSP2-4.5 (intermediate), SSP3-7.0 (high), SSP5-8.5 (very high, worst-case). SSP (Shared Socioeconomic Pathways) = narrative about future population, economic growth, technology, land use, and governance
- **Limitations**: clouds (parameterized, biggest uncertainty — unresolved at grid scale). Precipitation (distribution, intensity — models struggle to capture extreme rainfall accurately). Regional projections (less reliable than global averages). Models can't predict exact year of events
- **Verification**: models successfully predict warming trend, vertical temperature structure, seasonal cycles, cooling after volcanic eruptions (e.g., Pinatubo 1991). They reproduce 20th-century climate better with greenhouse gases than without. Tested by "hindcasting" past climate — models reproduce past decades with remarkable accuracy

## Climate Change Impacts
- **Temperature**: global average +1.2°C (2023 vs 1850-1900). Land warming ~2x faster than ocean. Arctic warming ~4x faster than global average (Arctic amplification — ice-albedo feedback + atmospheric heat transport). 2023 = hottest year in 125,000 years (based on proxy data from ice cores + ocean sediments). 2024 possibly even hotter (due to El Niño + continued greenhouse gas growth)
- **Extreme weather**: heatwaves (more frequent, intense, long, "heat domes"). Heavy precipitation (7% more moisture per degree of warming — Clausius-Clapeyron: warmer air holds more water vapor — approx 7% more H₂O per °C). Droughts (more severe, longer in subtropics — due to increased evaporation + changes in atmospheric circulation). Wildfires (hotter + drier = larger burned area, longer fire season, more severe fire behavior. Mega-fires in Western US, Australia, Canada, Siberia). Hurricanes/ tropical cyclones (more intense, possibly slower-moving (more rainfall), higher storm surge)
- **Sea level rise**: 8" (20 cm) since 1900, accelerating (1.3 mm/yr 1900-1990, 3.4 mm/yr 1993-2015, 4.5+ mm/yr 2015-2023). Sources: thermal expansion (50% — water expands as it warms) + ice melt from glaciers + Greenland + Antarctic (50% — since 2010, ice sheet melt dominates). Projections: 1-3 ft by 2100 (low emissions), 2-7 ft (high emissions) — depending on Antarctic behavior. 10+ ft possible in coming centuries (committed from current warming alone)
  - Greenland ice sheet: 7m sea level equivalent, losing mass at accelerating rate (now 300+ billion tons/yr). Surface melting on Greenland now happens even in winter (rare before 2010). Thwaites Glacier ("Doomsday Glacier"): West Antarctica, 2 ft sea level in its basin alone, vulnerable to collapse from warm ocean water. Underwater ice cliff instability could double previous estimates, but timescale debated
- **Ocean acidification**: 30% acidity increase since industrial revolution (pH dropped from 8.2 to 8.1). Direct impact on shell-forming organisms (corals, pteropods, oysters, clams) — can't build shells. Potential: disrupt whole food webs. At 2°C warming, 99% of tropical coral reefs lost (inevitable under most emissions scenarios)

## Mitigation & Solutions
- **Energy**: solar PV (cost decreased 90%+ since 2010, now cheapest electricity source in history, but intermittent). Wind (same — onshore cheapest, offshore more expensive but steady). Energy storage (batteries — Li-ion costs down 85% since 2010, grid-scale Li-ion, pumped hydro, compressed air). Nuclear (fission — carbon-free, consistent, expensive new build, high costs, regulatory hurdles. Gen IV: smaller, safer, cheaper; still not commercial)
- **Electrification**: heat pumps (efficient heating+cooling, 2-5x more efficient than electric resistance. Cold climate heat pumps work at -10°F- -30°F). EV (electric vehicles — charging from grid increasingly renewable; battery production emissions amortized over vehicle lifetime vs ICE). Induction stoves (faster, safer, more efficient than gas)
  - Electrification + decarbonized grid = 50-70% emissions reduction in buildings + transport
- **Carbon removal**: nature based (reforestation, afforestation, improved forest management, soil carbon, wetland restoration — cheaper, co-benefits, but limited durability). Direct Air Capture (DAC: Climeworks, Carbon Engineering — capture CO₂ from ambient air, $500-1000/ton, high energy use, needs to scale 10,000x). BECCS (bioenergy + carbon capture — grow biomass, burn for energy, capture + store CO₂. Land use concerns, scalability uncertain). Enhanced weathering (crush silicate rocks, spread on land — CO₂ reacts, forms carbonate; slow, uncertain, but high potential)
- **Methane**: capture fugitive emissions from oil/gas, reduce food waste, changes to livestock diet + manure management, reduce rice paddy flooding. Direct methane removal technology not ready
