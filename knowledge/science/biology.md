# Biology & Human Body Basics

## Cell Biology
- Cell types: prokaryotic (bacteria, no nucleus) vs eukaryotic (plants/animals/fungi, has nucleus)
- Organelles: nucleus (DNA), mitochondria (energy), ribosomes (protein), ER (synthesis), Golgi (packaging)
- Mitochondria: power plant of cell — ATP production, has its own DNA (inherited from mother)
- Cell membrane: phospholipid bilayer, selectively permeable
- Cells divide: mitosis (body cells, 2 identical), meiosis (sex cells, 4 unique, half chromosomes)
- Stem cells: undifferentiated, can become any cell type
- Apoptosis: programmed cell death (remove damaged/unneeded cells)

## DNA & Genetics
- DNA structure: double helix, A-T and G-C base pairs
- Genes: segments of DNA that code for proteins
- Chromosomes: 23 pairs (46 total) — 22 autosomes + 1 sex pair (XX female, XY male)
- Dominant/recessive: dominant allele masks recessive (brown eyes dominant over blue)
- Genotype: genetic makeup (Bb). Phenotype: physical expression (brown eyes)
- Mutation: change in DNA sequence — can be neutral, harmful, or beneficial (evolution)
- CRISPR: gene editing tool — cuts DNA at specific location, can delete/insert/modify genes
- Epigenetics: environment influences which genes are expressed (diet, stress, exercise)

## Human Body Systems
- **Circulatory**: heart (4 chambers) pumps blood through arteries (away) → capillaries → veins (return)
  - 60,000 miles of blood vessels, ~5L blood, heart beats ~100,000 times/day
  - Blood: red cells (oxygen), white cells (immune), platelets (clotting), plasma (fluid)
- **Respiratory**: nose → trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli (gas exchange)
  - 400M alveoli, surface area ~70m² (size of tennis court)
  - Oxygen in, CO₂ out — diffusion driven by concentration gradient
- **Nervous**: brain (86B neurons) + spinal cord + peripheral nerves
  - Neuron: dendrites (receive), cell body, axon (send), synapse (gap between neurons)
  - Neurotransmitters: dopamine (reward/movement), serotonin (mood/sleep), acetylcholine (muscle/memory)
- **Digestive**: mouth → esophagus → stomach (acid + enzymes) → small intestine (nutrients absorbed) → large intestine (water absorbed) → rectum
  - Small intestine: ~20 ft long (most absorption happens here)
  - Gut microbiome: trillions of bacteria that help digest food, regulate immune system, affect mood
- **Immune**: innate (immediate, non-specific) + adaptive (targeted, memory)
  - White blood cells: T cells (kill infected), B cells (make antibodies), macrophages (eat pathogens)
  - Vaccines: expose immune system to harmless version, creates memory (antibodies + T cells)
- **Endocrine**: glands that release hormones into blood
  - Thyroid: metabolism (TSH, T3, T4)
  - Pancreas: insulin (lowers blood sugar), glucagon (raises)
  - Adrenal: cortisol (stress), adrenaline (fight/flight)

## Evolution
- Natural selection: organisms with traits better suited to environment survive and reproduce more
- Key mechanisms: mutation (new variation), natural selection (favorable traits spread), genetic drift (random changes in small populations)
- Humans evolved from apelike ancestors in Africa ~6M years ago
- Closest living relatives: chimpanzees (share ~98.8% DNA)
- Evolution is not "aiming" for perfection — it's "good enough" for current conditions

## Ecology
- Food chain: producer (plants) → primary consumer (herbivore) → secondary (carnivore) → tertiary (top predator)
- Decomposers: bacteria, fungi — break down dead material, return nutrients to soil
- Biomes: tropical rainforest, desert, tundra, grassland, taiga, temperate forest, aquatic
- Carbon cycle: CO₂ in air → photosynthesis → plants → eaten → respiration → CO₂ back
- Nitrogen cycle: atmospheric N₂ → bacteria fix to ammonia → plants use → animals eat → decompose → back
- Human impact: climate change (CO₂ from burning fossil fuels), deforestation, pollution, biodiversity loss

## Common Ailments
- Common cold: virus, rest + fluids + time (antibiotics don't work)
- Flu: virus, symptoms more severe than cold, annual vaccine recommended
- Allergies: immune overreaction to harmless substance (pollen, dust, peanuts)
- Antibiotic resistance: overuse of antibiotics → bacteria evolve resistance (MRSA)
- Autoimmune: immune attacks own body (type 1 diabetes, rheumatoid arthritis, lupus, MS)
- Inflammation: body's response to injury/infection — redness, heat, swelling, pain, loss of function
- Fever: body raises temperature to kill pathogens (< 39°C usually fine, > 40°C concerning)

## Interesting Numbers
- Human body: ~37 trillion cells, ~40 billion bacteria (mostly in gut), 206 bones (at birth 270, fuse as we grow)
- Heart: 60-100 bpm resting, ~2.5 billion beats in average lifetime
- Lungs: ~500M alveoli, 10,000L air/day
- Brain: ~86B neurons, ~100 trillion connections, 2% body weight but uses 20% of energy
- Skin: largest organ, ~2m², sheds ~500M cells/day
- Bones: strongest material in body (femur can support 1,500 kg standing)
- Blood vessels: ~100,000 km (<2.5x around Earth)
- Taste buds: ~10,000, replaced every 2 weeks
