# Neuroscience Basics

## The Neuron
- **Structure**: dendrites (receive signals), cell body (soma, integrates input), axon (sends signal, covered in myelin sheath), axon terminals (release neurotransmitters at synapse)
- **Myelin**: fatty insulation around axon — speeds signal transmission 50x (saltatory conduction, signal jumps between Nodes of Ranvier)
  - Multiple sclerosis: immune system attacks myelin → slowed/blocked nerve signals
  - White matter = myelinated axons (connections). Gray matter = cell bodies (processing)
- **Synapse**: gap between neurons (~20nm) — electrical signal → chemical (neurotransmitter release) → electrical (on receiving neuron)
  - ~100 trillion synapses in human brain
- **Action potential**: "all-or-nothing" electrical signal — threshold reached → voltage-gated Na+ channels open → depolarization (+40mV) → K+ repolarizes → refractory period (can't fire again immediately)
  - Resting potential: -70mV. Firing rate varies: 1-100+ times/second
  - Frequency coding: stronger stimulus = more frequent firing, not bigger action potentials
- **Synaptic pruning**: unused connections are eliminated — critical in development (use it or lose it) and continues throughout life (learning)

## Brain Regions & Functions
- **Prefrontal cortex** (PFC): executive functions — decision-making, planning, impulse control, personality, social behavior
  - Last to mature (mid-20s) — explains teen impulsivity
  - Damage: Phineas Gage (railroad spike through PFC → personality changed, lost impulse control)
- **Amygdala**: emotion processing — fear, threat detection, emotional memory
  - "Fight or flight" center — activates before conscious awareness (we feel fear before we know why)
  - Overactive in anxiety disorders, PTSD. Underactive in psychopathy
  - Amygdala hijack: when emotional response overrides rational thinking
- **Hippocampus**: memory formation, spatial navigation
  - New memories are temporarily stored here, then consolidated to cortex (sleep is critical!)
  - Alzheimer's: hippocampus is one of first areas damaged (early symptom: trouble forming new memories)
  - London taxi drivers have larger hippocampi (spatial navigation practice changes brain structure)
- **Anterior cingulate cortex** (ACC): error detection, conflict monitoring, emotional regulation
  - Lights up when you make a mistake or feel pain (both physical and social rejection)
- **Insula**: interoception (awareness of body state), empathy, disgust
  - Gut feelings — physical sensations that inform emotional decisions
- **Basal ganglia**: movement initiation, habit formation, reward (part of dopamine pathway)
  - Parkinson's disease: dopamine neurons in substantia nigra die → movement difficulty
  - Habits are stored here, not in conscious decision-making areas
- **Cerebellum**: coordination, balance, fine motor, timing, also involved in cognition (learning, attention)
  - Contains ~80% of brain's neurons despite being only 10% of volume

## Neuroplasticity
- The brain changes throughout life — neurons create new connections, strengthen existing ones, and can even grow (neurogenesis in hippocampus)
- **Long-term potentiation** (LTP): repeated activation strengthens synapses — cellular basis of learning ("neurons that fire together wire together")
- **Long-term depression** (LTD): weakens unused connections — equally important (clearing out old patterns)
- **Critical periods**: windows when brain is especially plastic for specific skills (language before ~7 years, vision before ~8 years)
  - Amblyopia (lazy eye): must be treated in childhood — adult brain can't rewire
  - Second language learned after childhood: never quite the same as native (different brain processing)
- **After injury**: brain can reorganize — functions can shift to neighboring areas (more in younger brains)
  - Stroke recovery: neuroplasticity-based therapy (constraint-induced movement therapy)
- **Experience-dependent plasticity**: adult brain changes with practice — juggling, musical instrument, meditation all show measurable structural changes in weeks

## The Nervous System
- **Central nervous system** (CNS): brain + spinal cord
- **Peripheral nervous system** (PNS): all nerves outside CNS
  - Somatic: voluntary movement, sensory input
  - Autonomic: involuntary functions
    - Sympathetic: fight or flight (stress response)
    - Parasympathetic: rest and digest (relaxation)
    - Enteric: "second brain" — gut nervous system (100M+ neurons, produces 90% of serotonin)
- **HPA axis** (Hypothalamus-Pituitary-Adrenal): stress response system
  - Stress → hypothalamus → CRH → pituitary → ACTH → adrenal glands → cortisol
  - Chronic stress = constantly elevated cortisol = damages hippocampus, suppresses immune system, increases abdominal fat, disrupts sleep

## Sensory Systems
- **Vision**: light → retina (rods for dim light, cones for color) → optic nerve → thalamus → visual cortex (occipital lobe)
  - 120M rods (black/white, low light), 6M cones (color, detail). Blind spot where optic nerve exits
  - Visual cortex has specialized areas: V4 (color), V5 (motion), fusiform face area (recognizing faces)
- **Audition**: sound waves → outer ear → eardrum (vibrates) → ossicles (middle ear, amplify) → cochlea (inner ear, hair cells convert vibration to neural signal) → auditory cortex (temporal lobe)
  - Hair cell damage is permanent (most common hearing loss cause) — protect ears from loud sounds
  - Cochlear implant: bypasses damaged hair cells, directly stimulates auditory nerve
- **Olfaction** (smell): odor molecules → olfactory epithelium → olfactory bulb → directly to limbic system and cortex (bypasses thalamus = more direct emotional connection)
  - Only sense with direct access to memory/emotion centers — explains why smells trigger vivid memories
  - Humans can detect ~1 trillion different scents
- **Somatosensory** (touch): skin receptors (pressure, vibration, temperature, pain) → spinal cord → thalamus → somatosensory cortex (parietal lobe)
  - Homunculus: map of body in somatosensory cortex — larger areas for more sensitive body parts (fingertips, lips, tongue)
- **Pain**: not just sensory — modulated by attention, emotion, expectation, context
  - Gate control theory: non-painful input can "close the gate" to painful input (why rubbing a stubbed toe helps)
  - Placebo effect: expectation of relief → brain releases endorphins (real physiological effect)
  - Chronic pain: pain system becomes sensitized — pain persists after tissue heals

## Consciousness & Sleep
- **Global workspace theory**: consciousness arises from information integrated across multiple brain regions — what's in the "workspace" is what we're conscious of
- **Default mode network** (DMN): brain areas active when daydreaming, mind-wandering, thinking about self and others
  - Overactive in depression (rumination). Reduced by meditation, psychedelics, flow states
  - Active during: social cognition, autobiographical memory, future planning, moral reasoning
- **Sleep**: not passive — active brain state essential for health
  - NREM: slow waves, memory consolidation (facts), growth hormone, immune function
  - REM: dreaming, emotional processing, memory integration, creativity
  - Glymphatic system: clears waste products from brain during sleep (including amyloid-beta linked to Alzheimer's)
  - Sleep deprivation: impairs attention, memory, decision-making, emotional regulation, immune function — after 24 hours, impairment equal to BAC of 0.10%
- **Neurofeedback**: real-time brain activity display → learn to control brain waves (used for ADHD, anxiety, peak performance)
