# Astrophysics

Stars, galaxies, cosmology, and the physics of astronomical objects.

## Stars
- **Formation**: Molecular cloud (mostly H₂) → gravitational collapse → protostar (heats up, infrared) → fusion ignition (core reaches ~10M K for H→He fusion) → main sequence star. Jean's mass determines minimum cloud mass for collapse
- **Main sequence**: 90% of star life — hydrogen fusion in core. Location on HR diagram (temperature vs luminosity): hotter = bigger, brighter, shorter lived. Mass determines everything: massive stars burn hot and fast, low-mass stars burn cool and slow
- **Stellar classification**: O (hottest, >30,000K, blue, massive, rare) → B → A → F → G (Sun 5778K, yellow) → K → M (coolest, <3,500K, red, common). Mnemonic: "Oh Be A Fine Girl/Guy, Kiss Me"
- **Life after main sequence**:
  - Low-mass (Sun-like): Red giant → helium flash → horizontal branch → planetary nebula (ejects outer layers) → white dwarf (Earth-sized, carbon/oxygen core, electron degenerate)
  - Massive (>8 solar masses): Red supergiant → nuclear fusion up to iron (fusion to heavier elements is endothermic, core can't support → collapse) → core collapse supernova (Type II — neutron star or black hole)
  - Most massive (>20-30 solar masses): Direct collapse to black hole (may produce gamma-ray burst)
- **Nuclear fusion**: Proton-proton chain (Sun-like stars — 4H → He + energy). CNO cycle (massive stars — carbon acts as catalyst). Triple-alpha process (He → C in red giants). Advanced burning: C, Ne, O, Si → iron peak elements (onion-shell structure in massive stars)

## Compact Objects
- **White dwarf**: Electron degeneracy pressure. Mass <1.4 M☉ (Chandrasekhar limit — exceeds it → Type Ia supernova). A cooling — eventually becomes black dwarf (doesn't exist yet — universe too young)
- **Neutron star**: Core collapse supernova remnant. ~1.4-2.2 M☉. Radius ~10 km. Density ~10¹⁷ kg/m³ (sugar cube weighs 100 million tons). Neutron degeneracy pressure. Pulsars: rotating neutron stars with magnetic beams (first discovered 1967, Jocelyn Bell). Millisecond pulsars: spun up by accreting matter from companion
- **Black hole**: Escape velocity > c. Event horizon (R_s = 2GM/c²). Stellar-mass (3-100 M☉). Supermassive (10⁶-10¹⁰ M☉ at galactic centers — Sgr A* 4.3M M☉). Intermediate-mass (100-10⁵ M☉ — rare)
- **Supernova types**:
  - Type Ia: White dwarf accretes mass, exceeds Chandrasekhar — thermonuclear explosion. Standard candle for cosmology (same intrinsic brightness ~10⁹ solar luminosity)
  - Type II: Core collapse of massive star. Hydrogen present in spectrum. Peak ~10⁸ solar luminosity. Remnant = neutron star or black hole
  - Type Ib/Ic: Core collapse of massive star that lost outer layers (hydrogen/helium envelope stripped)

## Galaxies
- **Types (Hubble sequence)**: Elliptical (E0 almost round → E7 elongated — little gas/dust, old stars, red), Spiral (Sa tight spiral → Sc loose, with bulge + disk. Milky Way = SBbc — barred spiral), Irregular (no defined shape — Small Magellanic Cloud, starburst galaxies)
- **Milky Way**: Barred spiral, 100,000 ly diameter, 1,000 ly thick (disk). 200-400 billion stars. Sun 26,000 ly from center, orbits every 230 million years. Spiral arms: density waves (star formation triggered as gas enters arm). Central bulge: old stars + Sgr A* supermassive black hole. Halo: globular clusters, dark matter
- **Galaxy clusters**: Groups of galaxies bound by gravity (50-1000 galaxies). Virgo Cluster (closest, 54 Mly away, 1,300+ galaxies). Coma Cluster (rich, 10,000+ galaxies). Intracluster medium: hot gas (10⁷-10⁸ K) emitting X-rays — total gas mass exceeds galaxy mass
- **Active galactic nuclei (AGN)**: Supermassive BH accreting matter → bright emission across spectrum. Types: Seyfert (spiral host, bright nucleus, broad emission lines), Quasars (very distant, extremely luminous — most powerful persistent sources in universe), Blazars (jet pointed at Earth — rapid variability)
- **Galaxy formation**: Hierarchical merging — small galaxies merge to form larger. ΛCDM: dark matter halos form first, gas falls in, stars form. Mergers trigger starbursts, can turn spirals into ellipticals

## Cosmology
- **Big Bang**: Universe ~13.8 Gyr from hot dense state. Cosmic microwave background (CMB) at 2.725 K discovered 1965 (Penzias-Wilson). Last scattering surface at z~1100 (380,000 years after BB). Temperature fluctuations ~1:10⁵ seed galaxy formation
- **Expansion & acceleration**: Hubble-Lemaître law: v = H₀d. H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc (Hubble tension: CMB 67.4 vs local 73.0 — unsolved). Dark energy (Λ) drives acceleration — discovered 1998 via Type Ia supernovae. 68% of universe dark energy, 27% dark matter, 5% ordinary matter
- **Large-scale structure**: Filaments (galaxy chains), voids (empty regions), walls (like the Sloan Great Wall). Cosmic web — largest structures. Baryon acoustic oscillations (BAO): sound waves frozen into matter distribution — standard ruler for cosmology
- **Dark matter**: Evidence: galaxy rotation curves (Zwicky 1933, Rubin 1970s), gravitational lensing, CMB power spectrum, galaxy cluster dynamics. Non-baryonic, cold (non-relativistic). Weakly interacting massive particles (WIMPs) — not detected yet. 80% of matter in universe is dark

## Observational Techniques
- **Electromagnetic spectrum**: Radio (synchrotron radiation, molecular lines — ALMA, VLA), Infrared (cool dust, protostars, redshifted optical — JWST), Optical (visible light — Hubble, ground-based 8-10m telescopes), UV/X-ray (hot gas, AGN — Chandra, XMM-Newton), Gamma-ray (GRBs, pulsars — Fermi)
- **Gravitational waves**: LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA — detect spacetime ripples from binary mergers. First detection GW150914 (2015, 2 BHs 1.3 billion ly away, 3 solar masses radiated as gravitational waves). Working towards LISA (space-based, 2030s)
- **Multi-messenger astronomy**: Combining EM + GW + neutrinos. GW170817 (2017): merging neutron stars detected in gravitational waves + gamma rays + optical/infrared kilonova ("gold factory") + X-ray/radio afterglow
