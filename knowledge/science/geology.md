# Geology & Earth Science

## Earth's Structure
- **Crust**: 5-70 km thick — oceanic (basalt, 5-10 km, denser, younger) vs continental (granite, 30-70 km, less dense, older up to 4 billion years)
  - Mohorovicic discontinuity (Moho): boundary between crust + mantle — detected by seismic wave speed change
- **Mantle**: 2,900 km thick — mostly solid silicate rock (peridotite), flows VERY slowly over geologic time (convection ~1-5 cm/year — moves tectonic plates)
  - Lithosphere: crust + upper rigid mantle (broken into tectonic plates). Asthenosphere: partially molten, plastic layer below lithosphere (plate movement)
  - 410 and 660 km discontinuities: phase changes in olivine (mineral structure changes at these depths)
- **Core**: outer (liquid iron + nickel, 2,200 km thick — generates Earth's magnetic field via dynamo effect, flows as liquid metal), inner (solid iron + nickel, 1,220 km radius, 5,500°C — hotter than sun's surface! 360 GPa pressure. Grows ~1 mm/year as outer core freezes onto inner core)

## Plate Tectonics
- **Evidence**: continental fit (South America + Africa), matching fossils/rock types across oceans (Mesosaurus in both Brazil + West Africa), seafloor spreading (magnetic stripes symmetric on both sides of mid-Atlantic ridge — records magnetic reversals over time), earthquake/volcano distribution (Ring of Fire = convergent plate boundaries)
  - Paleomagnetism: iron minerals in cooling lava align with Earth's magnetic field — records position relative to equator/pole
- **Divergent boundaries**: plates separate — mid-ocean ridges (Mid-Atlantic Ridge, East Pacific Rise — new crust created), continental rifts (East African Rift — eventually new ocean basin, Red Sea = early stage ocean)
  - Mid-Atlantic Ridge runs down center of Atlantic Ocean, ~15,000 km, produces ~2.5 cm/year new crust
- **Convergent boundaries**: plates collide — subduction (oceanic under oceanic → volcanic island arcs: Japan, Philippines, Aleutians. Under continental → continental arcs: Andes, Cascade volcanoes. Deep ocean trenches: Mariana Trench, Peru-Chile Trench)
  - Continental-continental collision: no subduction (both buoyant) → mountains (Himalayas — India colliding with Asia, still rising ~5mm/yr, Tibetan Plateau formed)
- **Transform boundaries**: plates slide past — San Andreas Fault (California), Alpine Fault (New Zealand), Anatolian Fault (Turkey). Earthquakes, no volcanoes
- **Hotspots**: mantle plumes — Hawaii (Pacific plate moving over stationary hotspot → chain of islands, oldest to youngest NW to SE. Kilauea active, Loihi forming). Yellowstone (continental hotspot, past under Snake River Plain, currently under Yellowstone — supervolcano. Last supereruption 640,000 years ago. Not overdue — magma chamber partially molten, no imminent eruption. Caldera)
- **Rates**: plates move 1-10 cm/year (about as fast as fingernails grow). Atlantic widens ~2.5 cm/yr. Pacific shrinks as subduction around Ring of Fire consumes older crust

## Rocks & Minerals
- **Igneous**: cooled from magma — intrusive (slow cooling below surface, large crystals: granite, gabbro, diorite, pegmatite — crystals +6" possible). Extrusive (fast cooling at surface, fine-grained/glassy: basalt, rhyolite, obsidian (volcanic glass), pumice (floats! — frothy lava, gas bubbles), scoria, tuff (volcanic ash lithified))
  - Bowen's reaction series: order minerals crystallize from cooling magma (olivine first → pyroxene → amphibole → biotite → quartz last)
- **Sedimentary**: compressed sediment layers — elastic (sandstone, shale, conglomerate — from eroded older rocks), chemical (limestone, rock salt, gypsum — precipitated from water), organic (coal, chalk, some limestone — from organic remains)
  - Features: strata/layers, fossils, cross-bedding, ripple marks, mud cracks — preserve ancient conditions
  - Coal: peat → lignite → bituminous → anthracite (heat + pressure increase over time/carbon content)
- **Metamorphic**: changed by heat + pressure — foliated (layered: slate, schist, gneiss) vs non-foliated (marble from limestone, quartzite from sandstone)
  - Metamorphic grade: low (slate, 200-300°C) → medium (schist) → high (gneiss, 600-700°C)

## Geologic Time
- **Hadean** (4.6-4.0 billion years ago): Earth forms, molten, moon forms (giant impact hypothesis — Theia collided with Earth, debris coalesced into moon. Evidence: moon composition similar to Earth's mantle, no iron core). Oldest materials: zircon crystals (Jack Hills, Australia, 4.4 billion years old)
- **Archean** (4.0-2.5 Ga): first crust, first life (stromatolites — 3.5 Ga, cyanobacteria mats. Burgess Shale fossils: 508 million years, Cambrian explosion exceptional preservation)
- **Proterozoic** (2.5-0.541 Ga): oxygen buildup (Great Oxidation Event ~2.4 Ga — cyanobacteria produced O₂, killed anaerobic life, caused Snowball Earth glaciations). First complex cells. Plate tectonics operating
- **Phanerozoic** (541 Ma-present): "visible life" — Cambrian Explosion (541 Ma, most major animal groups appear in fossil record within ~20 million years. Best seen at Burgess Shale 508 Ma)
  - Paleozoic (541-252 Ma): fish, land plants, amphibians, reptiles. Permian mass extinction (252 Ma — "Great Dying" 96% species, likely from Siberian Traps volcanism + CO₂ + ozone depletion)
  - Mesozoic (252-66 Ma): dinosaurs, mammals appear, first birds. KT extinction (66 Ma — asteroid impact Chicxulub, Yucatan, 10 km, 72% species extinct, ended dinosaurs)
  - Cenozoic (66 Ma-present): mammals diversify, humans appear (~300,000 years ago, geologically yesterday)

## Earthquakes
- **Causes**: sudden energy release along fault (elastic rebound theory — stress builds, rock bends, then ruptures, snapping back = seismic waves)
  - Aftershocks: smaller quakes after mainshock as fault adjusts. Foreshocks: smaller before (cannot predict). Swarms: many similar-sized (volcanic, geothermal areas, Yellowstone)
- **Seismic waves**: P-wave (primary, longitudinal, fastest, travels through solids+liquids — 5-7 km/s), S-wave (secondary, transverse, slower, only through solids — 3-4 km/s). Surface waves (Love, Rayleigh — slowest, most destructive, cause shaking that damages buildings)
  - P-wave shadow zone: 103°-143° from epicenter — no P-waves detected (because outer core is liquid, S-waves can't travel through liquid. This proved that outer core is liquid!)
- **Magnitude scales**: Richter (local magnitude, 1935, accurate for small nearby quakes). Moment magnitude (Mw, modern, more accurate for ALL sizes — based on seismic moment = area × slip × rigidity). Each 1.0 increase = 10x wave amplitude, 32x energy released
  - Mercalli intensity (scale I-XII based on observed effects/damage, varies by location). Modified Mercalli: useful for damage assessment, maps intensity not magnitude
- **Where they happen**: Ring of Fire (Pacific — 90% of world's earthquakes), Alpine-Himalayan belt (15%), mid-Atlantic ridge, intraplate (rare — New Madrid 1811-12, strongest in US history, made Mississippi flow backward temporarily. Charleston 1886)
- **Prediction**: impossible (short-term). Possible: long-term probability (based on recurrence intervals, fault slip rates). No reliable precursors (animal behavior, radon, foreshocks — not reproducible, not reliable)
  - Parkfield experiment (California): predicted quake by 1993—occurred 2004. GPS, creepmeters, strainmeters didn't help predict beyond probabilistic

## Volcanoes
- **Types**: Shield (Hawaii — broad, gentle slopes, fluid basalt lava, non-explosive. Eruption: lava flows. Low silica, low gas). Stratovolcano/composite (Mt. Rainier, Fuji, Pinatubo, Vesuvius — steep, explosive, alternating lava+ash layers. High silica = higher viscosity. High gas pressure = explosive). Cinder cone (small, single eruption, scoria. Sunset Crater AZ, Paricutin Mexico — grew 400m in 9 years!)
  - Supervolcano (caldera): Yellowstone, Toba (Indonesia ~74,000 years ago — largest eruption in 25 million years, caused 6-10 year volcanic winter, human population bottleneck maybe down to few thousand). Not mountain-shaped — caldera (depression from collapsed magma chamber)
- **Eruption style**: Hawaiian (lava fountains + flows), Strombolian (mild explosions, cinder), Vulcanian (moderate explosions, ash), Plinian (catastrophic, high ash column — Vesuvius 79 AD, Pinatubo 1991, St. Helens 1980). Phreatomagmatic: magma + water = steam explosion (very violent)
- **Hazards**: lava flow (slow, rarely kills), pyroclastic flow (fastest, hottest — 500+ mph, 1000°C. Pompeii killed by pyroclastic surge. Herculaneum: instant death, brains vitrified). Ash fall (collapse roofs, aviation hazard (Jet engines fail in ash cloud—2010 Eyjafjallajökull shut down European air travel), breathing hazard, water contamination). Lahars (volcanic mudflow — bigger hazard than lava. Rain/snowmelt mobilizes loose ash. Nevado del Ruiz 1985: lahar killed 25,000 in Armero, Colombia). Poison gas (CO₂, SO₂). Tsunami (Krakatoa 1883 — 36,000 killed by tsunami. Anak Krakatoa collapse 2018: 400+ killed)
  - VEI (Volcanic Explosivity Index): 0 (non-explosive, Hawaiian) to 8 (supervolcano, Yellowstone/Toba). Like Richter scale for volcanoes. Logarithmic

## Fossils
- **Formation**: organism buried rapidly in sediment → minerals replace organic material → fossil. Most organisms don't fossilize (need rapid burial, low oxygen, hard parts)
  - Types: body fossil (bones, shells, teeth), trace fossil (footprints, burrows, coprolites — fossilized poop, trackways reveal behavior), mold (impression left when dissolved), cast (mineral fills mold), replacement (original material replaced by silica, pyrite, calcite — petrified wood), carbonization (thin film of carbon preserved — plant fossils, feathers)
- **Index fossils**: short-lived, widespread species used to date rock layers (trilobites, ammonites, graptolites). Good index fossils: evolve fast, wide geographic range, abundant, easily identifiable
- **Radiometric dating**: decay of radioactive isotopes at known rates — C-14 (to 50,000 years, organic). Potassium-argon (potassium-40 → argon-40, half-life 1.3 billion years, volcanic rock — good for dating early hominids). Uranium-lead (U-238 → Pb-206, half-life 4.5 billion years — oldest method, zircon crystals in earliest rocks). Carbon-14: only for organic, calibrate against tree rings, 50,000 year limit
  - Half-life: time for half of parent isotope to decay. After 1 half-life: 50% parent, 50% daughter. After 10: 0.1% parent remaining (~99.9% daughter)
