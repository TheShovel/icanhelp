# Earth & Space Science

## Earth Structure
- **Crust**: outermost, 5-70 km thick (thinner under ocean, thickest under mountains)
- **Mantle**: ~2900 km thick, solid rock that flows slowly (convection drives plate tectonics)
- **Outer core**: liquid iron + nickel, generates magnetic field (protects from solar wind)
- **Inner core**: solid iron + nickel, ~5500°C (hotter than sun's surface), ~12,000 km deep
- **Lithosphere**: crust + upper mantle, broken into tectonic plates
- **Asthenosphere**: partially molten layer below lithosphere, allows plate movement

## Plate Tectonics
- 7 major plates: Pacific, North American, South American, Eurasian, African, Antarctic, Indo-Australian
- **Divergent**: plates separate → new crust (mid-Atlantic ridge, East African rift)
- **Convergent**: plates collide → mountains (Himalayas: India colliding with Asia) or subduction (trenches, volcanoes)
- **Transform**: plates slide past → earthquakes (San Andreas fault)
- Earthquakes: measured by Richter scale (magnitude) — each 1.0 = 10x stronger shaking, 32x more energy
- Volcanoes: form at subduction zones, divergent boundaries, and hot spots (Hawaii)

## Atmosphere
- Layers: troposphere (weather, 0-12 km), stratosphere (ozone layer, 12-50 km), mesosphere (50-80 km), thermosphere (80-700 km), exosphere (>700 km)
- Composition: 78% N₂, 21% O₂, 1% Ar + CO₂ + trace gases
- Greenhouse effect: CO₂, H₂O, CH₄ (methane) trap heat — natural (keeps Earth ~33°C warmer) + human-enhanced (climate change)
- Ozone layer: in stratosphere, blocks 95% of UV radiation — depleted by CFCs (now healing after ban)
- Water cycle: evaporation → condensation → precipitation → runoff → repeat
- Weather vs climate: weather = short-term conditions, climate = long-term averages (30 years+)

## Ocean
- Covers 71% of Earth's surface
- Average depth: ~3700 m. Deepest point: Mariana Trench (~11,000 m)
- Ocean currents: driven by wind, temperature, salinity (thermohaline circulation)
- Gulf Stream: warm current from Gulf of Mexico to Europe — keeps Europe warmer than it should be
- Tides: caused by moon's gravity (2 high + 2 low per day, moon's pull stronger than sun's)
- El Niño/La Niña: Pacific Ocean temperature changes → global weather effects
- Ocean acidification: CO₂ absorbed by ocean → carbonic acid → harms coral, shellfish

## Solar System
- **Sun**: ~1.4M km diameter, mostly H + He, fusion (H→He), 4.6B years old, ~5B years left
- **Planets (inner)**: Mercury (smallest, no atmosphere, 880°F day / -290°F night)
  - Venus: hottest (~900°F), runaway greenhouse, rotates backward, similar size to Earth
  - Earth: liquid water, life, 1 moon
  - Mars: red (iron oxide), thin atmosphere, 2 moons (Phobos, Deimos), had water in past
- **Planets (outer)**: Jupiter (largest, Great Red Spot storm, 95 moons including Europa)
  - Saturn: rings (ice + rock), 146 moons (Titan has atmosphere, lakes of methane)
  - Uranus: rotates on side (tilted 98°), 27 moons
  - Neptune: fastest winds (2,000 km/h), 16 moons
- **Dwarf planets**: Pluto, Eris, Makemake, Haumea, Ceres (asteroid belt)
- **Asteroid belt**: between Mars and Jupiter — leftover planet formation
- **Kuiper belt**: beyond Neptune — Pluto, comets
- **Oort cloud**: distant cloud of comets, edge of solar influence

## Stars & Universe
- **Life cycle**: nebula → protostar → main sequence (fusion of H) → red giant (exhaust H) → white dwarf (small stars) or supernova → neutron star/black hole (massive stars)
- HR diagram: plots stars by luminosity vs temperature (main sequence = most stars)
- Sun: yellow dwarf (G2V), medium-sized, ~300,000x Earth mass
- Nearest star: Proxima Centauri (4.24 light years away)
- Milky Way: 100,000 light years across, ~100-400 billion stars, 1 supermassive black hole (Sagittarius A*)
- Galaxies: spiral (Milky Way, Andromeda), elliptical, irregular
- Light year: distance light travels in one year = ~5.88 trillion miles = ~9.46 trillion km
- Expansion of universe: galaxies are moving away from us (redshift) — discovered by Hubble, 1929
- Big Bang: universe began ~13.8B years ago from an infinitely dense point, has been expanding ever since

## Space Exploration
- 1961: first human in space (Yuri Gagarin, Vostok 1)
- 1969: first moon landing (Apollo 11, Neil Armstrong + Buzz Aldrin)
- 12 humans have walked on moon (all Apollo missions, 1969-1972)
- ISS: 1998-present, 16 countries, orbits every 90 min, 400 km above Earth
- Hubble Space Telescope: 1990-present, deep space images
- James Webb Space Telescope: 2021-present, infrared, sees farther (early universe)
- Private: SpaceX (reusable rockets, Starship), Blue Origin, Virgin Galactic
- Mars rovers: Perseverance (2021), Curiosity (2012) — searching for signs of life
- Voyager 1: 1977-present, exited solar system, farthest human-made object (24B km+ away)

## Climate Change
- Primary cause: burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) → CO₂
- CO₂ levels: 280 ppm (pre-industrial) → 420+ ppm (2024) — highest in 800,000 years
- Global temperature: ~1.2°C warmer than pre-industrial — 9 of warmest 10 years since 2014
- Effects: sea level rise (20 cm since 1900, accelerating), more extreme weather, ocean acidification
- Feedback loops: ice melts (less reflection), permafrost thaws (releases methane), more warming
- Solutions: renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro), electric vehicles, energy efficiency, carbon capture, reforestation
- Paris Agreement: limit warming to 1.5-2°C (current policies: ~2.7°C by 2100)
