# Ancient Civilizations

Major civilizations of the ancient world — cultures, achievements, and legacy.

## Mesopotamia (3500-539 BCE)
- **Sumer** (3500-2000 BCE): First cities (Uruk, Ur, Lagash). Invented writing (cuneiform 3200 BCE). Ziggurats (stepped temple towers). Epic of Gilgamesh (oldest literature). Polytheistic pantheon (An, Enlil, Enki, Inanna). Math: base-60 system (still used: 60 seconds, 360 degrees). Wheel, plow, sailboat, beer brewing
- **Akkadian Empire** (2334-2154 BCE): Sargon of Akkad — first empire builder. Semitic language (Akkadian). Empire collapsed from climate change + invasion (Gutians)
- **Babylon** (1894-539 BCE): Hammurabi's Code (1754 BCE — 282 laws, lex talionis "eye for an eye"). Hanging Gardens (one of 7 Wonders). Nebuchadnezzar II. Ishtar Gate. Enuma Elish (creation myth). Mathematics: quadratic equations, Pythagorean theorem 1,000 years before Pythagoras
- **Assyria** (2025-609 BCE): Militaristic empire (Nineveh capital). First organized armies (iron weapons, chariots, siege engines, cavalry). Library of Ashurbanipal (30,000 cuneiform tablets). Known for brutality to conquered peoples. Fell to Babylonians + Medes

## Ancient Egypt (3100-30 BCE)
- **Old Kingdom** (2686-2181 BCE): Pyramid Age — Great Pyramid of Giza (Khufu, 2560 BCE, 481 ft, 2.3 million stone blocks). Sphinx. Centralized state, pharaoh = living god. Collapsed from drought (2200 BCE)
- **Middle Kingdom** (2055-1650 BCE): Reunification (Mentuhotep II). Expansion into Nubia. Literature "golden age" — Story of Sinuhe. Osiris cult popularized. Second Intermediate Period: Hyksos invasion (horse + chariot introduced)
- **New Kingdom** (1550-1069 BCE): Empire period. Hatshepsut (female pharaoh, trade expedition to Punt). Akhenaten (monotheistic Aten cult — world's first monotheism?). Tutankhamun (KV62 tomb discovered 1922 — intact, 5,000+ artifacts). Ramesses II (1279-1213 BCE — longest reign, many monuments, Battle of Qadesh). Valley of the Kings, Karnak, Luxor
- **Achievements**: Hieroglyphics (deciphered 1822 by Champollion via Rosetta Stone). Papyrus. Advanced medicine (Edwin Smith Papyrus — surgical treatise). Mummification (elaborate preservation of body). Solar calendar (365 days). Geometry (pyramid construction, land surveying after Nile floods)

## Indus Valley (Harappan) Civilization (2600-1900 BCE)
- Major cities: Mohenjo-daro, Harappa, Dholavira. Advanced urban planning — grid streets, standardized fired bricks, sophisticated drainage/sewage systems (world's first). Great Bath (Mohenjo-daro — ritual purification?)
- Standardized weights and measures. Trade with Mesopotamia. Undeciphered script (Indus script, ~400 symbols). Decline: gradual (climate change, drought, river shifting) — not invasion theory (disproven)
- Seals carved with animals (unicorn, bull, elephant). Cotton textiles (earliest known). Terracotta figurines

## Ancient China (Shang through Han, 1600 BCE - 220 CE)
- **Shang Dynasty** (1600-1046 BCE): First historically confirmed dynasty. Oracle bone script (earliest Chinese writing — divination on turtle shells/ox shoulder blades). Bronze ritual vessels. Urban centers (Anyang). Ancestor worship
- **Zhou Dynasty** (1046-256 BCE): Mandate of Heaven (justification for rule). Feudal system. Iron metallurgy. Axial Age: Confucius (Kongzi — ethics, filial piety, ren/benevolence, li/ritual), Laozi (Daoism — wu wei/effortless action, Dao), Sunzi (The Art of War). Hundred Schools of Thought
- **Qin Dynasty** (221-206 BCE): First Emperor (Qin Shi Huang). Standardized writing, currency, weights/measures, axle widths. Terracotta Army. Great Wall expansion. Legalism (harsh laws). Fell quickly after First Emperor's death
- **Han Dynasty** (206 BCE - 220 CE): Golden age — 400 years. Silk Road trade (Zhang Qian missions). Paper invented (Cai Lun, 105 CE). Civil service exams (Confucian classics). Population 60 million. Decline: eunuch corruption, Yellow Turban rebellion, Three Kingdoms period

## Ancient Greece (1600-146 BCE)
- **Minoan** (Crete, 2700-1450 BCE): Palace of Knossos. Linear A (undeciphered). Artistic, seafaring. Bull-leaping fresco. Volcanic eruption (Thera 1600 BCE) weakened civilization
- **Mycenaean** (1600-1100 BCE): Linear B (early Greek — deciphered). Trojan War (perhaps historical, ~1200 BCE). Fortress palaces (Mycenae, Tiryns). Collapse in Bronze Age collapse
- **Archaic & Classical** (800-323 BCE): City-states (polis) — Athens (democracy), Sparta (military state). Persian Wars (490-479 BCE — Marathon, Thermopylae, Salamis). Periclean Athens. Philosophy: Socrates (Socratic method), Plato (Academy, Theory of Forms, Republic), Aristotle (Lyceum, logic, ethics, biology). Drama: Aeschylus, Sophocles (Oedipus), Euripides, Aristophanes. Architecture: Parthenon, classical orders (Doric, Ionic, Corinthian). Sculpture (contrapposto). Hippocrates (medicine — Hippocratic Oath). Peloponnesian War (431-404 BCE, Athens vs Sparta)
- **Hellenistic** (323-146 BCE): Alexander the Great conquered Persian Empire to India. Spread Greek culture (koine Greek, syncretism). Library of Alexandria. Science/ math: Euclid (Elements — geometry), Archimedes (buoyancy, levers), Eratosthenes (Earth's circumference), Aristarchus (heliocentric model). Stoa (Zeno), Epicureanism

## Ancient Rome (753 BCE - 476 CE)
- **Republic** (509-27 BCE): Senate, patricians vs plebeians. Twelve Tables (450 BCE — law code). Punic Wars (264-146 BCE — Rome vs Carthage, Hannibal crossed Alps). Conquest of Greece, Gaul (Caesar, 58-50 BCE). Rise of military commanders/ civil wars (Marius vs Sulla, Caesar vs Pompey). Assassination of Caesar (44 BCE)
- **Empire** (27 BCE - 476 CE): Augustus (27 BCE - 14 CE) — Pax Romana (200 years peace). Roman engineering: aqueducts, roads (50,000 miles), concrete (Pantheon dome — largest unreinforced concrete dome, 43m), arches, amphitheaters (Colosseum 80 CE). Law (jurisprudence, innocent until proven guilty). Latin language. Emperors: Nero (fire of Rome 64 CE), Trajan (max extent), Marcus Aurelius (Stoic philosopher), Diocletian (split empire), Constantine (Christianity legalized 313 CE, Constantinople built). Decline: economic, military crises, barbarian invasions, split empire. Last Western emperor deposed 476 CE
- **Legacy**: Roman law basis of European legal systems. Latin roots of Romance languages (French, Spanish, Italian, Portuguese, Romanian). Alphabet (via Etruscans). Architecture (arches, domes, concrete). Republican government ideal. Christianity spread through Roman infrastructure
