# World Religions Overview

## Major Religions by Adherents

### Christianity (~2.4 billion)
- **Core beliefs**: one God, Jesus Christ is son of God and savior, died for humanity's sins, resurrected, salvation through faith, Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
- **Branches**: Catholic (1.3B — Pope, sacraments, tradition), Protestant (900M — Bible alone, faith alone, grace alone — Luther, Calvin, Anglicans, Baptists, Methodists, Pentecostals), Orthodox (260M — Eastern/Greek/Russian, icons, tradition)
  - Key division: Great Schism (1054) split Catholic and Orthodox. Protestant Reformation (1517) split Protestants from Catholic
- **Sacred text**: Bible (Old Testament + New Testament — 66 books in Protestant, more in Catholic/Orthodox with Apocrypha/Deuterocanonical books)
- **Practices**: baptism, communion/Eucharist, prayer, church attendance, Christmas (Dec 25), Easter (March-April)

### Islam (~1.9 billion)
- **Core beliefs**: one God (Allah), Muhammad is final prophet, Quran is literal word of God, angels, Day of Judgment, predestination
  - Five Pillars: Shahada (declaration of faith), Salat (prayer 5x/day facing Mecca), Zakat (charity 2.5% of wealth), Sawm (fasting Ramadan), Hajj (pilgrimage to Mecca once in lifetime if able)
- **Branches**: Sunni (85-90% — elected caliphs, consensus), Shia (10-15% — Imams from Muhammad's family descendants, Ali). Sufism (mystical dimension, both Sunni and Shia)
- **Sacred text**: Quran (revealed to Muhammad in Arabic over 23 years, 114 chapters/surahs). Hadith (sayings and actions of Muhammad)
- **Practices**: mosque attendance, Friday prayer, halal (permissible) dietary rules (no pork, alcohol), Ramadan fasting (sunrise to sunset), modesty in dress (hijab/headscarf common for women, varies by culture)

### Hinduism (~1.2 billion)
- **Core beliefs**: dharma (duty/ethics), samsara (cycle of rebirth), karma (actions determine future), moksha (liberation from cycle), multiple paths to divine
  - Polytheistic with one underlying reality (Brahman) — many gods as manifestations (Trimurti: Brahma/creator, Vishnu/preserver, Shiva/destroyer)
- **Sacred texts**: Vedas (oldest, ~1500 BCE), Upanishads (philosophical), Bhagavad Gita (dialogue on duty and devotion), Ramayana and Mahabharata (epics)
- **Caste system**: varnas (Brahmins/priests, Kshatriyas/warriors, Vaishyas/traders, Shudras/laborers) + Dalits (formerly "untouchables") — socially stringent, officially abolished in India but persists
- **Practices**: puja (worship at home or temple), meditation, yoga (originated in Hinduism), festivals (Diwali/festival of lights, Holi/festival of colors), vegetarianism common, cremation of dead
- **Origins**: ~2000-1500 BCE in Indus Valley, no single founder — evolved over thousands of years

### Buddhism (~520 million)
- **Core beliefs**: Four Noble Truths (life is suffering/suffering caused by craving/craving can end/ path to end is Eightfold Path)
  - Eightfold Path: right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration
  - Goal: nirvana (end of suffering, liberation from samsara/rebirth cycle)
  - Middle Way: avoid extremes of indulgence and asceticism
- **Branches**: Theravada (Southeast Asia, oldest, monastic focus), Mahayana (East Asia — Zen, Pure Land, Tibetan/Vajrayana — bodhisattvas who delay nirvana to help others)
  - Zen (Chan): direct meditation (zazen) over scripture, koan riddles, sudden enlightenment
- **Sacred texts**: Tripitaka/Pali Canon (Theravada), Mahayana sutras, Tibetan Book of the Dead
- **Practices**: meditation (vipassana/insight, samatha/calm), mindfulness, temple offerings, Buddha images as meditation aid (not worshipped as god)
- **Founded by**: Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha, "the awakened one") in India ~500 BCE

### Judaism (~15 million)
- **Core beliefs**: one God, covenant with Abraham and Moses, Torah revealed at Sinai, chosen people (not superiority — responsibility to follow God's laws), Messiah to come
  - 613 mitzvot (commandments) in Torah
- **Branches**: Orthodox (traditional, halakha/religious law binding), Conservative (less strict, tradition with adaptation), Reform (most liberal, individual autonomy)
- **Sacred text**: Torah (first 5 books of Moses — Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy), Talmud (rabbinic discussions/ laws, oral tradition), Tanakh (= Hebrew Bible, same as Christian Old Testament but different organization)
- **Practices**: Shabbat (Friday sunset to Saturday sunset — rest, no work, family dinner, synagogue), kosher dietary laws (kashrut — no pork/shellfish, no meat + dairy together, separate utensils), bar/bat mitzvah (coming of age at 13), holidays (Passover, Yom Kippur/Day of Atonement — holiest, fasting, Rosh Hashanah/New Year, Hanukkah)
- **History**: continuous since ~1800 BCE (Abraham), temple in Jerusalem destroyed 70 CE → diaspora. Zionism → Israel 1948. 6 million killed in Holocaust (1941-1945)

### Other & Indigenous
- **Sikhism** (25M): one God, equality of all (rejects caste system), community service, 5 K's (uncut hair, comb, steel bracelet, ceremonial shorts, sword)
  - Golden Temple in Amritsar (India), Guru Granth Sahib (sacred text), langar (free community meal for all, regardless of religion/caste)
- **Shinto** (Japan, 3-4M): indigenous, kami (spirits in nature), ancestor worship, rituals, shrine visits. No founder, no scripture
- **Taoism** (China, 20M+): Tao ("the way") — harmony with nature, simplicity, wu wei (effortless action). Yin/yang symbol. Lao Tzu, Tao Te Ching
- **Confucianism**: ethical/philosophical system, not religion (debatable) — filial piety (respect parents/ancestors), social harmony, education, ritual propriety
- **Jainism** (5M): extreme non-violence (ahimsa — sweep ground before walking, wear mask to avoid inhaling insects), asceticism, karma, many Hindus
- **Zoroastrianism** (150K): one of oldest monotheistic religions, Persia (Iran), good vs evil struggle, influenced Judaism/Christianity/Islam
- **New religious movements**: Baha'i (unity of all religions), Mormonism/LDS, Jehovah's Witnesses, Unitarian Universalism
- **Indigenous traditions**: Native American spirituality (land/ancestor-based, varied by tribe), African Traditional (Yoruba, Ifá, Vodun), Aboriginal Dreamtime, Shamanism (Siberia, Amazon, many cultures), animism

## Key Concepts Across Religions
- **Golden Rule**: "treat others as you would have them treat you" appears in every major religious tradition (Christianity, Judaism, Islam, Buddhism, Hinduism, Confucianism, Jainism, Taoism, Zoroastrianism, Baha'i)
- **Afterlife**: varies — reincarnation (Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism), heaven/hell (Christianity, Islam, Judaism not emphasized), resurrection (Christianity, Islam), ancestral spirit world (indigenous), non-existence/annihilation (some Buddhists view nirvana as complete end)
- **Religious syncretism**: blending of traditions — Santeria (African + Catholic), Candomblé, Voodoo, Buddhism in China + Taoism/Confucianism, folk Catholicism in Latin America
- **Secularization**: decreasing religious affiliation in developed countries — but global religion is growing (higher birth rates in religious countries). By 2050, Islam projected to approach Christianity in numbers
- **Atheism**: belief no god exists. **Agnosticism**: belief that existence of god is unknown/unknowable. ~16% of world = religiously unaffiliated (includes atheist, agnostic, "spiritual but not religious")
