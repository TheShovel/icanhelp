# Education Systems

## K-12 Education
- **Structure by grade**: Pre-K (optional), Kindergarten (age 5), Elementary (1-5/6), Middle (6-8/7-8), High (9-12). Varies by district — some use K-8 + 9-12, others K-6 + 7-9 + 10-12. Compulsory education: generally ages 6-18 (varies by state — some to 16, some to 17). Kindergarten: mandatory in ~20 states
- **Curriculum**: state sets standards (Common Core for English/math in 41 states + DC), district chooses curriculum/textbooks. Standard subjects: English, math, science, social studies/history, PE, sometimes arts, foreign language, computer science. High school: Carnegie units (credits) required for graduation — typically 22-26 credits. Exit exams (some states). Diploma types: standard, honors, advanced/IB
- **Typical school year**: ~180 days, August/September to May/June. Some year-round (balanced calendar — shorter breaks, more frequent). School day: ~6.5 hours (8am-3pm typical). Variations: 4-day week (rural districts), block scheduling (90 min classes), traditional periods (45-50 min)
- **Student support**: IEP (Individualized Education Program — special education), 504 plan (disability accommodations), English Language Learner (ELL) services, gifted/talented, counseling, Title I (low-income school funding). RTI (Response to Intervention) — tiered academic/behavioral support

## Types of Schools
- **Public**: funded by local property taxes + state/federal funds. Free, open to all in attendance zone. Run by elected school board + district administration. Must follow state laws: curriculum, testing, teacher certification. Some allow open enrollment/choice across district
- **Charter**: publicly funded, independently operated. Freed from some regulations (hiring, curriculum, schedule) in exchange for accountability (performance contract — charter can be revoked). Run by nonprofit, university, or sometimes for-profit management companies. Lottery if oversubscribed. Mixed research outcomes — quality varies widely
- **Private**: tuition-funded. Not bound by most state education regulations. Types: secular (independent), religious (Catholic, Protestant, Jewish, Islamic — most common), Montessori, Waldorf, military, special needs. Average tuition: $12,000/yr (religious) to $25,000+ (independent day). Boarding schools: $40,000-70,000. May offer scholarships/financial aid
- **Magnet**: public schools with specialized curriculum (STEM, arts, language immersion). Designed for voluntary desegregation. Application/audition required. Free — part of public system. Often high-performing
- **Homeschool**: parent-led education at home. Legal in all 50 states. Regulation varies: high-regulation (PA, NY — require curriculum approval, testing, home visits) to low/none (TX, IN, OK). Approaches: structured (curriculum packages) to unschooling (child-led). ~3% of US students (growing post-COVID)
- **Online/virtual**: K-12 online schools (public or private). Full-time or supplemental. COVID accelerated adoption. Concerns: screen time, socialization, engagement, quality. Research: less effective on average than in-person for most students, but good option for some (bullied, medical, traveling, advanced athletes/performers)

## Higher Education
- **Types**: research university (R1/R2 — PhD programs, heavy research), master's university (primarily master's), liberal arts college (undergraduate focus, small classes), community college (2-year associate degrees, transfers, certificates), technical/vocational institute (career training), HBCU (Historically Black Colleges/Universities), HSI (Hispanic-Serving), Tribal colleges
- **Degrees**: Associate (AA, AS — 2 years, community college), Bachelor's (BA, BS — 4 years), Master's (MA, MS, MBA — 1-2 years post-bachelor's), Doctoral (PhD, EdD, MD, JD — 3-7 years), Professional (MD, JD, DVM, PharmD). Certificate programs (short-term, specific skills). Post-doc (research after PhD)
- **College admissions**: high school GPA (most important), course rigor (AP/IB/honors), standardized tests (SAT/ACT — many now test-optional), extracurriculars, essays, letters of recommendation, demonstrated interest, legacy (less weight in recent years). Acceptance rates: highly selective (<10%), selective (10-30%), moderately selective (30-60%), open admission (community colleges). Deadlines: Early Decision (binding), Early Action (non-binding), Regular Decision, Rolling
- **Community college**: 2 years, low cost ($3,500/yr avg). Transfer pathway to 4-year (articulation agreements). Good for: saving money, testing interests, academic catch-up, associate degrees, career certificates (nursing, welding, IT). Reverse transfer: community college credits applied retroactively after 4-year transfer
- **Vocational/trade**: focused training for specific careers (electrician, plumbing, HVAC, welding, cosmetology, dental assistant, trucking). 6 months to 2 years. Lower cost than 4-year. High job placement rates. Apprenticeships: paid on-the-job training + classroom instruction (registered with Department of Labor). 800,000+ apprentices in US

## Financial Aid
- **FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid)**: required for federal and most state/institutional aid. Opens October 1 each year (now changing to December for 2024-25). Uses prior-prior year tax info. Student Aid Index (SAI, replaced EFC) determines need. Must file every year. FAFSA Simplification Act (2021): fewer questions, expanded Pell eligibility
- **Grants (free money)**: Pell Grant (federal, up to $7,395 for 2024 — need-based, undergraduate), FSEOG (federal supplemental, limited), state grants (vary — Cal Grant, NY TAP, Texas Grant), institutional grants (from colleges). TEACH Grant (for teaching in high-need areas — converts to loan if conditions unmet)
- **Scholarships (free money)**: merit (GPA, test scores), athletic, need-based, identity-based (first-gen, racial/ethnic, women in STEM), major-specific, community/volunteer, essay contests. Search: Fastweb, Scholarships.com, GoingMerry, College Board, local community foundations. Avoid: application fees (scams). Apply early, apply often — average student applies to 8-10
- **Loans (must repay)**: Federal Direct Subsidized (need-based, government pays interest while in school), Direct Unsubsidized (anyone, interest accrues immediately), Direct PLUS (parents/grad students, credit check). Federal Perkins (discontinued). Private loans (higher rates, fewer protections — avoid unless maxed federal). Loan forgiveness: Public Service Loan Forgiveness (10 years of nonprofit/government work), Teacher Loan Forgiveness (5 years), income-driven repayment plans (SAVE, PAYE, IBR — 10-20% income for 20-25 years, then forgiven)
- **Work-study**: federal program, part-time job on/off campus (community service focus). Wages at least federal minimum. Earnings don't count against SAI. Apply via FAFSA. Not guaranteed — limited funds
- **529 plans**: state-sponsored tax-advantaged investment accounts for education. Earnings grow tax-free (federal + state) when used for qualified expenses (tuition, fees, room/board, computers, K-12 up to $10k/yr). Can transfer between beneficiaries. No income limit. High contribution limits.

## Study Abroad
- **Types**: semester/year abroad (immersive, earning credits at host university), summer programs (short, intensive, often themed), faculty-led (group with home professor), exchange (direct swap with foreign university — pay home tuition), third-party provider (CIEE, API, IES, SIT — more support, higher cost), direct enrollment (apply directly to foreign university), internship/research abroad
- **Cost**: tuition (same as home for exchange, plus for programs), airfare, housing, meals, visa fees, insurance, personal expenses. Financial aid often applies (check policy). Scholarships: Gilman (Pell recipients), Boren (national security languages, service requirement), Fulbright (graduate), DAAD (Germany), individual program scholarships. Host country may offer lower cost of living
- **Planning**: start 6-12 months before. GPA requirement (often 2.5-3.0 min), passport (renew if <6 months validity), visa (some take 3+ months), housing (dorm/homestay/apartment), health insurance (international coverage), course registration approval (ensure credits transfer), immunizations, bank/phone setup, emergency contacts. Orientation: cultural adjustment, safety, academics
- **Language**: programs in English available in non-English countries. Language immersion programs available (may require proficiency). ESL programs for language improvement
- **Benefits**: cultural competence, language skills, independence, career differentiation, personal growth. 10% higher graduation rates, higher starting salary. Some programs offer research/internship component. Best outcome: long-term, deep immersion (not island programs — programs where you mainly interact with Americans abroad)

## Online Learning
- **Major platforms**: Coursera (university courses, specializations, degrees — paid certificates), edX (nonprofit, MIT/Harvard founded — audit free, certificates paid), Khan Academy (K-12, free), Udacity (tech/nanodegrees, paid), Udemy (any topic, frequent sales), Skillshare (creative, subscription), LinkedIn Learning (professional, included with Premium), Duolingo (languages, freemium), Brilliant (math/science, subscription). Many offer financial aid
- **Formats**: self-paced (start anytime, complete on own schedule), cohort-based (fixed start, structured schedule, peer interaction), live online (synchronous classes via Zoom), hybrid (mix in-person + online). MOOCs: Massive Open Online Courses — large enrollment, some with live sessions, peer grading, forums
- **Credentials**: MOOC certificates (course-level, from platform), Specializations (series of courses + capstone — Coursera), MicroMasters (graduate-level series — edX, can count toward degree), Professional Certificates (job-ready skills — Google IT, IBM Data Science), Nanodegrees (Udacity), online degrees (fully online bachelor's/master's — often cheaper, flexible). Employer recognition varies — Google/IBM certs well-regarded in tech
- **Benefits**: flexible schedule, lower cost, accessibility, wide selection, learn from top universities, repeat material. Drawbacks: requires self-discipline, less networking, fewer hands-on components, completion rates low (~5-15%), varies in quality
- **Accessibility**: captions (many auto-generated), transcripts, screen reader support, downloadable materials. Good platforms offer accommodations. Learner forums for peer support

## Special Education
- **IDEA (Individuals with Disabilities Education Act)**: federal law (1975, revised). Guarantees Free Appropriate Public Education (FAPE) in Least Restrictive Environment (LRE) for eligible children (birth-21). 13 disability categories (specific learning disability, speech/language, autism, emotional disturbance, intellectual disability, deaf/hard of hearing, blind/visually impaired, orthopedic impairment, other health impairment (incl. ADHD), traumatic brain injury, multiple disabilities, deaf-blindness). Child Find: schools must identify and evaluate all children with disabilities
- **IEP (Individualized Education Program)**: legal document developed annually by team (parents, teachers, specialists, administrator). Includes: present levels, annual goals (measurable), services (speech, OT, PT, counseling, etc.), accommodations/modifications, participation in standardized testing, transition plan (by age 16). Parents can request IEP meeting anytime. Dispute resolution: mediation, due process hearing, complaint to state education agency. Parent rights: Prior Written Notice, independent educational evaluation (IEE) at public expense if disagree with school evaluation
- **504 Plan**: Section 504 of Rehabilitation Act (1973). For students with disability who do NOT qualify for IEP (or need accommodations but not specialized instruction). Broader eligibility than IDEA (not tied to categories). Accommodations examples: extra time on tests, preferential seating, breaks, reduced homework, behavioral supports. Fewer procedural protections than IEP, but still enforced by Office for Civil Rights
- **LRE (Least Restrictive Environment)**: continuum of placements — general education classroom (with/without supports), resource room (pull-out for specific subjects), separate classroom (self-contained), separate school, residential, home/hospital. Must educate with non-disabled peers to maximum extent appropriate. "Inclusion": placing students with disabilities in general education with appropriate supports — research shows improved outcomes
- **Early intervention**: Part C of IDEA — services for infants/toddlers (birth-3) with developmental delays. IFSP (Individualized Family Service Plan). Services at home, daycare, or center — speech, physical, occupational therapy. Transition at age 3 to Part B (preschool special education)
