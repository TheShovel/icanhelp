# Mythology & Folklore

## Mythological Archetypes
- **Creation myths**: every culture explains how world began — Cosmic Egg (Hindu, Chinese, Finnish), Earth Diver (creation from underwater — many Native American, Siberian), World Parents (sky father + earth mother separated — Greek Ouranos+Gaia, Egyptian Nut+Geb, Maori Rangi+Papa), Ex Nihilo/from nothing (Abrahamic: God creates from nothing, Egyptian Atum self-creates)
  - Flood myths: nearly universal (Noah/Abrahamic, Deucalion/Greece, Manu/India, Utnapishtim/Mesopotamia — likely originated from ancient catastrophic floods in various regions)
- **Hero's journey** (Monomyth — Joseph Campbell): call to adventure, refusal, supernatural aid, crossing threshold, trials, approach, ordeal, reward, road back, resurrection, return with elixir
  - Examples: Theseus and Minotaur, Heracles 12 labors, Jason and Argonauts, Perseus, Gilgamesh, and many modern stories (Star Wars, Harry Potter, Lord of the Rings)
- **Trickster**: disrupts order, breaks rules, brings change — Loki (Norse), Coyote (Native American), Anansi (West African/Caribbean spider), Hermes (Greek), Reynard (European fox), Maui (Polynesian), Eshu (Yoruba), Sun Wukong/Monkey King (Chinese)
  - Tricksters often bring knowledge/benefits to humans (Prometheus stole fire, Maui fished up islands, Coyote stole fire)

## Greek & Roman Mythology
- **Primordial**: Chaos → Gaia (Earth) + Ouranos (Sky) → Titans (Cronus + Rhea) → Olympian gods (Zeus + siblings)
  - Titans: Oceanus, Hyperion, Themis, Iapetus, Atlas (holds up sky), Prometheus (created humans, stole fire, chained to rock — eagle eats liver daily)
  - Giants: born from Gaia's blood when Ouranos castrated — fought Olympians (Gigantomachy)
- **Olympians**: Zeus/Jupiter (king, lightning, sky), Hera/Juno (queen, marriage), Poseidon/Neptune (sea, earthquakes, horses), Hades/Pluto (underworld, dead), Demeter/Ceres (agriculture, harvest), Athena/Minerva (wisdom, warfare — born from Zeus's head), Apollo (sun, music, prophecy, medicine), Artemis/Diana (hunt, moon, twin of Apollo), Ares/Mars (war), Aphrodite/Venus (love, beauty — born from sea foam, married to Hephaestus), Hephaestus/Vulcan (forge, fire, crafts — lame, created Pandora), Hermes/Mercury (messenger, travelers, thieves, psychopomp — leads souls to underworld), Dionysus/Bacchus (wine, ecstasy, theater), Hestia/Vesta (hearth, home)
- **Underworld**: Styx (river of oath, boundary), ferryman Charon (pays obol coin under tongue), Cerberus (3-headed dog), judges (Minos, Rhadamanthus, Aeacus), Tartarus (punishment), Elysium (rewards), Asphodel Meadows (neutral). Orpheus descends to retrieve Eurydice (looked back)
- **Heroes**: Perseus (Medusa, saved Andromeda), Theseus (Minotaur, labyrinth — Ariadne's thread), Heracles/Hercules (12 labors — Hydra, Nemean Lion, Augean Stables, Cerberus), Jason (Argonauts, Golden Fleece — Medea helps then vengeance), Odysseus (Odyssey — Trojan War veteran, 10-year journey home, Cyclops, Sirens, Circe, Calypso), Achilles (Trojan War, invulnerable except heel — concept of "Achilles heel"), Hector (Trojan prince, killed by Achilles)
- **Trojan War**: Eris ("for the fairest" — golden apple), Paris judges Aphrodite (most beautiful woman: Helen of Sparta), abduction → war. Trojan Horse (Odysseus' idea). Aeneas escapes Troy → founds Roman ancestry (Virgil's Aeneid)

## Norse Mythology
- **Cosmology**: 9 realms on World Tree Yggdrasil — Asgard (gods, Valhalla), Midgard (humans), Jotunheim (giants), Vanaheim (Vanir gods), Alfheim (elves), Svartalfheim/Muspelheim (fire giants), Niflheim (ice/mist, Hel — realm of dead), Helheim (dishonorable dead)
  - Creation: Ymir (primeval giant) killed → body becomes world. First humans: Ask and Embla (from two trees)
- **Gods**: Odin (All-Father, wisdom, war, poetry — sacrificed eye for wisdom, hung on Yggdrasil 9 days for runes, ravens Huginn+Muninn/Thought+Memory, Sleipnir 8-legged horse), Thor (thunder, strength, hammer Mjölnir — goats draw chariot), Freyja (love, beauty, fertility, war — half of slain go to her hall Fólkvangr), Freyr (fertility, peace, harvest), Baldr (beautiful, loved, killed by mistletoe — Loki's trick), Tyr (war, justice — one hand (Fenrir bit off)), Heimdall (watchman, Gjallarhorn — blows at Ragnarok), Loki (trickster, shape-shifter, NOT necessarily evil — father of Hel, Fenrir, Jörmungandr — causes Baldr's death, bound with serpent's venom)
- **Ragnarok**: prophecied end of world — Surtr (fire giant) burns, Fenrir swallows Odin, Thor kills Jörmungandr but dies from poison, Loki + Heimdall kill each other, world sinks into sea, then rises anew (Líf and Lífþrasir survive)
  - Valhalla: half of warriors who die in battle go here (Odin's hall), train for Ragnarok. Other half: Fólkvangr (Freyja). Dishonorable dead: Hel (cold, dark)

## Egyptian Mythology
- **Creation**: Atum (self-created) → Shu (air) + Tefnut (moisture) → Geb (earth) + Nut (sky) → Osiris, Isis, Set, Nephthys
- **Important gods**: Ra/Re (sun god — travels through underworld at night, fought Apophis/serpent, various forms: Khepri morning, Ra midday, Atum evening)
  - Osiris (god of underworld, resurrection — killed by Set, resurrected by Isis, became lord of dead). Isis (magic, motherhood — most powerful, revived Osiris, protected Horus). Set/Seth (chaos, desert, storms — killed Osiris). Horus (falcon, sky — son of Isis+Osiris, lost eye fighting Set → restored → symbol of protection/ healing "Eye of Horus", pharaohs = Horus incarnate). Anubis (jackal-headed, embalming, weighing of heart — guides dead to judgment). Thoth (ibis-headed, writing, wisdom, magic — recorded judgment). Ma'at (goddess of truth, justice, cosmic order — feather weighed against heart)
- **Afterlife**: Book of the Dead (spells + instructions for afterlife). Weighing of heart against feather of Ma'at — if heart lighter = enter Field of Reeds (paradise with same life as on Earth, but perfect). If heavier = devoured by Ammit (hippo-lion-crocodile hybrid)
  - Mummification preserved body (Ka/soul needed physical basis). Elaborate tombs (pyramids, Valley of the Kings) with grave goods

## Mesopotamian Mythology
- **Sumerian/Akkadian/Babylonian/Assyrian** — oldest recorded myths (3rd millennium BCE)
- **Gods**: Anu (sky, supreme), Enlil (air, king of gods — separated heaven+earth, sent flood), Enki/Ea (wisdom, fresh water, crafts — warned Utnapishtim of flood, created humans to serve gods), Inanna/Ishtar (love, war, sex — descended to underworld, killed, resurrected; goddess of contradictions). Marduk (Babylon chief god — killed Tiamat/primeval sea monster, created world from her body, became chief later)
  - Ishtar's descent: stripped at each of 7 gates, hung dead for 3 days, resurrected by Enki's emissary — seasonal dying-and-rising god motif
- **Epic of Gilgamesh**: earliest surviving great work of literature (~2100 BCE). King Gilgamesh (demigod, 2/3 god 1/3 human, tyrant → hero) + Enkidu (wild man created by gods as balance, becomes closest friend)
  - After Enkidu's death, Gilgamesh seeks immortality — meets Utnapishtim (Mesopotamian Noah, survived flood). Learns immortality is for gods only. Returns home, appreciates what he has. Utnapishtim's flood story predates Biblical flood account

## Hindu Mythology
- **Trimurti**: Brahma (creator, 4 heads, not widely worshipped), Vishnu (preserver, protector — 10 avatars including Rama and Krishna), Shiva (destroyer + regenerator — third eye, Ganga in hair, Nataraja dance of creation/destruction, lingam symbol)
  - Vishnu avatars: Matsya (fish saves Manu from flood), Kurma (tortoise supports mountain), Varaha (boar lifts Earth from cosmic ocean), Narasimha (half-man half-lion kills demon Hiranyakashipu), Vamana (dwarf, 3 steps cover universe), Parashurama (warrior with axe), Rama (Ramayana epic), Krishna (Mahabharata, Bhagavad Gita), Buddha (some traditions), Kalki (future, horse-riding, yet to come)
- **Devas**: gods, live in heaven (Svarga). Asuras: anti-gods, constantly battle devas. Churning of ocean of milk: devas + asuras churn cosmic ocean for amrita (immortality nectar) — produces: Lakshmi (goddess of wealth, Vishnu's consort), Apsaras (celestial dancers), poison (Shiva drinks it, throat turns blue — Neelakantha "blue-throated")
- **Epics**: Ramayana (Rama rescues Sita from Ravana demon-king with help of Hanuman monkey-god — epic battle journey, symbols of dharma/duty/devotion)
  - Mahabharata (largest epic poem — ~200,000 verses, 18 books). Bhagavad Gita = philosophical dialogue within — Krishna advises Arjuna (warrior) about duty, dharma, yoga, devotion, right action without attachment
  - Kurukshetra War: Pandavas vs Kauravas (family feud). Every warrior chooses side, world's armies involved

## Other Notable Mythologies
- **Japanese**: Kami (spirits of nature/ancestors — Shinto). Izanagi + Izanami (create Japanese islands). Amaterasu (sun goddess, imperial family descended from her, hid in cave → dance brought her out). Susano'o (storm god, killed Yamata-no-Orochi 8-headed serpent, found Kusanagi sword). Tsukuyomi (moon god)
- **Celtic**: Otherworld (Tír na nÓg — land of youth, fairies/sidhe). Cú Chulainn (Irish hero, warp spasm battle frenzy, defends Ulster single-handed). Tuatha Dé Danann (old Irish gods, defeated by Milesians → retreated to Otherworld)
  - Arthurian legend: King Arthur (Round Table, knights, Camelot, Excalibur sword in stone/Nineveh lake), Merlin (wizard), Lancelot + Guinevere (tragic love), Grail quest
- **Aztec**: Huitzilopochtli (sun + war god). Quetzalcoatl (feathered serpent, wind, knowledge, civilization). Tezcatlipoca (smoking mirror, fate). Tlaloc (rain). Mictlantecuhtli (underworld). World created and destroyed 5 times (5 suns). Human sacrifice = feeding the sun
  - Key myth: bones of ancestors retrieved from underworld by Quetzalcoatl, ground and sprinkled with his blood → humans created
- **Chinese**: Pangu (cosmic egg, creates world — grows 10ft/day for 18,000 years, body becomes features). Nüwa (snake-bodied goddess, repairs sky after battle, creates humans from mud — modeling by hand = rich, flung mud = poor/commoner). Monkey King/Sun Wukong (Journey to the West — Taoist+Buddhist, rebel against heaven, powerful, trickster)
  - Eight Immortals (Daoist legendary figures, each with unique power/object), Cowherd and Weaver Girl (star-crossed lovers, separated by Milky Way, meet once/year on magpie bridge — Qixi festival)
