# World Literature & Great Books

## Epic Poetry

### The Epic of Gilgamesh (~2100 BCE, Mesopotamia)
- Oldest surviving great work of literature (Sumerian, 12 tablets, cuneiform). Discovered 1853 at Nineveh by Hormuzd Rassam. Some tablets missing; reconstructed from fragments across Near East
- **Story**: Gilgamesh (king of Uruk, 2/3 god, 1/3 human, tyrant → hero). Gods create Enkidu (wild man) as balance; after fighting, they become close friends. After Enkidu's death, Gilgamesh quests for immortality. Meets Utnapishtim (Mesopotamian Noah, survivor of Great Flood). Lesson: immortality is for gods; humans must find meaning in mortal life, relationships, and legacy. Key quotes: "The life that you are seeking you will never find. When the gods created man they allotted him death, but life they retained in their own keeping."

### The Iliad & The Odyssey (Homer, ~750 BCE, Greece)
- **Iliad**: Trojan War — Achilles' anger (wrath at Agamemnon for taking his war prize Briseis), Patroclus's death, Hector's death. Epic similes, heroic code, kleos (glory). Trojan Horse, fate, mortality. Composed in dactylic hexameter. 15,693 lines
  - Characters: Achilles (invulnerable except heel — "Achilles heel". Best warrior, rage). Hector (Trojan prince, family man, killed by Achilles). Patroclus (Achilles' companion, wears his armor into battle, killed by Hector → Achilles returns to war)
- **Odyssey**: Odysseus returns from Trojan War — 10-year journey home (1 year actual travel, 9 years stuck). Cyclops (Polyphemus — Odysseys says "Nobody" as his name), Sirens (bind self to mast), Circe (turns men to pigs), Calypso (holds him 7 years), Scylla + Charybdis (between a rock + a hard place), Penelope (faithful wife weaving + unraveling). Themes: cunning > strength, nostos (homecoming), hubris (Odysseus taunts Cyclops after escape → Poseidon curses him)
  - Structure: in medias res (starts in middle). Frame narrative (Odysseus telling his adventure at Phaeacian court, Book 9-12). Telemachy: first 4 books focus on Telemachus, his journey to learn about father

### The Aeneid (Virgil, 19 BCE, Rome)
- Aeneas (Trojan prince, son of Anchises + Venus) flees Troy after fall → journeys to Italy → founds Roman line (Romulus + Remus descendants). Written to glorify Rome under Augustus. "Arms and the man I sing." Dido + Aeneas (tragic love, she kills herself when he leaves → eternal enmity between Carthage + Rome). Descent to underworld (Book 6: Aeneas sees future Roman heroes parade before him — Augustus, Romulus, etc.). Pietas: duty to the gods, family, nation. Key: "Flectere si nequeo superos, Acheronta movebo" (If I cannot move Heaven, I will raise Hell)

### Divine Comedy (Dante Alighieri, 1320, Italy)
- Allegorical journey through Hell (Inferno), Purgatory (Purgatorio), Paradise (Paradiso). 3 cantiche, 33 cantos each + 1 intro = 100 total. Terza rima (interlocking rhyme scheme: ABA BCB CDC). Written in Tuscan Italian (not Latin) — elevated vernacular, established modern Italian
  - Virgil guides through Inferno/Purgatorio (Human Reason). Beatrice leads through Paradiso (Divine Love). Hell: 9 circles descending (Limbo, Lust, Gluttony, Greed, Wrath, Heresy, Violence, Fraud, Treachery — Satan in center of Earth, frozen in ice chewing three arch-traitors: Judas, Brutus, Cassius). Purgatory: 7 terraces (Seven Deadly Sins). Paradise: concentric spheres of heaven
  - Famous: "Abandon all hope, ye who enter here" (Inferno, Gate of Hell). Circle 2: Paolo + Francesca (eternally blown by winds of passion — "Galeotto was the book and he who wrote it: that day we read in it no further" — they stopped reading Lancelot and kissed). Count Ugolino: gnawing on enemy's head for eternity

## Novels

### Don Quixote (Cervantes, 1605-1615, Spain)
- First modern novel? (considered). Alonso Quijano reads too many chivalric romances → goes mad → becomes Don Quixote, knight-errant, with Sancho Panza (peasant squire). Windmills = giants (he charges them). Dulcinea (peasant girl idealized as princess). Interpolated stories, metafiction (Part 2: characters have read Part 1). Purpose: parody of romance, commentary on illusion vs reality. Themes: idealism vs pragmatism, madness vs sanity

### Pride and Prejudice (Jane Austen, 1813, England)
- Elizabeth Bennet (intelligent, witty, prejudice) + Mr. Darcy (proud, reserved truly good). Opening: "It is a truth universally acknowledged, that a single man in possession of a good fortune, must be in want of a wife." Social commentary (marriage, class, gender). Irony, free indirect discourse. Other novels: Sense and Sensibility, Emma, Mansfield Park, Persuasion, Northanger Abbey. Stays primarily domestic — comedy of manners

### Moby-Dick (Herman Melville, 1851, USA)
- Ishmael (narrator), Captain Ahab (monomaniacal pursuit of white whale). Opens: "Call me Ishmael." Queequeg (Polynesian harpooneer). Chapters alternate: narrative + whaling encyclopedia (science of whaling — classification, anatomy, ecology, economics, ship customs). Ahab: lost leg to Moby Dick → pursues with "'pequod' — ship as microcosm of multi-ethnic American crew)"
  - Themes: obsession, fate vs free will, good vs evil, the nature of the universe, truth vs appearance. Unsustainable life: "All visible objects, man, are but as pasteboard masks." Whale = inscrutable mystery of existence. Ending: "And only the orphaned boat's crew escaped to tell the tale — carried off on a coffin buoy." Commercial failure in Melville's lifetime; rediscovered 1920s as great American novel

### Crime and Punishment (Fyodor Dostoevsky, 1866, Russia)
- Raskolnikov (impoverished St. Petersburg student) murders pawnbroker + her sister. Theory: extraordinary men (Napoleon) are above conventional morality → can commit crimes for higher purpose (his: use money to do good). After: paranoia, guilt, isolation (can't connect with anyone — even family, friend Razumikhin, detective Porfiry). Sonechka Marmeladova (prostitute with Christian faith — Sonya the pale thin girl in yellow dress) urges confession
  - Themes: redemption through suffering (Orthodox Christian), rationalism vs faith, alienation in modern city, psychological realism. Ends: Raskolnikov confesses, sent to Siberia; Sonya follows. His redemption begins

### Anna Karenina (Leo Tolstoy, 1878, Russia)
- Parallel stories: Anna (aristocratic, married to Karenin, falls in love with Vronsky → society rejects → despair → train suicide). Levin (landowner, searches for meaning in family + faith + agricultural work with peasants). Opens: "All happy families are alike; each unhappy family is unhappy in its own way." Levin = Tolstoy's alter ego (philosophical searches, marriage to Kitty)
  - Themes: love vs duty, social hypocrisy, meaning of life, agrarian vs urban life. Anna chooses passion over convention → isolated, jealous, eventually destroyed. Key: Anna on the train after reconciliation with Vronsky — paranoia, thinks all men look at her, "vengence is mine, I will repay" (Epigraph: God's justice, not human). Last chapters: Levin on his porch, watching stars, content with faith + daily life

### The Great Gatsby (F. Scott Fitzgerald, 1925, USA)
- Jay Gatsby (self-made millionaire, mysterious past) in love with Daisy Buchanan (married to Tom — wealthy old money). Narrator: Nick Carraway. Jazz Age (1920s), Long Island, East Egg vs West Egg. The green light at end of Daisy's dock (Gatsby reaching for it = reaching for dream)
  - Themes: American Dream, class (old money vs new), wealth and corruption, the past (can't repeat the past? "Can't repeat the past? Of course you can!"). Told in 9 chapters. Ends: Gatsby shot by George Wilson (Myrtle's husband). Nobody attends funeral except Nick + Gatsby's father. Nick renounces the East: "We beat on, boats against the current, borne back ceaselessly into the past"

### One Hundred Years of Solitude (Gabriel García Márquez, 1967, Colombia)
- **Magic realism** (fantastical elements treated as normal). Story of Macondo (fictional Colombian town) + Buendía family across 7 generations. José Arcadio Buendía, Úrsula Iguarán, Colonel Aureliano Buendía (32 failed uprisings), Remedios the Beauty (ascends to heaven while hanging laundry). Plague of insomnia + forgetfulness (need signs naming everything). The gypsy Melquíades' prophecies — the family history revealed in Sanskrit manuscripts, translated at the end by last Buendía

### 1984 (George Orwell, 1949, England)
- **Dystopia** — Oceania, Big Brother (face on posters "Big Brother is watching you"). Newspeak (language designed to reduce thought: doublethink, thoughtcrime). INGSOC (English Socialism). Winston Smith works at Ministry of Truth (rewriting history). Love affair with Julia. Room 101 (worst fear). Ministry of Love, O'Brien (agent of Thought Police). Ends: "He loved Big Brother." Themes: totalitarianism, surveillance, truth, language control, psychological manipulation
  - Parallel: Animal Farm (1945, allegory of Russian Revolution — "All animals are equal, but some are more equal than others")

### To Kill a Mockingbird (Harper Lee, 1960, USA)
- Scout Finch (narrator, 6-year-old girl), Atticus Finch (lawyer, defending Tom Robinson, Black man wrongly accused of raping white woman in 1930s Alabama). Boo Radley (neighbor recluse, emerges to save children at end). "Mockingbirds": innocent people harmed (Tom Robinson killed, Boo Radley would be hurt by publicity). "Shoot all the bluejays you want, if you can hit 'em, but remember it's a sin to kill a mockingbird." Scout's first day of school, the mob at jailhouse, the trial, the final walk home. Themes: racism, justice, empathy (walk in someone's skin), moral courage
