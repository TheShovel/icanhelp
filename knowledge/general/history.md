# World History (Major Events & Eras)

## Prehistory (before 3000 BCE)
- Homo sapiens emerged ~300,000 years ago in Africa
- Out of Africa migration (~70,000-50,000 BCE): humans spread across globe
- Agricultural Revolution (~10,000 BCE): farming → settlements → civilization
  - Domestication of plants (wheat, rice, corn) and animals (goats, cattle, pigs)
  - Permanent settlements: Jericho (9000 BCE), Çatalhöyük (7500 BCE)
- Neolithic: polished stone tools, pottery, weaving, first villages, earliest religious sites (Göbekli Tepe ~9600 BCE)

## Ancient Civilizations (3000 BCE - 500 CE)
- **Mesopotamia** (Iraq): Sumer (first cities, writing cuneiform ~3200 BCE, wheel), Akkad, Babylon (Hammurabi's Code ~1754 BCE), Assyria
- **Ancient Egypt**: pharaohs, pyramids (Great Pyramid ~2560 BCE), hieroglyphics, papyrus, unified ~3100 BCE, fell to Rome 30 BCE
- **Indus Valley** (Pakistan/India): Harappa, Mohenjo-Daro — advanced urban planning, plumbing, standardized weights, writing indecipherable
- **Ancient China**: Xia, Shang (oracle bones, bronze), Zhou (Mandate of Heaven, Confucius ~500 BCE, Taoism), Qin (first emperor, Great Wall began, Terracotta Army), Han (Silk Road, paper invented)
- **Classical Greece**: city-states (Athens, Sparta), democracy (Athens ~508 BCE), philosophy (Socrates, Plato, Aristotle), Alexander the Great (~336-323 BCE, conquered Persia to India)
- **Roman Republic/Empire**: republic (509-27 BCE) → empire (27 BCE-476 CE). Law, roads, aqueducts, Latin, spread Christianity. Split into East/West. Fall of West Rome 476 CE (often cited as end of ancient era)
- **Persian Empire** (Iran): largest empire of ancient world (~550 BCE under Cyrus the Great) — tolerance of conquered peoples (first human rights charter, Cyrus Cylinder)
- **Mesoamerica**: Olmec (first major civilization, colossal heads), Maya (calendar, writing, mathematics, cities like Tikal and Chichen Itza)
- **Ancient India**: Maurya Empire (Ashoka, Buddhism), Gupta Empire (Golden Age, mathematics: zero invented, astronomy, medicine)

## Middle Ages / Medieval (500 - 1500 CE)
- **Byzantine Empire**: Eastern Rome continued 1,000 more years (capital Constantinople/Istanbul). Justinian's Code, preserved Greek/Roman knowledge. Fell 1453 to Ottomans
- **Islam's Golden Age** (750-1258): scientific, cultural, economic flourishing in Middle East, Spain, North Africa. Algebra, medicine (Avicenna), astronomy, preservation of Greek texts
  - Abbasid Caliphate: Baghdad as world's knowledge center (House of Wisdom)
  - Islamic Spain (Al-Andalus): coexistence of Muslims, Christians, Jews
- **Middle Ages in Europe**: feudalism (king → lords → knights → peasants), manorialism, Catholic Church dominated
  - Charlemagne (800 CE crowned Emperor), Viking age (793-1066), Norman conquest of England (1066)
  - Crusades (1096-1291): religious wars over Holy Land
  - Magna Carta (1215): limited king's power, due process, influenced modern law
  - Black Death (1347-1351): killed 30-60% of Europe's population → labor shortages → serfdom decline
  - Hundred Years' War (1337-1453): England vs France
- **Mongol Empire** (1206-1368): largest contiguous land empire (Genghis Khan). Connected East and West via trade routes — facilitated tech/culture exchange (gunpowder, paper, printing from China)
- **Renaissance** (14th-17th century Europe): rebirth of classical learning, art (Da Vinci, Michelangelo, Raphael), science (Copernicus, Galileo), printing press (Gutenberg ~1450, revolutionized information)
- **Mali Empire** (West Africa): wealthy from gold trade, Mansa Musa (~1324) — richest person in history, built Timbuktu as learning center

## Early Modern (1500 - 1800)
- **Age of Exploration**: Columbus (1492 to Americas), Vasco da Gama (1498 to India by sea), Magellan (circumnavigation 1519-1522)
  - Colonialism: European powers colonized Americas, Africa, Asia — extracted resources, enslaved people, spread Christianity
  - Transatlantic slave trade (16th-19th centuries): 12+ million Africans forcibly transported to Americas
- **Reformation** (1517): Martin Luther's 95 Theses → Protestant break from Catholic Church → religious wars in Europe (Thirty Years' War 1618-1648)
- **Age of Enlightenment** (18th century): reason, individual rights, science, separation of church and state. Thinkers: Locke (natural rights), Rousseau (social contract), Voltaire (free speech), Montesquieu (separation of powers)
- **American Revolution** (1775-1783): 13 colonies became US — Declaration of Independence (1776), Constitution (1787)
- **French Revolution** (1789-1799): monarchy overthrown → Reign of Terror → Napoleon (Emperor, conquered Europe, defeated at Waterloo 1815)
- **Scientific Revolution**: Copernicus (heliocentric), Newton (gravity, physics), Bacon (scientific method), Harvey (circulation of blood)

## Modern Era (1800 - Present)
- **Industrial Revolution** (1760-1840 in Britain, spread globally): steam engine, factories, railways, mass production → urbanization, labor movements, capitalism vs communism debate
- **Nationalism & Empire**: unification of Italy (1861) and Germany (1871). European "Scramble for Africa" (1880s-1914). Opium Wars (China forced open by Britain). Meiji Restoration (Japan industrializes rapidly)
- **World War I** (1914-1918): 20+ million dead. Trench warfare, chemical weapons. Trigger: assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand. Ended with Treaty of Versailles (harsh on Germany → shaped WWII)
  - Russian Revolution (1917): monarchy overthrown → USSR (communist, 1917-1991)
- **Great Depression** (1929-1939): global economic collapse → unemployment 25% in US → New Deal, rise of fascism
- **World War II** (1939-1945): 70-85 million dead (most in human history). Holocaust (6M Jews killed). Nuclear bombs (Hiroshima/Nagasaki). Ended with Allied victory. Divided world into US-led West and USSR-led East (Cold War)
  - United Nations founded (1945) to prevent future wars
- **Cold War** (1947-1991): nuclear arms race, proxy wars (Korea 1950-53, Vietnam 1955-75, Afghanistan 1979-89), space race (USSR: Sputnik 1957, Gagarin 1961; US: moon landing 1969), Berlin Wall (1961-1989)
- **Decolonization** (1945-1975): India 1947 (Gandhi's nonviolent resistance), African nations mainly 1950s-60s, Indochina wars. Many new nations struggled with borders drawn by colonizers
- **Civil Rights Movement** (US 1950s-60s): MLK, Rosa Parks, civil rights legislation. Apartheid South Africa fell 1994 (Mandela)
- **Fall of Soviet Union** (1991): USSR dissolved, Eastern Europe independent — end of Cold War
- **Digital Revolution** (1970s-present): personal computers (Apple, IBM PC 1981), internet (ARPANET 1969 → public 1990s), smartphones (iPhone 2007), social media, AI
- **9/11** (2001): terrorist attacks on US → War on Terror, Iraq War (2003-2011), Afghanistan (2001-2021)
- **21st century trends**: globalization, climate change crisis, social media revolution, COVID-19 pandemic (2020-2023, 7M+ deaths officially), AI explosion (ChatGPT 2022), rising authoritarianism, wealth inequality
