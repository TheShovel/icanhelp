# Medieval History

Major events, structures, and developments of the Middle Ages (c. 500-1500 CE).

## Early Middle Ages (500-1000)
- **Fall of Rome aftermath**: Western Europe fragmented into Germanic kingdoms — Franks (Gaul), Visigoths (Spain), Ostrogoths (Italy), Anglo-Saxons (Britain). Manorialism (self-sufficient estate), feudalism developing. Loss of central authority, trade decline, urban depopulation
- **Byzantine Empire**: Eastern Roman Empire continued. Justinian (527-565) — reconquered Italy/N Africa, codified Roman law (Corpus Juris Civilis). Hagia Sophia built (537). Greek replaced Latin. Theme system (military provinces). Iconoclasm controversy (726-843 — destruction of religious images). Macedonian Renaissance (867-1056)
- **Rise of Islam**: Muhammad (570-632) — revelations → Quran. Rashidun Caliphate (632-661) conquered Arabia, Persia, Levant, Egypt. Umayyad (661-750) — Spain to India. Abbasid (750-1258) — Baghdad, Golden Age of Islam (science, math, medicine, philosophy, translation of Greek works). House of Wisdom. Islamic Spain (Al-Andalus, 711-1492) — Cordoba center of learning
- **Carolingian Empire**: Franks under Charlemagne (768-814) — conquered much of W Europe. Crowned Emperor 800 (Holy Roman Empire). Carolingian Renaissance (revival of learning, monastic schools). Treaty of Verdun (843) divided empire into 3 kingdoms
- **Vikings** (793-1066): Scandinavian raiders/traders/colonizers. Longships (flexible, shallow draft). Reached Russia (Rus'), Constantinople (Varangian Guard), Iceland, Greenland, Vinland (North America, c. 1000). Norse mythology, sagas. Christianization ended Viking Age

## High Middle Ages (1000-1300)
- **Feudalism**: Pyramid: King → Greater Lords (dukes, counts) → Lesser Lords (barons, knights) → Peasants/serfs. Land = fief in exchange for military service. Manor: lord's demesne + peasant strips. Three-field system (crop rotation improved yields)
- **Norman Conquest (1066)**: William of Normandy defeated Harold Godwinson at Hastings. Brought Norman feudalism to England. Domesday Book (1086 — survey of all land/holdings)
- **Crusades** (1095-1291): Pope Urban II called for reclaiming Holy Land from Muslims. First Crusade (1099) — captured Jerusalem. Crusader states. Saladin recaptured Jerusalem (1187). Third Crusade (Richard Lionheart vs Saladin). Fourth Crusade (1204) — sacked Constantinople. Children's Crusade (1212 — tragedy). Effects: trade increase, knowledge transfer (Islamic science), weakened Byzantium, heightened religious tension
- **Magna Carta (1215)**: English barons forced King John to sign — limited royal power, due process, rule of law. Basis for constitutional law. "No taxation without representation"
- **Medieval Church**: Pope supreme authority (Gregorian Reform, Investiture Controversy — Pope vs Emperor). Cathedral schools → universities (Bologna 1088, Paris 1150, Oxford 1167). Scholasticism (Thomas Aquinas — Summa Theologica, reconciling Aristotle + Christianity). Mendicant orders (Franciscans, Dominicans). Heresy (Cathars/Albigensians — crusade against them). Inquisition established (1231)
- **Warfare developments**: Chain mail → plate armor (full plate by 1400). Longbow (English victories at Crécy 1346, Agincourt 1415). Crossbow. Siege weapons (trebuchet, battering ram, siege tower). Castles evolved (motte-and-bailey → stone concentric castles)

## Late Middle Ages (1300-1500)
- **Black Death** (1347-1351): Bubonic plague from Asia (silk road), carried by fleas on rats. Killed 30-60% of European population (75-200 million). Effects: labor shortage → peasant wages ↑, feudal system weakened, religious doubt, persecution of Jews (scapegoated), artistic focus on death (danse macabre)
- **Hundred Years' War** (1337-1453): England vs France over French throne. Major battles: Crécy (1346), Poitiers (1356), Agincourt (1415). Joan of Arc (1429-31) — turned tide for France, burned as heretic, later saint. Cannon used effectively. England lost all continental possessions except Calais
- **Western Schism** (1378-1417): Multiple popes (Rome vs Avignon). Damaged Church authority. Resolved at Council of Constance (1414-1418). John Hus burned — Czech proto-Reformation
- **Rise of Nation-States**: France (Louis XI, Valois consolidation), England (War of Roses 1455-1485 → Tudor victory, Henry VII), Spain (Ferdinand + Isabella unified Spain 1469, reconquista completed 1492, expelled Jews), Portugal (Henry the Navigator — exploration)
- **Social changes**: Peasant revolts (French Jacquerie 1358, English Peasants' Revolt 1381). Decline of serfdom in W Europe. Growth of towns and merchant class (burghers). Hanseatic League (trade network N Germany to Baltic). Guilds (artisan/craft associations controlled trade)
- **Technology**: Heavy plow, horse collar, three-field system (agriculture revolution). Watermills, windmills. Mechanical clock (late 13th century). Eyeglasses (1286). Gunpowder (from China via Mongols — changed warfare). Printing press (Gutenberg, 1450 — movable type, revolutionized information)

## Late Medieval/ Early Renaissance
- **Fall of Constantinople** (1453): Ottoman Turks under Mehmed II conquered Byzantine capital. End of Eastern Roman Empire. Greek scholars fled to Italy (fueled Renaissance). Ottoman Empire became major power
- **Renaissance begins**: 14th century Italy (Petrarch, Dante, Giotto). Humanism (focus on classical learning, human potential). Medici family (Florence patron). Early Ren: Brunelleschi (dome of Florence Cathedral), Donatello, Masaccio. Printing press accelerated spread of ideas
- **Exploration**: Portuguese reach India (Vasco da Gama 1498), Columbus (1492 — Spanish voyage west to reach East, discovered Americas). Beginning of colonial era
