# Archaeology

Methods, periods, and major discoveries in human prehistory and history.

## Methods
- **Survey**: Walking transects across landscape, recording surface artifacts. Remote sensing: ground-penetrating radar (GPR), magnetometry (detect buried walls/ ditches), LIDAR (airborne laser scanning reveals hidden features under forest cover). Aerial photography (crop marks, soil marks reveal buried features)
- **Excavation**: Grid system for recording provenience (exact 3D location). Stratigraphy: law of superposition (lower layers are older). Harris matrix diagrams show temporal relationships. Unit excavation by natural layers, not arbitrary levels
- **Dating methods**: Radiocarbon (C-14 — organic material up to 50,000 years), dendrochronology (tree rings — calibration for C-14), thermoluminescence (heated materials — pottery, burned flint), potassium-argon (volcanic rock — millions of years), optically stimulated luminescence (OSL — when sediment last saw sunlight), archaeomagnetism (magnetic field recorded in fired clay)
- **Artifact analysis**: Typology (classification by form/function), lithic analysis (stone tools — reduction sequence, use-wear, microwear), ceramic analysis (paste, temper, firing temp, form, decoration), zooarchaeology (animal bones — species, age, butchery marks), paleoethnobotany (plant remains — flotation for seeds, phytoliths, pollen)
- **Bioarchaeology**: Human remains — paleopathology (disease, trauma), diet (stable isotopes C13/N15), migration (strontium isotopes), DNA (aDNA — population genetics, ancestry, pathogens), dentition (wear patterns, caries)

## Hominin Evolution
- **Sahelanthropus tchadensis** (~7 mya): Earliest known hominin. Small brain (350cc). Found in Chad. Possible bipedal (foramen magnum position)
- **Australopithecus afarensis** (~3.9-2.9 mya): "Lucy" (1974, Ethiopia, 40% skeleton). Bipedal but still climbed. Brain 400cc. Height ~3.5 ft. Footprints at Laetoli (Tanzania, 3.6 mya) show fully bipedal gait
- **Homo habilis** (~2.4-1.4 mya): "Handy man" — first tool maker (Oldowan — simple flake tools). Brain 600cc. Found with stone tools at Olduvai Gorge (Tanzania)
- **Homo erectus** (~2 mya - 100kya): First hominin out of Africa. Larger brain (900 cc). Acheulean handaxes (symmetrical, standardized). Controlled fire (1 mya). Dmanisi (Georgia) skulls show variation
- **Homo neanderthalensis** (~400k-40kya): Europe/W Asia. Larger brain than H. sapiens. Mousterian tool industry (Levallois technique — prepared core). Buried dead (Shanidar cave — possible flowers). Interbred with modern humans (1-2% DNA in non-Africans)
- **Homo sapiens** (~300kya-present): Oldest fossils from Jebel Irhoud (Morocco, 300kya). Omo Kibish (Ethiopia, 200kya). Out of Africa ~70-50kya. Replaced (and interbred with) Neanderthals/Denisovans. Behaviorally modern: art, symbolism, complex tools (Blade technology, bone tools), long-distance trade

## Major Cultural Periods
- **Paleolithic (Old Stone Age)**: ~3.4 mya - 10,000 BCE. Hunter-gatherer. Stone tools (Oldowan, Acheulean, Mousterian, Upper Paleolithic blades). Cave art (Chauvet 37kya, Lascaux 17kya, Altamira 14kya). Venus figurines (Venus of Willendorf 25kya)
- **Mesolithic (Middle Stone Age)**: 10,000-5,000 BCE (varies). Post-Ice Age adaptation. Microliths (small stone blades), bow and arrow, dog domestication. Sedentism increasing (but no farming)
- **Neolithic (New Stone Age)**: 10,000-4,000 BCE (varies). Agricultural revolution — domestication of plants (wheat, barley, rice, corn, legumes) and animals (sheep, goat, pig, cattle). Permanent settlements (Çatalhöyük, Jericho, Mehrgarh). Pottery, polished stone axes, weaving, megalithic monuments (Göbekli Tepe 9500 BCE — world's oldest temple complex)
- **Bronze Age**: ~3300-1200 BCE (Fertile Crescent, varies regionally). Metallurgy (copper + tin = bronze — harder). Writing (Sumerian cuneiform 3200 BCE, Egyptian hieroglyphs 3100 BCE). Urbanization, states, organized religion, trade networks. Bronze Age collapse (1200 BCE — multiple civilizations collapsed simultaneously from drought, invasion, earthquake)
- **Iron Age**: ~1200 BCE onward (Varies by region). Iron metallurgy (cheaper and more abundant than bronze). Alphabet (Phoenician 1050 BCE). Classical empires (Greece, Rome, Persia, India, China)

## Archaeological Theory
- **Culture-historical**: Early 20th century — identify groups by artifact types, track migration/ diffusion. Childe: "Neolithic Revolution" and "Urban Revolution" concepts
- **Processual (New Archaeology)**: 1960s — Binford, Flannery. Scientific, hypothesis-testing, systems theory. Focus on adaptation, ecology, settlement patterns. Culture as adaptive system
- **Post-processual**: 1980s + — Hodder, Shanks, Tilley. Critique of objectivity in archaeology. Emphasis on meaning, agency, gender, power, indigenous perspectives, multivocality. Symbolic and cognitive archaeology

## Famous Sites & Discoveries
- **Göbekli Tepe** (Turkey, 9500 BCE): Megalithic T-shaped pillars with animal carvings. Built by hunter-gatherers before pottery or farming. Revolutionized understanding of Neolithic — suggests religion drove settled life
- **Çatalhöyük** (Turkey, 7100-5700 BCE): Large Neolithic settlement. Houses entered through roof. No streets. Wall paintings, bull horns, burials under floors. 8,000 inhabitants
- **Stonehenge** (UK, 3000-2000 BCE): Megalithic circle. Bluestones from Wales (200 miles). Aligns with summer solstice sunrise. Funerary/ ceremonial. Construction over 1,500 years
- **Troy** (Turkey, 3000 BCE - 500 CE): Schliemann (1871) excavated, destroyed much. Multiple layers (Troy I-IX). Trojan War (Homer, likely Troy VI/VIIa ~1200 BCE)
- **Terracotta Army** (China, 210 BCE): Qin Shi Huang's tomb, 8,000 life-size clay soldiers. Each unique face. Pit discovered 1974 by farmers
- **Machu Picchu** (Peru, 1450 CE): Inca mountain estate. Never found by Spanish. Rediscovered 1911 by Hiram Bingham. Intact architecture, astronomical alignments
- **Dead Sea Scrolls** (Qumran, Israel, 1947-56): 900+ manuscripts including oldest OT copies (250 BCE-68 CE). Essene community library. Shed light on Second Temple Judaism
