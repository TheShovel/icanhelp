# The Cold War (1947-1991)

Geopolitical tension between US-led Western Bloc and USSR-led Eastern Bloc.

## Origins (1945-1953)
- **Post-WWII division**: Yalta (Feb 1945) and Potsdam (July 1945) conferences divided Europe. Soviet occupation of Eastern Europe → satellite states (Poland, Czechoslovakia, Hungary, Romania, Bulgaria, East Germany). "Iron Curtain" (Churchill 1946)
- **Truman Doctrine (1947)**: US policy of containing communism — provide military/economic aid to countries resisting Soviet pressure. First applied to Greece and Turkey ($400M aid). Basis for decades of US foreign policy
- **Marshall Plan (1948-51)**: $13B ($150B in 2024 $) to rebuild Western Europe. Required economic cooperation (basis for EU). USSR refused for itself and satellites — created Comecon (Soviet economic bloc)
- **Berlin Blockade (1948-49)**: USSR blocked land access to West Berlin. Western allies airlifted supplies for 11 months — 277,000 flights, 2.3M tons. USSR backed down. Demonstrates Western resolve
- **NATO formed (1949)**: North Atlantic Treaty Organization — collective defense (Article 5: attack on one = attack on all). Original 12 members. Expanded to include Greece/Turkey (1952), West Germany (1955). Warsaw Pact (1955) as Soviet response

## Nuclear Arms Race
- **US atomic monopoly (1945-49)**: Trinity test (July 1945), Hiroshima/Nagasaki (Aug 1945). USSR shocked by US capability — accelerated their program
- **Soviet bomb (1949)**: First Soviet atomic test (Joe-1) — earlier than US intelligence predicted. Spy network (Klaus Fuchs, Julius and Ethel Rosenberg) transferred secrets. Started arms race
- **Hydrogen bomb**: US Ivy Mike test (1952) — first thermonuclear weapon (yield 10.4 megatons). USSR followed (1953, Sakharov). Castle Bravo (1954) — 15Mt, unexpected fallout contaminated Japanese fishing boat Lucky Dragon
- **ICBMs**: Soviet R-7 Semyorka (1957) — first intercontinental ballistic missile. Sputnik (Oct 1957) shocked the West. "Missile gap" fear drove US military buildup. Mutual Assured Destruction (MAD) doctrine: both sides can destroy each other even after first strike
- **Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)**: USSR placed nuclear missiles in Cuba (90 miles from US). US naval quarantine. 13 days of intense diplomatic tension. Secret deal: USSR removes Cuba missiles, US removes Turkey missiles + promises not to invade Cuba. Closest the world came to nuclear war
- **Arms control treaties**: Partial Test Ban (1963 — bans atmospheric testing), NPT (1968 — non-proliferation, 5 recognized nuclear states), SALT I (1972 — limits ICBMs), ABM Treaty (1972 — limits anti-missile systems), SALT II (1979 — never ratified but mostly observed), INF Treaty (1987 — eliminates intermediate-range missiles), START I (1991 — reduces strategic weapons)

## Proxy Wars
- **Korean War (1950-53)**: North Korea (supported by USSR/China) invaded South Korea. UN coalition led by US pushed back to near Chinese border → China intervened → stalemate at 38th parallel. Armistice 1953 (still technically war). 2.5M dead. Major war that established Cold War pattern of containment
- **Vietnam War (1955-75)**: France defeated at Dien Bien Phu (1954) → Vietnam divided at 17th parallel. US supported South Vietnam, fearing "domino theory" (if one SE Asian country fell to communism, others would follow). Gulf of Tonkin (1964) → US escalation. 500K+ US troops by 1968. Tet Offensive (1968) turned US public against war. Paris Peace Accords (1973). Fall of Saigon (1975). 3M dead (mostly Vietnamese). "Vietnam syndrome" = US reluctance to engage in protracted foreign wars
- **Soviet-Afghan War (1979-89)**: USSR invaded Afghanistan to prop up communist government. US (via Pakistan ISI) armed mujahideen (including Osama bin Laden). Soviet quagmire — 15K Soviet dead, 1M+ Afghan. Soviet withdrawal 1989. Afghanistan then collapsed into civil war (→ Taliban → 9/11)
- **Other conflicts**: Angolan Civil War (USSR/Cuba vs US/South Africa), Sandinista vs Contras in Nicaragua, Ogaden War (Ethiopia vs Somalia), Yom Kippur War (Israel vs Egypt/Syria — superpowers nearly clashed)

## Détente & Renewed Tension
- **Détente (1969-79)**: Relaxation of tensions between US/USSR. Nixon visited China (1972), Soviet Union (1972). SALT I, ABM Treaty. Helsinki Accords (1975) — recognized post-WWII borders (mollified USSR) + human rights commitments (used by dissidents). US-Soviet joint space mission (Apollo-Soyuz 1975)
- **Soviet invasion of Afghanistan (1979)**: Ended détente. US boycotted 1980 Moscow Olympics. USSR boycotted 1984 Los Angeles Olympics. Carter Doctrine: US would fight for Persian Gulf access
- **Reagan buildup (1981-88)**: Massive military spending ($2.2T over 5 years). SDI ("Star Wars") — missile defense system (never fully deployed). "Evil empire" rhetoric. Pershing II missiles in Europe (countered Soviet SS-20s). Reagan's military pressure contributed to Soviet economic strain
- **Solidarity movement (Poland, 1980)**: Lech Wałęsa led independent trade union — 10M members. Martial law imposed (1981). But movement survived underground — contributed to later collapse

## End of the Cold War (1985-1991)
- **Gorbachev reforms**: Became Soviet leader 1985. Perestroika (economic restructuring — limited market reforms), Glasnost (openness — free speech, media liberalization), New Thinking (foreign policy: "common European home", withdraw from Afghanistan, reduce nuclear weapons). Gorbachev's goal was to reform communism, not end it
- **Fall of Eastern Bloc (1989)**: Peaceful revolutions across Eastern Europe — Poland (June — first semi-free elections, Solidarity wins), Hungary (opened border with Austria — East Germans fled through), East Germany (mass protests, Berlin Wall fell Nov 9), Czechoslovakia (Velvet Revolution), Bulgaria/Romania (violent in Romania — Ceausescu executed). All communist governments fell
- **Reunification of Germany (1990)**: East/West Germany reunited under Helmut Kohl. Gorbachev agreed to united Germany in NATO (security guarantees to USSR). "Two-plus-four" treaty (East/West Germany + US/UK/France/USSR)
- **Dissolution of USSR (1991)**: Failed coup by hardliners (Aug 1991) accelerated collapse. Republics declared independence (Ukraine Dec 1 referendum). Soviet Union formally dissolved Dec 26, 1991. Gorbachev resigned Dec 25. Boris Yeltsin president of independent Russia

## Key Technologies
- **Space Race**: Sputnik (1957), Explorer 1 (1958). Yuri Gagarin (1961 — first human in space). John Glenn (1962 — first American orbit). Apollo 11 (1969 — Moon landing). Space station: Salyut, Mir, ISS (post-Cold War cooperation). Apollo-Soyuz (1975 — first US-Soviet joint mission). Both sides saw space as technological prestige
- **Intelligence**: CIA vs KGB. U-2 spy plane (flown over USSR 1956-60 — Gary Powers shot down 1960). SR-71 Blackbird (Mach 3, 85,000 ft — never shot down). Corona satellite program (secret US photo reconnaissance). Soviet GRU military intelligence. East German Stasi (secret police — built extensive files on citizens)
- **Codebreaking**: Venona project (US intercepted Soviet diplomatic traffic — cracked Soviet codes, revealed atomic spies). NSA vs FAPSI signals intelligence. British GCHQ, French DGSE
