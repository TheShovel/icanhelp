# World Wars

World War I and World War II — causes, major events, and consequences.

## World War I (1914-1918)
- **Causes (MAIN)**: Militarism (arms race, Germany vs Britain naval), Alliances (Triple Entente: France, Russia, Britain vs Triple Alliance: Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy), Imperialism (colonial competition), Nationalism (Balkan tensions, Slavic nationalism vs Austria-Hungary). Immediate trigger: assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand (June 28, 1914, Sarajevo, by Gavrilo Princip)
- **Western Front**: Trench warfare from Belgium to Switzerland. Stalemate after German Schlieffen Plan failed (Battle of the Marne 1914). Major battles: Verdun (1916 — 700,000 casualties), Somme (1916 — 1M+ casualties, 60,000 British day 1), Passchendaele (1917). New weapons: machine gun, poison gas (chlorine, mustard), tanks (first used 1916), flamethrower, aircraft (reconnaissance → fighters → bombers)
- **Eastern Front**: More mobile. Germany defeated Russia at Tannenberg (1914) and Masurian Lakes. Russian Revolution (1917) — Bolsheviks took power, Treaty of Brest-Litovsk (March 1918, Russia ceded huge territory). Collapse of Austria-Hungary
- **Other fronts**: Gallipoli (1915 — failed Allied invasion of Ottoman Empire, ANZAC forces). Italian front (Isonzo River, Caporetto). Middle East (Lawrence of Arabia, Arab Revolt, British conquered Palestine/ Mesopotamia). Africa (German colonies captured)
- **US entry (1917)**: Unrestricted submarine warfare (U-boats sank Lusitania 1915, Sussex Pledge broken). Zimmermann Telegram (Germany proposed Mexico alliance against US). American Expeditionary Force (Pershing) arrived 1917-18, tipped balance
- **1918**: Germany launched Spring Offensive (failed). Allied Hundred Days Offensive broke Hindenburg Line. German navy mutiny, Kaiser abdicated. Armistice Nov 11, 1918. 20M dead (9M military, 11M civilians). Spanish Flu (1918-19) killed 50M+ more
- **Aftermath**: Treaty of Versailles (1919) — Germany blamed, reparations $33B, lost territory/colonies/military, War Guilt clause. League of Nations (US never joined). Russian Civil War. New nations (Poland, Czechoslovakia, Yugoslavia). Redrawing of Middle East (Sykes-Picot, British/French mandates)

## Interwar Period (1919-1939)
- **Great Depression (1929-1939)**: Wall Street Crash Oct 1929 → global economic crisis. German hyperinflation (1923, wheelbarrow of marks for bread). Unemployment 25%+ (US), 30%+ (Germany). New Deal (FDR 1933-37). Protectionism (Smoot-Hawley tariff 1930) worsened depression
- **Rise of Fascism**: Mussolini's Italy (1922 — March on Rome). Nazi Germany (Hitler appointed Chancellor Jan 1933, Reichstag Fire → Enabling Act, totalitarian state). Franco's Spain (Spanish Civil War 1936-39). Japanese militarism (Manchuria 1931, Second Sino-Japanese War 1937)
- **Appeasement**: Germany remilitarized Rhineland (1936), Anschluss with Austria (1938), Munich Agreement (1938 — gave Sudetenland to Germany). Hitler violated Munich, took Czechoslovakia (March 1939). Nazi-Soviet Pact (Aug 1939 — division of Poland)

## World War II (1939-1945)
- **European Theater**: Germany invaded Poland (Sept 1, 1939) → Britain/France declared war. Blitzkrieg (lightning war — tanks + air power). Fall of France (1940, 6 weeks). Battle of Britain (1940 — Luftwaffe vs RAF, Germany lost air superiority). North Africa (Rommel vs Montgomery, El Alamein 1942). Operation Barbarossa (June 1941 — Germany invaded USSR, largest invasion in history). Stalingrad (1942-43 — turning point, German 6th Army destroyed). D-Day (June 6, 1944 — Allied invasion of Normandy). Germany surrendered May 8, 1945 (VE Day)
- **Pacific Theater**: Japan expansion: Manchuria 1931, China 1937, French Indochina 1940. Pearl Harbor (Dec 7, 1941 — surprise attack on US Pacific Fleet). US entered war. Japan conquered Philippines, SE Asia, Pacific islands. Battle of Midway (June 1942 — US sank 4 Japanese carriers, turning point). Island hopping (Guadalcanal, Iwo Jima, Okinawa — fierce resistance). Atomic bombs: Hiroshima (Aug 6, 1945), Nagasaki (Aug 9). Japan surrendered Sept 2 (VJ Day)
- **Home front**: Total war — economies converted to war production. Rationing (food, gas, rubber). Women in workforce (Rosie the Riveter). Japanese-American internment (US, 120,000). Bombing of civilians (London Blitz, Dresden firebombing, Tokyo firebombing)
- **Holocaust**: Nazi genocide of 6M Jews (67% of European Jewish population). Also targeted: Roma (Gypsies, 250,000), disabled (200,000+), Slavic civilians, Soviet POWs (3M), homosexuals, political opponents. Concentration camps (Auschwitz-Birkenau, Treblinka, Sobibor — extermination camps). Einsatzgruppen (mobile killing squads). Ghettos (Warsaw Ghetto Uprising 1943). Liberation 1944-45
- **Technology**: Radar, sonar, Jet aircraft (Me 262), V-2 rockets (first ballistic missile), Enigma code-breaking (Alan Turing, Bletchley Park), Proximity fuze, Penicillin (mass production developed for war), Nuclear weapons (Manhattan Project)

## Aftermath
- **Cold War began** (1947-1991): US vs USSR. Iron Curtain divided Europe. NATO (1949) vs Warsaw Pact (1955). Berlin Blockade/Airlift (1948-49). Arms race (nuclear weapons — MAD doctrine). Korean War (1950-53). Decolonization (India 1947, African nations 1950s-60s)
- **United Nations** founded (1945) — replaced League. Permanent Security Council (US, UK, France, USSR/Russia, China). Universal Declaration of Human Rights (1948)
- **Marshall Plan** (1948-51): US $13B to rebuild Western Europe. Led to economic miracle. Prevented communist takeover
- **Nuremberg Trials** (1945-46): Nazi leaders prosecuted for crimes against humanity. Established precedent: "I was just following orders" not a defense. Tokyo Trials similarly for Japan
- **Casualties**: 70-85M total (25M military, 50M+ civilians). USSR: 27M (highest). China: 15-20M. Germany: 7M. Poland: 5-6M (18% of population, highest percentage). Japan: 3M. US: 420,000
