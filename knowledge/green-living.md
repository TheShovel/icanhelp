# Environmental Issues & Green Living

## Individual Impact
- Biggest actions (highest CO₂ reduction per person):
  1. Live car-free (2.4 tons CO₂/yr saved)
  2. Avoid one long-haul flight (1.6 tons)
  3. Eat plant-based diet (0.8 tons)
  4. Switch to renewable energy (1.5 tons)
  5. Buy fewer new things (varies)
- Reduce plastic: reusable bags, bottles, containers; avoid single-use
- Food waste: 1/3 of all food is wasted — meal plan, store properly, compost scraps
- Clothing: fashion industry = 10% of global CO₂ + massive water use — buy less, buy used, repair
- Home energy: LED bulbs (90% less energy than incandescent), smart thermostat, weatherstrip, insulate

## Recycling
- **Check local rules** — recycling varies by municipality
- Clean containers: rinse food residue off, remove caps (often separate), flatten cardboard
- Recyclable: paper/cardboard, glass bottles, metal cans, #1 and #2 plastic (bottles, jugs)
- NOT recyclable curbside: plastic bags (return to store), styrofoam, greasy pizza boxes, shredded paper, flexible packaging
- Wishcycling: putting non-recyclable items in recycling hoping they'll be recycled → contaminates batch
- Glass: fully recyclable forever (doesn't degrade like plastic)
- Plastic: most plastic can only be recycled once or twice (downcycling) — reduction is better than recycling

## Sustainable Food
- Eat seasonally and locally (farmers markets, CSA boxes)
- Reduce beef: beef has 10x the carbon footprint of chicken, 50x of beans
- Plant-based meals: beans + rice = complete protein, cheaper, lower impact
- Grow your own: herbs easiest, then tomatoes/lettuce/peppers
- Composting: kitchen scraps + yard waste → nutrient-rich soil
  - Green (nitrogen): fruit, veggies, coffee grounds, grass clippings
  - Brown (carbon): leaves, cardboard, paper, wood chips
  - Ratio: ~3 parts brown to 1 part green

## Fast Fashion
- 100 billion garments produced yearly, 60% end up in landfill within a year
- Microplastics from synthetic clothes (polyester, nylon) wash into oceans
- Alternatives: thrift/second-hand, buy fewer higher-quality items, repair, rent for occasional wear
- Brands: Patagonia, REI, Eileen Fisher, Nudie Jeans (more sustainable options)
- Wash: full loads, cold water, air dry (less wear, less energy)
- Care: mend small tears, replace buttons, resole shoes — extend life

## Water Conservation
- Shorter showers (~5 min vs 10 min saves 25L+)
- Turn off tap while brushing teeth (saves ~8L/min)
- Fix leaky faucets (1 drip/sec = 20L/day wasted)
- Low-flow showerheads + aerators on faucets
- Rain barrel for garden watering
- Native plants: need 50-80% less water than lawns
- Full loads only for dishwasher/washing machine

## Energy at Home
- LED bulbs: use 75% less energy, last 15-25x longer than incandescent
- Phantom power: electronics draw power when "off" — use power strips, turn off when not in use
- Fridge: clean coils yearly, set to 37-40°F, don't leave door open
- Washer/dryer: cold water wash, high-efficiency machines, line-dry when possible
- Windows: double/triple pane, thermal curtains, weatherstripping
- Solar panels: payback in 6-12 years depending on location + incentives
- Home battery (Tesla Powerwall, etc): store solar energy, backup during outages

## Biodiversity
- 1 million species at risk of extinction (IPBES report)
- Primary causes: habitat loss (farming, development), climate change, pollution, invasive species, overfishing
- Help locally: native plant garden (supports pollinators), reduce pesticide use, leave some wild areas
- Support: national parks, wildlife reserves, conservation organizations
- Avoid: palm oil from deforested areas (check RSPO certified), products with endangered animal parts
- Invasive species: don't release pets into wild, clean boots/boats between areas

## Climate Action
- **Individual**: reduce own emissions (diet, transport, energy, stuff)
- **Community**: join local environmental group, community garden, car share
- **Systemic**: vote for climate action, contact representatives, support carbon pricing
- **Work**: if possible, choose employers with sustainability commitments
- **Money**: divest from fossil fuels, bank at green banks, invest in sustainable companies
- Most impactful: systemic change > individual action — but both matter
- Don't let perfection be enemy of progress — imperfect action > no action
- Climate anxiety is real: you're not alone, action reduces helplessness, take breaks from doomscrolling
