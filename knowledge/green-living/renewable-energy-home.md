# Renewable Energy at Home

## Rooftop Solar PV Basics
- kW = power capacity (panel rating). kWh = energy produced over time (kW × hours).
- Rule of thumb: 1 kW DC ≈ 4–5 kWh/day in sunny US climates (1,400–1,800 kWh/kW-year).
- System size: annual kWh use ÷ 1,400 (sunny) to ÷ 1,100 (cloudy) = kW needed.
- A 6 kW system (~18–20 panels) covers ~8,000–9,000 kWh/year for avg US home.
- South-facing, 15–30° tilt, no shade 9am–3pm maximizes output.

## Solar Payback & Incentives
```
Federal ITC: 30% of system cost off taxes (through 2032).
Payback: net cost ÷ annual savings. Typical US: 7-12 years.
Levelized cost (LCOE): ~$0.06-0.12/kWh vs grid $0.15-0.30/kWh.
System cost: ~$2.50-3.50/W installed before incentives ($15-21k for 6kW).
```
After payback, free electricity for remaining 15–25 yr panel life. Panels degrade ~0.5%/year.

## Net Metering
- Excess solar exported to grid earns bill credits (1:1 in full-retail states).
- Self-consumption beats export: use daytime loads (dishwasher, EV, AC).
- NEM 2.0/3.0 (CA) cuts export value; batteries improve economics there.
- Check utility policy + interconnection fees before sizing.

## Solar Thermal (Water Heating)
- Flat-plate / evacuated-tube collectors heat water to 120–180°F directly.
- Covers 50–80% of water heating (2nd-largest home load after HVAC).
- Cost ~$3,000–6,000 installed; payback 4–8 years; 30% federal tax credit applies.
- Freeze protection (glycol loop) needed in cold climates.

## Small Wind
- Viable only with avg wind ≥10–12 mph (class 3+) and zoning/height clearance.
- Residential turbine 1–10 kW: ~$3,000–8,000/kW installed (high $/kWh vs solar).
- Tower height matters most: double height can double output. Rarely cost-effective urban.

## Battery Storage
```
Capacity in kWh (energy). Power in kW (delivery rate).
Depth of Discharge (DoD): LiFePO4 safe to 80-100% DoD; lead-acid only 50%.
Usable = rated kWh × DoD. 10 kWh battery ≈ half-day backup for essentials.
Cost: ~$400-800/kWh installed (Tesla Powerwall ~$1,000/kWh all-in).
```
Size battery to critical loads (fridge, lights, comms), not whole house, for best ROI.

## Heat Pumps vs Resistive
- Heat pump: 300–400% efficient (COP 3–4) moving heat vs 100% resistive.
- Air-source heats/cools; ground-source (GSHP) COP 4–5, higher install cost.
- Resistive (baseboard, electric water heater) is 3–4x more expensive to run.
- Pair heat pump + solar to decarbonize heating at lowest cost.

## Comparison of Home Options
| Option | Typical cost | Pros | Cons |
|--------|--------------|------|------|
| Rooftop PV | $15–21k (6kW) | 30% ITC, 7–12yr payback, 25yr life | Needs roof space/sun, upfront cost |
| Solar thermal | $3–6k | Cheap water heat, 4–8yr payback | Only water, freeze risk |
| Small wind | $3k–8k/kW | Good windy sites | Needs 10+ mph, zoning, tall tower |
| Battery | $400–800/kWh | Backup, self-consumption | Slow payback alone |
| Heat pump | $4–12k | 3–4x efficient, heats+cools | Cold-climate output drop |

## Quick Wins
- Get 3 quotes via EnergySage; compare $/W and panel warranty (25yr).
- Add a home energy monitor (Sense, Emporia) to find waste before sizing solar.
- Start with efficiency: insulation + heat pump cuts the system size you need.
- Check local rebates + net metering rules; they change the math most.
