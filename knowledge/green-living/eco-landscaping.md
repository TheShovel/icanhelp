# Eco Landscaping

## Native Plants
- Native species evolved for local climate/soil; need less water, fertilizer, and care.
- Support local pollinators, birds, and beneficial insects better than exotics.
- Find regional lists via native plant societies, extension offices, or Audubon databases.
- Match plant to site: sun/shade, wet/dry, soil pH — right plant, right place.
- Start with 3–5 natives (e.g., coneflower, milkweed, switchgrass) and expand yearly.
- Avoid invasive ornamentals (e.g., burning bush, English ivy) that escape cultivation.

## Pollinator Gardens
- Provide continuous bloom spring–fall: early (crocus, salvia), mid (bee balm), late (aster).
- Include host plants for caterpillars (milkweed for monarchs, dill/parsley for swallowtails).
- Plant in clusters (3+ of a kind) so pollinators find them efficiently.
- Avoid neonicotinoid-treated plants ("N-P-free" labels); they harm bees.
- Offer bare soil patches and a shallow water source for bees and butterflies.

## Rain Gardens & Drainage
- Dig a shallow (4–8 in) basin in a low spot to catch roof/runoff; size ~20–30% of drainage area.
- Use moisture-loving natives: Joe-Pye weed, sedges, rushes, cardinal flower.
- Position at least 10 ft from foundations; layer sand/gravel/soil for infiltration.
- Reduces stormwater runoff and filters fertilizers/pesticides before they reach streams.
- Pair with rain barrels (collect ~0.6 gal per sq ft of roof per 1 in of rain).

## Permeable Paving
- Replace impervious surfaces to let water infiltrate: permeable pavers, gravel, porous concrete.
- Use for driveways, paths, patios; reduces runoff and heat island effect.
- Base layer of crushed stone (4–8 in) supports load and stores water.
- Avoid sealing; sweep instead of hosing; refill joints with sand annually.
- Stepping-stone paths through planting reduce paved area dramatically.

## Mulching
- Apply 2–3 in organic mulch (shredded bark, leaves, wood chips) keeping it off stems.
- Suppresses weeds, retains moisture, moderates soil temperature, builds soil.
- Replenish annually; use fallen leaves as free mulch ("leaf mold" over winter).
- Avoid dyed/plastic mulch; choose arborist chips (often free from tree services).
- Living mulch: low groundcovers replace bare soil between larger plants.

## Lawn Alternatives
| Alternative | Best For | Care |
|-------------|----------|------|
| Clover (white) | Low-traffic lawns | Mow rarely, fixes nitrogen |
| Moss | Shade, acid soil | No mow, needs moisture |
| Meadow/wildflower | Large areas | Mow 1–2x/yr |
| Native grass mix | Dry sites | Low water, infrequent cut |
| Groundcover (thyme) | Sunny paths | Drought-tolerant, walkable |

## Reducing Fertilizer & Runoff
- Test soil before fertilizing; most lawns need little or none.
- Use slow-release or organic amendments (compost, composted manure) over synthetics.
- Leave grass clippings ("grasscycling") to return nitrogen naturally.
- Sweep fertilizer off hard surfaces back onto lawn to prevent waterway runoff.
- Reduce irrigation; deep, infrequent watering builds deeper roots.

## Companion Planting Basics
- Tomatoes + basil: improves flavor, repels pests.
- Carrots + onions: onion scent masks carrot fly.
- Beans + corn + squash: "Three Sisters" — nitrogen, support, ground cover.
- Marigolds throughout: deter nematodes and aphids.
- Avoid planting same family together year after year (rotate to limit disease).

## Lawn Problem → Eco Solution
| Problem | Eco Solution |
|---------|-------------|
| Bare patches | Clover or reseed with fescue, topdress compost |
| Weeds | Corn gluten meal (pre-emergent), hand-pull, dense planting |
| Brown/thirsty | Raise mower to 3 in, deep-water less often |
| Grubs | Nematodes (beneficial), milky spore, skunk-proof |
| Poor soil | Topdress 1/4 in compost yearly |
| Erosion | Groundcover, terraces, rain garden at low point |

## Quick Wins
- Convert one strip of lawn to native pollinator beds this season.
- Set mower blade to 3 in and leave clippings; stop fertilizing.
- Add a rain barrel and a 2–3 in mulch layer to beds.
- Remove one invasive ornamental and replace with a native.
- Plant a cluster of 3 pollinator-friendly natives near the sunniest spot.
