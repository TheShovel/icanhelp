# Sustainable Food Systems

## Food Carbon Footprint Ranking
Greenhouse-gas emissions per kg of food product (approx. CO2e, lifecycle). Lower on the list is better.

| Food | Relative CO2e (kg/kg) | Notes |
|------|----------------------|-------|
| Beef (beef herd) | ~60–99 | Highest; methane + land use |
| Lamb & mutton | ~20–24 | High methane per kg |
| Cheese | ~12–21 | Dairy concentration |
| Pork | ~7 | Lower than ruminants |
| Poultry | ~5–6 | Far below beef |
| Eggs | ~4–5 | Moderate |
| Tofu / legumes | ~2 | Plant protein |
| Vegetables | ~1–2 | Low; varies by transport |
| Fruits | ~1–1.5 | Low; season matters |
| Nuts | ~0.3–0.5 | Low but water-intensive |

A single beef meal can exceed a week of vegetarian meals in emissions.

## Seasonal Eating
- Buy in-season produce: lower energy for heating/growing and fewer "food miles."
- Northern hemisphere summer: tomatoes, berries, zucchini, corn, stone fruit.
- Winter: root veg, squash, cabbage, citrus, stored apples.
- Use frozen produce off-season; freezing locks nutrition at peak and cuts waste.
- Farmers markets and CSAs reduce packaging and transport distance.
- Grow a few herbs/windowsill greens year-round for freshness.

## Reducing Food Waste
- Fridge at 0–4 °C (32–40 °F); freezer at -18 °C (0 °F) or below.
- FIFO: "first in, first out" — rotate older items to the front.
- Plan portions: ~1 cup cooked grain + 1/2 cup protein + 1 cup veg per adult meal.
- Freeze surplus: bread, herbs (in oil), overripe bananas, broth, leftovers.
- Use veggie scraps for stock; citrus peels for cleaner; regrow green onions.
- Understand date labels: "best by" = quality, not safety; trust your senses.

## Produce Storage & Shelf Life
| Item | Best Storage | Approx. Life |
|------|--------------|--------------|
| Leafy greens | Bag with paper towel, crisper | 5–7 days |
| Carrots | Sealed container, submerged water | 2–4 weeks |
| Potatoes | Cool, dark, ventilated (not fridge) | 1–3 months |
| Onions/garlic | Cool, dry, dark, separate from potatoes | 1–2 months |
| Apples | Crisper drawer, separate from greens | 4–6 weeks |
| Berries | Dry, unwashed, fridge; wash before eat | 3–7 days |
| Bananas | Counter, away from other fruit | 5–7 days |
| Tomatoes | Counter, out of sun (not fridge) | 5–7 days |

## Plant-Forward Diets
- "Flexitarian" pattern: meat a few times/week, plants as base.
- Try "Meatless Monday" then expand; swap half the ground meat for lentils/mushrooms.
- Build meals around legumes, whole grains, veg, nuts, seeds.
- Choose lower-impact proteins: legumes > poultry > pork > ruminants.
- Dairy: opt for smaller amounts, or fortified plant milks (soy/oat).

## Local & Regenerative Concepts
- Local: shorter supply chain, supports regional farms, often fresher.
- Regenerative: practices that rebuild soil carbon (cover crops, rotational grazing, no-till).
- Pasture-raised and grass-fed ruminants can have better land stewardship, but still high emissions.
- Certifications to look for: Organic, Regenerative Organic Certified, Fair Trade, Animal Welfare Approved.
- Compost food scraps (see composting.md) to close the nutrient loop at home.

## Quick Wins
- Replace 2 red-meat meals/week with legumes or poultry.
- Freeze leftovers the night you cook instead of discovering them later.
- Shop from a list built on what you already have (inventory first).
- Start one compost method (bin, worms, or municipal pickup).
- Buy imperfect/ugly produce to cut farm-level waste.
