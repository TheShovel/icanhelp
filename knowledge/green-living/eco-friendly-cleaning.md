# Eco-Friendly Cleaning

## Core Ingredients
- **White vinegar (5% acetic acid)**: cuts grease, dissolves mineral deposits, deodorizes.
- **Baking soda (sodium bicarbonate)**: mild abrasive, deodorizer, gentle scrub.
- **Castile soap (liquid)**: biodegradable surfactant; dilutes for many surfaces.
- **Citric acid**: descaler for kettles, coffee makers, dishwashers (stronger than vinegar).
- **Hydrogen peroxide (3%)**: disinfectant/whitener; store in opaque bottle (light degrades it).
- **Olive oil / beeswax**: furniture polish, not a cleaner.

## DIY Recipes (ratios)
```
All-purpose spray: 1 part vinegar + 1 part water + few drops castile. (glass, counters, floors)
Glass cleaner: 2 cups water + 1/4 cup vinegar + 1 Tbsp cornstarch (no streaks).
Scrub paste: 1/2 cup baking soda + 2 Tbsp castile + water to paste. (sinks, tubs)
Dish soap: 1 cup castile + 1 Tbsp washing soda + 1 cup water. (a little goes far)
Floor wash: 1 bucket warm water + 1/4 cup castile + 1/4 cup vinegar.
Descaler: 1-2 Tbsp citric acid per 1L water; soak 30 min, rinse. (kettles, showers)
Drain freshener: 1/2 cup baking soda + 1/2 cup vinegar, wait 15 min, flush hot.
Wood polish: 1 part olive oil + 1 part lemon juice, rub thin, buff.
```

## What NOT to Mix (dangerous)
```
BLEACH + VINEGAR/ACID  → chlorine gas (toxic, suffocation risk)
BLEACH + AMMONIA       → chloramine gas (toxic)
BLEACH + HYDROGEN PEROXIDE → peracetic acid (irritant, corrosive)
VINEGAR + CASTILE SOAP → separates, curdles (use separately, rinse between)
HYDROGEN PEROXIDE + VINEGAR → peracetic acid if stored mixed (okay sequential, rinse)
```
Never store mixed cleaners. Use one product, rinse, then the next.

## When to Use What Surface
- **Vinegar**: glass, stainless, ceramic, sealed stone-safe? NO on marble/granite (acid etches).
- **Baking soda**: enamel, stainless, tubs, oven interior (non-toxic scrub).
- **Castile**: floors, dishes, hands, most washable surfaces (dilute well).
- **Citric acid**: coffee/tea scale, toilet rim, shower glass (avoid aluminum).
- **Hydrogen peroxide**: cutting boards, toilets, mold spots (disinfect, then rinse).
- **Avoid**: vinegar on natural stone, waxed wood, cast iron, aluminum, egg spills (cooks).

## Reducing Single-Use Wipes
- Swap paper towels for **Swedish dishcloths** (absorbent, machine-wash 200x).
- **Microfiber cloths** + spray cleaner replace 90% of wipes; wash, reuse.
- **Reusable mop pads** (cotton) instead of disposable sweep pads.
- Compostable wipes only if certified (OK Compost / BPI); most "flushable" are not.
- One cloth per task/room prevents cross-contamination (color-code).

## Stain / Problem → Solution
| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Greasy stovetop | Vinegar + baking soda paste, scrub, rinse |
| Soap scum | Vinegar spray, then baking soda scrub |
| Hard-water scale | Citric acid soak / vinegar wipe |
| Clogged drain (minor) | Baking soda + vinegar + hot water |
| Mildew on grout | Hydrogen peroxide spray, sit 10 min, scrub |
| Coffee/tea stains | Citric acid soak or baking soda paste |
| Sticky residue | Castile soap + warm water |
| Odors (fridge, bin) | Open box baking soda; wipe with vinegar |
| Tarnished copper | Lemon + salt scrub, rinse |
| Wine on carpet | Salt blot, then hydrogen peroxide + dish soap |

## Disinfection Reality Check
- Vinegar/baking soda clean but do NOT reliably kill viruses/bacteria.
- For true disinfection use 3% hydrogen peroxide (let sit 5–10 min) or diluted bleach (1:10 with water, 1 min contact) — never mix.
- High-touch points (handles, switches, toilets) deserve real disinfectant periodically.

## Quick Wins
- Keep a spray bottle of 1:1 vinegar-water + microfiber cloth at the sink.
- Buy ingredients in bulk; refill bottles; skip scented chemical cleaners.
- Wash cloths at 60°C to sanitize; air-dry to avoid mildew.
- Label bottles clearly; store peroxide away from light.
