# Green Building Materials

## Insulation Comparison
R-value per inch, relative cost, and trade-offs. Higher R = better thermal resistance.

| Material | R-value/in | Relative Cost | Pros | Cons |
|----------|-----------|---------------|------|------|
| Fiberglass batts | ~2.9–3.8 | $ | Cheap, widely available | Irritant, gaps if poorly fit |
| Mineral wool | ~3.0–4.0 | $$ | Fire-resistant, sound damp | Heavier, pricier |
| Cellulose (recycled paper) | ~3.1–3.8 | $–$$ | High recycled content, dense | Needs dry install, settles |
| Sheep wool | ~3.5–3.8 | $$$ | Renewable, buffers humidity | Cost, pest considerations |
| Spray foam (closed-cell) | ~6.0–7.0 | $$$$ | Air seal + high R | Fossil-based, pro install |

Rule of thumb: attic target R-38 to R-60; walls R-13 to R-21; floors R-19 to R-30 (climate-dependent).

## Low-VOC Paints & Finishes
- VOC = volatile organic compounds; choose <50 g/L (flat) or <150 g/L (other) for low-VOC.
- Look for GREENGUARD Gold, Green Seal, or Master Painters Institute certification.
- Zero-VOC lines: many major brands offer <5 g/L tint bases.
- Water-based (acrylic latex) over solvent-based; avoid oil-based where possible.
- Cure fully (2–4 weeks) before occupying tightly sealed rooms; ventilate while painting.
- Natural options: clay/lime plaster, milk paint, mineral paints (no synthetic binders).

## Wood: Reclaimed & Certified
- FSC (Forest Stewardship Council): verifies responsible forest management.
- Reclaimed/salvaged wood: barn timber, pallet wood, demolition lumber — low new-impact.
- Look for SFI or PEFC as secondary certifications.
- Avoid tropical hardwoods without verified certification (deforestation risk).
- Use formaldehyde-free plywood / NAUF (no-added-urea-formaldehyde) for indoor air.
- Local species reduce transport emissions vs imported exotics.

## Bamboo, Cork & Alternatives
- Bamboo: fast-growing grass; use for flooring, panels, cutting boards. Choose low-VOC adhesive.
- Cork: harvested from bark without felling trees; flooring, wall tiles, insulation boards.
- Linoleum (true linoleum): linseed oil + cork/wood flour; biodegradable, durable.
- Recycled-content: glass-countertops, composite decking (HDPE + wood fiber), rubber flooring.
- Wool carpet: renewable, low-VOC vs synthetic nylon.

## Renovation Decision Table
| Need | Conventional | Greener Choice | Why |
|------|-------------|----------------|-----|
| Flooring | Vinyl plank | FSC oak / cork / linoleum | Lower VOC, renewable |
| Countertop | Laminate/stone import | Recycled glass / butcher block | Recycled or local |
| Wall paint | Standard latex | Low/zero-VOC certified | Indoor air quality |
| Insulation | Fiberglass | Cellulose or mineral wool | Recycled/fire-safe |
| Decking | Treated lumber | Composite (recycled) or FSC | Durability, recycled |
| Cabinets | Particleboard | FSC plywood, NAUF | Formaldehyde-free |

## Quick Wins
- Paint only with low-VOC certified products and ventilate during/after.
- Choose FSC or reclaimed wood for any new trim, shelving, or furniture.
- Top up attic insulation to R-38+ before upgrading windows (better ROI).
- Request NAUF plywood and formaldehyde-free products for indoor builds.
- Reuse existing fixtures, doors, and hardware instead of replacing.
