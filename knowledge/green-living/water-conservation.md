# Water Conservation

## Indoor: Low-Flow Fixtures
- Showerheads: replace 2.5 GPM with 1.5–1.75 GPM (WaterSense). Saves ~2,700 gal/person/year.
- Faucet aerators: swap 2.2 GPM for 1.0–1.5 GPM. Costs <$5, installs by hand in 5 min.
- Toilets: old 3.5–7 GPM (gal/flush) → 1.28 GPM HET. Dual-flush splits 0.8/1.28.
- Dishwasher: ENERGY STAR uses ~3 gal/load vs hand-wash ~20 gal. Always run full loads.
- Washing machine: front-load uses 13 gal vs top-load 30+ gal per load.

## Indoor: Toilet Displacement & Leaks
```
Leak test: 10 drops food coloring in tank, wait 15 min. Color in bowl = leak.
Displacement: fill 2L bottle with water + pebbles, place in tank away from flapper. Saves ~0.5 gal/flush.
Flapper: replace every 3-5 yr ($5). Worn flapper = 200+ gal/day silent leak.
```
A single dripping faucet (1 drip/sec) wastes ~3,000 gal/year. Fix with new washer ($0.50).

## Indoor: Dishwasher vs Hand-Wash
- Hand-washing with running tap: ~20 gal/load. Filling basin: ~5 gal.
- ENERGY STAR dishwasher: ~3 gal/load and sanitizes better (hotter water).
- Scrape, don't pre-rinse. Use eco/air-dry setting. Run only when full.
- Mistake: pre-rinsing under running water negates all savings.

## Indoor: Greywater Reuse
- Sink/shower water (excluding toilet) can irrigate plants via bucket or branched drain.
- Laundry-to-landscape: divert washer discharge to mulch basins with a 3-way valve ($30 DIY).
- Never store greywater >24h (pathogens). Use plant-based, salt-free, boron-free soap.
- Check local code: many US states allow simple laundry/gravity greywater without permit.

## Outdoor: Irrigation Efficiency
- Drip irrigation: 90% efficient vs sprinkler 50–70%. Emitters 0.5–2 GPH.
- Water 1–2x/week deep (1" total) not daily shallow. Root depth = drought resistance.
- Timing: pre-dawn (4–6am) minimizes evaporation. Avoid windy/midday watering.
- Smart controller (Rachio, Orbit B-hyve): weather-adaptive, cuts outdoor use 20–50%.
- Rain sensor / soil moisture sensor: prevents watering when not needed.

## Outdoor: Rain Barrels & Xeriscaping
```
Rain barrel: 55 gal under downspout. 1" rain on 1,000 sq ft roof = ~600 gal captured.
Connect 2-3 barrels in series for capacity. Use screened inlet + overflow to garden.
Xeriscape 7 principles: plan, soil improve, zone by water need, mulch, low-water plants, efficient irrigation, reduce turf.
```
Replace turf with native/drought-tolerant plants: cuts landscape water 50–75%. Mulch 2–3" retains soil moisture.

## Quick Wins
- 5-min shower timer (or Navy shower) → ~12.5 gal saved per shower.
- Keep a pitcher of drinking water in fridge instead of running tap to cool.
- Full loads only in washer/dishwasher.
- Turn off tap while brushing (saves ~4 gal/min).
- Cover pool/spa to cut evaporation up to 95%.
