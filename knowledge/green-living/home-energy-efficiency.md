# Home Energy Efficiency

## Overview
Residential buildings account for ~20% of US energy consumption. Most homes waste 20-30% of energy through inefficiencies. Systematic improvements can cut bills 30-50% with 5-10 year paybacks.

## Energy Audit (DIY or Professional)

### Professional Audit ($200-600, often subsidized)
- **Blower door test**: Quantifies air leakage (ACH50)
- **Thermal imaging**: Finds missing insulation, thermal bridges
- **Duct blaster**: Tests duct leakage
- **Combustion safety**: Checks CO, draft, spillage
- **Report**: Prioritized recommendations with ROI

### DIY Audit Checklist
```
[ ] Check attic insulation depth (target R-38 to R-60)
[ ] Feel for drafts: windows, doors, outlets, penetrations
[ ] Inspect ductwork: disconnected, crushed, uninsulated
[ ] Check water heater temp (120°F max)
[ ] Review utility bills: baseline usage, seasonal patterns
[ ] Test refrigerator seal (dollar bill test)
[ ] Check fireplace damper seal
[ ] Identify vampire loads (kill-a-watt meter)
```

## Air Sealing (Highest ROI)

### Priority Air Leaks (Biggest to Smallest)
1. **Attic bypasses**: Chimney chase, plumbing vents, wiring holes, recessed lights, attic hatch
2. **Basement/crawlspace**: Rim joist, sill plate, penetrations
3. **Living space**: Window/door frames, baseboards, outlet boxes, fireplace
4. **Ductwork**: Joints, boots, register connections

### Materials
| Gap Size | Material |
|----------|----------|
| < 1/4" | Caulk (siliconized acrylic) |
| 1/4" - 1" | Expanding foam (low-expansion for windows) |
| > 1" | Rigid foam + foam sealant |
| Chimney | Metal flashing + high-temp caulk |
| Recessed lights | Airtight IC-rated covers |

### Target: < 3 ACH50 (Passive House: 0.6)
- Typical old home: 10-20 ACH50
- Code minimum (2018 IECC): 3-5 ACH50
- **Every 1 ACH50 reduction ≈ 10-15% heating/cooling savings**

## Insulation

### R-Value Targets (Climate Zone Dependent)
| Zone | Attic | Walls (Cavity + Continuous) | Floor | Basement Wall | Slab Edge |
|------|-------|----------------------------|-------|---------------|-----------|
| 1-2 | R-38 | R-13 + R-5 | R-13 | R-10 | R-5 |
| 3 | R-49 | R-20 + R-5 | R-19 | R-15 | R-10 |
| 4-5 | R-60 | R-20 + R-10 | R-30 | R-15 | R-15 |
| 6-7 | R-60 | R-20 + R-10 | R-38 | R-20 | R-20 |
| 8 | R-60 | R-20 + R-10 | R-38 | R-20 | R-20 |

### Insulation Types
| Type | R/inch | Cost | Best For | Notes |
|------|--------|------|----------|-------|
| Fiberglass batts | 2.9-3.8 | $ | Walls, floors | Easy DIY, gaps reduce effectiveness |
| Blown cellulose | 3.2-3.8 | $ | Attics, dense-pack walls | Recycled, good air barrier |
| Blown fiberglass | 2.2-2.7 | $ | Attics | Settles less than cellulose |
| Spray foam (closed) | 6.0-7.0 | $$$ | Rim joists, roofs, difficult areas | Air + vapor barrier, high GWP blowing agents |
| Spray foam (open) | 3.5-3.8 | $$ | Walls, roofs | Vapor permeable, sound dampening |
| Rigid foam (XPS) | 5.0 | $$ | Basement walls, exterior | Moisture resistant |
| Rigid foam (Polyiso) | 6.5 | $$ | Roofs, walls | Highest R/inch, foil faced |
| Mineral wool | 3.0-3.3 | $$ | Fire-rated, sound, exterior | Fireproof, hydrophobic |

### Critical Details
- **Attic**: Air seal first, then insulate. Baffles at eaves for ventilation.
- **Walls**: Dense-pack cellulose best for existing. Exterior rigid foam prevents thermal bridging.
- **Basement**: Interior rigid foam + frame wall, or exterior waterproofing + foam.
- **Rim joist**: Spray foam or rigid foam + caulk/foam edges.
- **Knee walls**: Insulate + air seal both sides.

## HVAC Systems

### Heat Pumps (Recommended for Most)
| Type | Efficiency | Best For | Cost |
|------|------------|----------|------|
| Air-source (ducted) | 300-400% HSPF2 8-10 | Most homes, retrofit | $8-15k |
| Air-source (mini-split) | 400-500% HSPF2 10-12 | Additions, zones, no ducts | $4-8k/zone |
| Ground-source (geothermal) | 400-600% COP 4-6 | New builds, large lots | $20-40k |
| Air-to-water | 300-450% | Radiant floor, retrofit hydronic | $15-25k |

### Sizing (Critical!)
- **Manual J load calculation**: Required for proper sizing
- **Oversizing**: Short cycles, poor dehumidification, comfort issues
- **Right-size**: Runs longer cycles, better efficiency, comfort
- **Rule of thumb is wrong**: 1 ton/600 sq ft often 2-3x oversized

### Ductwork
- **Location**: Inside conditioned space (best), insulated attic (R-8 min)
- **Sealing**: Mastic + fiberglass mesh tape (not duct tape!)
- **Leakage target**: < 4 CFM25 per 100 sq ft (total), < 2 to outside
- **Insulation**: R-8 supply, R-6 return in unconditioned space

### Controls
- **Smart thermostat**: Learning, geofencing, remote access
- **Setbacks**: 8°F heating, 4°F cooling (heat pumps: smaller setbacks)
- **Zoning**: Motorized dampers + multiple thermostats
- **ERV/HRV**: Required for tight homes (<3 ACH50)

## Water Heating

### Heat Pump Water Heater (HPWH)
- **Efficiency**: 300-350% UEF (vs 95% gas, 100% electric resistance)
- **Requirements**: 700+ cu ft air space, 50°F+ ambient, condensate drain
- **Noise**: 50-55 dB (like refrigerator)
- **Best in**: Garage, basement, utility room
- **Not in**: Conditioned space (steals heat), tight closets

### Solar Thermal
- **Efficiency**: 60-80% (vs PV 20%)
- **Best for**: High hot water use, pool heating
- **Freeze protection**: Drainback or glycol

### Tankless (Gas/Electric)
- **Pros**: Endless hot water, space savings
- **Cons**: High peak demand, may need service upgrade, cold water sandwich
- **Best for**: Low simultaneous use, point-of-use

### Simple Upgrades
- **Temperature**: 120°F (saves 4-22% vs 140°F)
- **Insulation**: Tank wrap (R-8+), pipe insulation (first 6')
- **Timer**: Electric only (gas = minimal savings)
- **Low-flow fixtures**: 1.5 GPM showerhead saves 2,700 gal/yr

## Windows & Doors

### Upgrade Priority
1. **Air seal existing**: Weatherstripping, caulk, storm windows
2. **Interior storm windows**: Low-e, $50-150/window, DIY
3. **Replacement**: When frames failing, major renovation

### Window Specs
| Metric | Target | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| U-factor | ≤ 0.30 (North), ≤ 0.25 (South) | Lower = better insulation |
| SHGC | ≤ 0.40 (North), ≤ 0.25 (South) | Solar heat gain |
| VT | > 0.50 | Visible transmittance |
| AL | ≤ 0.30 cfm/ft² | Air leakage |

### Frame Materials
| Material | U-factor | Cost | Maintenance |
|----------|----------|------|-------------|
| Vinyl | Good | $ | Low |
| Fiberglass | Better | $$ | Very low |
| Wood-clad | Good | $$$ | Medium |
| Aluminum | Poor (thermal break needed) | $$$ | Low |

## Appliances & Lighting

### Refrigerator
- **Top freezer**: Most efficient (10-25% less than side-by-side)
- **Size**: 16-20 cu ft optimal; empty space wastes energy
- **Features to avoid**: Through-door ice/water (+15-20%), anti-sweat heaters
- **Maintenance**: Clean coils annually, check door seals

### Laundry
- **Washer**: Front-load (HE) uses 50% less water/energy
- **Dryer**: Heat pump dryer 50% less energy (ventless option)
- **Behavior**: Cold wash, full loads, air dry when possible

### Lighting
- **All LED**: 75% less energy, 25x life
- **Color temp**: 2700K (warm) living, 4000K (cool) task
- **CRI**: >90 for quality light
- **Controls**: Dimmers, occupancy sensors, daylight sensors
- **Outdoor**: Motion + photocell, shielded (dark sky compliant)

## Renewable Energy

### Solar PV
```
Sizing: Annual kWh / (peak sun hours × 365 × 0.75)
Example: 12,000 kWh / (4.5 × 365 × 0.75) = 9.7 kW

Cost: $2.50-3.50/W installed
Federal tax credit: 30% (through 2032)
Payback: 5-10 years typical
Lifetime: 25-30 years (80% output at 25 yr)
```

### Battery Storage
- **Backup**: Critical loads (fridge, lights, medical, well pump)
- **Time-of-use arbitrage**: Charge off-peak, discharge peak
- **Sizing**: 10-20 kWh typical whole-home
- **Costs dropping**: $1000-1500/kWh installed

### Community Solar
- No roof needed, renters eligible
- 10-15% bill savings typical
- Portable if moving within utility territory

## Smart Home Energy Management

### Monitoring
- **Whole-home**: Sense, Emporia Vue, Eyedro (circuit-level)
- **Outlet-level**: Smart plugs, Shelly, Tasmota
- **Utility portal**: Green Button Data, hourly usage

### Automation
- **Load shifting**: Water heater, EV, dryer to off-peak
- **Demand response**: Utility programs ($50-200/yr)
- **PV self-consumption**: Run loads when solar producing
- **Pre-cooling/heating**: Before peak rates

## Financing & Incentives

### Federal (Inflation Reduction Act)
| Incentive | Amount | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| 25C Tax Credit | 30% up to $1,200/yr | Insulation, windows, doors, heat pumps |
| 25D Tax Credit | 30% | Solar, battery, geothermal |
| HOMES Rebate | Up to $8,000 | Whole-home energy reduction |
| HEAR Rebate | Up to $14,000 | Heat pump, panel, wiring (income-qualified) |

### Utility & State
- **Rebates**: Heat pumps, HPWH, insulation, smart thermostats
- **On-bill financing**: Pay through utility bill
- **PACE financing**: Property tax assessment (commercial mostly)
- **Low-income**: WAP, LIHEAP, utility programs

### Tax Strategies
- **Bundle improvements**: Maximize 25C annual cap
- **Timing**: Claim credit year installed
- **Documentation**: Receipts, AHRI certificates, Manual J
