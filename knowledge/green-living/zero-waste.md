# Zero Waste Lifestyle

## Overview
Zero waste aims to send nothing to landfill or incineration by following the 5 Rs: Refuse, Reduce, Reuse, Recycle, Rot (in order of priority).

## The 5 Rs in Practice

### 1. REFUSE - Say No
- **Freebies**: Promotional items, flyers, samples
- **Single-use**: Straws, utensils, bags, cups
- **Junk mail**: Opt out (DMAchoice, CatalogChoice, PaperKarma)
- **Receipts**: Ask for digital or decline
- **Packaging**: Choose unpackaged, bring containers

### 2. REDUCE - Need Less
- **Audit purchases**: 30-day wait rule for non-essentials
- **Borrow/rent**: Tools, party supplies, formal wear
- **Digital over physical**: Books, movies, documents
- **Multi-use items**: One quality item vs many cheap
- **Consumables**: Concentrated, refillable, package-free

### 3. REUSE - Extend Life
- **Containers**: Jars, bottles, boxes for storage
- **Clothing**: Repair, alter, swap, donate
- **Electronics**: Repair, refurbish, pass on
- **Packaging**: Bubble wrap, boxes for shipping
- **Water**: Pasta water for plants, shower warm-up bucket

### 4. RECYCLE - Properly
- **Know local rules**: They vary wildly
- **Clean & dry**: Contamination ruins loads
- **Separate**: Paper, glass, metal, plastic by type
- **Special streams**: Batteries, electronics, textiles, plastic film
- **Buy recycled**: Close the loop

### 5. ROT - Compost
- **Backyard**: Yard waste + food scraps
- **Vermicomposting**: Worms for apartments
- **Bokashi**: All food waste including meat/dairy
- **Municipal**: Curbside or drop-off
- **Community**: Gardens, farms, ShareWaste app

## Room-by-Room Zero Waste

### Kitchen
**Shopping**
- Bulk bins: Bring jars/bags (tare weight marked)
- Produce bags: Mesh/cotton for fruit/veg
- Reusable bags: Keep in car/purse/bike
- Refill stations: Soap, oil, vinegar, honey
- CSA/farmers market: Seasonal, package-free

**Storage**
- Glass jars: Pantry staples, leftovers, ferments
- Beeswax wraps: Bowl covers, cheese, produce
- Silicone bags: Freezer, snacks, sous vide
- Cloth bowl covers: Rising dough, covering bowls

**Cleaning**
- All-purpose: Vinegar + water + citrus peels
- Scrub: Baking soda + castile soap
- Windows: Vinegar + water + newspaper
- Floors: Hot water + castile soap
- Dishes: Bar soap, brush, Swedish dishcloth

**Food Waste Prevention**
- Meal plan: Shop list from inventory
- Proper storage: Extends life 2-5x
- Eat leftovers: Designated night
- Freeze: Bread, herbs, overripe fruit, broth
- Scraps: Veggie stock, citrus cleaner, regrow

### Bathroom
**Personal Care**
- Bar soap: Body, face, hair, shaving
- Shampoo/conditioner bars: Or refill
- Toothpaste: Tabs, powder, or DIY
- Deodorant: Cream in jar, crystal, DIY
- Floss: Silk in glass, water flosser
- Toothbrush: Bamboo, replaceable head
- Razor: Safety razor, metal blades
- Period: Cup, cloth pads, period underwear
- Tissues: Handkerchiefs, cloth wipes

**Cleaning**
- Toilet: Vinegar + baking soda
- Mirror: Vinegar water
- Drain: Baking soda + vinegar monthly
- Scrub: Bon Ami, baking soda paste

### Laundry
- Detergent: Strips, powder in cardboard, refill
- Stain stick: Bar soap or DIY
- Fabric softener: Vinegar in rinse cycle
- Dryer balls: Wool, 3-6 per load
- Line dry: Rack, line, retractable
- Microfiber filter: Guppyfriend, Cora Ball

- Electronics: Refurbished, repair, recycle

### Office/School

## Special Categories

### Grocery Shopping Strategy
```
1. Check pantry/fridge first
2. Meal plan from what you have
3. Make list with quantities
4. Shop perimeter first (produce, bulk)
5. Bring all containers/bags
5. Weigh/tare containers
7. Buy only what you'll eat
8. Proper storage immediately
```

### Bulk Buying Guide
| Item | Container | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Grains/beans | Wide-mouth jars | Label with bin # |
| Nuts/seeds | Jars or cloth bags | Freeze 48hr for pests |
| Flours | Jars or paper bags | Freeze 48hr |
| Spices | Small jars | Buy small amounts |
| Oils/vinegars | Bottles with pour spout | Funnel helps |
| Honey/nut butter | Wide-mouth jars | Warm to pour |
| Soaps/cleaners | Refill bottles | Note dilution |
| Pet food | Large bucket | Check bulk pet stores |

## Advanced Zero Waste

### DIY Replacements
```
Toothpaste: 4 Tbsp coconut oil + 2 Tbsp baking soda + 15 drops peppermint oil
Deodorant: 1/4 cup baking soda + 1/4 cup arrowroot + 4 Tbsp coconut oil + essential oil
All-purpose cleaner: 1 part vinegar + 1 part water + citrus peels (steep 2 weeks)
Laundry detergent: 1 cup washing soda + 1 cup borax + 1 bar grated castile soap
Dish soap: 1 cup liquid castile + 1 Tbsp washing soda + 10 drops lemon oil
```

## Measuring Progress

### Waste Audit (Weekly)
1. Collect all waste for 1 week
2. Sort: Landfill, recycle, compost, avoidable
3. Weigh/photograph each category
4. Identify top 3 avoidable items
5. Create action plan

### Metrics to Track
- **Landfill volume**: Jar/bag per week/month
- **Recycling contamination**: % clean
- **Food waste**: lbs composted vs landfilled
- **Plastic pieces**: Count single-use avoided
- **Money saved**: Bulk vs packaged, repair vs replace

### Annual Goals
- **Year 1**: 50% reduction, composting established
- **Year 2**: 75% reduction, all rooms converted
- **Year 3**: 90%+ reduction, advocacy/sharing

## Common Challenges & Solutions

| Challenge | Solution |
|-----------|----------|
| No bulk store | Online bulk (Azure Standard, Thrive), co-op, request at store |
| Roommates/family | Lead by example, make it easy, don't police |
| Travel | Kit in carry-on, research destinations, offset |
| Medical needs | Prioritize health, recycle what you can, advocate |
| Time constraints | Batch prep, simple swaps first, progress not perfection |
| Cost concerns | Start free (refuse, reduce), bulk saves long-term |
| Limited recycling | Terracycle, mail-back, advocate for better |
| Composting in apartment | Worm bin, bokashi, community drop-off, pickup service |
