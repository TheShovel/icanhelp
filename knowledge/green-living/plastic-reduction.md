# Plastic Reduction

## The 7 Resin Codes
Plastics carry a chasing-arrows number (1–7) identifying the polymer. Recyclability varies sharply by code and by local facility.

| Code | Polymer | Common Items | Recyclability |
|------|---------|--------------|---------------|
| 1 PET | Polyethylene terephthalate | Water/soda bottles, food jars | Widely curbside-recycled |
| 2 HDPE | High-density polyethylene | Milk jugs, detergent, piping | Widely curbside-recycled |
| 3 PVC | Polyvinyl chloride | Pipes, shower curtains, cling film | Rarely recycled; avoid |
| 4 LDPE | Low-density polyethylene | Bread bags, squeezable bottles | Often drop-off film only |
| 5 PP | Polypropylene | Yogurt tubs, caps, straws | Growing curbside acceptance |
| 6 PS | Polystyrene | Styrofoam, disposable cups | Rarely recycled; avoid |
| 7 Other | Mixed/PC, bioplastics | Multi-layer, BPA items | Usually not recyclable |

Codes 1 and 2 are the safe bets. Codes 3, 6, and 7 should be avoided when possible.

## Single-Use Swaps
| Replace | With | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Plastic bags | Cotton/mesh totes, keep spares in car | One bag = 700+ uses over lifetime |
| Water bottles | Stainless steel (18/8) or glass | Saves ~167 bottles/yr per person |
| Food containers | Glass, stainless, silicone | Freezer- and microwave-safe options |
| Straws | Stainless, bamboo, silicone, or none | Skip entirely for most drinks |
| Cling wrap | Beeswax wraps, silicone lids, bowls | Beeswax lasts ~1 yr with care |
| Coffee cups | Insulated travel mug (16–20 oz) | Many cafés give discount |
| Cutlery | Bamboo spork or metal kit | Carry in zero-waste kit |

## Buying in Bulk
- Bring wide-mouth jars and cloth bags; note tare weight at the scale before filling.
- Target: grains, beans, nuts, flour, oats, spices, coffee, tea, soap, oil, vinegar.
- Freeze dry goods 48 hr after bulk purchase to kill pantry pests.
- Choose concentrates and refills over pre-mixed single bottles.
- Shop perimeter and farmers markets; avoid aisle items wrapped in rigid plastic.

## Avoiding Microplastics
- Never microwave or store hot food/liquid in plastic; heat accelerates leaching and fragmenting.
- Avoid plastic kettles and boiling-water plastic; use glass, steel, or ceramic.
- Synthetic textiles (polyester, acrylic, nylon) shed fibers; wash in Guppyfriend/Cora Ball, use cold gentle cycles, buy natural fibers.
- Many tea bags contain polypropylene seals; switch to loose-leaf with a steel infuser.
- Skip exfoliating "microbead" products (banned in many regions but check labels: PE, PP).
- Dust and vacuum regularly; indoor air and dust concentrate microplastic particles.

## What to Avoid / Swap To
| Avoid | Swap To | Why |
|-------|---------|-----|
| Bottled water | Filter + refillable bottle | Cost + plastic |
| Plastic produce bags | Reusable mesh | Single-use waste |
| PS takeout clamshells | Bring your own container | Not recycled |
| Plastic wrap | Beeswax/silicone | Compostable/reusable |
| Synthetic sponges | Loofah, cellulose, brush | Biodegradable |
| Disposable razors | Safety razor + steel blades | Long-term waste cut |
| Plastic toothbrushes | Bamboo or replaceable-head | Compostable handle |

## Quick Wins
- Refuse straws, lids, and utensils by default when ordering.
- Keep a "go kit" (bottle, cup, bag, utensils) in car and backpack.
- Switch one recurring purchase (milk, detergent, coffee) to refillable.
- Replace cling film with a single set of beeswax wraps this week.
- Audit trash for one week; target the top 3 plastic items for swaps.
