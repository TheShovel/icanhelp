# Sustainable Transportation

## Walking & Cycling
- Shortest, zero-emission trips under 2 miles are ideal by foot/bike.
- E-bike replaces car trips up to ~15 miles at 15–28 mph assist.
- Cargo bikes handle groceries/kids; replace second car for many households.
- Health: cycling 10 mi/day ≈ 1,000 kcal/week, cuts CVD risk ~10%.
- Safety: lights front/rear, helmet, bike lanes; commute via low-traffic routes.

## E-Bikes
```
Range: 20-50 mi per charge (250-750W motor, 300-700Wh battery).
Cost: $800-3,000; quality commuter ~$1,500. Battery replacement ~$400.
Charge: 3-6 hrs from wall; remove battery, store 20-80% for longevity.
Maintenance: chain lube monthly, tire pressure 40-65 psi, brake pads yearly.
Class 1 (pedal-assist 20mph), Class 2 (throttle), Class 3 (28mph pedal-assist).
```
E-bike energy: ~10–20 Wh/mile vs car ~300 Wh/mile → 15–30x less energy.

## Public Transit
- Bus/rail emits ~1/4 the CO2 per mile of a solo car (per passenger).
- Monthly pass often cheaper than parking + fuel for daily commuters.
- Combine with bike: transit + last-mile e-bike beats car on cost and time in cities.
- Plan with transit apps (Transit, Citymapper); off-peak avoids crowding.

## Electric Vehicles (EVs)
```
Efficiency: ~15-25 kWh/100km (BEV). ICE ~ 7-10 L/100km.
Charging L1: 120V, ~5-8 km/hr (overnight trickle, no install).
Charging L2: 240V, ~30-60 km/hr (home wallbox or public).
Charging L3 (DC fast): 50-350 kW, 10-80% in 20-40 min.
Range: 250-500 km typical new BEV; cold weather cuts 20-30%.
```
Range anxiety is mostly psychological: 95% of trips <80 km; charge at home nightly.

## Total Cost of Ownership (EV vs ICE)
- Electricity ~$0.04–0.08/km vs petrol ~$0.12–0.18/km.
- Maintenance: EV has ~no oil, fewer parts; ~$0.06/km vs ICE $0.10/km.
- Incentives: US federal 30% tax credit up to $7,500 (income/MSRP caps apply).
- Depreciation higher on early EVs; battery warranties 8yr/160,000 km typical.

## Carpooling & Ride-Share
- Occupancy 1.2→2.0 people/trip halves per-person emissions.
- Apps: Waze Carpool, BlaBlaCar, Slack/company commute groups.
- HOV lanes + toll discounts reward 2+ occupants.
- Combine errands into one trip; avoid cold starts (warm engine = efficient).

## Fuel-Efficient Driving (ICE/Hybrid)
```
Smooth accel: gentle throttle saves 10-20% vs aggressive.
Maintain 90-100 km/h: each +10 km/h above raises drag ~10%.
Tire pressure: +10 psi under max cuts rolling resistance ~2-3%.
Remove roof rack/extra weight: 45 kg ≈ +1-2% fuel.
Coast/anticipate: lift early, use engine braking, avoid hard braking.
Idle off: >10 sec idle wastes more than restart (modern stop-start).
```

## Vehicle Comparison
| Type | Fuel use | Range | Upfront cost | Best for |
|------|----------|-------|--------------|----------|
| ICE | 7-10 L/100km | 600+ km | Lowest | Long trips, no charging |
| Hybrid (HEV) | 4-5 L/100km | 800+ km | +$3-5k | City + highway mix |
| PHEV | 1-2 L/100km + plug | 40-80 km EV | +$5-10k | Short EV commute |
| BEV | 15-25 kWh/100km | 250-500 km | +$8-15k | Daily <300km, home charge |

## Quick Wins
- Replace shortest car trips (<3 km) with bike/walk — biggest per-trip savings.
- Combine errands; keep tires inflated; drive smoothly.
- If buying, size EV range to daily need (not rare road trips) + home L2 charger.
- Use transit pass + bike for commute; sell second car if viable.
