# Community Climate Action

## Individual vs Collective Impact
Individual actions (diet, flying, driving) matter but are capped by systemic constraints. Collective action changes the rules that bound individual choice. A useful framing: personal change builds credibility and skills; collective change moves the lever that scales to millions.

```
personal action   — reduces your own footprint, models behavior, builds habits
collective action — shifts policy, infrastructure, and norms (larger multiplier)
both combined     — personal practice + organizing = highest real-world effect
```

Consumption choices alone cannot fix emissions locked into grid, transit, and land-use systems. Treat voting and organizing as climate actions with the largest marginal impact.

## Joining Existing Initiatives
Low-effort entry points that already have structure and momentum:

```
community garden      — local food, soil carbon, social resilience; find via city parks dept
tool library          — borrow drills/saws instead of buying; cuts embodied emissions
repair cafe           — volunteer fixers extend device/garment life; search "repaircafe.org"
energy co-op          — group bulk-buy of solar/heat pumps; community-owned generation
mutual aid network    — disaster prep + resource sharing; resilient to supply shocks
```

Start by attending one meeting before committing. Most groups need helpers more than leaders.

## Starting Something New
If nothing local exists, seed it:

```
pick one narrow gap   — e.g. "no bike repair night near the university"
recruit 3–5 founders  — avoid solo burnout; split comms, logistics, money
use free infra        — Signal group, Meetup/fedi, library room, school bulletin
run a pilot event      — one repair cafe or swap; measure attendance, not perfection
hand off early         — document process so it survives your departure
```

Keep governance lightweight. A shared doc + rotating facilitator beats a 20-page charter.

## Advocacy & Systems Leverage
Direct the largest emissions levers through institutions:

```
local council         — comment on zoning, transit, building codes; 2-min public slots
voting                — prioritize climate-aligned candidates at every level
divestment            — move bank/pension funds out of fossil fuels (check bank scorecards)
public comment        — utility rate cases and permit hearings shape local energy mix
union / workplace     — push procurement and commute policies from inside
```

One council rezoning for transit-oriented housing can outstrip a lifetime of personal flying cuts.

## Carbon Footprint Context
Understand where emissions actually come from before prioritizing:

| Sector (global avg) | Share | High-leverage personal move |
|---------------------|-------|------------------------------|
| Energy (heat/elec)  | ~25%  | heat pump, insulation, co-op solar |
| Transport           | ~15%  | EV/bike/transit, fewer flights |
| Food                | ~25%  | less red meat & dairy, local |
| Goods/services      | ~35%  | buy less, repair, shared access |

Wealthy households emit far above average; the top 10% globally emit ~10x the bottom 10%. Your advocacy multiplier grows with your civic access.

## Actions by Effort vs Impact
| Action | Effort | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| Join a tool library | Low | Med | Instant, social |
| Attend council meeting | Low | High | Policy lever |
| Start repair cafe | High | High | Replicable |
| Switch to co-op energy | Med | Med | Needs group |
| Organize neighbors | Med | High | Compounds |
| Divest bank funds | Low | Med | Signal + capital |

Prioritize High/Med-impact, Low-effort first; reserve High-effort builds once a core group exists.
