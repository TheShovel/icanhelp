# Personal Finance Basics

## Budgeting
- **50/30/20 rule**: 50% needs (rent, food, utilities), 30% wants (entertainment, dining out), 20% savings/debt
- **Zero-based budget**: every dollar assigned a job, income - expenses = 0
- **Envelope system**: cash in labeled envelopes for each category, when empty stop spending
- Track spending for 30 days to see where money actually goes
- Review and adjust budget monthly

## Savings
- **Emergency fund**: 3-6 months of essential expenses in high-yield savings account
- **Emergency fund first** before investing or extra debt payments
- High-yield savings account (HYSA): currently ~4-5% APY, FDIC insured
- Money Market Account (MMA): similar to HYSA, sometimes with check writing
- CDs: higher rate for locking money for fixed term (penalty for early withdrawal)
- Automate savings: set up auto-transfer from checking to savings on payday

## Banking
- **Checking**: daily transactions, bills, debit card — usually no interest
- **Savings**: earn interest, limited withdrawals (6/month federal rule, now relaxed)
- **Online banks** (Ally, SoFi, Capital One 360): higher rates, fewer fees than brick-and-mortar
- **Credit unions**: non-profit, often better rates, require membership
- Avoid: monthly maintenance fees, overdraft fees, ATM fees outside network
- Direct deposit paycheck for fastest access

## Credit
- **Credit score range**: 300-850 (FICO)
  - Excellent: 750+ — best rates on loans
  - Good: 700-749
  - Fair: 650-699
  - Poor: below 650
- **Factors**: payment history (35%), amounts owed (30%), length of history (15%), new credit (10%), credit mix (10%)
- Pay statement balance in full every month — avoid interest
- Utilization: keep under 30% of total credit limit (lower is better)
- Never close oldest credit card (shortens history, lowers score)
- Check credit report annually at annualcreditreport.com (free)
- Late payments stay 7 years, bankruptcy 10 years

## Debt
- **Good debt**: mortgage, student loans (invests in future, usually lower rate)
- **Bad debt**: credit cards, payday loans (high interest, no asset)
- **Avalanche method**: pay minimum on all, extra to highest interest rate first (saves most interest)
- **Snowball method**: pay minimum on all, extra to smallest balance first (psychological wins)
- **Debt consolidation**: combine multiple debts into one loan, ideally lower rate
- **Balance transfer**: move credit card debt to 0% intro APR card (typically 3-5% fee)
- Student loans: federal have income-driven repayment plans, forgiveness options

## Investing Basics
- **Stock**: ownership in company — higher risk/reward
- **Bond**: loan to company/government — lower risk, fixed interest
- **Mutual fund**: pool of stocks/bonds managed by professionals
- **ETF** (Exchange Traded Fund): like mutual fund but trades like stock, lower fees
- **Index fund**: ETF/mutual fund that tracks an index (S&P 500, total market) — low fees, diversified
- **Compound interest**: returns earn returns — start early, time is the biggest factor
- **Dollar-cost averaging**: invest fixed amount regularly regardless of price — reduces timing risk
- **Rule of 72**: 72 / annual return = years to double money (e.g., 72/10% = 7.2 years)

## Retirement Accounts
- **401(k)**: employer-sponsored, pre-tax contributions, employer match is free money (contribute at least to match)
- **IRA** (Individual Retirement Account): $7,000/yr (2024, under 50), $8,000 if 50+
- **Roth IRA**: contributions after-tax, withdrawals tax-free in retirement (income limits apply)
- **Traditional IRA**: contributions pre-tax (deductible), taxed on withdrawal
- **HSA** (Health Savings Account): triple tax-advantaged (pre-tax in, tax-free growth, tax-free for medical expenses)
- Target-date funds: automatically adjust risk as you approach retirement age

## Taxes
- **Gross income**: total before tax
- **Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**: gross minus deductions (IRA, student loan interest, HSA)
- **Taxable income**: AGI minus standard or itemized deductions
- **Standard deduction 2024**: $14,600 single, $29,200 married filing jointly
- **Marginal tax brackets**: progressive — only income in each bracket is taxed at that rate
- **Withholding**: check W-4 to ensure enough is withheld (avoid big bill or big refund)
- **Tax-advantaged accounts**: 401(k), IRA, HSA reduce taxable income
- Keep records for 3-7 years in case of audit
- File electronically, direct deposit refund

## Insurance
- **Health insurance**: get through employer, marketplace, or government (Medicaid/Medicare)
  - Premium: monthly payment
  - Deductible: amount before insurance pays
  - Copay: fixed amount per visit
  - Out-of-pocket maximum: most you'll pay in a year
- **Renters/homeowners**: covers property, liability
- **Auto**: liability (required), comprehensive, collision, uninsured motorist
- **Life**: term life (cheap, fixed term) vs whole life (expensive, investment component — usually not worth it)
- **Disability**: protects income if you can't work — more likely than death during working years

## Common Mistakes
- Living paycheck to paycheck with no emergency fund
- Carrying credit card debt month to month
- Not contributing to 401(k) enough to get full employer match
- Buying new cars (depreciates 20%+ immediately)
- Timing the market instead of time in market
- Not having health insurance (one accident can bankrupt you)
- Ignoring retirement until 40s/50s
- Lifestyle inflation: spending more as you earn more
