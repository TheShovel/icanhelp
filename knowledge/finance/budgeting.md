# Budgeting & Saving

Personal budgeting methods, saving strategies, and expense tracking.

## Budgeting Methods
- **50/30/20 rule (Elizabeth Warren)**: 50% after-tax income for needs (housing, food, utilities, transport, minimum debt payments), 30% for wants (dining, travel, entertainment, hobbies), 20% for savings/debt (investments, emergency fund, extra debt payments, retirement). Good starting point, adjust for your situation
- **Zero-based budget**: Every dollar assigned a job — income − expenses = 0. Allocate to categories until nothing left. Most detailed, requires tracking every transaction. Used in You Need A Budget (YNAB) software
- **Envelope system**: Cash for variable expenses in labeled envelopes (groceries, dining, entertainment, gas). When envelope empty, stop spending. Good for overspenders in specific categories
- **Pay-yourself-first**: Automate savings/retirement first, spend rest. Save 15-20% of income before paying bills. Savings are non-negotiable, budget remaining expenses
- **Reverse budgeting**: Track income, subtract savings goals, spend the rest freely. Requires discipline not to overspend savings portion

## Expense Tracking
- **Apps**: YNAB (zero-based budgeting, $99/year), Mint (free, owned by Credit Karma), Personal Capital/Empower (free, good for net worth/investments), EveryDollar (free Ramsey version + paid), Goodbudget (digital envelope system)
- **Manual tracking**: Spreadsheet (Google Sheets/Excel) — categories, monthly totals, formulas. Most customizable, requires consistency
- **Categories**: Housing (rent/mortgage, utilities, insurance, property tax), Transport (car payment, gas, insurance, maintenance, tolls), Food (groceries, dining), Healthcare (insurance premiums, copays, prescriptions), Personal (clothing, grooming, subscriptions), Debt (credit cards, loans), Savings (emergency, retirement, sinking funds)
- **Review frequency**: Daily (quick check of spending), Weekly (reconcile categories), Monthly (full budget review + adjust next month)

## Emergency Fund
- **Amount**: 3-6 months of essential expenses. If variable income, self-employed, or single income: 6-9 months. If dual income stable jobs: 3 months
- **Where to keep**: High-yield savings account (HYSA — 4-5% APY currently), money market account. NOT in stock market (too volatile)
- **What counts as emergency**: Job loss, major medical expense, urgent home repair (roof leak, broken furnace), car repair needed for work. NOT: vacation, TV sale, Christmas gifts
- **Building it**: Start with $1,000 (Dave Ramsey baby step 1), build to 1 month expenses, then 3, then 6. Use windfalls (tax refund, bonus, gift money) to accelerate

## Saving Strategies
- **Automate savings**: Set up automatic transfers on payday to HYSA, IRA, investment account. "Set and forget" — reduces willpower needed
- **Sinking funds**: Save monthly for predictable irregular expenses — car insurance (if paid semi-annual), property taxes, holidays, car repairs, travel. Divide annual cost by 12, automate monthly transfer
- **52-week money challenge**: Save $1 week 1, $2 week 2... $52 week 52 = $1,378 total. Or reverse (start high, decrease)
- **No-spend challenge**: Choose period (week, month, season) where you only spend on necessities. Cook all meals, no shopping, free entertainment. Resets spending habits, reveals wasteful patterns
- **Cash stuffing (envelope system 2.0)**: Each category gets cash envelope. Social media trend popularized on TikTok — seeing physical cash disappear curbs spending
- **Rounding up apps**: Acorns, Qapital — round purchases to nearest dollar, invest the difference. Good for beginners, but fees eat returns on small balances

## Cutting Expenses
- **Housing**: Refinance mortgage (if rates lower), rent out room, negotiate rent, move to cheaper area
- **Food**: Meal planning (reduce food waste 30%), cook at home (vs dining cost 3x), buy store brands, bulk bin staples (rice, beans, oats), limit takeout
- **Transportation**: Drive car longer (new car payment avg $700/mo), bike/walk/public transit, shop insurance annually, proper tire inflation (save 3% gas)
- **Subscriptions**: Audit recurring charges — streaming services, apps, gym, boxes. Average person spends $200+/month on unused subscriptions. Use Rocket Money or manual audit
- **Insurance**: Bundle home+auto, raise deductibles ($500→$1000), shop every 2-3 years, check discounts (good driver, auto-pay, loyalty, paperless)
- **Energy**: LED bulbs (use 75% less energy), programmable thermostat (set back 7-10°F while away/ sleeping saves 10% yearly), seal drafts, unplug electronics (vampire drain = 5-10% of electric bill)

## Frugal Mindset
- **Cost-per-use**: Convert purchase price to cost per use. $200 boots worn 200 days/year for 3 years = $0.33/day. Cheap $50 boots lasting 6 months = $0.42/day. Quality often cheaper long-term
- **30-day rule**: For non-essential purchases over $50, wait 30 days. Often the urge passes. Write it down with cost, revisit monthly
- **Opportunity cost**: Money spent on X could have grown at 7% in market for 30 years. "Is this worth my future self's wealth?"
- **BIFL (Buy It For Life)**: Invest in items that last decades — cast iron cookware, quality boots, wool clothing, stainless steel kitchen tools. Subreddit r/BuyItForLife
- **Library over buying**: Books, movies, audiobooks, magazines, museum passes, tool libraries. Free entertainment
