# Emergency Fund

## Purpose
An emergency fund covers unexpected necessary expenses without high-interest debt. It is for job loss, urgent medical costs, car repairs, home repairs, travel for family emergencies, or insurance deductibles. It is not for planned purchases or routine bills.

## Starter Target
Start with a small buffer such as $500–$1,000. This handles many common shocks and prevents overdrafts. After high-interest debt is controlled, grow toward a larger fund.

## Full Target
A common target is 3–6 months of essential expenses. Use the higher end if income is variable, dependents rely on you, your job is unstable, or insurance deductibles are high. Use the lower end if income is stable, expenses are low, and multiple earners share the household.

## What Counts as Essential Expenses
Include rent or mortgage, utilities, groceries, transportation, insurance premiums, minimum debt payments, medications, childcare, and basic communication. Exclude vacations, restaurants, subscriptions, upgrades, and extra debt payments.

## Where to Keep It
Use a safe, liquid account: high-yield savings, money market deposit account, or credit union savings. Avoid stocks, crypto, long CDs with penalties, or anything that can lose value right when you need cash.

## Building Plan
```text
monthly contribution = target amount / months to target
```
Automate transfers after payday. If money is tight, start with a tiny automatic transfer and add windfalls such as refunds, bonuses, gifts, or side-income until the starter fund is complete.

## When to Use It
Use the fund for necessary, urgent, and unexpected costs. If a cost is predictable, create a sinking fund instead. Examples: annual insurance, holiday gifts, tires, school fees, property taxes, and planned medical appointments.

## Refill Rule
After using the fund, pause nonessential spending and rebuild it before increasing lifestyle spending. If the emergency reveals a recurring expense, update the budget so the fund is not used for the same issue repeatedly.

## Debt Balance
If you have high-interest debt, keep a starter emergency fund while attacking the debt. Without any buffer, one surprise bill can force new borrowing. After expensive debt is gone, expand the emergency fund.
