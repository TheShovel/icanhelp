# Investment Fundamentals

## Overview
Investing is the act of allocating resources (usually money) with the expectation of generating income or profit. Unlike saving, investing involves risk but offers higher potential returns over the long term.

## Core Concepts

### Risk vs Return
```
Risk-Free Rate → Government Bonds → Corporate Bonds → Stocks → Alternatives → Speculation
     ~4%              ~5-6%              ~6-8%          ~10%        ~8-15%          >15% (high loss risk)
```

### Time Value of Money
- **Compound Interest**: Earnings on earnings (the "eighth wonder of the world")
- **Rule of 72**: Years to double = 72 ÷ annual return %
  - 7% return → doubles in ~10.3 years
  - 10% return → doubles in ~7.2 years

### Inflation Impact
- **Real Return** = Nominal Return - Inflation
- Historical US inflation: ~3% average
- Cash loses purchasing power over time

## Portfolio Construction

### Modern Portfolio Theory
- **Efficient Frontier**: Maximum return for given risk
- **Diversification**: Only free lunch in investing
- **Correlation**: Combine assets that move differently

### Classic Allocations

#### 60/40 Portfolio (Traditional)
- 60% Stocks (Total Market)
- 40% Bonds (Intermediate Treasury)
- Historical: ~8-9% return, ~10% volatility

#### Three-Fund Portfolio (Bogleheads)
```
US Total Stock Market     60%  (VTI/VTSAX)
International Stock       20%  (VXUS/VTIAX)
US Total Bond Market      20%  (BND/VBTLX)
```
- Rebalance annually or when >5% off target

#### All-Weather (Ray Dalio)
```
Stocks                    30%
Long-term Treasuries      40%
Intermediate Treasuries   15%
Gold                       7.5%
Commodities               7.5%
```
- Designed for all economic environments

#### Age-Based (Glide Path)
| Age | Stocks | Bonds | Cash |
|-----|--------|-------|------|
| 20s-30s | 90-100% | 0-10% | 0% |
| 40s | 80% | 20% | 0% |
| 50s | 70% | 25% | 5% |
| 60s | 60% | 35% | 5% |
| 70s+ | 50% | 40% | 10% |

### Factor Investing (Smart Beta)
| Factor | Premium | Implementation |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| **Value** | 3-5% | Low P/E, P/B, P/FCF |
| **Size (Small Cap)** | 2-3% | Small cap tilt |
| **Momentum** | 5-8% | 12-month winners |
| **Quality** | 2-4% | High ROE, low debt |
| **Low Volatility** | 2-3% | Low beta stocks |
| **Dividend Yield** | 1-2% | High sustainable yield |

### Active Management
- **When it works**: Inefficient markets (small caps, emerging, bonds)
- **Costs matter**: 1% fee = ~25% of returns over 30 years
- **Persistence**: Few managers beat index consistently after fees

## Account Types & Tax Efficiency

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts (Priority Order)

#### 1. 401(k)/403(b) Match
- **Free money**: 100% immediate return on match
- Limit 2024: $23,000 (+ $7,500 catch-up 50+)
- Traditional or Roth options

#### 2. HSA (Triple Tax Advantage)
- **Contributions**: Pre-tax / tax-deductible
- **Growth**: Tax-free
- **Withdrawals**: Tax-free for qualified medical
- Limit 2024: $4,150 individual / $8,300 family
- **Invest the balance**, pay medical out of pocket

#### 3. Roth IRA
- **Contributions**: After-tax
- **Growth**: Tax-free
- **Withdrawals**: Tax-free (qualified)
- Limit 2024: $7,000 (+ $1,000 catch-up 50+)
- Income limits: Phase out $146-161k single / $230-240k married

#### 4. Traditional IRA
- **Deductible** if no workplace plan or low income
- **Non-deductible** → Backdoor Roth strategy
- RMDs start at 73 (75 in 2033)

#### 5. Mega Backdoor Roth
- After-tax 401(k) contributions → In-plan Roth conversion
- Up to $69,000 total (2024) minus pre-tax/Roth + match
- Plan must allow after-tax + in-service distribution

#### 6. Taxable Brokerage
- No limits, no penalties
- **Tax-efficient placement**: See below
- Tax-loss harvesting available

### Asset Location (Tax Efficiency)
| Account Type | Best Holdings |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Tax-Free (Roth/HSA)** | Highest expected return (Small cap, EM, REITs, Growth) |
| **Tax-Deferred (Traditional 401k/IRA)** | Bonds, REITs, High-yield, Active funds, Commodities |
| **Taxable** | Broad index funds (low turnover), Muni bonds, I-Bonds, ETFs |

## Investment Strategies

### Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA)
- Invest fixed amount regularly regardless of price
- Reduces timing risk, emotional discipline
- **Lump sum statistically wins 2/3 of time** but DCA reduces regret

### Rebalancing
| Method | Trigger | Pros |
|--------|---------|------|
| **Calendar** | Quarterly/Annually | Simple, disciplined |
| **Threshold** | ±5% from target | Responsive, fewer trades |
| **Cash Flow** | New contributions to underweight | No transaction costs |
| **Combined** | Annual + 5% bands | Best of both |

### Tax-Loss Harvesting
1. Sell losing position in taxable account
2. Buy similar (not "substantially identical") replacement
3. Use loss to offset gains + $3,000 ordinary income
4. Carry forward excess losses indefinitely
- **Wash Sale Rule**: Can't repurchase same/substantially identical within 30 days

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- **Single stock**: ≤5% of portfolio (≤1% if speculative)
- **Sector**: ≤20-25%
- **Single country**: ≤Home bias (US ~60% of world market)
- **Alternatives**: ≤10-15% total

### Stop-Loss / Exit Rules
- **Trailing stop**: 15-20% below peak (trend following)
- **Fundamental stop**: Thesis broken (not price)
- **Time stop**: Re-evaluate thesis annually

### Hedging (Advanced)
- **Protective Puts**: Insurance on positions
- **Collar**: Sell call + buy put (limits upside/downside)
- **Inverse ETFs**: Short exposure (daily reset, decay)
- **Tail Risk**: Long-dated OTM puts, long vol

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| **Market Timing** | Stay invested, DCA |
| **Chasing Performance** | Buy low, rebalance to target |
| **High Fees** | Use index funds/ETFs <0.1% |
| **Overconcentration** | Diversify, position limits |
| **Emotional Decisions** | Written IPS, automate |
| **Ignoring Taxes** | Asset location, tax-loss harvesting |
| **No Plan** | Write Investment Policy Statement |
| **Leverage** | Avoid margin, 2x/3x ETFs for long-term |

## Investment Policy Statement (IPS) Template

```
1. OBJECTIVE
   - Goal: [Retirement / House / Education / Wealth]
   - Time Horizon: [X years]
   - Required Return: [X% real]
   - Risk Tolerance: [Conservative/Moderate/Aggressive]

2. ASSET ALLOCATION
   - Strategic Target: [X% Stocks / Y% Bonds / Z% Other]
   - Rebalancing: [Annual / 5% bands / Cash flow]
   - Rebalancing Method: [Sell/Buy / Cash flow directed]

3. INVESTMENT SELECTION
   - Vehicle: [Index funds / ETFs / Individual securities]
   - Providers: [Vanguard / Fidelity / Schwab / Other]
   - Expense Ratio Limit: [≤0.1% core, ≤0.5% satellite]

4. CONSTRAINTS
   - Liquidity Needs: [X months expenses in cash]
   - Tax Considerations: [Asset location strategy]
   - Ethical/ESG: [Exclusions / Tilts / None]
   - Leverage: [None / Limited / Specified]

5. MONITORING & REVIEW
   - Performance Benchmark: [Appropriate index blend]
   - Review Frequency: [Quarterly / Semi-annual]
   - Trigger for Change: [Strategy drift / Life change / Fee increase]

6. BEHAVIORAL GUARDRAILS
   - No individual stocks >5%
   - No panic selling (48-hour rule)
   - Annual "pre-mortem" review
```

## Monitoring & Benchmarks

### Appropriate Benchmarks
| Portfolio | Benchmark |
|-----------|-----------|
| US Total Stock | CRSP US Total Market / S&P 500 |
| International | FTSE All-World ex-US / MSCI EAFE |
| Bonds | Bloomberg US Aggregate |
| 60/40 | 60% S&P 500 / 40% Bloomberg Agg |
| Global | MSCI ACWI / FTSE All-World |

### Key Metrics to Track
- **Total Return**: Price + dividends + interest
- **Risk-Adjusted Return**: Sharpe Ratio (return - risk-free) / volatility
- **Maximum Drawdown**: Peak to trough decline
- **Tracking Error**: Deviation from benchmark
- **Cost Ratio**: All-in fees as % of portfolio
