# Personal Finance Fundamentals

## Overview
Personal finance is the management of money and financial decisions for an individual or family. It covers budgeting, saving, investing, insurance, taxes, retirement, and estate planning.

## Budgeting

### The 50/30/20 Rule
```
50% → Needs (housing, food, utilities, transport, insurance)
30% → Wants (dining out, entertainment, hobbies, travel)
20% → Savings/Debt (emergency fund, retirement, debt payoff)
```

### Zero-Based Budgeting
Every dollar has a job before the month begins:
```
Income - Expenses - Savings - Debt = $0
```

### Budget Categories
| Category | Typical % | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Housing | 25-35% | Rent/mortgage, property tax, HOA |
| Transportation | 10-15% | Car payment, gas, insurance, maintenance |
| Food | 10-15% | Groceries, dining out |
| Utilities | 5-10% | Electric, water, internet, phone |
| Insurance | 5-15% | Health, life, disability, auto |
| Savings | 10-20% | Emergency, retirement, goals |
| Debt | 5-15% | Student loans, credit cards |
| Personal | 5-10% | Clothing, personal care, gifts |
| Entertainment | 5-10% | Streaming, hobbies, travel |

## Emergency Fund

### Target Amount
| Situation | Target |
|-----------|--------|
| Stable job, dual income | 3 months expenses |
| Single income, variable income | 6 months expenses |
| Freelancer/contractor | 9-12 months expenses |
| High-risk job | 6-12 months expenses |

### Where to Keep
1. **High-Yield Savings Account** (HYSA) - 4-5% APY, FDIC insured
2. **Money Market Account** - Similar to HYSA, check writing
3. **Treasury Bills** - 4-5%, state tax exempt
4. **Short-term Bond Funds** - Slightly higher yield, small risk
5. **CD Ladder** - Locked rates, less liquid

**NOT**: Checking account, cash at home, stocks, crypto, credit cards

## Debt Management

### Debt Types by Priority
1. **Toxic Debt** - Payday loans, title loans (>100% APR)
2. **High-Interest** - Credit cards (15-30% APR)
3. **Moderate-Interest** - Personal loans, private student loans (5-15%)
4. **Low-Interest** - Federal student loans, mortgage (3-7%)
5. **Strategic Debt** - 0% promo, business loans with ROI

### Payoff Strategies

#### Avalanche Method (Mathematically Optimal)
1. List debts by interest rate (highest first)
2. Pay minimums on all
3. Put extra toward highest rate
4. Roll payment to next when paid off

#### Snowball Method (Psychological Wins)
1. List debts by balance (smallest first)
2. Pay minimums on all
3. Put extra toward smallest balance
4. Roll payment to next when paid off

#### Hybrid Approach
- Quick wins: Pay off any debt < $1,000 first
- Then avalanche for the rest

### Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)
```
DTI = Monthly Debt Payments / Gross Monthly Income

Front-end (housing only): < 28%
Back-end (all debt): < 36% (ideal), < 43% (max for mortgage)
```

### Refinancing Considerations
- Rate reduction ≥ 0.5-1%
- Break-even: Closing costs / Monthly savings = Months to break even
- Don't extend term unless necessary
- Watch for prepayment penalties

## Credit Scores

### FICO Score Components
| Factor | Weight | Tips |
|--------|--------|------|
| Payment History | 35% | Never miss payments, auto-pay |
| Credit Utilization | 30% | Keep < 10% (reporting date matters) |
| Length of History | 15% | Keep oldest cards open |
| Credit Mix | 10% | Revolving + installment |
| New Credit | 10% | Limit hard inquiries |

### Score Ranges
```
800-850: Exceptional
740-799: Very Good
670-739: Good
580-669: Fair
300-579: Poor
```

### Optimization Tactics
- Pay before statement date (lowers reported utilization)
- Request credit limit increases (soft pull)
- Become authorized user on old account
- Dispute errors (annualcreditreport.com)
- Keep 1-2 cards active with small recurring charges

## Retirement Planning

### The 4% Rule (Safe Withdrawal Rate)
```
Required Portfolio = Annual Expenses × 25

Example: $60,000/year expenses → $1.5M portfolio
```
*Based on 30-year retirement, 60/40 portfolio, 95% success rate*

### More Conservative Approaches
- 3.5% rule → 28.6× expenses (longer retirement, lower returns)
- 3% rule → 33× expenses (very conservative)
- Dynamic withdrawals (Guyton-Klinger, VPW)

### Retirement Milestones
| Age | Action |
|-----|--------|
| 50 | Catch-up contributions start |
| 55 | Rule of 55 (401(k) penalty-free if separated) |
| 59½ | IRA/401(k) penalty-free withdrawals |
| 62 | Social Security earliest (reduced) |
| 65 | Medicare eligibility |
| 66-67 | Full Social Security (FRA) |
| 70 | Max Social Security benefit |
| 73/75 | RMDs begin (born 1951-1959 / 1960+) |

### Social Security
```
Full Retirement Age (FRA):
Born 1943-1954: 66
Born 1955-1959: 66 + 2 months per year
Born 1960+: 67

Early (62): -30% vs FRA
Delayed (70): +24% vs FRA (8%/year)

Spousal: 50% of partner's FRA benefit
Survivor: 100% of deceased's benefit
```

## Tax Optimization

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts
| Account | Contribution | Growth | Withdrawal |
|---------|--------------|--------|------------|
| Traditional 401(k)/IRA | Pre-tax | Tax-deferred | Ordinary income |
| Roth 401(k)/IRA | After-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free (qualified) |
| HSA | Pre-tax | Tax-free | Tax-free (medical) |
| 529 | After-tax | Tax-deferred | Tax-free (education) |

### Tax-Loss Harvesting
1. Sell losing positions in taxable account
2. Buy similar (not "substantially identical") replacement
3. Offset up to $3,000 ordinary income/year
4. Carry forward excess losses indefinitely

### Asset Location
```
Taxable Account:        Stocks (qualified dividends, LTCG)
                        Municipal bonds
                        Low-turnover index funds

Tax-Deferred (Trad):    Bonds, REITs, High-yield
                        Active funds (high turnover)
                        Commodities

Tax-Free (Roth/HSA):    Highest growth potential
                        Small-cap value, Emerging markets
```

## Insurance

### Essential Coverage
| Type | Purpose | How Much |
|------|---------|----------|
| Health | Medical expenses | Employer/Marketplace |
| Term Life | Income replacement | 10-12× income, 20-30 year term |
| Disability | Income if unable to work | 60-70% income, own-occupation |
| Auto | Liability + vehicle | State min + umbrella |
| Home/Renters | Property + liability | Replacement cost |
| Umbrella | Extra liability | $1M+ (if assets > $500k) |

### Not Usually Needed
- Whole/Universal life (investment + insurance)
- Accidental death (covered by term)
- Credit insurance
- Extended warranties
- Pet insurance (often self-insure better)

## Estate Planning

### Essential Documents
1. **Will** - Asset distribution, guardianship
2. **Durable Power of Attorney** - Financial decisions if incapacitated
3. **Medical Power of Attorney** - Healthcare decisions
4. **Advance Directive/Living Will** - End-of-life wishes
5. **HIPAA Authorization** - Medical info access
6. **Beneficiary Designations** - 401(k), IRA, life insurance, POD/TOD accounts
7. **Trust** (if needed) - Probate avoidance, complex distributions

### Probate vs Non-Probate
```
Probate Assets (will controls):
- Solely owned property
- Tenants in common
- No beneficiary designation

Non-Probate (pass directly):
- Joint tenancy with right of survivorship
- Beneficiary designations (retirement, life insurance)
- POD/TOD accounts
- Trust assets
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **No emergency fund** → Credit card debt spiral
2. **Not getting 401(k) match** → Leaving free money
3. **Carrying credit card balance** → 20%+ guaranteed loss
4. **Timing the market** → Missing best days kills returns
5. **Lifestyle creep** → Saving % drops as income rises
6. **No disability insurance** → Income is biggest asset
7. **Whole life insurance** → Expensive, low returns
8. **Ignoring fees** → 1% fee = ~25% less over 30 years
9. **No estate plan** → State decides, probate costs
10. **Comparing to others** → Social media ≠ reality
