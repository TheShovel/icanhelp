# Credit Score & Debt Management

How credit scores work, building credit, and managing debt.

## Credit Score Factors (FICO)
- **Payment history (35%)**: Pay all bills on time — 30+ days late drops score 60-110 points. Bankruptcies (200 pt drop, stays 7-10 years), charge-offs, collections, foreclosures, judgments all severely damaging
- **Credit utilization (30%)**: Total credit used / total available credit. Keep below 30% per card and overall. Below 10% = best scores. Maxed cards = big red flag regardless of payment history
- **Length of credit history (15%)**: Average age of accounts. Older = better. Don't close oldest cards (shortens history). New accounts lower average age
- **Credit mix (10%)**: Mix of installment loans (auto, mortgage, student) and revolving credit (credit cards). Too many of one type slightly reduces score
- **New credit (10%)**: Hard inquiries (when you apply for credit) — each drops score 2-5 points for 12 months, stays 2 years. Multiple inquiries in 14-45 days for same loan type count as one (rate shopping)
- **FICO ranges**: Exceptional (800-850), Very Good (740-799), Good (670-739), Fair (580-669), Poor (300-579). Average US score ~714

## Building Credit
- **Authorized user**: Being added to responsible family member's card — their good history appears on your report (some card issuers report). Effective for jumpstarting score
- **Secured credit card**: Deposit = credit limit ($200-500). Use lightly, pay off monthly. Graduates to unsecured after 6-12 months. Discover IT, Capital One Quicksilver secured
- **Student credit card**: Lower limits, easier approval — for beginners with student income
- **Credit-builder loan**: Small loan ($300-1000) held by credit union/CD. Money released after you finish paying. Self, Credit Strong
- **Experian Boost**: Add utility, phone, streaming payments to credit report. Small but immediate boost for thin files
- **Rent reporting**: Services like Rental Kharma, PayYourRent report on-time rent payments to bureaus. Not all landlords report

## Credit Reports
- **Three bureaus**: Equifax, Experian, TransUnion — each may have slightly different info. Lenders don't always check all three
- **Free weekly reports**: AnnualCreditReport.com — legally entitled to one free report per bureau per week (was annual, now weekly permanently). No score included
- **Free scores**: Credit Karma (VantageScore 3.0, not FICO — can differ 20-50 pts), many credit cards provide FICO score for free (Discover, Chase, Amex), MyFICO.com (paid, all versions)
- **Disputing errors**: 1 in 5 reports have errors. Dispute online with each bureau (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion). Must respond within 30 days. Remove inaccurate negative items. Furnisher (creditor) must verify — if they can't, item removed

## Common Credit Mistakes
- **Closing old credit cards**: Never close oldest card — keeping it open with $0 balance maintains account age and available credit. Annual fee on old card? Downgrade to fee-free version
- **Missing payment by 30+ days**: Late payment reported to bureau. Set autopay for minimum at minimum, calendar reminders for full payment
- **Maxing cards even if paid monthly**: High utilization depresses score even if paid in full. Request credit limit increases (without spending more)
- **Too many hard inquiries**: Don't apply for multiple cards in short period. Each inquiry drops score. Wait 6-12 months between applications
- **Co-signing a loan**: You're equally responsible — if they miss, your credit damaged. Hard to remove yourself. Only co-sign if you could pay the debt yourself

## Debt Management
- **Debt snowball (Dave Ramsey)**: List debts smallest to largest (regardless of interest rate). Pay minimum on all, throw extra at smallest. Psychological wins keep motivation. Mathematically suboptimal but behaviorally effective
- **Debt avalanche**: List debts highest interest to lowest. Pay minimum on all, throw extra at highest interest. Saves most money on interest. Mathematically optimal
- **Debt consolidation**: Personal loan (7-36% APR — good credit needed) or balance transfer credit card (0% for 12-21 months, 3-5% transfer fee). Only works if you stop using the paid-off cards
- **Debt settlement**: Negotiate with creditors to accept less than owed. Typically 40-60% of balance. Damage: accounts marked "settled" (almost as bad as "charge-off"), taxable forgiven debt as income. Last resort before bankruptcy
- **Credit counseling**: Nonprofit agencies (NFCC or ACCC approved) — negotiate lower interest/ payments. Must close credit cards (no new credit). Do NOT use for-profit debt settlement companies (heavy fees, often scams)
- **Bankruptcy**: Chapter 7 (liquidation — discharge most debts, keep exempt assets, 3-6 months, stays 10 years). Chapter 13 (repayment plan 3-5 years, keep assets, stays 7 years). Should be last resort — but sometimes best option to start over

## Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio
- **Front-end**: Housing costs / gross monthly income. Ideal <28%
- **Back-end**: Total debt payments / gross monthly income. Ideal <36%, max 43% for qualified mortgage
- DTI doesn't affect credit score but affects loan approval (mortgage, auto)
- Improve DTI: Increase income, pay down debt, don't take on new debt

## Credit Card Strategy
- **Always pay statement balance in full** — credit card interest (avg 24.84% APR) destroys wealth. If you can't pay in full, reduce spending, not build points
- **Rewards cards**: 2% cashback (Citi Double Cash, Wells Fargo Active Cash), 3-6% on categories (Chase Freedom, Discover rotating). Sign-up bonuses $200-600 per card. Churning = open cards for bonuses, close after a year — only for disciplined
- **Annual fees**: Only worth if benefits exceed fee. Travel cards (Chase Sapphire Preferred $95, Amex Platinum $695) for frequent travelers. Downgrade before renewal if not worth it
- **Grace period**: 21-25 days between statement date and due date. Pay balance in full by due date = no interest. Even $1 short on full balance triggers interest on entire amount
