# Taxes & Tax Filing (US)

## Key Concepts
- **Gross income**: all income before deductions
- **Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**: gross minus "above the line" deductions (IRA, HSA, student loan interest, alimony paid)
- **Taxable income**: AGI minus standard/itemized deduction — this is what you're taxed on
- **Marginal tax rate**: rate on last dollar earned (not all dollars). 2024 brackets: 10%, 12%, 22%, 24%, 32%, 35%, 37%
  - If you're in 22% bracket, only income ABOVE the lower bracket thresholds is taxed at 22%
  - Effective tax rate = total tax / total income (usually much lower than marginal rate)
- **Withholding**: taxes taken from each paycheck. W-4 tells employer how much to withhold
  - Too much = refund. Too little = owe + possible penalty
  - Adjust W-4 for major life changes (marriage, baby, second job)
- **Filing status**: Single, Married Filing Jointly, Married Filing Separately, Head of Household, Qualifying Widow(er)
  - Choosing correctly matters — married filing jointly usually best, head of household has wider brackets

## Common Deductions (reduce taxable income)
- **Standard deduction 2024**: $14,600 single, $29,200 married joint, $21,900 head of household
  - Most people take standard deduction (simpler, usually larger unless you have big deductions)
- **Itemized deductions**: take instead of standard if you have more:
  - Mortgage interest (on first $750k of debt)
  - State and local taxes (SALT) capped at $10,000
  - Charitable donations
  - Medical expenses exceeding 7.5% of AGI
- **Above-the-line deductions** (even if you take standard deduction):
  - Traditional IRA contributions
  - HSA contributions
  - Student loan interest (up to $2,500)
  - Self-employed health insurance premiums
  - Retirement contributions for self-employed (SEP IRA, Solo 401k)

## Credits (reduce tax OWED, dollar for dollar)
- **Child Tax Credit**: up to $2,000/child under 17 ($1,600 refundable)
- **Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)**: for low-to-moderate income workers — refundable, amount depends on income + kids
- **Child and Dependent Care Credit**: up to $3,000 for one dependent, $6,000 for two+ (daycare, summer camp)
- **Education credits**: American Opportunity Tax Credit ($2,500/student, first 4 years, partially refundable), Lifetime Learning Credit ($2,000, no limit on years)
- **Saver's Credit**: for low-income retirement contributions (50% of first $2,000 contributed)
- **Energy credits**: solar panels (30%), EV tax credit (up to $7,500, income limits)
- **Premium Tax Credit**: for marketplace health insurance — income between 100-400% federal poverty level

## Filing Your Taxes
- **Deadline**: April 15 (October 15 if extension filed)
  - Extension to FILE, not to PAY — estimate and pay by April 15 to avoid penalties
- **Software**: TurboTax (most popular, upcharges), FreeTaxUSA (cheap, good), Cash App Taxes (free), H&R Block
- **IRS Free File**: if AGI under $79,000, free filing through IRS partners
- **Documents needed**: W-2 (employment), 1099-NEC (freelance), 1099-INT/DIV (interest/dividends), 1099-MISC (misc income), 1098 (mortgage interest), tuition statement (1098-T), prior year AGI (for signature)
- **Last 3 years**: you can still file and get refund if you missed a year
- **E-file + direct deposit**: fastest refund (most within 21 days)
- **State taxes file separately**: most states have separate return

## Self-Employment / Freelance
- **Schedule C**: profit/loss from business. Deduct legitimate business expenses (home office, equipment, software, internet, travel, meals 50%)
- **Self-employment tax**: 15.3% (Social Security + Medicare) on net earnings — you pay both employer and employee portions
  - Deduct half of SE tax as an above-the-line deduction
- **Quarterly estimated taxes**: due April 15, June 15, Sept 15, Jan 15 (of next year) — penalty if underpaid
  - Pay 100% of prior year tax (110% if high income) to avoid penalty
- **HSA**: tax-deductible, grows tax-free, withdraw tax-free for medical expenses — triple tax advantage

## Investments & Taxes
- **Capital gains**: profit from selling assets
  - Short-term (held <1 year): taxed as ordinary income (higher)
  - Long-term (held >1 year): 0%, 15%, or 20% (much lower)
  - Tax-loss harvesting: sell losing investments to offset gains (deduct up to $3,000 of ordinary income)
- **Dividends**: qualified (taxed at capital gains rates) vs non-qualified (ordinary income)
- **Retirement**: Roth (after-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals), Traditional (pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal)
  - 401k/IRA withdrawals in retirement count as income
- **Crypto**: treated as property — every sale, trade, or crypto→crypto transaction is a taxable event (like selling stock)

## Common Mistakes
- Missing income: IRS gets copies of all W-2s and 1099s — they know
- Math errors: software helps, but double-check
- Wrong filing status: especially head of household (has specific rules about dependents)
- Forgetting deductions: state sales tax (instead of income tax if larger), HSA, student loan interest
- Signing without reviewing: check all numbers, especially if using a paid preparer
- Not filing at all: even if you can't pay, FILE — failure to file penalty is 10x failure to pay
- IRS scams: IRS will NOT call, text, email, or DM you — they send mail. Don't pay with gift cards

## IRS Audits
- Random (very rare) or triggered by irregularities (large deductions relative to income, round numbers, home office deduction)
- Mail audit: most common — send documentation. Office audit: go in person. Field audit: IRS comes to you (most intensive)
- Keep records 3 years (6 if self-employed, 7 if claiming bad debt deduction, forever for assets)
- If you made honest mistake: amend return (Form 1040-X). Better than waiting for IRS to catch it
- Audit probability under 0.5% for most filers, higher for high income and business returns

## Tax Pro vs DIY
- **DIY**: simple returns (W-2, standard deduction, no investments) — use free software
- **CPA/Enrolled Agent**: self-employed, rental property, small business, investments, crypto, complex situations
- **Cost**: $50-150 DIY, $200-500 CPA for simple return, $500+ for complex
- **Signs you need a pro**: own a business, have rental property, sold a house, exercised stock options, inherited money, moved states mid-year, digital nomad
- **Preparer tax identification number (PTIN)**: verify your preparer has one
