# Real Estate Investing

## Investment Strategies

- **Buy-and-Hold (long-term rental):** Purchase property, rent out for ongoing cash flow + appreciation. Core strategy for building long-term wealth. Requires property management (or self-manage), maintenance reserves, tenant management. Returns: cash flow (monthly) + tax benefits (depreciation, mortgage interest deduction) + appreciation over time. Target: 5–10% CoC return.
- **BRRRR (Buy-Rehab-Rent-Refinance-Repeat):** Buy distressed property below market, renovate (value-add), rent to market rate, refinance (cash-out at new appraised value based on 75% LTV), recover original investment, repeat on next deal. Requires good contractor network, accurate rehab estimates, and understanding ARV (After Repair Value). Rule of thumb: total investment (purchase + rehab) ≤ 75% ARV.
- **Fix-and-Flip:** Buy distressed property, renovate quickly (2–6 months), sell for profit. Higher risk/ higher return. Requires knowledge of local market timeline, liquidity for holding costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance during reno), and exit strategy (if market slows, can rent instead of sell). Target profit: 15–30% ROI (or 6-figure profit on $300K+ projects).
- **Short-term rentals (Airbnb/VRBO):** Rent furnished properties by night/week. Potential for higher income than long-term rental. More management intensive (cleaning, guest communication, turnover). Subject to local regulations (some cities restrict short-term rentals or require permits/license/occupancy tax). Seasonality matters. Target: 10–20% CoC return.
- **Whole-sailing:** Assign contract (find deal, sign contract, assign to end buyer for fee). No capital needed — legal but regulated (need real estate license in some states). Fee typically $5K–$20K. Requires strong buyer list and negotiating skill.
- **REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts):** Publicly traded (stock exchange — e.g., O, VNQ) or private/non-traded. Own shares in real estate portfolio (commercial, residential, healthcare, data centers). Dividends paid (90%+ of taxable income must be distributed). Liquidity (public REITs trade like stocks). Lower minimum investment ($0–$1,000). Lower return potential than direct ownership but less hands-on.
- **Real Estate Syndications (passive investment):** Pool money with other investors (equity or debt). Sponsor/operator finds deal, manages project. Investors receive preferred return (8–10% typically) + profit split. Minimum investment $25K–$100K. Requires accredited investor status ($200K+ annual income or $1M+ net worth). Due diligence on sponsor track record.

## 1031 Exchange (Tax Deferred)

- **What it is:** IRC Section 1031 allows deferral of capital gains tax when exchanging investment property for "like-kind" property. Must be held for business/investment (not personal residence). Both relinquished and replacement property identified.
- **Timeline:** 45 days to identify potential replacement properties (up to 3 properties unlimited value, or any number up to 200% of relinquished value). 180 days total to close on replacement property. Use qualified intermediary (QI) — funds held by QI, not you (constructive receipt triggers tax).
- **Can exchange into:** Any real estate held for investment: single-family rentals, commercial, raw land, even vacation rental (if used as investment). Can do reverse exchange (buy replacement first then sell relinquished) via QI arrangement.
- **Boot (taxable):** If exchange cash received (cash boot) or debt relief exceeds new debt (mortgage boot). Gain recognized = lesser of gain realized or boot received. Reduce boot by reinvesting all proceeds + new mortgage equal to or greater than old.

## Financing Options

- **Conventional mortgage (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac):** 15–30 yr fixed or ARM (adjustable rate). 5% down (owner-occupied) — rental requires 20–25% down. Requires good credit (680+, best rates 740+). Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio — 43% max conventional. Rental income can offset PITI (new DTI / rent offset calculation).
- **FHA (Federal Housing Administration):** 3.5% down, owner-occupied only (max 4 units). MIP (mortgage insurance premium) required entire loan life if <10% down. Self-sufficient rental — can use rental income from other units to qualify. Best for small multifamily (duplex/triplex/4-plex) house-hacking.
- **VA (Veterans Affairs):** 0% down for eligible veterans. Owner-occupied only. No PMI but VA funding fee (2.3% for first time, waived if disability). Great for multi-unit house hacking.
- **Portfolio loans (bank-held / non-QM):** Held by local bank or credit union (not sold to Fannie/Freddie). More flexible underwriting — may consider DSCR only, not personal income. Terms 1–5 yr balloon or 15–25 yr amortization. Typically 20–30% down. Best for investors with strong rental history but complex income.
- **DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio) loans:** Qualification based on property cash flow, not personal income. DSCR = NOI ÷ annual debt payments. 0.75–1.25x minimum (1.0 = break-even). Rates 1–3% higher than conventional. 20–30% down. Asset-based — good for self-employed, new investors, portfolio growth.
- **Private money / hard money:** Short-term (6–24 months), high interest (8–15%), points (2–4). Based on ARV (lend up to 70% ARV). Used for fix-and-flips / BRRRR (short-term bridge to refinance). Faster closing (1–2 weeks). Requires exit strategy (refinance or sale).
- **Seller financing / owner carry:** Seller acts as bank. Flexible terms (rate, amortization, balloon). Avoids bank origination fees. Best when seller has no mortgage or low basis. Request offer with seller financing.

## Key Metrics

- **Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate):** NOI ÷ property value (purchase price). Independent of financing = pure property return metric. Formula: (Rental Income - Operating Expenses) ÷ Purchase Price. Good range: 5–10% depending on market. Lower cap = lower return but more stable market (high demand). Higher cap = higher return but higher risk/less stable market.
- **Cash-on-Cash Return (CoC):** Annual pre-tax cash flow ÷ total cash invested (down payment + closing costs + renovation). Measures return on actual cash outlay — accounts for leverage. Target: 8–12% minimum for buy-and-hold. 15–25% for BRRRR. Formula: (NOI - Debt Service) ÷ Total Cash Invested.
- **NOI (Net Operating Income):** Total rental income (including parking, laundry, other) minus total operating expenses (property taxes, insurance, management, maintenance, HOA, utilities paid by owner). **Excludes mortgage payment** (debt service). Key for valuation: NOI ÷ Cap Rate = Property Value.
- **Cash Flow:** NOI - Debt Service (mortgage P&I). Positive = profit monthly. Rule of thumb: $100–$200 per door per month is reasonable. Negative cash flow is acceptable only for appreciation plays (risky).
- **GRM (Gross Rent Multiplier):** Property price ÷ annual gross rental income. Quick screening tool — lower = better. 10–12 is average for SFR.
- **1% Rule:** Monthly rent ≥ 1% of purchase price (e.g., $200K house → $2,000+/mo rent). Quick filter for positive cash flow potential. Higher cost markets may achieve 0.5–0.7%.
- **50% Rule:** Operating expenses = ~50% of gross rent (including vacancy = 5–10% annual). Net = 50% of gross — debt service comes from remaining 50%. Quick estimate.
- **ROI (Return on Investment):** (Gain from Investment - Cost of Investment) ÷ Cost of Investment. For flips: (Sale Price - All Costs) ÷ Total Investment. For buy-and-hold: annual cash flow ÷ total cash invested.

## Property Management vs Self-Managing

- **Self-managing pros:** Save 8–10% management fee (significant on multi-property). Control over tenant selection, maintenance timing. Direct relationship with tenants. Cons: 24/7 availability needed. Landlord-tenant law knowledge required. Eviction handling stressful. Tenant complaints / bad tenant management. Time commitment ~2–5 hrs/week per property.
- **Professional management fee:** Typically 8–12% of monthly rent. Leasing fee: 50–100% of first month's rent for tenant placement (often negotiable). Maintenance markup: 10–20% margin on contractor costs. Good managers handle tenant screening, rent collection, maintenance coordination, lease enforcement, evictions.
- **When to hire management:** Over 5 units. Investor is remote (>50 miles). Full-time job makes tenant calls impossible. Portfolio growth requires systems. Nearing retirement / want passive income. Bad at saying "no" to tenant requests.
- **Tenant screening essentials:** Credit score (min 600–650 recommended). Criminal background check. Eviction history. Income verification (≥3× rent as gross monthly income). Landlord references (call previous landlords). Employment verification. Tenant screening services: TransUnion SmartMove, Cozy (now Apartments.com), MyRental.
- **Lease terms:** Most states default to month-to-month. 1-year lease standard for single-family. Include: rent amount, due date, late fees (state limit — typically 5%), security deposit rules (separate account in some states), maintenance responsibilities, pet policy, subletting prohibition, early termination fee. Use local apartment association lease forms (legal-compliant).
