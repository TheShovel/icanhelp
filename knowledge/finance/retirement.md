# Retirement Planning

## Account Types
- **401(k)**: employer-sponsored, pre-tax contributions, $23,000 limit (2024), employer match typically 3-6%
- **Traditional IRA**: pre-tax, $7,000 limit (2024, $8,000 if 50+), income limits for deduction phaseout
- **Roth IRA**: after-tax, same contribution limits, tax-free withdrawals in retirement, income limits ($161k single / $240k married 2024 phaseout)
- **Roth 401(k)**: after-tax, higher limits than Roth IRA, no income phaseout, employer match is pre-tax
- **SEP IRA**: for self-employed/sole proprietor, up to 25% of compensation or $69,000 (2024)
- **Solo 401(k)**: for self-employed with no employees, employee + employer contributions up to $69,000 (2024)
- **HSA** (Health Savings Account): triple tax-advantaged (pre-tax contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses), invest and let it grow

## Investment Order (USA)
1. 401(k) up to employer match (guaranteed 100% return)
2. Pay off high-interest debt (>5% APR)
3. Max out Roth IRA (or Traditional)
4. Max out 401(k) to annual limit
5. HSA if eligible (invest, don't spend)
6. Taxable brokerage account

## Withdrawal Rules
- **59½**: penalty-free retirement account withdrawals (except Roth contributions anytime)
- **72(t)**: substantially equal periodic payments — early withdrawals without penalty
- **Roth 5-year rule**: account must be open 5 years before earnings are tax-free
- **RMDs** (Required Minimum Distributions): start at age 73 for Traditional 401(k)/IRA (SECURE Act 2.0)
- Social Security: full retirement age 67 (1960+), early at 62 (reduced 30%), delayed to 70 (increases 8%/year)

## Safe Withdrawal Rate
- **4% rule**: historically safe for 30-year retirement (Bengen study), withdraw 4% of portfolio year 1, adjust for inflation
- **3-3.5%**: more conservative for early retirement (longer horizon)
- Sequence of returns risk: bad market in early retirement years can devastate portfolio

## Asset Allocation by Age
- **20s-30s**: 90% stocks / 10% bonds — aggressive, long time horizon
- **40s**: 80/20 — start adding bonds for stability
- **50s**: 70/30 or 60/40 — capital preservation
- **60+**: 60/40 to 50/50 — income generation, sequence-of-returns protection
- **Rule of 110**: (110 - age) = % in stocks, rest in bonds

## Common Pitfalls
- Cashing out 401(k) when changing jobs — roll over instead
- Not rebalancing annually — drifting allocation adds risk
- Ignoring fees — 1% higher fees costs ~28% of portfolio over 30 years
- Market timing — missing the 10 best days over 20 years cuts returns by half
- Overconcentration in employer stock — lack of diversification
