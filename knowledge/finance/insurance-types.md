# Insurance Types

Health, auto, home, life, disability, and liability insurance basics.

## Health Insurance
- **ACA plans**: Individual/family plans through HealthCare.gov or state exchanges. Open enrollment Nov 15 - Jan 15 (special enrollment for life events: job loss, marriage, birth). Income-based subsidies (premium tax credits) up to 400% FPL
- **Employer-sponsored**: Most common — group plans, employer pays 70-80% of premium. Must offer COBRA continuation for 18-36 months after leaving job (you pay full premium + 2% fee)
- **HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)**: Lower premiums, primary care physician (PCP) manages care, referrals needed for specialists. Must use in-network providers except emergencies. Least flexibility
- **PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)**: Higher premiums, can see any provider (in-network cheaper), no referral needed. Most flexibility. Out-of-network coverage (usually 50-60% coinsurance)
- **HDHP + HSA (High Deductible Health Plan + Health Savings Account)**: Lower premiums, high deductible ($1,600 individual / $3,200 family 2024). HSA: tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical expenses. Triple tax advantage. Invest HSA funds if not needed soon
- **Deductible**: Amount you pay before insurance starts paying. Annual reset
- **Co-pay**: Fixed dollar amount per visit ($20-50 for primary care, $50-100 for specialist)
- **Co-insurance**: Percentage you pay after deductible (20% typical). No cap — catastrophic risk
- **Out-of-pocket maximum**: Most you'll pay per year ($9,450 individual / $18,900 family 2024 max allowed). After hitting this, insurance pays 100%
- **Medicare**: 65+ or certain disabilities. Parts: A (hospital — premium free if 10+ years work), B (medical — $174.70/mo standard 2024), D (prescriptions), Medigap (supplemental), Advantage (Part C — private plan replacing A+B+D). Not free — premiums, deductibles, co-pays
- **Medicaid**: Low-income — eligibility varies by state (expanded in 40 states). Free or very low cost. 138% FPL for expansion states

## Auto Insurance
- **Coverages**: Liability (bodily injury + property damage — covers damage YOU cause), Collision (covers your car damage), Comprehensive (theft, vandalism, weather, animal strike), Uninsured/Underinsured motorist, PIP/MedPay (medical for you regardless of fault)
- **Minimum coverage**: State minimums are low (often 25/50/25 = $25k injury/person, $50k total, $25k property) — insufficient for serious accident. Recommend 100/300/100 + $1M umbrella
- **Factors affecting rate**: Age (young and old = higher), driving record, location, credit score (in most states), vehicle type, annual mileage, marital status, claims history
- **Discounts**: Multi-policy (bundle home+auto), multi-car, good driver, good student (B avg), defensive driving course, low mileage, telematics (snapshot/insurance app tracks driving), paid-in-full
- **Claims**: Report promptly, document damage (photos), get estimates. At-fault accident raises rates 20-50% for 3-5 years. Consider paying out-of-pocket for damage under $1-2k to avoid rate increase

## Homeowners Insurance
- **Dwelling**: Structure coverage (rebuild cost, not market value). Replacement cost vs actual cash value (replacement minus depreciation — more expensive, better)
- **Personal property**: Contents (50-70% of dwelling). Scheduled items (jewelry, art, collectibles — separate coverage needed above limits)
- **Liability**: Covers if someone injured on your property. $100k-500k typical. Umbrella policy recommended above $300k
- **Additional living expenses**: Hotel, food, laundry if home uninhabitable from covered loss. Usually 20-30% of dwelling coverage, limited time (12-24 months)
- **Exclusions**: Flood (separate NFIP policy — $100k structure, $50k contents max), Earthquake (separate policy or endorsement), Earth movement, Mold (limited), Wear and tear, Pest infestation, War/nuclear
- **Renters insurance**: Covers personal property + liability. Cheap ($15-30/month). Landlord's insurance doesn't cover tenant's belongings
- **Replacement cost vs cash value**: Replacement = new equivalent. Cash value = minus depreciation. Replacement recommended for dwelling (actual cash value leaves you underinsured)

## Life Insurance
- **Term life**: Pure death benefit, fixed period (10, 20, 30 years). Level premiums for term. Convertible to permanent (no health exam). Cheapest option for most needs
- **Permanent (whole, universal, variable)**: Aka "cash value" insurance. Much more expensive — premiums 5-15x term. Cash value grows tax-deferred. Rarely optimal for average person — invest difference in market
- **How much**: 10-15x your annual income. More for: young children, non-working spouse, special needs dependents, large mortgage. Less for: no dependents, significant existing assets
- **Who needs it**: Anyone with dependents who'd suffer financially from your death. Single with no dependents: usually none. Stay-at-home parent: needs it (replacement cost for childcare/household labor is substantial)
- **Employment-based**: Usually 1-2x salary. Not enough as primary coverage. Loses when you leave job (some portable via conversion). Supplement with individual policy

## Disability Insurance
- **More important than life insurance**: 25% of 20-year-olds will be disabled before retirement. 1 in 4 workers will have a disability lasting 12+ months
- **Short-term**: Covers 3-6 months waiting period, usually 60-80% of salary. Often through employer
- **Long-term**: Benefits start after 90-180 days elimination period. Pay 60% of salary typically. Benefit period: 2 years, 5 years, to age 65, or lifetime. To age 65 recommended
- **Own occupation**: More expensive — covers if you can't do your specific job (doctor can't perform surgery = disabled, even if could teach). Best coverage
- **Any occupation**: Cheaper — covers only if you can't do ANY job. Avoid if possible
- **Employer LTD**: Benefits taxable if employer paid premiums (most common). Individual policy: benefits tax-free (paid with after-tax dollars)

## Umbrella Insurance
- **$1-5M excess liability**: Covers above auto/home/boat liability limits. Kicks in when underlying coverage exhausted
- **Relatively cheap**: $200-400/year for $1M. Covers legal defense costs
- **Who needs**: Net worth > $500k, rental properties, trampoline/pool/large dog, active teens driving, public figure, volunteer board member
- **Usually require**: 250/500 auto liability, $300k homeowners liability minimums
