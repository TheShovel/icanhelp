# Menopause

Perimenopause, menopause, and postmenopause — symptoms, management, and hormone therapy.

## Phases
- **Perimenopause**: Transition period, 4-8 years before final period — erratic ovulation, fluctuating hormones (estrogen rises and falls unpredictably). Usually age 45-55
- **Menopause**: 12 consecutive months without a period — ovaries stop producing eggs, estrogen drops to very low levels. Average natural menopause age: 51
- **Postmenopause**: After menopause — low estrogen levels for remainder of life
- **Premature menopause**: Before age 40 — can be natural (primary ovarian insufficiency, POI) or induced (surgery, chemo, radiation)
- **Surgical menopause**: Oophorectomy (ovary removal) — sudden, severe symptoms (no gradual transition)

## Symptoms
- **Vasomotor symptoms**: Hot flashes (sudden upper body warmth, sweating, flushing — 80% of women), night sweats, chills. Can last 4-7 years (some >10 years)
- **Sleep disturbances**: Night sweats, insomnia, sleep-disordered breathing — estrogen affects thermoregulation and sleep architecture
- **Vulvovaginal atrophy (GSM)**: Vaginal dryness, itching, burning, recurrent UTIs, painful intercourse (dyspareunia), urinary urgency/frequency. Does not improve without treatment (progressive)
- **Mood changes**: Irritability, depression risk increases (perimenopause particularly), anxiety — related to hormone fluctuations, sleep disruption, life stage
- **Cognitive changes**: Brain fog, difficulty concentrating, memory lapses — estrogen receptors dense in brain regions for memory/executive function
- **Bone loss**: Rapid decline in bone density (1-2% per year in first 5 years after menopause) → osteoporosis risk
- **Metabolic changes**: Weight gain (especially abdominal), increased cholesterol, insulin resistance
- **Skin/hair changes**: Thinner skin, more wrinkles, hair thinning/loss, increased facial hair

## Hormone Therapy (HT/MHT)
- **Benefits**: Most effective for vasomotor symptoms (hot flash reduction 75-90%), prevents bone loss, improves sleep/quality of life, treats GSM
- **Risks**: Breast cancer (slightly increased with estrogen + progestin after 5+ years — lower with estrogen alone), VTE/blood clot (2-3x increased, highest in first year), stroke (low absolute risk if started <60)
- **Timing hypothesis**: HT safest if started within 10 years of menopause or age <60 — benefit-risk best in this window
- **Regimens**: 
  - Systemic estrogen (patch, gel, spray, pill) — patch has lowest clot risk (transdermal avoids first-pass liver metabolism)
  - Progestin (needed if uterus intact — prevents endometrial cancer) — micronized progesterone (Prometrium) better than synthetic (Provera) for sleep/mood
  - Tibolone (SERM with estrogenic/progestogenic/androgenic effects) — used in some countries
  - Vaginal estrogen (cream, tablet, ring) — for GSM only, minimal systemic absorption, can be used long-term
- **Compounded bioidentical hormones**: Not FDA-approved, quality varies, avoid — regulated bioidentical products (FDA-approved estradiol patches/gels/pills) are preferred
- **Contraindications**: Breast cancer (current/history), endometrial cancer, unexplained vaginal bleeding, VTE history, stroke history, liver disease

## Non-Hormonal Treatments
- **Hot flashes**: SSRIs/SNRIs (paroxetine/Paxil — FDA approved, venlafaxine/Effexor), gabapentin, pregabalin, clonidine, oxybutynin
- **Lifestyle**: Layers of clothing, avoid triggers (spicy food, hot drinks, alcohol, stress), cooling devices (fans, cooling towels, handheld fans), paced breathing (slow, deep breaths at onset)
- **Weight-bearing exercise**: Walking, jogging, resistance training — preserves bone density
- **Vaginal moisturizers**: Replens, hyaluronic acid products — use 3x/week, not just for intercourse. Lubricants for sexual activity (water-based, silicone-based)
- **Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT)**: Effective for hot flash management — reduce distress, improve coping
- **Supplements with limited evidence**: Black cohosh (some benefit for hot flashes, possible liver toxicity), soy isoflavones (modest benefit), vitamin D + calcium (bone health — calcium 1200mg/day, vitamin D 800-1000 IU/day)

## Long-Term Health
- **Bone density screening (DXA)**: Recommended at age 65 (or earlier with risk factors) — T-score: -1 to -2.5 = osteopenia, <-2.5 = osteoporosis
- **Cardiovascular disease**: #1 cause of death in women post-menopause — estrogen protects endothelium, lipids, vascular reactivity. Monitor BP, cholesterol, glucose after menopause
- **Breast cancer screening**: Continue mammograms every 1-2 years through at least age 75
- **Colon cancer screening**: Through age 75 (colonoscopy every 10 years, or FIT yearly)
- **Pelvic floor health**: PFPT (pelvic floor physical therapy) for prolapse/urinary incontinence — very effective
- **Sexual health**: Continue safe sex (STI risk persists — condoms if not in monogamous relationship)
