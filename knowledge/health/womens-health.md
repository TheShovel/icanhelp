# Women's Health Basics

## Menstrual Cycle
- Average cycle: 28 days (21-35 normal). Day 1 = first day of bleeding
- **Follicular phase** (days 1-13): period, then estrogen rises, egg matures
- **Ovulation** (day ~14): egg released, fertile window (~5 days before to 1 day after)
  - Signs: clear stretchy discharge (egg white), mild cramping (mittelschmerz), libido up
- **Luteal phase** (days 15-28): progesterone rises, if no pregnancy → period starts
- **PMS**: mood swings, bloating, fatigue, breast tenderness, food cravings
  - Exercise, reduce salt/caffeine/sugar, calcium + magnesium supplements may help
- **PMDD**: severe PMS that disrupts daily life — depression, anger, anxiety before period
  - Treatment: SSRIs (continuous or luteal phase only), birth control, therapy
- Track cycles: apps (Clue, Flo), paper calendar — helps predict period, ovulation, identify irregularities

## Contraception
- **Combined pill**: estrogen + progestin, 91-99% effective with perfect use
  - Take same time daily, missed pill = backup contraception 7 days
  - Side effects: nausea (1st month), breast tenderness, mood changes, blood clot risk (small)
- **Progestin-only pill ("mini-pill")**: smaller window (3 hours), good if can't take estrogen
- **IUD**: copper (10-12 years, no hormones, heavier periods) or hormonal (3-8 years, lighter periods)
  - >99% effective, most effective LARC (Long-Acting Reversible Contraceptive)
- **Implant (Nexplanon)**: arm, 3 years, >99% effective
- **Shot (Depo-Provera)**: every 3 months, 94% effective, can cause weight gain, bone density loss with long use
- **Ring (NuvaRing)**: vaginal, 3 weeks in 1 out, 91-99% effective
- **Patch (Xulane)**: skin, weekly, 91-99% effective
- **Condom**: 82-98% effective (typical-perfect), only protection against STIs
- **Fertility awareness**: track cycle + temp + mucus — 76-88% effective, requires training + consistency
- **Emergency contraception**: Plan B (up to 72 hours, less effective if over 165 lbs), Ella (up to 120 hours, more effective), copper IUD (up to 5 days, most effective)
- **Sterilization**: tubal ligation, bilateral salpingectomy (tube removal — also reduces ovarian cancer risk)

## Common Conditions
- **UTI (Urinary Tract Infection)**: burning when peeing, urgency, frequency, cloudy/bloody urine
  - See doctor (antibiotics needed), drink water, avoid sex until resolved, cranberry may help prevent (not treat)
  - Prevention: pee after sex, wipe front to back, stay hydrated
- **Yeast infection**: itching, burning, thick white discharge (cottage cheese), redness
  - OTC antifungal (Monistat) or prescription (Diflucan pill), not an STI
  - Triggers: antibiotics, high sugar, pregnancy, tight synthetic clothing
- **BV (Bacterial Vaginosis)**: fishy odor, thin gray discharge, itching — not an STI but more common with sex
  - Prescription antibiotic (metronidazole gel or pill), can recur
- **Endometriosis**: uterine-like tissue grows outside uterus — severe period pain, painful sex, infertility
  - Average 7-10 years to diagnose — advocate for yourself
  - Treatment: pain management, hormones, surgery (laparoscopic excision), hysterectomy (last resort)
- **PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)**: irregular periods, excess androgen (acne, facial hair, hair loss), ovarian cysts
  - Most common hormonal disorder in women (1 in 10)
  - Treatment: birth control (regulate periods), metformin (insulin resistance), lifestyle (diet + exercise)
- **Fibroids**: non-cancerous uterine growths — heavy bleeding, pressure, pain
  - Treatment: monitoring, hormones, surgery (myomectomy), UAE (uterine artery embolization)
- **Ovarian cysts**: usually benign, can cause pain if rupture/twist — most resolve on their own
  - Warning signs: sudden severe pelvic pain, vomiting, fever — torsion is emergency

## Pregnancy Basics
- **Prenatal care**: first appointment 8-12 weeks, monthly visits until 28 weeks, biweekly 28-36, weekly 36-birth
- **Prenatal vitamins**: folic acid (400-800 mcg, prevents neural tube defects), iron, calcium, DHA
- **What to avoid**: alcohol, smoking, raw/undercooked food, deli meat (listeria), high-mercury fish (tuna, swordfish), hot tubs/saunas, retinol skincare (switch to azelaic acid or glycolic)
- **Trimesters**: 1st (0-13 weeks) — fatigue, nausea, breast tenderness. 2nd (14-27) — "golden period" energy returns, baby moves. 3rd (28-40) — discomfort, back pain, frequent urination
- **Warning signs**: severe headache, vision changes, upper right abdominal pain, bleeding, leaking fluid, decreased fetal movement — call doctor immediately
- **Miscarriage**: 15-20% of known pregnancies, usually first trimester, not caused by stress/exercise/sex — most are chromosomal abnormalities
- **Postpartum**: "baby blues" (1-2 weeks, mood swings, crying) vs postpartum depression (2+ weeks, can't bond, hopeless, suicidal thoughts — get help)

## Mammograms & Breast Health
- Routine mammograms start at 40-50 (varies by guidelines, discuss with doctor)
- Self-exams: know your normal — no longer officially recommended as routine but awareness matters
- Breast pain: usually hormonal, rarely cancer. Lumps: most are benign (cysts, fibroadenomas)
- Breast cancer signs: new lump, dimpling (orange peel), nipple changes, discharge, skin changes
- Risk factors: age, family history, BRCA gene mutation, dense breasts, alcohol, hormone therapy, obesity

## Pap Smears & Gynecological Health
- Pap smear: every 3-5 years starting at 21 (screens for cervical cancer)
- HPV vaccine: ages 9-45, prevents HPV types causing 90% of cervical cancer + genital warts
- Pelvic exam: check uterus, ovaries, cervix — frequency based on age/risk
- Menopause: average age 51 (45-55 normal). Symptoms: hot flashes, night sweats, vaginal dryness, mood changes, sleep problems
  - HRT (Hormone Replacement Therapy): effective for symptoms, discuss risks with doctor
  - Non-hormonal: SSRIs, gabapentin, lifestyle (cool room, layered clothing, exercise)

## Pelvic Floor Health
- Pelvic floor = muscles supporting bladder, uterus, rectum
- **Kegels**: contract pelvic floor (stop urine flow sensation), hold 3-10 sec, relax fully between, 3 sets of 10/day
  - Important during pregnancy, postpartum, and as we age
  - OVER-tightening is also a problem — pelvic floor physical therapy can help
- **Prolapse**: organs bulge into vagina — feels like pressure, heaviness, something falling out
  - Risk: childbirth, age, heavy lifting, chronic coughing
  - Treatment: pelvic PT, pessary (support device), surgery
- **Incontinence**: leaking urine when coughing/sneezing/laughing/exercising (stress incontinence) or sudden urgency (urge incontinence)
  - Treatment: pelvic PT, bladder training, medication, surgery (sling)
