# Thyroid Conditions

Hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, thyroid nodules, and management.

## Hypothyroidism
- **Primary hypothyroidism**: Underactive thyroid gland — low T4, elevated TSH. Most common cause: Hashimoto's thyroiditis (autoimmune — anti-TPO antibodies)
- **Symptoms**: Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, hair loss, brittle nails, depression, brain fog, hoarseness, myalgias, bradycardia, menstrual irregularities
- **Subclinical hypothyroidism**: Elevated TSH (4.5-10 mIU/L) with normal T4. Treat if TSH >10 or symptomatic or pregnant or positive TPO antibodies
- **Treatment**: Levothyroxine (Synthroid, Levoxyl, Tirosint) — synthetic T4. Take on empty stomach, wait 30-60 min before eating. Avoid calcium/iron within 4 hours, fiber, coffee
- **Dosing**: Start 1.6 mcg/kg (50-75 mcg typical adult), adjust every 6-8 weeks until TSH normal (0.5-4.5 mIU/L). Elderly/ CAD start lower (12.5-25 mcg)
- **Myxedema coma**: Severe, untreated hypothyroidism → altered mental status, hypothermia, bradycardia, hypoventilation — medical emergency. IV levothyroxine + stress dose steroids + supportive care

## Hyperthyroidism
- **Primary hyperthyroidism**: Overactive thyroid — low TSH, elevated T4/T3. Causes: Graves' disease (autoimmune — TSI antibodies), toxic nodular goiter (Plummer's disease), subacute thyroiditis
- **Symptoms**: Weight loss despite increased appetite, heat intolerance, sweating, palpitations/tachycardia, tremor, anxiety/irritability, insomnia, frequent bowel movements, exophthalmos (Graves' eye disease — bulging eyes, lid lag)
- **Thyroid storm**: Life-threatening — fever >38°C, severe tachycardia/arrhythmia, confusion, vomiting/diarrhea. Precipitated by infection, surgery, iodine load. Treatment: beta blocker (propranolol), thionamide (PTU/methimazole), steroids, iodine (after thionamide)
- **Treatment**: 
  - Antithyroid drugs: Methimazole (Tapazole) or propylthiouracil (PTU — pregnancy first trimester). Side effects: agranulocytosis (rare — sore throat = stop drug, check CBC), hepatotoxicity (PTU > methimazole)
  - Radioactive iodine (RAI) ablation: I-131 orally → destroys thyroid tissue over 6-12 weeks → hypothyroidism (lifelong levothyroxine)
  - Thyroidectomy: Surgical removal — for large goiters, suspected cancer, Graves' ophthalmopathy, failed RAI

## Thyroid Nodules
- **Prevalence**: 50% of people have nodules by age 60 on ultrasound — >90% benign
- **Workup**: TSH, thyroid ultrasound (document size, composition, vascularity, suspicious features), radioactive iodine scan (hot = benign, cold = suspicious)
- **Malignancy risk**: Higher in young (<20), old (>70), male, radiation exposure, family history, hard/fixed nodule on exam, recent growth, hoarseness
- **Biopsy (FNA)**: Indicated for nodules >1-1.5cm with suspicious features or >2cm without. Bethesda categories (I-VI): nondiagnostic → benign → atypia → suspicious → malignant
- **Thyroid cancer**: Papillary (85% — excellent prognosis >95% survival), follicular (10%, slightly worse), medullary (MTC — associated with MEN2, RET mutation), anaplastic (rare, highly aggressive)

## Hashimoto's Thyroiditis
- **Autoimmune**: Anti-TPO and/or anti-thyroglobulin antibodies → gradual thyroid destruction → eventual hypothyroidism
- **Most common cause of hypothyroidism** in iodine-sufficient areas. 5-10x more common in women
- **Associated with**: Other autoimmune conditions (celiac disease, Type 1 diabetes, pernicious anemia, Addison's, RA)
- **Goiter**: May develop early (thyroid enlarges trying to maintain hormone production) → eventually atrophies
- **Diagnosis**: Positive TPO antibodies, elevated TSH (if hypothyroid), characteristic ultrasound (heterogeneous, hypoechoic)

## Pregnancy & Thyroid
- **Fetal brain development**: Depends on maternal T4 supply (especially first trimester). Untreated hypothyroidism → lower child IQ
- **Increased T4 needs**: ~30-50% dose increase during pregnancy — check TSH every 4 weeks until stable, adjust accordingly (goal TSH 0.2-2.5 in first tri, 0.3-3 in second/third)
- **Postpartum thyroiditis**: 5-10% of women — transient hyperthyroidism (2-4 months postpartum) then hypothyroidism, may become permanent. Autoimmune (TPO antibodies)

## Iodine
- **Essential for thyroid hormone synthesis**: 150 mcg/day (adults), 220 mcg (pregnancy), 290 mcg (breastfeeding)
- **Sources**: Iodized salt (1/2 tsp = 150 mcg), seafood, dairy, eggs. Seaweed very high (kelp can cause hyperthyroidism)
- **Deficiency**: Goiter, hypothyroidism, cretinism (if severe during pregnancy — mental retardation + deafness + motor deficits). Still common in parts of Africa, Asia, Europe
- **Excess**: Can cause hyperthyroidism (Jod-Basedow) or hypothyroidism (Wolff-Chaikoff block) in susceptible individuals
