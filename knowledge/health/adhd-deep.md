# ADHD Deep Dive

ADHD across the lifespan — diagnosis, treatment, and management strategies.

## Diagnosis
- **DSM-5 criteria**: 5+ symptoms (for ages 17+, 6+ for younger) persisting 6+ months, present before age 12, present in 2+ settings, interferes with functioning, not better explained by another condition
- **Inattentive type**: Careless mistakes, difficulty sustaining attention, doesn't listen when spoken to, fails to finish tasks, disorganized, avoids sustained mental effort, loses things, easily distracted, forgetful
- **Hyperactive-impulsive type**: Fidgets, leaves seat, runs/climbs when inappropriate, unable to play quietly, "on the go", talks excessively, blurts out, difficulty waiting turn, interrupts/intrudes
- **Combined type**: Both inattentive + hyperactive-impulsive criteria met
- **Adults often misdiagnosed**: Depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder — especially in women (undiagnosed inattentive type). Women diagnosed later, often after their children are diagnosed

## Executive Function Deficits
- **Working memory**: Forgets instructions mid-task, loses train of thought, can't hold multiple details simultaneously. Written lists visible (phone/whiteboard) compensate
- **Time blindness**: "Now or not now" perception — chronic lateness, underestimating task duration, difficulty with deadlines. External time awareness tools essential
- **Task initiation (paralysis)**: Difficulty starting tasks despite wanting to — not laziness. Dopamine deficit makes non-preferred tasks feel impossible. Need micromotivation
- **Emotional dysregulation**: Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD) — extreme emotional pain from perceived rejection/criticism. Meltdowns disproportionate to trigger. Not in DSM but very common
- **Sustained attention**: Hyperfocus on interesting tasks (can't stop), inability to focus on boring tasks (can't start). Interest-based nervous system (not importance-based like neurotypical)

## Medication
- **Stimulants (first-line)**: Methylphenidate (Ritalin, Concerta, Focalin) and Amphetamine (Adderall, Vyvanse, Dexedrine). Increase dopamine/norepinephrine. Immediate release (IR, 4hr) vs extended release (ER, 8-12hr). 70-80% response rate
- **Non-stimulants**: Atomoxetine (Strattera) — SNRI, takes 4-8 weeks for full effect. Guanfacine (Intuniv) and Clonidine (Kapvay) — alpha agonists, for hyperactivity/impulsivity, often adjunct to stimulant
- **Side effects**: Appetite suppression (eat protein-rich breakfast before meds kick in, high-calorie snacks, delayed lunch), insomnia (take ER early AM, avoid caffeine), headache, dry mouth, anxiety/jitteriness (dose too high or wrong med)
- **Dosing**: Start low, go slow. Partial response better than max dose (diminishing returns). "Holidays" (weekends off) controversial — reduces tolerance but can cause rebound. Vyvanse (prodrug) harder to abuse = less tolerance
- **Off-label/experimental**: Modafinil, Wellbutrin (bupropion), MAOIs, certain antidepressants (for comorbid conditions). NAC (N-acetylcysteine), L-tyrosine, omega-3s (modest evidence)

## Behavioral Strategies
- **Externalize executive function**: Use tools instead of willpower — calendars with alerts, phone timers, checklists, visual schedules, pill organizers. "Don't remember, just check"
- **Body doubling**: Working alongside someone else (they don't need to help, just presence helps focus). In-person or virtual (Focusmate, ADHD Discord servers)
- **Pomodoro adaptation**: 25 min work / 5 min break — but allow switching tasks between pomodoros. "Task hopping" is OK if you return. Use app blockers (Freedom, Cold Turkey) during work periods
- **Basket method (organization)**: Everything goes into one of 4 baskets: Action (needs doing soon), Waiting (delegated/pending), Archive (done), Read/Reference. No pile of "decide later"
- **Two-minute rule for everything**: If takes <2 min, do it NOW. ADHD brain overestimates task time — this bypasses the "Ugh" response
- **Open loops capture**: Write down ideas, tasks, worries immediately (phone note, pocket notebook). Brains can't relax until loop is closed. GTD capture step essential for ADHD
- **Habit stacking**: Anchor new habit to existing one — "After I brush my teeth (existing), I will take my meds (new)". Works because routine bypasses decision fatigue

## Environment Design
- **Reduce friction for good habits**: Keep meds + water on nightstand, gym bag packed, healthy snacks at eye level in fridge, phone charger away from bed
- **Increase friction for bad habits**: Remove social media from phone, keep TV unplugged, delete games, use website blockers, keep junk food in hard-to-reach place
- **Visual organization**: Transparent containers (see everything), open shelving (out of sight = out of mind, literally), bins with labels, one-touch organization (put away in same place every time)
- **Clutter threshold**: ADHD brains process visual noise more slowly. Reduce decor, use closed storage, clear surfaces. Less visual input = less cognitive load

## Relationships & ADHD
- **ADHD paralysis misinterpreted**: Partner sees "laziness", person with ADHD sees "I literally cannot start, and I hate myself for it". Education about executive function differences critical
- **Fair play / division of labor**: ADHD partner may struggle with household "mental load" (remembering, planning, initiating). Use shared task lists, clear ownership of tasks. Outsourcing (cleaning service, meal prep) is relationship investment
- **Rejection sensitivity**: Criticize behavior, not person. "Leaving dishes in sink" not "You're so messy". Request change clearly: "Please put dishes in dishwasher before bed"
- **Time blindness in relationships**: "I'll be ready in 5 min" ≠ 5 min for ADHD. Add buffer, use countdown timers. Partner can give "30 min warning" before events

## Workplace Accommodations
- **Disclosure optional**: In US, ADA covers reasonable accommodations. Examples: written instructions (not just verbal), noise-canceling headphones, flexible schedule (core hours but flexible start/end), frequent check-ins, project management software, reduced distractions
- **Task management**: Break projects into tiny steps (next action specific), use kanban (Trello, Notion), daily stand-up with self. Focus on output, not hours
- **Auditory processing**: Ask for written follow-ups after meetings. Record meetings (with permission). Take notes during conversations. Summarize back: "So what I heard was..."
