# Cancer Basics

Types, screening, treatment modalities, and terminology.

## Cancer Biology
- **Hallmarks (Hanahan & Weinberg)**: Sustained proliferative signaling, evading growth suppressors, resisting cell death, enabling replicative immortality, inducing angiogenesis, activating invasion/metastasis, reprogramming energy metabolism, evading immune destruction
- **Tumor suppressor genes**: p53 (guardian of genome — mutations in >50% cancers), Rb, BRCA1/2, APC. Inactivated (two-hit hypothesis — both copies need mutation)
- **Oncogenes**: Ras (mutated in 30% of cancers), Myc, HER2, EGFR — activated by mutation/amplification. Single mutation can drive growth
- **Metastasis**: Primary tumor → invade local tissue → intravasate into blood/lymph → survive circulation → extravasate → colonize distant site — organ tropism (some cancers prefer lung, liver, bone, brain)
- **Angiogenesis**: Tumors recruit blood vessels via VEGF — anti-angiogenic drugs (bevacizumab/Avastin) block this

## Common Cancer Types
- **Breast**: Most common in women (excluding skin) — screening mammography starting at 40-50 (USPSTF). Hormone receptor (ER/PR) and HER2 status guide treatment
- **Lung**: Leading cause of cancer death — 85% non-small cell (adenocarcinoma, squamous, large cell), 15% small cell. Lung cancer screening (low-dose CT) for high-risk (30 pack-year smoking history, age 50-80)
- **Colorectal**: Third most common — screening colonoscopy starting at 45 (average risk). Polyp removal prevents progression to cancer
- **Prostate**: Most common in men — PSA screening controversial (overdiagnosis of indolent cancer). Gleason score grades aggressiveness
- **Skin**: Basal cell (most common, rarely fatal), squamous cell (can metastasize), melanoma (most dangerous — ABCDE: Asymmetry, Border irregular, Color variation, Diameter >6mm, Evolution)
- **Pancreatic**: Very poor prognosis — often advanced at diagnosis (no symptoms until late). CA19-9 tumor marker
- **Lymphoma**: Hodgkin (Reed-Sternberg cells, good prognosis) vs Non-Hodgkin (many subtypes — some aggressive, some indolent)

## Treatment Modalities
- **Surgery**: Remove tumor + margin of healthy tissue — may be curative if caught early. Debulking reduces tumor burden for advanced cases
- **Chemotherapy**: Cytotoxic drugs kill rapidly dividing cells — platinum-based (cisplatin, carboplatin), taxanes (paclitaxel), anthracyclines (doxorubicin). Side effects: hair loss, nausea, bone marrow suppression, neuropathy
- **Radiation**: External beam (linear accelerator) or brachytherapy (implanted seeds). Kills cancer cells via DNA damage. Side effects depend on site: fatigue, skin changes, fibrosis
- **Targeted therapy**: Drugs targeting specific molecular drivers — tyrosine kinase inhibitors (imatinib for CML, erlotinib for EGFR-mutant lung), PARP inhibitors (olaparib for BRCA-mutated)
- **Immunotherapy**: Checkpoint inhibitors (pembrolizumab/Keytruda, nivolumab/Opdivo — block PD-1/PD-L1, unleash immune system against cancer), CAR-T cell therapy (engineer patient's T cells to recognize cancer), immune checkpoint inhibitors
- **Hormone therapy**: For hormone-sensitive cancers — tamoxifen/aromatase inhibitors (breast cancer), ADT/androgen deprivation therapy (prostate cancer)
- **Combination therapy**: Most cancers treated with multiple modalities — neoadjuvant (before surgery), adjuvant (after surgery to eliminate micrometastases)

## Staging
- **TNM System**: T (tumor size/invasion), N (lymph node involvement), M (metastasis). Combined into Stage 0-IV
- **Stage 0**: Carcinoma in situ — pre-invasive
- **Stage I-III**: Local to regional spread — potentially curable
- **Stage IV**: Metastatic — usually incurable but treatable (months to years of survival)
- **Staging determines treatment**: Stage I may be surgery alone; Stage III may need chemo + radiation + surgery; Stage IV is systemic therapy

## Screening
- **Breast**: Mammogram every 1-2 years starting 40-50 (USPSTF recommends 50-74; ACOG recommends 40+ annual)
- **Cervical**: Pap smear every 3 years (21-65) + HPV co-testing (30-65)
- **Colorectal**: Colonoscopy every 10 years (starting 45) or FIT test yearly
- **Lung**: Low-dose CT annually (age 50-80, 20 pack-years, current or quit <15 years ago)
- **Prostate**: PSA blood test — discuss shared decision-making (age 55-69)

## Prevention
- **No smoking**: #1 preventable cause — 30% of all cancer deaths
- **Sun protection**: SPF 30+ broad spectrum, avoid tanning beds — prevent skin cancers
- **HPV vaccine**: Prevents cervical, anal, oropharyngeal, penile cancers — recommended at age 11-12
- **Hepatitis B vaccine**: Prevents liver cancer
- **Diet/Exercise**: Healthy weight (obesity increases risk of 13 cancer types), limited alcohol (any amount increases risk), fiber-rich diet, limited processed/red meat
