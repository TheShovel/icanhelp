# Yoga Advanced & Wellness

## Beyond the Mat: Yoga Philosophy
- **The Eight Limbs of Yoga** (Patanjali's Yoga Sutras): not just poses. 1. Yamas (ethical restraints — non-violence/ahimsa, truthfulness/satya, non-stealing/asteya, celibacy/brahmacharya, non-possessiveness/aparigraha). 2. Niyamas (personal observances — purity/saucha, contentment/santosha, discipline/tapas, self-study/swadhyaya, surrender to higher/isvara pranidhana). 3. Asana (posture — the physical practice we know as "yoga"). 4. Pranayama (breath control). 5. Pratyahara (sense withdrawal). 6. Dharana (concentration). 7. Dhyana (meditation). 8. Samadhi (enlightenment/bliss — union with the divine, all is one)
  - Limbs 1-2 (ethics), 3-5 (body/ breath), 6-8 (mind). Only one limb (asana) is physical poses
- **Types of yoga beyond asana**: Karma yoga (action/service — selfless work, helping others without attachment to result), Bhakti yoga (devotion — chanting, prayer, love), Jnana yoga (knowledge — study, inquiry, wisdom texts), Raja yoga (royal/meditation — the eight limbs path)
- **Chakras**: 7 energy centers along spine: Root/Muladhara (base of spine, survival, safety), Sacral/Svadhisthana (lower belly, creativity, sexuality), Solar Plexus/Manipura (stomach, will, personal power), Heart/Anahata (chest, love, compassion), Throat/Vishuddha (throat, communication, truth), Third Eye/Ajna (brow, intuition, wisdom), Crown/Sahasrara (top of head, enlightenment, connection to divine)
  - Controversial: chakras not anatomically verifiable, but can be useful framework for body-mind connection AND some correlates to nerve plexuses, endocrine glands. "As if" model: useful for meditation

## Advanced Pranayama (Beyond Basics)
- **Ujjayi**: slight glottal constriction = ocean sound waves. Lengthen inhale + exhale equally (maybe 4-5 seconds each). Build to: 6 second inhale, 8 second exhale (count or use metronome). When to use: throughout vinyasa, calming anxiety, warming
- **Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril)**: thumb closes right nostril, inhale left 4 count, close left with ring finger, release right, exhale right 8 count (longer exhale = parasympathetic = calming). Inhale right 4, close right, exhale left 8 — repeat. 5-10 rounds. Benefits: balances hemispheres, reduces stress, anxiety, blood pressure
- **Kapalabhati (Skull Shining)**: active exhale (sniff out through nose, belly pulls in hard + fast), passive inhale (belly relaxes). Start 20 breaths, then inhale deep, hold, release slowly. Build to 50, 100. Contraindications: high blood pressure, glaucoma, hernias, pregnancy, heart conditions
- **Bhastrika (Bellows Breath)**: fast, POWERFUL inhale + exhale through nose (equal). Start 10-20 rounds, deep inhale, hold, exhale. Build to 30-50. VERY energizing — don't do before bed. Like running in place with breath. Good for: energy, clearing lungs, warming before practice
- **Sitali / Sitkari (Cooling Breaths)**: Sitali: curl tongue into tube, inhale through tongue, exhale through nose. Sitkari: teeth together, lips apart, inhale through teeth (hissing sound), exhale through nose. Both: cooling, calming, for high Pitta/heat/anger. Lower blood pressure, cool body temp. Good in hot weather or after heated practice

## Meditation in Yoga
- **Purpose**: Dhyana (limb 7) — not just relaxation, but training the mind. Yoga meditation = awareness expansion, not thought suppression. "Yogas chitta vritti nirodhah" — yoga = stilling the fluctuations of the mind
- **Types within yoga**: Trataka (candle gazing — focus on ghee lamp or candle flame at eye level, no blinking, until eyes water, then close and visualize afterimage, practice 5-15 min. Improves concentration, eyesight, memory). Yoga Nidra (yogic sleep — guided deep relaxation in savasana 20-45 min, systematic body scan, sankalpa/intention, rotation of consciousness through 61 points. Scientifically studied: reduces anxiety, improves sleep, PTSD, chronic pain. One hour yoga nidra = 4 hours deep sleep)
- **Mantra meditation**: Om (A-U-M = creation/preservation/transformation, all sounds, beginning/middle/end). So Hum ("I am that" — "So" on inhale, "Hum" on exhale — connects individual self with universal
- **Mindfulness meditation (Vipassana derived)**: anchor on breath, notice when mind wanders, bring back without judgment. 10-20 min morning + evening

## Mudras (Hand Gestures)
- **Jnana mudra**: thumb + index finger touch, other 3 extended. Gyan mudra: knowledge, wisdom, concentration. Why: may affect energy flow in subtle body, used in meditation as settled position
- **Chin mudra**: same as jnana but palms up. Receptive, open
- **Anjali mudra**: palms together at heart. Used in Namaste, prayer, centering
- **Bhairava mudra**: left hand on top of right in lap. Stillness, meditation
- **Dhyana mudra**: both hands in lap, right on left, thumbs touching. Deep meditation
- **Vayu mudra**: thumb on index finger, other 3 extended. Reduce air element — good for Vata balancing, helps with anxiety, restlessness, gas

## Yoga for Specific Conditions
- **Anxiety**: forward folds (calm nervous system), legs up the wall (restorative = activates parasympathetic. 5-20 min), child's pose, gentle breathing (ujjayi with extended exhale — 4 count in, 8 count out = triggers parasympathetic). Avoid: fast vinyasa, backbends (stimulating/sympathetic), breath of fire, inversions (can increase anxiety in some). Yin/restorative best
- **Depression**: backbends (heart-openers = invigorate, stimulate. Cobra/bhujangasana, bridge/setu bandha, camel/ustrasana. 3 min per pose. May affect adrenal glands, sympathetic stimulation), inversions (fresh blood flow to brain), strong vinyasa, sun salutations. Avoid: all child's pose in a dark room (can fuel withdrawal). Movement + breath > stillness when depressed
- **Back pain**: Cat/cow (gentle spine movement — on hands and knees, inhale drop belly (cow), exhale round spine (cat), breath-synchronized for 1-2 min). Child's pose (knees wide). Sphinx pose (gentle backbend). Reclined spinal twist (supine twist = releases back muscles). Avoid: deep forward folds (strain discs if acute disc issue), full wheel, intense backbends, heavy core work. Core = gentle engagement, not 100 crunches
- **Neck/shoulder tension**: Eagle arms (garudasana arms — cross R under L, wrap L around, bring to face height, sit up tall + lift elbows to parallel, 30 sec each side). Shoulder shrugs (lifts to ears then release). Thread the needle (on all 4s, reach hand under opposite arm, shoulder to ground). Ear to shoulder (gentle lateral neck stretch). Cat/cow with head movement (turn head side to side in cat/cow)
- **PMS/menstruation**: supported poses (hips elevated), forward folds (calm), legs up the wall (cramps, heavy flow, restless legs), gentle supine twists (cramps, lower back). Avoid: inversions (shoulder stand, headstand — traditionally and some modern teachers advise against during heavy days due to theory of "apana vayu" = energy flows downward during period). Strong core work, intense standing poses (exhausting). Restorative, yin, gentle hatha best
- **Sleep**: Yoga Nidra (studies = improves sleep quality, reduces insomnia. 20-30 min before bed). Legs up wall (5-10 min). Reclined bound angle (supta baddha konasana — soles of feet together, knees apart, supported). Gentle forward fold. Corpse pose with blanket roll under knees (lumbar support). Avoid within 2 hours of bed: vigorous practice, breath of fire, backbends, inversions. Stimulating = wake up, not sleep

## Advanced Poses & Transitions
- **Arm balances**: Crow (bakasana — hands on floor, knees on triceps, shift weight forward, feet lift. Engage core, look forward NOT down, may need block under forehead or try one foot at a time). Side crow (eka pada koundinyasana — twist, elbow into hip, both feet off). Eight-angle pose (astavakrasana). Flying pigeon (eka pada galavasana). Requires: wrist flexibility, core strength, fear of falling forward (put pillow in front)
- **Inversions**: Headstand (sirsasana — NOT on crown of head! Forearm + interlace hands, crown lightly on floor, 80% weight on forearms. Kick up with control/ pike up with core. Strong neck: contraindicated for neck injury and glaucoma and pregnancy and high blood pressure and during menstruation (varies by teacher). Wall for safety. Don't stay too long: practice over weeks/months, build from 30 sec). Handstand (adho mukha vrksasana — kick up to wall, engage core strongly, look between hands). Forearm stand (pincha mayurasana). Shoulderstand (salamba sarvangasana — blanket under shoulders NOT under neck). NOT beginner poses — practice under guidance
- **Backbends deeper**: Wheel/upward bow (urdhva dhanurasana — warm up thoroughly: cat/cow, camel, bridge, locust, bow). Lie back with hands by ears (fingertips toward shoulders), press up (bring awareness to low back: lift hips first), straighten arms. Hold 20-30 sec. Lower slowly. Repeat 3x. Put block between thighs: helps activate legs. After wheel: gentle counterposes (child's pose, forward fold, gentle supine twist)

## Yoga & Anatomy
- **Psoas**: deep hip flexor connecting lumbar spine to femur — often tight from sitting. Tight psoas = lower back pain, hip tightness. Poses: low lunge (anjaneyasana — knee down, back foot flat to ceiling, lift torso, raise arms, maybe backbend. If knee sensitive: pad knee), bridge pose, reclined hero pose (supta virasana). Release is slow — don't force
- **SI joint (sacroiliac)**: where pelvis meets sacrum — irritated by asymmetry (wide-legged standing poses, one-legged poses, deep twists, lateral bends). Signs: local pain above tailbone, one side, burning/ache. Poses to avoid/ modify: asymmetrical standing poses (warrior I, triangle — engage core, shorten stance), deep seated twists (chair twist for less SI intensity), wide-legged forward folds. Safe: symmetrical poses: child's pose, cat/cow, bridge, supine twists with both knees together
- **Hip external rotators** (piriformis, gemelli, obturators): often tight from sitting, can compress sciatic nerve = sciatica/glute pain (deep ache below glute, radiating down back of leg). Poses: pigeon pose (knee outside hand — approach gently, flex front foot, keep hips square, may stay upright or fold over front leg), figure 4/thread needle on back, reclined pigeon (appyogi's roll). If sharp pain: stop
