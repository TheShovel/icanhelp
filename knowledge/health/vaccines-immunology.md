# Vaccines & Immunology

## Immune System Basics
- **Innate immunity**: immediate, non-specific — skin, mucus, stomach acid, phagocytes (macrophages, neutrophils), complement proteins, inflammation
  - First line of defense: physical barriers (skin, mucous membranes), chemical (stomach acid, antimicrobial enzymes in tears/saliva)
  - Second line: phagocytes eat pathogens, inflammation (heat, swelling, redness = increased blood flow + immune cells)
- **Adaptive immunity**: specific, develops memory — slower first response, faster subsequent (B cells + T cells)
  - B cells: produce antibodies (proteins that bind to specific antigens). When activated → plasma cells (produce millions of antibodies) + memory B cells (wait for future exposure)
  - T cells: helper T cells (activate B cells + other immune cells), killer/ cytotoxic T cells (kill infected cells), memory T cells, regulatory T cells (prevent autoimmune attacks)
  - Key feature: specificity (targets ONE pathogen) + memory (responds faster on reinfection)
- **Antigen**: substance that triggers immune response (part of pathogen — protein, polysaccharide, etc.)
- **Antibody** (immunoglobulin): Y-shaped protein that neutralizes pathogens — binds to antigen, marks for destruction, blocks infection
  - IgG (most abundant in blood, crosses placenta — maternal immunity to newborn), IgM (first produced in initial infection, short-lived), IgA (mucous membranes — saliva, tears, breastmilk, respiratory/gut linings), IgE (allergies, parasites), IgD (B cell activation)
- **Memory**: after infection, memory B and T cells persist for years/decades — provide rapid response to reinfection (this is what vaccines create without the dangerous infection)

## How Vaccines Work
- **Principle**: expose immune system to harmless version of pathogen → creates memory → rapid response when real pathogen appears
  - Vaccinated person may still get infected (no vaccine is 100% effective) but much less likely to have severe disease, hospitalization, or death
  - Herd immunity: when enough of population is immune, pathogen can't spread easily — protects those who can't be vaccinated (infants, immunocompromised, elderly)
- **Types**:
  - **Live attenuated**: weakened live virus — strongest immune response (closest to natural infection), 1-2 doses usually enough. Examples: MMR (measles, mumps, rubella), chickenpox, yellow fever, nasal spray flu vaccine (FluMist)
    - Cannot give to severely immunocompromised. Rarely: can cause mild form of disease
  - **Inactivated/killed**: dead virus — safe for immunocompromised, need more doses (3-4 for polio). Example: polio (IPV), hepatitis A, rabies, flu shot (injectable), whole-cell pertussis
  - **Subunit / recombinant**: only specific antigen pieces (not whole pathogen) — very safe, well-tolerated, need boosters. Examples: HPV, hepatitis B, shingles (Shingrix — highly effective 97% in 50+), acellular pertussis, pneumococcal (Prevnar/Pneumovax)
  - **mRNA**: genetic instructions for antigen protein — new technology (COVID-19 vaccines). Body produces antigen, immune system responds. Advantages: fast to develop, no live virus needed, strong immune response. Examples: Pfizer-BioNTech, Moderna COVID-19
    - mRNA does NOT enter nucleus (no interaction with DNA), degrades quickly in body, does NOT change your genetic code
  - **Viral vector**: harmless virus delivers antigen gene (J&J COVID-19, Ebola). First used for COVID-19 (AstraZeneca, J&J — rare blood clotting TTS identified, weighed against COVID hospitalization risk which was much higher)
  - **Toxoid**: inactivated bacterial toxin (tetanus, diphtheria). Prevents disease caused by toxin, not the bacteria itself

## Vaccine Schedule (US — CDC, 2024)
- **Infants/children**: birth (HepB), 2 months (HepB, DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV, RV), 4 months (same), 6 months (same + flu 6mo+), 12-15 months (MMR, varicella, HepA, PCV booster), 4-6 years (DTaP, IPV, MMR, varicella boosters), 11-12 (Tdap, HPV, MenACWY, flu seasonal)
- **Adults**: Tdap booster every 10 years (tetanus/diphtheria/pertussis), flu shot yearly, shingles (Shingrix 2 doses at 50+), pneumococcal (PCV15/20 at 65+ or earlier with risk factors), RSV (60+ or pregnant), COVID-19 updated booster annually
- **Travel vaccines**: hepatitis A, typhoid, yellow fever (required for some countries — International Certificate of Vaccination), Japanese encephalitis, rabies (pre-exposure for high-risk), cholera

## Vaccine Safety
- **Monitoring systems**: VAERS (Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System — passive, anyone can report, includes all reports not necessarily caused by vaccine), Vaccine Safety Datalink (active surveillance, large databases comparing vaccinated vs unvaccinated populations)
  - VAERS = not proof of causation — it's a flagging system (finds potential signals to investigate). If you get a cold after vaccine, you should report it — doesn't mean vaccine caused it
- **Common side effects**: sore arm, low-grade fever, fatigue, headache, muscle ache — typically 1-2 days, sign immune system is responding
  - Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis): very rare (1-2 per million doses), observation period 15-30 min after vaccination
- **Ingredients**: antigens, adjuvants (aluminum salts — boost immune response, safe at vaccine doses. You ingest more aluminum in an antacid tablet than in all vaccines combined), stabilizers (gelatin, sugar), preservatives (thimerosal — removed from nearly all childhood vaccines since 2001, mercury-free formulations available), trace amounts of egg protein, antibiotics (neomycin — trace)
  - Thimerosal: ethylmercury (different from methylmercury — doesn't accumulate in body, excreted quickly). Causal link to autism: ZERO evidence (original Wakefield study retracted, author lost license, replicated 20+ times with null result)

## Common Myths & Debunking
- "Vaccines cause autism" — ORIGINAL STUDY FRAUDULENT AND RETRACTED (Andrew Wakefield, 1998). 20+ subsequent studies involving millions of children found NO link. Wakefield's license revoked
  - Autism is primarily genetic (80%+ heritability). If vaccine timing correlates with diagnosis, it's because autism symptoms emerge around same age MMR is given (15-18 months) — temporal correlation ≠ causation
- "Too many vaccines overload the immune system" — infants' immune systems handle thousands of antigens daily (from food, environment, bacteria). All childhood vaccines combined = tiny fraction of what immune system encounters and processes naturally
  - In 1980s: 7 vaccines, ~3,000 antigens. Today: 14 vaccines, ~150 antigens (better science = fewer antigens per vaccine)
- "Natural immunity is better" — natural immunity from infection is often stronger but comes at cost of the disease (hospitalization, death, long-term complications). Measles: 1 in 5 hospitalized, 1 in 1,000 encephalitis, 1-2 in 1,000 die. Polio: 1 in 200 permanent paralysis. Vaccines provide immunity WITHOUT the disease
- "mRNA vaccines alter DNA" — mRNA does NOT enter cell nucleus (where DNA lives), can't integrate, degrades within hours/days. It's like giving chef a recipe — recipe doesn't become part of kitchen
- "The flu vaccine gives you the flu" — flu shot is inactivated (dead) virus — can NOT cause flu. Nasal spray (live attenuated) can cause mild cold-like symptoms. Feeling achy/feverish after flu shot = immune response, not flu
- "We don't need vaccines for rare diseases" — they're rare BECAUSE of vaccines. Polio was paralyzing 35,000 people/year in US in 1940s — now eliminated, thanks to vaccine

## Herd Immunity Thresholds
- Measles: 95% vaccination needed (most contagious — R0 = 12-18, one infected person infects 12-18 others in unvaccinated population)
- Polio: 80-85%. COVID-19 (original): 70-80% (variants with higher transmissibility need higher, plus waning immunity requires boosters)
- Whooping cough (pertussis): 92-94%. Diphtheria: 85%. Rubella: 83-85%. Mumps: 75-86% (outbreaks still happen in vaccinated populations — lower efficacy, needs close contact)
- When vaccination rates drop below threshold, outbreaks return (Measles: 2019 US outbreaks in undervaccinated communities — largest in decades. Whooping cough: resurgence linked to waning immunity from acellular vaccine and lower coverage)

## Vaccine Development & Approval
- **Timeline**: normally 10-15 years (preclinical → Phase I safety → Phase II dosing → Phase III efficacy → review → manufacturing → distribution)
  - COVID-19 vaccines: ~11 months (unprecedented — not cutting corners but overlapping phases, massive funding, existing mRNA platform research since 2000s, high infection rates meant faster results)
  - No steps were skipped — regulatory standards met (FDA EUA = same safety standards as full approval, reviewed by independent experts)
- **Phases**: Phase I (20-100 people, safety), Phase II (hundreds, dosing), Phase III (thousands, efficacy vs placebo), Phase IV (ongoing post-approval monitoring — for rare side effects, long-term effectiveness)
- **Adjuvants**: aluminum salts, MF59 (oil-in-water emulsion for flu), AS01 (liposome-based for shingles), CpG 1018 (for hepatitis B)
  - Help immune system respond more strongly — reduce amount of antigen needed per dose
