# OCD (Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder)

OCD is characterized by obsessions (intrusive thoughts) and compulsions (repetitive behaviors to neutralize anxiety).

## Symptoms
- **Obsessions**: Unwanted, intrusive thoughts/images/urges — contamination, doubt/checking, symmetry/order, taboo thoughts (harm, sexual, religious)
- **Compulsions**: Repetitive behaviors or mental acts performed to reduce obsession-related anxiety — washing, checking, counting, repeating, praying, seeking reassurance
- **Cycle**: Obsession → Anxiety → Compulsion → Temporary relief → More obsessions (obsession becomes stronger)
- **Insight spectrum**: Good/fair insight (recognizes thoughts are irrational), poor insight (believes them), absent insight/delusional

## Types
- **Contamination**: Fear of germs/contaminants → excessive washing, avoidance, cleaning rituals
- **Checking**: Fear of harm due to negligence → repeatedly checking locks, stove, appliances
- **Symmetry/Order**: Feeling things must be "just right" → arranging items symmetrically, repeating actions until they feel right
- **Harm OCD**: Intrusive thoughts of harming self or others → reassurance seeking, avoidance of knives/tall places, mental review
- **Sexual OCD**: Unwanted sexual thoughts about children, family, inappropriate partners → avoidance, checking arousal, confession seeking
- **Scrupulosity**: Religious/moral obsessions → excessive prayer, confession, reassurance from religious authorities
- **Hoarding** (separate diagnosis in DSM-5): Difficulty discarding items, distress at throwing away

## Treatment
- **ERP (Exposure and Response Prevention)**: Gold standard therapy — voluntarily expose to obsession trigger, refrain from compulsion. Example: touch doorknob in public restroom, wait 30 min before washing
- **CBT**: Cognitive restructuring — challenge distorted beliefs about responsibility, threat probability, need for certainty
- **SSRIs**: Fluoxetine (Prozac), Fluvoxamine (Luvox), Sertraline (Zoloft), Paroxetine (Paxil) — high doses often needed
- **Clomipramine (Anafranil)**: Tricyclic antidepressant — effective but more side effects (anticholinergic)
- **Deep Brain Stimulation**: For severe, treatment-resistant OCD — electrodes in ventral striatum/ anterior limb of internal capsule

## ERP Principles
- **Hierarchy**: Create fear ladder from least to most triggering — start at 40% anxiety, work up to 90%
- **Habituation**: Anxiety naturally decreases over time if compulsion is prevented (90-120 min for peak to drop)
- **No reassurance**: Therapist/family don't answer "Is it safe?" "Did I hit someone?" — reassurance seeking is a compulsion
- **Response prevention ≠ thought suppression**: Don't push away thoughts — accept them without reacting

## Related Disorders (OCD Spectrum)
- **Body Dysmorphic Disorder**: Preoccupation with perceived flaws in appearance — mirror checking, grooming rituals, cosmetic surgery seeking
- **Trichotillomania**: Hair-pulling disorder — urges to pull hair, tension before, relief/gratification after
- **Excoriation Disorder**: Skin-picking disorder — repetitive picking causing tissue damage
- **Hoarding Disorder**: Persistent difficulty discarding possessions — regardless of value
- **Tourette Syndrome**: Motor/vocal tics — often co-occurs with OCD

## Misconceptions
- "I'm so OCD" (about liking organization) trivializes the disorder — OCD causes significant distress/impairment
- OCD is not perfectionism — it's driven by fear and anxiety, not desire for order
- People with OCD rarely act on violent/sexual intrusive thoughts — they are distressed BY them
- Counting/tapping rituals are not superstition — they are anxiety-reduction compulsions
