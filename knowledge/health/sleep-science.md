# Sleep Science & Optimization

## Overview
Sleep is a biological necessity for cognitive function, physical recovery, immune health, and emotional regulation. Adults need 7-9 hours; quality matters as much as quantity.

## Sleep Architecture

### Sleep Stages (90-minute cycles)
```
Cycle 1:  N1 → N2 → N3 → N2 → REM  (~90 min)
Cycle 2:  N1 → N2 → N3 → N2 → REM  (~90 min)
Cycle 3:  N1 → N2 → N2 → REM       (less N3)
Cycle 4:  N1 → N2 → N2 → REM       (more REM)
Cycle 5:  N1 → N2 → N2 → REM       (mostly REM)
```

| Stage | Name | Duration | Function |
|-------|------|----------|----------|
| N1 | Light transition | 1-7 min | Falling asleep |
| N2 | Light sleep | 10-25 min | Memory consolidation, motor skills |
| N3 | Deep/Slow-wave | 20-40 min | Physical repair, growth hormone, immune |
| REM | Rapid Eye Movement | 10-60 min | Emotional processing, creativity, learning |

### Key Metrics
- **Sleep Efficiency**: Time asleep / Time in bed (>85% good)
- **Sleep Latency**: Time to fall asleep (10-20 min normal)
- **WASO**: Wake After Sleep Onset (<30 min good)
- **Deep Sleep %**: 13-23% of total
- **REM %**: 20-25% of total

## Circadian Rhythm

### Master Clock
- **SCN (Suprachiasmatic Nucleus)** in hypothalamus
- Entrained by light (especially blue 460-480nm)
- Controls melatonin, cortisol, body temperature

### Optimal Timing
```
6:00 AM   Cortisol peak (wake signal)
7:00 AM   Melatonin stops
10:00 AM  Peak alertness
2:00 PM   Post-lunch dip (circadian)
6:00 PM   Peak physical performance
9:00 PM   Melatonin rises (dim light)
10:30 PM  Sleep gate opens
2:00 AM   Deepest sleep
4:00 AM   Lowest body temperature
```

## Light Management

### Morning (Critical for Entrainment)
- **10,000+ lux** within 30 min of waking
- Outside: 10 min sunny, 20 min cloudy
- Light therapy lamp: 10,000 lux at 12-18 inches
- No sunglasses (unless driving)

### Evening (Melatonin Protection)
- **< 10 lux** 2-3 hours before bed
- Blue blockers: Orange/red lenses after sunset
- f.lux / Night Shift / Dark mode on devices
- Dim red lights in bathroom/hallway
- Blackout curtains or eye mask

### Bedroom
- Pitch black (can't see hand)
- 65-68°F (18-20°C)
- White noise / earplugs if needed
- No LED indicator lights

## Sleep Hygiene

### The 3-2-1 Rule
```
3 hours before bed: No heavy meals, alcohol
2 hours before bed: No work, intense exercise
1 hour before bed:  No screens, dim lights, wind down
```

### Wind-Down Routine (30-60 min)
1. Brain dump (write tomorrow's tasks)
2. Warm shower/bath (core temp drop induces sleep)
3. Stretching / yoga / progressive muscle relaxation
4. Reading (physical book, dim light)
5. Meditation / breathwork / NSDR
6. Lights out

### Bedroom Rules
- Bed = Sleep + Sex only
- No phone, TV, work, eating
- If not asleep in 20 min → get up, do boring thing until sleepy

## Nutrition & Supplements

### Timing
- **Caffeine cutoff**: 8-10 hours before bed (half-life 5-6 hrs)
- **Alcohol**: 3+ hours before (fragments sleep, suppresses REM)
- **Large meals**: 3 hours before
- **Hydration**: Front-load, taper evening

### Evidence-Based Supplements
| Supplement | Dose | Timing | Evidence |
|------------|------|--------|----------|
| **Magnesium Glycinate** | 200-400mg | 1 hr before | Moderate (relaxation) |
| **Glycine** | 3g | 30 min before | Moderate (temp drop) |
| **L-Theanine** | 200-400mg | 30 min before | Moderate (relaxation) |
| **Melatonin** | 0.3-1mg | 30-60 min before | Strong (circadian shift) |
| **Apigenin** | 50mg | 30 min before | Weak (chamomile) |
| **Tart Cherry** | 30ml juice | 1 hr before | Weak (melatonin) |

### Avoid
- Melatonin >3mg (downregulates receptors)
- Benadryl/DPH (anticholinergic, cognitive risk)
- Alcohol as sleep aid (rebound wakefulness)
- CBD (inconsistent dosing, drug interactions)

## Exercise & Sleep

### Timing Effects
| Time | Effect on Sleep |
|------|----------------|
| Morning | Best for circadian entrainment |
| Afternoon | Good, deep sleep increase |
| Evening (<3hr) | May delay onset (core temp) |
| Late night | Avoid (cortisol, alertness) |

### Types
- **Resistance training**: Increases deep sleep
- **Cardio**: Increases total sleep, efficiency
- **Yoga/stretching**: Reduces latency, improves quality
- **HIIT late**: Can impair if <3 hours before bed

## Tracking & Data

### Wearables (Accuracy Ranking)
1. **EEG headbands** (Dreem, Muse) - Gold standard consumer
2. **Oura Ring** - Good for trends, HRV, temp
3. **Whoop** - Good strain/recovery, sleep staging
4. **Apple Watch / Garmin** - Decent duration, poor staging
5. **Phone apps** - Poor (movement only)

### Key Metrics to Track
- **HRV trend** (not absolute) - Recovery indicator
- **Resting HR trend** - Fitness/stress
- **Body temp deviation** - Illness/ovulation
- **Respiratory rate** - Baseline stability
- **Sleep consistency** (wake time) - Most important

### Don't Obsess
- Single night data
- Stage percentages (consumer devices inaccurate)
- "Sleep score" (proprietary algorithms)
- Orthosomnia (anxiety from tracking)

## Common Disorders

### Insomnia
- **Acute**: <3 months, stress-related
- **Chronic**: >3 months, 3+ nights/week
- **CBT-I** (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy for Insomnia) - First-line treatment
  - Sleep restriction
  - Stimulus control
  - Cognitive restructuring
  - Relaxation training

### Sleep Apnea
- **OSA**: Airway collapse, snoring, gasping
- **CSA**: Brain doesn't signal breathing
- **Screening**: STOP-BANG questionnaire
- **Diagnosis**: Home sleep test or polysomnography
- **Treatment**: CPAP, oral appliance, weight loss, positional therapy

### Other
- **Restless Leg Syndrome**: Iron deficiency, dopamine
- **Circadian Rhythm Disorders**: DSPS, ASPS, shift work
- **Parasomnias**: Sleepwalking, night terrors, REM behavior disorder

## Special Populations

### Shift Workers
- Bright light at shift start
- Blue blockers commuting home
- Dark, cool bedroom + white noise
- Melatonin 0.5mg before day sleep
- Strategic napping (20 min or 90 min)
- Consistent schedule even days off

### Parents/Newborns
- Sleep when baby sleeps (first 3 months)
- Split shifts with partner
- Safe co-sleeping if chosen
- Sleep training options (4-6 months)
- Accept temporary disruption

### Older Adults
- Earlier circadian phase (advanced sleep phase)
- Less deep sleep, more fragmentation
- Medical comorbidities, medications
- Same sleep need, harder to get
- Treat underlying causes (apnea, pain, prostate)

### Athletes
- 8-10 hours + naps
- Sleep extension improves performance
- Track HRV for training readiness
- Travel: arrive 1 day per time zone
- Bank sleep before competition

## Napping

### Ideal Nap
| Type | Duration | Benefit |
|------|----------|---------|
| Power nap | 10-20 min | Alertness, no inertia |
| Full cycle | 90 min | Complete cycle, creativity |
| Avoid | 30-60 min | Sleep inertia (wake in deep) |

### Rules
- Before 3 PM (protects night sleep)
- Dark, quiet, cool
- Set alarm
- Coffee nap: Espresso → 20 min nap → wake as caffeine hits

## When to See a Doctor
- Snoring + witnessed apneas + daytime sleepiness
- Chronic insomnia >3 months despite CBT-I
- Excessive sleepiness despite 7-9 hours
- Restless legs disrupting sleep
- Acting out dreams (REM behavior disorder)
- Morning headaches + unrefreshing sleep

## Quick Reference: 7-Day Reset
```
Day 1: Fixed wake time (even weekends), morning light
Day 2: Caffeine cutoff 10 hrs before bed
Day 3: No alcohol, 3-hr food cutoff
Day 4: Wind-down routine, phone outside bedroom
Day 5: Bedroom 65°F, pitch black
Day 6: Morning exercise, evening stretch
Day 7: Review data, adjust one thing
```
