# Exercise & Fitness Basics

## Types of Exercise
- **Cardio**: running, cycling, swimming, jumping rope — heart/lung health, calorie burn
- **Strength**: weight lifting, bodyweight, resistance bands — muscle, bone density, metabolism
- **Flexibility**: stretching, yoga, pilates — range of motion, injury prevention
- **Balance**: single-leg stands, tai chi — fall prevention, core stability

## Recommended Weekly Activity
- Adults: 150 min moderate OR 75 min vigorous cardio per week
- Plus 2+ days strength training (all major muscle groups)
- Moderate: brisk walking (can talk but not sing), 3+ mph
- Vigorous: jogging/running (can't say more than few words)

## Bodyweight Exercises (no equipment)
- **Push-ups**: chest, shoulders, triceps — start on knees if needed
- **Squats**: quads, glutes, hamstrings — keep back straight, knees over toes
- **Planks**: core — keep body straight line, don't sag hips
- **Lunges**: legs, balance — front knee at 90°, back knee near ground
- **Pull-ups**: back, biceps — use assist bands or negatives if can't do one
- **Burpees**: full body — squat → plank → push-up → jump
- **Dips**: triceps, chest — use chair or bench
- **Glute bridges**: glutes, lower back — lift hips up, squeeze glutes at top

## Weight Training Principles
- **Progressive overload**: gradually increase weight/reps/sets to keep making gains
- **Compound exercises**: squats, deadlifts, bench press, rows, overhead press — most efficient
- **Isolation exercises**: bicep curls, tricep extensions, leg curls — target specific muscles
- **Reps**: 1-5 strength, 6-12 hypertrophy (muscle growth), 15+ endurance
- **Rest**: 3-5 min between heavy sets, 1-2 min for hypertrophy, 30-60 sec for endurance
- **Form over weight**: bad form causes injury, not gains

## Running Basics
- Start with run/walk intervals (e.g., 1 min run + 2 min walk, repeat)
- Increase weekly mileage by no more than 10%
- Cadence: ~170-180 steps/min to reduce injury risk
- Land on midfoot, not heel
- Replace shoes every 300-500 miles
- Couch to 5K (C25K) is a proven beginner program
- Warm up: dynamic stretches (leg swings, walking lunges)
- Cool down: static stretches after run

## Stretching
- **Dynamic** (before exercise): leg swings, arm circles, torso twists, walking lunges
- **Static** (after exercise): hold 20-30 sec, no bouncing
- Key stretches: hamstrings, quads, hip flexors, chest, shoulders, lower back, calves
- Stretch to mild tension, not pain

## Common Injuries & Prevention
- **Runner's knee**: pain around kneecap — strengthen quads, don't increase mileage too fast
- **Shin splints**: pain along shin bone — proper shoes, gradual increase, calf stretches
- **Plantar fasciitis**: heel pain in morning — calf stretches, frozen water bottle roll
- **Tennis elbow**: outer elbow pain — eccentric wrist exercises, rest
- **Lower back pain**: weak core + tight hips — strengthen core, stretch hip flexors
- General: warm up properly, don't overtrain, rest days are essential

## Rest & Recovery
- Muscles grow during rest, not during workouts
- Sleep 7-9 hours for optimal recovery
- Active recovery: light walking, stretching, yoga on rest days
- Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness (DOMS): 24-72 hr after workout — normal, light movement helps
- If joint pain (not muscle soreness): take a break, ice, see doctor if persistent
- Rest days: 1-2 per week minimum

## Motivation Tips
- Schedule workouts like appointments
- Start small: 10 min is better than 0 min
- Find an activity you actually enjoy
- Track progress (weights, times, photos)
- Workout buddy = accountability
- Habit stacking: attach exercise to existing habit (e.g., after coffee)
- Missing one day is fine — missing two becomes a pattern
