# Exercise Science & Training Principles

## Overview
Exercise physiology studies how the body responds and adapts to physical activity. Understanding these principles enables effective program design for health, performance, and longevity.

## Fundamental Principles

### SAID Principle (Specific Adaptations to Imposed Demands)
> The body adapts specifically to the type of stress placed upon it.

| Training Stress | Primary Adaptation |
|-----------------|-------------------|
| Heavy load (1-5 RM) | Neural strength, myofibrillar hypertrophy |
| Moderate load (6-12 RM) | Sarcoplasmic + myofibrillar hypertrophy |
| Light load (15+ RM) | Muscular endurance, metabolic |
| High velocity | Power, rate of force development |
| Long duration | Aerobic capacity, mitochondrial density |
| Stretch/ROM | Flexibility, fascicle length |

### Progressive Overload
Gradual increase in stress to drive continued adaptation:
```
Methods (apply one at a time):
1. Load ↑ (weight)
2. Volume ↑ (sets × reps)
3. Frequency ↑ (sessions/week)
4. Density ↓ (rest intervals)
5. Range of motion ↑
6. Tempo ↓ (slower eccentric)
7. Exercise complexity ↑
8. Intensity techniques (drop sets, rest-pause)
```

### Dose-Response Relationship
```
Volume landmarks (per muscle/week):
Maintenance:     6-10 sets
Minimum Effective: 10-12 sets
Optimal:         12-20 sets
Maximum Recoverable: 20-25 sets (advanced)
```

### Fatigue Management
```
Fatigue types:
- Peripheral (muscle): Metabolite accumulation, damage
- Central (CNS): Neural drive reduction
- Psychological: Motivation, perceived effort

Recovery hierarchy:
1. Sleep (7-9 hrs)
2. Nutrition (protein, calories, carbs)
3. Stress management
4. Active recovery / deloads
5. Supplements (marginal)
```

## Resistance Training

### Rep Ranges & Outcomes
| Reps | %1RM | Primary Outcome | Rest |
|------|------|-----------------|------|
| 1-3 | 90-100% | Max strength, neural | 3-5 min |
| 4-6 | 85-90% | Strength + hypertrophy | 2-3 min |
| 6-8 | 80-85% | Hypertrophy + strength | 2-3 min |
| 8-12 | 70-80% | Hypertrophy | 1.5-2 min |
| 12-15 | 60-70% | Hypertrophy + endurance | 1-1.5 min |
| 15-20 | 50-60% | Endurance, metabolic | 1 min |
| 20+ | <50% | Endurance, rehab | 30-60 sec |

### Proximity to Failure
- **RPE 10**: Failure (no more reps possible)
- **RPE 9**: 1 rep left (grinder)
- **RPE 8**: 2 reps left (solid)
- **RPE 7**: 3 reps left (comfortable)
- **RPE 6**: 4 reps left (warm-up)

**Hypertrophy**: RPE 7-9 (1-3 RIR)
**Strength**: RPE 8-10 (0-2 RIR)
**Beginners**: RPE 6-8 (learn technique)

### Exercise Selection Hierarchy
```
Tier 1: Compound (multi-joint, high load)
  Squat, Deadlift, Bench, Overhead Press, Row, Pull-up, Dip

Tier 2: Compound variations / assistance
  Lunge, RDL, Incline Press, Chin-up, Push-up, Hip Thrust

Tier 3: Isolation (single-joint)
  Curl, Extension, Raise, Fly, Calf Raise, Crunch

Ratio: 60-70% Tier 1, 20-30% Tier 2, 10-20% Tier 3
```

### Periodization Models

#### Linear (Classic)
```
Mesocycle 1: Hypertrophy (3×10-12, 65-75%)
Mesocycle 2: Strength (4×4-6, 80-85%)
Mesocycle 3: Power (5×1-3, 85-95%)
Mesocycle 4: Peaking (1-3×1-3, 90%+)
Deload between each
```

#### Undulating (DUP)
```
Week 1: Mon 3×10, Wed 3×6, Fri 3×3
Week 2: Mon 3×8, Wed 3×5, Fri 3×2
Week 3: Mon 3×6, Wed 3×4, Fri 3×1
```

#### Block (Conjugate)
```
Block 1 (4-6 wk): Accumulation (volume)
Block 2 (4-6 wk): Transmutation (intensity)
Block 3 (2-4 wk): Realization (peaking)
Block 4 (1-2 wk): Deload/Transition
```

### Sample Programs

#### 3-Day Full Body (Beginner/Intermediate)
```
A: Squat 3×8, Bench 3×8, Row 3×10, RDL 3×10, OH Press 2×10, Plank 3×30s
B: Deadlift 3×6, OHP 3×8, Pull-up 3×8, Lunge 3×10, Incline Press 3×10, Face Pull 3×15

Schedule: A-B-A, B-A-B (alternating)
Progression: Add 2.5-5 lbs when top of rep range hit for all sets
```

#### 4-Day Upper/Lower (Intermediate)
```
Upper A: Bench 4×6, Row 4×8, Incline DB 3×10, Face Pull 3×15, Curl 3×12, Tri Ext 3×12
Lower A: Squat 4×6, RDL 3×8, Leg Press 3×10, Leg Curl 3×12, Calf 4×15, Plank 3×45s
Upper B: OHP 4×6, Pull-up 4×8, Flat DB 3×10, Rear Delt 3×15, Hammer Curl 3×12, Pushdown 3×12
Lower B: Deadlift 3×5, Front Squat 3×8, Bulgarian 3×10, Hip Thrust 3×12, Calf 4×15

Schedule: Upper A, Lower A, Rest, Upper B, Lower B, Rest, Rest
```

#### 5-Day Body Part Split (Advanced)
```
Mon: Chest/Back (horizontal push/pull focus)
Tue: Legs (quad dominant)
Wed: Rest
Thu: Shoulders/Arms (vertical push/pull + accessories)
Fri: Legs (hip dominant)
Sat: Weak points / Conditioning
Sun: Rest
```

## Cardiovascular Training

### Energy Systems
| System | Duration | Intensity | Primary Fuel | Recovery |
|--------|----------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| ATP-PCr | 0-10 sec | Maximal | Phosphocreatine | 3-5 min |
| Glycolytic | 10 sec - 2 min | High | Glucose/glycogen | 1-3 min |
| Oxidative | 2 min+ | Low-Moderate | Fat + glucose | Continuous |

### Training Zones (5-Zone Model)
| Zone | Name | %Max HR | %VO2max | RPE | Duration | Adaptation |
|------|------|---------|---------|-----|----------|------------|
| 1 | Recovery | 50-60% | <55% | 1-2 | Unlimited | Active recovery, fat ox |
| 2 | Aerobic Base | 60-70% | 55-75% | 2-3 | 45-180 min | Mitochondria, capillarization |
| 3 | Tempo | 70-80% | 75-90% | 4-5 | 20-60 min | Lactate threshold ↑ |
| 4 | Threshold | 80-90% | 90-100% | 6-7 | 10-30 min | VO2max, lactate clearance |
| 5 | VO2max | 90-100% | 100%+ | 8-10 | 3-8 min | Cardiac output, max power |

### Zone 2 Training (Foundation)
- **80% of cardio volume** should be Zone 2
- **Talk test**: Can hold conversation comfortably
- **Maffetone**: 180 - age = max HR (adjust ±5)
- Builds aerobic base, mitochondrial efficiency, fat oxidation

### HIIT Protocols
| Protocol | Work | Rest | Sets | Total | Frequency |
|----------|------|------|------|-------|-----------|
| Tabata | 20s | 10s | 8 | 4 min | 1-2×/wk |
| Wingate | 30s | 4 min | 4-6 | 20-30 min | 1×/wk |
| 4×4 Norwegian | 4 min | 3 min | 4 | 28 min | 1-2×/wk |
| 10×1 | 1 min | 1 min | 10 | 20 min | 1-2×/wk |
| Sprint Interval | 10-30s | 60-120s | 6-10 | 10-20 min | 1×/wk |

### Concurrent Training (Strength + Cardio)
- **Interference effect**: Cardio can blunt strength/hypertrophy
- **Minimize**: Separate sessions by 6+ hours, or different days
- **Priority**: Do goal-focused training first in session
- **Best combo**: Strength + Zone 2 (low interference)
- **Worst combo**: Strength + HIIT leg-focused (high interference)

## Mobility & Flexibility

### Types of Stretching
| Type | When | Duration | Mechanism |
|------|------|----------|-----------|
| Static | Post-workout, separate session | 30-60s × 2-4 | Autogenic inhibition, viscoelastic |
| Dynamic | Warm-up | 10-15 reps | Neural activation, temp ↑ |
| PNF | Post-workout, partner | 6s contract + 30s stretch | GTO inhibition, reciprocal inhibition |
| Ballistic | Sport-specific only | - | High injury risk |

### Mobility vs Flexibility
- **Flexibility**: Passive ROM (stretch tolerance)
- **Mobility**: Active ROM (strength + control through range)
- **Train mobility**: Controlled Articular Rotations (CARs), PAILs/RAILs, loaded stretching

### Key Areas to Address
```
Ankle dorsiflexion → Squat depth, running
Hip flexion/IR/ER → Squat, deadlift, athletic
Thoracic extension/rotation → Overhead, pressing, posture
Shoulder flexion/ER → Overhead, pull-up, bench
Wrist extension → Push-up, front rack, handstand
```

## Recovery Modalities

### Evidence Hierarchy
```
Tier 1 (Strong evidence): Sleep, nutrition, load management
Tier 2 (Moderate): Active recovery, compression, cold water immersion
Tier 3 (Weak/mixed): Massage, foam rolling, stretching, sauna
Tier 4 (Minimal): Percussion guns, e-stim, cupping, supplements
```

### Sleep Optimization
- 7-9 hours, consistent schedule
- Cool (65-68°F), dark, quiet
- No caffeine after 2 PM
- No alcohol 3 hrs before bed
- Morning light exposure
- Wind-down routine

### Nutrition for Recovery
```
Post-workout (0-2 hrs):
- Protein: 0.4-0.5 g/kg (20-40g)
- Carbs: 1-1.2 g/kg (if glycogen depleted)
- Fluid: 1.5× weight lost

Daily:
- Protein: 1.6-2.2 g/kg (4-5 meals × 0.4-0.55 g/kg)
- Carbs: 4-7 g/kg (athlete), 3-5 g/kg (recreational)
- Fat: 0.8-1.2 g/kg
- Creatine: 5g daily
- Vitamin D3: 2000-5000 IU
- Omega-3: 2-3g EPA/DHA
```

### Deload Strategies
```
Frequency: Every 4-8 weeks (or when performance drops)
Methods:
- Volume deload: 50% sets, same intensity
- Intensity deload: Same volume, 50-60% load
- Frequency deload: Fewer sessions
- Complete rest: 3-7 days (overreached)
```

## Special Populations

### Older Adults (65+)
- **Priority**: Power, balance, bone density
- **Frequency**: 2-3×/wk resistance, daily balance
- **Intensity**: 70-85% 1RM (safe with supervision)
- **Exercises**: Machines → free weights progression
- **Fall prevention**: Single-leg, perturbation training

### Youth (6-17)
- **Focus**: Movement literacy, fun, variety
- **Strength**: Safe at all ages (no growth plate risk)
- **Guidelines**: 60 min/day moderate-vigorous
- **Resistance**: 2-3×/wk, bodyweight → external load
- **Avoid**: Early specialization, max testing

### Pregnancy
- **ACOG**: 150 min/wk moderate aerobic + resistance
- **Avoid**: Supine after 1st trimester, Valsalva, contact, fall risk
- **Modify**: Reduce intensity by 10-20%, monitor RPE
- **Core**: Diaphragmatic breathing, pelvic floor, avoid coning
- **Postpartum**: 6-8 wk clearance, progressive return

## Injury Prevention & Rehab

### Load Management
```
Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio (ACWR)
- Acute: 1-week load
- Chronic: 4-week rolling average
- Sweet spot: 0.8-1.3
- Injury risk ↑ >1.5
```

### Common Issues
| Issue | Common Cause | Rehab Focus |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Low back pain | Load spikes, poor hinge | Core stability, hip mobility, graded exposure |
| Knee pain (PFPS) | Quad weakness, hip control | Quad strengthening, glute med, patellar taping |
| Shoulder impingement | Scapular dyskinesis, posterior capsule | Serratus, lower trap, posterior capsule mobs |
| Tendinopathy | Load spike, compression | Isometric → slow heavy → energy storage |
| Hamstring strain | Eccentric weakness, fatigue | Nordics, lengthened eccentrics, sprint progression |

### Return to Play Criteria
- No pain with sport-specific movements
- Strength ≥90% contralateral
- Power/speed ≥90%
- Psychological readiness (confidence)
- Passed sport-specific testing

## Monitoring & Testing

### Strength Testing
```
1RM Prediction (Epley): 1RM = Weight × (1 + Reps/30)
Rep Max Testing: Safer than true 1RM
Velocity-Based: Mean propulsive velocity correlates with %1RM
```

### Hypertrophy Tracking
- Body weight (weekly avg)
- Circumference measurements (monthly)
- Progress photos (monthly, standardized)
- Strength in hypertrophy rep ranges

### Cardiovascular Testing
- **VO2max**: Lab (gold), watch estimate, Cooper test (12-min run)
- **Resting HR**: Morning, weekly avg (↓ = fitness ↑)
- **HRV**: Morning, lnRMSSD trend (↑ = recovery ↑)
- **Lactate threshold**: Portable analyzer or field test
