# Yoga & Stretching

## Yoga Styles
- **Hatha**: slow, basic poses, good for beginners — focus on alignment, hold poses longer
- **Vinyasa**: flowing, breath-synchronized movement, sun salutations — more athletic
- **Ashtanga**: fixed sequence of poses, same every time, physically demanding, 90 min
- **Iyengar**: alignment-focused, use props (blocks, straps, blankets), hold poses long
- **Yin**: passive, floor poses held 3-5 minutes, targets deep connective tissue — meditative, complements "yang" exercise
- **Restorative**: total relaxation, props support body, hold 10+ min — for deep rest
- **Hot yoga**: room heated 95-105°F — increased flexibility, sweat, detox (controversial — heat stress risk)
- **Power yoga**: fitness-focused vinyasa, strength-building, faster pace

## Sun Salutation A (Surya Namaskar)
1. **Mountain** (Tadasana): stand tall, feet together, arms at sides, weight balanced
2. **Upward Salute** (Urdhva Hastasana): inhale, sweep arms overhead, palms together, slight backbend
3. **Forward Fold** (Uttanasana): exhale, hinge at hips, fold forward, knees slightly bent, hands to floor
4. **Halfway Lift** (Ardha Uttanasana): inhale, flat back, fingertips or shins, gaze forward
5. **Plank**: exhale, step/jump back, straight line from head to heels, engage core
6. **Chaturanga** (Four-Limbed Staff): lower halfway, elbows at 90°, body in straight line
7. **Upward Dog** (Urdhva Mukha Svanasana): inhale, press through hands, open chest, tops of feet down
8. **Downward Dog** (Adho Mukha Svanasana): exhale, lift hips back, heels toward floor, head between arms
9. **Step/jump forward** between hands (inhale), halfway lift
10. Forward fold → inhale sweep up → exhale hands to heart

## Essential Poses
- **Downward Dog**: hands shoulder-width, feet hip-width, hips up and back, spine long, heels reaching down — grounding, strengthening, whole body stretch
  - Press firmly through palms, rotate inner arms forward, keep shoulders away from ears
  - Walk dog (bend one knee, then other) to loosen calves/hamstrings
- **Warrior I** (Virabhadrasana I): front knee bent 90°, back foot at 45°, hips squared forward, arms up
- **Warrior II**: front knee bent, back foot parallel to back edge of mat, arms parallel to floor, gaze over front hand — hip opener, leg strengthener
  - Tips: stack front knee over ankle, back leg straight + strong, torso centered over hips
- **Triangle** (Trikonasana): legs wide, front foot forward, reach forward hand to shin/block/floor, other arm up — side stretch, leg strength, balance
- **Tree** (Vrksasana): standing, foot to inner thigh/calf (NOT knee), hands at heart or overhead — balance, focus
  - Find focus point (drishti) that doesn't move. Press foot into thigh AND thigh into foot
- **Child's Pose** (Balasana): knees wide, big toes together, sit back on heels, forehead to mat, arms forward or alongside — rest, release lower back
- **Corpse Pose** (Savasana): lie flat, legs slightly apart, arms at sides palms up, eyes closed — final relaxation (hardest pose: completely relax while awake)

## Breathing (Pranayama)
- **Ujjayi breath** (ocean breath): slight constriction at back of throat, breath sounds like waves — used throughout vinyasa to connect movement to breath
- **Nadi Shodhana** (alternate nostril): close right nostril, inhale left, close left, exhale right, inhale right, close right, exhale left — balances nervous system
- **Kapalabhati** (skull shining breath): short powerful exhales through nose (forceful abdominal contraction), passive inhales — energizing, heat-building
- **Sheetali** (cooling breath): curl tongue like tube, inhale through mouth, exhale through nose — cools body

## Benefits of Regular Practice
- **Flexibility**: increases range of motion — consistent practice > intense occasional stretching
- **Strength**: many poses require holding body weight (plank, chaturanga, arm balances) — builds functional strength
- **Balance**: both physical balance and proprioception — improves with practice, prevents falls as we age
- **Stress reduction**: lowers cortisol, activates parasympathetic nervous system — calms mind
- **Body awareness**: better posture, recognize tension before it becomes pain
- **Pain management**: studies show yoga effective for chronic low back pain (as good as or better than physical therapy)

## Props & Modifications
- **Blocks**: bring floor closer, provide stability (chest openers, standing poses)
- **Straps**: extend reach (seated forward fold with strap around feet), assist in shoulder/hip stretches
- **Blankets**: under hips in seated poses (pelvis higher than knees), under knees in kneeling, warmth in savasana
- **Bolster**: support in restorative/yin poses — can use firm pillows instead
- **Wall**: inversions (legs up wall), standing poses (heel against wall for balance), backbends

## Common Mistakes
- **Locking joints**: micro-bend in elbows/knees to prevent hyperextension
- **Rounding lower back**: hinge from hips (not waist) in forward folds, engage lower abs
- **Shoulders to ears**: consciously relax shoulders down away from ears, broaden collarbones
- **Holding breath**: breathe! If you're holding breath, the pose is too intense — back off
- **Comparing to others**: everyone's body is different — focus on your own experience, not the person next to you
- **Pushing into pain**: sensation vs pain — good sensation is mild stretch, pain = stop or modify
- **Over-rotating standing foot**: in Warrior II, back foot should be about 90° to front foot, not more

## Starting a Home Practice
- 10-15 min minimum (morning or evening) — consistency > duration
- Online resources: Yoga with Adriene (free, beginner-friendly, huge library), Down Dog app (customizable), DoYogaWithMe
- Equipment: yoga mat (sticky, non-slip), comfortable clothing, water, quiet space
- Sequence structure: centering → warm-up → sun salutations → standing poses → seated poses → supine → savasana
- Listen to your body: rest when needed, modify when necessary, avoid poses that cause pain

## Safety
- **Neck**: no weight on neck (shoulder stand use blanket under shoulders, not under neck). No rolling head in neck circles (half circles only)
- **Knees**: don't lock, don't twist while weight-bearing on bent knee, pad knees on hard surfaces
- **Lower back**: in forward folds, hinge from hips, keep slight bend in knees, engage core for support
- **Wrist pain**: distribute weight through entire hand (not just wrist), grip with fingertips, use fists or forearms as alternatives in plank
- **Glaucoma**: avoid inversions (head below heart). High blood pressure: avoid long holds in inversions, avoid kapalabhati. Pregnancy: avoid deep twists, inversions, hot yoga, belly-down poses

## Key Sanskrit Terms
- Asana: seat/posture (poses end in -asana)
- Namaste: "the light in me honors the light in you" — spoken at end of class
- Drishti: focused gaze point (helps balance)
- Bandha: energetic lock (Mula = root/perineum, Uddiyana = lower belly, Jalandhara = throat)
- Vinyasa: breath-synchronized movement
- Chaturanga: four-limbed staff (low push-up)
- Dandasana: staff pose (seated, legs straight)
- Sukhasana: easy cross-legged seat
