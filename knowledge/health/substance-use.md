# Alcohol & Substance Use

## Standard Drink Definitions (US)
- 12 oz beer (5% ABV) = 1 standard drink
- 5 oz wine (12% ABV) = 1 standard drink
- 1.5 oz distilled spirits (40% ABV / 80 proof) = 1 standard drink
- Many drinks exceed standard: craft beers (7-12% ABV), wine pours (6+ oz), cocktails (2-3+ oz liquor)

## Low-Risk Drinking Guidelines
- **US guidelines**: men ≤ 2 drinks/day, women ≤ 1 drink/day — NOT an average (not 14/week for men, then all Friday night)
- **Health risks increase** with ANY alcohol consumption: cancer risk (breast, liver, colon, esophagus), heart damage, brain damage, liver disease
  - 2023 Canada guidelines: 2 drinks/week = low risk, 3-6 = moderate, 7+ = increasingly high risk
  - No amount is completely safe — but 1-2 drinks/week is very low risk for most people
- **Binge drinking**: 4+ drinks in 2 hours (women), 5+ (men) — dangerous, increases accident/injury, overdose, hangover severity

## Hangover
- Causes: dehydration (alcohol is diuretic), inflammation, acetaldehyde (toxic byproduct), disrupted sleep, low blood sugar, stomach irritation, withdrawal
- Prevention: eat before drinking, pace yourself (1 drink/hour max), alternate water, choose lighter drinks (darker drinks = more congeners = worse hangover)
- "Hair of the dog": delays hangover, doesn't cure it — you're just still drunk
- There is NO cure (not even IV fluids — study showed no difference). Time is the only cure
- Help: hydrate (water + electrolytes), eat (complex carbs + protein), sleep, ibuprofen (NOT Tylenol/acetaminophen — toxic to liver after alcohol)

## Recognizing Problematic Use
- CAGE questionnaire: Have you ever felt you should CUT DOWN? Annoyed by criticism? GUILTY about drinking? EYE-OPENER (drink in morning)?
  - 2+ "yes" = likely alcohol use disorder
- Signs: drinking alone, more than intended, blackouts, neglecting responsibilities, continued use despite problems, withdrawal symptoms, tolerance (need more for same effect)
- AUDIT (Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test): 10-question screening tool (online, free, validated)
- About 29M US adults (11%) have alcohol use disorder — very common, treatable

## Quitting or Cutting Back
- **Moderation**: set limits (drink tickets for a party, no drinks on weeknights), alternate with water, track drinks, try low-ABV options
- **Sober curious**: take a break (Dry January, Sober October) — many find they feel better, sleep better, save money
- **Abstinence**: for severe AUD — many find easier to quit completely than moderate
  - AA (Alcoholics Anonymous): 12-step program, free, widely available, spiritual approach. Works for some, not for others
  - SMART Recovery: science-based, non-religious, CBT approach — alternative to AA
  - Medication: naltrexone (reduces cravings), acamprosate, disulfiram (makes you sick if you drink) — effective, underused
- **Withdrawal**: mild (anxiety, sweating, insomnia, tremors) → severe (seizures, delirium tremens — can be fatal)
  - Severe withdrawal requires medical detox (benzodiazepines, monitoring) — don't quit cold turkey if you drink heavily daily

## Cannabis
- Active ingredient: THC (psychoactive, gets you high) and CBD (non-psychoactive, anxiety/pain relief)
- Effects: relaxation, euphoria, altered perception, increased appetite, impaired short-term memory and coordination
  - Onset: inhaled (minutes), edible (30-90 min, more intense, lasts longer). Dosing edibles: "start low, go slow" (2.5-5mg THC, wait 2 hours before more)
- Risks: addiction (~9% of users, ~17% if started as teen), impaired driving (do NOT drive after cannabis), lung damage if smoked, anxiety/paranoia (especially high THC, low CBD strains), CHS (cannabinoid hyperemesis syndrome — cyclic vomiting, hot showers help)
  - Cannabis use disorder: ~30% of daily users meet criteria — withdrawal (irritability, insomnia, anxiety, craving, nausea) is real but not dangerous
- Legal status: varies by state/country — recreational legal in 24 US states + DC, medical in 38+ (2024). Still illegal federally (Schedule I)

## Vaping & Nicotine
- **Vaping epidemic**: especially among teens — 1 in 4 high school seniors vape
  - E-cigarettes are NOT harmless but harm reduction vs smoking (likely 95% less harmful than cigarettes — UK Public Health England)
  - Risks: lung damage (EVALI — from vitamin E acetate in illicit THC vapes), nicotine addiction (very high in Juul/Puff Bar), gateway to smoking for some
  - Quitting: nicotine replacement (patch + gum/lozenge best), prescription (varenicline/Chantix, bupropion/Zyban), therapy, quitline 1-800-QUIT-NOW
- **Smoking**: leading preventable cause of death (480,000/year US, 8 million/year globally)
  - Half of smokers die from smoking-related disease. Life expectancy: 10 years shorter
  - Quitting benefits: 20 min after last cigarette (heart rate drops), 24 hours (CO gone), 1 year (heart disease risk half), 10 years (lung cancer risk half)
  - Most smokers need 6-11 attempts before quitting for good — don't give up

## Other Substances
- **Opioids** (prescription painkillers, heroin, fentanyl): highly addictive, respiratory depression is fatal — leading cause of drug overdose deaths (70%+ of US drug overdose deaths involve fentanyl)
  - Fentanyl: 50x more potent than heroin, responsible for most overdose deaths — often mixed with other drugs (cocaine, counterfeit pills) without user's knowledge
  - Naloxone (Narcan): reverses opioid overdose — nasal spray, available without prescription, everyone should carry it
  - MAT (Medication-Assisted Treatment): methadone, buprenorphine (Suboxone), naltrexone — gold standard for opioid use disorder, reduces overdose risk by 50%+
- **Stimulants** (cocaine, methamphetamine, Adderall): increased heart rate, blood pressure, energy, euphoria followed by crash
  - Cocaine: heart attack/stroke risk even first use, high addiction potential
  - Methamphetamine: severe dental problems (meth mouth), skin picking, psychosis, rapid addiction
- **Benzodiazepines** (Xanax, Valium, Klonopin): prescribed for anxiety, highly addictive, dangerous withdrawal (seizures), especially dangerous mixed with alcohol (respiratory depression)
- **MDMA** (ecstasy, molly): serotonin release → euphoria, empathy, energy. Risks: overheating/dehydration (dance clubs), serotonin syndrome (with certain meds), neurotoxicity debated, often adulterated

## Harm Reduction
- **Test your drugs**: fentanyl test strips (detect fentanyl in other drugs), reagent testing kits (DanceSafe) — legal in most states
- **Don't use alone**: have someone sober present who can call 911, administer Narcan (for opioids)
- **Start low, go slow**: with any new substance, take small dose, wait 2+ hours before more (especially edibles)
- **Know Good Samaritan laws** (all 50 states have some form): you won't be prosecuted for calling 911 about an overdose — saves lives
- **CPR + Narcan**: if someone is unresponsive and breathing abnormally: call 911, give Narcan, rescue breathing (opioids stop breathing before heart stops)
- **Recovery is possible**: millions of people recover from substance use disorders — treatment works, people heal, don't give up hope
