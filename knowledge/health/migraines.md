# Migraines & Headache Disorders

## Headache Types
- **Tension-type**: most common. Bilateral, pressing/tightening (band around head), mild-moderate, no nausea/vomiting. Not aggravated by routine physical activity. Lasts 30 min-7 days. Can be episodic (<15/month) or chronic (≥15/month). Treatment: OTC pain relievers (ibuprofen, naproxen, acetaminophen). Caffeine can help (but overuse → medication overuse headache). Amitriptyline for prevention. NOT associated with aura
- **Migraine**: moderate-severe, pulsating, unilateral (60% of time but can be both sides), aggravated by routine activity (can't walk up stairs). 4-72 hours. Plus: nausea/vomiting (80%), photophobia (light sensitivity), phonophobia (sound sensitivity), osmophobia (smell sensitivity). Aura (20-30%): visual (scintillating scotoma — zigzag lines, blind spots, flashing lights), sensory (tingling moving up arm), speech. Aura typically lasts 15-60 min before headache phase. Prodrome (hours to days before): mood changes, food cravings, neck stiffness, yawning, fatigue, frequent urination. Postdrome (after headache resolves): exhausted, confused, "hangover"
  - Triggers: stress (most common), hormonal (menstrual — estrogen drop), sleep changes (too much/too little), weather (barometric pressure shifts), certain foods (aged cheese, chocolate, red wine, processed meats with nitrates, MSG in some people — BUT evidence weak, triggers vary widely between individuals). Bright lights, strong smells (perfume, smoke, fumes). Fasting or skipping meals. Not everyone has clear triggers. IMPORTANT: medication overuse headache (rebound headache from taking acute meds >10-15 days/month — can turn migraine into chronic daily headache)
  - Abortive treatment: triptans (sumatriptan, rizatriptan, eletriptan) — most effective, prescription. Best taken at FIRST sign (not after headache peaks). Work by constricting cranial blood vessels + blocking pain pathways. Triptans: available as pills, dissolving tablets, nasal spray, injection (sumatriptan injection = fastest). CGRP antagonists/nasals (ubrogepant/Ubrelvy, rimegepant/Nurtec) — newer, fewer side effects (no vasoconstriction, safer for patients with cardiovascular risk). NSAIDs (high dose naproxen 500-550mg, ibuprofen 600-800mg) at onset. Antiemetics (metoclopramide) for nausea. Caffeine (some combination like Excedrin Migraine — acetaminophen + aspirin + caffeine)
  - Preventative: daily meds to reduce frequency/severity. Beta blockers (propranolol). Antidepressants (amitriptyline, venlafaxine). Anticonvulsants (topiramate, valproate). CGRP monoclonal antibodies (Aimovig, Ajovy, Emgality — monthly injection, very effective, expensive, insurance prior authorization). Botox (for chronic migraine — ≥15 headache days/month). 31 injections every 12 weeks (the "PREEMPT protocol" — across 7 head/neck areas). Nutraceuticals: magnesium, riboflavin (B2), CoQ10 — weak evidence but harmless + some patients respond. Avoid triggers + lifestyle: regular sleep + meals + exercise plus hydration
- **Cluster headache**: excruciating, unilateral, orbital/supraorbital/temporal pain (like hot poker in eye). 15-180 min, 1-8 per day (cluster periods lasting weeks to months with remissions). Autonomic symptoms: ipsilateral conjunctival injection (red eye), lacrimation (tearing), nasal congestion, ptosis (drooping eyelid), sweating. Restlessness/agitation during attack (pacing, rocking — unlike migraine where person wants to lie still). More common in men (3x), smokers
  - Abortive: high-flow oxygen (12-15 L/min non-rebreather, often works in 15 min). Triptan injection (sumatriptan 6mg SC) or nasal spray. Dihydroergotamine (DHE). Prevention: verapamil (calcium channel blocker, high dose — up to 480-720mg). Lithium. Prednisone burst. Greater occipital nerve block
- **Medication overuse headache (MOH)**: headache 15+ days/month, caused by taking acute headache meds >10-15 days/month (triptans >10 days, OTC painkillers >15 days, combination analgesics >10 days). Withdrawal = temporary worsening then improvement. Treatment: stop overused medication (abruptly for triptans, taper for opioids/ barbiturates under medical supervision). Bridge therapy (long-acting NSAID or steroid burst). Preventative starts
- **New daily persistent headache (NDPH)**: headache from clear onset ("like a switch flipped"), daily continuous from start for 3+ months. Very difficult to treat (often medication-resistant). Two subtypes: self-limited (resolves within 2 years), refractory (persists forever). Exclude: post-traumatic, low CSF pressure (spontaneous intracranial hypotension)

## When to Seek Urgent Care (Red Flags)
- **SNOOP4**: Systemic symptoms (fever, weight loss, unexplained). Neurologic symptoms (altered consciousness, confusion, loss of sensation — beyond aura). Onset: sudden/severe/thunderclap (worst headache of life, peak within 1 minute — subarachnoid hemorrhage). Older age (>50, new headache). Previous headache history change (pattern change, new type). Postural: worse standing up (low pressure) vs lying down (high pressure). Papilledema: optic nerve swelling (id ICP). Cancer history (new headache + hx cancer = brain metastasis until proven otherwise). Pregnancy/postpartum (cerebral venous sinus thrombosis risk — new severe headache)
- Thunderclap headache: EMERGENCY — think subarachnoid hemorrhage, cerebral venous sinus thrombosis, pituitary apoplexy, reversible cerebral vasoconstriction syndrome, cervical artery dissection. Need CT brain (non-contrast, sensitive for acute blood) + lumbar puncture if CT negative
- Headache + fever + stiff neck → meningitis. Kernig's (+ = can't extend knee 90° when hip flexed 90°) and Brudzinski's signs (passive neck flexion → hip/knee flexion)

## Headache Prevention Lifestyle
- **Consistency**: regular sleep (same wake/bed time every day including weekends). Regular meals (don't skip meals). Regular exercise (moderate, consistent, not extreme). Hydration (dehydration common trigger). Caffeine: limit to 1-2/day, consistent timing, avoid afternoon/evening. Don't oversleep on weekends (sleep changes trigger migraine). Keep headache diary: track triggers, patterns, frequency, severity, meds + response
- **Supplements**: magnesium (400-600mg glycinate — gentle on stomach, best absorbed). Riboflavin (Vitamin B2, 400mg). CoQ10 (300mg). Melatonin (3mg at night — for prevention). Evidence: all GRADE B ("probably effective")
- **Biofeedback + relaxation**: temperature biofeedback (hand warming training) — strong evidence for migraine prevention. CBT (stress management, catastrophic thinking reduction). Progressive muscle relaxation. Mindfulness meditation (reduces stress → may reduce headache)
