# Addictions & Recovery

## What is Addiction
- **Characteristics**: compulsive substance use or behavior despite negative consequences, loss of control, craving, continued use despite harm
  - Not a moral failing — it's a chronic brain disease (brain changes in reward, motivation, memory circuits)
  - American Medical Association classified alcoholism as disease in 1956. WHO and NIH recognize addiction as chronic brain disorder
- **Disease model**: addiction changes brain structure/function long after substance use stops — the "switch" flips from voluntary use to compulsive use
  - Dopamine pathways hijacked — natural rewards (food, sex, social) no longer register as rewarding
  - Prefrontal cortex (impulse control) weakened — limbic system (craving) strengthened
- **Risk factors**: genetics (40-60% heritability), trauma/ACE (adverse childhood experiences), mental health conditions (dual diagnosis), environment (stress, exposure, peer pressure), age of first use (earlier = higher risk)
  - Not everyone who uses becomes addicted — some brains more vulnerable
- **Addiction vs habit**: habit = behavior done automatically, still has control. Addiction = compulsive, loss of control, continued despite harm, withdrawal. Habits don't cause withdrawal

## Types of Addiction
- **Substance**: alcohol, opioids (heroin, fentanyl, prescription painkillers), stimulants (cocaine, meth, Adderall), cannabis, benzodiazepines (Xanax, Valium), nicotine, caffeine
- **Process/behavioral**: gambling (only behavioral addiction in DSM-5), gaming (gaming disorder in ICD-11), internet/social media, shopping, sex/love, food (binge eating), exercise (can be excessive — harms health/relationships)
  - Behavioral addictions activate same brain pathways as substances
  - Social media: designed to be addictive (variable rewards, infinite scroll, notifications) — by intention (persuasive design)

## Alcohol Use Disorder (AUD)
- **Symptoms**: drink more/bigger than intended, can't cut down, spend time drinking/recovering, craving, neglect roles, continue despite problems, give up activities, use in dangerous situations, tolerance, withdrawal
  - DSM-5: mild (2-3 symptoms), moderate (4-5), severe (6+)
- **Withdrawal**: starts 6-12 hours after last drink — anxiety, insomnia, sweating, shaking, nausea, heart racing
  - Severe: seizures, hallucinations, delirium tremens (DTs — confusion, fever, hallucinations, can be fatal). DT's medical emergency — 5-15% mortality without treatment
  - Medical detox: benzodiazepines (Librium, Ativan), fluids, monitoring, vitamins (thiamine to prevent Wernicke-Korsakoff syndrome/brain damage)
- **Treatment**: CBT (identify triggers, change thinking), motivational enhancement (build motivation to change), 12-step (AA), medication (naltrexone reduces cravings, acamprosate reduces withdrawal discomfort, disulfiram causes sickness if drinking)

## Gambling Addiction
- **Symptoms**: preoccupied, need to bet more, unsuccessful attempts to stop, restless/irritable when trying to cut down, chase losses, lie to hide, risk relationships/job, rely on others for money
  - Only behavioral addiction classified alongside substance addictions in DSM-5
  - Most dangerous: chasing losses ("I'll win it back") — leads to more gambling, more losses
- **Self-help**: self-exclusion from casinos/online gambling, limit cash/cards, give control of finances to trusted person, Gamblers Anonymous (12-step), Gam-Anon (family support)
  - Install Gamban/BetBlocker (block gambling sites on devices). Self-exclude from all gambling venues (national program available)

## Gaming & Screen Addiction
- **Signs**: loss of interest in other activities, can't reduce play time despite wanting to, lying about time spent, using gaming to escape negative moods, neglecting sleep/meals/hygiene, withdrawal (irritability, restlessness when can't play), continued play despite harm to grades/job/relationships
- **Screen time**: social media uses variable rewards (like slot machines) — notifications, likes, infinite scroll
  - Dark patterns: designed to keep you on platform as long as possible
  - Dopamine loops: check → reward (like/comment) → check again → small intermittent reward
- **Digital detox**: scheduled screen-free time, grayscale phone display (reduces appeal), delete social media apps (use browser only), no phone in bedroom at night, app timers (use them, ignore override urge), one screen at a time (no TV + phone)
  - Phone: remove all social media and news apps. Keep: messaging (WhatsApp/iMessage), maps, camera, calendar. Browser works fine for everything else (less convenient = less use)

## Recovery Principles
- **Abstinence vs harm reduction**: abstinence (complete cessation) works for some, harm reduction (reduce use/minimize harm) works for others
  - Abstinence: AA/NA (12-step), SMART Recovery, residential treatment
  - Harm reduction: controlled drinking (moderation management), methadone/buprenorphine for opioids, safe injection sites, needle exchange, naloxone distribution
- **Stages of change** (Prochaska & DiClemente):
  1. Pre-contemplation: not seeing problem, not considering change
  2. Contemplation: aware of problem, ambivalent, thinking about change (within 6 months)
  3. Preparation: planning to change soon, small steps (within 30 days)
  4. Action: actively changing behavior (<6 months sober)
  5. Maintenance: sustained change (6+ months)
  6. Relapse: common (normal part of change process — return to contemplation/action)
  - Relapse doesn't mean failure — it's part of the process. Most people cycle through stages multiple times before sustained recovery
- **Triggers**: people, places, things associated with use — identify and plan for them
  - HALT: Hungry, Angry, Lonely, Tired — high-risk states for relapse
  - CBT: identify automatic thoughts that lead to use, challenge them, develop alternative behaviors

## Support Options
- **12-Step** (AA, NA, Al-Anon): free, everywhere, spiritual, peer support, sponsorship — works for many, not for everyone (rates similar to other treatments)
  - Criticisms: religious overtones (uses "higher power"), abstinence-only, disease model not universal
- **SMART Recovery**: science-based, no spiritual component, CBT approach, self-empowerment — online meetings, secular
- **Therapy**: CBT (most evidence for addiction), motivational interviewing (builds motivation), contingency management (tangible rewards for abstinence — strong evidence), DBT (emotion regulation)
- **Medication**: naltrexone (alcohol/opioids), methadone/buprenorphine/naltrexone (opioids), acamprosate (alcohol), varenicline/Chantix (nicotine), bupropion (nicotine)
  - MAT (Medication-Assisted Treatment) is gold standard for opioid use disorder — reduces overdose mortality 50%+
  - Methadone: daily clinic visits, tightly regulated. Buprenorphine/Suboxone: prescribed by doctor/home take, lower overdose risk
- **Rehab**: residential treatment (28-90 days), intensive outpatient (IOP, 3-5 sessions/week), partial hospitalization (PHP, daily)
  - Inpatient rehab for: severe addiction, dangerous withdrawal, co-occurring mental health conditions, unstable living situation, previous failed outpatient attempts

## Supporting Someone in Recovery
- **DON'T**: enable (give money, make excuses, lie for them, take over their responsibilities). Enabling = protecting from natural consequences, preventing them from hitting bottom
  - "I'm just helping" vs "I'm enabling" test: am I making it easier for them to continue using?
- **DO**: encourage treatment, set boundaries, attend Al-Anon/Nar-Anon (family support), be patient (relapse is common), celebrate milestones (24 hours, 30 days, 1 year), remove stigma (it's a health condition)
- **Intervention**: structured conversation with professional interventionist (rarely works as shown on TV — actually can damage relationships). Family-based interventions with professional guidance can be effective
- **CRAFT** (Community Reinforcement and Family Training): evidence-based approach for families — positive reinforcement when sober, natural consequences when using, encourage treatment entry (more effective than confrontational interventions)

## Maintaining Recovery
- **Build new routines**: replace using time/activities with healthy ones (exercise, hobbies, social connection)
  - Boredom is a major relapse trigger — recovery requires building a life you want to be present for
- **Support system**: sober friends, meetings, sponsor/therapist, family. Isolation = high risk
- **Exercise**: reduces depression, anxiety, cravings; improves sleep, self-esteem, brain recovery
  - Studies: exercise helps repair dopamine systems damaged by addiction
- **Sleep**: addiction disrupts sleep — prioritize sleep hygiene (regular schedule, no screens, cool room)
- **Nutrition**: substance use depletes nutrients — balanced diet supports brain recovery (B vitamins, omega-3s, amino acids)
- **Mindfulness**: meditation helps with craving (ride the wave — cravings last 5-20 min, pass like a wave, don't act on them)
- **Service**: helping others in recovery (sponsorship, meeting service) strengthens your own recovery
