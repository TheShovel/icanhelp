# Dental & Vision Care

## Dental Hygiene
- Brush 2x/day, 2 min each time, soft-bristled toothbrush
- Technique: 45° angle at gum line, small circular motions, all surfaces
- Electric toothbrush: more effective than manual for most people (pressure sensor, timer)
- Replace toothbrush / electric head every 3-4 months (sooner if bristles frayed)
- **Floss daily**: C-shape around each tooth, go below gum line — brushing alone misses 35% of tooth surfaces
  - Floss picks / interdental brushes: better than not flossing at all, slightly less effective than string floss
  - Water flosser: good for braces, implants, gum pockets
- **Tongue scraping**: removes bacteria that cause bad breath
- Mouthwash: therapeutic (with fluoride, anti-gingivitis) > cosmetic (just freshens breath)
- Don't rinse mouth immediately after brushing (wait 30 min or just spit excess) — fluoride needs contact time

## Common Dental Problems
- **Cavities (caries)**: tooth decay from acid produced by bacteria feeding on sugar
  - Prevention: fluoride (toothpaste, tap water, treatments), limit sugar frequency (not just amount), sealants
  - Small cavities: filling. Larger: crown. Deep: root canal or extraction
- **Gingivitis**: red, swollen, bleeding gums — reversible with better brushing/flossing
- **Periodontitis**: gum disease with bone loss — irreversible, leads to tooth loss if untreated
  - Risk: smoking, diabetes, genetics, poor oral hygiene
  - Treatment: deep cleaning (scaling + root planing), surgery in advanced cases
- **Tooth sensitivity**: pain from hot/cold/sweet/sour — exposed dentin from gum recession or enamel erosion
  - Treatment: sensitive toothpaste (potassium nitrate), fluoride varnish, bonding
  - Causes: aggressive brushing, acidic diet, teeth grinding, gum disease
- **Teeth grinding (bruxism)**: wear at night, jaw pain, headaches, worn teeth
  - Nightguard (custom from dentist > OTC boil-and-bite)
  - Stress management, avoid caffeine/alcohol before bed
- **Bad breath (halitosis)**: usually from bacteria on tongue or gum disease — mouthwash is temporary fix
  - Brush tongue, floss, stay hydrated, see dentist
  - If persistent despite good hygiene: may indicate sinus infection, GERD, tonsil stones
- **Wisdom teeth**: usually come in 17-25, often impacted (no room) → extraction
  - Symptoms: pain, swollen gums, crowding, infection (pericoronitis)

## Dental Visits
- Every 6 months for checkup + cleaning (some people need every 3-4 months for periodontal maintenance)
- X-rays: typically once/year (bitewings to check between teeth), full mouth every 3-5 years
- Dental anxiety: tell dentist, use numbing gel, nitrous oxide (laughing gas), sedation options available
- Emergency: knocked-out tooth — hold by crown (not root), rinse gently, put back in socket if possible or store in milk/saline, see dentist within 1 hour

## Vision Care
- **20-20-20 rule**: every 20 min, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds — reduces digital eye strain
- **Eye exam**: every 2 years (ages 18-60), annually if >60, have glasses, diabetes, or family history of eye disease
- **Digital eye strain**: dry eyes, headache, blurred vision — blink consciously, adjust screen (arm's length, slightly below eye level), use artificial tears, consider blue-light glasses (limited evidence but some find them helpful)
- Staring at screens reduces blink rate by 60% (normal 15-20 blinks/min → 5-7)
- **Blue light**: screens emit blue light — disrupts sleep if used before bed (use night mode/warm filter after sunset)
- **UV protection**: sunglasses block 99-100% UVA+UVB — UV damage accumulates, causes cataracts, macular degeneration
- **Contact lenses**: wash hands before handling, never sleep in non-extended wear lenses (risk corneal ulcer), replace case monthly, don't swim/shower in them
  - Acanthamoeba: rare but serious eye infection from water — don't rinse lenses in tap water
- **LASIK/PRK**: vision correction surgery — candidates need stable prescription, healthy corneas, no dry eye
  - Most achieve 20/20 or better. Side effects: dry eyes, glare, halos (usually temporary)
- **Presbyopia**: age-related near vision loss (around 40-50) — need reading glasses or multifocal lenses
- **Floaters**: common, usually harmless — see doctor if sudden increase + flashes (could be retinal detachment)

## Common Vision Problems
- **Nearsighted (myopia)**: can't see far — nearsightedness is increasing worldwide (50% of young adults in US/EU)
  - Slowed by: time outdoors (2+ hrs/day in childhood), low-dose atropine, specialized contact lenses
- **Farsighted (hyperopia)**: can't see close clearly
- **Astigmatism**: cornea is irregularly shaped (like football not basketball) — blurred at all distances
- **Cataracts**: lens becomes cloudy — common with age (50+), surgery is safe and effective
  - Signs: cloudy/foggy vision, colors faded, glare/ halos at night, frequent prescription changes
- **Glaucoma**: increased eye pressure damages optic nerve — no early symptoms, leading cause of preventable blindness
  - Routine eye exams catch it early. Treatment: eye drops (reduce pressure), laser, surgery
  - Risk factors: age 60+, family history, African heritage, diabetes
- **Macular degeneration**: central vision loss (age-related, dry type more common, wet type more severe)
  - Amsler grid test at home (wavy lines = see doctor)
  - AREDS2 vitamins (specific formula) may slow dry AMD progression
  - Risk: smoking (major factor), UV exposure, diet low in leafy greens
- **Diabetic retinopathy**: diabetes damages blood vessels in retina — leading cause of blindness in working-age adults
  - Prevention: blood sugar control, annual dilated eye exam
