# Autoimmune & Chronic Illness

## What is Autoimmunity
- **Autoimmune disease**: immune system mistakenly attacks body's own cells/tissues — over 80 recognized autoimmune diseases, affecting ~10% of population (24M Americans). Women affected 75% more than men (X chromosome + hormones + environmental factors)
  - "The body attacking itself" — immune system can't distinguish self from non-self. Loss of self-tolerance (normally T cells that react to self are eliminated in thymus — breakdown = autoreactive T cells escape)
  - Rising incidence: autoimmune increasing 3-9% per year — suggesting environmental triggers (hygiene hypothesis: fewer infections = under-trained immune system = overreacts. Also diet, chemicals, antibiotics, microbiome changes)
- **Commonality**: many more people have autoantibodies (immune system attacking self) than have active disease — presence of autoantibody may precede clinical disease by years (early detection may allow prevention)

## Common Autoimmune Diseases

### Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
- **What**: immune system attacks synovium (lining of joints) — inflammation, pain, swelling, stiffness, bone erosion, deformity. Symmetrical (both sides usually same joints)
  - Morning stiffness >30 min (distinguishes from osteoarthritis, which stiffens <30 min). Affects hands, wrists, feet, knees
- **Diagnosis**: blood tests: rheumatoid factor (RF), anti-CCP (more specific for RA, often present years before symptoms), CRP + ESR (inflammation markers). Joint X-ray/ultrasound/MRI
- **Treatment**: DMARDs (disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs — methotrexate most common first-line). Biologics (TNF inhibitors: Humira, Enbrel; newer: JAK inhibitors: Xeljanz, Rinvoq). Steroids for flares (prednisone short-term). Physical therapy, joint protection
  - Early aggressive treatment = better outcomes. Goal: remission or low disease activity. Methotrexate + folic acid for side effect: oral ulcers. Most need both MTX + biologic

### Lupus (Systemic Lupus Erythematosus - SLE)
- **What**: multi-organ autoimmune — skin, joints, kidneys, brain, heart, lungs. "The great imitator" because symptoms mimic many other diseases
  - Butterfly rash (malar rash across cheeks + nose — triggered by sun). Photosensitivity, mouth/nose ulcers, arthritis, serositis (pleurisy = chest pain), kidney inflammation (lupus nephritis — most serious complication, can lead to kidney failure), neurological (seizures, psychosis)
- **Diagnosis**: ANA (antinuclear antibodies — 95% of SLE patients have positive ANA. But: many healthy people also have low positive ANA — not diagnostic alone). Anti-dsDNA, anti-Sm (more specific for lupus), complement C3/C4 low, urinalysis, kidney biopsy if suspected nephritis
- **Treatment**: hydroxychloroquine/Plaquenil (ALL lupus patients — reduces flares, improves survival, protective for kidneys). Immunosuppressants for organ involvement (mycophenolate/CellCept, cyclophosphamide). Biologics (Benlysta/belimumab — for active SLE, reduced flare rate). Steroids short-term for flares
  - Sun protection is critical: UV triggers flares. SPF 50+ daily, hats, sun-protective clothing

### Multiple Sclerosis (MS)
- **What**: immune attacks myelin sheath of nerves in brain + spinal cord → scarring (sclerosis), disrupting nerve signals. "Demyelinating disease" — symptoms depend on which part of CNS affected
  - Types: Relapsing-Remitting (RRMS, 85% — attacks then partial/full recovery. Most common starting type). Secondary Progressive (SPMS — after 10-20 years, RRMS becomes progressive). Primary Progressive (PPMS — gradual worsening from start, no attacks)
- **Symptoms**: optic neuritis (vision loss, pain with eye movement — common first symptom), numbness/tingling, weakness, fatigue (most disabling symptom for many), balance problems (ataxia), bladder/bowel dysfunction, cognitive changes (brain fog, memory, processing speed, "cog fog")
- **Diagnosis**: MRI (brain + spine — lesions/plaques). Lumbar puncture (oligoclonal bands in CSF). Evoked potentials (measure nerve signal speed). Rule out mimics
- **Treatment**: disease-modifying therapies (DMTs — injectable: interferon, Copaxone; oral: Tecfidera, Gilenya, Aubagio; infusion: Tysabri, Ocrevus, Lemtrada). Steroids for acute attacks. Symptom management (fatigue: modafinil, Amantadine; spasticity: baclofen; pain). Physical therapy
  - Early DMT → less disability long-term. More aggressive DMT earlier for active disease = better outcomes

### Type 1 Diabetes
- **What**: immune destroys insulin-producing beta cells in pancreas. Without insulin → body can't use glucose → high blood sugar. NOT related to lifestyle/obesity (distinct from Type 2)
- **Diagnosis**: sudden onset (often in childhood/young adult — can occur at any age. Late onset: LADA/Latent Autoimmune Diabetes of Adults, sometimes misdiagnosed as Type 2 initially). High blood sugar, autoantibodies (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, insulin), low C-peptide (body not producing insulin)
- **Treatment**: insulin (multiple daily injections: long-acting/basal once a day + rapid-acting/bolus with meals. Or insulin pump worn at all times — auto-delivery from patch/tube). Continuous glucose monitor (CGM — Dexcom, Freestyle Libre. Greatly improves control. Eliminates finger sticks for many). Automated insulin delivery systems. Blood glucose checks (finger sticks × 4-10/day, or CGM reduces them severely). Diet: carb counting, insulin:dose matching
  - Hypoglycemia (low blood sugar, insulin too much for available sugar): can cause unconsciousness, death. Treat rapidly: 15g fast-acting glucose (juice, glucose tabs, candies), check again in 15
  - Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA, high blood sugar + no insulin, body burns fat = acidic ketones = medical emergency — vomiting + high blood sugar + deep/labored Kussmaul breathing)

### Hashimoto's Thyroiditis
- **What**: immune attacks thyroid gland → hypothyroidism (underactive thyroid). Most common autoimmune disease (affects 5% of population, 10x more women than men)
  - Symptoms: fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, dry skin/hair, constipation, depression, memory problems, muscle aches
- **Diagnosis**: TSH (elevated), free T4 (low), TPO antibodies (thyroid peroxidase — present in 90% of Hashimoto's)
- **Treatment**: levothyroxine (T4, synthetic thyroxine — replace missing hormone). Dose adjusted by TSH levels (2-4 times/yr. Brand name Synthroid; generic levothyroxine often works, but some feel better on specific brand. Need consistent brand if switching pharmacies). Monitor: too high (hyperthyroid symptoms = anxiety, weight loss, palpitations) vs too low (hypothyroid)
  - Hashimoto's may have periodic normal episodes. But TSH trend rises over years

### Celiac Disease
- **What**: immune reaction to gluten (protein in wheat, barley, rye) → damages small intestine (villous atrophy — villi flatten → reduce nutrient absorption). NOT an allergy — autoimmune (gluten + genetics + environment trigger). 1 in 100 people have celiac (60-70% undiagnosed)
- **Symptoms**: GI (diarrhea, bloating, abdominal pain, weight loss). Non-GI (fatigue (most common), anemia, osteoporosis, infertility, rash (dermatitis herpetiformis — intensely itchy bumps on elbows, knees, butt. Not hives — specific rash of celiac), neurological (ataxia, neuropathy), brain fog). Asymptomatic celiac also common — damage to intestines occurs silently
- **Diagnosis**: blood test (tTG-IgA is best initial screening). Upper endoscopy with small bowel biopsy (gold standard — confirms villous atrophy). Genetic test (HLA-DQ2/DQ8 — 95% of celiacs carry these. Good for ruling out if negative; positive not diagnostic — 30-40% of general population carries either gene) — Must be eating gluten for accurate testing
- **Treatment**: strict lifelong gluten-free diet — wheat, barley, rye, and any derivative. NO amount of gluten is safe — even crumbs matter. Hidden sources: soy sauce, beer, oats (unless certified gluten-free due to cross-contamination), some medications, lip balms, communion wafers. Non-responsive celiac: check hidden gluten, consider refractory celiac (1-2% — needs immunomodulation)
  - Gluten-free labeling in US: FDA has standard for "gluten-free" (<20 ppm). Certified gluten-free (GFCO): <10 ppm. Oats: purity protocol oats avoid wheat cross-contamination

## Invisible Illness & Chronic Pain
- **Invisible illness**: chronic conditions with no visible signs (chronic fatigue, fibromyalgia, autoimmune, Ehlers-Danlos/hypermobility/long COVID). Patients often disbelieved, accused of "faking" or "just tired"
  - Spoon theory (Christine Miserandino): each person has spoons = limited energy per day. Every task costs spoons. Chronic illness = fewer spoons total. Once you run out, you can't do more
- **Chronic pain**: pain lasting >3 months, beyond normal healing time. Causes: arthritis, fibromyalgia, nerve damage, autoimmune, EDS. Changes nervous system (central sensitization — pain persists after original injury healed, nervous system becomes hypersensitive)
  - Treatment: multimodal (pain psychology — CBT/ACT, physical therapy, medication [gabapentin/pregabalin for nerve pain, tricyclic antidepressants for sleep+pain, SNRIs for fibromyalgia?], sleep hygiene, stress reduction, graded exercise). Opioids for chronic pain: limited efficacy, high risk — not first line
  - Pacing: balance activity + rest — don't do too much on good days → crash. Graded exposure: slowly build activity tolerance without triggering flare. Use energy conservation: stop before pain forces you to

## Living with Autoimmune
- **Flare management**: know triggers (stress, infection, poor sleep, overexertion, certain foods, sunlight, weather changes). Have action plan: rest + medications + stress reduction + avoid triggers + contact doctor if symptoms severe
  - Identify signs of flare vs. illness vs. infection (which can be misinterpreted as flare symptoms)
- **Medication adherence**: most DMARDs/ biologics are immunosuppressants — increase infection risk. Vaccinations (flu, pneumonia, shingles recommended; avoid live vaccines on biologics). Regular blood monitoring (liver, kidney, blood counts)
- **Fatigue management**: MOST common symptom across autoimmune diseases not IBS or skin issues. Pace yourself — prioritize, delegate, rest. Energy conservation strategies. Medications: sometimes low-dose stimulants, wake-promoting agents (modafinil). Sleep is critical:
   - good sleep hygiene, treat sleep apnea if comorbid, evaluate for depression/sleep disorders. Graded exercise therapy with caution — start low, go slow

## The Gut Connection
- **Gut microbiome**: emerging evidence links gut bacteria to autoimmune (molecular mimicry — bacteria may resemble body proteins, cross-reactive T cells = autoimmunity)
  - Probiotics: mixed evidence for prevention or treatment. Generally: Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium strains safe, may help some symptoms (IBS, inflammation). Not curative
  - Dietary approaches: anti-inflammatory (Mediterranean diet — Omega-3s, vegetables, whole grains, olive oil). Elimination diets (e.g., autoimmune protocol AIP — eliminates grains, dairy, eggs, legumes, nightshades, nuts, seeds — for a short period (6-8 weeks) in some people to identify triggers). Gluten-free may help even without celiac for some (non-celiac gluten sensitivity or even other autoimmune conditions benefit from diet changes)
