# Common Illnesses & When to See a Doctor

## Respiratory Infections
- **Common cold**: runny nose, sore throat, cough, sneezing, mild fever — 7-10 days, no antibiotics
  - Treatment: rest, fluids, saline spray, honey (for cough, >1 year old), zinc lozenges (may reduce duration)
  - Contagious: 1-2 days before symptoms, 5-7 days after
- **Flu (influenza)**: sudden onset, high fever (100-104°F), body aches, headache, fatigue, dry cough
  - Antivirals (Tamiflu) if started within 48 hours — reduces severity/duration
  - Annual vaccine reduces risk 40-60%, also reduces severity if you get it
  - Emergency signs: difficulty breathing, chest pain, confusion, severe dehydration
- **COVID-19**: fever, cough, shortness of breath, loss of taste/smell, fatigue, sore throat
  - Test to confirm, isolate 5+ days, antivirals (Paxlovid) if high risk (65+, immunocompromised, chronic conditions)
- **Bronchitis**: cough with mucus (clear → yellow/green), chest congestion, wheezing — usually viral
  - Symptoms last 2-3 weeks. Antibiotics only if bacterial (rare)
- **Pneumonia**: fever, chills, cough with colored mucus, shortness of breath, chest pain when breathing deep
  - Can be bacterial (needs antibiotics) or viral — see doctor if suspected
  - Vaccines: pneumococcal (recommended 65+, and younger with certain conditions)

## Digestive Issues
- **Food poisoning**: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, stomach cramps — 1-24 hours after eating contaminated food
  - Most resolves in 1-3 days. Hydration is key (sips water, electrolyte solutions)
  - See doctor: bloody diarrhea, fever >102°F, can't keep liquids down >24 hours, severe pain
- **Stomach flu (gastroenteritis)**: vomiting, diarrhea, fever, stomach pain — viral, 1-3 days
  - BRAT diet (bananas, rice, applesauce, toast) after vomiting stops
  - Avoid: dairy, fatty/spicy food, caffeine until recovered
- **GERD/Acid reflux**: heartburn, regurgitation, chest discomfort after eating
  - Triggers: spicy/fatty food, caffeine, alcohol, chocolate, eating late, lying down after meals
  - Treatment: antacids (Tums), H2 blockers (Pepcid), PPIs (Prilosec, Nexium)
  - If frequent (>2/week) or severe: see doctor for long-term management
- **IBS**: abdominal pain, bloating, diarrhea and/or constipation — no structural damage found
  - Triggers vary: FODMAPs (fermentable carbs), stress, hormones, certain foods
  - Treatment: low FODMAP diet (elimination + reintroduction), fiber, peppermint oil, stress management
- **Constipation**: <3 bowel movements/week, hard dry stools, straining
  - Increase fiber (25-35g/day), water (2L+), exercise. OTC: Miralax (gentle), senna (stimulant)
  - Docusate (stool softener) is minimally effective. Chronic: see doctor

## Skin Issues
- **Eczema**: dry, itchy, red, scaly patches — often behind knees, inside elbows, on hands
  - Moisturize (thick cream > lotion), avoid harsh soaps, cotton clothing
  - Flare treatment: OTC hydrocortisone, prescription steroids, calcineurin inhibitors
- **Acne**: clogged pores (whiteheads, blackheads, pimples, cysts)
  - Mild: OTC benzoyl peroxide or salicylic acid. Moderate: adapalene (Differin, OTC)
  - Severe/cystic: see dermatologist — prescription topicals, oral antibiotics, spironolactone, isotretinoin (Accutane)
  - Don't pick/ pop — causes scarring, spreads bacteria
- **Athlete's foot**: itchy, burning, peeling between toes — fungal, treat with OTC terbinafine (Lamisil) for 1-4 weeks
  - Prevention: dry between toes after shower, don't share towels, wear flip-flops in gym showers
- **Ringworm**: circular red rash with raised border — fungal, same treatment as athlete's foot
- **Cellulitis**: red, warm, swollen, tender area of skin (usually leg) — bacterial infection, NEEDS antibiotics (oral or IV)
  - Emergency if: spreading rapidly, fever, red streaks

## Ear, Nose & Throat
- **Ear infection**: ear pain (worse lying down), fever, trouble hearing — common in children
  - Often viral and resolves on its own. Watch and wait 48-72 hours unless severe
  - Antibiotics if: severe, both ears infected (children), under 6 months, not improving
- **Strep throat**: severe sore throat (sudden), fever >101°F, swollen lymph nodes, no cough
  - TEST needed (rapid strep or culture) — treated with antibiotics (prevent rheumatic fever)
  - Contagious until 24 hours on antibiotics
- **Sinusitis**: facial pressure/pain, nasal congestion, green/yellow discharge, headache, tooth pain
  - Most is viral (10-14 days). Treatment: saline rinse (Neti pot), steam, decongestant (short term)
  - See doctor if: >10 days, severe facial pain, fever, or "double worsening" (getting better then worse again)
- **Tonsillitis**: sore throat, red/swollen tonsils (may have white spots), fever, trouble swallowing
  - Viral vs bacterial (strep test). Recurrent (7+ episodes/year) → consider tonsillectomy

## When to Go to ER
- Chest pain or pressure (especially with shortness of breath, nausea, jaw/arm pain)
- Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath not improving
- Sudden severe headache (worst of your life)
- Sudden vision changes, slurred speech, weakness on one side (stroke signs)
- Severe abdominal pain (rigid, tender, can't move)
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Head injury with loss of consciousness, confusion, or vomiting
- Seizure (first one, or >5 minutes)
- Severe allergic reaction (trouble breathing, swelling face/throat)
- Suicidal thoughts or self-harm (or go to ER / call 988)
- Broken bone with deformity or bone sticking out

## When to See Primary Care
- Fever >103°F not improving with meds, or >100.4°F in infant under 3 months
- Unexplained weight loss (5%+ in 6 months)
- New lump or bump (especially breast, testicle, neck)
- Persistent cough (>3 weeks)
- Changes in bowel or bladder habits (blood, pain, frequency)
- Fatigue that interferes with daily life for >2 weeks
- Rash that's spreading, painful, or not improving
- Wound that's red, warm, swollen, or not healing
- Persistent heartburn, headaches, or joint pain
- Mental health concerns (depression, anxiety that's affecting daily life)
