# PTSD & Trauma

Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder and trauma-related conditions.

## Criteria (DSM-5)
- **Exposure**: Direct experience, witnessing, learning about trauma to close family/friend, or repeated exposure to aversive details (first responders)
- **Intrusion symptoms**: Involuntary, distressing memories, nightmares, flashbacks (feeling/acting as if event recurring), psychological distress at reminders, physiological reactions
- **Avoidance**: Avoidance of thoughts/feelings associated with trauma, avoidance of external reminders (people, places, situations)
- **Negative alterations in mood/cognition**: Inability to recall key aspects, exaggerated negative beliefs ("world is dangerous", "I'm bad"), distorted blame, persistent negative emotions, detachment, loss of interest, inability to feel positive emotions
- **Arousal/reactivity changes**: Irritability/anger outbursts, reckless behavior, hypervigilance, exaggerated startle response, concentration problems, sleep disturbance
- **Duration**: Symptoms >1 month; impairment in functioning

## Complex PTSD (C-PTSD)
- Additional symptoms beyond PTSD: affect dysregulation (difficulty calming down), negative self-concept (worthlessness, shame), interpersonal difficulties (distrust, difficulty maintaining relationships)
- Results from prolonged, repeated trauma (childhood abuse, domestic violence, human trafficking, political imprisonment)
- C-PTSD is in ICD-11 but not DSM-5 (which uses PTSD + "dissociative subtype" specifier)

## Treatment Approaches
- **Prolonged Exposure (PE)**: Imaginal exposure (recounting trauma in detail during session) + in vivo exposure (approaching feared but safe situations) — reduces avoidance
- **Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)**: Identify and challenge stuck points — "I could have prevented it", "Others can't be trusted", "It's my fault"
- **EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)**: Bilateral stimulation (eye movements, taps, tones) while recalling trauma — controversial mechanism but supported by research
- **Trauma-Focused CBT**: For children/adolescents — includes child + parent, gradual exposure, parenting skills
- **Somatic Experiencing**: Focus on body sensations, releasing trapped fight/flight/freeze energy — less structured, growing evidence
- **Medication**: SSRIs (sertraline/Zoloft, paroxetine/Paxil) — FDA-approved for PTSD; Prazosin for nightmares

## Polyvagal Theory (Stephen Porges)
- **Ventral vagal** (social engagement): Calm, connected, present — "safe" state
- **Sympathetic** (fight/flight): Activated, mobilized — danger response
- **Dorsal vagal** (freeze): Collapse, dissociation, numbness — extreme threat response
- Trauma treatment aims to increase ventral vagal capacity and flexibility between states

## Dissociation
- **Derealization**: Feeling world is unreal, dreamlike, foggy
- **Depersonalization**: Feeling detached from one's own mind/body — watching from outside
- **Dissociative amnesia**: Unable to recall important trauma information
- **Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID)**: Distinct identity states — severe, chronic childhood trauma
- Grounding techniques: 5-4-3-2-1 (name 5 things you see, 4 you feel, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste)

## Support for Loved Ones
- **Don't pressure**: Let trauma survivor control pace of disclosure, treatment decisions
- **Validate feelings**: "That sounds really hard" not "Just think positive"
- **Avoid triggers**: Ask if certain topics/places should be avoided or approached gradually
- **Support without fixing**: Listening is more helpful than giving advice or solving problems
- **Take threats seriously**: Any mention of suicide should be evaluated — ask directly "Are you thinking about killing yourself?"
- **Vicarious trauma**: Caregivers can develop secondary trauma — practice self-care, consider own therapy

## Resources
- **Crisis lines**: 988 (US Suicide & Crisis Lifeline), Crisis Text Line (text HOME to 741741)
- **VA**: Veterans Crisis Line (1-800-273-8255, press 1), VA PTSD resources for combat-related trauma
- **Support groups**: NAMI, Sidran Institute, local trauma survivor groups
- **Apps**: PTSD Coach (VA), Breathe2Relax, CBT-I Coach (insomnia common in PTSD)
