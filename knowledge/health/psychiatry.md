# Psychiatry & Mental Health Treatment

## Diagnostic Categories (DSM-5)

### Anxiety Disorders
- **Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD)**: excessive worry about multiple things (work, health, family, daily routines) more days than not for ≥6 months. Difficulty controlling worry. Physical: muscle tension, fatigue, restlessness, insomnia, irritability, difficulty concentrating. Treatment: SSRIs (escitalopram/Lexapro, sertraline/Zoloft — first-line), SNRIs (venlafaxine/Effexor — also first-line), buspirone (Buspar, less common). CBT (cognitive restructuring — challenging catastrophic thinking patterns). Lifetime prevalence: ~5-7%, women 2x men. Often chronic, waxes/wanes with stress
- **Panic Disorder**: recurrent unexpected panic attacks + fear of having another. Panic attack: sudden intense fear + 4+ symptoms (racing heart, sweating, shaking, shortness of breath, chest pain, nausea, dizziness, chills/heat, numbness/tingling, derealization, fear of dying/losing control). Avoidance behavior (avoid places where panic occurred — can lead to agoraphobia if extended). Treatment: SSRIs, CBT (identifying triggers + reducing fear of the attack itself), interoceptive exposure (provoking physical sensations that mimic panic to decrease fear). Panic attacks ≠ heart attack: symptoms nearly identical, both get evaluated at ER
- **Social Anxiety Disorder**: intense fear of social situations where scrutinized (public speaking, eating in front of others, meeting new people, performing). Fear of negative evaluation. Physical symptoms (blushing, sweating, shaking, nausea, voice tremor). Avoidance → impaired functioning. Treatment: SSRIs, CBT (exposure therapy gradually approaching feared social situations), beta-blockers (propranolol — for performance anxiety specifically, 20-40mg before event)
- **Specific Phobia**: intense fear of specific object/situation (heights, flying, spiders, enclosed spaces, blood, needles, vomit, etc.). Recognizing as excessive. Avoidance. Treatment: exposure therapy (most effective — graded exposure hierarchy. Systematic desensitization: relax → imagine → real. Something like: picture spider, then look at photo, then watch video, then visit pet store tank, then be in same room at increasing distance). 7-9% lifetime prevalence

### Mood Disorders
- **Major Depressive Disorder (MDD)**: depressed mood OR loss of interest (anhedonia) for ≥2 weeks, plus 5+ symptoms (sleep changes, appetite/weight changes, fatigue, psychomotor agitation/retardation, worthlessness/guilt, concentration problems, recurrent thoughts of death. Must be new or worse than baseline). Significant distress/impairment. Specifiers: melancholic, atypical (mood reactive + excessive sleep + appetite + leaden paralysis + rejection sensitivity), seasonal (SAD — fall/winter onset), peripartum (pregnancy + postpartum — "baby blues" 50-80%, postpartum depression 10-15%. Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale for screening)
  - PHQ-9: self-report depression severity (0-27). Score ≥10 = probable MDD. PHQ-9 also assesses functional impairment. Must assess suicide risk (PHQ-9 item 9 = suicidal thoughts). Treatment: SSRIs (fluoxetine/Prozac, sertraline/Zoloft, escitalopram/Lexapro), SNRIs (venlafaxine/Effexor, duloxetine/Cymbalta), bupropion/Wellbutrin (no sexual side effects — but lower for anxiety, seizure risk at high doses), CBT (behavioral activation: schedule pleasant activities, structure daily routines, break tasks into small steps). For severe: TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation), ECT (electroconvulsive therapy), ketamine (Spravato/esketamine nasal spray, IV ketamine). 17% lifetime prevalence
- **Persistent Depressive Disorder (Dysthymia)**: chronic depressed mood for ≥2 years, plus 2+ symptoms (poor appetite/overeating, insomnia/hypersomnia, low energy, low self-esteem, poor concentration, hopelessness). Never without symptoms >2 months. Often earlier age of onset. "Double depression" = dysthymia + superimposed MDD episode
- **Bipolar I Disorder**: at least one manic episode (≥1 week, elevated/expansive/irritable mood, increased goal-directed activity, plus 3+ symptoms: grandiosity, decreased need for sleep, pressured speech, racing thoughts, distractibility, increased risky activity (spending, sex, investments), flight of ideas). Causes marked impairment (hospitalization common). Psychosis possible (delusions consistent with mood — grandiose). Depressive episodes usually also present. Treatment: mood stabilizers (lithium — gold standard, decreases suicide risk; valproate/Depakote; lamotrigine/Lamictal for depression, better for bipolar depression). Atypical antipsychotics (olanzapine/Zyprexa, quetiapine/Seroquel, aripiprazole/Abilify, cariprazine/Vraylar). Do NOT treat with antidepressant alone (SSRI can trigger mania). 1% lifetime
- **Bipolar II Disorder**: hypomanic episodes (≥4 days, similar but less severe than mania — NOT causing marked impairment/psychosis) + major depressive episodes. More depressive episodes than hypomanic. High suicide risk. Often misdiagnosed as MDD (patient reports depression, doesn't report hypomania unless asked). Treatment: lamotrigine first-line; quetiapine/Seroquel; avoid antidepressants without mood stabilizer (high switch rate to hypomania)

### Psychotic Disorders
- **Schizophrenia**: ≥2 active symptoms for 1 month (delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech, grossly disorganized/catatonic behavior, negative symptoms — affective flattening, alogia (poverty of speech), avolition (reduced motivation to start + complete goal-directed tasks). Continuous signs for ≥6 months. Prodromal phase: social withdrawal, odd beliefs, deteriorating function. Onset: late teens-30s, men earlier. Treatment: antipsychotics (first generation — haloperidol/Haldol, chlorpromazine/Thorazine — older, more movement side effects (tardive dyskinesia). Second generation — risperidone/Risperdal, olanzapine/Zyprexa, quetiapine/Seroquel, aripiprazole/Abilify, clozapine/Clozaril — last resort (treatment-resistant, unique efficacy) but requires blood monitoring (agranulocytosis risk). Psychological: CBT for psychosis, social skills training, supported employment. Family therapy: reduce expressed emotion to prevent relapse. 1% lifetime, severe functional impairment
- **Brief Psychotic Disorder**: sudden onset of psychotic symptoms lasting 1 day to 1 month, full return to premorbid. Often triggered by extreme stress. Good prognosis
- **Schizoaffective Disorder**: continuous psychotic symptoms (schizophrenia) + mood episodes (depression or mania). Requires at least 2 weeks of psychosis WITHOUT mood symptoms (to differentiate from mood disorder with psychotic features) AND mood symptoms present for majority of illness duration. Treatment: antipsychotic + mood stabilizer/antidepressant

### Personality Disorders (Cluster A: Odd/Eccentric)
- **Paranoid**: pervasive distrust + suspiciousness (others' motives interpreted as malevolent). Reluctant to confide, reads hidden meanings, bears grudges, questions loyalty. 2-4% prevalence
- **Schizoid**: detachment from social relationships, restricted emotional expression. Neither wants nor enjoys close relationships, chooses solitary activities, little interest in sexual relationships. 3-7%
- **Schizotypal**: acute discomfort with close relationships, cognitive/perceptual distortions (odd beliefs/magical thinking, unusual perceptual experiences), eccentric behavior. Odd speech/thinking (vague, metaphorical, stereotyped). 4% prevalence, meaning struggles with forming bonds

### Personality Disorders (Cluster B: Dramatic/Emotional/Erratic)
- **Antisocial (ASPD)**: disregard for rights of others since age 15 (≥18 years old). Failure to conform to law (repeated arrests), deceitfulness, impulsivity, irritability/aggressiveness, reckless disregard for safety, irresponsibility, lack of remorse. Must have evidence of conduct disorder before age 15. 3% men, 1% women. NOT the same as "psychopathy" — overlap but psychopathy has callous unemotional traits, not in DSM-5
- **Borderline (BPD)**: instability in interpersonal relationships, self-image, affects + marked impulsivity beginning by early adulthood. Fear of abandonment, pattern of unstable intense relationships (idealization → devaluation), identity disturbance, impulsivity (spending, sex, substance, reckless driving), recurrent suicidal behavior/self-harm, emptiness, anger, transient paranoid ideation/dissociation. 2-6% prevalence. Treatment: DBT (Dialectical Behavior Therapy — gold standard: mindfulness, distress tolerance, emotional regulation, interpersonal effectiveness). DBT created specifically for BPD by Marsha Linehan. Self-harm = emotional regulation strategy, not necessarily suicidal — but suicide rate high (up to 10%). Not the same as bipolar (often confused — BPD rapid mood shifts within hours triggered by events vs bipolar mood episodes lasting days-weeks)
- **Histrionic**: excessive emotionality + attention seeking. Uncomfortable when not center of attention, inappropriate sexually seductive behavior, rapidly shifting shallow emotions, uses physical appearance to draw attention. 2%
- **Narcissistic (NPD)**: grandiosity (fantasies + behavior), need for admiration, lack of empathy. Exaggerates achievements, believes self "special," requires excessive admiration, sense of entitlement, interpersonally exploitative, lacks empathy, envious of others, arrogant. Grandiose (overt — obvious) vs vulnerable (covert — fragile, self-esteem regulation, defensive grandiosity emerges when challenged). 1% prevalence, men > women

### Cluster C (Anxious/Fearful)
- **Avoidant (AvPD)**: social inhibition, feelings of inadequacy, hypersensitivity to negative evaluation. Avoids occupations/relationships involving significant interpersonal contact. Preoccupied with being criticized/rejected. Views self as socially inept. NOT the same as social anxiety (wants relationships but avoids; social anxiety = fears performing socially). 2-3%
- **Dependent**: excessive need to be taken care of → submissive/clinging. Difficulty making daily decisions without excessive advice, needs others to take responsibility for major life areas, difficulty disagreeing, feels helpless when alone. 1%
- **Obsessive-Compulsive (OCPD)**: preoccupation with orderliness, perfectionism, control (NOT the same as OCD). Preoccupied with details/rules/lists to extent that point is lost, perfectionism interferes with completion, excessive devotion to work (excluding leisure), inflexible about morality/ethics, unable to discard worn-out objects (hoarding), reluctant to delegate, miserly spending. 4-8%, men more

### OCD & Related
- **OCD**: obsessions (recurrent, persistent thoughts/ urges/images, intrusive, unwanted — NOT excessive worry about real problems. Trying to ignore/neutralize with another thought/action) + compulsions (repetitive behaviors/mental acts performed in response to obsession, aimed at preventing/reducing anxiety/preventing something bad, but NOT connected realistically to what they prevent — or excessive). Common: contamination (hand washing, checking), symmetry/ordering, forbidden/harm thoughts, religious/scrupulosity. Insight: good → absent/delusional. Treatment: SSRIs (high dose — fluoxetine up to 80mg, sertraline up to 200mg. Fluvoxamine brand Luvox also effective). ERP (Exposure + Response Prevention: CBT gold standard — expose to obsession trigger, prevent compulsion. Therapist-guided hierarchy. For contamination: touch dirty door handle, don't wash hands for 10 min, then 30, then 60, etc.). For severe/refractory: clomipramine (Anafranil — TCA, older, more side effects), augmentation with low-dose antipsychotic, TMS, DBS (deep brain stimulation) for intractable
  - OCPD (personality traits) vs OCD (distinct disorder): OCPD = pervasive pattern of orderliness/perfectionism (EGO-syntonic = aligns with self-image, they think their approach is RIGHT). OCD = intrusive, unwanted, ego-dystonic (person recognizes obsessions as unreasonable, resists them). Eg: OCPD person organizes can labels facing out because it's "proper". OCD person organizes them because fear catastrophe if not aligned
- **Body Dysmorphic Disorder**: preoccupied with perceived defect(s) in appearance that are NOT observable or appear slight to others. Repetitive behaviors (checking mirror, seeking reassurance, comparing, skin picking — may pick at skin for hours), cognitive (belief they look ugly/real deformity). Compared to others. Association: high with OCD, depression, eating disorders. 2.5% prevalence
- **Hoarding Disorder**: persistent difficulty discarding possessions, regardless of actual value, due to perceived need to save + distress at discarding. Clutter (or in severe cases: living spaces unusable for purpose (sleeping in chair, not bed). Level I-V: clean to unsanitary/hazardous). Difficulty with organizing. Insight: good to absent/delusional. Treatment: CBT for hoarding (challenging beliefs about possessions, sorting + discarding practice, relapse prevention). NOT solved by cleaning/organizing alone — belongings return without psychological intervention. Must be sustained behavioral change
