# Dermatology — Skin Health

## Common Conditions

- **Acne**: clogged pores + inflammation + Cutibacterium acnes overgrowth. Mild: OTC benzoyl peroxide (2.5-10%, kills bacteria, anti-inflammatory), salicylic acid (exfoliates), adapalene 0.1% (retinoid, OTC since 2016). Moderate-severe: prescription topical (tretinoin, clindamycin, dapsone), oral antibiotics (doxycycline, minocycline — short-term only), spironolactone (females, hormonal), isotretinoin (severe cystic — requires monthly monitoring for pregnancy + labs). No evidence toothpaste helps — irritates skin
- **Eczema (atopic dermatitis)**: dry, itchy, inflamed skin — broken barrier + immune overreaction. Triggers: allergens, irritants, stress, weather changes. Management: daily moisturizer (thick creams/ointments, not lotions), topical corticosteroids (hydrocortisone 1% OTC for mild, stronger by prescription), topical calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus/pimecrolimus — steroid-free, safe for face/groin), wet wraps for flares. Bathing then moisturize within 3 minutes (soak + seal)
- **Psoriasis**: autoimmune — accelerated skin cell turnover (every 3-5 days vs 28). Red plaques with silvery scales on elbows, knees, scalp, lower back. Nail pitting, joint pain (psoriatic arthritis in 30%). Treatments: topical (corticosteroids, vitamin D analogs — calcipotriene), phototherapy (UVB narrowband 3x/week), systemic (methotrexate, cyclosporine, biologics like adalimumab, secukinumab). Not contagious
- **Rosacea**: chronic facial redness, flushing, visible blood vessels, papules/pustules, thickened skin (rhinophyma). Triggers: sun, heat, spicy food, alcohol, caffeine, stress. Treatments: metronidazole/ivermectin/azelaic acid topicals, oral doxycycline (low-dose anti-inflammatory), laser (pulsed dye laser, IPL) for vessels. Avoid topical steroids on face — worsens rosacea
- **Contact dermatitis**: irritant (chemical burn — soap, solvents — immediate) vs allergic (type IV delayed hypersensitivity — poison ivy, nickel, latex — 24-72 hrs). Treatment: remove trigger, cool compresses, topical steroids, antihistamines (for itch, not histamine-driven for delayed type). Patch testing identifies allergens

## Skincare Routine

- **Morning**: cleanser (gentle, non-stripping) → antioxidant (vitamin C L-ascorbic acid 10-20%, pH < 3.5) → moisturizer → sunscreen SPF 30+ (last step, every day, even indoors — windows let in UVA)
- **Evening**: double cleanse if makeup/sunscreen (oil cleanser then water-based) → treatment (retinoid, exfoliant — not same night) → moisturizer (heavier at night) → eye cream (optional)
- **Actives order**: thinnest to thickest consistency. Wait 30 seconds between layers for absorption. Retinoids: use at night, 2-3x/week to start (purge for 4-6 weeks normal), then increase. Sunscreen mandatory if using retinoids/AHAs
- **Exfoliation**: chemical (AHAs like glycolic/lactic — surface, for dry/dull. BHAs like salicylic — oil-soluble, into pores, for acne/oily) vs physical (gentle only — avoid microbeads, walnut scrubs = microtears). 1-3x/week max, not with retinoids same night

## Sunscreen

- **Mineral (physical)**: zinc oxide, titanium dioxide. Sits on skin, reflects/scatters UV. Broad spectrum inherently. Zinc oxide = best UVA protection. White cast common (micronized reduces, tinted eliminates). Good for sensitive skin, rosacea
- **Chemical (organic)**: avobenzone, octinoxate, octisalate, homosalate, oxybenzone. Absorb UV → convert to heat. Usually cosmetically elegant (no white cast). Avobenzone degrades quickly — needs stabilizers (octocrylene). Oxybenzone controversial (absorbed systemically, FDA requested more safety data — known endocrine effects in animals, human relevance unclear)
- **SPF**: SPF 15 blocks ~93% UVB, SPF 30 ~97%, SPF 50 ~98% — diminishing returns. No SPF blocks 100%. Reapply every 2 hours (or after swimming/sweating). Use 1/4 tsp for face, shot glass for body. Broad spectrum = UVA + UVB protection. PA++++ highest UVA (Japan rating). SPF in moisturizer = not enough (most people underapply; use dedicated sunscreen)

## When to See a Dermatologist

- Mole changes (ABCDE — see below), new growths, sores that won't heal
- Acne not responding to OTC after 3-4 months
- Rash covering >10% body surface, persistent, or unclear cause
- Hair loss (sudden or patchy), nail abnormalities
- Skin cancer screening: baseline at age 35-40 earlier if high risk (fair skin, many moles, family history, extensive sun exposure, history of tanning bed use)

## ABCDEs of Melanoma Detection

- **A**: Asymmetry — one half doesn't match other
- **B**: Border — irregular, scalloped, poorly defined edges
- **C**: Color — multiple colors (tan, brown, black, red, white, blue)
- **D**: Diameter — >6mm (pencil eraser), though melanomas can be smaller
- **E**: Evolving — changing in size, shape, color, or new symptom (bleeding, itching, crusting)
- **UGP rule**: Ugly Duckling — mole that looks different from others. Most important sign. Photograph moles every 3 months. Any new mole after age 30 should be checked

## Skin Types (Baumann Classification)

- Dry: lack of oil, tight/rough/flaky. Use cream cleanser, rich moisturizer (ceramides, squalane, shea butter), avoid foaming cleansers. Oily: excess sebum, shiny, large pores. Gel cleanser, lightweight moisturizer, niacinamide, salicylic acid. Combination: dry cheeks + oily T-zone. Sensitive: easily irritated, stings/burns. Fragrance-free, minimal ingredients, barrier repair
