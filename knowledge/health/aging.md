# Aging & Healthy Aging

## Physical Changes
- **Muscle mass**: lose 3-8% per decade after 30 — accelerates after 60 (sarcopenia)
  - Prevention: resistance training (weights, bands, bodyweight) — never too late to start
  - Protein: 1.2-2.0 g/kg body weight/day (higher than younger adults)
- **Bone density**: peaks at 30, decreases after — women lose faster after menopause
  - Prevention: weight-bearing exercise (walking, jogging, dancing), calcium (1,200 mg/day), vitamin D (800-1000 IU/day), strength training
- **Metabolism**: slows ~1-2% per decade — largely due to muscle loss, NOT inevitable if you maintain muscle
- **Vision**: presbyopia (need reading glasses ~40-50), cataracts (cloudy lens, surgery), floaters increase
  - Annual eye exam after 60
- **Hearing**: presbycusis (high frequency hearing loss) — common, gradual
  - Get hearing test if people sound muffled, trouble in crowds, or you turn up TV
  - Hearing aids = better cognitive outcomes (untreated hearing loss is #1 modifiable dementia risk factor)
- **Skin**: thinner, less elastic, dryer, slower healing
  - Protection: sunscreen daily, moisturize, don't smoke (causes wrinkles + sagging)
- **Balance**: declines with age, leading cause of injury (falls)
  - Prevention: balance exercises (tai chi, yoga, standing on one leg), physical therapy if needed
  - Home safety: grab bars in bathroom, non-slip mats, adequate lighting, remove trip hazards

## Brain Health & Cognition
- **Neuroplasticity**: brain can form new connections at any age — "use it or lose it"
- **Cognitive reserve**: built by education, reading, learning new skills, bilingualism — delays dementia symptoms
- **Mild cognitive impairment (MCI)**: noticeable decline that doesn't interfere with daily life — not dementia, but higher risk
  - Some MCI is reversible (caused by sleep apnea, B12 deficiency, medication side effects, depression, thyroid)
- **Dementia**: not normal aging — Alzheimer's (60-80%), vascular, Lewy body, frontotemporal
  - Warning signs: memory loss disrupting daily life, difficulty with familiar tasks, confusion with time/place, personality changes, withdrawal
  - NOT normal: forgetting recently learned information, getting lost in familiar places, poor judgment
  - Normal aging: occasionally forgetting names, misplacing things, slow word recall
- **Brain-healthy lifestyle**: exercise (best thing for brain), Mediterranean diet, social connections, mental stimulation (learning, puzzles, reading), sleep, blood pressure control, hearing treatment

## Emotional & Social
- **Social connections**: strongest predictor of healthy aging — lonelier people have faster cognitive decline, more disease, earlier death
  - Stay connected: regular calls/friends, group activities (book clubs, walking groups, volunteering, classes)
  - Making new friends later in life: shared activities, neighborhood groups, senior centers, religious communities
- **Purpose**: having a reason to get up = longer, healthier life (retirement, volunteering, hobbies, caregiving, mentoring)
- **Mental health**: depression is NOT normal in aging — often overlooked, treated as "grumpy old person"
  - Signs: loss of interest, sleep changes, fatigue, hopelessness, physical complaints (pain, GI issues)
  - Treatment: therapy, medication, social connection, physical activity
- **Retirement identity**: many people struggle with loss of purpose — plan ahead (what will fill your time?)
  - "Retirement from" vs "retirement to" — have something to retire TO, not just away from

## Common Health Conditions
- **Heart disease**: #1 killer — prevention = blood pressure control, cholesterol management, exercise, no smoking, Mediterranean diet
- **Arthritis**: osteoarthritis (wear and tear, knees, hips, hands) vs rheumatoid (autoimmune, joint inflammation)
  - Treatment: exercise (strengthen muscles around joints), weight loss (reduce joint load), pain management, joint replacement if severe
  - Movement is better than rest — joints need motion for lubrication
- **Osteoporosis**: brittle bones — fractures (hip, spine, wrist) are life-altering
  - Screen: DEXA scan at 65 (women) or earlier with risk factors
  - Treatment: bisphosphonates, calcium/vitamin D, weight-bearing exercise, fall prevention
- **Diabetes type 2**: prevention = weight management, exercise, diet (reduced sugar/refined carbs)
  - Control = prevents complications (kidney, vision, nerve, heart)
- **High blood pressure**: "silent killer" — no symptoms, damages arteries over decades
  - Goal: <130/80 most people. Lifestyle: DASH diet, reduce sodium, exercise, weight, stress
- **Cancer risk**: increases with age — screening (mammogram, colonoscopy, skin check, prostate discussion with doctor)
  - Prevention: don't smoke, limit alcohol, healthy weight, sun protection, HPV vaccine

## Medications
- Polypharmacy: 5+ medications increases risk of interactions, falls, confusion
- Review medications annually with doctor — stop anything no longer needed (deprescribing)
- Over-the-counter: be careful with NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen — kidney/stomach damage with long-term use in older adults)
- Anticholinergic burden: Benadryl, some sleep aids, some bladder meds — linked to dementia risk, use lowest effective dose shortest duration

## Advance Care Planning
- **Living will**: medical treatments you do/don't want (resuscitation, ventilation, feeding tube)
- **Healthcare proxy / POA**: person who makes medical decisions if you can't — discuss wishes with them in detail
- **Will / estate plan**: who gets what, executor, guardian for minor children
- **Long-term care**: Medicare does NOT cover assisted living or nursing home — plan ahead
- **Discuss with family**: what matters to you at end of life (quality of life vs longevity, at home vs hospital)

## Practical Tips
- **Fall-proof your home**: grab bars in shower, by toilet, railings on both sides of stairs, nightlights, no throw rugs, clear pathways
- **Bathroom safety**: shower chair, hand-held shower head, raised toilet seat if needed
- **Driving**: eventual cessation is nearly universal — plan for alternatives (rideshare, public transit, family, senior transport)
  - Warning signs: near misses, getting lost, dents/ scrapes, trouble merging, slower reaction time
- **Foot care**: check feet daily (especially diabetics), professional pedicure is fine, good shoes with support
- **Hearing aids**: don't delay (average wait 7-10 years after diagnosis) — better hearing = better relationships, less dementia
- **Assistive technology**: medication reminders, personal emergency response (Life Alert), smart home (voice assistants for lights/locks), large-print options, captioned phones
