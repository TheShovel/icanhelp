# Parenting: Newborn Care

## First Weeks Home

### Sleeping
- **Safe sleep**: back to sleep (every sleep, SIDS risk 50% less than tummy). Firm mattress, tight sheet. No blankets, pillows, toys, bumpers in crib. Room share (NOT bed share) for first 6 months (recommended by AAP). Swaddle: arms in (prevent startle reflex waking), hips free (wrap loose around legs — tight hips = hip dysplasia risk). Swaddle: wearable blanket (arms in or out) after newborn stops trying to break out. Stop swaddling when baby starts rolling (2-4 months)
  - Sleep location: bassinet or pack-n-play next to parent's bed. Flat surface only (not bouncer, car seat, swing, rocker for unsupervised sleep). Crib: no drop-side (banned)
- **Newborn sleep patterns**: 14-17 hours/day, 2-4 hour stretches. Wake to feed (breastfed babies need to nurse often — 8-12x/day, first few weeks). Newborn stomach = cherry (day 1) → walnut (day 3) → apricot (week 1) → egg (week 2) → large egg (1 month). Frequent feeding is normal + necessary
  - "Sleep when baby sleeps": first rule of newborn survival. Chores can wait. Accept help from others
- **SIDS prevention**: back to sleep, firm surface, no soft objects, no overheating (dress one more layer than you'd wear, check neck temperature not hands, temp 68-72°F. Overheating = SIDS risk). Pacifier at naps + bedtime (reduces SIDS risk, even if falls out). Breastfeeding reduces SIDS. Room-sharing with parent (not bed-sharing). Avoid smoke exposure, alcohol/drugs. Tummy time when awake (start with 1-2 min, build up, prevents flat head + builds neck/shoulder strength)

### Feeding
- **Breastfeeding**: feed on demand (8-12x in 24 hrs). Watch for hunger cues: rooting, sucking hands, smacking lips (crying is LATE sign). Good latch: mouth wide, flanged lips, chin touching breast, more areola visible above nipple. Pain ≠ normal (could be bad latch, tongue tie, infection). Wet diapers: 6+ per day by day 4-5 (pale/clear urine). Stools: meconium (black tarry) day 1-2, transition (green) day 3-4, yellow seedy (breastfed) by day 5. Weight: loses 5-7% in first days, regains by 2 weeks
  - Engorgement: breast hard, full, warm. Feed frequently, hand express or pump to soften. Cold pack after feeding (10 min). Warm before feeding to stimulate letdown. Reverse pressure softening: press around areola for 1 min to soften, helps baby latch. For sore nipples: lanolin cream or APNO (all-purpose nipple ointment, prescription), gel soothies, silverette cups
  - Colostrum: first milk (days 1-5) — thin, yellow, immunoglobulins, only small amounts but that's all baby needs. "Nature's perfect first food"
- **Formula feeding**: follow package instructions exactly (too much water = not enough calories, too little = dehydration risk). Sterilize bottles/nipples for first 3 months (boil 5 min or dishwasher sanitize). Newborn: 1-2 oz per feeding every 2-3 hours. Increase as baby grows. Don't prop bottle (choking + ear infection + overfeeding). Hold baby semi-upright. Throw away unfinished formula after 1 hour (bacteria risk)
- **Both**: burp after each feeding (over shoulder, on lap, sit up with support). Newborn stomach = tiny, needs frequent small feeds. Signs of hunger: rooting, fists to mouth, lip smacking. Full cues: turns away, falls asleep, releases latch/ bottle. Don't force finish the bottle (overfeeding leads to spit-up + obesity risk)

### Diapering
- **Frequency**: newborn: 8-12 diapers/day. Change before feeding (or after if comfortable). Clean: front to back (especially for girls — prevent UTIs). Pat dry, apply barrier cream (zinc oxide/ petroleum jelly) to prevent diaper rash. For boys: cover penis with diaper to prevent being sprayed (but don't pull back foreskin — it retracts naturally, just wash outer area)
- **Diaper rash**: frequent changes, air time, barrier cream (zinc oxide or petroleum jelly). If yeast (bright red, bumps, satellite lesions): antifungal cream (Lotrimin, nystatin — Rx). If not improving in 3 days: see doctor
- **Signs of dehydration**: fewer than 6 wet diapers/day after day 5, dark urine, dry mouth, no tears when crying, sunken soft spot (fontanelle), lethargic. Unusual: fewer wet diapers + vomiting? Call doctor immediately

### Bathing
- **Sponge bath**: until umbilical cord falls off (1-3 weeks). Wash face with water only, body with mild baby soap. Keep belly button dry (fold diaper down). No submersion until cord stump off. Sponge bath on changing pad or towel on floor (not harmful)
- **Tub bath**: 2-3x/week enough. Water 100°F (test with elbow — warm, not hot). Support head + neck. Never leave unattended (even for 1 second — babies drown in 2" of water). Pat dry, moisturize if dry. Brush cradle cap on scalp with soft brush before wash + gentle shampoo

### Umbilical Cord Care
- Keep clean + dry. Fold diaper below stump. Sponge bath until it falls off (1-3 weeks). No tub bath until after. Clean with water if soiled, pat dry. Signs of infection: red skin around base, pus, foul odor, bleeding (small amount is normal when stump falls off). Call doctor if suspect infection

## Common Concerns

### Jaundice
- Yellow skin/eyes (bilirubin from normal RBC breakdown — newborn liver immature). Normal: mild (especially breastfed babies). Phototherapy: blue light (breaks down bilirubin in skin). When to worry: jaundice spreads to arms/legs (usually starts from face), baby very sleepy/ can't feed, dark urine, pale stools. Unconjugated (normal newborn type) vs conjugated bilirubin (sign of liver problem, needs investigation)
  - Treatment: frequent feeding (flushes bilirubin out in stool). Phototherapy (bili lights, bili blanket at home or in hospital). Exchange transfusion for severe (usually >20-25 mg/dL range). Kernicterus: bilirubin in brain (rare but causes permanent hearing loss + movement disorder). Treated promptly, kernicterus is preventable

### Colic
- Crying >3 hours/day, >3 days/week, >3 weeks. Starts ~2-3 weeks, peaks ~6 weeks, resolves by 3-4 months. Cause: unknown (theory: GI discomfort, underdeveloped nervous system, temperament). NOT gas (gas drops not effective, but gripe water/ simethicone won't harm). Soothing: 5 S's (Harvey Karp) — Swaddle, Side/Stomach position, Shush, Swing, Suck. Wear/ carry baby. Motion (car ride, stroller walk). White noise (can mimic womb sounds — loud shushing). Warm bath
  - Don't shake baby! (Shaken baby syndrome = brain damage, death). If overwhelmed: place baby safely in crib, step away for 5 min to calm down, call someone for help. It's OK to walk away when crying is overwhelming — even 5 minutes can help reset

### Reflux (GER)
- Spitting up after feeds — common (most babies "happy spitters"). Milk + stomach acid comes back up because lower esophageal sphincter immature. If gaining weight well + not in pain: just messy (no treatment needed). Keep upright 20-30 min after feeding. Burp frequently. Smaller more frequent feeds
  - GERD (disease): poor weight gain, forceful vomiting, pain/arching during/after feeds, refusal to eat, blood in stool (esophagitis). Treatment: thickened feeds (rice cereal in formula/ expressed breast milk, 1 tsp per oz — slit nipple crosscut). PPI (lansoprazole/Prevacid, omeprazole — if fails, EGD by GI). H2 blocker (famotidine/Pepcid). GER vs GERD: happy spitter vs irritable, arching, refusing to eat

### Thrush
- White patches on tongue/cheeks that CAN'T be wiped off (milk can). Painful nursing (baby + mother — sore nipples, shooting breast pain). Yeast infection (Candida albicans). Treatment: Nystatin suspension for baby (swab inside mouth 4x/day, 7-14 days). For breastfeeding mother: Nystatin cream on nipples after feeds + fluconazole (Diflucan) if persistent. Sterilize pacifiers/bottle nipples, wash all bras/pads in hot water + bleach

## When to Call Doctor
- **Fever**: >100.4°F rectal in newborn <3 months is EMERGENCY (immature immune system, serious bacterial infection risk — meningitis, UTI, sepsis). For 3-6 months: >102°F call. All ages: if fever + lethargic, not drinking, difficulty breathing, or unresponsive — ER
- **Not feeding**: <6 wet diapers in 24 hrs; baby too sleepy to feed; feeds less than 8x/day; no stool for 48 hrs (normal for breastfed after 6 weeks). Vomiting everything (forceful — projectile, green bile). Decreased feeding: can lead to dehydration + hypoglycemia
- **Breathing**: flaring nostrils, chest retractions, grunting, blue/blue lips/tongue (cyanosis), >60 breaths/min at rest. Any breathing difficulty = ER. RSV (bronchiolitis): common winter in infants <1 year, wheezing, nasal flaring. Worry: breathing fast while NOT crying
- **Behavior**: inconsolable crying, lethargic (can't wake, floppy, doesn't respond), seizure-like movements (jerking, staring, rhythmic), arched back (sign of severe reflux/ meningitis, later signs of kernicterus or brain infection). "Something's just not right" — trust your gut, call doctor
- **Poop**: black stools after day 3 (should be meconium only first 2 days). White/chalky stools (sign of liver problem). Red blood in stool (could be milk allergy, anal fissure, infection). In breastfed: infrequent but not constipated stool can go 5-7 days without poop after 4-6 weeks — normal
