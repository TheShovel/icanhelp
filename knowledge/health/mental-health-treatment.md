# Mental Health: Therapy & Treatment Approaches

## Therapy Modalities

### Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)
- **Most evidence-based** for anxiety, depression, OCD, PTSD, eating disorders. Typically 12-20 sessions. Focus on present (not past). Addresses distorted thinking + maladaptive behaviors
- **Cognitive triangle**: thoughts → feelings → behaviors. Changing one affects others. Example: "No one likes me" (thought) → "I feel lonely" (feeling) → "I avoid parties" (behavior) → proves no one likes me (thought reinforced). Goal: identify + challenge distorted thoughts, change behavior patterns
- **Cognitive distortions**: all-or-nothing (black + white). Catastrophizing (assuming worst). Fortune telling (predicting negative future). Mind reading (assuming others think negatively of you). Should statements (rigid rules). Emotional reasoning (believing feelings = truth). Labeling ("I'm a failure" vs "I failed that test"). Personalization (taking blame for things outside control)
  - Cognitive restructuring: identify automatic thought → evaluate evidence → generate balanced thought. "My friend didn't text back" → "Maybe she's busy" (not "she hates me").
- **Behavioral activation**: schedule pleasant activities + track mood. Break avoidance cycle in depression. Small steps → feeling better. Action first, motivation follows (not "I need to feel motivated first")
  - Activity scheduling: plan activities rated by mastery + pleasure. Rate mood before/after → see that action improves mood. Start simple (watch a show, take 10 min walk, call a friend)
- **Exposure therapy**: for anxiety, phobias, OCD, PTSD. Grade hierarchy: rate fear (0-100). Start: low-fear item (look at spider photo, 30 = SUDS). Progress: up hierarchy (watch video, visit pet store, be in same room at distance). The key: habituate (fear response decreases with prolonged exposure without avoidance). Combine with response prevention (ERP) for OCD: expose to trigger, prevent compulsion. Watch fear diminish naturally

### Dialectical Behavior Therapy (DBT)
- **Originally for BPD** (borderline personality disorder). Now used for emotion dysregulation, self-harm, suicidal behavior. Balance acceptance + change (dialectic: "I accept myself as I am AND I need to change")
- **Four modules**: mindfulness (observe, describe, participate — non-judgmentally. "What" skills + "How" skills). Distress tolerance (survive crisis without making it worse: TIPP — Temperature (cold water on face activates dive reflex), Intense exercise, Paced breathing, Paired muscle relaxation. STOP skill: Stop, Take a step back, Observe, Proceed mindfully). Emotion regulation (identify + label emotions, reduce vulnerability — PLEASE skills: treat Physical illness, balance Eating, avoid mood-Altering substances, balance Sleep, get Exercise). Interpersonal effectiveness (ask for what you need, say no, maintain relationships: DEAR MAN — Describe, Express, Assert, Reinforce — stay Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate)
  - DBT skills groups (weekly) + individual therapy + phone coaching. Skills training: weekly, 24 weeks full curriculum. Designed for high-suicidality + self-harm

### Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT)
- **Accept** difficult thoughts + feelings (don't fight them). **Commit** to values-based action. "Don't stop the waves, learn to surf."
- **Six core processes**: defusion (observe thoughts, don't fuse with them — "I'm having the thought that I'm worthless" vs "I'm worthless"). Acceptance (allow feelings to be present without fighting). Present moment (flexible attention). Self as context (connected, observing self beyond thoughts/feelings). Values (what matters to you). Committed action (take steps aligned with values)
  - Example: anxiety before public speaking → accepting anxiety + committing to giving the speech anyway (values: connection, growth, courage). "I can be anxious AND give a good speech."

### Psychodynamic Therapy
- **Insight-oriented**: unconscious patterns from childhood + past relationships affect current behavior. Free association, dream interpretation, transference (feelings from past transferred to therapist). Longer term (months to years). Less evidence for specific disorders, but helpful for: personality issues, chronic relationship problems, meaning-of-life concerns
  - Defense mechanisms: projection (attributing own feelings to others), denial (refusing to accept reality), rationalization (explaining away with logic). More mature: sublimation (channel feelings into creative/productive), humor, altruism

### EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
- **For PTSD** — bilateral stimulation (eye movements, taps, tones) while recalling trauma. Mechanism: helps brain process traumatic memories (like REM sleep during trauma processing). Relatively short (8-12 sessions). Strong evidence for PTSD. Divided: can re-traumatize if not done correctly (need trained EMDR therapist). Most effective: therapy + trauma processing; not all trauma survivors need bilateral stimulation (CBT is also effective)

## Medication Management

### Antidepressants
- **SSRIs** (Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors): first-line. Citalopram (Celexa), escitalopram (Lexapro), fluoxetine (Prozac), paroxetine (Paxil), sertraline (Zoloft). Common SE: nausea (first week), sexual dysfunction (60% SSRIs), insomnia/ sedation (varies), weight gain (paroxetine most, escitalopram mild). Takes 4-6 weeks for full effect. Prozac = activating (good for low energy, can cause anxiety early). Paxil = sedating (good for anxiety but worst weight gain)
- **SNRIs**: venlafaxine (Effexor XR), duloxetine (Cymbalta), desvenlafaxine (Pristiq). For depression + anxiety + chronic pain (duloxetine for diabetic neuropathy, fibromyalgia). Side effects: similar SSRIs + BP elevation (venlafaxine at high dose). Discontinuation syndrome (Effexor has notorious withdrawal — dizziness, brain zaps). Taper EXTREMELY SLOWLY over weeks
- **Atypicals**: bupropion (Wellbutrin) — no sexual side effects, activating (good for low energy ADHD), but increases anxiety, seizure risk (must not use with eating disorders or high seizure risk). Mirtazapine (Remeron) — sedating, good for sleep + appetite, weight gain + sedation, good for depression + insomnia
- **MAOIs**: phenelzine (Nardil), tranylcypromine (Parnate). Very effective for treatment-resistant depression. Dietary restrictions (no aged cheese, cured meat, fermented foods, some wines — tyramine interaction can cause hypertensive crisis). New delivery: Emsam patch (selegiline) — fewer dietary restrictions. Last resort only now because of safer SSRIs/SNRIs

### Anti-Anxiety
- **Benzodiazepines**: alprazolam (Xanax), clonazepam (Klonopin), lorazepam (Ativan), diazepam (Valium). Fast-acting, for acute anxiety/ panic. Risk: tolerance, dependence, withdrawal (seizures, can be fatal), falls in elderly. "As needed" only, not daily. Use 1-2 mg ativan for panic episode, but don't prescribe 3 months supply. Taper off after prolonged use — do not stop suddenly (seizure + potentially fatal)
- **Buspirone**: non-addictive, for GAD. Takes 2-4 weeks. Less effective than SSRI/ SNRI for many. Higher doses needed (30-60mg/day). Mild, good for elderly/those with substance use history
- **Beta blockers**: propranolol for performance anxiety (stage fright). 10-40mg before event

### Mood Stabilizers
- **Lithium**: gold standard for bipolar I. Decreases suicide risk 5x. Requires blood monitoring (level 0.6-1.2 mEq/L, toxicity >1.5). Side effects: thirst, tremor, kidney/long-term, thyroid impairment. Better: sustained release. Baseline labs (Cr, TSH, pregnancy test) + follow-up q3-6 months
- **Valproate (Depakote)**: for bipolar mania. Liver + platelet monitoring. Side: weight gain, hair loss, PCOS in women. Caution: women of childbearing age
- **Lamotrigine (Lamictal)**: for bipolar depression (better for depression than mania). Well tolerated. Risk: Stevens-Johnson rash (rare 0.8% in adults, 0.03% in children — stop at first sign). Slow titration: start 25mg → increase slowly every 2 weeks to minimize rash risk

### Antipsychotics
- **Typical (first generation)**: haloperidol (Haldol), chlorpromazine (Thorazine). High potency: EPS (extrapyramidal symptoms — muscle stiffness, tremors). Low potency: sedation + weight gain. Tardive Dyskinesia (TD): involuntary movements (face, mouth, tongue, trunk — can be permanent) with typical 20%/yr
- **Atypical (second generation)**: risperidone (Risperdal), olanzapine (Zyprexa), quetiapine (Seroquel), aripiprazole (Abilify), ziprasidone (Geodon). Less EPS, more metabolic effects (olanzapine: 10-20 lb weight gain, diabetes. Quetiapine: sedation, weight gain. Aripiprazole: least weight gain, akathisia (restlessness). Clozapine: for treatment-resistant schizophrenia, requires WBC monitoring (agranulocytosis = low neutrophils, infection risk)
  - All antipsychotics carry a black box warning for increased risk of death in elderly patients with dementia-related psychosis

## When to See a Therapist
- **Signs**: symptoms interfering with daily functioning (work, school, relationships) for 2+ weeks. Feeling sad, anxious, angry, numb persistently. Loss of interest in activities. Sleep/appetite changes. Withdrawal from others. Difficulty concentrating. Substance use to cope. Thoughts of self-harm or suicide (call 988 immediately). "Not sick enough" is NOT a reason to avoid therapy — early intervention works better
- **Choosing a therapist**: approach (CBT for anxiety/depression, DBT for BPD/ emotion dysregulation, EMDR for trauma, psychodynamic for insight/ identity). Experience with your issue. Insurance/self-pay ($150-300/session). Personal connection matters (try 3 sessions, if no connection, try another). Online: BetterHelp, Talkspace, Zocdoc, PsychologyToday.com (search by insurance, issue, location)
  - Evidence: 50-60% of therapy clients improve after 8-12 sessions. >75% improve after 6 months. Early response to therapy (within first 4-6 sessions) strongly predicts recovery
