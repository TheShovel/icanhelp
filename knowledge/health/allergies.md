# Allergies & Asthma

Types, triggers, treatment, and emergency management.

## Allergy Types
- **Seasonal allergic rhinitis (hay fever)**: Pollen (trees spring, grasses summer, weeds fall) → sneezing, runny nose, itchy eyes, congestion
- **Perennial allergies**: Year-round — dust mites, pet dander, mold, cockroach droppings
- **Food allergies**: IgE-mediated — most common: peanuts, tree nuts, milk, eggs, soy, wheat, fish, shellfish, sesame. Symptoms range from oral itching to anaphylaxis
- **Drug allergies**: Penicillin (most common), cephalosporins, sulfa drugs, NSAIDs, chemo agents. True allergy vs intolerance (side effect without immune involvement)
- **Insect venom**: Bee/wasp stings, fire ants — can cause large local reaction or systemic anaphylaxis
- **Contact dermatitis**: Poison ivy/oak/sumac (urushiol oil), nickel (jewelry), latex, fragrances, preservatives. Delayed (type IV hypersensitivity) — red, itchy, blistering at contact site
- **Atopic dermatitis (eczema)**: Chronic itchy, inflamed skin — associated with allergies, asthma. Barrier defect + immune dysregulation

## Treatment
- **Antihistamines**: 
  - 1st gen: diphenhydramine (Benadryl) — sedating, anticholinergic (dry mouth, drowsiness, cognitive impairment)
  - 2nd gen: loratadine (Claritin), cetirizine (Zyrtec), fexofenadine (Allegra), levocetirizine (Xyzal) — non-sedating, preferred for daily use
  - Nasal: azelastine (Astelin), olopatadine (Patanase)
- **Intranasal corticosteroids**: First-line for allergic rhinitis — fluticasone (Flonase), triamcinolone (Nasacort), mometasone (Nasonex). Daily use, takes 1-2 weeks for full effect
- **Decongestants**: Pseudoephedrine (Sudafed oral), oxymetazoline (Afrin nasal spray) — spray limited to 3 days (rebound congestion)
- **Allergy immunotherapy (allergy shots)**: Subcutaneous immunotherapy (SCIT) — incremental exposure builds tolerance. 3-5 year course. Sublingual immunotherapy (SLIT) — tablets/drops under tongue (grass, ragweed, dust mites)
- **Epinephrine (EpiPen)**: First-line for anaphylaxis — IM injection into outer thigh (through clothing). Can repeat after 5 min. Call 911. After epinephrine, antihistamine + steroid as adjunct

## Anaphylaxis
- **Diagnosis**: Sudden onset — skin/mucosal (hives, swelling, flushing) + respiratory (wheezing, stridor, dyspnea) OR reduced BP (dizziness, syncope) OR severe GI (vomiting, diarrhea)
- **Common triggers**: Food (peanut, tree nuts, shellfish, milk, egg), insect sting, medication (antibiotics, NSAIDs), latex
- **Biphasic reaction**: 1-4% have second wave 1-8 hours after initial resolution — observe in ER for 4-6 hours, prescribe epinephrine autoinjectors
- **Action plan**: Avoid trigger, carry epinephrine at all times, wear medical alert jewelry, inform restaurants/school/work

## Asthma
- **Pathophysiology**: Chronic airway inflammation + bronchoconstriction + mucus hypersecretion. Triggers: allergens, exercise, cold air, respiratory infections, smoke, stress
- **Symptoms**: Wheezing, chest tightness, shortness of breath, cough (often worse at night/early morning)
- **Diagnosis**: Spirometry — FEV1/FVC <0.70; reversibility with bronchodilator (FEV1 improves ≥12% + 200mL)
- **Step-up therapy** (GINA guidelines):
  - Step 1: Low-dose ICS-formoterol as-needed (SMART therapy)
  - Step 2: Low-dose ICS maintenance + as-needed SABA
  - Step 3: Medium-dose ICS-LABA
  - Step 4: Medium-dose ICS-LABA + LAMA (tiotropium)
  - Step 5: High-dose ICS-LABA + LAMA + oral corticosteroids / biologics
- **Rescue inhaler**: Albuterol (Ventolin, ProAir) — short-acting beta agonist (SABA). Use >2x/week = poor control, need controller med increase
- **Controller inhalers**: ICS (fluticasone, budesonide) + LABA (salmeterol, formoterol) — daily use, prevent inflammation
- **Biologics**: Omalizumab (Xolair — anti-IgE), mepolizumab (Nucala — anti-IL5), dupilumab (Dupixent — anti-IL4/IL13) — for severe eosinophilic asthma
- **Peak flow monitoring**: Personal best → green zone (80-100%), yellow (50-80%), red (<50% — seek emergency care)
- **Asthma action plan**: Written plan with daily controller use, rescue use, zone-specific instructions

## Environmental Control
- **Dust mites**: Allergy-proof mattress/ pillow covers (zippered, allergen-impermeable), wash bedding in hot water (130°F) weekly, reduce humidity <50%
- **Mold**: Fix leaks, dehumidifier in basement, clean visible mold with detergent, replace moldy drywall
- **Pets**: Keep out of bedroom, HEPA air purifier, wash pet weekly, remove carpet
- **Pollen**: Keep windows closed, run AC with HEPA filter, shower after being outdoors, change clothes
- **Cockroaches**: Professional pest control, seal cracks, keep food sealed, remove garbage daily
