# Psychiatry: Child & Adolescent

## Developmental Considerations

### ADHD in Children
- **Diagnosis**: symptoms before age 12, in 2+ settings (home + school). Inattention (careless mistakes, difficulty sustaining attention, doesn't listen when spoken to, loses things, forgetful, easily distracted, avoids sustained mental effort, fails to follow through). Hyperactivity/impulsivity (fidgets, leaves seat, runs/climbs when inappropriate, can't play quietly, "on the go," talks excessively, blurts answers, can't wait turn, interrupts). 5+ symptoms for those <17 (6+ for 17+). Must impact function
  - Presentations: predominantly inattentive (ADD — more common in girls, often missed). Hyperactive-impulsive. Combined. Inattentive: more likely diagnosed in school age; hyperactive more likely diagnosed in preschool
- **Treatment**: behavioral therapy (first-line for preschool). Parent training in behavior management (PCIT, parent-child interaction therapy; Incredible Years, Triple P for parent management training). Medication (stimulants: methylphenidate/Ritalin/Concerta, amphetamine/Adderall/Vyvanse — first-line for school-age + adolescents). Stimulants: 70-80% effective. Non-stimulants: atomoxetine/Strattera (SNRI), guanfacine ER/Intuniv (α-2 agonist, for impulsivity + oppositional behavior). School accommodations: 504 plan (classroom modifications) or IEP (special education)
  - "Not just bad parenting" — but parenting strategies help. Parent training reduces conflict + improves outcomes. Use token economies, clear expectations, consistent consequences
- **ADHD in girls**: often inattentive, daydreamy, internalizes, less disruptive, missed diagnosis (2:1 male:female in childhood, 1:1 in adults). More anxiety + depression. Untreated: worse outcomes academically + socially

### Autism in Children
- **Early signs** (red flags by 12-18 months): no babbling/pointing/gestures by 12 months. No single words by 16 months. No 2-word phrases by 24 months. Loss of language/social skills (regression). Poor eye contact, doesn't respond to name, doesn't engage in pretend play. Atypical sensory responses (fixation on texture/lights/running fingers over surfaces, covers ears to sounds, eats only 3 foods). Repetitive movements (hand flapping, rocking, spinning)
  - Screening: M-CHAT at 18 + 24 months (pediatrician). If positive: formal diagnostic evaluation (ADOS-2 — gold standard)
- **Intervention**: early intensive behavioral intervention (EIBI) — ABA (Applied Behavior Analysis) most evidence. BUT: ABA controversial (autistic self-advocates say harmful — seeks to suppress autistic behavior, cause trauma). Need: acceptance + accommodations + teaching skills not enforcing "normal." Person-centered, strengths-based approaches preferred by self-advocates. Speech therapy, OT (sensory integration, fine motor), social skills training
  - Early intervention (ages 2-5) = best outcomes (neuroplasticity). Structured teaching (TEACCH). Tablet-based communication (PECS — Picture Exchange Communication System; AAC — augmentative + alternative communication for non/minimally verbal). No effective medication for core autism symptoms (no "ASD drug"). Treat comorbidities: irritability (risperidone, aripiprazole — FDA approved for irritability in autism), ADHD, anxiety
- **Adolescent transition**: puberty can increase challenges (sensory issues, rigid behaviors, social anxiety). Benefits of learning self-advocacy + identity as autistic person. Consider ASD as identity (neurodiversity) vs disability (deficit model). Many autistic teens have anxiety + depression + school refusal

### Anxiety in Children
- **Separation anxiety**: excessive distress when away from parent (>4 weeks). Refuses to go to school, sleep alone, be apart. Peaks 7-9 years. Treatment: CBT (gradual exposure, parent involvement), SSRIs if severe. School refusal: graded return plan
- **Generalized anxiety**: excessive worry about many things (school, health, family, world, catastrophes). Need for reassurance. Physical (headaches, stomachaches). Treatment: CBT, SSRIs (sertraline, fluoxetine first-line)
- **Social anxiety**: fear of social situations, performance, being judged. Can look like tantrums/avoidance in younger children. Peaks early adolescence
- **Panic disorder**: rare before adolescence. Episodic attacks of terror + physical symptoms. Secondary avoidance (panic → avoid triggers → maintain fear). Adolescents may miss school + isolate
  - Scared of death: during panic, may ask "Am I dying?" — reassure, not alarm
- **Selective mutism**: speaks at home but not at school/public. Typically age 3-5 at onset. Treatment: behavioral (graded exposure + reward for speaking), SSRIs. Not "just shy"

### Depression in Children
- **Signs**: may be irritable, not sad. Withdrawal, loss of interest, decline in grades, sleep/appetite changes, fatigue, physical complaints (headache, stomachache). Hopelessness, self-criticism. Suicidal ideation (ask directly). Children may not say "I'm depressed" — they act withdrawn, angry, or stop doing things they used to enjoy
- **Treatment**: psychotherapy (CBT, IPT-A — interpersonal therapy for adolescents). SSRI (fluoxetine/Prozac first-line — FDA approved for 8+. Escitalopram/Lexapro for 12+. Sertraline/Zoloft for 6+ for OCD). Black box warning: SSRIs increase suicidal thoughts in children/adolescents (rare, monitor closely, most benefit outweighs risk for moderate-severe depression). Combination therapy (CBT + SSRI = best results — TADS study)
  - TADS (Treatment for Adolescents with Depression Study): combination fluoxetine + CBT best, CBT alone moderate, fluoxetine alone good, placebo least

### Suicide Risk in Youth
- **Risk factors**: previous attempt, mood disorder, substance use, family history, LGBTQ+ (especially if not supported), bullying, access to means, impulsivity, social isolation. Warning signs: giving away possessions, withdrawal, talking about death/not wanting to be here, sudden calm after depression (means made decision)
- **Assessment**: ask directly ("Are you having thoughts of killing yourself?"). DOES NOT increase risk (contrary to myth — asking reduces risk). Assess: thoughts, plan, means, intent, timeline, previous attempts, protective factors (parents, connection, future orientation)
- **Safety plan**: remove means (lock up firearms, meds). Supervision (not alone). Emergency contacts (988 crisis line, parents, therapist). Coping strategies, reasons for living

## Parenting & Discipline
- **Time-out**: 1 min per year of age (max 5 min). For specific behaviors. Remove attention (not scary or isolating). After: brief explanation + talk about appropriate behavior. Use sparingly
- **Natural consequences**: let child experience natural outcome of choice (if don't wear coat, you'll be cold). Appropriate for safety issues (don't run into street). Set limit + empathize
- **Positive reinforcement**: catch child being good (specific praise + attention). Token economy for desired behaviors (chart with stickers → reward). 5 positive interactions : 1 negative (Gottman ratio). Connection before correction
- **Ignore extinction burst**: if you stop responding to tantrum, it may get WORSE before it gets better (extinction burst). Consistency critical — giving in after burst teaches persistence. Safer: for safe behaviors (not hitting, not dangerous, not self-harm)
