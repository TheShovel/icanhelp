# Heart Disease

Coronary artery disease, heart failure, arrhythmias, and prevention.

## Coronary Artery Disease (CAD)
- **Pathophysiology**: Atherosclerosis — plaque buildup (cholesterol, inflammation, smooth muscle) in coronary arteries → narrowing → reduced blood flow to heart muscle
- **Stable angina**: Chest pain/pressure with exertion, relieved by rest or nitroglycerin — predictable pattern
- **Acute Coronary Syndrome**: Unstable angina (new/worsening, at rest) → NSTEMI (partial blockage) → STEMI (complete blockage, ST elevation on EKG)
- **Heart attack symptoms**: Chest pressure/pain (may radiate to left arm, jaw, back), shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat, fatigue — women more likely to have atypical symptoms (fatigue, indigestion, back pain)
- **Diagnostic tests**: EKG, troponin (blood test — elevated with heart muscle damage), stress test (exercise or chemical), coronary angiogram, coronary CT angiogram

## Heart Failure
- **HFrEF (reduced ejection fraction)**: <40% EF — weakened pumping. Treated with guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT)
- **HFpEF (preserved ejection fraction)**: ≥50% EF — stiff heart, can't relax/fill properly. More common in women, elderly, hypertension. Fewer medication options
- **Symptoms**: Shortness of breath (especially lying flat — orthopnea, waking up gasping — PND), edema (ankles, legs, abdomen), fatigue, rapid weight gain from fluid retention
- **Treatments**: ACE inhibitors/ARBs, beta-blockers, diuretics (furosemide/Lasix for fluid), SGLT2 inhibitors (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin — benefit even non-diabetic, especially HFrEF), spironolactone (mineralocorticoid antagonist), sacubitril/valsartan (Entresto) for HFrEF

## Arrhythmias
- **Atrial Fibrillation (AFib)**: Most common — irregularly irregular rhythm, increased stroke risk (CHA2DS2-VASc score determines anticoagulation). Rate control (beta blockers, CCB) or rhythm control (antiarrhythmics, cardioversion, ablation)
- **Ventricular tachycardia (VT)**: Fast, life-threatening — from heart's lower chambers. Requires cardioversion if unstable. ICD (implantable cardioverter-defibrillator) for prevention
- **Bradyarrhythmias**: Slow heart rate <60 — if symptomatic (syncope, fatigue) → pacemaker
- **Symptoms**: Palpitations (fluttering, skipping beats), syncope (fainting), dizziness, chest discomfort

## Risk Factors
- **Modifiable**: High blood pressure (leading risk factor), high LDL cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity, physical inactivity, unhealthy diet (high saturated fat, trans fat, sodium, added sugar), excessive alcohol, stress
- **Non-modifiable**: Age (increases with age), male sex (women catch up after menopause), family history, genetics
- **Lipid panel**: Total cholesterol, LDL ("bad" — target <100 mg/dL, <70 for high risk), HDL ("good" — >40 men, >50 women), triglycerides (<150)
- **Metabolic syndrome**: 3+ of: abdominal obesity, high triglycerides, low HDL, high blood pressure, high fasting glucose — greatly increases CAD risk

## Prevention
- **Diet**: Mediterranean diet — olive oil, fish, nuts, vegetables, whole grains, limited red meat, moderate wine. Reduce saturated fat (<7% calories), eliminate trans fat (partially hydrogenated oils), limit sodium (<2300mg/day)
- **Exercise**: 150 min/week moderate aerobic (brisk walking, cycling, swimming) OR 75 min vigorous + strength training 2x/week
- **Blood pressure**: Target <130/80 — DASH diet (low sodium, high potassium), exercise, weight loss, meds if needed
- **Aspirin**: Low-dose (81mg) for secondary prevention only (already had heart attack/stroke) — no longer recommended for primary prevention in most (bleeding risk outweighs benefit)
- **Statins**: Lower LDL, reduce inflammation — atorvastatin (Lipitor), rosuvastatin (Crestor). Primary prevention if 10-year risk >7.5% (ASCVD risk calculator) or LDL >190. Common side effects: muscle aches (usually no CK elevation — try different statin, alternate day dosing)

## Warning Signs & Emergency
- **Stroke (FAST)**: Face drooping (smile — one side doesn't move), Arm weakness (one arm drifts down), Speech difficulty (slurred/strange), Time to call 911
- **What to do for chest pain**: Sit down, chew 325mg aspirin (if not allergic), take nitroglycerin if prescribed, call 911 — don't drive yourself
- **CPR**: 100-120 compressions/min (beat of "Stayin' Alive"), 2 inches deep, hands-only (no breaths for adults — better survival)
- **AED**: Turn on, follow voice prompts — pads on bare chest (upper right, lower left), delivers shock only if needed (analyzes rhythm)
