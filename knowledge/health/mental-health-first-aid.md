# Mental Health First Aid & Wellbeing

## Overview
Mental health first aid is the help offered to a person developing a mental health problem, experiencing a worsening of an existing mental health problem, or in a mental health crisis. Just like physical first aid, it's given until appropriate professional help is received or the crisis resolves.

## Mental Health First Aid Action Plan (ALGEE)

### A - Approach, Assess, Assist
- **Approach** the person in a safe, private, non-judgmental way
- **Assess** for risk of suicide or harm
- **Assist** with any immediate safety concerns

### L - Listen Non-Judgmentally
- Give full attention
- Use verbal and non-verbal cues (nodding, eye contact)
- Reflect back: "It sounds like you're feeling..."
- Avoid interrupting, judging, or giving advice immediately
- Validate: "That sounds really difficult"

### G - Give Support & Information
- Offer empathy and hope
- Provide information about mental health
- Normalize: "Many people experience this"
- Share resources (helplines, websites, apps)

### E - Encourage Professional Help
- Discuss options: GP, therapist, psychiatrist, EAP
- Offer to help find providers
- Normalize seeking help: "It's like seeing a doctor for physical health"

### E - Encourage Other Supports
- Self-help strategies
- Peer support groups
- Family/friend support
- Lifestyle factors (sleep, exercise, nutrition)

## Recognizing Common Mental Health Problems

### Depression
**Signs (2+ weeks)**:
- Persistent low mood or irritability
- Loss of interest/pleasure in activities
- Significant weight/appetite changes
- Sleep disturbances (insomnia/hypersomnia)
- Fatigue/loss of energy
- Feelings of worthlessness/guilt
- Difficulty concentrating
- Thoughts of death/suicide

**Approach**: "I've noticed you haven't been yourself lately. I'm concerned about you."

### Anxiety Disorders
**Generalized Anxiety**: Excessive worry about multiple things, difficulty controlling worry, restlessness, fatigue, concentration problems, irritability, muscle tension, sleep disturbance

**Panic Attacks**: Sudden surge of intense fear peaking within minutes:
- Palpitations, sweating, trembling
- Shortness of breath, choking sensation
- Chest pain, nausea, dizziness
- Fear of losing control or dying

**Social Anxiety**: Fear of social situations, scrutiny, embarrassment

**Approach for panic**: Stay calm, breathe with them, reassure it will pass, don't leave them alone

### Bipolar Disorder
**Manic Episode** (1+ week):
- Elevated/expansive/irritable mood
- Inflated self-esteem/grandiosity
- Decreased need for sleep
- Pressured speech, racing thoughts
- Distractibility
- Increased goal-directed activity
- Risky behavior (spending, sex, investments)

**Depressive Episode**: Same as major depression

**Approach**: During mania, reduce stimulation, ensure safety, encourage professional help

### Psychosis
**Signs**:
- Hallucinations (hearing/seeing things others don't)
- Delusions (fixed false beliefs)
- Disorganized thinking/speech
- Disorganized/abnormal behavior
- Negative symptoms (flat affect, withdrawal)

**Approach**: Stay calm, don't argue with delusions, ensure safety, encourage professional help

### Substance Use Disorders
**Signs**:
- Using more/longer than intended
- Unable to cut down
- Cravings
- Tolerance/withdrawal
- Neglecting responsibilities
- Continued use despite problems

**Approach**: Express concern without judgment, offer help finding treatment

### Eating Disorders
**Anorexia**: Restriction, fear of weight gain, body image disturbance
**Bulimia**: Binge eating + compensatory behaviors (purging, exercise)
**Binge Eating**: Recurrent binges without compensation

**Approach**: Express concern for health (not weight), encourage professional help

## Crisis Situations

### Suicidal Ideation
**Warning Signs**:
- Talking about death/suicide
- Feeling hopeless, trapped, burden
- Increased substance use
- Withdrawal, mood changes
- Giving away possessions
- Saying goodbye

**Direct Questions** (ASK DIRECTLY):
- "Are you thinking about suicide?"
- "Do you have a plan?"
- "Do you have the means?"
- "Have you tried before?"

**If YES to plan + means**:
1. **Don't leave them alone**
2. **Remove means** (pills, weapons, etc.)
3. **Call crisis line**: 988 (US), local emergency
4. **Stay with them** until help arrives
5. **Follow up** after crisis

### Non-Suicidal Self-Injury (NSSI)
- Cutting, burning, scratching, hitting
- Usually for emotional regulation, not suicide
- **Approach**: Calm, non-judgmental, focus on underlying distress, encourage professional help

### Panic Attack First Aid
1. Stay with them, remain calm
2. Move to quieter space if possible
2. Encourage slow breathing: "Breathe with me - in for 4, hold 4, out 6"
3. Reassure: "This will pass, you're safe"
4. Don't dismiss: "It's just anxiety"
5. After: Encourage professional help if recurring

### Trauma/PTSD Crisis
- Flashbacks, dissociation, extreme distress
- **Grounding**: "5-4-3-2-1" - name 5 things you see, 4 feel, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste
- Don't touch without permission
- Encourage professional trauma therapy

## Communication Skills

### Helpful Responses
| Instead of... | Try... |
|---------------|--------|
| "Cheer up" | "I'm here for you" |
| "It could be worse" | "That sounds really hard" |
| "Just snap out of it" | "How can I support you?" |
| "I know how you feel" | "I can't imagine how hard this is" |
| "Have you tried...?" | "What has helped before?" |
| "You shouldn't feel that way" | "Your feelings are valid" |

### Active Listening
- **SOLER**: Sit Squarely, Open posture, Lean forward, Eye contact, Relax
- Paraphrase: "So what you're saying is..."
- Clarify: "Can you tell me more about...?"
- Summarize: "So far you've said..."
- Validate emotions: "It makes sense you'd feel that way"

### Motivational Interviewing (Brief)
- **OARS**: Open questions, Affirmations, Reflections, Summaries
- Roll with resistance: Don't argue
- Develop discrepancy: "How does this fit with your goals?"
- Support self-efficacy: "You've handled hard things before"

## Self-Care for First Aiders

### Before Helping
- Assess your own capacity
- Set boundaries: "I can listen for 20 minutes"
- Know your limits - you're not a therapist

### After Helping
- Debrief with colleague/supervisor (confidentially)
- Practice self-care: walk, breathe, hydrate
- Watch for vicarious trauma signs:
  - Intrusive thoughts about their situation
  - Avoidance, hypervigilance
  - Sleep disturbances
  - Emotional numbing

### Building Resilience
- Regular supervision/debriefing
- Maintain work-life boundaries
- Peer support network
- Personal therapy if needed

## Special Populations

### Youth (12-18)
- **Signs**: School decline, withdrawal, irritability, sleep changes, risk-taking
- **Approach**: Side-by-side activities (walking, driving) often easier than face-to-face
- **Resources**: Teen Line (800-852-8336), Crisis Text Line, Trevor Project (LGBTQ+)

### Older Adults
- **Signs**: Often somatic complaints, cognitive complaints, withdrawal
- **Risk**: Highest suicide rate in males 75+
- **Approach**: Respect autonomy, address isolation, medical comorbidity

### LGBTQ+
- **Higher risk**: Discrimination, family rejection, minority stress
- **Affirming approach**: Use correct name/pronouns, know specific resources
- **Trevor Project**: 866-488-7386 (LGBTQ+ youth)

### Veterans
- **Veterans Crisis Line**: 988 then 1
- **Vet Centers**: Free counseling for combat veterans
- **VA Mental Health**: VA.gov/mental-health

### Cultural Considerations
- Mental health stigma varies widely
- Some cultures express distress somatically
- Family involvement may be expected/required
- Spiritual/religious beliefs may be central
- Use interpreters, not family members for translation

## Building a Mentally Healthy Environment

### Workplace
- **Psychological safety**: OK to ask questions, admit mistakes
- **Manager training**: Mental health awareness, accommodation duty
- **EAP promotion**: Regular reminders, easy access
- **Flexible work**: Autonomy reduces burnout
- **Peer support**: Mental health champions/allies

### School/University
- **Mental health literacy**: Curriculum integration
- **Counseling access**: Low barriers, diverse providers
- **Peer programs**: Active Minds, peer counseling
- **Crisis protocols**: Clear, practiced, communicated

### Community
- **Mental Health First Aid training**: 8-hour certification
- **Peer support groups**: NAMI Connection, DBSA
- **Crisis intervention teams (CIT)**: Police + mental health
- **Warm lines**: Non-crisis peer support

## Self-Help Strategies (Psychoeducation)

### For Low Mood/Depression
- **Behavioral Activation**: Schedule small pleasant/achievement activities
- **Thought Records**: Identify → Challenge → Replace negative thoughts
- **Sleep Hygiene**: Consistent schedule, no screens before bed
- **Exercise**: 30 min moderate, 3-5x/week (start small)

### For Anxiety/Worry
- **Worry Time**: Schedule 15 min/day for worry, postpone rest
- **Cognitive Restructuring**: What's the evidence? Alternative view?
- **Exposure**: Gradual approach to feared situations
- **Relaxation**: Progressive muscle relaxation, diaphragmatic breathing

### For Stress/Burnout
- **Boundary Setting**: Learn to say no, delegate
- **Recovery Rituals**: Transition rituals (work → home)
- **Micro-breaks**: 5 min every hour, 15 min every 2 hours
- **Values Alignment**: Connect tasks to personal values

## Certification & Training

### Mental Health First Aid (MHFA)
- **Standard**: 8-hour course (adult/youth)
- **Instructor**: 5-day certification
- **Providers**: National Council for Mental Wellbeing (US), MHFA International
- **Specializations**: Higher Ed, Public Safety, Fire/EMS, Military/Veterans, Older Adults

### Other Trainings
- **QPR** (Question, Persuade, Refer) - 1-2 hour suicide prevention
- **ASIST** (Applied Suicide Intervention Skills Training) - 2-day
- **safeTALK** - 3-hour suicide alertness
- **CIT** (Crisis Intervention Team) - 40-hour for law enforcement

## Legal & Ethical Considerations

### Duty to Warn/Protect (Tarasoff)
- Varies by jurisdiction
- Generally: If specific threat to identifiable victim → duty to protect
- Consult supervisor/legal

### Confidentiality Limits
- Imminent harm to self/others
- Child/elder abuse (mandated reporting)
- Court order
- Varies by role (therapist vs. layperson)

### Good Samaritan Laws
- Most jurisdictions protect lay rescuers acting in good faith
- Don't abandon once you start helping
- Act within your training

### Knowledge
- Local crisis resources (walk-in centers, mobile crisis)

## Follow-Up After Crisis

### Immediate (24-48 hrs)
- Check in: "Thinking of you, how are you?"
- Ensure professional appointment scheduled
- Safety plan review

### Short-term (1-2 weeks)
- Regular brief check-ins
- Encourage treatment engagement
- Monitor for deterioration

### Long-term (1-3 months)
- Periodic check-ins
- Celebrate progress
- Relapse prevention planning
