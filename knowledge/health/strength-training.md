# Physical Fitness & Strength Training

## Strength Training Principles
- **Progressive overload**: to get stronger, you must continuously challenge your muscles beyond their current capacity — increase weight, reps, sets, or decrease rest time (frequency variable: train each muscle group 2x/week for optimal growth)
  - If you can do 3x12 with a weight easily, increase weight (by 5 lbs or 2.5 kg). If you can't finish 3x8, lower weight
  - Double progression: stay at weight until you can hit all reps all sets, then add weight and drop reps, repeat cycle
- **Compound vs isolation**: compound = multiple joints/muscles (squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, pull-up, row — most efficient, more total work, best for building overall strength + muscle). Isolation = single joint (bicep curl, tricep extension, leg curl, calf raise — to target specific weak points after compounds)
- **Rep ranges**: 1-5 = strength (heavy, nervous system adaptation). 6-12 = hypertrophy (muscle growth, "bodybuilding" range). 15-30 = muscular endurance (lighter, more pump). ALL ranges build muscle — the TOTAL VOLUME (sets × reps × weight) matters more than exact rep ranges
  - The "hypertrophy rep range" (6-12) works especially well for time-efficient muscle growth, but all rep ranges build muscle when taken close to failure
- **Rest between sets**: 3-5 min for strength (heavy compounds). 1-2 min for hypertrophy. 30-60 sec for endurance
- **Time under tension**: total time muscle under load. 30-60 seconds per set for hypertrophy (tempo: slow eccentrics 2-3 seconds). Controlled reps > fast/cheated
- **Form**: always prioritize FORM over weight — bad form causes injury, not gains. Record yourself, watch for: spine neutral (not rounded under load), knees tracking over toes (not valgus collapsing), full ROM (partial reps build partial gains; deep stretch at bottom + near full lockout top). If you can't control the weight, it's too heavy

## Major Exercises by Muscle Group

### Push (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps)
- **Bench press**: flat (mid-chest, overall pec mass). Incline (upper chest). Decline (lower chest, often overrated — flat+incline hits lower chest anyway). Dumbbell > barbell for ROM + safer (shoulders)
  - Form: feet planted, arch upper back (scapulae retracted — "bend the bar"), touch mid-chest, press up and slightly back. Don't bounce, full lockout. Elbows 45° from body (not 90° — shoulder impingement risk)
- **Overhead press** (strict press): standing (full core engagement) > seated. Barbell (traps more involved, heavier) vs dumbbells (independent, more ROM). The "king of upper body strength" — pressing power from floor up
  - Form: grip just outside shoulders, bar at clavicle, slight lean back, press head through (move head back to clear bar path), lockout overhead. Lower to front of shoulders
- **Push-ups**: bodyweight, always available. Variations: wide (more chest), close/triceps, decline (upper chest), pike (shoulders), one-arm (advanced, core)
  - Can be main pushing movement: 15-30 reps at chest, incline/decline for variation
- **Dips**: chest (lean forward, elbows out) or triceps (upright, elbows back). Add weight (dip belt) when +15 reps easy. Great push movement — "upper body squat" (straight bar or V bar - whichever better for shoulder health)
- **Lateral raise**: side delts — use lighter weight, focus on raising with ELBOWS not hands, slight lean forward, pinkies up at top (like pouring pitcher). Don't swing — strict
- **Tricep pushdowns**: cable, straight bar or rope — lean forward, keep elbows fixed at sides, press down, squeeze at bottom. Overhead extension (cable or dumbbell) = long head. Kickbacks = isolation

### Pull (Back, Biceps, Rear Delts)
- **Deadlift**: hips at bar height, bar over midfoot, back flat (not rounded — pull shoulder blades together, brace core, breathe), drive through heels, bar stays in contact with legs, lockout hips + knees. "World's strongest man" lifts: 1,104lbs/500kg by Hafþór Júlíus Björnsson (2020). The deadlift: hardest but best overall strength/back builder
  - Conventional (feet shoulder-width, hands inside knees, uses hips more) vs Sumo (feet wide, hands inside, arms between knees, uses legs more). For taller people, sumo may be more comfortable
  - Don't lift max weight without perfect form. Use straps for grip when grip fails before back
- **Pull-ups/Chin-ups**: overhand/pronated (lats, wider back. "Queen of back exercises") vs underhand/supinated (biceps, lower lats). Can't do one? Negatives (jump up, lower 5 secs) or assisted machine/bands. Do: full hang at bottom, chin over bar at top, no kipping (unless CrossFit)
  - Grease the groove: do sets throughout day (not all at once — accumulate volume). Builds reps fast through daily practice
- **Barbell row**: bent over back ~45° (not parallel — higher more lower back strain), bar to belly button, squeeze lats, elbows drive back. Pendlay row: from floor each rep, no momentum — strict row. Yates row: underhand grip, more upright, to lower abs
- **Lat pulldown**: substitute for pull-up. Focus on squeezing lats, driving elbows down, chest up. Wide grip (lats) vs V-bar (focus mid-back/lower lats)
- **Cable/seated row**: target mid-back, rhomboids, rear delts. Squeeze shoulder blades together, keep chest up, elbows close. Straight bar or V-handle

### Legs
- **Squat**: feet shoulder-width (slightly wider), toes slightly out, back straight — sit back into hips as if reaching back to sit on a chair vs straight down (knee travel). Goal: hips below parallel to ground in bottom. "King of all exercises" (works quads, glutes, hamstrings, lower back, core, overall mass builder and functional strength)
  - High bar (bar on traps/upper back — more upright — targets quads) vs Low bar (bar on rear delts — leans forward — targets glutes/hams, allows heavier weight)
  - Safety squat bar alternative if shoulder mobility limits. Always practice bracing (val salva maneuver = hold breath, brace abdominals all around)
- **Leg press**: heavy quad + glute builder. Feet low on platform = more quads, high = glutes + hams. Don't lock knees at top! Go through full ROM. Can load heavier than squat (2-3x body weight eventual)
- **Romanian deadlift (RDL)**: hamstring + glute focus. Hinge at hips, slight bend in knees, slide bar down legs until hamstrings stretch (mid-shin), squeeze glutes to come up. Hip hinge, not lower back rounding — back straight as you go down
- **Lunges**: walking (each step = lunge + bring back foot forward). Reverse (easier on knees — more glute activation than forward lunges). Bulgarian split squat (rear foot on bench — killer for glutes + quads + balance). Elevate front foot for deeper stretch
- **Leg curl**: hamstring isolation (lying vs seated). Seated hits short head differently. Hamstrings are 1/3 of leg mass, often neglected

## Core
- **Plank**: full body tension (squeeze glutes + quads, brace abs). Not just holding — actively press floor away. Side plank: obliques
- **Hanging leg raise**: raise legs to parallel (abs) or to bar (advanced). Core + hip flexor. Knee raises if can't do full leg raises (same movement, easier)
- **Cable/Pallof press**: anti-rotation — core strength to resist rotation. Cable from side, hold at chest, press straight forward, fight rotation. Hold 3 sec, repeat
- **Farmer's walk**: pick up heavy weights (dumbbells, farmer handles), walk 30-50 ft. No losing upright posture (slight lean). Full body: grip, core, traps, glutes, legs
- **Ab wheel**: roll out fully extended, pull back with abs. Start at knees (kneeling), advanced: standing. Don't collapse back (keep hips tucked)
  - Six-pack? Visible abs = low body fat first (men ~12% body fat, women ~18-20% for visibility — varies genetically). Training builds muscle size. You can have strong abs without them being visible

## Splits & Programs
- **Push/Pull/Legs (PPL)**: 3-6 days/week. Push (chest/shoulders/triceps), Pull (back/biceps/rear delts), Legs. Flexible, good for intermediates
  - Sample PPL: Day 1 Push (bench press, OHP, incline, lateral raise, triceps pushdown). Day 2 Pull (deadlifts, pull-ups, rows, curls, rear delt fly). Day 3 Legs (squat, RDL, lunges, leg curls, calf raise). 3 days on, 1 off, repeat
- **Upper/Lower**: 4 days/week (Upper A + Lower A + rest + Upper B + Lower B + rest + rest). Good for intermediates with focus on some parts each upper/lower day
- **Full body**: 3x/week — good for beginners (frequency high, learns every lift every workout). Compound focus
- **Starting Strength**: beginner barbell program — squat 3×5, bench 3×5, deadlift 1×5, OHP 3×5, power clean. Linear progression (add 5 lbs every workout until can't — deload — advance to intermediate). Old school, proven
- **Stronglifts 5×5**: similar, 5 reps × 5 sets for squat/bench/row/OHP. Deadlift 1×5. 3 days/week. Simple but heavy volume on lower back

## Programming
- **Warm-up**: 5-10 min (light cardio + dynamic stretches + mobility drills + practice the movement light sets). Not static stretching! Dynamic (leg swings, hip circles, arm circles, walking lunges, cat-cow, banded glute activation for squat, deadlift). Then 1-2 warm-up sets per exercise at 40-70% of working weight
- **Sets per exercise**: 3-5 for big compounds, 2-3 for accessories. Total weekly volume per muscle: 10-20 working sets (spread over 2-4 sessions). Beginners: 6-9 sets total, compound focus, 2x/week each
- **Progression**: add weight when you hit target reps for ALL sets. Microload (2.5 lbs/1.25 kg plates) for upper body lifts (big jumps harder for bench presses). Double progression for accessories
- **Deload**: every 4-8 weeks — reduce weight/volume 40-60% for a week. Allows recovery, reduces injury risk, returns you stronger. Deload week = lighter load, maintain frequency

## Nutrition for Muscle
- **Protein**: 1.6-2.2g per kg of body weight (0.7-1g per lb). BCAAs not needed (whole protein already provides). Spread throughout day (20-40g per meal). 2g/kg is plenty; more = extra mostly oxidization (no damage but also no extra muscle past ~2.2 g/kg)
- **Calories**: surplus (200-500 cal/day above maintenance) for muscle gain. Surplus: slow gaining: +100-200/day. Recomposition: +100-200 above maintenance for slow gain + small deficit at other meals. Faster gaining: +300-500/day. Don't dirty bulk (gain too fast = mostly fat). Body fat gain is partly genetic
- **Meal timing**: not critical (except pre/post workout — protein within 2-3 hours of training is beneficial but total daily protein/protein timing across day is primary driver). Total daily protein + calories matter more than timing "window"
- **Pre-workout**: carbs + protein 1-3 hours before. Caffeine (200-400mg) optional — can improve performance, focus
