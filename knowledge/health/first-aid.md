# First Aid - Emergency Response

## Overview
First aid is immediate care given to an injured or ill person until professional medical help arrives. The goal: preserve life, prevent worsening, promote recovery.

## Primary Assessment (DRSABCD)

### D - Danger
- Check for danger to yourself, bystanders, victim
- Make scene safe before approaching
- If unsafe, call emergency services, don't enter

### R - Response
- Tap shoulders, shout "Are you okay?"
- Check for response (verbal, movement, eye opening)
- Unresponsive = medical emergency

### S - Send for Help
- Call emergency services (911/999/112)
- Provide: location, what happened, number of victims, condition
- Send someone for AED if available
- Stay on line with dispatcher

### A - Airway
- Open airway: head-tilt, chin-lift
- If trauma suspected: jaw thrust (no head tilt)
- Look, listen, feel for breathing (max 10 seconds)

### B - Breathing
- Normal breathing? → Recovery position, monitor
- Not breathing / gasping (agonal) → Start CPR
- Give 2 rescue breaths if trained (30:2 ratio)

### C - CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)
```
Adult CPR (puberty+):
1. 30 chest compressions
   - Center of chest, lower half of sternum
   - Depth: 2-2.4 inches (5-6 cm)
   - Rate: 100-120/min
   - Allow full recoil
2. 2 rescue breaths (if trained)
   - Head tilt-chin lift, pinch nose
   - Breath over 1 second, chest rise
3. Repeat cycles

Child CPR (1-puberty):
- 1 or 2 hands, 1/3 chest depth (~2 inches)
- 30:2 (1 rescuer), 15:2 (2 rescuers)

Infant CPR (<1 year):
- 2 fingers, 1/3 chest depth (~1.5 inches)
- 30:2 (1 rescuer), 15:2 (2 rescuers)
- Cover mouth AND nose for breaths
```

### D - Defibrillation (AED)
- Turn on AED, follow voice prompts
- Expose chest, dry if wet
- Apply pads (diagram on pads)
- Stand clear during analysis
- Shock if advised → immediate CPR 2 min → reanalyze
- No shock advised → CPR 2 min → reanalyze

## Life-Threatening Conditions

### Cardiac Arrest
- Unresponsive, not breathing normally
- **Action**: CPR + AED immediately
- **Survival drops 7-10% per minute without CPR**

### Heart Attack (Myocardial Infarction)
- Chest pain/pressure (may radiate to arm, jaw, back)
- Shortness of breath, nausea, cold sweat, lightheadedness
- Women: atypical symptoms (fatigue, indigestion, back pain)
- **Action**: Call 911, chew 325mg aspirin (if not allergic), rest, monitor

### Stroke (BE FAST)
```
B - Balance: Sudden loss
E - Eyes: Vision changes
F - Face: Drooping (ask to smile)
A - Arm: Weakness (raise both arms)
S - Speech: Slurred/strange
T - Time: Call 911 immediately

Note time of onset (tPA window: 3-4.5 hours)
```

### Severe Bleeding
```
1. Direct pressure with clean cloth/gauze
2. If soaks through: ADD more layers, DON'T remove
3. Elevate limb above heart (if no fracture)
4. Pressure points (femoral, brachial) if needed
5. Tourniquet (last resort, life over limb)
   - 2-3 inches above wound (not joint)
   - Tighten until bleeding stops
   - Note time applied
   - DO NOT REMOVE
```

### Choking (Conscious Adult/Child)
```
1. Ask "Are you choking?" - universal sign: hands to throat
2. Encourage coughing
3. Back blows: 5 firm blows between shoulder blades
4. Abdominal thrusts (Heimlich): 5 inward/upward thrusts
5. Alternate until object expelled or unconscious

If unconscious: Start CPR (chest compressions may dislodge)
Check mouth between cycles, remove visible object only
```

### Infant Choking (<1 year)
```
1. 5 back blows (face down on forearm, head lower)
2. 5 chest thrusts (2 fingers, center of chest)
3. Repeat until object out or unconscious
```

### Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction)
```
Signs: Difficulty breathing, swelling (face/lips/tongue), hives, 
       wheezing, vomiting, dizziness, sense of doom

Action:
1. Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) IMMEDIATELY
   - Thigh, through clothing, hold 3-10 seconds
2. Call 911
3. Second dose in 5-15 min if no improvement
4. Lie flat, legs raised (unless breathing difficulty)
5. Monitor breathing, start CPR if needed
```

## Common Injuries

### Burns
| Degree | Signs | Action |
|--------|-------|--------|
| 1st (Superficial) | Red, painful, no blisters | Cool water 10-20 min, aloe, OTC pain med |
| 2nd (Partial) | Blisters, red, swollen, painful | Cool water 10-20 min, don't pop blisters, loose sterile dressing, seek care if >3" or face/hands/genitals |
| 3rd (Full) | White/charred, leathery, NO pain (nerves destroyed) | Call 911, DON'T remove stuck clothing, cover loosely, treat for shock |

**Chemical burns**: Flush with water 20+ minutes, remove contaminated clothing
**Electrical burns**: Ensure power off, call 911 (internal damage), treat entry/exit wounds

### Fractures & Sprains
```
RICE Method:
Rest: Immobilize, don't use
Ice: 20 min on/off (barrier), first 24-48 hrs
Compression: Elastic bandage (not too tight)
Elevation: Above heart level

Signs of fracture: Deformity, bone protruding, grating, unable to move
Splint: Immobilize joint above AND below fracture
Check circulation (capillary refill <2 sec) before/after splinting
```

### Head Injury
```
Call 911 if:
- Loss of consciousness (even brief)
- Confusion, memory loss
- Vomiting (>1 time)
- Severe headache worsening
- Seizure
- Unequal pupils
- Clear fluid from ears/nose
- Neck pain/stiffness

DO NOT move unless in danger
Monitor breathing, be ready for CPR
```

### Eye Injuries
```
Foreign object: Don't rub, flush with water/saline
Chemical: Flush 20+ minutes, call 911
Blunt trauma: Cold compress, seek care
Penetrating: DON'T remove object, stabilize, call 911
```

### Seizures
```
DO: Protect from injury, cushion head, time it, turn on side after
DON'T: Restrain, put anything in mouth, give water/food
Call 911 if: >5 min, multiple, first seizure, injured, pregnant, diabetic, in water
```

### Diabetic Emergencies
```
Hypoglycemia (Low sugar - more common emergency):
- Shaky, sweaty, confused, hungry, irritable
- Conscious: 15g fast carbs (juice, glucose tabs, soda) → recheck 15 min
- Unconscious: Glucagon IM/IN, call 911

Hyperglycemia (High sugar):
- Thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision
- Call 911 if confused, vomiting, fruity breath (DKA)
```

### Heat Illness
| Condition | Signs | Action |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Heat Cramps | Muscle spasms, sweating | Rest, cool, electrolytes |
| Heat Exhaustion | Heavy sweat, weakness, nausea, headache, cool/clammy skin | Cool place, fluids, cool compresses, monitor |
| Heat Stroke | Hot/dry skin, confusion, seizure, >104°F | **911**, cool aggressively (ice bath, wet sheets, fan) |

### Cold Injuries
```
Frostbite: White/waxy skin, numbness
- Warm gradually (98-105°F water), no rubbing, no refreezing

Hypothermia: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, drowsy
- Remove wet clothes, warm center first (chest, neck, head, groin)
- Warm drinks if conscious, call 911
```

## Poisoning
```
Call Poison Control: 1-800-222-1222 (US)

DO: Identify substance, amount, time, weight/age
DON'T: Induce vomiting (unless directed), give anything by mouth if unconscious

Carbon Monoxide: Headache, dizziness, nausea, cherry-red skin
- Get to fresh air, call 911, 100% oxygen
```

## Special Situations

### Pregnancy
- CPR: Hand position slightly higher on sternum
- Recovery position: Left side (prevents vena cava compression)
- AED: Safe to use, place pads avoiding breast tissue

### Drowning
- Rescue breathing can start in water if safe
- 5 initial rescue breaths before compressions
- All near-drowning victims need medical evaluation (secondary drowning risk)

### Electrical Injury
- Ensure power OFF before touching
- Call 911 (cardiac arrhythmia risk, internal burns)
- Treat burns, monitor for cardiac arrest

## First Aid Kit Essentials

### Basic (Home/Car)
- Adhesive bandages (various sizes)
- Sterile gauze pads (2x2, 4x4)
- Roller gauze (2", 3")
- Adhesive tape
- Antiseptic wipes/solution
- Antibiotic ointment
- Hydrocortisone cream
- Pain relievers (acetaminophen, ibuprofen)
- Antihistamine (diphenhydramine)
- Tweezers, scissors
- Instant cold pack
- Elastic bandage (ACE)
- Triangle bandage (sling)
- Non-latex gloves (2+ pairs)
- CPR face shield/mask
- Emergency blanket
- First aid manual

### Advanced (Workplace/Outdoor)
- Tourniquet (CAT/SOF-T)
- Hemostatic gauze (QuickClot)
- Chest seal (HyFin)
- SAM splint
- Trauma shears
- Nasal/oral airways
- Bag-valve mask
- Glucose gel
- Epinephrine auto-injector (if prescribed)
- Burn gel/dressing
- Eye wash
- Snake bite kit (if applicable)

## Legal Considerations

### Good Samaritan Laws
- Protects lay rescuers acting in good faith
- Must act within training level
- No gross negligence
- Varies by jurisdiction

### Consent
- **Expressed**: Conscious adult agrees
- **Implied**: Unconscious, minor without guardian, confused
- **Refusal**: Competent adult can refuse (document)

## Quick Reference Card (Print & Carry)
```
EMERGENCY ACTION STEPS:
1. CHECK scene safety
2. CHECK person (tap, shout)
3. CALL 911 / get AED
4. CARE: CAB (Compressions, Airway, Breathing)

CPR RATES:
Adult: 30:2, 2-2.4" deep, 100-120/min
Child: 30:2 (1 rescuer), 15:2 (2 rescuer), 2" deep
Infant: 30:2 (1), 15:2 (2), 1.5" deep

CHOKING: 5 back blows → 5 abdominal thrusts
INFANT CHOKING: 5 back blows → 5 chest thrusts

BLEEDING: Direct pressure → Elevate → Tourniquet
BURNS: Cool 10-20 min → Cover loosely
SHOCK: Lay flat, legs up 12", keep warm
SEIZURE: Protect head, time it, side after
STROKE: BE FAST → Time = Brain
```
