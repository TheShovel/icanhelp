# Pregnancy & Childbirth

## Prenatal Care Schedule
- **First trimester (wk 0-13)**: first appointment 8-12 wks (confirm pregnancy, ultrasound, bloodwork: CBC, blood type, Rh, STI screen, rubella, varicella, hepatitis B, HIV, A1C/diabetes screen, thyroid). Genetic screening (NIPT: cell-free fetal DNA, optional, detects trisomies 21/18/13, sex chromosome aneuploidies — high accuracy >99% for T21 done at 10+ wks). NT (nuchal translucency ultrasound 11-14 wks). Dating ultrasound confirms due date. Morning sickness (nausea/vomiting: eat small frequent meals, ginger, avoid triggers, B6 25mg + doxylamine Unisom 12.5mg at night = prescription Diclegis). Hyperemesis gravidarum (severe vomiting, weight loss, hospitalization for fluids/nutrition)
- **Second trimester (wk 14-27)**: monthly visits. Anatomy scan (~20 wks: detailed ultrasound of fetal organs, limbs, brain, heart, spine, kidneys). Quad screen (maternal blood: AFP, hCG, estriol, inhibin A — screens for spina bifida, Down syndrome, trisomy 18). Glucose screening (24-28 wks: 1hr glucose challenge test, if fail → 3hr OGTT for gestational diabetes. 75g glucose load for some clinics). Fetal movement: felt ~18-22 wks (quickening), become regular by 28 wks (should feel 10 movements by 2 hours when focused)
- **Third trimester (wk 28-40)**: visits every 2 wks (28-36), then weekly (36-birth). Group B strep test (vaginal/rectal swab @ 36-37 wks — if positive: IV antibiotics during labor to prevent newborn infection). Gestational diabetes screen (24-28 wks). RhoGAM for Rh-negative mother at 28 wks. Cervical checks: rarely needed until labor? Not routinely done (can cause false labor/bleeding). Option to discuss from 36-37 wks

## Common Discomforts & Self-Help
- **Heartburn (GERD)**: progesterone relaxes lower esophageal sphincter. Small frequent meals, no spicy/greasy/acidic foods, don't lie down after eating (wait 2-3 hrs). Tums, Pepcid (famotidine) category B. Prop up with pillows
- **Back pain**: strengthen glutes + abs before pregnancy. Cat-cow, pelvic tilts, side-lying exercises. Support belt. Prenatal massage (certified prenatal masseuse). Avoid heavy lifting; don't bend from waist (squat). Good posture: knees slightly soft, ribs over hips, chin tucked
- **Leg cramps**: likely magnesium deficiency. Stretch calf before bed. Stay hydrated. Magnesium glycinate 200-400mg. Increase water intake. If severe ongoing: rule out DVT (unilateral, with swelling/redness)
- **Hemorrhoids**: constipation pressure + progesterone slows digestion. High fiber, water, stool softeners (Colace docusate — safe). Witch hazel pads, sitz baths, Anus HC 1% OTC cream (short term)
- **Round ligament pain**: sharp groin pain (stretching ligament attaching uterus to pelvis). Sudden movement or cough. Change position slowly, support belly, side-lying rest. Sharp + severe + with bleeding/vomiting = rule out ovarian torsion
- **Pelvic girdle pain (PGP)**: pain at pubic symphysis + SI joints. Avoid single-leg motions (stairs, walking legs apart). Pelvic floor PT — can make a huge difference with exercises + bracing belt

## Labor & Delivery
- **Signs of labor**: regular contractions (5-1-1: 5 min apart, 1 min long, 1 hour). Water breaking (amniotic fluid — clear/hint of pink/blood-tinged) — call provider (may be clear or meconium stained). Bloody show (mucus plug — brownish/pink, can mean hours-several days). Back pain + pressure + cramping
  - Prodromal/ false labor: irregular contractions, no cervical change, often at night. Dehydration or a full bladder sometimes mimic prelabor. Rest, walk, hydrate to assess
- **Stages of labor**: Stage 1 latent (0-6 cm, early labor), active (6-10 cm, strong regular contractions, faster dilation). Stage 2 (full dilation to birth, pushing: active maternal effort + contraction urges). Stage 3 (placenta delivery: typically 5-30 min after birth, given oxytocin/methergine to contract uterus). Stage 4 (recovery: 1-2 hours after — skin-to-skin, monitor bleeding. Fundal massage every 15 min to prevent/treat PPH)
- **Pain management**: unmedicated (counterpressure, water immersion/tub, nitrous oxide, movement, positions, TENS). Epidural (most effective: local anesthetic + opioid injected into epidural space of spine. Can reduce sensation, may prolong 2nd stage slightly increases risk of instrumental delivery, but mother can still feel pressure to push). IV pain meds (fentanyl or Stadol — less effective, crosses placenta, baby may be sleepy at birth)
  - Epidural process: anesthesiologist places catheter in lower back, takes 10-20 min, works within 15 min, can be bolused continuously. Can still move legs, walk with assistance. "Walking epidural" lasts several hours, adjustable level
- **Induction**: medical or elective (≥39 wks). Methods: cervical ripening (prostaglandin gel/misoprostol, cervical balloon/Foley). Pitocin (synthetic oxytocin IV — labor stimulation). Artificial rupture of membranes (AROM/amniotomy)
- **C-section**: 32% of US births. Reasons: failure to progress, fetal distress, breech, placenta previa, multiples, previous C-section (can attempt VBAC/VBAC: vaginal birth after cesarean, success rate 60-80%, risk of uterine rupture 0.5-1% with VBAC vs scarred uterus). C-section recovery: 4-6 weeks no heavy lifting, careful with abdominal pressure. Major abdominal surgery (not minor). Incision: horizontal (Pfannenstiel/bikini line, common) vs vertical (emergency — classic)

## Postpartum
- **Vaginal recovery**: bleeding/lochia (rubra red → serosa pink → alba yellow, 4-6 wks). Perineal care (ice packs, witch hazel pads, sitz bath, peri bottle). Stool softeners to prevent constipation (first postpartum BM often feels scary, but softeners + fiber + water help)
- **C-section recovery**: wound care (keep clean + dry, staples/sutures removed 5-10 days post). No heavy lifting (>10 lbs baby). Pain management (ibuprofen + Tylenol alternating, around the clock). Incision is low transverse, takes 6-8 weeks for full healing of deeper layers
- **Breastfeeding**: colostrum first 2-5 days (thin, yellow, immune-rich, small colostrum amounts normal — baby stomach size of cherry first day). Transition to mature milk ~3-5 days. Latch: mouth wide, flanged lips, more areola below nipple than above. Painful nursing ≠ normal (see lactation consultant — tongue tie? poor latch? mastitis? thrush?)
  - Engorgement (day 3-5): full, hard, hot breasts. Treat: feed/express to soften, cold packs after feeding (vasoconstriction reduces swelling. Before feeding: warmth + hand express if too full for latch)
  - Mastitis: red, hot, wedge-shaped area on breast, fever, flu-like symptoms. Treatment: continue breastfeeding or pump (emptying infected breast helps). Antibiotics (dicloxacillin, cephalexin — safe with breastfeeding). If not improving in 24 hours: abscess needs drainage. Commonly from incomplete milk removal + bacteria from baby's mouth/skin (Staph aureus)
- **Postpartum mental health**: "baby blues" (50-80% — mood swings, crying, anxiety, first 2 weeks, resolves on own). Postpartum depression (10-15% — 2+ weeks, depressed mood, loss of interest, can't sleep when baby sleeps, anxiety, guilt, bonding difficulty). Postpartum anxiety (panic attacks, constant worry). Postpartum psychosis (rare 0.1-0.2% — hallucinations, delusions, confusion, severe insomnia — EMERGENCY, immediate medical evaluation). PPD: treat with therapy + medication (SSRIs safe in breastfeeding: sertraline/Zoloft, fluoxetine best studied? Actually paroxetine less preferred for breastfeeding). Postpartum psychosis: medical emergency — medication (lithium, antipsychotics, benzodiazepines) + possible hospitalization
- **Postpartum checkup**: 6-week visit (examine uterus size, incisions, mood screening, birth control options, bleeding/healing). Pelvic floor PT (for incontinence, pelvic pain, prolapse — not routinely offered but HIGHLY recommended, especially after vaginal delivery)
