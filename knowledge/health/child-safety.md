# Child Safety & First Aid

## Childproofing by Age
- **Newborn (0-6 months)**: sleep safety — back to sleep (SIDS reduction 50%+ since Back to Sleep campaign began 1994), firm mattress, no blankets/pillows/bumpers/toys in crib, room share (not bed share) 6 months minimum. Car seat: rear-facing (newborn insert, harness at or below shoulders), 45° recline. Water temp <120°F (scald prevention, lower water heater setting or anti-scald valve). Crib slats no more than 2-3/8" apart, no drop-side cribs (banned in US 2011)
- **Crawling (6-12 months)**: outlet covers (tamper-resistant outlets are best — built-in shutters. Removeable plastic outlet covers are choking hazard). Corner protectors (sharp furniture corners). Anchor furniture (tip-over: dressers, TVs, bookshelves — 2-3 deaths/year per 10 million households, but happens. Anchor brackets, $5-10 each, into stud). Stair gates (top AND bottom, hardware mount at top (more secure) pressure mount at bottom, no accordion gates (head entrapment). Move all cords up (blinds cords = strangulation hazard), remove tablecloths (baby pulls = everything falls)
- **Toddler (1-3 years)**: stove knob covers, oven lock, refrigerator lock, cabinet locks (magnetic = best, no visible hardware). Toilet lock (drowning in buckets/toilets — toddler top-heavy can fall in, can't get out). Window guards (screens prevent bugs NOT falls — window stops prevent opening >4", guards with quick release for fire escape). Poison: all meds/cleaners/laundry pods/vitamins locked up ABOVE counter level (child resistant ≠ child proof). Choking: no whole grapes (quarter lengthwise), hot dogs (slice lengthwise then crosswise), hard candy, nuts, popcorn, marshmallows, raw carrots (until ~age 4)
- **Preschool (3-5 years)**: bike helmet (every time!), teach swim lessons (but never trust alone), pedestrian safety (hold hands crossing street), playground safety (supervision, age-appropriate equipment, surface: rubber mulch/wood chips > concrete. Falls from 6 ft = concrete = serious head injury)
- **School age (6-12)**: internet safety (parental controls, no personal info online, stranger danger is outdated: focus on behavior not strangers. Most child abuse is by known adults), crossing streets, stranger awareness (safe adults: teachers, police, trusted neighbors. Not: "don't talk to strangers" (child may not ask for help when lost). Instead: "ask people behind a counter/with a badge for help")

## Common Child Emergencies
- **Choking**: infant (<1 year): support head/neck, 5 back blows (between shoulder blades, face-down on forearm), 5 chest thrusts (2 fingers, center of chest, just below nipple line, 1.5" deep). Alternate. DO NOT: finger sweep (pushes object deeper), hold upside down
  - Child >1 year: Heimlich maneuver (stand behind, fist above navel, thrust inward/upward)
- **Fever**: >100.4°F rectal in infant <3 months = CALL DOCTOR IMMEDIATELY (emergency — risk of serious bacterial infection). 3-6 months with fever = call doctor same day. >6 months: monitor, treat discomfort (not number). Fever is body's immune response — treating fever doesn't speed recovery, just comfort. Medicate for comfort, not just number. Warning signs: lethargic (difficult to wake), not drinking, inconsolable, difficulty breathing, rash, stiff neck, seizure
- **Febrile seizure**: 6 months-5 years, temperature spike triggers seizure — 5% of children. First aid: time it, clear area, position on side (recovery position — seizure vomit clear from airway vs back). Don't restrain, NOTHING in mouth. Call 911 if >5 min, first seizure, difficulty breathing, turning blue. Usually harmless — doesn't cause brain damage. After: doctor same day
- **Burns**: first/(small second-degree: cool running water 10-15 min (NOT ice — causes further damage), aloe, loosely wrapped. NOT: butter, toothpaste, egg white (folk remedies = infection risk). Severe burn >3 inches/ or deep (white/charred) = seek emergency care
  - Scald prevention: test bath water (elbow test), turn pot handles inward on stove, no hot drinks near baby/in lap (most common baby scald: pulled hot drink from table/edge)
- **Head injury**: fall from changing table/bed (common) — if child alert, crying, normal behavior = monitor 24 hours. Warning signs: ANY loss of consciousness, vomiting >2 times, severe headache, unequal pupils, confusion/sleepiness (not just tired, can't wake), seizure, weakness/numbness, clear fluid from nose/ears. When in doubt: ER
- **Drowning**: SECOND leading cause of death in children 1-4 (after birth defects). Silent! (not like movies — no splashing or yelling — head low, mouth at water level, vertical body, no arm waving). CPSS: Supervision constant (no distractions — phone, reading, socializing), barriers (4-sided fence with self-closing gate around pool, door alarms/alarms for pool/hot tub). Learn CPR
  - Dry drowning (laryngospasm = airway closes): child inhales water, vocal cords spasm, water doesn't enter lungs but no oxygen either — happens quickly. Symptoms: breathing difficulty, coughing, chest retractions, confusion. Secondary drowning (hours later): water in lungs causes inflammation. Seek medical if any water aspiration + ANY breathing trouble. But these are VERY rare — pool fencing is #1 prevention

## Car Seat Safety
- **Rear-facing**: stay as long as possible (until max height/weight for seat — at least 2 years, ideally 3-4). 5x safer than forward-facing (head/neck/spine protection — crash disperses across whole back vs neck strain forward). Legs touching back seat is fine (leg injuries very rare, flex naturally)
  - Tightness: harness should not pinch (flat, no twists). Chest clip at armpit level. Tight: CAN'T pinch harness at collarbone
- **Forward-facing** (harness): transition only when child exceeds rear-facing limits. Use top tether (third anchor point reduces head movement 4-6", critical!). Harness at or above shoulder height
- **Booster seat**: 4'9" AND 8-12 years old — whichever comes last. Seatbelt should fit properly (lap belt on thighs, not belly. Shoulder belt across center of shoulder, not neck — shoulder belt position adjuster. Typically 4'9" height and 80-120 lbs before booster not needed)
  - NEVER seatbelt only for child under 4'9" (seatbelt positioned over abdomen in crash = internal organ damage + spinal cord. Booster positions belt correctly)
- **Front seat**: 13+ years recommended (airbag injury to smaller passengers. 13 is approximate based on size). Up to this point: back seat is 50% safer (increased survivability in crash regardless of airbag)

## Poison Prevention
- **Call Poison Control** 1-800-222-1222 (US). Save number in phone NOW before emergency
  - Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed (aspiration risk, caustic substances cause more damage on way up)
- **Common child poisonings**: medications (grandparents' purse/bags most common — pills left on counter, in daily pill organizer), cleaning products, laundry pods/packets (concentrated, very toxic, sweet colors = attractive. Tide Pod challenge!), button batteries (lithium coin cell = emergency — can burn through esophagus in 2 hours. If ingested: go to ER immediately, X-ray to verify. DON'T wait for symptoms). Magnets (multiple = attract across intestines = perforation/obstruction = emergency surgery)
- **Symptoms**: unexplained drooling, burns around mouth, strange breath odor, nausea/vomiting, sleepiness/unconsciousness, seizure, difficulty breathing
- **Prevention**: store all meds/cleaners up/ locked (not under sink), keep in original containers, no food containers for chemicals, dispose of old meds (take-back programs), keep purses/bags up high out of reach

## Other Safety
- **Falls**: most common injury (changing tables, stairs, baby walkers [banned in Canada — babies fall downstairs, reach higher items]. NEVER use baby walker with wheels! Stationary entertainer = fine). Use gates at stairs top+bottom
- **Window falls**: screens DON'T prevent falls — install window stops (prevent opening >4"), window guards. Screens hold only ~1 lb
- **Button batteries & magnets**: check all toys/remotes/electronics — secure battery compartment with screwdriver if possible. Store spare batteries out of reach. Swallowed = emergency room
- **Toy safety**: age labels (choking hazard warnings for small parts, small balls, balloons). Check for recalls at CPSC.gov. Avoid certain toys: old painted lead issues (vintage toys), used car seats (expired, missing parts, not crash-tested after date)
- **Outdoor play**: sunscreen (SPF 30+, zinc-based better, reapply 2 hours/swim/sweat. Mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide/titanium dioxide) vs chemical (avobenzone, oxybenzone), mineral better for sensitive skin), tick checks (after playing in woods/grass). Playground surfaces: soft (rubber mulch, wood chips > gravel > concrete/asphalt)
