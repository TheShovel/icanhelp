# CBT (Cognitive Behavioral Therapy)

Evidence-based therapy for depression, anxiety, and other conditions.

## Core Principles
- **Cognitive triangle**: Thoughts → Feelings → Behaviors — interconnected. Changing one affects the others. Focus on thoughts (cognitive) and actions (behavioral)
- **Situation → Automatic thoughts → Reaction**: Not events themselves but interpretation of events determines emotional/behavioral response. "The event doesn't cause the feeling — the thought about the event does"
- **Here-and-now focus**: Address current problems and practical solutions, not past causes. "Why" less important than "what to do now"
- **Time-limited, goal-oriented**: Usually 12-20 sessions. Specific goals (reduce panic attacks from daily to weekly, return to work, stop avoiding crowds). Homework between sessions essential
- **Collaborative empiricism**: Therapist and client work as team. Client is expert on own experience. Therapist guides with CBT tools. Test beliefs like scientific hypotheses

## Cognitive Distortions (Common Thinking Traps)
- **All-or-nothing thinking**: See things in black/white categories. "If I'm not perfect, I failed." One slip → complete failure. Magnitude: shades of gray
- **Catastrophizing**: Assume worst case will happen. "If I make a mistake, I'll get fired and become homeless." Probability overestimation + severity overestimation. Ask: "What's the most likely outcome?"
- **Mind reading**: Assume others are thinking negatively about you without evidence. "They think I'm boring/stupid/awkward." Check: "Do I have actual evidence? Could there be other explanations?"
- **Fortune telling**: Predict the future negatively. "The meeting will be terrible, and I'll embarrass myself." Same as mind reading but about future. Test: has this prediction happened before?
- **Should statements**: Rigid rules about self/others/world. "I should be more productive." "They shouldn't be so slow." Creates guilt, anger. Replace "should" with "would like" or "prefer"
- **Overgeneralization**: One negative event → universal pattern. "I failed this test → I'm bad at everything." Words "always", "never", "everything" are red flags
- **Emotional reasoning**: Feelings = facts. "I feel anxious → the situation is dangerous." "I feel stupid → I am stupid." Feelings reflect thoughts, not objective reality
- **Discounting the positive**: Dismiss achievements as luck, others' kindness. "Anyone could have done that." "They were just being nice." Actively practice accepting positive without minimizing
- **Labeling**: Attaching global negative label. "I'm a loser" instead of "I made a mistake." "They're an idiot" instead of "They did something thoughtless." Labels = fixed identity, not behavior
- **Personalization**: Take responsibility for events outside your control. "My child got in trouble because I'm a bad parent." "It's my fault they're upset." Distinguish responsibility vs blame

## Cognitive Restructuring (Thought Challenging)
- **Identify automatic thought**: "What went through my mind just before I felt [emotion]?" Write it down
- **Examine the evidence**: "What facts support this thought? What facts don't?" Not feelings — specific, concrete evidence
- **Alternative thinking**: "Is there another way to see this?" "What would I tell a friend in this situation?" Generate balanced perspective
- **Decatastrophizing**: If the worst really happened, what would you do? How would you cope? You're more resilient than you think
- **Socratic questions**: 
  - "Am I 100% sure this thought is true?"
  - "What's a more balanced way to see this?"
  - "What would I tell a friend with this thought?"
  - "What's the worst that could happen? How would I cope?"
  - "What's the most likely outcome?"
- **Behavioral experiments**: Test beliefs with real-world experiments — "I believe I'll panic and need to leave the store" → go to store, measure actual anxiety

## Behavioral Activation
- **For depression**: Depression → decreased activity → more depression. Break cycle by scheduling activities, even without motivation. "Do first, motivation follows"
- **Activity schedule**: Hour-by-hour plan of daily activities. Rate each for mastery (sense of accomplishment) and pleasure (enjoyment). Identify patterns: which activities improve mood?
- **Graded task assignment**: Break overwhelming tasks into tiny steps. Task: clean room → Step 1: set timer 5 min, pick up trash. Success builds momentum
- **Pleasant event scheduling**: Schedule activities that bring joy — even 10-15 min daily. Counteracts depression's narrowing of experience
- **TRAP/TRAC**: Trigger → Response → Avoidance Pattern → Alternative Coping. Identify avoidance, replace with approach coping

## Exposure Therapy
- **For anxiety**: Gradual, repeated exposure to feared situations/ sensations/ thoughts. Fear decreases through habituation (anxiety naturally decreases if you stay in situation long enough)
- **Fear hierarchy**: Least → most feared situations. Rate each 0-100 (SUDS — Subjective Units of Distress Scale). Start at bottom (SUDS 30-40)
- **Stay long enough**: Must stay until anxiety decreases (typically 10-30 min). If you leave during peak, fear maintained (reinforces safety behavior)
- **No safety behaviors**: Avoid subtle avoidance (clenching fists, distraction, checking exits). Full engagement required for learning "I can handle this"
- **Types**: In vivo (real-life situations), imaginal (trauma memories, feared scenarios — for PTSD), interoceptive (physical sensations — for panic attacks), response prevention (for OCD — resist compulsion after exposure)

## Core Beliefs & Schemas
- **Deepest level of cognition**: Rules and assumptions formed in childhood. "I'm unlovable", "I'm incompetent", "I'm worthless", "Others can't be trusted". Automatic thoughts grow from these beliefs
- **Downward arrow technique**: Identify automatic thought → "If that were true, what would it mean about you?" → repeat until core belief emerges
- **Core belief worksheet**: Collect evidence FOR and AGAINST the core belief. Include concrete examples. Rate belief strength (0-100%) before and after
- **Self-compassion integration**: Treat yourself with kindness when core belief activated. "Of course I feel worthless sometimes — my past taught me that. But I'm learning a new way."
