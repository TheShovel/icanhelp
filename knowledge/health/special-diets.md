# Nutrition for Special Diets

## Low-Carb & Ketogenic
- **Macros**: <50g net carbs/day (standard low carb). <20g for keto (strict, designed to induce ketosis — body burns fat for fuel, produces ketones from liver). 70-80% fat, 15-25% protein, 5-10% carbs. Ketosis: blood ketones 0.5-3.0 mM. Not weight loss only — evidence for epilepsy (very strong), T2D (strong), some neurodegenerative
  - Net carbs = total carbs - fiber - sugar alcohols (erythritol, xylitol, allulose — subtract half of sugar alcohols for maltitol, which spikes blood sugar like half sugar). Fiber: doesn't count toward carbs because humans don't digest. Subtract fully
  - Eat: meat, fish, eggs, cheese, butter, cream, olive oil, avocado, nuts, seeds, non-starchy veggies (leafy greens, broccoli, cauliflower, zucchini, peppers, asparagus). Avoid: bread, pasta, rice, grains, fruit (except berries small amounts), sugar, potatoes, beans, milk
  - Supplements: electrolytes (sodium 3-5g, potassium 1-3g, magnesium 300-500mg). Keto "flu" first week: headache, lethargy, brain fog, irritability — from electrolyte depletion (sodium + potassium loss with insulin drop). Prevent: salt food, drink broth, electrolyte powder
  - Not for: kidney disease, pregnancy, type 1 diabetes (unless with close medical monitoring), those with eating disorder history

## Diabetic Diet
- **Goal**: stable blood sugar (avoid spikes/drops). Carbs: consistent amount per meal (45-60g typical per meal for men, 30-45g for women — varies widely by insulin sensitivity, size, activity). Types: low glycemic index (GI <55) = slower absorption. GI ≠ GL: glycemic load = GI × (g carbs per serving)/100 = more relevant. GI 100 = pure glucose. Beans + lentils (low GI), whole grains > refined
  - Counting: 1 carb serving = 15g. Read labels: total carbs not sugar. Insulin: match carb intake to insulin dose. For type 2: may reduce/stop meds with weight loss + exercise under medical supervision
- **Plate method**: 1/2 non-starchy vegetables, 1/4 protein, 1/4 carbs (or double non-starchy vegetables, use 1/4 carb 1/4 lean protein + fat). Non-starchy: greens, broccoli, cauliflower, tomatoes, peppers, cucumber, mushrooms, zucchini, green beans
- **CGM** (Continuous Glucose Monitor): for type 1 (decreases A1c, fewer lows, less worry) and type 2 (understand how foods affect individual glucose response). Time-in-range: 70-180 mg/dL = target for many (goal >70% in range). Dawn phenomenon: morning glucose rises due to cortisol + growth hormone release

## Heart-Healthy (Mediterranean/DASH)
- **Mediterranean**: EVOO (extra virgin olive oil) as main fat, fish 2x/week, moderate dairy, red meat limited (few times/month), lots of veg + legumes + whole grains + nuts + fruit. Red wine in moderation (1 glass/day). Proven in PREDIMED + Lyon Heart trials: 30% reduction in CVD + mortality
- **DASH**: Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension: low sodium (<2300 mg/day, ideally 1500), high potassium (fruit + veg: 4,700mg), calcium, magnesium. Emphasize fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean protein, low-fat dairy, nuts, seeds. Proven to lower BP as much as some meds (systolic -8 to -14 mmHg)
- **Reduce**: saturated fat (<7-10% calories), trans fat (minimize), added sugar (<25g women, <36g men), sodium (<2300mg). Increase: omega-3 (fatty fish 2x/week, flax, chia), fiber (25-30g/day), plant sterols (nuts, seeds, avocado, whole grains). Limit alcohol

## Gluten-Free & Celiac
- **Gluten sources**: wheat (bread, pasta, baked goods, seitan, couscous, farro, bulgur, pita, naan, panko, breading, many sauces + soups + soy sauce (unless tamari wheat-free), beer (unless gluten-free). Barley (malt, malt vinegar, beer). Rye (bread, whiskey)
- **Gluten-free grains**: rice, corn, quinoa, oats (certified gluten-free — oats are often cross-contaminated from wheat in growing/processing: 50% of celiacs react to standard oats). Millet, sorghum, teff, buckwheat, amaranth, cassava/yucca, almond flour, coconut flour, chickpea flour. GF flours combine Xanthan gum (binder, 1/2-1 tsp per cup flour) for baking elasticity
- **Cross-contamination**: separate toaster, cutting board, colander, colander, cooking surfaces, butter/jam jars (double dipping crumbs), shared kitchen, eating out (verify restaurant protocols: many have GF menus, Celiac-specific kitchen protocols). Oats: choose purity protocol (no field cross-contamination, no shared combine) rather than mechanical (sorted). Dedicated GF facility vs shared
- **Hidden gluten**: soy sauce, marinades, salad dressings, soups, broth, bouillon, malt, beer, licorice, communion wafers, some medications (binders). Imitation crab, processed meats (sausages). Many personal care products (gluten in lotions/lip balms not absorbed but celiacs avoid for safety). Some medications — check with prescriber

## Vegan & Plant-Based
- **Protein**: legumes (lentils, chickpeas, black beans, edamame, kidney beans, pinto beans, tofu, tempeh, seitan (wheat gluten). Sources per meal: 15-25g protein. Tofu: 10-20g per 1/2 cup, tempeh 15g per 1/2 cup, lentils 9g per 1/2 cup cooked. Protein quality: soy is complete (contains all essential amino acids). Eat variety: legumes + grains, complement (not need same meal)
- **Iron**: non-heme (less absorbed — pair with vitamin C). Lentils, spinach, fortified cereals, beans. Vitamin C + iron-rich food together = 3x absorption. Best plant iron sources: fortified cereal, lentils, tofu, kidney beans, chickpeas. RDA: 1.8x higher for vegetarians (33mg women)
- **Vitamin B12**: ONLY from animal sources or supplements. Tofu? Not unless fortified. Vegan sources: nutritional yeast (if fortified), plant milks (if fortified), cereals (if fortified), supplements (mandatory: 25-100mcg daily or 2000mcg once/week). CHOOSE: methylcobalamin or cyanocobalamin (both effective). Deficiency symptoms: fatigue, tingling hands/feet, memory loss, macrocytic anemia, irreversible nerve damage
- **Calcium**: fortified plant milks (300mg/cup), tofu set with calcium (200-300mg/1/2 cup), calcium-set tofu, kale, collard greens, broccoli (low oxalate = bioavailable), fortified OJ. RDA 1000mg for adults (1200mg for women 50+/70+)
- **Omega-3 DHA**: algae oil supplement (200-300mg DHA/day). ALA (flaxseed, chia, walnuts) converts poorly: 5-15% to EPA, less to DHA. DHA = key for brain, eye health. Algae oil (from microalgae) is the only direct plant source of DHA. Vegan omega-3 supplement: recommended for all vegans, especially pregnancy/breastfeeding
- **Zinc**: pumpkin seeds, chickpeas, cashews, fortified cereals. Phytates in whole grains bind zinc (soak/sprout to reduce). RDA for vegetarians: may need 50% more due to lower absorption
- **Iodine**: iodized salt, seaweed (sparingly — can be too high). DON'T rely on sea salt or pink salt (less iodine unless labeled). Seaweed (kelp, nori, dulse): test before depending, content varies widely. Vegan supplements often include iodine

## Elimination & Low-FODMAP
- **FODMAPs**: Fermentable Oligosaccharides (wheat, onion, garlic), Disaccharides (lactose), Monosaccharides (fructose — excess, honey, mango, apple), Polyols (stone fruits (naturally polyol — stone fruits contain sorbitol, stone = pit fruit. Apples, pears, stone fruits, avocado, mushrooms, cauliflower)
- **Elimination phase** (2-6 weeks): avoid all high-FODMAP foods. Then REINTRODUCE each FODMAP group one at a time over 3-4 days to determine tolerance. Challenge each type (fructose, lactose, fructans, GOS, mannitol, sorbitol) over 3 days with escalating doses
  - Common high FODMAP: wheat, onions, garlic, beans, lentils, apples, pears, watermelon, stone fruits, milk/yogurt, cashews, pistachios, mushrooms, cauliflower, honey, high fructose corn syrup. Low FODMAP: rice, oats, quinoa, potatoes, carrots, zucchini, spinach, bell peppers, tomatoes, bananas, blueberries, strawberries, oranges, lactose-free dairy, almond milk, eggs, meat, fish
  - Only for IBS (not general diet): does help 70% of IBS patients with symptoms. Dietitian-guided recommended. Not meant to be long-term — restrict only during elimination + reintroduction. Reintroduced foods individualize: find YOUR personal FODMAP tolerance (many tolerate some groups, not all)
