# Posture & Ergonomics

## Perfect Sitting Posture
- **Chair height**: feet flat on floor, knees at 90° (or slightly lower than hips). Thighs parallel to floor. If too low: hip angle <90°, slouching. If feet can't reach floor: footrest
- **Pelvis**: neutral (not tucked under/wheelchair or with excessive arch). Sit on sit bones (ischial tuberosities — the two bony points you can feel when you rock on your pelvic bones. If you feel pressure on your tailbone, you're sitting on it. Front of pelvis slightly higher than back.)
  - Lumbar support: small of back should contact chair back. If no built-in: small lumbar cushion or rolled towel. The natural lumbar curve (inward) needs support to prevent slumping
- **Shoulders**: relaxed (not elevated toward ears). Slightly back (shoulder blades retracted and down). Chest slightly open (imagine proud, not rigid) — opens up the ribcage enables better breathing
- **Head**: ears over shoulders (not forward). Chin slightly tucked (not jutting forward). Forward head posture = increases load on cervical spine (head weighs 10-12 lbs, each inch forward = 10 more lbs on neck = head in front of body center of gravity = recruiting more muscle to hold it up)
- **Arms**: elbows at 90-110°, wrists straight (not bent up/down, touching keyboard/mouse). Shoulders relaxed, elbows by sides. Wrist rest: for palming, not typing on. Use a split ergonomic keyboard if pain persists with pronated forearm. Mouse: close to keyboard, avoid reaching. Use a vertical mouse to keep forearm in neutral (palm orientation: "handshake" neutral position)

## Standing Posture
- **Plumb line ideal**: ear, shoulder, hip, knee, ankle — in vertical line (imaginary line connecting these: ear over shoulder, shoulder over hip, hip over knee). From side: ear over shoulder, shoulder over hip, hip over ankle. From back: head centered over spine, shoulders level, hips level, ankles even
- **Common misalignments**: forward head (ears in front of shoulders). Rounded shoulders (shoulders forward of ears). Anterior pelvic tilt (belly forward, lower back arched — tight hip flexors + weak glutes). Posterior pelvic tilt (tailbone tucked, lower back flat — tight hamstrings + weak glutes). Hyperextended knees (locked back). How to self-test: stand against wall, heels 2" from wall. Natural back curve should allow hand to fit between lower back + wall (one hand thickness). If too much space: anterior tilt. If no space: posterior tilt
- **Standing desk**: alternate between sitting + standing (30-60 min each). Start gradually (1-2 hours/day total standing, build over weeks). Anti-fatigue mat (soft, encourages micro-movements). One foot on small stool or low footrest can help reduce back strain
  - Monitor position: top of screen at or slightly below eye level, arm's length distance. For bifocal/ varifocal users: lower screen to read through lower part of glasses

## Desk Setup (Ergonomic)
- **Monitor**: top of screen at eye level (slightly below). Arm's length distance (18-25"). If dual monitors: primary screen directly in front of you, secondary at slight angle to side (turn head, not neck). Use external laptop stand + separate keyboard/mouse (laptop screen too low without stand)
- **Keyboard**: flat or negative tilt (front slightly lower than back) to keep wrists straight. Split keyboard (Microsoft Sculpt, Kinesis Freestyle, Logitech Ergo K860, or ZSA Moonlander) for shoulder + wrist comfort. Palm rest: use while not typing, not while actively typing (can put pressure on carpal tunnel)
- **Mouse**: at same height as keyboard, close to body. Ergonomic mouse or trackball or vertical mouse reduces forearm strain. Use keyboard shortcuts to reduce mouse use
- **Chair**: adjustable height, armrests (elbows at 90° with shoulders relaxed), lumbar support, seat depth (2-3" between back of knee + seat). If seat too long: pressure behind knees. If seat too short: less thigh support. Headrest for reclining

## Stretches & Exercises
- **Upper traps/neck**: ear to shoulder stretch (30 sec each side). Chin tucks (pull head back like making double chin, 5-10 sec). Side bend: opposite ear, over.
- **Chest stretch**: doorway stretch (arms at 90°, lean through doorway, feel stretch in chest). Corner stretch (both arms on doorframe, lean in). Hold 30 sec each
- **Lower back**: cat-cow on floor. Child's pose. Knee to chest (supine). Pelvic tilts (rock anterior to posterior — flatten back into floor). Standing back bend (hands on low back, lean back, look up slightly)
- **Hip flexors**: kneeling lunge stretch (quad stretch: kneeling lunge, push hips forward. Hold 30 sec each side). Pigeon stretch (glute/piriformis)
- **Glute activation**: glute bridges (supine, bend knees, lift hips, squeeze glutes. 15-20 reps). Clamshells (side-lying, lift top knee while keeping feet together, squeeze glute med). Helps prevent lower back pain by taking load off lumbar
- **Desk exercises**: every hour: standing up + walking 2 min. Shoulder rolls (5 each direction). Wrist circles + finger stretches. Chin tucks (good for forward head). Lumbar rolls (arching + flattening back while seated). Eye break: 20-20-20 rule (every 20 min, look at something 20 ft away for 20 seconds)

## Common Ergonomics Problems
- **Carpal tunnel syndrome**: median nerve compression at wrist-wrist. Numbness/tingling in thumb, index, middle, half of ring finger (hand diagram test: mark where symptoms are). Worse at night (flexed wrist sleep position). Wrist splint at night (neutral. 80% effective for nocturnal symptoms). Check: keyboard height (wrists extended), mouse (too far, causing wrist deviation). Treatment: NSAIDs, PT, steroid injection (often temporary relief), surgical release (open vs endoscopic, very effective)
- **De Quervain's tenosynovitis**: thumb side wrist pain (tenderness at radial styloid — base of thumb side). Finkelstein test: tuck thumb into fist, bend wrist down — sharp pain = positive. New baby + poor lifting form: new mom texting all day? Common: "baby wrist" from holding baby's head and lifting with thumbs, plus typing/texting. Treatment: thumb spica splint, NSAIDs, PT, steroid injection, surgery release
- **Low back pain from sitting**: weak glutes + hip flexors + tight hamstrings. Core: dead bug + bird dog + side plank. Standing desk (30 min per day increase into full work). Walking breaks (5 min every hour) — proven to reduce low back pain
- **Tech neck / text neck**: forward head posture from phone/laptop use. Head weight: 10-12 lbs neutral; 60 lbs at 60° forward tilt (like holding a 6-year-old child!) (studies estimate). Prevent: raise phone to eye level, use laptop stand, chin tucks, strengthen deep neck flexors. Symptoms: neck pain, upper back pain, tension headaches
