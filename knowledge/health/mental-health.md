# Mental Health & Wellbeing

## Sleep Hygiene
- Adults need 7-9 hours per night
- Consistent sleep/wake time (even weekends)
- No screens 30-60 min before bed (blue light suppresses melatonin)
- Keep bedroom cool (65-68°F), dark, quiet
- No caffeine after 2 PM
- Avoid alcohol before bed (disrupts REM sleep)
- If can't sleep after 20 min: get up, read in dim light, return when sleepy
- Morning sunlight exposure within 30 min of waking sets circadian rhythm

## Stress Management
- **Deep breathing**: 4-7-8 method — inhale 4 sec, hold 7, exhale 8 (repeat 4x)
- **Progressive muscle relaxation**: tense each muscle group 5 sec, release 10 sec, head to toes
- **Box breathing**: inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4 (military/first responder technique)
- **Grounding 5-4-3-2-1**: name 5 things you see, 4 you can touch, 3 you hear, 2 you smell, 1 you taste
- Regular exercise reduces stress hormones (cortisol) and increases endorphins
- Nature exposure: 10+ min outdoors reduces stress, improves mood
- Reducing caffeine can lower anxiety significantly

## Anxiety Management
- **Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)**: identify distorted thoughts, challenge them, replace with realistic ones
- Common distortions: catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking, mind reading, overgeneralization
- **Worry time**: schedule 15 min daily to worry (write worries, postpone until then)
- **Acceptance**: anxiety is uncomfortable but not dangerous — let it be there without fighting it
- Limit news/social media consumption (constant crisis triggers anxiety)
- Professional help: therapist (CBT, ACT, DBT), psychiatrist (medication if needed)

## Depression Management
- **Behavioral activation**: do activities even when you don't feel like it — mood follows action
- Break tasks into tiny steps (e.g., "put on socks" instead of "shower")
- Social connection: even brief interaction helps (call a friend, go to a store)
- Sunlight + exercise: 15-30 min morning walk is clinically effective
- **Antidepressants**: SSRIs (Prozac, Zoloft, Lexapro) take 4-6 weeks to work, common side effects initially
- Emergency: **988** Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (US), **111** (UK), text HOME to **741741**

## Healthy Habits Building
- **Atomic Habits**: make it obvious, attractive, easy, satisfying
- Habit stacking: "After [current habit], I will [new habit]"
- Two-minute rule: new habit should take under 2 minutes to start
- Don't break the chain: mark each day you do the habit on a calendar
- Focus on systems, not goals — identity-based habits ("I'm a healthy person" not "I want to lose weight")
- Missing once is fine; never miss twice

## Social Health
- Humans need social connection for mental health (loneliness is as harmful as smoking 15 cigs/day)
- Quality over quantity: 3-5 close relationships matter more than 50 acquaintances
- Join groups aligned with interests (book clubs, sports, volunteering, hobby groups)
- Set boundaries: it's OK to say no to social events when you need rest
- Conflict resolution: use "I feel" statements, listen to understand not to respond

## Emotional Regulation
- Name emotions to tame them ("I feel frustrated" activates prefrontal cortex)
- Emotions last 90 seconds physiologically (anything longer is thoughts re-triggering)
- Opposite action: if you want to withdraw (depression), reach out; if you want to attack (anger), be kind
- Journaling: 5 min free write about feelings — reduces rumination
- RAIN: Recognize, Allow, Investigate, Non-identify (with emotion)

## When to Seek Professional Help
- Symptoms last 2+ weeks and interfere with daily functioning
- Can't work, go to school, or maintain relationships
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide (call 988/emergency immediately)
- Substance use to cope
- Trauma/PTSD symptoms (flashbacks, hypervigilance, avoidance)
- Eating disorders (restriction, bingeing, purging)
- No "need to be sick enough" rule — if you're struggling, you deserve help
