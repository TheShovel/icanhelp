# Diabetes

Types, management, and complications of diabetes mellitus.

## Types
- **Type 1**: Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells — absolute insulin deficiency. Onset typically childhood/young adulthood. Requires lifelong insulin. 5-10% of diabetes cases
- **Type 2**: Progressive insulin resistance + relative insulin deficiency. Associated with obesity, sedentary lifestyle, genetics. 90-95% of cases. Managed with lifestyle, oral meds, eventually insulin
- **Gestational Diabetes**: Glucose intolerance during pregnancy — resolves after delivery but increases risk of T2DM later
- **LADA (Latent Autoimmune Diabetes in Adults)**: Slow-onset Type 1 — diagnosed >30, initially responds to oral meds but progresses to insulin dependence

## Diagnosis
- **Fasting plasma glucose** ≥126 mg/dL (7.0 mmol/L) after 8hr fast
- **HbA1c** ≥6.5% (48 mmol/mol) — reflects average glucose over 2-3 months
- **Oral glucose tolerance test** 2hr glucose ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L)
- **Random glucose** ≥200 mg/dL (11.1 mmol/L) with symptoms (polyuria, polydipsia, weight loss)
- **Prediabetes**: Fasting glucose 100-125 mg/dL, HbA1c 5.7-6.4%

## Medication
- **Metformin (Glucophage)**: First-line for T2DM — decreases hepatic glucose production, improves insulin sensitivity. GI side effects common (diarrhea, nausea). No hypoglycemia
- **Sulfonylureas (glipizide, glyburide)**: Increase insulin secretion — risk of hypoglycemia and weight gain
- **DPP-4 inhibitors (sitagliptin, linagliptin)**: Increase incretin levels, modest glucose reduction, weight neutral
- **SGLT2 inhibitors (canagliflozin, empagliflozin, dapagliflozin)**: Excrete glucose in urine — weight loss, heart/kidney protection regardless of glucose control. Risk of UTIs, DKA (even with normal glucose)
- **GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide/Ozempic, dulaglutide/Trulicity, tirzepatide/Mounjaro)**: Slow gastric emptying, increase insulin, suppress glucagon, promote weight loss. Cardiovascular benefit
- **Insulin**: Types — rapid-acting (lispro, aspart, glulisine — onset 5-15 min), short-acting (regular — 30 min), intermediate (NPH — 2hr), long-acting (glargine/Lantus, detemir/Levemir — ~24hr), ultra-long (degludec/Tresiba — >42hr)

## Blood Sugar Monitoring
- **Self-monitoring**: Fingerstick — before meals, at bedtime, when symptoms of hypoglycemia/hyperglycemia occur. For Type 1: 4-6x daily minimum
- **CGM (Continuous Glucose Monitor)**: Dexcom, Freestyle Libre — subcutaneous sensor reads glucose every 5 min. Trend arrows show direction/rate of change. Alarms for highs/lows
- **Target ranges**: Fasting <130 mg/dL, post-prandial (2hr after meals) <180 mg/dL, HbA1c <7% for most adults (individualized: older/frail may target 7.5-8.5%)

## Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)
- **Threshold**: <70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L)
- **Symptoms**: Sympathetic (shaking, sweating, palpitations, hunger, anxiety) → Neuroglycopenic (confusion, slurred speech, drowsiness, seizure, unconsciousness)
- **Treatment**: "Rule of 15" — consume 15g fast-acting carbs (4oz juice, 1 tbsp honey, 3 glucose tabs), recheck in 15 min, repeat if <70. If unconscious: glucagon injection (nasal or injectable)
- **Prevention**: Regular meals, adjust insulin before exercise, bedtime snack if glucose <150 before bed

## Hyperglycemia & DKA
- **Hyperglycemia**: >180-250 mg/dL — thirst, frequent urination, blurred vision, fatigue
- **DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis)**: Type 1 emergency — lack of insulin → fat breakdown → ketones → metabolic acidosis. Blood glucose >250, pH <7.3, ketones in blood/urine. Symptoms: nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, Kussmaul breathing (deep + rapid), fruity breath, altered mental status. Treatment: IV fluids, insulin drip, electrolyte replacement
- **HHS (Hyperosmolar Hyperglycemic State)**: Type 2 emergency — extreme hyperglycemia >600 mg/dL without significant ketones. Severe dehydration, altered mental status. More gradual onset than DKA but higher mortality

## Long-Term Complications
- **Microvascular**: Retinopathy (leading cause of blindness in working-age adults), nephropathy (leading cause of kidney failure), neuropathy (peripheral — numbness/pain in feet, autonomic — gastroparesis, ED, orthostatic hypotension)
- **Macrovascular**: CAD (2-4x increased risk), stroke, PAD
- **Foot complications**: Neuropathy + PAD → ulcers → infection → amputation. Prevent: daily foot inspection, appropriate footwear, podiatry

## Lifestyle Management
- **Carb counting**: Match insulin dose to carbs — 1 unit insulin per 10-15g carbs typical
- **Exercise**: Improves insulin sensitivity for 24-48 hours — check glucose before exercise, have fast-acting carbs available
- **Sick day rules**: Never skip insulin, check glucose every 2-4 hours, stay hydrated, check ketones if glucose >250, seek help if vomiting
- **Travel**: Prescriptions with generic names (different brand names abroad), carry insulin in carry-on (does not require refrigeration if <30 days), bring extra supplies
