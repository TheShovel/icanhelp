# Men's Health

## Reproductive Health
- **Testicular cancer**: most common cancer in men 15-35 — highly treatable if caught early
  - Self-exam monthly: roll each testicle between thumb and fingers, feel for lumps, swelling, or changes
  - Signs: lump, heaviness, dull ache, sudden fluid collection
- **Prostate health**: prostate enlarges with age (BPH — benign prostatic hyperplasia) — very common after 50
  - Symptoms: frequent urination (especially at night), weak stream, hesitation, dribbling
  - BPH treatment: alpha-blockers (tamsulosin), 5-alpha-reductase inhibitors (finasteride), minimally invasive procedures
- **Prostate cancer**: #2 cause of cancer death in men (after lung) — usually slow-growing
  - Screening: discuss with doctor at 50 (45 if Black or family history), PSA blood test + digital rectal exam
  - Most prostate cancer is slow-growing — active surveillance often recommended over immediate treatment
  - Symptoms often don't appear until advanced — screening matters
- **ED (erectile dysfunction)**: affects 30M+ US men — often an EARLY warning sign for heart disease
  - Causes: vascular (60-70%), neurological, hormonal (low testosterone), psychological, medication side effects
  - Lifestyle: exercise (especially cardio), weight loss, sleep, reduce alcohol, quit smoking
  - Treatment: PDE5 inhibitors (sildenafil/Viagra, tadalafil/Cialis — prescription), vacuum devices, injections, implants
- **Low testosterone**: symptoms — low libido, ED, fatigue, depression, loss of muscle, weight gain, irritability
  - Normal range: 300-1000 ng/dL (varies by lab)
  - Treatment: TRT (testosterone replacement therapy — gel, injection, pellets) — benefits vs risks (sleep apnea, prostate, heart) discuss with doctor
  - Lifestyle: weight loss (especially belly fat), strength training, sleep, reduce alcohol improve testosterone

## Common Conditions
- **Heart disease**: #1 killer of men (1 in 3)
  - Prevention: blood pressure control (<130/80), cholesterol management (LDL <100), no smoking, exercise 150 min/week, Mediterranean diet, limit alcohol
  - Symptoms: chest pain/pressure, shortness of breath, jaw/back pain, nausea, fatigue
  - Men often have "classic" heart attack symptoms (vs women who may have subtler signs)
- **Type 2 diabetes**: men at higher risk — prevention = weight management, exercise, reduced sugar
  - Untreated = heart disease, blindness, kidney failure, amputations, ED
- **Lung cancer**: #1 cancer killer — 90% linked to smoking. Quitting at any age extends life
- **Colorectal cancer**: #3 cancer — screening starting at 45 (colonoscopy every 10 years)
  - Signs: blood in stool, change in bowel habits, unexplained weight loss, abdominal pain
- **Skin cancer**: men over 50 have highest risk of melanoma — often on back (hard to see)
  - Daily SPF 30+, yearly skin check with dermatologist, watch for changing moles (ABCDE: Asymmetry, Border irregular, Color variation, Diameter >6mm, Evolving)

## Mental Health
- **Men die by suicide 3.5x more than women** (but women attempt more often)
  - Men use more lethal means (firearms)
  - Men are less likely to seek help for mental health (stigma, "tough it out" socialization)
- **Signs of depression in men**: irritability, anger, aggression, risk-taking, substance use, withdrawal, physical pain (headaches, digestive), loss of interest in hobbies
  - Men often don't say "I'm sad" — they show anger, blame, or retreat
- **Toxic masculinity**: cultural pressure to be stoic, self-reliant, dominant, unemotional — harmful to health
  - Men with rigid gender role beliefs have worse health outcomes, fewer close relationships, shorter lives
  - Real strength: asking for help, expressing emotions, having close friendships, being vulnerable
- **Therapy**: effective for men when matched with right therapist — men may prefer action-oriented approaches (CBT, behavioral activation)
- **Support**: friends matter — men's friendships tend to be activity-based (side-by-side) vs women's (face-to-face) — both valid, but depth matters for health

## Sexual Health
- **STI testing**: routine testing important — many STIs are asymptomatic (chlamydia, gonorrhea, syphilis, HIV, HPV)
  - After new partner: test. Routine: yearly if sexually active with multiple partners
  - Condoms: prevent most STIs (not all — HPV/herpes can transmit via skin contact)
- **HPV vaccine (Gardasil)**: approved up to age 45 — prevents genital warts and HPV-related cancers (penile, anal, throat)
- **HIV prevention**: PrEP (pre-exposure prophylaxis, daily pill or bimonthly injection) — 99% effective
  - PEP (post-exposure prophylaxis): within 72 hours of exposure, 28-day course
- **Vasectomy**: permanent contraception — minor procedure, quick recovery (2-3 days rest, 2 weeks no heavy activity)
  - >99% effective. Reversal is possible but expensive and not guaranteed
  - Doesn't affect testosterone, erections, ejaculation, or pleasure (no visible semen difference except no sperm)

## Self-Care
- **Preventive care**: annual physical, blood pressure check, cholesterol panel, colorectal screening (45+)
  - PSA discussion with doctor at 50 (45 if high risk)
- **Weight management**: belly fat (visceral fat) is particularly dangerous for men (heart disease, diabetes, sleep apnea)
  - Cannot spot-reduce fat — overall weight loss reduces belly fat
  - Waist circumference goal: <40 inches (102 cm)
- **Sleep**: men with sleep apnea often don't know it — loud snoring + daytime sleepiness = see doctor
  - CPAP changes lives (better energy, blood pressure, mood, brain function)
- **Hair loss**: male pattern baldness affects 50% by 50
  - Treatments: minoxidil (Rogaine, topical), finasteride (Propecia, pill, stops DHT), hair transplant
  - Acceptance is also valid — shaved head > comb-over
- **Relationships**: men with strong social connections live longer, healthier lives
  - Married men live longer than unmarried — but low-quality marriage is worse than being single
  - Close friendships protect mental health — maintain them, don't rely solely on partner for emotional support
