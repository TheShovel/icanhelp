# Cake Decorating & Piping

## Buttercream Types
- **American buttercream**: butter + powdered sugar + milk/cream + vanilla. Simple, sweet, crusts (forms light crust on outside). Best for piping flowers + kids' cakes. Ratio: 2 parts sugar to 1 part butter by weight
- **Swiss meringue buttercream**: egg whites + sugar → heated to 160°F → whipped to stiff peaks → butter added. Silky, less sweet, not crusty. Best for smooth finishes. More stable than American in warm weather? no, still melts, but silkier. Best for fillings
- **Italian meringue buttercream**: hot sugar syrup (240°F) whipped into egg whites → butter. Most stable, silkiest, hardest to make (must cook sugar to exact temp). Best for: hot climates, intricate piping, layered cakes that need structural stability
- **German buttercream**: pastry cream + butter. Rich, less sweet, rarely used for piping. Good for filling layered cakes. Very soft
- **Vegan buttercream**: vegetable shortening + margarine/butter + powdered sugar. Works, but taste suffers (use high-quality shortening + vegan butter + vanilla/almond extract)

## Piping Techniques
- **Piping tips**: round (#1-12) for writing, dots, lines. Star (#16-35, open/closed) for rosettes, shells, stars. Leaf (#352, 366) for leaves. Petal (#103-127) for roses, ruffles, drop flowers. Basketweave (#45-48) for woven patterns. French tip (closed star, #4B/ #6B) for rosettes + borders. Drop flower tip (#225, 1M)
  - Piping bag: disposable plastic (convenient, no cleaning) vs reusable (parchment, canvas, silicone). Coupler: allows quick tip change without changing bags (metal or plastic coupler ring attaches to inside of bag, then holds tip + outer screw ring). Cut bag tip to expose enough room for coupler
  - Fill: fold bag top over hand (creating cuff), fill half full (don't overfill), twist closed, keep even pressure with dominant hand, guide with other. Hold tip close to surface (for smooth piping) or above (for height like rosettes). Even pressure: consistent flow; release pressure before pulling away (reduces tails)

### Classic Piping Skills
- **Rosette**: star tip (1M), start center, circle outward, spiral finish. Practice even pressure + spiral. Hold perpendicular, apply steady, increase pressure, keep spiral even. Pull away in center to finish
- **Stars**: star tip, hold perpendicular, squeeze, lift straight up (for single stars). Continuous border: squeeze as you move along the base, release at end
- **Shell border**: star tip, squeeze while moving slightly forward, then pull back and release (creates scallop shape). Overlap next shell on tail of previous. Repeat around base of cake
- **Writing**: round tip (#2-4). Practice pressure control: squeeze → move → release before you lift. Strings of letters: practice cursive (continuous movement), pressure control for line width
- **Ruffle**: petal tip, wide end down, wiggle back and forth as you move along base. The side-to-side motion creates ruffles. For borders: angle tip. For full cake coverage: work in rows, each row overlapping bottom of previous. Can use technique for filling entire cake with ruffled buttercream skirt
- **Roses**: petal tip (#104-125). Start with small cone base (#12 round tip — frosting cone). Add overlapping petals in spiral, rotating cake stand. Each petal: wide end down (touching base), narrow end up. Overlap each petal slightly over previous. Practice on flower nail + parchment square: pipe cone base, then 3 small inner petals (encircling cone), then 5-7 larger outer petals, each overlapping

## Cake Smoothing
- **Crumb coat**: thin layer of frosting over entire cake (seals in crumbs). Chill 15-30 min (crumb coat should be thin enough to show cake through— then you get a clean final coat). Apply second (finishing) coat after crumb coat set
- **Smoothing**: bench scraper (metal), turntable (revolving stand, $20-40). Hold scraper against cake at 45° angle, rotate turntable, excess frosting scraped off. Use warm scraper (dip in hot water, dry) for smoother finish. Final pass: scrape at cake height from bottom to top in one continuous rotation (+ one final rotation around top)
- **Edges**: sharp = bench scraper pressed firmly against top edge. Straight = offset spatula for top smooth with no visible frosting lip. Clean edge: run bench scraper around at 90° to level top + side at one intersection

## Ganache & Fondant
- **Ganache**: chocolate + cream. 1:1 (pourable glaze — for drip cakes), 2:1 (firm — for truffles, filling), 3:1 (stiff — for modeling/truffle centers). Glaze: pour over chilled cake, let drip down sides at edges (drip cake). For a mirror-like shine: use 74%+ dark chocolate, add light corn syrup? 1t per 300g ganache = adds shine
- **Fondant**: rolled sugar paste. Roll 1/8" thick, drape over cake, smooth with fondant smoother. Pros: smooth, professional finish, sculptable. Cons: tastes sweet (not great), dries out. Use buttercream under fondant (crumb coat + frosting layer, not just fondant on cake). Marshmallow fondant: homemade = better taste (melt marshmallows + powdered sugar + shortening). Store-bought (Satin Ice, Wilton) vs homemade (tastes better, cracks less after a few days)
- **Fondant decorations**: use gel food coloring (not liquid — dissolves fondant). Gum paste (adds Tylose/Carboxymethylcellulose (CMC) to fondant for stronger, faster-drying flowers, figures). Let dry 24-48 hours before placing on cake. Use edible glue (water + gum paste mix), vodka (evaporates quickly, good for painting)

## Common Tools
- Turntable ($20-40, essential for serious decorating). Offset spatula (straight + small angled for detail). Bench scraper (metal, 6-8" wide). Piping bags + tips + couplers. Flower nail. Cake leveler (wire/knife — for even layers). Cake board (cardboard circle, covered with foil or plastic). Cake turntable with a non-slip surface, or use a damp paper towel under the board
- Piping gel: clear gel for writing on cakes, gluing fondant decorations, adding shine. Like hair gel, use sparingly
