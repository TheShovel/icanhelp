# Wine & Spirits Knowledge

## Wine Types
- **Red**: Cabernet Sauvignon (full-bodied, high tannin, dark fruit, oak, aging potential — Bordeaux, Napa, Coonawarra). Pinot Noir (light-bodied, low tannin, red fruit, earthy — Burgundy, Oregon, Sonoma. Delicate, made famous by "Sideways"). Merlot (medium-bodied, soft, plum — Right Bank Bordeaux, Washington). Syrah/Shiraz (full-bodied, spicy, peppery — Rhône, Australia). Zinfandel (medium-full, jammy, high alcohol — California). Malbec (medium-full, dark fruit, smoky — Argentina). Tempranillo (medium, leather, cherry — Rioja, Spain). Nebbiolo (high tannin + acid, tar+roses — Barolo, Barbaresco). Sangiovese (medium, cherry, herbal — Chianti, Brunello)
- **White**: Chardonnay (full-bodied, oak/butter or crisp/mineral — Burgundy, California). Sauvignon Blanc (light, crisp, citrus, grassy — Loire, New Zealand). Riesling (fruity, high acid, sweet to dry — Germany, Alsace). Pinot Grigio (light, crisp, neutral — Italian/California). Moscato (light, sweet, low alcohol — Italy). Albariño (crisp, citrus, saline — Spain). Viognier (full, floral, peach — Rhône). Chenin Blanc (crisp/honeyed — Loire, South Africa)
- **Sparkling**: Champagne (traditional method, Chardonnay+Pinot Noir+Pinot Meunier, France). Prosecco (tank method, Glera grape, Italy). Cava (traditional method, Spain). Crémant (French traditional method outside Champagne). Lambrusco (red sparkling, Italy). Pétillant Naturel (pét-nat, ancestral method, cloudy, trendy)
- **Dessert/fortified**: Port (red, fortified, sweet — Portugal). Sherry (dry or sweet, flor aged — Spain). Sauternes (noble rot sweet white — Bordeaux). Tokaji (botrytized Hungarian sweet wine). Ice wine (frozen grapes pressed — Canada, Germany). Madeira (oxidized heat-aged, shelf-stable)

## Wine Tasting
- **Look**: clarity (clear = good), color (red purple → brick with age; white pale straw → gold with age). Swirl: release aromas. Legs/ tears: indicate higher alcohol/sugar, not quality
- **Smell**: primary (fruit: apple, citrus, berries, tropical). Secondary (oak: vanilla, cedar, toast, coconut. MLF: butter, cream. Lees: bread, biscuit). Tertiary (aged: leather, tobacco, mushroom, dried fruit). Faults: wet cardboard (cork taint TCA). Vinegar (volatile acidity — too much acetic acid). Sherry/ nutty + oxidized (too much oxygen — intentional in some wines like sherry, but flaw in white wines). Sulfur (struck match, burnt rubber — often blows off with air)
- **Taste**: sweet/sour/salt/bitter/umami. Tannin (bitter, drying — red wines). Acidity (crisp, mouthwatering). Body (light → full). Finish (length of flavor after swallow — longer = better quality. Measured in seconds: short <5 sec, medium 5-15, long >15). Quality: balance (acidity + tannin + alcohol + fruit + oak), complexity (multiple layers of flavor), intensity, length
- **Serving temperature**: reds 55-65°F (slightly cool). Whites 45-55°F (not ice cold). Full reds: 60-68°F. Light reds: 55-60°F. Sparkling: 40-50°F. Over-chilling mutes flavor (cold kills fruit + aroma). Under-chilling reds: alcohol heat dominates
- **Decanting**: young red (aerate, soften tannins — 30 min +), old red (separate sediment — pour carefully). Most reds benefit from some air. Young bold reds: decant 1-2 hours. Old fragile reds: decant just before serving (to separate sediment, don't over-oxygenate)

## Spirits & Cocktail Knowledge

### Whiskey
- **Scotch**: single malt (100% malted barley from single distillery). Blended (grain + malt whiskies — Johnnie Walker, Dewar's). Regions: Highland (fruity, heather), Speyside (rich, sherry — Macallan, Glenfiddich), Islay (peaty, smoky — Laphroaig, Ardbeg). Lowland (light), Campbeltown (salty, briny). Age statement: 12 years = minimum age of youngest whiskey in bottle. No age statement (NAS) = can be any age. Single malt: $40-200+
- **Bourbon**: American, at least 51% corn, new charred oak barrels, no aging minimum (straight bourbon ≥2 years). Sweet, vanilla, caramel — Maker's Mark, Buffalo Trace, Wild Turkey, Blanton's. Must be made in USA (not just Kentucky, but 95% is). Rye: at least 51% rye, spicier than bourbon
- **Irish whiskey**: triple-distilled, smooth, light — Jameson, Bushmills, Redbreast. Typically lighter + sweeter than scotch
- **Japanese**: modeled on scotch, elegant — Suntory (Hibiki, Yamazaki, Hakushu), Nikka (Yoichi, Miyagikyo). Complex, expensive

### Vodka
- **Neutral spirit**: distilled from grain/potatoes (or grapes, beets, rice). "Good vodka = smooth + clean" — but how much does quality matter? In blind tastings, cheap brands often beat expensive ones. For cocktails: mid-range is fine (Tito's, Smirnoff, Ketel One, Absolut). For mixing: no need for $50+ premium

### Gin
- **Distilled with juniper berries** (minimum: juniper must be dominant botanical flavor). London Dry: no sugar added, all natural botanicals — Beefeater, Tanqueray, Bombay Sapphire. Plymouth: slightly sweeter, protected origin. Old Tom: sweetened gin. New Western/Contemporary: less juniper forward, more creative botanicals (Hendrick's cucumber+rose, The Botanist, Roku Japanese). Favorite for classics: Beefeater (dry martini + negroni). Favorite for complex: Hendrick's (aviation, cucumber + rose, gin and tonic)

### Rum
- **Light/white**: column still, light flavor, for cocktails (daiquiri, mojito). Bacardi, Plantation 3 Star
- **Gold/amber**: aged 1-5 years, medium body. Appleton, Mount Gay
- **Dark**: aged longer, heavy molasses flavor, for sipping/ti punches. Gosling's, Myers's, Plantation dark. Agricole: from fresh cane juice not molasses — grassy, funky — Rhum JM, Clement
- **Demerara**: Guyana, heavy, rich for old-fashioned. Lemon Hart, Hamilton 151 for floaters

### Tequila & Mezcal
- **Tequila**: 100% agave only (mixto = 51% agave + sugar — avoid). Blanco/silver (unaged, grassy, pepper). Reposado (2-12 months in oak, smoother). Añejo (1-3 years, wood, vanilla, scotch-like). Extra Añejo (3+ years). Premium: Don Julio, Casamigos (bright for mixing), Fortaleza, El Tesoro, Siete Leguas, Ocho
- **Mezcal**: any agave type, roasted in pits = smoky. Origin: Oaxaca (hearts of agave in underground pits). Del Maguey, Los Amantes, Ilegal, Montelobos, Bozal, Mezcal Vago. Pechuga: mezcal finished with a raw chicken/turkey breast suspended in the still during distillation — adds depth, not poultry flavor. Very traditional, niche, rare
