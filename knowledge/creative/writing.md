# Creative Writing & Storytelling

## Story Structure
- **Three-act structure**: Setup (introduce characters, world, conflict) → Confrontation (rising action, obstacles) → Resolution (climax, aftermath)
- **Hero's Journey** (Joseph Campbell): ordinary world → call to adventure → refusal → mentor → crossing threshold → tests → approach → ordeal → reward → road back → resurrection → return with elixir
- **Save the Cat** beat sheet: 15 beats including opening image, theme stated, catalyst, debate, break into 2, B-story, fun and games, midpoint, bad guys close, all is lost, dark night of soul, break into 3, finale, final image
- **Dan Harmon story circle**: character in zone of comfort → wants something → enters unfamiliar situation → adapts → gets what they want → pays heavy price → returns to familiar situation → changed

## Elements of Fiction
- **Character**: protagonist (main), antagonist (opposition), deuteragonist (sidekick/foil)
  - Show don't tell: show fear through sweaty palms, stuttering, avoiding, not "he was scared"
  - Character arc: flat (unchanged), positive (grows), negative (falls), transformational (entirely different)
  - Motivation: every character wants something, even minor ones
- **Setting**: time, place, atmosphere — should influence story, not just be backdrop
- **Plot**: cause and effect chain (not just "and then" but "therefore" or "but")
- **Theme**: underlying message (love, sacrifice, power corrupts, identity)
- **Conflict**: person vs person, self, society, nature, technology, fate
- **Point of View**: 1st person (I), 2nd (you — rare), 3rd limited (he/she — one character's thoughts), 3rd omniscient (knows all)

## Writing Process
1. **Prewrite**: brainstorm, outline, research, character sketches
2. **Draft**: write without editing (perfectionism kills first drafts)
3. **Revise**: big changes — structure, plot holes, character consistency, pacing
4. **Edit**: line edits — sentence flow, word choice, grammar, punctuation
5. **Proofread**: final check for typos, formatting errors
- Separate drafting and editing — they use different brain modes
- Read aloud to catch awkward phrasing
- Cut: adverbs (walked slowly → shuffled), passive voice (was walking → walked by)
- Kill your darlings: remove clever lines if they don't serve the story

## Dialogue
- Each character should sound distinct (vocabulary, rhythm, tics)
- "Said" is invisible — don't overuse alternatives (exclaimed, retorted, posited)
- Action beats reveal character: "I don't know." She twisted her ring. vs "I don't know." He shrugged.
- Subtext: what characters DON'T say matters more than what they do
- Overlap and interruption for realism
- Avoid exposition dumps in dialogue ("As you know, Bob, we've been friends since...")

## Poetry
- Forms: sonnet (14 lines, iambic pentameter, rhyme scheme), haiku (5-7-5 syllables), limerick (AABBA), free verse (no rules)
- Devices: metaphor, simile, alliteration, assonance, onomatopoeia, enjambment
- Concrete imagery > abstract statements ("the bruised sky" vs "it was sad")
- Show, don't tell applies to poetry too
- Read poetry aloud to hear rhythm and sound

## Writer's Block
- Low-stakes writing: 5 min free write (whatever comes to mind, no editing)
- Change medium: type → pen → voice recorder
- Write the scene you're excited about (skip the boring bridge chapter)
- Read something in a similar genre for inspiration
- Writing prompts: "what if", "the door opened and...", "the last thing I expected was..."
- Lower standards temporarily — you can't edit a blank page
- Step away: walk, shower, drive — brains solve problems in the background

## Publishing Options
- **Traditional**: query agent → agent pitches publisher → advance + royalties — hard to get in
  - Query letter: hook (1-2 sentences), synopsis, author bio, comp titles (similar books)
- **Self-publishing**: Amazon KDP, IngramSpark — full control, 70% royalty, you handle all work
- **Hybrid**: pay a publisher a fee for services (often scam, be careful)
- **Web serial**: Royal Road, Wattpad, AO3 — build audience first
- Magazine/short story: submit to literary magazines (Duotrope, Submittable)
- Make a dollar with writing = hardest part of writing
