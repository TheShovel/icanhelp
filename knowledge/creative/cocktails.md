# Cocktails & Home Bartending

## Bar Tools
- **Shaker**: Boston (metal + glass tin) or Cobbler (3-piece with built-in strainer)
- **Jigger**: measuring tool (1oz + 2oz or 0.5oz + 1oz sides)
- **Strainer**: Hawthorne (with spring, for shaking), fine mesh (for double-straining), julep (for stirred drinks)
- **Muddler**: crush herbs/fruit. **Bar spoon**: long handle, stirring
- **Juicer**: citrus reamer or squeeze press
- **Ice**: large cubes (slow dilution), crushed (tiki drinks), sphere (old fashioned)
- **Garnish tools**: peeler (citrus twists), channel knife, toothpicks

## Essential Spirits
- **Whiskey**: bourbon (sweet, American), rye (spicy, American), scotch (smoky, Scottish), Irish (smooth)
- **Vodka**: neutral, flexible — good quality doesn't need to be expensive
- **Gin**: juniper-forward — London Dry (Beefeater, Tanqueray), Plymouth (softer), New Western (Hendrick's, botanical)
- **Rum**: white/silver (mixing), gold/amber (aged, medium), dark (full-bodied, cooking/ti punches)
- **Tequila**: blanco (unaged, grassy), reposado (aged 2-12 months, smoother), añejo (1-3 years, sipping)
  - 100% agave only — "mixto" tequila has added sugar
- **Mezcal**: smoky (agave roasted in pits), from Oaxaca, sipping spirit
- **Brandy/Cognac**: grape-based, aged — Cognac (region in France), Armagnac, pisco
- **Liqueurs**: sweetened spirits — triple sec (orange), Campari (bitter), vermouth (aromatized wine), Amaretto (almond), Baileys (cream), Chartreuse (herbal)

## Classic Cocktails
```
# Old Fashioned
2 oz bourbon or rye
1 sugar cube (or 1/4 oz simple syrup)
2 dashes Angostura bitters
Muddle sugar + bitters, add spirit, large ice cube, stir, garnish orange twist

# Negroni
1 oz gin
1 oz Campari
1 oz sweet vermouth
Stir with ice, strain into rocks glass over large ice, garnish orange peel

# Martini
2.5 oz gin or vodka
0.5 oz dry vermouth
Stir with ice, strain into chilled coupe, garnish olive or lemon twist
（Ratio varies — "dry" = less vermouth "dirty" = splash olive brine）

# Margarita
2 oz tequila blanco
1 oz lime juice
0.75 oz triple sec
Shake, strain over ice (rocks) or frozen blend, salt rim optional

# Daiquiri
2 oz white rum
1 oz lime juice
0.75 oz simple syrup
Shake, strain into chilled coupe — NOT frozen daiquiri (different drink)

# Whiskey Sour
2 oz bourbon
0.75 oz lemon juice
0.75 oz simple syrup
0.5 oz egg white (optional — dry shake first, then shake with ice, strain)

# Manhattan
2 oz rye whiskey
1 oz sweet vermouth
2 dashes Angostura bitters
Stir, strain into coupe, garnish cherry

# Espresso Martini
2 oz vodka
1 oz espresso (fresh)
0.75 oz coffee liqueur (Kahlua)
0.5 oz simple syrup
Shake hard (20 sec for foam), strain, garnish coffee beans
```

## Cocktail Ratios (Template)
- **Sour**: 2 spirit + 1 citrus + 1 sweet
- **Old fashioned**: spirit + sugar + bitters
- **Martini**: spirit + vermouth
- **Highball**: spirit + carbonated mixer (gin + tonic, whiskey + ginger ale)
- **Tiki**: spirit + multiple citrus + multiple sweeteners + spice

## Technique
- **Stir** (not shake): spirit-only drinks (Old Fashioned, Martini, Manhattan) — 30 sec with ice, strain
- **Shake**: drinks with citrus, juice, egg, cream (Sour, Margarita, Daiquiri) — 10-15 sec until shaker frosty
- **Double-strain**: through Hawthorne + fine mesh (removes ice shards, herb bits)
- **Dry shake**: shake without ice first (for egg white drinks) to emulsify, then shake with ice to chill
- **Muddling**: gentle press (don't obliterate herbs — releases bitterness)
- **Ice dilution**: stirred drinks want 20-30% dilution, shaken drinks 15-25% — timing matters
- **Chill glass**: fill with ice or put in freezer — drink goes in cold, stays cold longer

## Simple Syrup
- **Rich simple**: 2 parts sugar : 1 part water (dissolve, store refrigerated, lasts longer)
- **Regular simple**: 1:1 ratio
- Flavored: infuse with herbs (rosemary, thyme), spices (cinnamon, cardamom), fruit (strawberry, jalapeño)
- **Demerara simple**: brown sugar for Old Fashioneds, Manhattans — richer flavor
- **Honey syrup**: 3 parts honey : 1 part water (honey is too thick to pour plain)

## Stocking a Home Bar (Starter)
- **Spirits**: bourbon/rye, gin, vodka, white rum, tequila blanco
- **Vermouth**: sweet (red) + dry (white) — keep refrigerated, oxidizes in weeks
- **Liqueurs**: triple sec (Cointreau > triple sec), Angostura bitters, Campari
- **Citrus**: lemons + limes, fresh squeezed (bottled juice is not the same)
- **Ice**: get a good ice tray (large cubes, silicone)
- **Garnishes**: oranges, lemons, limes, cherries (Luxardo > bright red), olives
- Glassware: rocks glass, highball, coupe, martini glass

## Entertaining Tips
- Batch cocktails: multiply recipe and serve from a pitcher (no dilution from shaking each individually)
- Pre-batch and refrigerate: add dilution water (calculate 20% of final volume) so it's ready to pour
- Ice: have enough (1 lb/person minimum) — buy bags or make ahead
- Serve one signature cocktail + wine/beer options — don't be a short-order mixologist
- Garnishes: prepare ahead (citrus wheels, twists, herbs) — makes service faster
- Non-alcoholic: same effort for mocktails — spirit alternatives (Seedlip, Ritual) or just serve quality juice/soda with garnish
