# Drawing & Visual Art Basics

## Materials
- **Pencils**: H (hard, light) → B (soft, dark). HB = middle. 2B-6B for sketching, H for detail
- **Paper**: sketchbook (layout paper), drawing paper (smoother), bristol board (for ink/marker)
- **Erasers**: kneaded (lifts, no smudge), plastic/vinyl (clean erase), electric (for highlights)
- **Ink**: fineliners (Pigma Micron), brush pens (Fude nibs), dip pens (nib + inkwell)
- **Digital**: tablet (Wacom, Huion, iPad + Procreate), stylus, drawing software (Photoshop, Krita, Clip Studio)

## Basic Skills
- **Lines**: straight (whole arm motion, not wrist), curved (smooth, practice spirals)
- **Shapes**: everything is composed of basic shapes (circles, squares, triangles, cylinders)
- **Contour drawing**: draw outline without looking at paper — trains hand-eye coordination
- **Gesture drawing**: 30 sec - 2 min sketches to capture movement/pose — quick, loose, no detail
- **Blind contour**: draw subject without looking at paper at all — teaches observation

## Shading & Value
- **Value scale**: white → light gray → mid gray → dark gray → black (9-step scale)
- **Hatching**: parallel lines. Cross-hatching: overlapping lines at angles
- **Stippling**: dots — more dots = darker. Time-consuming but precise
- **Blending**: smudge with finger/tortillon/brush — smooth gradients (soft look)
- **Scumbling**: circular scribble motion — textured shading
- **Light source**: consistent direction, highlights on closest point, cast shadows opposite
- Core shadow: darkest part on object. Cast shadow: shadow the object throws. Reflected light: light bouncing back into shadow

## Perspective
- **One-point**: all lines converge to single vanishing point on horizon (looking down a road)
- **Two-point**: two vanishing points (corner of building)
- **Three-point**: three vanishing points (looking up at skyscraper — adds height/depth)
- **Foreshortening**: object appears shorter when angled toward viewer (arm pointing at you)
- **Atmospheric perspective**: far objects — lighter, less detail, bluer (mountains in distance)
- Horizon line at eye level — objects above it show bottom, objects below show top

## Color Theory
- **Primary**: red, yellow, blue (RYB for painting) — can't be mixed from other colors
- **Secondary**: green, orange, purple — mix two primaries
- **Tertiary**: red-orange, yellow-orange, yellow-green, blue-green, blue-purple, red-purple
- **Complementary colors**: opposite on wheel (red/green, blue/orange, yellow/purple) — contrast
- **Analogous colors**: adjacent on wheel (blue, blue-green, green) — harmonious
- **Warm colors**: red, orange, yellow — advance in composition
- **Cool colors**: blue, green, purple — recede
- **Color harmony**: complementary (high contrast), analogous (calm), triadic (vibrant, 3 evenly spaced on wheel)
- **Tint**: color + white (pastel). **Shade**: color + black (dark). **Tone**: color + gray (muted)

## Composition
- **Rule of thirds**: divide canvas 3x3, place focal points on intersections
- **Golden ratio**: ~1:1.618 — found in nature, aesthetically pleasing
- **Leading lines**: guide eye through composition (roads, rivers, limbs)
- **Negative space**: empty space around subject — can be as important as subject
- **Balance**: symmetrical (formal, stable) vs asymmetrical (dynamic, interesting)
- **Framing**: use foreground elements (branches, arches) to frame subject
- **Depth**: foreground (close), midground, background (far) — creates 3D feel in 2D

## Anatomy Proportions
- **Head**: ~1/8 of body height. Eyes at midpoint of head
- **Eyes**: one eye width between them. Fits 5 eye widths across face
- **Nose**: bottom aligns with ear lobes. Length = from brow to bottom of nose
- **Mouth**: corners align with center of eyes
- **Ears**: from eyebrow to bottom of nose
- **Figure**: 7.5 heads tall average (8 for heroic). Shoulders = 2 head widths
- Hands: size of face (palm to fingertips = chin to hairline)
- Elbow at waist level. Wrist at crotch level. Fingertips at mid-thigh

## Practice Routine
- Draw every day (even 10 min) — skill is built, not gifted
- Still life: draw objects from observation (fruit, bottles, fabric)
- Master studies: copy artists you admire to learn their techniques
- Photo studies: draw from photos, then from life
- Draw what you see, not what you think you see
- Flip drawing upside down: breaks brain's "symbol" mode, forces observation
- Keep all your drawings — look at old work to see progress
- 10,000 hours to mastery, but significant improvement in 3-6 months of daily practice
