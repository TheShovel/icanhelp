# Video Editing Basics

## Software
- **DaVinci Resolve**: professional grade, free version is powerful, color grading standard
- **Premiere Pro**: industry standard, subscription ($20/mo), well integrated with After Effects
- **Final Cut Pro**: Mac only, fast, optimized M-series chips, one-time purchase
- **CapCut**: free, easy, mobile + desktop, good for social media
- **Shotcut / Olive**: free, open source, cross-platform
- **Kdenlive**: Linux-friendly, free, good for beginners

## Timeline Basics
- **Timeline**: tracks stacked vertically, time moves left to right
- **Tracks**: video (V1, V2...) and audio (A1, A2...) — layer tracks for overlays
- **Playhead**: vertical line showing current position
- **In/Out points**: mark start and end of clip to trim
- **Ripple edit**: trim and close gap automatically (shifts all later clips)
- **Roll edit**: adjust cut point between two clips without changing total duration
- **Slip edit**: change clip's in/out without moving it on timeline (same duration)
- **J-cut**: audio starts before video (sound bridges scene change)
- **L-cut**: video changes before audio (video leads, audio continues from previous)

## Editing Workflow
1. **Logging**: import all footage, label takes, mark favorites, add notes
2. **Assembly**: rough cut — place best takes in order, ignore fine cuts
3. **Fine cut**: trim precisely, adjust pacing, remove dead space
4. **B-roll**: overlay supplementary footage to cover cuts, illustrate points
5. **Audio**: clean dialogue (remove background noise, normalize levels), add music/sound effects
6. **Color correction**: fix white balance, exposure, match shots
7. **Color grading**: stylistic look (warm/cool, film emulation, teal/orange)
8. **Titles & graphics**: lower thirds, captions, transitions
9. **Export**: choose format + settings for delivery platform

## Cuts & Transitions
- **Hard cut**: instant switch (most common, invisible when well-timed)
- **Jump cut**: same shot with section removed (intentional for vlogs, online content)
- **Match cut**: cut between visually similar shots (shape, color, movement)
- **Cross dissolve**: gradual fade between clips (classic, overused by beginners)
- **Dip to black/white**: fade to black (passage of time, end of scene)
- **J and L cuts**: audio leads or trails the video (professional, natural feel)
- Avoid: star wipes, page peels, excessive transitions (look amateur)

## Audio
- Dialogue: -12 to -6 dB peak, -24 to -18 LUFS (broadcast standard)
- Background music: -30 to -20 dB (should never overpower dialogue)
- Room tone: 30 sec of silent room recording — use to fill gaps, smooth edits
- **Noise reduction**: remove hum, hiss (DaVinci Fairlight, Audacity, Adobe Audition)
- **Compressor**: evens out volume (quieter parts louder, louder quieter)
- **Limiter**: prevents clipping (max volume ceiling)
- **Keyframes**: automate volume changes over time (duck music under dialogue)
- Crossfade: smooth transition between audio clips (avoid clicks/pops)

## Color
- **Color wheel**: Lift (shadows), Gamma (midtones), Gain (highlights)
- **Primary correction**: fix exposure, white balance, contrast first
- **Secondary correction**: isolate specific color/area for adjustment (skin tone, sky)
- **Scopes**: waveform (luminance), vectorscope (color), histogram (distribution)
- **Saturation**: more = vibrant, less = muted. Desaturated skin tones look dead
- **Teal/orange**: teal shadows + orange skin tones = Hollywood blockbuster look (Teal = complementary to Orange)
- **LUT** (Look Up Table): preset color grade — quick starting point, needs adjustment per footage
- **Film emulation**: mimic Kodak/Fujifilm stock looks

## Export Settings
- **Codec**: H.264 (most compatible, good quality/size), H.265/HEVC (smaller, newer), ProRes (editing)
- **Resolution**: 1080p (standard), 4K (detailed, larger file)
- **Frame rate**: 24fps (film), 30fps (TV/web), 60fps (sports/smooth)
- **Bitrate**: higher = better quality + larger file — 15-25 Mbps for 1080p H.264
- For YouTube: H.264, 1080p+, 24/30fps, high bitrate
- For social: H.264, 1080p, 30fps, square 1:1 for IG, vertical 9:16 for TikTok/Reels

## Storytelling
- 3-act structure applies to video too (setup → conflict → resolution)
- Show don't tell — visual storytelling > narration
- Pacing: vary scene length — don't stay on one shot too long
- Hook: grab attention in first 5 seconds
- Rule of 3: group information in threes (more memorable)
- B-roll: covers narrative edits, adds visual interest, illustrates the spoken message
- Music selection: sets emotional tone (upbeat, tense, sad, epic)
- Sound design: ambient sounds, foley (footsteps, doors), subtle effects = immersion
