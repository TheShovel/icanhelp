# Music Basics

## Music Theory
- **Notes**: A, B, C, D, E, F, G (repeat). Sharps (#) up a half step, flats (b) down
- **Half step**: adjacent piano keys (E→F, B→C are half steps — no black key between)
- **Whole step**: 2 half steps (C→D, D→E)
- **Octave**: same note 8 steps apart (C4→C5) — double frequency
- A4 = 440 Hz (standard tuning reference)

## Scales
- **Major scale**: W-W-H-W-W-W-H (Happy) — C major: C D E F G A B C
- **Natural minor scale**: W-H-W-W-H-W-W (Sad) — A minor: A B C D E F G A
- **Pentatonic**: 5 notes — major (1-2-3-5-6) and minor (1-b3-4-5-b7) — most used in rock/blues
- **Chromatic**: all 12 notes — used for tension, passing notes
- **Blues scale**: minor pentatonic + flat 5 (b5)

## Chords
- **Major chord**: root + major 3rd + perfect 5th (C E G) — happy
- **Minor chord**: root + minor 3rd + perfect 5th (C Eb G) — sad
- **Diminished**: root + minor 3rd + diminished 5th (C Eb Gb) — tense
- **Augmented**: root + major 3rd + augmented 5th (C E G#) — unstable
- **Seventh chords**: add minor 7th to major chord (C E G Bb) — C7, bluesy
- **Major 7th**: add major 7th (C E G B) — Cmaj7, dreamy/jazzy
- **Suspended 2nd/4th**: replace 3rd with 2nd or 4th (C D G / C F G) — open, floating
- **Power chord**: root + 5th only (C G) — no 3rd, neither major/minor — rock/punk

## Chord Progressions (Common)
- I-IV-V-I (C F G C) — rock, blues, everything
- I-V-vi-IV (C G Am F) — "Axis of Awesome" progression (Let It Be, No Woman No Cry)
- ii-V-I (Dm G C) — jazz standard
- I-vi-IV-V (C Am F G) — 50s progression
- vi-IV-I-V (Am F C G) — pop punk

## Rhythm
- **BPM**: beats per minute (60 = slow, 120 = normal, 160+ = fast)
- **Time signature**: 4/4 (common), 3/4 (waltz), 6/8 (swing feel)
- Notes: whole (4 beats) → half (2) → quarter (1) → eighth (1/2) → sixteenth (1/4)
- Dotted: 1.5x length (dotted quarter = 1.5 beats)
- Triplets: 3 notes in space of 2 (swing feel)

## Instruments
- **Guitar**: 6 strings (EADGBE), fretboard, acoustic (hollow) vs electric (solid + amp)
- **Piano/Keyboard**: 88 keys (52 white, 36 black), melody right hand, chords/bass left hand
- **Bass guitar**: 4 strings (EADG), lower octave than guitar, rhythmic foundation
- **Drums**: kick (bass drum), snare, hi-hat, crash/ride cymbals, toms
- **Voice**: soprano (highest), alto, tenor, bass (lowest)

## Reading Music (piano)
- **Treble clef**: G clef — EGBDF (Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge) lines, FACE spaces
- **Bass clef**: F clef — GBDFA (Good Boys Deserve Fudge Always) lines, ACEG spaces
- Staff: 5 lines, 4 spaces. Ledger lines for notes above/below

## Digital Music
- **DAW** (Digital Audio Workstation): Ableton Live, FL Studio, Logic Pro, GarageBand (free)
- **MIDI**: digital note data (no audio) — pitch, velocity, duration, can change instrument
- **VST/AU**: plugin instruments and effects (synths, reverb, compression, EQs)
- **Sample**: recorded sound snippet — MPC, drum machines
- **Loop**: repeating audio clip
- **Compression**: reduces dynamic range (quieter parts louder, louder parts quieter)
- **EQ**: boosts/cuts specific frequencies (highs, mids, lows)
- **Reverb**: simulates room sound (hall, room, plate, spring)

## Ear Training
- Interval: distance between two notes (unison, 2nd, 3rd, etc.)
- Perfect intervals: unison, 4th, 5th, octave — easy to identify
- Major 3rd: sounds happy (first two notes of "When the Saints")
- Minor 3rd: sounds sad ("Greensleeves")
- Perfect 5th: open, hollow sound ("Twinkle Twinkle")
- Relative pitch: identify intervals by comparing to known reference (more practical to learn than perfect pitch)
