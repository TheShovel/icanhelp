# Advanced Cooking Techniques

## Knife Cuts
- **Julienne**: 1/8" × 1/8" × 2" matchsticks — carrots for stir-fry, garnishes
- **Brunoise**: 1/8" cubes (diced julienne) — mirepoix for sauces, soups
- **Small dice**: 1/4" cubes — soups, stews, sauces
- **Medium dice**: 1/2" cubes — potatoes, carrots for stews
- **Large dice**: 3/4" cubes — root vegetables for roasting
- **Chiffonade**: leafy greens/herbs rolled and sliced into ribbons — basil, spinach, kale. Roll leaves tightly, slice perpendicular to roll
- **Diagonal cut**: oval slices (more surface area = cooks faster, more attractive) — carrots, celery, zucchini
- **Rondelle**: round slices — cucumbers, carrots, zucchini (uniform, for salads/even cooking)
- **Paysanne**: thin square/ triangle slices (about 1/2" across, 1/8" thick) — decorative, cooks fast — traditional for vegetable soups (minestrone)

## Sauce Mother Sauces (French)
1. **Béchamel**: milk + roux (butter + flour) — base for cheese sauce (Mornay), cream sauces
   - Roux: equal fat + flour by weight. Cook to blond (for white sauce, 2-3 min), add cold milk, whisk, simmer 10 min
2. **Velouté**: light stock (chicken/veal/fish) + blond roux — base for cream sauces (add cream at end)
   - Allemande: velouté + egg yolks + cream + lemon. Supreme: chicken velouté + cream
3. **Espagnole** (brown): brown stock (veal/beef) + brown roux + tomato paste + mirepoix — base for demi-glace (classic reduction, rich meat sauces)
   - Demi-glace: Espagnole + brown stock, reduced by half (takes hours, freeze for later)
4. **Tomato**: tomatoes + mirepoix + herbs + stock — marinara, pomodoro, pasta sauces
   - Use San Marzano tomatoes (whole canned DOP), crush by hand, cook slowly
5. **Hollandaise**: emulsified egg yolks + clarified butter + lemon — for eggs Benedict, asparagus, fish
   - Sabayon/whisk over gentle heat (or blender method: hot butter slowly into egg yolks + lemon with blender running)
   - Fix broken hollandaise: start new yolk, slowly whisk broken sauce into it

## Emulsions
- **Temporary**: vinaigrette (oil + vinegar, whisk/shake — separates quickly). Ratio: 3:1 oil:acid (classic), 2:1 (tart)
  - Mustard as emulsifier (adds stability, flavor). Minced shallot, herbs, honey
- **Permanent**: mayonnaise, hollandaise, aioli (egg yolk or other emulsifier creates stable bond that doesn't separate)
  - Mayonnaise: egg yolk + acid (lemon/vinegar) + oil (drizzle SLOWLY at first while whisking/blending). 1 cup oil per egg yolk
  - Lemon, salt, mustard (optional). Add garlic = aioli. Add chipotle = chipotle mayo
  - Fix broken mayo: start new yolk, slowly whisk broken mixture into it
- **Stabilized**: add gelatin, xanthan gum, or lecithin (for culinary foams/air)
  - Xanthan gum: potent (pinch per cup liquid), stabilizes vinaigrettes/cocktails/sauces — use sparingly (0.1-0.3% of weight), blend in (whisk creates lumps)

## Sous Vide (Precision Water Bath)
- **How it works**: food sealed in vacuum bag (or zip-top bag, water displacement method), cooked at precise temperature in water bath (Anova, Joule — $100-200)
  - Immersion circulator + large pot or insulated container. Set temp, wait for water to reach temp, drop bag in
- **Why**: perfect doneness every time (exact temp, can't overcook). Cooks edge-to-edge without gradient. Set and forget
  - Steak: 129°F for 1-4 hours (longer = more tender but slightly different texture). Sear in screaming hot pan/cast iron/torch at END (30-60 sec per side). DRY meat thoroughly before searing (moisture prevents browning)
  - Chicken breast: 145°F for 1-4 hours (juicy, tender — lower temp than traditional because it's pasteurized by time at lower temp)
  - Eggs: 145°F 45 min (soft, spreadable). 165°F 13 min (firm white, creamy yolk) — perfect poached eggs in shell
  - Pork chop: 140°F for 1-4 hours (safety: USDA temp 145°F, but pork is safe at 140°F for > 45 min — pasteurization is time+temperature)
  - Vegetables: 183°F 1 hour (carrots to perfect tenderness, concentrated flavor)
- **Safety**: food must reach temp within 2 hours max — don't cook below 130°F for > 2.5 hours (bacterial growth danger zone)
  - For low temp cooking (< 130°F): limit to 2-4 hours max depending on thickness and initial bacterial load
- Finish: sear (steak), torch (better precision than pan), fry (chicken skin), glaze (with blowtorch for caramelized sugar)

## Smoking
- **Cold smoke**: 70-90°F, 1-12 hours — imparts flavor without cooking. Requires dedicated setup (cold smoke generator, long tube). Cheese, salmon (lox), nuts, salt, spices
  - Cold smoking salmon: cure first (salt+sugar+herbs, 12-24hr), rinse, form pellicle (dry surface), cold smoke 4-12 hours
- **Hot smoke**: 225-275°F, cooks + smokes simultaneously — ribs, brisket, pork butt, chicken, fish
  - Wood chunks/chips on charcoal or smoker box for gas/pellet
- **Pellicle**: dry tacky surface on meat/fish formed after air drying — smoke adheres to it (important for cold smoking)

## Plating & Presentation
- **Odd numbers**: 3 or 5 elements on plate look more appealing than even numbers (rule of thirds in plating)
- **Height**: stack/lean elements for dimension — don't spread flat. Spoon swoosh sauce base, protein on top
- **Color**: contrast on plate — green vegetable + orange carrot + brown protein + white sauce. Garnish with fresh herbs (bright green pop)
- **Negative space**: leave some plate rim visible — crowded plate = messy. Wipe rim clean (fingerprint = unappetizing)
- **Sauce**: pool or swoosh on bottom, dot around plate, or dress on top — don't drown food
  - Quenelle: oval dollop (use two spoons to shape) — elegant way to serve ice cream, mousse, sorbet, whipped cream
- **Garnish**: edible and purposeful — herbs (fines herbes: chervil, chives, tarragon, parsley), microgreens, edible flowers (nasturtium, viola, borage), citrus zest, flaky sea salt (Maldon), finishing oil (olive, truffle, walnut, pumpkin seed)

## Essential Pantry
- **Oils**: extra virgin olive oil (finishing/salads/dips), regular olive oil (cooking — cheaper, higher smoke point), neutral oil (canola/grapeseed/avocado — high smoke point for searing/frying), sesame oil (finish, flavor), coconut oil (baking, Thai curries)
  - Smoke points: avocado 520°F, ghee 485°F, canola 400°F, olive 375-410°F, butter 350°F, EVOO 325°F
- **Vinegars**: white wine (light, salads), red wine (hearty, braises), balsamic (reduced for glaze, Caprese), apple cider (pork, slaw, BBQ sauce), rice (Asian, sushi), sherry (Spanish, complex), champagne (delicate vinaigrettes), malt (fish+chips, English pub)
- **Salt**: kosher (Diamond Crystal = best, less salty per volume = harder to oversalt). Sea salt/fleur de sel/Maldon (finishing). Table salt (iodized — fine, can oversalt easily)
  - Diamond Crystal vs Morton: DC = flake, less dense, pinch is less salt. Morton = dense, more salty per pinch. Follow recipe's salt type or measure by weight
- **Stock**: homemade > store-bought (bone broth, veg scraps, chicken bones — simmer 4-24 hours, strain, freeze). Store-bought: low sodium preferred (control salt)
  - Better Than Bouillon: best shelf-stable option (paste, refrigerate after opening). Bouillon cubes = salty, msg-heavy
- **Aromatics**: onion, garlic, ginger, shallot, scallion, leek, celery, carrot, fennel, chili pepper, lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaf, bay leaf, thyme, rosemary, sage, tarragon, dill, cilantro, parsley (flat-leaf/Italian = more flavor than curly)
  - Mirepoix: 2 onion : 1 celery : 1 carrot — base for Western soups/stews/braises
  - Sofrito: onion + garlic + tomato + bell pepper — base for Spanish/Latin cooking
  - Holy trinity: onion + celery + bell pepper — base for Cajun/Creole (gumbo, jambalaya)
  - Battuto/Soffritto: Italian version — onion + carrot + celery + garlic + pancetta (optional)
