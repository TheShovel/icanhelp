# Fashion Design & Pattern Making

## Pattern Making Basics
- **Sloper/block**: basic pattern fitted to standard size — no seam allowance, no style. Used as base for all designs. Make from measurements (custom fit) or use standard size (8-24). Bodice block: front + back pieces, darts at shoulders + waist. Skirt block: front + back, darts at waist. Sleeve block: set-in, one-piece + two-piece. Pant block: front + back, crotch curve, waist shaping. Adjust SLOPER by redrawing darts, adding fullness, changing neckline, adding seam lines
- **Dart manipulation**: move darts to different locations (bust dart to princess seam, shoulder, armhole, French dart from side seam). "Slash + spread" method: cut pattern line, pivot to close existing dart, new dart opens at desired location. Rules: darts need to point to apex (bust point, shoulder blade). Don't rotate across armhole/collar opening; use two darts if needed
  - Princess seam: replace darts with curved seams through apex — eliminates need for darts. Center front + side front panel. Two panels meet at apex, separate at curve — flattering fit
- **Ease**: wearing ease (minimum for movement — 1-2" at bust, 1" at waist). Design ease (extra fullness beyond wearing ease: gathers, pleats, A-line). Negative ease (stretch fabrics: less than body measurement = fitted/ compression)
- **Grading**: increase/decrease pattern to different sizes. Standard size increments: 1" bust, 1-1.5" waist, 1-1.5" hip between sizes. Grade rules: specific measurements translated + distributed around the pattern. Grading computers: use software (Gerber, Lectra, Optitex, CLO 3D) or manual with grade ruler
  - Not just scaling up — each point shifts by different amount (bust shift differs from hip shift). Grade rules are set for each pattern point

## Draping
- **Fabric on dress form** — pin + shape directly. Better for: fluid fabrics, complex draping, couture. Mark lines, remove, transfer to paper. Use muslin (cheap cotton for draping). Grainline: mark center front/back, horizontal lines at bust/waist/hip. Mark seam lines with pencil/tape. Transfer from fabric to paper pattern
  - Draping steps: press grainlines, smooth fabric over form, pin at center, create darts (fold excess to form shape), mark seam lines (pencil/tape), trace to paper pattern. For bias draping: drape on the bias (45° to grain for stretch and drape) — stretchy woven fabric (silk charmeuse, crepe de chine) falls differently on bias

## Garment Construction Order
1. **Staystitching**: stitch just inside seam allowance on curved edges (neckline, armhole) before handling — prevents stretching. Stitch direction: from shoulder down to center on each side. Direction: directional stitching (with grain = prevents distortion)
2. **Darts**: sew from wide end to point (backstitch at start, sew to point, leave long thread tail + tie knot — don't backstitch at point or it puckers). Press darts flat, then press direction (vertical darts toward center, horizontal darts downward, bust darts down, waist darts toward center)
3. **Seams**: sew right sides together. Finish edges (serger, zigzag, French seam, bound, turn + stitch). Press seams BEFORE crossing with another seam. Clip curves (curved outward: snip to within 1/8" of stitching, curved inward: notch small V's to prevent puckering)
4. **Facings**: finish inner edge (serger, hem). Understitch (stitch facing to seam allowance close to seam line — keeps facing from rolling out). Grade seams (trim seam allowances to different widths to reduce bulk). Clip curves
5. **Zipper**: install lapped, centered, invisible, exposed
6. **Sleeves**: ease cap to match armhole (two rows of gathering stitches between notches — pull to ease). Pin at notches, cap ease distributed evenly, sew with sleeve side up. Set-in sleeve insertion: ease cap, pin at shoulder + underarm + notches
7. **Hem**: try on + mark (not before). Deep hem (2-3" for dress pants, 1-1/4" for skirts). Blind stitch (invisible) or topstitch

## Textile Science
- **Fiber properties**: length (staple: short fibers — cotton, wool, linen vs filament: long — silk, synthetic). Strength (nylon strongest, wool weakest for same size). Elasticity (spandex > wool > cotton). Abrasion resistance (nylon > polyester > cotton > wool). Absorbency: cotton > rayon > wool > silk > synthetics. Flammability: cotton/rayon = burns easy, wool/silk = self-extinguishing, synthetics melt
- **Weaves**: plain (over-under, most common — broadcloth, poplin, taffeta, chiffon). Twill (over 2, under 1 — diagonal: denim, gabardine, chino, tweed — more durable, drapes well). Satin (float threads over multiple warps/wefts — shiny surface, snags easily). Jacquard (complex pattern woven in — brocade, damask, tapestry). Knits: weft (single yarn loops across — jersey) vs warp (multiple yarns vertical — tricot, raschel)
- **Finishes**: mercerization (cotton treated with NaOH → stronger, lustrous, better dye affinity — bright shiny cotton thread). Sanforization (mechanical preshrinking). Anti-wrinkle (resin treatment for cotton). Water repellent (DWR/Durable Water Repellent). Flame retardant

## Fashion Business
- **Seasons**: Spring/Summer (Feb-March shows, in stores Jan-June). Fall/Winter (Sept-Oct shows, in stores July-Dec). Pre-collections (cruise/resort, pre-fall) = important for commercial sales. "Fast fashion": Zara, H&M, Shein — copy runway trends in 2-4 weeks. Luxury: long lead times, small production runs
- **Line sheet**: collection overview (photos, style numbers, colors, pricing, wholesale cost). Minimum order quantities. Production timeline: sketch → pattern → sample → pattern corrections → production pattern → grading → marker making (maximize fabric layout) → cutting → sewing → finishing → shipping
- **Costing**: fabric (35-50% of garment cost), labor (20-35%), trim/ findings (10-15%), overhead (10-15%), profit (5-20%). Wholesale price = cost × 2-2.5. Retail = wholesale × 2-3. 30% margin for boutique
