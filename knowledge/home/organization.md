# Home Organization & Decluttering

## Room-by-Room Systems

### Kitchen Organization
- **Zones**: storage (pantry, cabinets), prep (counters, cutting boards), cooking (stove, pots/pans), cleaning (sink, dishwasher, trash/recycling). Arrange items near where you use them. Utensils near stove, mugs near coffee maker, spices near prep area
  - "Work triangle" for organization: sink, stove, fridge — distance between them O(12-20 ft each leg). Don't block paths with island or bins
- **Pantry**: remove everything, group by category (canned, grains, pasta, spices, baking, snacks, oils, sauces, dried herbs/seasonings). Clear containers for dry goods (pasta, rice, flour — uniform containers = visual uniformity, stackable. Airtight keeps pests out). Lazy Susan for corners. Tiered shelves for cans (roll-down dispenser for soda cans, wire shelf for stacked items). Door racks for spices/condiments. Label everything with name + expiry date using label maker or chalk marker
  - FIFO (first in, first out): put new items behind existing, older items in front. Don't keep expired items. Inventory: take stock before shopping (avoid duplicates, wasted food). Date jars when you open or store
- **Cabinets**: use shelf risers (short items on bottom, taller above). Pull-out drawers (retrofit existing shelves with full-extension slides — far easier than reaching back). Drawer organizers (cutlery, utensils, measuring spoons — peg system, adjustable). Pull-out spice racks (narrow pull-out next to stove). Pots + pans: vertical divider or pull-out pot rack. baking sheets: vertical divider, or store in cabinet on side with rack. Lids: lid organizer (racks, baskets, pegboard inside door - or separate shallow drawer)
- **Countertops**: keep clear (store appliances you use daily: coffee maker, toaster, canisters). Everything else in cabinets. Knife block: magnetic strip on wall > block (sanitary, space-saving). Utensil crock: frequently used wooden/silicone spatulas, spoons, tongs. Cutting board: upright in slot between counter and backsplash, or leaning against backsplash with small catch tray

### Closet Organization
- **KonMari folding**: fold clothes into small rectangles, store VERTICALLY (file system) — everything visible, no stacking (you see all at once). Drawers: stand clothes on edge. Fold: tuck sleeves, fold sides, fold in half. Result: each item stands upright
  - Why: no more digging through piles. Pull one item without disturbing others
- **Seasonal swap**: rotate clothes seasonally (store off-season in bins under bed or on high shelf). Rule: if you didn't wear it last season, it goes in "maybe" box (if still not worn next season = donate). Capsule wardrobe: 30-40 items per season that all mix + match
- **Shoes**: shoe rack (visible, count pairs). Boots: use boot shapers to maintain shape. Sort by type + frequency of wear. Limit: same number of shoes as you use. One in, one out rule (new pair = old pair gone). Shoe cubbies for 10-15 pairs per person. Under-bed shoe storage for out of season
- **Accessories**: belt and tie rack (slide-out, wall-mounted behind door). Jewelry: drawer organizers (sectioned for necklaces: use non-slip mat and layer flat). Scarves: ladder rack. Hats: shelf hat rack. Bags: shelf for handbags, larger totes (stuff with acid-free tissue or old pillowcases to maintain shape). Use clear dividers + vertical stacking

### Bathroom Organization
- **Medicine cabinet**: clear horizontal rows by height. OTC meds: label with expiry date (use dry erase marker on mirror or list). Use small containers (bins/small baskets) for categories: daily care, first aid, dental, medications, hair, skin. Keep only what you use weekly — the rest goes in under-sink storage. Discard expired medications safely (take-back programs; some pharmacies have drop boxes; do NOT flush unless label specifically says to)
- **Vanity**: tall items: cleaning products, extra toilet paper stacked in basket, toiletries, backup supplies. Under sink: pull-out drawer (U-shape fits around plumbing. DIY or pre-made: two-tier sliding basket — lower for bottle storage, upper for daily items like hairbrush, hairdryer, curlers). Over toilet: cabinet with shelves (tall items in basket). Water-resistant baskets: wire, plastic, coated
- **Shower**: corner caddy (adhesive or tension pole). Shampoo/conditioner/body wash in matching dispenser bottles with labels. Squeegee for glass door after each use (prevents hard water stains). Keep only what you use weekly

### Home Office
- **Desk**: clear surface (monitor, keyboard, lamp, one notebook). Inbox/outbox tray for papers. Drawer organizers (pens, paper clips, sticky notes, washi tape, business cards). Cable management (under-desk tray for power strip + cables, cable ties, spiral wrap to gather wires). Document sorter (vertical file for active projects vs archive). Keep: active project visible. Archive: away in cabinets
- **Filing**: keep <1 year of paper (most documents digital). Active files: within arm's reach (recent bills, current projects). Archive: banker's box (label contents + date, store out of way). Digitize: scan receipts, statements, contracts (Evernote, Google Drive, Dropbox, Notion, searchable PDF). Shred sensitive documents after digitizing

## Decluttering Methods

### KonMari (Marie Kondo)
1. **Commit to declutter**. Visualize ideal lifestyle. 2. **Discard first** (then organize). 3. **By category, not location** (clothes → books → papers → miscellaneous → sentimental). 4. **Does it spark joy?** Hold each item. If not, thank it + discard. 5. **Everything has a home**. 6. **Vertical storage** (file folding). 7. **Containers** limit storage (a specific box for each category = natural limit)
  - Question for "joy": does it make you happy when you see it? Useful joy: requires less emotional response (potato peeler may not spark joy but has purpose — in KonMari, joy for utilitarian items = appreciation for its role)
  - Order matters: easier categories first build decision muscles for hardest (sentimental). Don't declutter sentimental items first

### Swedish Death Cleaning (Döstädning)
- Gentle declutter — sort gradually (don't leave burden for family). Start with "nobody would want this" (worn-out clothes, expired food, broken items, gifts you won't use, duplicates)
- At any age: simplify as you go, not just for old age. Think: "If I died tomorrow, would this item make my family's life harder?" Remove that item now. Not morbid — practical kindness
- Keep: meaningful items with clear plan (pass down, display). Share stories with family about items (write notes). Identify who gets what

### Minimalist Game
- Day 1: discard 1 item. Day 2: discard 2 items... Day 30: discard 30 items = 465 total. Remove from home: sell, donate, trash. Adapted version: 30 days of 1-30 items, any order (don't need to increase each day — just commit to 30 consecutive days of discarding). After: maintain with one-in-one-out

## Storage Solutions ($ vs $$)
- **Free**: shoe boxes (for drawer organizers — cover with wrapping paper/washi tape for uniform look). Egg cartons (small item storage: screws, beads, pins). Mason jars (pantry: bulk goods, office supplies). Toilet paper rolls (cord management — store charging cables sorted)
- **Cheap** ($): IKEA Skubb boxes (fabric storage boxes for closet bins, bins), IKEA Variera (pantry organizers: can racks, shelf inserts). Command hooks (temporary wall hooks for hanging). Tension rods (vertical dividers for baking sheets, under-sink — split vertical space for organizer). Baskets (wire mesh for visible storage; solid for hidden). Clear plastic bins (stackable, uniform sizes, with labels — not random mismatched, not 5 types mixed)
  - Shelf risers: display cans/tall items in separate rows. Drawer dividers: adjustable, separate utensils, tools, socks, etc.
- **Moderate** ($$): pull-out cabinet shelves (full-extension drawer slides make deep cabinets usable). Spice rack (door-mounted or drawer insert). Pegboard (wall storage for tools, craft supplies, kitchen tools — versatile, customizable with hooks + shelves. IKEA Skadis line, or Wall Control metal pegboard). Label maker (labels for everything: bins, shelves, drawers — using label maker or handwritten on white tape. Makes putting things away easy)

## Digital Declutter
- **Email**: unsubscribe from unwanted newsletters (Unroll.me, leave the mail list). Zero inbox: archive/delete everything after reading. Use filters / labels (auto-sort). Two-minute rule: if you can reply in <2 min, do it now. Use "Snooze" (Gmail) for emails to deal with later with a due date
- **Phone**: remove unused apps (if not used in 30 days, delete). Organize home screen: first page = most used (5-6 apps + folder for others). Second page = less used. Use folders (only one folder page?). Turn off notifications for non-essential apps (90% of notifications aren't needed — Instagram, shopping, games, news, stocks). Grayscale display: reduces phone addictiveness (color = dopamine priming)
- **Files**: clean desktop (zero files — use Downloads folder for temp, organize at day's end). Organize documents: <3 levels deep. Named by: YYYY-MM-DD-Project-Description. Archive old files >1 year. Digital photos: delete duplicates + blurry (use Photo organizer apps: Adobe Lightroom, Google Photos remove duplicates). Organize by date + event. Back up (3-2-1)
