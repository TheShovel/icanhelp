# Gardening Basics

## Plant Needs
- **Sun**: full sun (6+ hrs direct), partial sun (4-6 hrs), shade (<4 hrs)
- **Water**: most vegetables 1-1.5 inches/week (including rain) — deep soak less frequent > shallow daily
- **Soil**: loam (sand + silt + clay) is ideal. Test pH — most plants prefer 6.0-7.0
- **Nutrients**: N (nitrogen = leaves), P (phosphorus = flowers/fruit), K (potassium = roots/health)
  - NPK ratio on fertilizer: 10-10-10 = balanced, 5-10-5 = more blooms, 21-0-0 = high nitrogen (lawns)
- **Space**: check seed packet — overcrowding = disease + poor growth
- **Hardiness zone**: determines what perennials survive your winter (USDA zones, find yours online)

## Starting a Garden
1. Choose location (sun, access to water, protected from wind)
2. Prepare soil: remove grass/weeds, till 8-12", mix in compost
3. Raised beds: better drainage, warmer soil, easier access — 4x8 ft, 12" deep minimum
4. In-ground: cheaper, more space, more weeds. Containers: portable, limited root space
5. Start seeds indoors (6-8 weeks before last frost) or direct sow after frost
6. Harden off seedlings: gradually introduce to outdoors over a week
7. Plant on appropriate date for your zone (check frost dates)

## Easy Vegetables for Beginners
- **Tomatoes**: need support (cage/stake), full sun, warm. Cherry tomatoes easiest
- **Lettuce**: quick (30-60 days), partial shade OK, succession plant every 2 weeks
- **Green beans**: easy from seed, pole beans need trellis, bush beans don't
- **Zucchini**: very productive (3 plants feed a family), watch for powdery mildew
- **Radishes**: fastest (25-30 days), good for kids, grow in cool weather
- **Carrots**: need loose soil (no rocks), thin seedlings to 2" apart
- **Peppers**: need warmth, start indoors, bell peppers slower than hot peppers
- **Cucumbers**: vine = trellis, bush = container, need consistent water
- **Herbs**: basil, mint (invasive — container only!), rosemary, thyme, chives — easy, expensive at store

## Common Problems
- **Wilting**: overwatered (yellow + droopy) or underwatered (crispy + droopy) — check soil moisture
- **Yellow leaves**: overwatered (lower, mushy) or nitrogen deficiency (older leaves, pale)
- **Holes in leaves**: caterpillars (pick off), slugs (beer trap, diatomaceous earth)
- **White powder on leaves**: powdery mildew — improve air flow, neem oil, avoid overhead watering
- **Aphids**: tiny bugs on stems — spray with water, insecticidal soap, ladybugs eat them
- **Blossom end rot**: black bottom on tomatoes/peppers — calcium issue, inconsistent watering
- **No fruit on squash**: need pollination — plant flowers nearby, hand pollinate with brush
- **Birds**: netting, scare tape, decoys (move them, birds figure them out)

## Garden Maintenance
- Water at base (avoid leaves = less disease). Morning best (evaporation, less fungal)
- Mulch: 2-3" layers — straw, wood chips, grass clippings — retains moisture, suppresses weeds
- Weed regularly (small weeds easy, big weeds disturb roots)
- Fertilize: every 2-4 weeks during growing season, less for heavy feeders
- Prune: remove dead/diseased leaves, improve air flow, train vines
- Stake: tomatoes, peppers (if heavy), peas, beans
- Succession planting: plant new crop when old one finishes (continuous harvest)
- Crop rotation: don't plant same family in same spot yearly (prevents soil disease)

## Composting
- Greens (N): kitchen scraps, grass clippings, coffee grounds — wet, soft
- Browns (C): dry leaves, cardboard, paper, straw, wood chips — dry, brown
- Ratio: 3:1 browns:greens by volume
- Turn pile every 1-2 weeks for oxygen, keep moist (like wrung-out sponge)
- Hot compost: proper ratio + moisture + aeration = 130-160°F, ready in 3-6 months
- Cold compost: just pile it, takes 1-2 years
- Don't add: meat, dairy, diseased plants, weeds with seeds, pet waste
- Worms (vermicompost): red wigglers, great for small spaces/indoor

## Seasonal Tasks
- **Spring**: prepare soil, plant cool crops (peas, lettuce, spinach), start warm crops indoors
- **Summer**: harvest, water, weed, pest control, plant warm crops (tomatoes, peppers, squash)
- **Fall**: harvest remaining, plant cool crops for fall (kale, broccoli, garlic), add compost to beds
- **Winter**: plan next year, order seeds, clean/maintain tools, mulch perennials
- Last frost: safe to plant warm crops. First frost: harvest everything tender, cover remaining

## Tools
- Trowel: small digging. Pruners (secateurs): cutting stems/branches. Hoe: weeding
- Garden fork: turning soil. Spade: digging. Rake: leveling. Hose with adjustable nozzle
- Gloves: protect hands. Knee pad: comfort. Watering can: gentle watering for seedlings
- Clean tools after use (prevent disease spread). Oil wooden handles yearly
