# DIY & Home Repair

## Painting a Room
1. Clear room, cover floors (drop cloth), tape edges (painter's tape)
2. Patch holes (spackle + putty knife), sand smooth, clean walls
3. Prime if: new drywall, dark to light color, stains, glossy to matte
4. Cut in edges (brush around trim, ceiling, corners)
5. Roll walls: W pattern, overlapping passes, don't let roller dry out
6. Two thin coats > one thick coat (wait 2-4 hours between)
7. Remove tape while paint slightly wet (peels cleaner)
8. Tools: roller + frame + extension pole, 2" angled brush, tray, drop cloth

## Hanging Shelves / TV
1. Find studs (stud finder, or tap for hollow sound vs solid)
2. Use level! Mark both stud locations and bracket holes
3. Predrill holes (smaller than screw to prevent splitting)
4. Screw into studs (NOT drywall alone for anything heavy)
5. Drywall anchors: toggle bolts (heavy), molly bolts (medium), expansion anchors (light)
6. TV mount: lag bolts into studs, minimum 2 studs for large TV, use included hardware

## Fixing a Running Toilet
1. Remove tank lid, check flapper — most common cause
2. If flapper is warped/cracked: replace ($5 part, universal fit)
3. If chain too short/long: adjust so flapper seats properly
4. If water level too high: adjust float (screw on fill valve or bend float arm)
5. If fill valve leaking: replace fill valve assembly ($15, easy DIY)
6. If handle sticks: clean/lubricate or replace flush handle
7. If phantom flush (occasionally runs): bad flapper seal or debris on seal

## Fixing a Leaky Faucet
- **Compression faucet** (two handles): replace rubber washer at stem
- **Cartridge faucet** (single handle): replace cartridge (take old one to hardware store)
- **Ball faucet** (single handle with dome): replace cam/washer/spring assembly
- **Ceramic disk** (single handle, modern): clean or replace ceramic cartridge
- Steps: shut off water under sink, plug drain, disassemble carefully (remember order!), take parts to store, reassemble

## Power Drill Basics
- Chuck: tighten/loosen to change bits. Keyless: twist by hand. Keyed: use chuck key
- Speed: slow (high torque, drilling metal), fast (low torque, drilling wood/screwdriving)
- Torque setting (clutch ring): 1-20 — lower for small screws (prevents stripping), higher for large
- Drill bit: twist bit (wood/metal), spade bit (large holes wood), masonry bit (brick/concrete)
- Screwdriver bit: Phillips (PH1 PH2 PH3), flathead, Torx (T20 T25 common), hex, square
- Forward (tighten), reverse (loosen). Always use when driving long screws

## Furniture Assembly
- Read entire instructions first (check you have all parts and tools)
- Lay out all parts sorted by size/type. Hardware in cups/bowls
- Hand-tighten first, then tighten fully once aligned
- Use power drill with clutch set low (don't strip cheap particle board)
- If hole doesn't align: loosen adjacent screws, adjust, retighten
- Common issues: cam locks not fully turned (rotate 180° until click), dowels too tight (sand a bit)
- Apply felt pads to bottom to protect floors

## Weatherproofing
- **Windows**: apply caulk around frame gaps, weatherstripping tape on sash, window film kit for old single-pane
- **Doors**: weatherstripping around frame, door sweep at bottom (adjust or replace), draft stopper
- **Attic**: check insulation depth (R-30 to R-60 recommended), seal air leaks (around pipes, vents, recessed lights)
- **Basement/crawl**: seal foundation cracks (hydraulic cement), sump pump, vapor barrier on dirt floor

## Basic Carpentry
- **Cutting**: measure twice, cut once. Use speed square for 90° and 45° marks
- Circular saw: blade depth = 1/4" deeper than material, shoe flat, blade on waste side of line
- Hand saw: start with thumb guide, long smooth strokes, 45° angle, let saw do work
- Sandpaper grit: coarse (40-60) remove material, medium (80-120) smooth, fine (150-220) finish, very fine (320+) between coats
- Pocket holes (Kreg jig): angled hole + screw + glue = strong joint without visible screws
- Wood glue stronger than wood itself — clamp for 30+ min, full cure 24 hr

## Tool Safety
- Read manual before using new tool
- Safety glasses always (no exceptions)
- Ear protection for loud tools (saws, impact drivers)
- Dust mask for sanding, cutting, anything creating particles
- Disconnect power before changing blades/bits
- Don't wear loose clothing, tie back long hair, remove jewelry
- Keep tools sharp (dull tools are more dangerous than sharp — require more force, slip more)
- Never remove safety guards
- Unplug tools not in use (don't leave plugged in near children)
