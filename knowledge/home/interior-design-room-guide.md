# Interior Design: Room-by-Room Guide

## Entryway/Mudroom
- **Function**: drop zone for coats, shoes, bags, mail, keys. First impression of home. Must handle daily incoming + outgoing traffic
- **Essentials**: bench (sit to remove shoes — 18" high, 15-18" deep, with storage beneath or cubbies), hooks (at least 3-4 per person, varying heights for kids + adults, 65-72" from floor for adult, 45-55" for children). Shoe storage (cabinet preferred for visual clutter, or tiered rack). Surface + bowl for keys/mail (small table/ console 30-36" deep). Mirror (check appearance before leaving) ideally 24-36" wide
  - Rug: durable (indoor-outdoor, washable — Ruggable the most popular for mudrooms). Easy to clean, handle moisture. Boot tray (for wet shoes). 4 ft × 6 ft typical for entry
- **Mudroom if space**: lockers (each family member has own cubby + hooks + basket). Built-in bench (custom). Pet station (food bowls, leash hooks, towel for wet paws). Charging drawer for devices (outlet inside drawer)
- **Small space**: narrow console table (6-8" deep for mail + keys). Over-door hook rack. Wall-mounted coat hooks (not a freestanding rack — uses no floor space). Mirror + shelf above. Slim shoe cabinet (IKEA shoe cabinet 6" deep, folds flush to wall — packs more storage than any other type)

## Living Room
- **Seating arrangement**: conversation circle (facing each other, 5-10 ft between). TV = one side, other seating perpendicular/angled. Don't push all furniture against walls (float sofa to define zone). 12" from wall to back of sofa
  - Sofa sizes: 3-seater (72-84"), loveseat (48-60"), armchair (30-36"), chaise (connected to sectional — 12-18" extra). Sectional: L-shape (one side 84-100" + return 60-72"), U-shape (large rooms only). Distance from sofa to coffee table: 14-18". Walkway behind sofa: 30-36" (with clearance for chairs. TV viewing distance: 1.5-2.5 × diagonal screen size
  - Scale: oversized sofa in small room = cramped. Too-small scattered furniture = feels empty like cavern. Choose appropriately. Don't block windows or heat registers
- **Lighting**: 3 layers — ambient (overhead, maybe dimmer), task (reading lamp, floor lamp next to reading chair), accent (art light, picture light, uplighting on plants/archways). Table lamp height: shade at eye level when seated (40-44" from floor). Floor lamp: 55-65" from floor. Pendant: 60-66" from floor over side table. Avoid: single overhead light without dimmer (harsh, unflattering)
- **Coffee table**: 14-18" from sofa, height matched to sofa seat cushion or 2" lower. Size: 1/2 to 2/3 length of sofa. Shape: rectangle (most flexible), oval (good for high-traffic), round/ square (small rooms). Storage: drawers, lift-top, shelf, or basket
- **Side tables**: 22-24" high. Place within arm's reach of each seat (no more than 4-8" gap). Top: lamp + drink coaster + (optional) book or decoration. For high-traffic areas: metal or stone base > wood (dents easily). Nesting tables: flexible for multiple seating zones

## Dining Room
- **Table**: rectangular (standard — seats 6 comfortably 60-72" × 36-40"). Round (intimate — seats 4-6 with 40-54" diameter). Square (good symmetry for 4-6 but hard to fit many). Drop-leaf (flexible space). Extendable (expands for gatherings). Minimum: 30" per person width (stools = 24-30"). Depth: 12" each person's plate. Clearance: table edge to wall/other furniture = 48-60" for chairs + walkway
- **Chairs**: 18" seat height (standard side chair). Armchair: extra 6" width. Pull-out clearance: 18" behind chair when seated. Standard: 10-14 chairs for 10' table if rectangular. Chairs with arms: wider, need more space
- **Lighting**: chandelier over table, centered, 1/2 to 2/3 table width. 30-36" above table surface. Bottom of pendant = at eye level when standing (people should see each other under fixture). Dimmable for ambiance. For long table: 2 smaller pendants over island or table lengths over 8'
- **Buffet/sideboard**: storage for serving dishes, linens, silverware. Surface for serving buffet-style. Height: 32-36". 48-60" would be typical? Width 48-60" or longer. Provide counter space for serving. Clearance between table and buffet: 48-60" (people behind chairs need to pass + buffet users)

## Kitchen
- **Work triangle**: sink, stove, fridge — paths between them should total <26 ft (for efficient traffic flow). No leg of triangle <4 ft or >9 ft. No major traffic flow cutting through triangle. Island should not block triangle path
- **Countertops**: 36" high (standard). Deep: 25" standard depth (counter + backsplash). Prep zone: 36" min counter space next to sink. Landing zone on either side of stove (15" min), fridge (15" min landing zone). Island: 36" min clearance, 44-48" if multiple cooks. Standard island height: 36". Seating at island: 30" high (counter stools) for 36" counter, or leave 12-18" overhang for knee room
- **Cabinets**: upper cabinets 18" above counter (54" from floor bottom). Standard: 30-42" tall uppers. Deep drawers for pots/pans (pull-out > shelf). Drawer front: full-overlay (modern) vs inset (traditional)
  - Drawers: interior depth 6-8" for flatware, 12-15" for cooking tools, 20-24" for pots and pans (with pull-out, don't stack!). Roll-out trays for lower cabinets (pull-out shelves > shelves behind doors)
- **Backsplash**: 4" to full height (counter to bottom of cabinets). Subway tile (3x6, classic, cheap). Mosaic (glass / stone / metal). Quartz slab (seamless, matches countertop). Grout: epoxy (stain-proof) best for kitchen. Thinset for tile, caulk at corners + counter junction (silicone)
- **Sink**: undermount (clean, countertop wipes into sink — no lip/gap). Top-mount (drop-in, cheap). Material: stainless steel 16-18 gauge (thicker = quieter, less dent). Apron-front farmhouse sink (traditional, needs larger base cabinet). Single basin (best for large pots) vs double (separate wash + rinse). Sink depth: 9-10" standard. Faucet: minimum 16" height for pots, pull-down spray head
- **Countertops backsplash**: material: quartz (maintenance-free), granite (natural, needs sealing), marble (beautiful, etches, shows wear quickly), butcher block (warm, scratches, oil), solid surface (Corian, seamless, repairable)
- **Pantry**: walk-in (great if space — 24-36" deep + 4-5 ft wide for aisles). Pull-out (tall, narrow cabinet behind full-extension pull-out shelves. Deeper drawers for cans, jars. Shelf depth: 12-16". Can organize: lazy Susan (corner cabinets), tiered shelves (cans visible), door racks (spices, condiments). Zone: like a pantry: food, snacks, baking, breakfast. Keep like items together, store by frequency of use at eye level

## Bedroom
- **Bed placement**: center of wall, headboard against wall (not under window if possible). Doorside clearance for walking + making bed: 18" minimum. Walking path to bathroom and closet: 24-36". Bed in front of window: possible but often loses natural light + ease of opening window = suboptimal. Two nightstands (one each side) each 18-24" wide
- **Nightstands**: same height as mattress top (same height as mattress + box spring: ~25") — table at 25-28" high (standard mattress height). Need accessible from pillow. Lamp on each (task reading + ambiance). Charging outlets built-in
- **Dresser**: 30-42" wide. Place perpendicular to wall to create room divider? No, dresser against wall for walking path. Mirror above dresser (for getting ready). Height: back of dresser about 32-36". Closet: take advantage of full height — hang rods: double for shirts/pants/pants, long for dresses/coats (40" tall for shirts, 60" for long garments). Shelves above top rod for out-of-season items
- **Rug**: 2' beyond bed on each side + foot. Place: if king bed, 8x10' works, but 9x12' fits better. Area rug under bed extends 18-24" beyond on foot + sides
- **Lighting**: bedside reading lamp (task). Overhead on dimmer. Blackout curtains (sleep quality — the darker the better, cellular shades block light). Natural light: during day = good for waking. Layered: ceiling fan + overhead + reading light + maybe nightlight

## Bathroom
- **Vanity**: standard 34-36" cabinet height (comfort height 36" for many). Counter depth 22". Standard basin depth 19-22". Single or double sink (double needs 60"+ countertop). Mirror: 2-4" above backsplash, height depends (center at eye level ~60-66" from floor). Medicine cabinet (recessed preferred). If small: wall-hung toilet (floating = modern, easier to clean under). If tiny: pocket door (saves floor swing space)
- **Shower**: minimum 36×36" (walk-in). 32×60" (standard neo-angle/rectangle). Better: 42×60". Rain shower head (centered, 7-8 ft high). Handheld (for cleaning, shaving, pets, kids, elderly). Curb height: 2-3" (or curbless for aging in place, with linear drain). Width: 48" for comfortable. Bench: 12-18" deep, same height as seated + grab bar adjacent
- **Toilet**: 12" rough-in (standard). Elongated bowl (more comfortable). Chair height (17-19" seat height — easier for elderly). WaterSense label (1.28 GPF). Leave 15-18" from center to wall/toilet on each side
- **Ventilation**: exhaust fan vented to outside (not attic). Humidity sensor switch (turns off when humidity drops). 50 CFM minimum for small bath, 1 CFM/sq ft. Timer switch better than simple on/off — run 15-20 min after shower to clear moisture. Duct size: 4" minimum, rigid metal not flex (flexible duct = resistance + condensation + mold risk inside flex)
