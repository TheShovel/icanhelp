# Moving & Relocation

## Timeline

### 8+ Weeks Before
- Declutter: sell/donate/trash everything you don't want to move (moving costs by weight/volume — every item costs money)
  - Have a yard sale, list on FB Marketplace, donate for tax deduction
- Get quotes from 3+ moving companies (or rent truck for DIY)
  - Full service: most expensive, they pack + load + transport + unload
  - Self-service (PODS, U-HAUL U-Box): they drop container, you pack/load, they transport, you unload
  - DIY truck rental: cheapest but most work, need helpers
  - Check reviews (BBB, Yelp, Google) — moving scams exist (low quote, then hold belongings hostage)
- Book movers/truck (schedule fills up, especially summer, weekends, month-end)

### 4-6 Weeks Before
- Change of address: USPS (forward mail — $1.10, online), DMV (vehicle registration), voter registration, banks, credit cards, insurance, subscriptions, IRS
  - Make a list of ALL accounts that need address change — it's easy to miss one
- Transfer utilities (electric, gas, water, internet, trash): schedule disconnect at old + connect at new for same day
  - Internet/cable: often requires 2+ week lead time to install at new place
- Notify: employer (HR), landlords (written notice per lease terms, typically 30-60 days), insurance companies (home/renters, auto rates change by zip code)

### 2-3 Weeks Before
- Start packing non-essentials: off-season clothes, books, decor, kitchen extras, garage items
  - Packing supplies: boxes (various sizes), markers, tape, bubble wrap, packing paper, furniture blankets
  - Label boxes clearly: room + contents ("Kitchen - pots & pans"). Color-coded labels per room
  - Heavy items in small boxes (books in small boxes!), light items in large boxes
  - Pack a "first night" box: toiletries, change of clothes, meds, chargers, sheets, coffee, snacks

### 1 Week Before
- Finish packing everything except daily essentials (you'll need more time than you think)
- Confirm movers/truck rental: time, address, access (elevator? stairs? parking? loading dock?)
  - Reserve elevator if moving in/out of apartment building (often requires deposit)
- Arrange: parking permit for moving truck, building elevator reservation, key pickup/dropoff
- Clean old place (or hire cleaning service to get security deposit back if renting)
  - Landlord has 30 days to return deposit minus itemized deductions — clean thoroughly and photograph everything
- Defrost freezer (takes 24+ hours), drain garden hoses, final trash removal

### Moving Day
- Protect floors (ram board/ cardboard), doorways, walls — movers may not be careful
- Keep essentials bag with you: wallet, keys, phone, charger, 1st night supplies, important documents
- Take photos of old place (empty, clean) for deposit return
- Read water meter at both properties (if applicable)
- Tip movers: $20-40/person for local, $40-60/person for long distance, more for difficult moves (stairs, heavy items, hot weather)

## After Moving
- Unpack essentials first (bedroom, bathroom, kitchen) — you can live out of boxes for a week
- Set up bed and sheets before anything else (you need sleep after moving day)
- Change locks (you don't know who has keys)
- Set up internet ASAP (often takes days, coordinate before move)
- Update insurance (home/renters, auto — rates change with zip code)
- Find: emergency room, pharmacy, grocery store, gas station, hardware store
- Meet neighbors (introduce yourself within first week — makes you feel at home, builds community)
- Register to vote at new address (if changed states, update driver's license within 30-90 days depending on state)
- Find new: doctor, dentist, vet (ask current ones for recommendations)
- Explore: walking routes, parks, coffee shops, restaurants — "third places" help you feel at home

## Long Distance Move
- **Driving**: plan route, book hotel (don't drive more than 8 hours/day after a full day of loading)
  - Ship car (expensive but saves miles/wear)
- **Air travel**: ship belongings (freight, UPack, PODS) before you go — stay with friends/family or Airbnb while waiting
  - Fly with essentials only — everything else arrives later
- **Documents to keep on you** (never in moving truck): passport, birth certificate, marriage/divorce certificates, medical records, tax returns, diplomas, deeds, financial documents, pet vaccination records
- **Vehicle registration & license**: most states require new residents to get license and register car within 30-90 days
  - Check: smog check needed? New license test? (some states require written/knowledge test)

## Downsizing
- Before moving: measure new place (rooms, doorways, stairs, elevators) — furniture may not fit
  - Create floor plan with measurements — plan furniture layout BEFORE move
  - Large items that likely won't fit: king bed (if new room is small), sectional sofa (doesn't fit through narrow stairs/doorways), oversized desk/treadmill
- Sell/donate before move: moving costs $1-2/lb — it's often cheaper to buy new furniture at destination than pay to move old
  - "Is this worth paying $X to move it?" (cost to move: ~$1-2/lb)
- Digitalize: scan important documents, photos, records — then shred originals
- Sentimental items: this is hardest — keep only what matters most, take photos of the rest

## Moving with Pets
- Keep pets in quiet room on moving day (stressful, doors open, strangers in house)
- **Cats**: carrier is safest. Move cat last — bring to new place first, set up in bathroom/quiet room with litter box, food, water for 24-48 hours before letting explore
  - Don't let cat outside at new place for 2-4 weeks — they may try to return to old home
  - Microchip and collar with new info
- **Dogs**: walk before loading/ unloading. At new place, walk to explore neighborhood, set up bed/crate in quiet area
  - Maintain routine as much as possible (feeding, walks, bedtime)
- Update pet ID tags and microchip with new address immediately

## Moving with Kids
- Explain the move positively: adventure, new room, new friends, don't bad-mouth the old/move
- Let them pack their own special box (stuff that matters to them)
- Set up kid's room FIRST at new place — familiarity anchors them
- Maintain routines (bedtime, meals) throughout transition
- Say proper goodbyes: visit favorite places, have farewell party, exchange contact info with friends
- Signs of stress: regression (bedwetting, clingy), behavior changes — extra patience, reassurance, quality time
