# Concrete & Masonry

Working with concrete, brick, block, and stone for home projects.

## Concrete Basics
- **Composition**: Portland cement (10-15%) + water (15-20%) + fine aggregate/sand (25-30%) + coarse aggregate/gravel (30-45%) + air (5-8%)
- **Water-to-cement ratio**: Lower = stronger concrete. Target 0.45-0.50 (45-50 lbs water per 100lbs cement). Too much water weakens concrete (creates voids)
- **Curing**: Hydration reaction continues for weeks. Keep concrete moist for minimum 7 days — prevents cracking. Cure with wet burlap, cure compound spray, or plastic sheeting
- **Types**: 
  - 2500-3000 PSI: Standard slabs, walkways (most DIY)
  - 3500-4000 PSI: Driveways, garage floors, structural
  - 4000-5000 PSI: Heavy-duty, industrial
  - Fast-setting: Quickrete — sets in 20-40 min, good for posts, small repairs
  - High-early: Gets to strength in 3-7 days vs 28 for standard

## Mixing & Pouring
- **Slump test**: Measure consistency — fill cone in 3 layers, rod each 25 times, lift cone. Slump of 3-4" is typical for flatwork. Too stiff (1-2" slump) hard to work; too wet (5"+) weakens concrete
- **Prepping subgrade**: Remove organic material (sod, topsoil), compact soil (tamper or plate compactor), 4-6" compacted gravel base for slabs
- **Form work**: 2x4 or 2x6 lumber staked in place. Level forms — concrete will fill to form height. Use release oil on forms so they detach easily
- **Reinforcement**: Rebar (steel bars, 1/2" for slabs, 5/8" for footings) — placed in middle of slab depth. Wire mesh (6x6 — for thinner slabs, 4"). Tie with wire at intersections
- **Pouring**: Pour concrete, move in wheelbarrow or use concrete buggy. Don't dump in one spot and push — move slowly across forms
- **Screeding**: Straight 2x4 dragged across forms in sawing motion — levels concrete to form height
- **Floating**: Bull float (large flat tool) smooths surface, pushes gravel down. Done immediately after screeding
- **Edging**: Edger tool creates rounded edge — run along forms to prevent chipping
- **Joint cutting**: Control joints prevent random cracking — cut 1/4 slab depth with groover or concrete saw. Space joints 2-3x slab thickness (4" slab = joints every 8-12 ft)
- **Broom finish**: Drag broom across floated concrete for non-slip surface. Driveway, walkways, steps

## Brick & Block
- **Common brick types**: Clay brick (most common — red/tan/white), concrete block (CMU — 8x8x16 standard, hollow core), architectural block (split face, ground face)
- **Mortar types (ASTM C270)**:
  - Type N: General purpose, 750 PSI — for above-grade walls, veneers
  - Type S: Higher strength, 1800 PSI — for below-grade, retaining walls, load-bearing
  - Type M: Highest strength, 2500 PSI — for heavy loads, foundations
  - Type O: Low strength, 350 PSI — for interior/non-load-bearing
- **Laying brick/block**: Butter end with mortar → set in place with slight wiggling → tap level with trowel handle → scrape excess mortar. Check level every 2-3 bricks
- **Bond patterns**: Running bond (most common — bricks staggered by half length), stack bond (aligned grid — weaker, needs reinforcement), herringbone (45° pattern for paving), English bond (alternating stretchers and headers)
- **Weep holes**: Leave every 32" in bottom row of brick veneer — allows moisture to drain from cavity wall
- **lintels**: Steel angle over windows/doors in brick veneer — supports weight of brick above. Must extend 6" beyond opening each side
- **Tie walls**: Metal wall ties connect brick veneer to wood frame — every 16" horizontal, 24" vertical, nailed to sheathing

## Paving (Concrete Pavers)
- **Base preparation**: 6-8" compacted gravel base + 1" bedding sand (sharp sand, not play sand — drains better). Compact gravel in 3" lifts, using plate compactor
- **Laying patterns**: Running bond, herringbone, basket weave, random. Start from straight edge (usually house wall or existing path)
- **Cutting**: Wet saw (best), angle grinder with diamond blade, brick splitter, chisel and hammer for snap cut
- **Joint sand**: Polymeric sand (contains polymer — dries hard, prevents weed growth, ants). Sweep into joints, compact with plate compactor, sweep more, mist water (activates polymer). Standard sand needs annual sweeping
- **Edge restraint**: Plastic or concrete curbing around border — prevents pavers from shifting outward over time

## Stone
- **Flagstone**: Natural stone (bluestone, sandstone, limestone, slate) — irregular thickness and shape. Install on concrete base (solid) or gravel bed (dry laid). For patios, walkways
- **Stacked stone veneer**: Thin stone pieces applied to wall — natural (real stone slices) or manufactured (lightweight concrete). Install over metal lath + scratch coat
- **Dry stack walls**: No mortar — gravity holds. Taper slightly inward (batter) — 1" in per foot of height. Good for retaining walls under 3ft, garden walls
- **Mortared stone**: More permanent — mortar applied between stones. Mix Type S mortar with lime for workability. Clean stone with muriatic acid after mortar sets (24 hrs)

## Safety
- **Cement burns**: Wet concrete is caustic (pH 12-13) — can cause third-degree burns on skin contact. Wear rubber boots, gloves, long pants. Wash exposed skin with vinegar (neutralizes alkali)
- **Silica dust**: Cutting/grinding concrete produces crystalline silica — wear N95 or better respirator, use water to suppress dust
- **Lifting**: 80lb bags of concrete — team lift or use dolly. Prevents back injury
- **Mixing**: Dry cement powder can damage lungs — wear respirator when mixing large batches
