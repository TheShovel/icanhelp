# Indoor Plants & Gardening

## Light Requirements
- **Low light** (north window, <3 ft away): snake plant (Sansevieria — extremely tolerant, survives neglect, great for beginners. Architectural shape, NASA air cleaner study). ZZ plant (Zamioculcas — waxy leaves, survives dark corners). Pothos (Epipremnum — trailing, vine, easy, propagate from cuttings in water). Cast iron plant (Aspidistra — name appropriate, tolerates low light, neglect). Philodendron (heartleaf — trailing, easy, tolerates low light but slower growth)
  - Signs of insufficient light: leggy growth (stretch toward window), small leaves, slow/no growth, variegated leaves turn solid green (plant trying to maximize chlorophyll)
- **Medium/bright indirect** (east/west window, 3-6 ft from south window): monstera (Swiss cheese — climbing, huge leaves, fenestrations appear when mature). Fiddle-leaf fig (Ficus lyrata — popular, temperamental, needs consistent care. Avoid drafts + sudden changes). Rubber plant (Ficus elastica — easy, glossy leaves, grows tall). Calathea (prayer plant — dramatic leaf movements, needs humidity, fussy. Brown edges common from dry air/low humidity). Peace lily (Spathiphyllum — droops dramatically when thirsty, white spathe flowers). Spider plant (Chlorophytum — produces plantlets (spiderettes), easy, pet-friendly. Produces offsets on runners)
- **Bright direct** (south/west window, on sill): succulents (jade, aloe, echeveria — need direct sun, well-draining soil, infrequent water (let soil completely dry between)). Cactus (desert species). String of pearls (Senecio rowleyanus — trailing succulent, needs bright light. Sensitive to overwatering). Croton (Codiaeum — bright colorful leaves, drop leaves if moved — responding to changes)

## Watering
- **When to water**: finger test (1-2" deep = dry). For most houseplants: water when top 1-2 inches dry (stick finger in). Succulents: wait until completely dry + leaves slightly deflated. For moisture-lovers (ferns, calatheas, peace lilies): keep consistently moist but not wet. Water less in winter (less growth + more evaporation)
  - Overwatering: yellow leaves, mushy stems, fungus gnats (small flies in soil), mold on soil surface. Root rot: from standing water — roots turn brown, mushy. Cut away rotted roots, repot in fresh dry soil
  - Underwatering: wilting, crispy brown leaf tips, leaves drop, soil pulls away from pot sides. THOROUGHLY soak water until runs out drainage holes (don't just give sips). Bottom-watering: place pot in saucer of water, let soil absorb from below for 10-30 min. Helps prevent compacted soil + root rot for moisture-loving plants
- **Water quality**: most tap water OK (let sit out 24 hours to evaporate chlorine). Sensitive plants (calathea, peace lily, spider plant): brown tips from fluoride/chlorine (use filtered or rainwater). Signs of mineral buildup: white crust on pot rim (leach soil: water heavily 2-3 times, allowing to drain each time)
- **Potting**: drainage holes essential (no drainage = root rot). Pot size: 1-2" larger than root ball (too big = soil stays wet too long = root rot). Repot when: roots circling pot bottom or poking out drainage holes, water runs straight through, plant top-heavy

## Common Houseplants by Difficulty
- **Easy**: pothos, snake plant, ZZ plant, spider plant, philodendron, heartleaf philodendron. Peace lily. Aglaonema (Chinese evergreen — low light, slow growth, drought tolerant, great for beginners)
- **Medium**: monstera, rubber plant, aloe, jade, ferns (Boston, maidenhair — need CONSTANT humidity + moisture, more challenging), palms (parlor, areca — spider mites, need bright indirect). Dracaena (corn plant, Madagascar dragon tree — low/medium light, brown tips occasionally)
- **Challenging**: fiddle-leaf fig (dropping leaves from sudden changes, needs consistent care). Calathea/maranta (prayer plants — humidity-dependent, leaf crusting). Orchids (phalaenopsis/moth orchid — needs specific watering (weekly soak, let dry between), light, bark medium, not soil). Bonsai (daily care, precise watering, pruning, wiring). Venus flytrap (distilled water only, full sun outdoors in a tray of water, go dormant in winter = death indoors without cold)

## Propagation
- **Stem cuttings** (pothos, philodendron, monstera): cut below node (root bump), place in water until roots form (1-2 weeks), then soil. The node has cells to grow roots. Cuttings root in water: change water weekly to prevent rot. Once roots 1-2": plant in soil
- **Leaf cuttings** (snake plant, jade, succulent): cut leaf, dip in rooting hormone, place in damp soil. Snake plant: cut leaf into 3" segments, place bottom end in moist soil
- **Division** (spider plant, peace lily, ferns): separate root ball at repotting time. Ensure each division has both roots + leaves
- **Air layering** (monstera, rubber plant): notch stem, wrap in moist moss + plastic. Wait for roots, cut below new roots and pot the top cutting
