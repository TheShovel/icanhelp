# Home Water Leak Response

## Immediate Steps
1. Stop the water source if safe: turn off the fixture valve or main shutoff.
2. Cut power to wet areas if water is near outlets, appliances, or the breaker panel.
3. Move valuables, rugs, cardboard, and electronics away from water.
4. Photograph damage before cleanup for insurance or landlord records.
5. Remove standing water with towels, mop, wet/dry vacuum, or pump.

## Main Shutoff Basics
Know where the main water shutoff is before an emergency. Common locations include basement walls, crawlspaces, garages, utility rooms, or near the water meter. Turn clockwise for gate valves; quarter-turn valves usually stop water when the handle is perpendicular to the pipe.

## Drying Timeline
- First 24 hours: remove standing water, increase airflow, start dehumidification.
- 24–48 hours: pull wet baseboards or carpet padding if needed; monitor hidden moisture.
- After 48 hours: mold risk increases, especially in drywall, insulation, and particleboard.
Fast drying prevents more damage than cosmetic cleanup.

## Tools That Help
- Wet/dry vacuum for non-sewage water.
- Box fans for airflow after electrical hazards are handled.
- Dehumidifier to pull moisture from air and materials.
- Moisture meter for drywall, flooring, and trim.
- Pipe repair clamp or self-fusing silicone tape for temporary control.

## When to Call a Professional
Call a plumber for burst pipes, main line issues, unknown leaks, repeated pressure loss, water heater leaks, or anything behind walls you cannot isolate. Call water mitigation if water covers large areas, reaches insulation, affects multiple rooms, or sits for more than a day.

## Sewage or Floodwater
Do not treat sewage, storm floodwater, or toilet backup as ordinary clean water. Wear protective gear, avoid spreading contamination, discard porous contaminated materials, and use professional cleanup for significant exposure.

## Insurance Notes
Keep photos, receipts, plumber invoices, dates, and notes about what was wet. Do not make permanent repairs before documenting the loss unless repairs are needed to prevent further damage. Ask the insurer before discarding expensive damaged items.

## Prevention
- Install leak sensors under sinks, near water heaters, behind washers, and near toilets.
- Replace old braided supply lines and cracked washing machine hoses.
- Know how to shut off toilets, sinks, washer valves, and the main line.
- Keep gutters clear and grade soil away from the foundation.
