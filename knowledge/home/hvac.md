# Furnace & HVAC Troubleshooting

## Furnace Not Working

### No Heat - Gas Furnace
- **Check**: thermostat set to heat, set 5°+ above room temp. Gas valve on (valve handle parallel to pipe = on; perpendicular = off; no kink). Power to furnace (switch on unit or nearby — looks like light switch, sometimes red toggle). Circuit breaker not tripped
- **Pilot light** (older furnaces): check if lit. If not: follow pilot relight instructions on furnace (turn gas valve off → wait 5 min for gas to dissipate → turn to pilot → press + hold pilot button → light pilot with long lighter → hold 30-60 sec → release, turn to ON). Modern furnaces: electronic ignition (no standing pilot — intermittent spark or hot surface igniter). If igniter not glowing: bad igniter ($20-50, easy DIY). If igniter glows but no flame: gas valve problem
- **Flame sensor**: if flame lights for 2-3 seconds then goes out (sensor not detecting flame → shuts off gas). Clean sensor with fine grit sandpaper/steel wool (remove, gently sand, replace). Flame sensor is a rod in flame path — senses heat by ionization rod in flame (tiny electrical current passes through flame from rod to burner → proves flame)
- **No power / no response**: reset switch (red button on blower door panel — door must be closed for safety switch). Check float switch (if condensate drain clogged from high efficiency furnace — water backs up, safety switch cuts power to furnace). Check for other safety cutoffs (rollout switch, high limit switch — if tripped, hit reset button, find why it tripped — restricted airflow, dirty filter, overheat)
- **Blower runs but no heat**: igniter not working, gas valve not opening, flame sensor dirty, rollout switch tripped, blocked flue (snow or bird nest)
- **Blower doesn't run**: bad capacitor (humming noise? Start capacitor may be weak — $15-25). Bad motor (inductive or inducer). Limit switch defective, control board failure. Capacitor: test with multimeter (microfarad rating, +/- 10% typically)

### HVAC Maintenance Checklist
- **Monthly**: change air filter (MERV 8 minimum, 1" filters monthly, 4-5" filters every 3-6 months). Keep outdoor unit clear of debris, vegetation (2 ft clearance). Check condensate drain (flush with vinegar/bleach annually)
- **Annual tune-up**: clean evaporator coil (inside). Clean condenser coil (outside — hose down from inside out, remove debris from fins). Check refrigerant pressure (charge). Check electrical connections (tighten, verify amperage draw). Lubricate blower motor (if oil ports exist — many modern are sealed). Check flue pipe + chimney (gas). Clean burners + inspect heat exchanger for cracks
- **Heat pump**: similar to AC, plus reversing valve check. Auxiliary/emergency heat test. Defrost cycle check (board setting, outdoor thermostat). Air handler: clean drain pan + line

## Air Conditioning Issues
- **Not cooling**: check filter (dirty = frozen coil). Check outdoor unit running (fan spinning, compressor running). If outdoor unit not running: check capacitor (bad = humming). If running but not cooling: may be low refrigerant (call pro). Coil frozen: turn off AC, run fan only to thaw (could take 24 hrs). Dirty coils reduce airflow → freeze. Also: blockage? Low airflow: frozen coil, compressor struggling, running high current
- **Warm air from vents**: outside unit running? Not running. Thermostat set correctly? Wait. If running but not cooling: possibly low refrigerant (leak). Coil frozen (turn off, let thaw, run fan). Contact HVAC pro for refrigerant
- **Noisy AC**: squealing = bad blower belt (or bad bearing). Rattling = loose panel or debris in unit. Banging = compressor issue (internal mechanical or failing mounts) or loose part in compressor compartment
- **Short cycling** (turns on/off frequently): dirty filter, oversized AC (cools too fast, short runs, poor dehumidification, short equipment life). Thermostat location (near heat source causing rapid cycling). Refrigerant overcharge. High pressure switch tripping (dirty condenser)

## Thermostat Issues
- **Blank screen**: dead batteries (most common). Tripped breaker. Check thermostat wiring (disconnect from wall, verify wires firmly inserted). Reconnect, if no display: bad thermostat — replace. Many modern stats: need C wire (common) for power, or batteries as backup. C wire = provides constant 24V to thermostat without relying on battery + keeps Wi-Fi alive
- **Wrong temp reading**: thermostat in drafty location or near heat source (sunlight, kitchen, window). Move thermostat (wiring extension) or use remote sensor
- **Heat pump thermostat settings**: emergency heat = auxiliary heat only (electric resistance), expensive, use only if heat pump broken. Aux heat: may come on in defrost cycle. Heat pump economics: run HP down to its low temp cutoff (typically -10 to 20°F for cold climate) — cheaper than aux heat. Set aux heat lockout: don't run aux above 30-40°F unless needed

## Ductwork
- **Leaks**: tape (mastic sealant > foil tape > duct tape). Mastic: thick paint-on sealant, longest lasting. Seal all visible joints. Return side leaks draw unconditioned air from attic (dusty, hot, high humidity), supply side leaks lose conditioned air
- **Insulation**: attic ducts should be insulated (R-6-8 minimum, R-8-12 recommended). Uninsulated attic ducts lose 20-30% of cooling energy
- **Register placement**: supply registers near exterior walls; returns on interior walls, central location. Room doors undercut 1" to allow return airflow path. Return vents in each room or adequate path back to central return

## Heat Pump Specific
- **Defrost cycle**: outdoor unit runs in cooling mode briefly (2-5 min) to melt ice buildup from heat pump operation in cold weather. Water vapor + frost = defrost needed. During defrost: outdoor fan stops, compressor runs, hot gas reverses back to outdoor coil to heat it up, melts ice from outdoor coil (can produce steam). Indoor fan may stop or run electric strip heat during defrost to avoid blowing cold air
- **Balance point**: outdoor temperature where heat pump output = home heat loss. Below this: HP runs continuously + auxiliary heat needed. Typical ~30-40°F for standard HP, -10-20°F for cold climate. Use thermostat lockout: aux heat stays off until outside drops below set balance point
- **Emergency heat**: switch on thermostat (forces electric heat strips only). Use if heat pump broken. Very expensive (electric resistance 3-5x running cost of HP)
