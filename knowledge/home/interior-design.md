# Interior Design & Decorating

## Design Styles
- **Minimalist**: clean lines, neutral colors, uncluttered, function over form — "less is more"
  - Materials: white, concrete, glass, natural wood, lots of light
- **Scandinavian**: cozy minimalism — white walls, warm woods, soft textiles, plants
  - Hygge concept: comfort, warmth, candles, blankets, soft lighting
- **Mid-century modern**: 1950s-60s — clean lines, organic curves, tapered legs, geometric patterns
  - Materials: teak, walnut, leather, molded plastic
- **Industrial**: exposed brick, pipes, concrete, metal — open spaces, high ceilings, raw materials
  - Often in converted warehouses/lofts, dark color palette
- **Bohemian (Boho)**: eclectic, layered, colorful, patterns, textures, global influences
  - Lots of plants, floor cushions, tapestries, vintage pieces
- **Farmhouse**: rustic, reclaimed wood, shiplap, neutral with black accents, apron sinks
- **Modern farmhouse**: cleaner take on farmhouse — white, black windows, mixed metals, less rustic
- **Japandi**: Japanese + Scandinavian — minimalist, natural, warm, serene, high craftsmanship
- **Art Deco**: 1920s-30s — bold geometry, luxury materials (velvet, brass, marble), rich colors
- **Transitional**: blend of traditional and contemporary — comfortable, timeless, not trendy

## Color
- **60-30-10 rule**: 60% dominant (walls, large furniture), 30% secondary (upholstery, curtains), 10% accent (pillows, art, decor)
- **Color temperature**: warm (reds, oranges, yellows) → cozy, energetic. Cool (blues, greens, purples) → calm, spacious
- **Neutral colors**: white, beige, gray, taupe, greige — backdrop for accent colors, rooms feel larger
- **Dark colors**: make room feel smaller, cozier, more intimate — good for large rooms or accent walls
- **Light colors**: reflect light, room feels larger, airier — good for small or dark rooms
- **Accent wall**: one wall painted bold, wallpaper, or textured — creates focal point
- **Color flow**: adjacent rooms should harmonize (not match, but not clash) — use consistent undertones

## Room-by-Room
- **Living room**: focal point (fireplace, TV, large art), seating arranged for conversation, coffee table within reach of seating
  - Rug: large enough that front legs of furniture are on it (or all legs for formal)
  - Lighting: ambient (overhead), task (reading lamp), accent (art light, floor lamp)
- **Bedroom**: bed centered on wall, nightstands on both sides, no TV facing bed (if possible)
  - Good sleep environment: dark (blackout curtains), cool (65-68°F), quiet
  - Headboard defines the bed — even a large art piece works instead of traditional headboard
- **Kitchen**: work triangle (sink, fridge, stove) — efficient flow, less than 26 ft total perimeter
  - Countertops: 36" minimum workspace. Island: 42-48" gap for walkway
  - Open shelving (decorative only) + closed cabinets (practical storage)
- **Bathroom**: towel bars near shower/tub, mirror centered over sink, good lighting at mirror (side lights prevent shadows on face)
- **Entryway**: console table, mirror, shoe storage, coat hooks, key tray — first impression of home

## Furniture Arrangement
- **Traffic flow**: clear paths (24-36" minimum for walkways)
- **Conversation distance**: 5-10 feet between seating for comfortable conversation
- **Furniture scale**: don't fill room with oversized furniture (makes room feel small) OR tiny furniture in large room (feels empty)
  - Measure doorways and hallways before buying large furniture
- **Balance**: visual weight distributed evenly (not all heavy pieces on one side)
- **Focal point**: arrange furniture around it (fireplace, TV, window with a view, large artwork)
- **Rug size**: correct rug anchors the room, too small rug makes room look smaller

## Lighting Layers
- **Ambient**: general illumination (ceiling lights, chandeliers, track lighting)
- **Task**: focused light for activities (reading lamp, under-cabinet kitchen light, bathroom vanity)
- **Accent**: highlights features (art lights, picture lights, uplighting, spotlights)
- **Rule**: 3 sources of light minimum per room (not including windows)
- **Color temperature**: warm (2700-3000K) for living/bedroom, neutral (3500K) for kitchen/bath, cool for task lighting
- **Dimmers**: essential for flexibility — bright for tasks, dim for ambiance

## Window Treatments
- **Curtains/drapes**: floor length (hem just above floor or pool slightly), rod wider than window to allow light when open
  - High rod: mount near ceiling makes room feel taller
  - Layered: sheer (privacy + light) + opaque (blackout for bedroom)
- **Blinds**: cellular/honeycomb (energy efficient), wood/faux wood (classic), aluminum (cheap/modern)
- **Shades**: Roman (soft, fabric), roller (minimal, modern), bamboo (natural texture)

## Art & Decor
- **Art height**: center at eye level (57-60" from floor to center of piece)
- **Gallery wall**: plan on floor first, consistent spacing (2-3" apart), mix of frames but keep cohesive
- **Mirrors**: opposite windows to reflect light, make room feel larger — not opposite doors (reflects energy away in feng shui)
- **Plants**: improve air quality, bring life, texture, and color
  - Low light: snake plant, pothos, ZZ plant, philodendron
  - Bright indirect: monstera, fiddle leaf fig, rubber plant, calathea
- **Books**: arrange both vertically and horizontally, don't overstuff shelves
- **Personal items**: family photos, travel souvenirs, meaningful objects — makes space feel like YOURS

## Budget Tips
- Paint: cheapest way to transform a room ($30-50/gallon)
- Thrift: furniture from thrift stores/ estate sales/ FB Marketplace — paint or reupholster for custom look
- IKEA hacks: basic furniture + upgraded hardware/ paint/ legs = high-end look
- Decor: DIY art (large canvas, abstract), print your own photos, plants from cuttings
- Focus on the things you touch most: good couch, good bed — skimp on less-used items
- Renovation: kitchen/bath add most value, but don't over-improve for neighborhood
- **Temporary rentals**: renter-friendly changes (peel-and-stick wallpaper, removable backsplash, command strips, tension rods, floor mats as rugs)
