# Interior Painting

Preparation, techniques, materials, and finishing for professional-quality painting.

## Paint Types
- **Latex (water-based)**: Most common for walls/ceilings. Dries fast (30 min to touch, 2-4 hr recoat). Low VOC (less odor). Cleans up with soap and water. Flexible — less cracking with temperature changes. Lasts longer than oil in most applications
- **Oil-based (alkyd)**: Durable, smooth finish, self-leveling. For trim, cabinets, doors, high-moisture areas. Longer dry time (6-8 hr to touch, 24 hr recoat). Requires mineral spirits cleanup. Higher VOC. Yellowing over time in low-light areas. Less common now (water-based alkyds available)
- **Sheens**: Flat (no shine — hides imperfections, good for ceilings), Matte (slight lustre — walls, hiding still good), Eggshell (subtle shine — most common for walls, washable), Satin (medium shine — trim, kitchens, bathrooms, more durable), Semi-gloss (shiny — woodwork, cabinets, high-moisture, very durable), High-gloss (most durable, shows all imperfections — for accent, doors)
- **Primer**: Essential for: new drywall (seals, prevents uneven absorption), stained surfaces (blocks stains, smoke, water marks), drastic color changes (dark→light, light→dark), glossy surfaces (improves adhesion), patched repairs (spot-prime). Tint primer toward final color for best coverage
- **Ceiling paint**: Flat sheen, thicker consistency (less drips), often with stain-blocking. White or off-white by default. Paint ceiling before walls (cut in first)

## Preparation
- **Clean walls**: Wash with TSP (trisodium phosphate) or degreaser — removes grease, grime, smoke residue. Rinse thoroughly. Tack cloth removes dust after sanding. Dirty walls = paint doesn't adhere properly
- **Repair defects**: Fill nail holes and small cracks with spackling compound (fast-drying). Larger holes: joint compound + mesh tape. For holes >6": cut out damaged drywall, install new piece (California patch or backer board)
- **Sand**: Sand spackled areas smooth (220 grit). Sand glossy surfaces dull (120-150 grit) for adhesion. Dust walls before painting (vacuum + tack cloth). Don't oversand joint compound (exposes paper)
- **Caulk**: Fill gaps between trim and walls, crown molding corners, window/door casings. Use painters caulk (acrylic latex — paintable, flexible). Tool with finger or caulk tool. Wipe excess with damp sponge
- **Protect floors/ furniture**: Drop cloths (canvas best — non-slip, absorbent; plastic = slip hazard, paint pools). Move furniture to center, cover with plastic. Remove outlet/switch covers. Blue painter's tape on baseboards, window frames, door casings. FrogTape for sharper lines

## Tools
- **Rollers**: Thickness: 1/4" nap (smooth surfaces — cabinets, doors), 3/8" (slightly textured walls — standard), 1/2" (moderate texture — most common for walls), 3/4" (rough texture — stucco, popcorn ceilings). Roller frame: cage type (standard), mini (for trim, small areas). Extension pole (avoids ladder, better pressure)
- **Brushes**: Nylon/Polyester blend (for latex — good for all paints). Natural bristle (for oil-based only — swells in water). Angled sash brush (2-2.5" for cutting in edges). Trim brush (1.5-2" for straight edges). Foam brushes (for smooth finish on small projects — less brush marks)
- **Cutting in**: Steady hand or use edging tool (Shur-Line). Paint pad edgers for fast cutting (not as precise). Practice steady hand on cardboard first
- **Other tools**: 5-in-1 tool (open cans, scrape, clean roller), paint can opener, paint grid (fits in bucket vs roller tray — better for large jobs), mud pan (for spackle), putty knife, sanding block, painter's pyramid (paint doors on)

## Technique
- **Cut in corners**: Brush a 2-3" band around edges of room (ceilings, corners, trim) before rolling. Cut one wall at a time. Use consistent brush angle. Don't let cut-in dry before rolling
- **Rolling technique**: Load roller (roll in tray/bucket grid until evenly coated, not dripping). Apply in W or M pattern, then fill without lifting. Roll in one direction (per coat). Minimal pressure — let roller do work. Back-roll: final pass from wet to dry area without lifting
- **Multiple coats**: Two coats minimum for best results (especially light colors over dark). Let first coat dry fully per manufacturer instructions. Lighter third coat if needed. Don't skimp — color uniformity matters
- **No lap marks**: Keep a "wet edge" — roll full wall from top to bottom without stopping mid-wall. If paint starts to set, stop at natural break (corner, window, door). Thin coats minimize lap marks
- **Painting ceilings**: First coat perpendicular to window, second coat parallel. Don't overwork — ceilings show roller marks. Use extension pole, avoid reaching

## Specific Surfaces
- **Trim & baseboards**: Use semi-gloss or satin. Paint with smooth strokes, not too much paint. Remove painter's tape immediately after paint set (not dry — pull away at 45° angle before film fully dries to avoid peeling). Two thin coats preferred
- **Doors**: Lay flat if possible (remove hinges). Paint in sections: panels first (brush), then center rails, outer rails, then edges. Use foam roller for smooth finish. Light sand between coats
- **Cabinets**: Clean thoroughly with degreaser. Sand with 120 grit. Prime with stain-blocking primer. Apply with foam roller/ high-density mini roller (for smooth finish). Consider cabinet-specific paint (enamel or cabinet paint). 3-4 coats for durability
- **Texture walls**: Use thicker nap roller (1/2"-3/4") to get paint into crevices. Don't over-roll (flattens texture). May need more paint than smooth walls
- **Stain blocking**: For water stains (ceiling leaks), smoke, crayon, marker — use shellac-based primer (Zinsser BIN) or latex stain-blocker (Kilz). Water stains will bleed through regular paint

## Cleanup & Storage
- **Brushes/rollers**: Latex: wash with warm soapy water until water runs clear. Spin roller in roller cleaner (attaches to hose) or squeeze dry. Reshape brush, store in paper sleeve or hang. Oil: mineral spirits → soapy water → dry. Never leave brush standing in water (deforms bristles)
- **Paint storage**: Seal can lid tightly (tap with hammer using wood block). Label with room name and date. Store upside down (lid seals with paint film). Keep in temperature-stable area (freezing ruins latex). Write formula sticker on top
- **Disposal**: Dry out leftover paint (add kitty litter or paint hardener). Dispose of dried paint in regular trash. Never pour paint down drain. Check local hazardous waste for oil-based/liquid paint

## Common Mistakes
- **Skipping primer**: Especially on new drywall, dark colors, stained surfaces. Paint peels, requires extra coats, stains bleed through
- **Thin coats vs thick coats**: Thin coats = better. Thick coats = drips, runs, longer dry time, visible brush/roller marks. Multiple thin coats better than one thick
- **Poor lighting**: Paint with good light (work lights if room dark). Inspect from different angles with raking light. You'll see missed spots, lap marks, thin coverage
- **Dried paint in roller**: Wrapped roller in plastic between coats (stays fresh hours). Use fresh roller for second coat of different color
- **Painting over wallpaper**: Never — primer may loosen wallpaper. Remove wallpaper first or install new drywall
