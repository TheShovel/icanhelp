# Home Pest Control

Prevention and management of common household pests.

## Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
- **Prevention first**: Seal entry points, remove food/water sources, eliminate harborage. Pesticides are last resort
- **Monitor**: Identify pest, locate source, assess severity. Not all insects are pests — many are beneficial
- **Threshold**: Act when pest population or damage exceeds acceptable level. A few ants is normal; trails of ants = action needed
- **Least-toxic methods first**: Physical (traps, barriers, caulking), mechanical (vacuum, removal), biological (beneficial insects), cultural (habitat modification), then chemical (targeted, low-toxicity)

## Ants
- **Common species**: Pavement ants (small, brown/black — nest under sidewalks/ foundation), Carpenter ants (larger, black — nest in damp wood, structural damage possible — sign of moisture problem, not termite), Odorous house ants (small, brown — smell like rotten coconut when crushed), Argentine ants (light brown — massive colonies, invasive)
- **Indoors**: Clean spills immediately, store food in sealed containers, take out trash regularly. Caulk cracks in foundation, around pipes, windows. Trim vegetation touching house (ant bridges)
- **Baits**: Gel or granular bait — worker ants carry back to colony, kill entire nest (including queen). Don't spray visible ants (kills only visible ones, colony survives). Terro liquid bait effective for many species. May take 2-7 days to eliminate colony
- **Carpenter ants**: Identify by sawdust piles (frass — finely shredded wood, insect body parts). They don't eat wood (unlike termites — they excavate galleries for nests). Fix moisture sources (leaky roof, leaky pipes). Replace rotted wood
- **Outdoor barrier**: Diatomaceous earth (DE — sharp microscopic particles cut ant exoskeleton, dehydrates them). Food-grade DE safe for pets. Reapply after rain

## Cockroaches
- **Species**: German (small, light brown, two dark stripes — most common indoor, rapid reproduction). American (large, reddish-brown, winged — sewers, basements). Oriental (medium, dark, prefer cool damp — outdoors). Brown-banded (small, prefer warm dry — appliances, electronics)
- **Prevention**: Clean kitchen thoroughly (no crumbs, no dirty dishes overnight). Fix plumbing leaks (roaches need water). Seal cracks (esp. around pipes entering walls). Store food in airtight containers. Empty garbage daily
- **Baits**: Gel baits (Advion, Maxforce) — apply in small dabs near hiding spots. Bait stations (for out of reach areas). Multiple roaches eat bait, return to nest, defecate poisoned feces → roaches eat it → domino effect
- **IGR (Insect Growth Regulator)**: Gentrol (hydroprene) stops nymphs from maturing, sterilizes adults. Disrupts reproduction cycle. Use with baits for full control
- **Professional treatment**: Fumigation for heavy infestations (entire structure sealed, tented, gas released). Heat treatment (entire structure heated to 120-140°F — kills all life stages). Not DIY — call licensed exterminator

## Termites
- **Signs**: Mud tubes (subterranean termites — highways from soil to wood). Damaged wood that sounds hollow when tapped. Swarmers (winged termites) near windows/doors in spring. Frass (drywood termites — small, ridged, pellet-like droppings). Discarded wings
- **Subterranean termites**: Most common and damaging — live in soil, build mud tubes to wood above. Moisture attracts them. Treatment: soil barrier (liquid termiticide — Termidor, Taurus SC — very effective, lasts 5-10 years). Bait stations (Senticon — slow-acting bait, colony elimination)
- **Drywood termites**: Live inside wood (no ground contact). Found in coastal/warm climates. No mud tubes. Treatment: fumigation (tent + gas), spot treatment (localized injection), heat treatment
- **Prevention**: No wood-to-ground contact (6"+ clearance between soil and siding). Proper drainage (water away from foundation). Remove dead trees, stumps, wood piles near house. Use termite-resistant wood (pressure treated for ground contact). Annual inspection by licensed company

## Rodents (Mice & Rats)
- **Entry points**: Mice can squeeze through 1/4" gap (dime-sized). Rats need 1/2" (quarter-sized). Check: gaps under doors, utility penetrations (HVAC, pipes, cables), gaps around windows, roof eaves, vents. Seal with copper mesh + expandable foam (copper won't rust like steel wool)
- **Signs**: Droppings (small dark pellets), gnaw marks, greasy rub marks along walls (from oils in fur), scratching noises in walls/ceiling (at night), nesting materials, food packages gnawed
- **Traps**: Snap traps (traditional — most effective, humane if killed instantly, use peanut butter bait, place perpendicular to wall with trigger facing wall). Electronic traps (Rat Zapper — kills with high voltage, no mess). Live traps (catch and release — must release >2 miles away or they return). Glue traps (inhumane — don't use; animal suffers, screams)
- **Rodenticides**: Poison baits — risk of secondary poisoning (pets/eagles/owls eat poisoned rodent). Warfarin-resistant rats common. Use only in tamper-resistant bait stations outdoors. Not recommended for indoor use (rodent dies in wall → smell for weeks)
- **Habitat modification**: Remove food sources (bird feeders, pet food, fallen fruit, garbage). Remove harborage (wood piles, overgrown vegetation, debris). Seal compost bin. Trim branches 3 ft from roofline (tree rat highways)

## Flies
- **House flies**: Breed in decaying organic matter (garbage, pet waste, compost). Control: tight garbage lids, clean garbage cans, clean pet areas promptly. Fly traps (outside). Fly paper (indoor). Screens on windows/doors
- **Fruit flies**: Breed in overripe fruit, fermenting liquids, drains. Control: remove fruit, clean drains (pour boiling water + baking soda + vinegar), apple cider vinegar trap (bowl covered with plastic wrap, small holes)
- **Drain flies (moth flies)**: Small, fuzzy, flutter around drains. Breed in organic film inside drains. Control: clean drain with enzyme cleaner or pipe brush, pour boiling water. Remove standing water in drip pans

## Bed Bugs
- **Identification**: Small, reddish-brown, flat, apple seed-sized. Bites in lines or clusters (often on arms, neck, face). Signs: small blood spots on sheets, rust-colored fecal spots on mattress, shed skins, sweet musty odor
- **Inspection**: Check mattress seams, box spring, bed frame, headboard, baseboards, nightstand. Use flashlight + credit card (scrape along seams). Inspection dogs available (very effective)
- **Treatment**: Heat treatment (whole room/ house heated to 120°F+ — kills all life stages, professional). Steam (directly on mattress, furniture — kills on contact, 200°F+). Diatomaceous earth (in cracks, behind baseboard — slow, takes days-weeks). Mattress encasements (traps existing bugs, prevents new ones). Professional chemical treatment (combined with heat)
- **Prevention**: When traveling: inspect hotel bed, elevate luggage on rack (not floor), keep luggage in bathroom first (hard surfaces). After travel: unpack over hard floor, wash all clothes in hot water (130°F+), dry on high heat (30 min kills bugs+eggs). Used furniture: inspect thoroughly, treat before bringing in
- **Do NOT**: Bug bombs/foggers (don't penetrate cracks, drive bugs deeper into walls. Can worsen infestation). Only use when combined with professional treatment
