# Home Upgrades & Renovation Projects

## Kitchen Backsplash Installation
- **Tile types**: ceramic subway (classic 3×6, easy to install, cheap $2-5/sq ft). Mosaic glass (sheets 12×12, expensive $10-30/sq ft, requires wet saw). Stone (marble, travertine, slate — needs sealing, $7-20/sq ft). Metal (stainless steel, copper — expensive, scratches show, $15-40/sq ft). Penny tile (circular tiles on mesh, small, busy, best as accent)
- **Tools**: tile cutter (wet saw $50/day rental or manual snap cutter for subway). Trowel (1/4×3/16" V-notch for small tile, 1/4×1/4" square notch for larger). Grout float (rubber, for pressing grout into joints). Sponge (for cleaning excess grout). Tile spacers (1/16-1/8" for subway, 1/4" for stone)
- **Install**: mark level line. Apply thinset (mortar) with trowel (hold at 45° to create ridges). Back butter each tile (apply thin skim of thinset with flat side for adhesion). Press tile firmly, use spacers. Cut tiles: use wet saw for straight cuts, angle grinder with diamond blade for curves, tile nippers for small cuts (snap small pieces). Let set 24 hrs. Grout: spread with float, press in diagonally (fill joints), sponge off excess 15-20 min later (too early = wash out grout from joints; too late = haze on tile hard to remove). Seal grout (sealer for porous tile) 72 hrs later

## Installing a Toilet
1. Remove old toilet (shut off water, flush, sponge out tank/bowl, unbolt floor, lift)
2. Replace wax ring (scrape old wax, install new one on flange). If tile floor >1/2" above flange: use extra-thick wax ring with plastic horn, or two wax rings stacked (stacked = extra thickness for higher tile)
3. Install new toilet bolts (T-bolts into flange). Set toilet down gently over bolts, press to seat wax ring, wiggle slightly. Do not rock side to side (can break porcelain)
4. Tighten nuts evenly (alternating), hand-tighten + 1/4 turn (overtightening cracks bowl). Connect water, check for leaks. Caulk around base (leave back open for leak detection)
5. Test flush. Adjust fill valve (water level 1/2" below overflow tube). Check flapper seal

## Installing a Vanity/Sink
1. Install vanity: place against wall, level (shim as needed). Secure to wall studs (screws through back rail). Connect drain pipe (P-trap, adjust height of drain stub-out with extension or cut tailpiece)
2. Sink installation: drop-in (cut hole in countertop, place sink, clamp from below). Undermount (attach sink to bottom of stone countertop before installing, heavy clips — professional recommended for stone)
3. Connect water supply lines (flexible braided stainless steel — use Teflon tape on threaded connections, hand-tight + 1/4 turn with wrench). Connect drain (slip nuts + rubber washers, hand-tighten)
4. Caulk sink to countertop (clear silicone, smooth with wet finger). Turn on water, check for leaks under sink (put paper towel under connections)

## Installing Ceiling Fan
1. Turn off power at breaker. Remove old fixture. Check: fan-rated electrical box (required for fan support — marked "For fan support" or load rated 70 lbs). If not fan-rated: install new box with fan-rated brace (old work brace: fits through 4" hole, expands against ceiling joists, supports 70+ lbs)
2. Assemble fan (motor, mounting bracket, blades, optional light kit). Follow manual. Most: connect bracket, wire (black=fan motor, blue=light, white=neutral, green=ground). Secure bracket. Attach fan. Install blades. Wire light kit
3. Test: turn on power, confirm remote/wall switch works, check for wobble (use balancing kit if needed). Run ceiling fan counterclockwise in summer (downward airflow, wind chill). Clockwise in winter (updraft pushes warm air down from ceiling)

## Installing Crown Molding
- **Cuts**: inside corners = cope joint (one piece butted to wall, other coped — cut 45° profile with coping saw, fine-tune with sandpaper). Outside corners = 45° miter (match angles, both cut to 45°, meet at 90° corner). For non-90° walls: measure actual angle with protractor/angle finder, divide by 2 for miter setting
- **Spring angle**: most crown = 38° (installed at 38° from wall). Cut upside down on miter saw (flat on saw table, positioned as it sits on wall) or use crown stops (multi-angle jig for compound miter saw). For 38° spring crown on 90° wall: set saw bevel to 33.9° and miter to 31.6° (or use compound charts). Flat cut: easier — crown flat on saw, set bevel + miter as chart says
- **Install**: nail with 18ga brad nailer (2" nails, hit studs if possible). Use glue (construction adhesive) on back for extra hold. Caulk (paintable caulk) fill corner gaps + nail holes. Paint: semi-gloss (easier to clean). Base shoe on floor after baseboards = no need for gaps at bottom

## Painting Cabinets
1. Remove doors + hardware. Label everything (tape with numbered code, note hinge location)
2. Clean with TSP (trisodium phosphate — degrease, remove buildup). Sand all surfaces (120 grit, then 220). Fill dents/ holes with wood filler
3. Prime: oil-based primer (Zinsser BIN shellac for water stains + knots). Or Stix (for laminates/ glossy). For laminate: use adhesion promoter + priming with BIN or specific bonding primer. Apply thin coats, sand 220 between
4. Paint: spray (best finish, overspray issues — use paint sprayer: HVLP, tent room with plastic. For small projects: spray with fine finish sprayer). Roll (foam roller for smooth surface, thin coats). Brush (for corners). Cabinet paint: enamel (self-leveling, dries hard — Benjamin Moore Advance, Sherwin Williams Emerald Urethane, or Urethan/Poly-based). 2-3 coats, sand 320 between coats
5. Reassemble after 24 hrs cure (enamel takes weeks to fully harden). Handle carefully for first 2 weeks. Don't slam doors
