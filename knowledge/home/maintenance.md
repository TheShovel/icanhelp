# Home Maintenance

## Cleaning Schedule
- **Daily**: make bed, wipe counters, do dishes, 5 min tidy
- **Weekly**: vacuum/mop floors, clean bathroom, change sheets, wipe mirrors, take out trash
- **Monthly**: clean windows, dust blinds, wipe baseboards, clean microwave/fridge, descale kettle
- **Seasonal**: shampoo carpets, wash curtains, clean gutters, service HVAC, drain water heater
- **Yearly**: deep clean appliances, check smoke detectors, inspect roof, seal grout

## Unclogging Drains
1. Pour boiling water down drain
2. 1/2 cup baking soda + 1/2 cup vinegar, cover 15 min, flush with hot water
3. Plunger over drain (seal overflow with wet cloth)
4. Remove trap under sink (bucket underneath, clean out)
5. Drain snake/auger for tough clogs
- Never use chemical drain cleaner on already-clogged drain (can create toxic fumes)
- Prevent: drain strainers, don't pour grease down sink, monthly hot water flush

## Unclogging Toilet
1. Plunger: firm seal, push slowly then pull sharply (not hard — can crack bowl)
2. If plunger doesn't work: bucket of hot water from waist height (not boiling)
3. Toilet auger (closet snake): crank into hole, pull back debris
4. Remove toilet (last resort): shut off water, flush remaining, unscrew from floor, replace wax ring

## Paint Touch-Ups
- Keep leftover paint in airtight jar labeled with room/date
- Stir paint thoroughly (don't shake — creates bubbles)
- Roller for walls (W pattern), brush for edges/corners
- Tape edges with painter's tape, remove while paint is still slightly wet
- Two thin coats better than one thick coat
- Latex paint cleans with soap+water; oil-based needs mineral spirits
- Touch up: feather edges beyond damaged area to blend

## Smoke Detectors
- Test monthly (press test button)
- Replace batteries twice/year (when clocks change)
- Replace entire unit every 10 years
- Have one on every floor and outside each bedroom
- Interconnected: when one goes off, all go off
- CO detector on each floor (carbon monoxide is odorless, deadly)
- Never paint a smoke detector

## Thermostat & HVAC
- Summer: 74-78°F when home, 80+ when away (every degree saves 3-5% on cooling)
- Winter: 68°F when home, 60-62°F when away/night
- Change HVAC filter every 1-3 months (cheap filters changed often > expensive filters rarely)
- Programmable/ smart thermostat saves ~10% on bills
- Ceiling fans counterclockwise in summer (cooling breeze), clockwise in winter (pushes warm air down)
- Seal windows: weatherstripping, caulk gaps, window film for old windows

## Pest Control
- **Ants**: find trail, clean with vinegar water, seal entry points, bait traps
- **Cockroaches**: keep kitchen spotless, no cardboard, seal cracks, gel bait
- **Mice/rats**: seal holes (steel wool + caulk), traps (snap > glue/poison), don't leave food out
- **Fruit flies**: remove ripe fruit, apple cider vinegar trap (bowl + plastic wrap + holes)
- **Spiders**: reduce clutter, vacuum webs, seal cracks (most spiders are beneficial)
- Prevention: keep food sealed, take trash out daily, fix leaks, declutter

## Basic Plumbing
- Know where main water shut-off valve is (usually basement/crawl space near street)
- Sink won't drain: try plunger, then remove trap
- Running toilet: usually bad flapper (cheap fix, $5 part)
- Low water pressure: clean aerator on faucet tip (unscrew, soak in vinegar)
- Dripping faucet: usually worn washer or O-ring
- Water heater: set to 120°F (prevents burns, saves energy), drain yearly to remove sediment
- For burst pipe: shut off main valve immediately, open faucets to relieve pressure

## Electrical Safety
- Circuit breaker panel: know where it is, label breakers
- GFCI outlets (in kitchen/bath/basement/garage) test monthly (press test/reset)
- Tripping breaker: unplug everything on that circuit, reset, plug back one by one to find issue
- Flickering lights: loose bulb, faulty switch, or wiring issue (call electrician)
- Never use extension cords as permanent wiring
- Outlet feels warm: potential fire hazard — stop using, call electrician
- Space heaters: 3 ft clearance from anything, plug directly into wall (not extension cord)
- Smoke/CO detector chirping: replace battery immediately

## Lawn & Garden Basics
- Mow high: taller grass shades roots, needs less water, fewer weeds
- Water deeply but less often (1" per week including rain) — early morning best
- Mulch: 2-3" layer retains moisture, suppresses weeds, regulates soil temp
- Compost: green (grass clippings, kitchen scraps) + brown (leaves, cardboard) = black gold
- Native plants: less water, less fertilizer, support local ecosystem
- Plant in spring/fall (not summer heat)
- Test soil pH before fertilizing — most plants prefer 6.0-7.0
