# Landscaping

Design, installation, and maintenance of outdoor spaces.

## Design Principles
- **Scale & proportion**: Hardscape elements (patios, paths) should suit house and yard size. Oversized elements overwhelm small spaces
- **Balance**: Symmetrical (formal — matching plants on both sides) or asymmetrical (informal — different elements with equal visual weight)
- **Unity**: Repeating materials, colors, or plant types creates cohesive design. Use 2-3 materials max (stone, wood, metal)
- **Focal point**: Draws eye — specimen tree, water feature, sculpture, interesting hardscape. One focal point per view
- **Transition**: Gradual change between areas — tall plants → medium → low, sun → shade, formal → natural
- **Line**: Curved lines feel natural, relaxing; straight lines feel formal, modern. Lines direct movement through space

## Hardscaping
- **Patios**: Concrete (poured, stamped, stained — most durable), pavers (interlocking concrete or stone — easier to repair), flagstone (natural, irregular — higher maintenance), gravel (cheapest, but weeds, uncomfortable to walk)
- **Walkways**: Main paths 4-5 ft wide (two people), secondary 2-3 ft wide. Stepping stones spaced for natural gait (24" apart center-to-center)
- **Retaining walls**: For sloped yards — materials: interlocking blocks (easiest DIY), natural stone, timber, poured concrete. Walls over 4ft need engineer assessment, drainage (perforated pipe behind wall)
- **Decks**: Pressure-treated pine (most common, needs staining every 2-3 years), cedar/redwood (naturally rot-resistant, ages gray), composite (Trex — no staining, but gets hot in sun), PVC (no maintenance, looks plastic). Joist spacing 16" OC standard
- **Fencing**: Functions (privacy, security, boundary, windbreak). Materials: Cedar (natural, ages gray), pressure-treated pine (painted/stained), PVC (no maintenance, less natural), chain link (cheapest but unattractive), wrought iron (ornamental)
- **Edging**: Separates lawn from beds — steel (cleanest look), plastic (cheap, can heave), stone/brick (heavy, permanent), concrete (permanent but cracks)

## Grading & Drainage
- **Slope away from house**: Minimum 5% grade (6" drop in first 10 ft from foundation) — prevents water pooling against basement
- **Swales**: Shallow ditches redirect water — grass lined (invisible), rock lined (decorative, french drain if perforated pipe added)
- **French drains**: Perforated pipe in gravel trench — collects groundwater, carries it to outlet. Slope of 1% minimum
- **Dry creek bed**: Decorative rocks in shaped swale — carries occasional runoff, looks natural with river rocks + boulders
- **Rain garden**: Shallow depression planted with water-tolerant native plants — collects roof/ driveway runoff, allows infiltration. 30% deeper than typical garden, plant in basins

## Planting Design
- **Foundation planting**: Plants along house foundation — layer from tall (corners) to medium to short. Leave 2-3 ft between mature plant width and house wall
- **Shrubs**: Evergreen (year-round structure — boxwood, holly, juniper) vs deciduous (seasonal interest — hydrangea, forsythia, lilac
- **Perennials**: Return yearly — daylilies, hosta (shade), coneflower, black-eyed Susan, lavender. Divide every 3-4 years
- **Annuals**: Replace yearly — petunia, marigold, impatiens (shade), zinnia, begonia. High maintenance but continuous color
- **Ground covers**: Replace lawn in difficult areas — creeping thyme (sun, walkable), pachysandra (shade), vinca minor, ajuga, sedum (dry/rocky)
- **Trees**: Shade trees (oak, maple, elm — 40-60ft+), ornamental (cherry, dogwood, redbud — 15-25ft, flowers), evergreen (spruce, pine, arborvitae). Plant at least 15-20ft from house

## Lawn Care
- **Mowing**: 1/3 rule — never cut more than 1/3 of grass height at once. Mow height: cool-season 3-4", warm-season 1.5-2.5". Keep blades sharp (dull blades tear grass, cause brown tips)
- **Watering**: 1-1.5 inches per week (including rain). Deep, infrequent watering (2x/week) better than daily light watering — encourages deep roots. Water early morning (reduces evaporation, disease)
- **Fertilizing**: Nitrogen (N) for green growth, phosphorus (P) for roots/blooms, potassium (K) for overall health. Slow-release organic (Milorganite) vs synthetic (Scotts). Fall fertilization most important for cool-season grass
- **Aeration**: Core aerate compacted soil — fall for cool-season, spring/early summer for warm-season. Improves water penetration, reduces thatch
- **Overseeding**: Spread seed over existing lawn to fill thin spots. Best in early fall (cool-season) or late spring (warm-season)

## Plant Selection by Site
- **Full sun (6+ hrs)**: Vegetables, roses, lavender, succulents, most annuals
- **Part shade (3-6 hrs)**: Hostas, ferns, astilbe, coral bells, hydrangea
- **Full shade (<3 hrs)**: Ferns, hosta (varieties), lamium, bleeding heart, Japanese forest grass
- **Wet soil**: Willow, red maple, river birch, iris, cattails, sedges
- **Dry soil**: Lavender, yarrow, sedum, ornamental grasses, Russian sage, yucca
- **Coastal (salt)**: Rugosa rose, juniper, sea thrift, beachgrass, bayberry
- **Deer-resistant**: Boxwood, lavender, rosemary, daffodil, yarrow, Russian sage, barberry
