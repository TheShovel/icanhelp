# Real Estate & Home Buying

## Renting vs Buying
- **Buying is NOT always better** — it's a leveraged investment with high transaction costs (5-6% fees when selling)
  - Buy if: plan to stay 5+ years, have stable income, can afford maintenance, want control
  - Rent if: staying <5 years, less responsibility preferred, lower monthly cost important, mobility needed
- **Rules of thumb**: buy if monthly mortgage ≤ 30% of gross income AND price-to-rent ratio > 15 (buying cheaper if ratio < 15)
  - Price-to-rent ratio: median home price / annual rent (e.g., $500k / $30k = 16.7 — borderline buy)
- **Hidden costs of owning**: maintenance (1-2% of home value/year), property taxes, insurance, HOA, utilities often higher, repairs (roof, HVAC, water heater = $5k-15k each, every 10-20 years)

## The Home Buying Process
1. **Prepare finances**: check credit (aim 740+ for best rates), save for down payment (3-20%+), get pre-approved (shows sellers you're serious), calculate total monthly cost (mortgage + tax + insurance + HOA + maintenance)
   - Down payment: 3% conventional, 3.5% FHA, 0% VA (military), 5-20% conventional. Under 20% = PMI (private mortgage insurance, extra $50-200/month until 20% equity)
   - Closing costs: 2-5% of purchase price (loan origination, appraisal, title, escrow, recording)
2. **Find a real estate agent**: buyer's agent is FREE to you (paid by seller typically 2.5-3% of sale price)
   - Interview agents — find someone who communicates well, knows the area, negotiates well
   - You want a full-time agent (not part-time), who's done 10+ transactions/year
3. **Make an offer**: agent advises on offer price based on comps (recent sales of similar homes)
   - Earnest money deposit: 1-3% of offer, held in escrow — shows good faith, refundable if contingencies not met
   - Contingencies protect you: inspection (must have), financing (must have — if loan falls through, you get deposit back), appraisal (if under appraised, can renegotiate or walk)
   - Escalation clause: automatically increase offer up to max if competing offers exist
4. **Inspection**: hire inspector ($400-700), attends inspection, asks questions. Major issues: foundation, roof, electrical, plumbing, HVAC, mold, termites, water damage
   - You can ask seller to fix, give credit, reduce price, or walk away (if inspection contingency)
   - Also consider: termite inspection, radon test, sewer scope, chimney inspection
5. **Appraisal**: bank requires appraisal to confirm home is worth loan amount — if under appraised, you pay difference or renegotiate
6. **Close**: final walk-through (verify repairs, no new damage), sign documents (mortgage, deed, disclosures), wire closing costs, get keys!

## Mortgage Types
- **Conventional**: best rates, 620+ credit, 3% down minimum
  - Conforming: meets Fannie Mae/Freddie Mac limits ($766,550 in most areas 2024). Non-conforming/jumbo: exceeds limit, higher rates, more strict
  - PMI drops at 78% LTV automatically (or you can request at 80%)
- **FHA**: 580+ credit, 3.5% down, more lenient — but higher fees (MIP mortgage insurance for life of loan unless 10% down)
- **VA**: 0% down, no PMI, competitive rates — for veterans/military — best loan type available
- **USDA**: 0% down for rural/suburban areas — income limits apply
- **Fixed rate**: rate stays same for entire loan (15, 20, 30 years). 30-year most common, 15-year higher payment but lower rate + less interest
- **Adjustable Rate Mortgage (ARM)**: lower initial rate (5/1, 7/1, 10/1 — fixed for X years then adjusts annually)
  - Good if: selling/refinancing before adjustment period. Risky: rates could skyrocket
- **Interest rate vs APR**: APR = rate + fees — use APR to compare total cost of loans

## Finding a Home
- Location > house: can change almost everything about a house. You can't change location, school district, commute, or neighbors
  - Check: flood zone (FEMA maps), noise (near highway/train/airport?), school quality if relevant, crime rates, commute time during rush hour, future development plans (new shopping center could increase property value or traffic)
- Prioritize what you can't change (lot, location, layout, natural light, noise) over cosmetics (paint, carpet, counters — those are cheap to change)
- Look at open houses, but also drive by at different times (noise, traffic, neighbor activity)
- After many viewings, you'll start to recognize quality and red flags — trust your gut

## Negotiating
- **Buyer's market** (more sellers than buyers): offer below asking, ask for closing costs, more contingencies
- **Seller's market** (more buyers than sellers): offer at/above asking, fewer contingencies, pre-inspection, write personal letter, larger earnest money
- Always negotiate — seller expects counter-offer. Worst they say is no
- Items to negotiate: price, closing costs, repairs from inspection, home warranty (seller pays, ~$500-800), move-in date, appliances included
- If appraisal comes in low: dispute (if comps justify), bring cash difference, ask seller to lower price, or walk away (if appraisal contingency)

## After Purchase
- **Homeowner's insurance**: required by lender. Covers fire, theft, liability, wind — NOT flood (separate policy if in flood zone)
- **Property taxes**: assessed value × mill rate — can change yearly. Appeal if over-assessed
- **Maintenance budget**: 1-2% of home value/year saved. Big ticket items: roof (20-30yr, $7-15k), HVAC (15-20yr, $5-12k), water heater (10-15yr, $800-1500), windows (20-30yr, $10-20k)
  - New home = less maintenance initially, but save anyway — it will come
- **Home equity**: difference between value and mortgage balance. Can borrow against (HELOC or home equity loan) for renovations, but don't treat home like ATM
  - Home prices fluctuate — 2008 saw 30%+ drops in many areas
- **Refinancing**: if rates drop 1%+ below your current rate, refinancing may save money (closing costs 2-5% of loan, need to stay in home long enough to recoup)
- **Renovations that add value**: kitchen (minor updates > full gut), bathroom, outdoor living, energy efficiency (windows, insulation). Over-improving = won't recoup cost
