# Home Theater & Audio Systems

## TV Technology
- **OLED**: perfect blacks (each pixel self-emits, can turn off completely), infinite contrast, wide viewing angle, no bloom. Burn-in risk (static elements like news ticker/logos can cause permanent retention over years). LG, Sony. Best for: dark rooms, movie lovers, color accuracy, HDR. 55-83"
  - OLED evo (LG): brighter than standard OLED, better for HDR. MLA (Micro Lens Array) on Panasonic MZ2000/G3 models: brighter still
- **QLED/LED**: LCD with quantum dot enhancement (brighter than OLED, no burn-in). Better HDR brightness, inferior black levels (blooming/bleeding light around bright objects on dark backgrounds). Samsung. Mini-LED: more dimming zones = better contrast (TCL, Sony, Samsung Neo QLED). Best for: bright rooms, mixed usage, gaming (no burn-in worry), HDR brightness (for sunlit scenes)
- **MicroLED**: next-gen, self-emissive like OLED but inorganic (no burn-in). Modular, expensive ($100K+ for 110"). Best: ultimate TV, commercially limited
- **Resolution**: 4K (3,840×2,160) — standard now. 8K (7,680×4,320) — limited content, expensive, not worth premium
- **HDR**: Dolby Vision (dynamic metadata per scene — 12-bit color, best). HDR10+ (dynamic, Samsung/Amazon). HDR10 (static metadata, baseline). HLG (broadcast). Dolby Vision: best HDR format, supported on LG, Sony, Panasonic, TCL, HiSense. Make sure your TV supports it

## Sound Systems
- **Soundbar**: simple, better than TV speakers. 2.1 (stereo + sub). 3.1 (center channel + LR + sub). 5.1 (surround). Virtual Atmos (simulated height channels, some effective). Best: Sonos Arc, Bose 900, Samsung Q990C, Sony HT-A7000. Most soundbars: better dialogue clarity, decent bass, easy setup. Limitations: limited surround separation, no true Atmos without separate rear speakers
- **AV Receiver**: for separate speakers (5.1, 7.1, Atmos). Setup: receiver powers speakers, source devices connect to receiver. Output: HDMI to TV (eARC for audio return). Speaker wire to speakers. Subwoofer cable to sub. Surround placement: at 110° from center (behind, slightly to side) for 5.1; in-ceiling for Atmos (or up-firing modules). Center: above/below TV, aimed at ear level. Receiver: Denon, Marantz, Sony, Yamaha, Onkyo
  - Dolby Atmos (5.1.2 = 5 ear-level + 1 sub + 2 height). .4 height = 4 ceiling/upfiring speakers
  - Room correction: Audyssey (Denon/Marantz), YPAO (Yamaha), Dirac Live (Arcam, Onkyo, NAD) — EQ + calibration using supplied mic. Dirac: preferred by enthusiasts (adjustable target curve)
- **Speakers**: bookshelf (compact, 2-way, for stands/shelves) vs floorstanding (taller, more bass, fuller). Center (dialogue). Surround (side/rear). Subwoofer (bass). Materials: soft dome tweeter (warm) vs metal (bright). Woofer cone: paper (natural), polypropylene (consistent), kevlar (light, stiff). Crossover: splits frequencies between drivers. Brands: KEF (Uni-Q coaxial driver), Bowers & Wilkins (diamond tweeters), Focal (beryllium tweeters), Klipsch (horn tweeter, efficient, dynamic), Polk (value)

## Speaker Placement
- **Stereo**: equilateral triangle (speakers + listener). 60° apart (domestic: 38-42° to each speaker + 60° to both). Tweeters at ear level. Toe-in: point speakers slightly toward listener, aim at ears (or behind head for wider soundstage). Wall distance: 1-3 ft from walls for rear-ported speakers. Boundary coupling from corner boost bass but can produce overblown 60-150 Hz
- **Surround**: 5.1 — center above/below TV, front L/R at TV sides, surrounds 110° angle, behind listener. Subwoofer: corner placement = more bass (calibration needed). Sub position: walk around room, mark spots where bass sounds fullest, place sub there (or in corner). Sub crawl: place sub in listening position, walk around room to hear where bass best, then place sub there (swap)
- **Atmos**: .2 ceiling speakers directly above listening position (or in front/behind). Upfiring modules bounce off ceiling — OK for reflective ceiling but inferior to actual overhead speakers

## Subwoofer
- **Types**: sealed (tight, accurate, less output). Ported (more boom, deeper extension, boomier, can be boomy if poorly designed). Typically: sealed for music quality, ported for home theater impact. Sizes: 8-12" (common), 15-18" (pro, deep bass). SVS, HSU, Rythmik, Monolith, JL Audio (high-end), REL (music-oriented). Placement: near front wall/corner, but varies by room. Sub integration: cross 80Hz (THX standard) with main speakers HPF 80Hz. Phase: adjust to blend (no cancellation). Room modes: bass nulls + peaks vary by listening position. Multiple subs help smooth response
- **Crossover**: set on receiver or sub plate amp. 80 Hz standard (THX) — redirects bass below 80 Hz to sub. For small bookshelves: 100-120 Hz. For large towers: 40-60 Hz. LFE: .1 channel full bandwidth (up to 120Hz). Phase control: align sub with mains for smooth bass transition. Normal 0°, may need to flip to 180° or adjust continuously (SVS sub can adjust continuously 0-180°)

## Streaming & Sources
- **Streaming devices**: Apple TV 4K (best UI, Dolby Vision + Atmos, fast, high bitrate). Roku (cheap, simple, good for most). Nvidia Shield TV / Shield Pro (best for local Plex + lossless Atmos TrueHD passthrough + AI upscaling). Fire TV (Amazon integration, ads, slower). Google Chromecast with Google TV (best UI for phone)
  - Smart TV apps: built-in are convenient but often slower, less supported. Apple TV has apps that get updated longer
- **Audio formats**: Dolby Digital+ (compressed surround, streaming). Dolby TrueHD (lossless, Blu-ray). DTS-HD MA (lossless). DTS:X (object-based like Atmos). LPCM (uncompressed). Atmos in TrueHD: lossless, from Blu-ray. Atmos in DD+: lossy, from streaming. For best quality: Blu-ray or high-bitrate streaming (Apple TV, Kaleidescape). Streaming services: Apple TV Movies/TV+, Netflix Premium 4K, Disney+, HBO Max, Amazon Prime Video. All use Dolby Digital+ (compressed) with Atmos when supported. Blu-ray/UHD Blu-ray disc: TrueHD/DTS-HD MA + Atmos/DTS:X bitstream — best quality
